## Mandatory Item 19 cohort-comparability rule

If Item 19 uses a pro-forma, illustrative, or comparable cohort whose
restaurants operated under a DIFFERENT royalty rate, fee tier, advertising
fund contribution, technology fee, or other material economic burden than the
terms offered to current new franchisees, A2 MUST surface this as a
high-severity contradiction or comparability warning.

Required extraction steps:

1. **Identify the historical cohort economics.**
   Read Item 19 and any accompanying footnotes or caveats. Extract the
   royalty rate, advertising fund rate, technology fee, and any other
   fee component used in the pro-forma / cohort computation. Record exact
   source pages and the verbatim caveat language (≤30 words per quote).

2. **Identify the current new-franchisee economics.**
   Read Item 6 (fee table) and Item 5 (initial franchise fee / ongoing fees)
   for the royalty rate, advertising fund rate, technology fee, and any other
   fee component that current new franchisees are required to pay. Record
   exact source pages.

3. **Compute the basis-point difference.**
   When the cohort rate and the current rate are both directly disclosed,
   compute the difference in basis points (e.g., 4.00% vs 5.00% = 100 bps).
   If not directly supported, note "not computable — rates not both disclosed"
   and skip the computation. Do NOT invent rates.

4. **Compute approximate dollar overstatement or understatement.**
   When both rates and Item 19 disclosed sales tiers (e.g., average gross
   sales, median gross sales, or quartile breakpoints) are directly
   supported, compute the approximate annual dollar impact at each disclosed
   tier.
   Example (McDonald's-style): if the pro-forma uses a 4% royalty but
   current new franchisees pay 5%, and the disclosed average gross sales
   are $X, the approximate OIBOC overstatement is X × 0.01.
   If sales tiers are not disclosed or rates are not both disclosed, note
   "not computable" and skip. Do NOT invent sales figures.

5. **Save the finding.**
   Create a structured entry with:
     - id: "item19_cohort_comparability_warning"
     - severity: "high"
     - cohort_royalty_rate: number or null
     - cohort_ad_fund_rate: number or null
     - cohort_tech_fee: number or null
     - current_royalty_rate: number or null
     - current_ad_fund_rate: number or null
     - current_tech_fee: number or null
     - basis_point_difference: number or null
     - dollar_impact_by_tier: array of {tier_label, tier_sales, annual_impact}
       or null
     - source_pages_item19: array of page numbers
     - source_pages_item6: array of page numbers
     - reasoning: string explaining the comparability gap
     - quote_fragments: array of short verbatim references (≤30 words each)

   Add this entry to:
   - `contradictions` family in `09_final_canonical.json`
   - `contradictions` family in `12_canonical_enriched_v2.json`
   - `unresolveds` family (as an open-question entry) in both canonical files
   - Section K (Contradictions) of `08_final_report.md`
   - Section F (Item 19) of `08_final_report.md` with full narrative

   Write the raw finding to `RT_depth_item19_cohort_comparability.json`.

If Item 19 does not exist, or the cohort uses the same fee structure as
current new franchisees, skip this rule and note in the scorecard:
"Item 19 cohort comparability: no discrepancy found (or Item 19 not present)."
