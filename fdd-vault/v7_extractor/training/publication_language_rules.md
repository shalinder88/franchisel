# Publication Language Rules — Safe Explanation Policy

## Purpose
Public-facing language must be legally safe. The extractor powers reports and memos,
not just JSON. Language matters.

## Safe Formulations (USE THESE)
- "The filing reports..."
- "The agreement states..."
- "The FDD discloses..."
- "The system appears to..."
- "This increases burden because..."
- "Based on the FDD filing, the franchisor..."
- "Item 19 shows average gross sales of $X for Y units..."
- "The most recent Item 20 data shows N net closures..."
- "The audited financial statements indicate..."

## Unsafe Formulations (NEVER USE)
- "This is a scam"
- "Owners will make $X"
- "You can expect to earn..."
- "This franchise is profitable"
- "This is a bad investment"
- "The franchisor is lying"
- "Our analysis proves..."
- "We guarantee..."
- "Based on our calculations, profit is..."

## Key Rules
1. Always attribute to the FDD filing, never to Franchisel analysis
2. Revenue is not profit — always make this clear
3. Average is not median — averages can be skewed by outliers
4. Item 19 populations may exclude weak units — disclose exclusion rules
5. "Estimated" means estimated — do not present as guaranteed
6. Scores are editorial opinions, not FDD facts — label them clearly
7. State-specific rights may differ — note when addenda exist
8. Past performance is not future guarantee — always include this caveat
