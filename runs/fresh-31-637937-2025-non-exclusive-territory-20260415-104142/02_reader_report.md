# Reader Report — Pillar To Post, Inc. (637937-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand & System:** Pillar To Post, Inc. — home inspection franchise operating since 1995; non-exclusive territories offered since 2009 (exclusive territories discontinued for new franchisees). Parent is FS Brands, Inc. (Delaware), ultimate parent is FirstService Corporation (publicly traded).
2. **System Size:** 412 US units (376 non-exclusive, 36 exclusive) and 43 Canadian units as of Dec 31, 2024. Zero company-owned outlets.
3. **Entity Structure:** Delaware corporation (incorporated July 11, 1994). Canadian affiliate Pillar To Post (Canada) handles Canadian operations. FS Brands is a multi-brand franchisor (CertaPro Painters, Paul Davis Restoration, California Closets, Floor Coverings International).
4. **Investment Range:** $102,690–$134,290 total initial investment, including $58,500 initial franchise fee (non-refundable) + $1,000 brand conference + $1,000 tablet = $60,500 paid to franchisor.
5. **Ongoing Fee Burden:** 7% royalty + 4% brand fee + per-inspection IT fee ($5.50) + inspection numbers ($28.50 each) + financial reporting ($30/mo) + EZBook ($29.75–$33.45/booking) + PTP360 ($3.10/inspection) + PTPHomeManual ($9.45/inspection) + brand conference ($900–$1,200/yr). Minimum royalties escalate from $0 to $2,328/mo over 5 years.
6. **Item 19:** FPR provided. 293 non-exclusive units reported for 2024. Group A (1 inspector): avg $137,522, median $124,002. Group B (2 inspectors): avg $269,792. Group C (3-5): avg $574,582. Group D (6+): avg $1,997,817. Systemwide sales $85.6M across 316 units (incl. exclusive).
7. **Growth Trajectory:** Net shrinking. Non-exclusive: 440→436→407→376 (2022–2024). Exclusive: 45→39→38→36. Total 2024 net change: -33 units. Only 12 new non-exclusive openings in 2024. Zero projected openings for 2025.
8. **Financial Health (FS Brands consolidated):** Total revenue $850.4M (2024), income from operations $82.7M, net income $60.1M. Total assets $591.2M, total equity $237.5M. Auditor PwC, unqualified opinion, no going concern.
9. **Territory:** Non-exclusive. Protected only from franchisees with territories *outside* yours. Existing exclusive-territory converts may have protected referral sources within your territory. Minimum gross revenue requirements: $100K yr1 escalating to $500K yr5.
10. **Contract Term:** 5 years initial, up to 5 renewals of 5 years each (max 30 years). Renewal fee $2,500. Must sign general release on renewal. May be asked to sign materially different terms.
11. **Noncompete:** 2 years post-term within old territory. Subject to state law overrides.
12. **Disputes:** Arbitration in Florida. Florida law. Subject to extensive state overrides (CA, IL, IN, MD, MI, MN, NY, ND, RI, SD, VA, WA, WI).
13. **Special Risks:** Mandatory minimum royalty payments regardless of sales. Out-of-state dispute resolution (Florida).
14. **Supplier Revenue:** $5.075M from required supplier purchases = 35.9% of franchisor's $14.1M total revenue. Required purchases ~64% of startup cost, ~50% of ongoing operating cost.
15. **Key Red Flag:** System is shrinking significantly — net -33 units in 2024 alone. Zero projected openings. Non-renewal rate notable (22 non-renewals in 2024 for non-exclusive).

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
Non-exclusive territory defined by counties/zip codes based on local realtor board boundaries. No standard size. Territory is "protected" only to the extent that franchisees with territories *outside* yours cannot operate in yours — but other franchisees assigned the *same* non-exclusive territory CAN compete with you.

Key territory provisions:
- Cannot operate outside territory without consent
- Cannot solicit outside territory (including internet, direct marketing)
- Existing exclusive-to-non-exclusive converts may have protected referral sources in your territory
- Territory may be modified at renewal
- Minimum Gross Revenue requirements: $100K (yr1), $200K (yr2), $300K (yr3), $400K (yr4), $500K (yr5)
- Must book 80% of inspections through EZBook if using it
- No right to acquire additional franchises
- Cannot relocate territory (can relocate home office within territory)
- Franchisor retains all rights to sell through alternative channels, internet, etc.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
"Pillar To Post Home Inspectors" registered USPTO principal register (Reg. #4614467, Sept 30, 2014). Owned by Canadian affiliate, exclusively licensed to US franchisor. No known infringements. Domain: pillartopost.com. No franchisee websites/social media without written approval.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
No patents. Copyrights claimed on forms, ads, materials, Manual. AI restriction: prohibited from entering proprietary information into public/open AI models.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
Full-time, exclusive (40+ hrs/week). Must successfully complete training and Foundations for Success. For entity franchisees, 50%+ beneficial owner must operate full-time. All shareholders/members/partners must sign personal guarantee.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on Sales
Must offer only approved services. Must offer Plus, Premium, and Prestige inspection packages. No recommending repairs to self or others. Cannot solicit outside territory. Existing exclusive-to-non-exclusive converts may have protected referral sources.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term & Renewal
- Initial term: 5 years
- Renewal: up to 5 additional 5-year terms (max 30 years)
- Renewal conditions: 6 months written notice, full compliance, current on all payments, $2,500 fee, sign general release (Exhibit G-4)
- **Materially different terms risk:** "You may be asked to sign a contract with materially different terms and conditions than your original contract." Fees on renewal capped at what is imposed on similarly-situated renewing franchisees. Territory boundaries may be modified at renewal.

### Termination
- By franchisee: Generally none, except 180-day option for converted businesses
- By franchisor without cause: Not applicable
- By franchisor with cause: Extensive list of curable (10/30 day cure) and non-curable defaults
- Non-curable: insolvency, bankruptcy, failure to complete training, failure to open, abandonment, conviction, public safety threat, unauthorized transfer, failure to meet minimum revenue for 2 consecutive years, confidentiality breach, false records, trademark misuse, refusal of inspection, inadequate insurance, 3+ notices in 12 months, failure to implement software, failure to attend conferences

### Transfer
- Requires franchisor approval. No transfer without approval except to entity franchisee formed per agreement.
- Conditions: transferee qualifies, completes training, $4,000 fee, $15,000 referral fee if applicable, cure all defaults, transferee signs new agreement (may have different terms), term of new agreement expires on original expiration date, general release required, extended E&O insurance for 2 years.
- Franchisor right of first refusal.

### Death/Disability
- Estate has 120 days to sell, assign, transfer, or apply to continue.

### Noncompete
- During term: no competing business
- Post-term: 2 years within old territory
- Subject to state law (CA, MN, SD, WA have material overrides)

### Dispute Resolution
- Arbitration in Florida (except certain claims)
- Litigation in Florida
- Florida law applies
- Subject to extensive state law overrides
