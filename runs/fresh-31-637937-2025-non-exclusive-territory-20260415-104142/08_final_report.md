# Final Diligence Report — Pillar To Post, Inc.
## Franchise Disclosure Document Analysis (Filing 637937-2025)
### Issuance Date: March 25, 2025

---

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System Identity:** Pillar To Post, Inc. is a Delaware-incorporated (1994) home inspection franchisor operating under the "Pillar To Post Home Inspectors" brand. The system has been franchising since March 1995.

2. **Entity Structure & Ownership:** Pillar To Post is a subsidiary of FS Brands, Inc. (Delaware), which is 97.18% owned by FS Property Services (U.S.) Inc., an indirect 100% subsidiary of FirstService Corporation (publicly traded). FS Brands operates five franchise systems: CertaPro Painters (303 US units), Paul Davis Restoration (245 US), California Closets (38 US), Floor Coverings International (281 US), and Pillar To Post (412 US). A Canadian affiliate, Pillar To Post (Canada), operates 43 Canadian units.

3. **System Size & Growth:** As of December 31, 2024, the system had 412 US units (376 non-exclusive, 36 exclusive) and zero company-owned outlets. The system is in significant decline: non-exclusive units dropped from 440 to 376 over 2022-2024 (net -64), with only 12 openings in 2024 against 21 terminations and 22 non-renewals. Exclusive units dropped from 45 to 36 over the same period. Zero projected openings across all states.

4. **Investment Range:** Total initial investment of $102,690 to $134,290, including a $58,500 non-refundable initial franchise fee. Home-based business — no real estate required.

5. **Fee Burden:** 7% royalty + 4% brand fee = 11% of gross revenues, plus per-inspection fees (IT $5.50, inspection numbers $28.50, PTP360 $3.10, PTPHomeManual $9.45 = $47.55 per inspection minimum), plus EZBook booking fee ($29.75–$33.45/booking), financial reporting ($30/mo), and mandatory brand conference ($900–$1,200/yr). Minimum royalties escalate from $0 to $2,328/month over 5 years regardless of revenue.

6. **Item 19 Highlights:** FPR provided with sales data only (no costs/EBITDA). For the majority Group A (1-inspector units, 206 of 293 units), median sales were $124,002 in 2024. Multi-inspector units perform significantly better: Group C (3-5 inspectors) median $523,732, Group D (6+) median $1,710,728. Systemwide average $475,361 for 2+ inspector units.

7. **Bottom-Quartile Risk:** A single-inspector franchisee earning at the Group A median ($124,002) faces a royalty+brand fee burden of ~$13,640 plus approximately $10,000+ in per-inspection fees (assuming ~200 inspections), $2,800+ in other tech/reporting fees, and minimum royalty obligations. Before personal compensation, operating expenses, vehicle costs, insurance, and marketing, the economics are extremely tight at median levels.

8. **Financial Health (FS Brands):** PwC-audited consolidated financials show $850.4M total revenue, $82.7M operating income, $60.1M net income for 2024. Total assets $591.2M, equity $237.5M. $30M dividend paid in 2024. No going concern. FS Brands guarantees Pillar To Post's franchise agreement obligations.

9. **Territory:** Non-exclusive — other franchisees can and do operate in the same territory. Minimum gross revenue requirements ($100K–$500K escalating over 5 years) with termination risk if not met for 2 consecutive years.

10. **Renewal:** 5-year initial term, up to 5 renewals. Must sign general release. May face materially different terms. Territory boundaries may be modified at renewal.

11. **Disputes:** Arbitration in Florida under Florida law, subject to extensive state overrides in 15 registration states.

12. **Special Risks:** Mandatory minimum royalty payments regardless of sales. Out-of-state dispute resolution in Florida.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
Pillar To Post, Inc. was incorporated in Delaware on July 11, 1994. It began franchising in March 1995, initially offering exclusive territories. Since 2009, only non-exclusive territories are offered to new franchisees (36 legacy exclusive territories remain). The franchisor is part of the FS Brands family, a multi-brand franchise platform owned by publicly traded FirstService Corporation.

There is no predecessor. One affiliate, Tele-Link Services, Inc., provides answering/telemarketing services to the system. The Canadian affiliate operates independently in Canada.

### Fee Stack

**Ongoing percentage fees (calculated monthly on Gross Revenues):**
- Royalty: 7% (with escalating monthly minimums: $0 yr1, $582 yr2, $1,164 yr3, $1,746 yr4, $2,328 yr5)
- Brand Fee: 4%
- **Combined: 11% of Gross Revenues**

**Per-inspection/per-transaction fees:**
- IT Fee: $5.50 per inspection
- Inspection Numbers: $28.50 each
- PTP360: $3.10 per inspection
- PTPHomeManual: $9.45 per inspection
- PTPFloorplan: $21–$350 (by house size, Prestige Package)
- PTPVirtualOpenHouse: $10.00 each
- EZBook Connections: $33.45/booking ($29.75 if 90%+ booked through)

**Fixed recurring fees:**
- Financial Reporting: $30/month
- Brand Conference: $900–$1,200/year (12 monthly installments)
- Business telephone: ~$50/month

**Event-driven fees:**
- Transfer: $4,000 + $15,000 referral fee (if franchisor referred buyer)
- Renewal: $2,500
- Entity transfer: $1,000
- Audit: cost of audit + underpayment + 18% interest (if >2% underreporting)
- Late payment interest: 18% per annum
- Attorney's fees and indemnification: variable

All fees are non-refundable, uniformly imposed, and payable by electronic funds transfer. All per-inspection/per-transaction fees may be adjusted once annually upon 90 days' notice.

### Initial Investment

Total investment: $102,690 to $134,290. The initial franchise fee of $58,500 (45–57% of total) is the largest component. The second-largest component is the automated marketing campaign ($10,000–$15,000), followed by additional working capital ($10,000–$20,000 for 3 months).

This is a home-based, owner-operator, service business model. No real estate build-out is required. The franchisor recommends operating from home. Vehicle required but can be purchased used ($3,000–$6,000). Most suppliers are franchisor-designated with no approved alternatives.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
The franchisor exercises extensive supplier control. Required purchases from the franchisor or designated suppliers represent approximately 64% of startup costs and 50% of ongoing operating costs. In 2024, the franchisor received $5,075,240 in revenue from required supplier purchases, representing 35.9% of its $14.1M total revenue.

Key required suppliers include: BFC Forms (marketing materials), Clarity Voice (telephone), One Contact Canada (answering services), The Branding Company (clothing), IDP Graphics (vehicle wraps), EMSL Analytics (environmental lab), BOSSCAT Home Services (PTPEstimates), Citadel Inspector Pro (insurance), and DocuSketch (PTP360 software). No franchise officer owns an interest in any supplier.

The supplier approval process requires a written request, up to 90 days for evaluation, and franchisee-paid testing costs. Approval may be revoked at franchisor's sole discretion.

### Training Program
Three-phase, approximately 11-week program:
- **Phase 1** (7 weeks): Technical home inspection skills — 104 hours classroom + 146 hours independent learning, including 14 practice inspections. All virtual from home office.
- **Phase 2** (1 week): Business leadership — 22.5 hours classroom + 15 hours independent. Strategic planning, financial planning, growth mindset.
- **Phase 3** (2 weeks): Sales and marketing — 36 hours classroom + 30 hours independent. Realtor relationships, marketing execution, certification.

State licensing requirements may extend the timeline. Continuing education is mandatory. Annual Brand Conference and one regional meeting are required.

### Technology Stack
The OnePoint software platform is the core technology — a proprietary bundle including Salesforce.com, DocuSign, Schedulo, Amazon cloud services, and PTPHomePage. The PTP360 software (from DocuSketch) creates 360-degree inspection photos, floorplans, and virtual open houses. Financial reporting uses Qvinci (QuickBooks integration).

The franchisor owns and has independent access to all data generated by its software, including customer lists. There are no contractual limitations on upgrade frequency or costs. Upon termination, franchisee may request PDF copies of inspection reports within 90 days.

### Marketing Fund Spending
Brand Fund (4% of Gross Revenues) spending breakdown for 2024:
- 36% marketing services (SEO, ORM, social media, PR, creative content)
- 25% administration
- 18% national media placement
- 13% miscellaneous marketing
- 8% real estate industry alliances/tradeshows

The Brand Fund financial statement is reviewed (not audited) and available only to the FAC chair and vice-chair, not to all franchisees. Administration at 25% of fund spending is notably high.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition and Carve-Outs
The territory is **non-exclusive**. It is defined by counties and/or zip codes based on local realtor board boundaries, with no standard size. The territory is "protected" only to the extent that franchisees with territories *outside* yours cannot operate in yours. However, other franchisees assigned to the *same* non-exclusive territory can and do compete directly with you.

Furthermore, existing franchisees who converted from exclusive to non-exclusive territories may have retained the right to exclusively solicit business from specific real estate agents or referral sources within your territory. These protected referral sources will be identified in an addendum to your franchise agreement.

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights
- Franchisee cannot operate outside territory or solicit outside territory (including via internet, catalog, telemarketing, direct mail)
- Franchisor reserves right to sell through alternative channels (internet, etc.) though has not yet done so
- If franchisor engages in e-commerce, orders for your territory will be offered to the closest franchise first
- If no franchise in territory accepts at franchisor's price, the order goes to out-of-territory franchisees or franchisor
- Franchisor may operate company-owned units in the future and solicit within your territory

### Minimum Revenue Requirements
Failure to meet minimum gross revenue requirements ($100K yr1 escalating to $500K yr5) for two consecutive years is a non-curable termination event. These are aggressive targets given that the median single-inspector unit earned $124,002 in 2024 — a new franchisee could be at risk of termination as early as year 3 (when the $300K minimum kicks in) unless they add inspectors and grow rapidly.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
Initial term is 5 years with up to 5 renewal terms of 5 years each (maximum 30 years). Renewal requires: (a) 6 months written notice, (b) full compliance, (c) current on all obligations, (d) $2,500 fee, and (e) execution of general release (Exhibit G-4).

**Materially different terms risk:** The FDD explicitly states that "you may be asked to sign a contract with materially different terms and conditions than your original contract." Fees on renewal are capped at what is imposed on similarly-situated renewing franchisees, and territory boundaries may be modified.

### Transfer Provisions
Transfer requires franchisor approval. Conditions include: transferee must qualify and complete training, $4,000 transfer fee, $15,000 referral fee if franchisor referred the buyer, cure all defaults, transferee signs new agreement (which may have different terms and expires on the original expiration date), general release required, and extended E&O insurance for 2 years post-transfer.

The franchisor has a right of first refusal to match any offer.

### Termination and Death/Disability
Extensive termination triggers including non-curable defaults (insolvency, failure to train, failure to open, abandonment, conviction, unauthorized transfer, failure to meet minimum revenue for 2 consecutive years, 3+ notices in 12 months, failure to implement software updates, failure to attend conferences). Curable defaults have 10 days (monetary) or 30 days (other) to cure.

On death or disability, the estate has 120 days to sell, assign, transfer, or apply to continue operations.

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term:** No competing business
- **Post-term:** 2 years within old non-exclusive territory
- **State overrides:** California (may be void under B&P Code 16600), Minnesota (prohibited from requiring), South Dakota (generally unenforceable), Washington (void unless employee earns $100K+/yr; independent contractor $250K+/yr)

### Dispute Resolution
Arbitration in Florida under Florida law for most disputes. Litigation must be in Florida if not subject to arbitration. Extensive state overrides:
- **California:** Arbitration venue provision may not be enforceable; California law may apply
- **Illinois:** Venue outside Illinois is void; Illinois law governs
- **Indiana:** Forum selection not enforceable; Indiana franchise law applies
- **Maryland:** Choice of forum and choice of law not applicable for MD Franchise Law claims
- **Michigan:** Arbitration/litigation outside Michigan is void
- **Minnesota:** Cannot require litigation outside MN, no jury trial waiver, no liquidated damages
- **New York:** NY General Business Law rights preserved
- **Rhode Island:** Venue outside RI void for claims under RI Franchise Investment Act
- **South Dakota:** Forum selection outside SD void
- **Washington:** Arbitration/mediation must be in WA or mutually agreed location

### Litigation History
One prior arbitration: KJ Loughery, Inc. v. Pillar To Post (2017). Master franchisee in NJ/PA asserted wrongful non-renewal, breach, and NJFPA claims. Settled February 2018 — franchisor purchased the master franchises and waived franchise fee for a unit agreement. No pending actions, no governmental actions.

## J. Unresolveds

1. **(HIGH) System shrinkage unexplained.** The system has lost 73 units in 3 years with zero projected openings. The FDD does not explain whether this reflects market conditions, franchisee dissatisfaction, strategic downsizing, or other factors. Source: Item 20, pp. 60-85.

Pillar To Post's standalone revenue, profitability, and financial position cannot be determined. This is standard for multi-brand parent FDDs but limits due diligence. Source: Item 21, p. 85.

3. **(MEDIUM) No cost data in Item 19.** The FPR provides sales data only — no cost of goods, operating expenses, or profitability metrics. A prospective franchisee cannot assess expected net income from FDD data alone. Source: Item 19, pp. 54-60.

4. **(LOW) Item 20 Table 1 exclusive count formatting issue.** The "2" shown for 2024 end-of-year exclusive outlets appears to be the net change (-2) rather than the ending count (36). Table 3 totals are definitive. Source: Item 20, p. 72.
