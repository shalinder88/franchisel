# 02 Reader Report — Ivybrook Academy 2025 (Shadow Fresh)

## A. Executive snapshot

Ivybrook Academy is a half-day preschool franchise offered by Ivybrook Franchising, LLC, a North Carolina LLC formed in March 2015 and headquartered in Weddington, NC. The disclosure document has an issuance date of March 28, 2025, as amended September 15, 2025. The system is small-to-mid but growing steadily: 40 franchised outlets plus one affiliate-owned school at year-end 2024, up from 17 franchised outlets at the start of 2022. There are 42 franchise agreements signed but not yet open and 16 projected new franchised openings in the next fiscal year, which is a large unopened backlog relative to current operating count and is itself called out as a Special Risk on the cover. A second Special Risk is the franchisor's financial condition, which the document says "calls into question" its ability to provide services and support; consistent with that risk, the Illinois addendum imposes a fee-deferral condition and the Virginia addendum discloses that the estimated initial investment ($540,700–$869,860) exceeds the franchisor's stockholder's equity of $345,862 as of December 31, 2024. A material late-cycle corporate event is disclosed in Item 1: on August 29, 2025 the franchisor and its affiliates McWilliams Education Services and Catapult Industries were acquired by Ivybrook OpCo (a subsidiary of Ivybrook HoldCo, in turn a subsidiary of Crux I Ivybrook (Aggregator), LP, a Delaware limited partnership with a Dallas principal address), reflecting a private-equity acquisition structure. Item 19 presents three substantive EBITDA tables (affiliate school 2A, 2-year+ franchisees 2B, 3-year+ franchisees 2C) plus revenue-stream ranges and a classrooms-to-revenue breakdown, so financial performance is unusually well disclosed for a system of this size. Items 3 and 4 are clean (no litigation, no bankruptcy). Exhibit J (audited financial statements) is present in the PDF but the text layer is image-only in this extraction path, so the detailed balance sheet, income statement, and auditor identity are not directly surfaced through text extraction and must be treated as not directly surfaced until a page-render / OCR retry is performed.

## B. Pass 1 — front matter, formats, fee stack, initial investment

The cover page identifies one format: a brick-and-mortar "School" operated in leased space, typically on or near main thoroughfares, offering a half-day preschool and optional private kindergarten. The single-unit total initial investment is $540,700 to $869,860, of which $82,500 is paid to the franchisor or affiliate. If a prospect signs a Multi-Unit Development Agreement (MUDA) for two or three schools, the first-school total initial investment rises to $575,700 to $929,860 because $35,000 of the second-school franchise fee is paid at MUDA signing; the third-school franchise fee ($25,000) is also paid at MUDA signing if three units are agreed. The initial franchise fee is $50,000 for the first unit, $35,000 for the second unit, and $25,000 for the third unit; none of these fees is refundable. A $10,000 Real Estate and Facility Coordination Fee and a $15,000 Training & Opening Support Fee are charged at or shortly after franchise agreement signing, and a $7,500 Décor Package is charged roughly 90 days before opening. Item 7 additionally itemizes construction and leasehold improvements of $277,300 to $518,860 (notes assume a $40/sf tenant improvement allowance on 6,000 sf), FF&E and décor $75,000–$105,000, outdoor playground $10,000–$25,000, signage $8,000–$14,000, computer hardware/software $1,000–$2,500, rent deposits $15,000–$25,000, insurance deposits $3,000–$5,000, professional fees $25,000–$35,000, grand opening advertising $20,000–$25,000 (paid entirely to an approved marketing supplier), and three months of additional funds $30,000–$35,000. If an existing School is being purchased from a current franchisee the initial franchise fee is waived and replaced with a $10,000 transfer training fee.

## C. Pass 2 — supplier control, operations, training, reporting, technology

Supplier control is substantial. Item 8 expressly discloses that the cost of items the franchisee must buy from the franchisor, its affiliates, or designated suppliers is between 70% and 90% of total purchases both in establishing and in operating the School; "Supplier Control" is one of the four Special Risks highlighted on the cover. The franchisor reserves the right to become the exclusive supplier of any product or service and currently designates itself for the Décor Package and Catapult (an affiliate) as the exclusive supplier of The Student Hub school-management and payment-processing software. Franchisees must use designated suppliers for classroom furniture, playground equipment, signage, marketing services, branded supplies, and must use the franchisor's approved architect and construction management company. Approval of alternate suppliers costs $500 per request, with criteria "not available to you", and the franchisor has 30 days to respond. The franchisor receives approximately $33,821 in supplier credits in 2024 (less than 2% of its $2,171,363 total revenue) from classroom-furniture and branded-supplies suppliers, which it does not share with franchisees; those credits are used for the annual franchisee conference. Catapult received approximately $36,000 from franchisees' Student Hub purchases in 2024. Drew McWilliams (co-founder, CEO) is a licensed real-estate agent and may receive referral fees or commissions from real estate brokers that assist franchisees, which is a disclosed conflict. Operations rules require at least one owner with 10% or more ownership interest to serve as Chief Executive Officer and to personally participate at least 20 hours per week during the first year; a School Administrator must provide on-premises supervision at least 40 hours per week; the CEO and Administrator may be the same person. Reporting burden includes monthly Gross Revenue reports, a $250-per-month technology fee, a 1.5%-per-month late interest charge, and audit rights that trigger full audit-cost reimbursement plus reimbursement if understatement exceeds 3%. Training is 80 hours pre-opening (12 hours business start-up/real estate, 24 hours marketing/enrollment/operations, 44 hours Ivybrook University/school setup) delivered through an online portal and at the affiliate's Charlotte, NC school, with at least one owner plus the School Administrator required to attend; post-opening "substitute or new" School Administrator training is charged at $500 per week. Technology obligations are broad: the franchisor can independently access all data on the franchisee's Technology at any time without notice, can require proprietary Technology subject to a separate license with the franchisor or affiliate, and disclaims all responsibility for malfunctions. Required tools currently include office PCs, iPads per classroom, QuickBooks, and The Student Hub.

## D. Pass 3 — territory, channels, encroachment

The Territory is "the lesser of a population of 125,000 people or a three-mile radius" around the accepted location, and is described as exclusive in the sense that the franchisor will not establish or license another Ivybrook Academy school within it; it is not exclusive against solicitation, and there are no other restrictions on the franchisor's ability to solicit customers inside the territory. The franchisor also reserves unusually broad carveouts: it retains the right to (a) operate Ivybrook Academy schools outside the Territory, (b) sell goods or services using the Marks through channels other than a brick-and-mortar school anywhere in the world including inside the Territory, with the caveat that it will not sell Ivybrook courses or curriculum through the Internet, (c) operate any other business of any kind under any other mark anywhere in the world including inside the Territory, and (d) direct advertising into the Territory. The franchisee is expressly prohibited from mail-order, internet, mobile, telemarketing, or catalog channels, and from marketing outside the Territory without prior written consent; the franchisor "retain[s] the right to control all online sales." A Designated Area for site search does not grant territorial exclusivity. Territory does not renew automatically on the same boundary — the franchisor may modify the Territory upon renewal. Relocation requires franchisor approval. Minimum sales targets are not applicable for a single unit, but under the MUDA the developer must comply with a mutually agreed development schedule and sign each subsequent franchise agreement within twelve months of opening the prior school.

## E. Pass 4 — contract burden and legal mechanics

Length of term is 15 years with two additional 10-year renewals subject to a sign-successor-agreement provision, payment of a renewal fee equal to the greater of $10,000 or 10% of the then-current initial franchise fee, signing of a general release, a current-requirements capital-expenditure condition, and compliance with all material provisions. The franchise agreement cannot be terminated by the franchisor without cause. Curable defaults include failure to maintain insurance, missed payments, or any failure to comply with a mandatory specification or standard in the Brand Standards Manual. Non-curable defaults include failure to select an accepted site, failure to complete training, material misrepresentation, felony conviction, unauthorized use of confidential information, abandonment for three or more consecutive days, unauthorized transfer, two-or-more reports understating amounts due by more than 1%, insolvency, two-or-more twelve-month compliance or reporting failures, and several additional specific grounds including two or more health-and-safety law violations. Liquidated damages on franchisor-for-cause termination equal the 12-month average monthly royalty multiplied by the number of months remaining in the term; an additional $25,000 transfer damages fee applies if a transfer is attempted without complying with agreement procedures. Post-termination non-competes run two years within 10 miles of the former franchise or within the Territory (greater of), or within 10 miles of any other Ivybrook Academy school, subject to state law. The franchisor has a right of first refusal on transfers, a 60-day right to purchase assets at fair market value after termination, and imposes an unlimited assignment right on its own side. Transfers also require a $10,000 transfer fee plus a $10,000 transfer training fee. Dispute resolution for most claims is mandatory mediation then arbitration in Charlotte, NC; North Carolina law is choice-of-law (subject to state law overrides); the cover flags "Out-of-State Dispute Resolution" as a special risk. Indemnification runs broadly from the franchisee, its owners, and guarantors to the franchisor and related parties for third-party claims and for claims arising out of School operation.
