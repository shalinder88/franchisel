# Reader Report — Ivybrook Academy FDD (637976-2025)

## A. Executive Snapshot

Ivybrook Franchising, LLC is a North Carolina LLC formed March 26, 2015, headquartered in Weddington, NC. The franchise offers a comprehensive half-day preschool concept catering to families seeking learning, enrichment, and socialization without full-day childcare. Franchisees may also offer private kindergarten. On August 29, 2025, Ivybrook OpCo LLC (a subsidiary of Crux I Ivybrook (Aggregator), LP through HoldCo) acquired all membership interests in Ivybrook Franchising, LLC and affiliates, indicating a private equity acquisition. The franchisor's affiliate Catapult develops and supplies the required Student Hub school management software. As of December 31, 2024, the system had 40 franchised outlets and 1 company-owned outlet (affiliate-operated), with 42 signed-but-not-opened agreements and 16 projected new franchised outlets. The total initial investment ranges from $540,700 to $869,860 for a single unit, or $575,700 to $929,860 under a MUDA. The FDD's Special Risks page flags financial condition concerns, supplier control, out-of-state dispute resolution, and a significant number of unopened franchises. Item 19 provides detailed EBITDA tables.

## B. Pass 1 — Front Matter, Formats, Fee Stack, Initial Investment

**Front matter:** The cover page identifies the franchisor and principal address. Pages 2–3 contain the standard "How to Use" guidance. Page 4 discloses four special risks: (1) out-of-state dispute resolution in NC, (2) franchisor financial condition concerns per Item 21, (3) supplier control with prices potentially higher than market, (4) significant number of signed-but-unopened franchises. Pages 5–6 contain Michigan-specific franchise investment law provisions.

**Formats:** The franchise is offered as a single-unit franchise agreement or through a Multi-Unit Development Agreement (MUDA) for 2–3 units. The MUDA is in Exhibit C. Under the MUDA, the franchise fee is $50,000 for unit 1, $35,000 for unit 2, and $25,000 for unit 3, all due at signing.

**Fee stack (Item 5):** Initial franchise fee is $50,000 (lump sum, non-refundable). Real estate and facility coordination fee is $10,000. Training and opening support fee is $15,000. Transfer training fee is $10,000 (in lieu of franchise fee for resale purchases). All Item 5 fees were uniformly applied in the last fiscal year.

**Item 6 — Other Fees:** The Item 6 table spans pages 12–16 with approximately 25 fee categories. Key recurring fees: royalty 7% of Gross Revenue (monthly); Brand Development Fund 1% of Gross Revenue (expected to rise to 2% in 2026); local marketing expenditure minimum 1% of Gross Revenue (yearly). Technology fee is $250/month. Marketing cooperative up to 2% (currently none). Transfer fee $10,000. Renewal fee is the greater of $10,000 or 10% of then-current initial franchise fee. Liquidated damages on termination for cause are calculated as average monthly royalty times remaining months. Late fees 1.5%/month. Non-compliance fee up to $1,000/week. Late reporting fine up to $1,000 per incident. All fees are payable to and collected by franchisor, non-refundable, and uniform (with reservation to change or waive). Notes define Gross Revenue comprehensively.

**Item 7 — Estimated Initial Investment:** The table includes 13 line items from franchise fee ($50,000) to additional funds for 3 months ($30,000–$35,000). Total: $540,700–$869,860. The largest variable is construction/leasehold improvements ($277,300–$518,860), which assumes a $40/sq ft tenant improvement allowance on a 6,000 sq ft space. Grand opening advertising is $20,000–$25,000. Professional fees (architect, construction manager) $25,000–$35,000. A separate MUDA table adds $35,000–$60,000 for additional unit franchise fees.

## C. Pass 2 — Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Reporting, Technology

**Item 8 — Supplier control:** Franchisor may require all goods/services from designated suppliers. Currently mandated: (1) classroom furniture from designated supplier, (2) branded supplies from designated supplier, (3) décor package from franchisor, (4) The Student Hub software from affiliate Catapult, (5) approved architect and construction management company, (6) designated marketing supplier. Franchisees must pay $500 to request evaluation of alternative suppliers. The CEO Drew McWilliams may receive real estate referral fees. In 2024 franchisor received approximately $33,821 in credits from designated furniture and supply suppliers. Franchisor and affiliates reserve the right to become approved or exclusive supplier of any product or service.

**Item 11 — Training:** Initial training program for at least one owner and School Administrator — two weeks in-person at an approved training facility, plus online/curriculum training approximately 2 months before opening. Training led by Daniela McElhaney and Katherine Bell (each 15+ years with brand, 20+ years in child education). Training fee is included in the $15,000 Training & Opening Support Fee. Periodic refresher training may be required at franchisee expense.

**Technology:** Required: office computers, iPads per classroom, QuickBooks, The Student Hub. Student Hub is supplied exclusively by affiliate Catapult. Approximate initial hardware cost $1,000–$2,500. Technology fee $250/month covers intranet, website, and other tech. Franchisor has unlimited rights to modify tech requirements and may charge sublicense fees. Franchisee bears all responsibility for tech operation and maintenance.

**Reporting:** Monthly gross revenue statement by 5th of month. Monthly P&L by 5th of month. Annual P&L and balance sheet within 30 days of year-end (cash basis; may require CPA review/audit). Initial investment report within 120 days of opening. Late reporting fine up to $1,000/incident.

**Brand Fund:** Started July 2018. 1% of Gross Revenue currently; expected to increase to 2% in 2026. Expenditure breakdown: social media advertising 55%, website expenses 30%, software solutions 10%, graphic design 3%, administrative 2%. No audited financial statement for Brand Fund was mentioned, but cooperatives must prepare annual financial statements. Franchisor has sole discretion over Brand Fund spending.

**Industry memberships:** NAEYC membership and accreditation required (after 2 years). NAREA membership required. Better Business Bureau accreditation required.

## D. Pass 3 — Territory, Channels, Encroachment

**Item 12 — Territory:** Territory defined as the lesser of 125,000 population or 3-mile radius around location. Franchisor reserves right to grant smaller or larger territory by mutual agreement. Territory may be designated by radius, political boundaries, streets, or geographic features. No right to additional outlets without MUDA.

**Channel restrictions:** Franchisee may only sell from brick-and-mortar location. No internet, mail order, telemarketing, mobile app sales. May not solicit or advertise outside territory without written permission. Franchisor retains right to control all online sales.

**Encroachment risk:** Franchisor retains rights to: (a) operate Ivybrook businesses outside territory, (b) sell branded merchandise/products anywhere including within territory (excluding courses/curriculum via internet), (c) acquire/operate businesses without using Marks anywhere, (d) advertise anywhere. No compensation for exercise of reserved rights. Franchisor has right but no current plan to operate under a different trademark with similar services. If franchisee is in breach, franchisor may modify territory protections.

## E. Pass 4 — Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

**Term/renewal (Item 17):** Initial term is 10 years. One 10-year renewal option. Renewal conditions: timely notice, current compliance, satisfaction of monetary obligations, sign current form agreement (may differ from original), make capital expenditures for system uniformity, sign general release, comply with current training. Renewal fee is the greater of $10,000 or 10% of then-current initial franchise fee.

**Termination:** Franchisee may terminate on 30 days' notice if franchisor materially breaches and fails to cure within 90 days. Franchisor cannot terminate without cause. Extensive list of noncurable defaults includes: failure to open/train, misrepresentation, felony conviction, unauthorized transfer, abandonment (3+ days), repeated understatement of fees, insolvency, government license loss, etc. Curable defaults include: failure to maintain insurance (10 days), failure to make payments (10 days), other defaults (15 days).

**Transfer (Item 17):** Transfer broadly defined. Requires prior written consent. Conditions include: all obligations current, general release, transferee meets standards, transferee signs current agreement, transfer fee ($10,000), transfer training fee ($10,000), restrictive covenant compliance. Franchisor has right of first refusal on proposed sales.

**Post-termination obligations:** Cease operations immediately. De-identify location within 10 days if franchisor doesn't exercise purchase/lease rights. Assign lease to franchisor on demand. Return all materials, customer data, telephone listings. Comply with noncompete. Pay liquidated damages if terminated for cause.

**Noncompete:** During term: no competitive business (childcare, daycare, preschool) by owners or spouses. Post-term: 2 years within 10-mile radius of location and territory (whichever is greater), and within 10-mile radius of any other Ivybrook location. Also 2 years within 10 miles of any Ivybrook location from which franchisee derived benefits. No solicitation of Ivybrook customers, employees, or suppliers for 2 years.

**Dispute resolution:** Mediation first, then binding arbitration in Charlotte, NC. Choice of law: North Carolina. Jury trial waiver. Prevailing party entitled to attorneys' fees. Subject to state law modifications per Exhibit B addenda.

**Guaranty (Exhibit F):** Unlimited personal guaranty required. Joint and several liability. Guarantor personally bound by all terms of franchise agreement including noncompete, non-solicitation, dispute resolution, and indemnification. Irrevocable.
