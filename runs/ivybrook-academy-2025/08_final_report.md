## 2. Format and development
- One format: brick-and-mortar half-day preschool in leased space (typically 6,000 sf), on or near main thoroughfares.
- Single-unit or 2-/3-unit Multi-Unit Development Agreement (Exhibit C). Subsequent franchise agreements must be signed within 12 months of first opening and within 24 months of second opening.

## 3. Fee stack
- Initial franchise fee: $50,000 (first), $35,000 (second, paid at MUDA signing), $25,000 (third, paid at MUDA signing). Transfer of existing School replaces the initial fee with a $10,000 transfer training fee.
- Other initial payments to franchisor or affiliate: $10,000 real estate and facility coordination fee + $15,000 training & opening support fee + $7,500 décor package = **$82,500 payable to franchisor/affiliate for single unit**.
- Royalty: **7% of Gross Revenue**, monthly by the 6th.
- Brand Fund: **1% of Gross Revenue**, monthly; **scheduled to rise to 2% in 2026**.
- Local marketing: 1% of Gross Revenue minimum, measured yearly.
- Marketing cooperative: currently none; maximum 2% of Gross Revenue.
- Technology fee: **$250/month**, franchisor may raise without cap.
- Grand opening marketing: minimum **$20,000 spend pre-opening, paid to franchisor's approved supplier**.
- Audit recovery: $1,500–$5,000 if understatement >3% or report failure.
- Late interest: 1.5%/month or highest state rate.
- Noncompliance: up to $1,000/week plus cost markup of 10% on cure costs.
- Renewal fee: greater of $10,000 or 10% of then-current initial franchise fee.
- Transfer fee $10,000 + transfer training $10,000; transfer damages $25,000 for unauthorized transfer.
- Liquidated damages on for-cause termination: 12-month avg monthly royalty × months remaining in term.
- Continuing-operation fee: greater of $1,000 or 150% of weekly royalty per week of month-to-month operation after expiration.

## 5. Supplier control

- Designated categories: classroom furniture, playground equipment, signage, marketing services, branded supplies, architect and construction-management firm, professional services.
- Alternate supplier approval: $500 fee; criteria not disclosed; 30-day review.
- 2024 supplier credits to franchisor: ~$33,821 (used for franchisee conference; not shared). Catapult 2024 revenue from franchisees: ~$36,000.
- Conflict: CEO Drew McWilliams is a licensed real estate agent and may receive referral fees from the broker assisting franchisees.

## 6. Training and operations burden
- 80 hours pre-opening classroom training across three phases (12/24/44), delivered online and at the affiliate's Charlotte, NC school, plus two weeks of in-person training at a training-approved School. Led by Daniela McElhaney and Katherine Bell.
- At least one owner (≥10% equity) must serve as CEO, in-premises at least 20 hrs/week in year 1; School Administrator must be on-premises 40 hrs/week. May be the same person.
- Ongoing refresher training required; substitute School Administrator training $500/week; additional assistance $500/day plus expenses.
- Operations manual (Brand Standards Manual): 242 pages across six sections — Preface & Introduction (32), Establishing My Franchise Business (76), Personnel (77), Administrative Procedures (13), Daily Procedures (17), Selling & Marketing (27).

## 7. Technology
- Required: office PCs, iPads per classroom, QuickBooks, The Student Hub (Catapult).
- $250/month technology fee; estimated $2,000–$3,000/year maintenance.
- Franchisor reserves the **right to independently access all franchisee data at any time without notice**; no contractual limit on that right.
- Franchisor controls all Online Presence (website, social, app, e-commerce, VR); franchisee cannot establish own online presence concerning the brand without written consent.

## 8. Territory
- Territory = lesser of 125,000 population or 3-mile radius. Exclusive only against other Ivybrook Academy schools. No protection against franchisor solicitation inside the Territory. Modifiable at renewal.
- Franchisee restricted to brick-and-mortar + approved off-site events; no internet, mail, mobile, or telemarketing channels. Franchisor retains "all online sales" control.
- Broad franchisor reserved rights including operating under different trademarks inside the Territory.

## 9. Term and legal mechanics
- Term: **15 years**; renewal: **two 10-year renewals** subject to successor agreement, capital-expenditure updates, general release, renewal fee (greater of $10,000 or 10% of then-current franchise fee).
- Franchisor cannot terminate without cause. Curable defaults include insurance, payments, specification compliance. Non-curable defaults include failure to complete site/training, misrepresentation, felony conviction, 2+ reports understating by >1%, insolvency, 2+ health/safety violations, unauthorized Marks use, 2+ 12-month compliance or reporting failures, minimum score failures, license loss, and a dozen other specific grounds.
- Post-term noncompete: **2 years** within 10 miles of the former location or within the Territory (greater of), or within 10 miles of any other Ivybrook Academy school.
- Dispute resolution: mediation then arbitration in Charlotte, NC. NC choice of law. Subject to state law overrides.
- Unlimited franchisor assignment. $25,000 transfer damages for unauthorized transfer.
- Exhibit F: unlimited personal guaranty from franchisee owners. Exhibit H: two separate restrictive-covenant forms (NDA/non-solicit and NDA/non-compete).

## 18. Depth-pass contract-burden additions (see `RT_depth_contract_burdens.json`)
- **Pre-authorized bank draft mandatory** (FA 3.12): franchisor may change payment method and adjust due dates unilaterally.
- **Payment-application override** (FA 3.11): franchisor may apply any franchisee payment to any past-due indebtedness in any priority, overriding franchisee designations.
- **Set-off right**: franchisor and affiliates may set off sums owed to franchisee against past-due indebtedness.
- **No good-faith covenant** (FA 21.2): explicit disclaimer of fiduciary relationship and any implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing, except as required by statute.
- **Franchisor owns all Franchised Business Data** (FA 13.17): franchisee has only a limited license to use its own operating data while the Agreement is in effect; data must be created and stored on franchisor-designated Technology; Customer List is franchisor property.
- **Unlimited system-change right** (FA 10.2): franchisor acknowledges required changes may require significant capital expenditures "unknown on the Effective Date and that cannot be fully amortized over the period of time then remaining in the Initial Term."
- **Open-ended refurbishment right** (FA 13.2/13.3): franchisor may require structural changes, remodeling, redecoration, and equipment replacement at franchisee expense to conform to then-current image.
- **Renewal conditioned on capital-expenditure uniformity** (FA 4.2.1.4).
- **FMV baseball arbitration of rent on franchisor lease takeover** (Lease Rider): independent appraiser selects most commercially reasonable rent from two proposals; 45-day fallback dual-appraiser mechanism.
- **Single-purpose entity requirement**: franchisee entity organizational documents must limit activities exclusively to development and operation of IVYBROOK ACADEMY; stop-transfer instructions required for corporate franchisees; all ownership changes must be reported.
- **Related-party joinder right**: franchisor may require owners/members/partners/directors/officers/principals to sign the Agreement or separate agreements.
- **Insurance minimums**: $2,000,000 per occurrence general liability; $100,000 employer liability; all-risk property insurance; workers comp per state law; procured 60 days before opening; franchisor named as additional insured, primary and non-contributory, with subrogation waiver.
- **Dispute-resolution injunctive carveout** (FA 23.7): franchisor may immediately seek preliminary injunctive relief without first mediating or arbitrating.
- **Jury-trial waiver** (FA 23.x): parties irrevocably waive jury trial.
- **Site acceptance deadline**: franchisee must obtain franchisor acceptance of a site within 6 months of franchise agreement; franchisor may terminate if not.
- **Two-week in-person training at training-approved School** (FA 8.1): beyond the 80-hour Ivybrook University program; one owner plus one other person required.
- **No explicit security interest** in franchisee assets surfaced in Exhibit A body; Exhibit F is a personal guaranty, not a collateral pledge. No estoppel certificate clause surfaced.

## 20. Diligence headline takeaways
- **Unit economics are directly disclosed** with three cohorts, revenue segmentation by classroom count, and benchmarking against the affiliate — rare for a 40-unit system. Median 3Y+ franchisee generated ~$886K Gross Revenue and $181K "EBITDA" in 2024, but owner comp is excluded from labor expense so this is pre-owner-comp operating profit, not take-home.
- **Financial condition of the franchisor is a meaningful risk** — three converging signals (cover special risk, IL deferral, VA equity disclosure) point to balance-sheet stress pre-acquisition. Aug 2025 PE acquisition may have recapitalized the franchisor but is not reflected in Exhibit J.
- **Growth is steady but unopened backlog is the largest signal**: 42 signed agreements unopened vs 40 operating. If pipeline conversion stalls, topline growth and royalty base will stall too. "Unopened Franchises" is explicitly one of the four cover Special Risks.
- **System is clean on attrition**: 0 terminations, 0 non-renewals, 0 transfers over 3 years. Only 2 "other ceased" events, both in Texas.
- **Supplier control is unusually intense** (70–90%) — one of the highest-end ranges among preschool franchises.
- **Territory is small** (3-mile radius / 125K population) and exclusivity is thin (no customer-solicitation protection).
- **Contract burden is high** on termination (liquidated damages × months remaining), technology (unrestricted franchisor data access), and online presence (franchisor-controlled) but moderate on initial training (80 hours) and noncompete (2 years / 10 miles).
