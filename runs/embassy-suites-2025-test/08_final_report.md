# Final Report — Embassy Suites by Hilton (2025 US FDD)

## A. Executive snapshot

1. **System**: 258 US Embassy Suites hotels as of 2024-12-31 under franchisor **Hilton Franchise Holding LLC** (Delaware LLC); parent Hilton Domestic Operating Company → **Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc.** (NYSE: HLT). 237 are franchised; 0 company-owned.
2. **Ownership history**: Franchisor took over US Embassy Suites franchising from its own subsidiary Embassy Suites Franchise LLC on 2015-03-30. Deeper lineage runs back to Promus Hotels (1995–2007) and Hilton Hotels Corporation predecessors (1984–1995). Blackstone-associated board chairman (Jonathan Gray).
3. **Investment**: $50,082,670 – $82,170,820 for a 176-suite new-build (excludes real estate, market study, insurance, interest). Construction & leasehold: $36M–$55M; FF&E: $6M–$9M; contingencies $3.6M–$5.5M. Up to $326,179 payable to Hilton or affiliates.
4. **Ongoing take**: Royalty 3.5% → 4.5% → 5.5% GRR over first 3 years (5.5% flat for COO/relicensing) + 4% Program Fee + 4.3% Hilton Honors. **Mature all-in % burden ≥ 13.8% of Gross Rooms Revenue**, excluding training, brand-conference, non-compliance, guest-assistance and many add-on fees.
5. **Item 19**: 220 Comparable Hotels. 2024 average room rate $184.12, avg occupancy 74.4%, RevPAR Index 121.7 — brand outperforms its STR comp sets on average. However **franchisee-managed hotels materially lag** company-managed: only 30.9% of FM hotels met the 2024 average room rate, vs 53.1% of CM hotels. **No cost, margin, or profit disclosure.**
6. **Bottom quartile**: 2024 room rate range $109.57–$375.87 and occupancy range 47.9%–95.6% indicate very wide performance dispersion.
7. **Financial health**: Franchisor HFH LLC is a royalty cash machine — 2024 revenue $1.50B, net income $1.49B, 99% net margin. Clean unqualified audit opinion by **Cherry Bekaert** dated 2025-03-18, no going concern. $1.5B distributed to Hilton affiliates in 2024 ($4.45B over 3 years).
8. **Growth trajectory**: Essentially flat — +1 net hotel per year for 3 consecutive years (234→235→236→237). 2024 saw first termination activity in the window (3 hotels: CO, MI, TX). 29 signed-unopened FAs; only 3 projected to open in 2025.
9. **Territory**: **No exclusive territory** under standard FA; optional Restricted Area Provision may be negotiated for New Development / Conversion. Critical forward-looking signal: "*Historically, we have extended the Restrictive Period for the full term of the Franchise Agreement; however, we do not intend to do so in the future*."
10. **Renewal**: **No renewal right.** Re-licensing at Hilton's discretion, potentially on materially different terms; current franchisee has no legal claim to re-franchise.
11. **Disputes**: Governing law New York; venue EDVA / Fairfax VA / NY NY; **no arbitration** except Puerto Rico. Franchisor may sue at hotel site.
12. **Litigation**: 5 pending actions, 4 of which are **active Sherman §1 antitrust class actions** (extended stay hotels via IDeaS; luxury hotels via IDeaS/Amadeus/STR). Unquantified exposure.
13. **Special risks**: Non-exclusive license + no cross-brand protection (other Hilton brands like Hilton Garden Inn / DoubleTree / Homewood can encroach); mandatory OnQ technology stack + mandatory annual Brand Conference + mandatory Hilton Honors participation + large pool of handling fees and non-compliance penalties.
14. **Franchisee confidentiality clause flag**: Some current/former franchisees have signed provisions restricting their ability to speak publicly about their experience.
15. **No independent franchisee association** registered.

## B. Fee stack, entry structure, initial investment

### B.1 Front matter / entity structure
Hilton Franchise Holding LLC is the franchisor, formed in Delaware in September 2007. Parent Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. (Delaware, 2016); ultimate parent Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). Four operating affiliates support franchisees: Hilton Reservations Worldwide (reservations), Hilton Supply Management (procurement, negotiated FF&E pricing with 4%–10% procurement fee), Hilton Honors Worldwide (loyalty), Hilton Systems Solutions (technology, OnQ). Immediate predecessor was Embassy Suites Franchise LLC (2007–2015), further predecessors run back to Promus Hotels (1995–2007) and HHC (1984–1995).

### B.2 Fee stack
**Initial (one-time)**:
- Franchise Application Fee: $100,000 New Dev/Conversion (+$400/room over 250), $200,000 Change of Ownership, $100,000 Re-licensing. Refundable less $7,500 processing fee pre-approval. In 2024, franchisees paid $7,500–$200,000 for COO.
- PIP fee $10,000 (Conversion/COO/Re-lic).
- OnQ computer system $70,576–$151,184 (hardware + software + install, from HSS).
- Training program $5,000–$17,500 (plus travel/lodging/wages for attendees).
- Opening Process Services $6,000.
- Other startup (GRO/Digital Floor Plan/Hilton Opening Transition Tool) $6,500.
- Eforea Spa initial $25,000 (only if spa installed).
- HSM procurement fees 4%–10% of project cost if HSM supplies FF&E.

**Ongoing**:
- **Monthly Royalty**: 3.5% → 4.5% → 5.5% GRR over first 3 years of NC/Conversion; 5.5% flat for COO/Re-licensing. Modified in 6 instances during 2024.
- **Monthly Program Fee**: 4% GRR. Can rise up to +1% over term.
- **Hilton Honors**: 4.3% of eligible guest folio.
- **Monthly Spa Royalty**: 2% Gross Spa Revenue (if Eforea).
- OnQ Connectivity $400–$600/mo; OnQ H/W & S/W Maintenance $1,735–$4,128/mo; OnQ Email $7.92/user/mo; Connected Room Maint $721–$1,062/mo; Delphi $858/user/yr.
- Brand Conference $2,500/attendee/yr.

**Event-driven / punitive**:
- Liquidated Damages on early termination = greater of Hotel Avg Monthly Royalty or System Avg Monthly Royalty × remaining months.
- Brand Non-Compliance Fee $65–$145/room/audit (cap $50,000 / 6 months).
- Stay Score Failure Fee up to $300/room OR 0.5% GRR annually.
- Service Quality Improvement Program $20,000–$50,000 per 6-mo period.
- Plans & Designs Late Fee $5,000/90 days.
- PIP/Renovation Failure Fee $10,000 every 6 months.
- Brand Non-Compliance Re-Eval up to $4,500; Special Audit $5,500.
- Guest Assistance Program $200–$300 per handled transaction + rebate up to full stay cost.

**Centralized payment and distribution fees**: Third-Party Reservation Charges up to $5.93/stay; FastPay up to $1.40/txn + up to 7% commission; Travel Planner Centralized Payment $0.18/txn + 10% commission; Unlimited Rewards $0.71–$3.84/stay (each may rise $0.21/yr).

**Commercial programs (auto-enrolled, opt-out)**:
- Hilton Advance Program 1.35% Digital Direct Revenue (cap $30/stay).
- Group Preferred Partnership Program up to $1.80/consumed room night (cap rising to $2.50) + commission.
- Hilton For Business Program up to 3% GRR per eligible consumed stay.
- Event Planner Bonus $0.0025–$0.0050/point.

## C. Supplier control, operations, technology, reporting

### C.1 Supplier restrictions
Mandatory Standards compliance across construction, FF&E, food & beverage, signage, logo items, OnQ, Guest Internet (StayConnected), GRO, Delphi, Connected Room, Digital Floor Plan, Digital Key, Hilton Opening Transition Tool. OnQ is proprietary and must be purchased from HSS. Logo items must come from approved suppliers (Hilton may derive profit). HSM is NOT mandatory except for Eforea spas (spa items must be from HSM). Hilton affiliates reported $1,101,161,898 in 2024 revenue from sales of goods/services/systems/supplies to franchisees of Hilton's subsidiaries (excluding royalty, program fees, pass-through commissions, HSM amounts) — providing visibility on scale of non-royalty franchisor extraction.

### C.2 Training
Mandatory training for GM and Commercial/Sales leaders must be completed within **90 days** of start date. Brand Conference mandatory annually ($2,500/attendee). Pre-opening training $5,000–$17,500 plus all attendee expenses. Hilton Core Sales Skills Training ($600/attendee). Other training programs up to $5,000/program/attendee.

### C.3 Technology stack (all mandated)
OnQ (reservations, property mgmt, revenue mgmt, inventory, forecast, learning); HPMS/PEP (property mgmt system, developed with HotelKey); Guest Internet (StayConnected); GRO (revenue optimization); Delphi (Amadeus sales/events); Connected Room; Digital Floor Plan; Digital Key; Hilton Opening Transition Tool. Must refresh OnQ at least every 3 years (or as Hilton specifies); Hilton may require refresh on COO/Relicensing.

### C.4 Marketing fund
Program Fee 4% GRR, monthly. Can rise up to +1% over term. Program Fee does not cover franchisee local marketing. Audit status: not explicitly confirmed in extraction (Program Fee account details not surfaced).

## D. Territory, channels, encroachment

**Non-exclusive license** to operate at a specified site. Standard FA grants no protected territory. Optional **Restricted Area Provision** may be negotiated for New Development and Conversion (not typically for COO/Re-licensing). Boundaries are case-by-case (cities, MSAs, counties, street/highway, radius). Restrictive Period is typically shorter than FA term.

**Carve-outs** from any Restricted Area: existing Brand hotels and approved sites; other Hilton brands (HGI, DT, Homewood, Hampton, Curio, Canopy, etc.) — explicitly NOT protected against; gaming hotels; vacation ownership/timeshare; chain acquisitions of 4+ hotels; replacement hotels.

**Material forward-looking signal**: *"Historically, we have extended the Restrictive Period for the full term of the Franchise Agreement; however, we do not intend to do so in the future."* This means new-cohort franchisees should expect **shorter** territorial protection than legacy franchisees.

Franchisees may compete with any Network Hotel (Hilton family). No conflict-resolution mechanism exists for territorial disputes. Strategic Partnerships and Other Businesses with third parties are broadly permitted; Strategic Partner competition may exist in the Restricted Area.

## E. Contract burden and legal mechanics

- **Term**: 23 years new construction; 10–20 years conversion; remaining term or negotiated for COO. Eforea Spa Amendment terminates on earlier of FA expiration or Spa Amendment termination.
- **Renewal**: None. Re-licensing at Hilton's sole discretion with potentially **"materially different terms and conditions"** — effectively no continuity right.
- **Transfer**: Permitted Transfers (no consent): public equity, <50% private holding without control change, intra-institutional fund; others (affiliates, family/trust, death, >50% private) require 60-day notice, consent, documents and processing fee. Change of Ownership Transfer requires 60-day notice, transferee meeting current standards, App Fee, new form FA, possible PIP fee. Transferee may not be Sanctioned Person or Competitor.
- **Death/disability**: Transfer to immediate family or family-formed entity without consent IF family meets standards within 1 year.
- **Termination by Franchisor — cause (curable, 10 days)**: Payment default, construction delay, insurance lapse. (30 days): Other FA/Manual/Standards breaches.
- **Interim remedies**: Suspend reservations, delist website, divert bookings to other Network hotels, disable software, revoke fee discounts, intervention/admin fees.
- **Non-curable defaults (16 enumerated grounds)**: Repeat breach within 24 months; 3 notices in 12 months; insolvency/bankruptcy; loss of possession; 5 consecutive closed days; contesting marks; reputational conduct; fraudulent reporting; unapproved transfer; sanctions violation; gaming regulatory adverse action; guarantor breach; public health/safety threat; becoming a Competitor; termination of other Hilton hotel agreements.
- **Liquidated damages**: Greater of Hotel's or System's Average Monthly Royalty × remaining months × approved rooms. Waived for condemnation/casualty.
- **Noncompete (in-term)**: Franchisee and Affiliates may not own or license a competing hotel brand; minority interest in Competitor permitted in some circumstances; may be a franchisee or manager of a Competitor.
- **Noncompete (post-term)**: **None.**
- **ROFR / Option to purchase**: None.
- **Dispute resolution**: Court only (no arbitration), except Puerto Rico (AAA/JAMS, English, Federal Rules). Court venue: EDVA Alexandria / Fairfax County VA / New York NY. Franchisor may sue at hotel site.
- **Choice of law**: New York.
- **Integration clause**: Only FA + Application + Guaranty + related signed agreements binding; disclaims oral promises (subject to state law).

**Litigation history** (Item 3):
- **Pending — 4 active Sherman Act §1 class actions**: Extended Stay Hotel antitrust (N.D.Cal., IDeaS); Dai v SAS (N.D.Cal., IDeaS); Segal v Amadeus (N.D.Ill.); Portillo v CoStar/STR (W.D.Wash.). All allege information-sharing agreements that allegedly allowed hotel defendants to set higher room rates.
- **Pending — 1 contract**: AAAA Property Partners v HFH (M.D.Fla., termination counterclaim, not yet served).
- **Concluded**: TX v Hilton DOC resort-fee settlement ($2.1M + disclosure injunction, Jan 2025); NE v Hilton DOC resort-fee settlement ($300K, Feb 2024); Portland Hotel Ownership Curio COO counterclaim ($30M demand, dismissed Jul 2022); San Pedro Inn Hampton wrongful-termination (settled Sept 2020, termination effective Apr 2021); Soule Hawaii resort-fee class ($178K + $20 vouchers, Aug 2015); US v HHC 1970 Sherman §1 consent decree (Portland suppliers).

## F. Item 19 — Financial performance representations

258 total Embassy Suites hotels in the US as of 2024-12-31. **220 Comparable Hotels** (32 company-managed, 188 franchisee-managed). Comparable = active ≥1 full calendar year, no brand/ownership change, no substantial damage or large capex, no substantial guest room changes, data available.

**Room Rate**: Avg $182.63 (2023) → **$184.12** (2024); Median $168.88 → $171.32. Range $109.57–$375.87 in 2024. Only **30.9% of franchisee-managed** hotels met or exceeded the average (vs 53.1% of company-managed). 46.3% of FM met median.

**Occupancy**: Avg 72.9% → **74.4%**; Median 73.8% → 75.3%. Range 47.9%–95.6% in 2024. 53.7% of FM hotels met avg occupancy (vs 62.5% of CM). Median-meeting: 48.9% FM.

**Occupancy Index** (vs STR comp set, 100 = fair share): Avg 110.3 → **111.8** — brand is ~12% above its STR comp set share.

**RevPAR Index**: Avg 121.1 → **121.7**; Median 123.2 → 124.0 — brand is ~22% above comp-set RevPAR. Range 47.1–206.7 in 2024 shows very wide dispersion.

**New Generation Comparable (≥2015 openings, "new prototype")**: 47 hotels (3 CM, 44 FM). Avg Room Rate $205.12 (2024), Avg Occupancy 76.9%. Only **29.5% of FM new-gen hotels met avg room rate**; 45.5% met avg occupancy. Range $142.80–$308.91. New-generation hotels do NOT systematically outperform the full comparable set on all metrics.

**Critical caveats**:
- **No cost or profit disclosure**. Franchisee must model operating costs, labor, utilities, F&B, debt service and Hilton-program fees independently.
- Franchisee-managed hotels materially underperform company-managed — the "average" is lifted by company-managed hotels.
- STR indices use portfolio weighting and competitive sets identified by the hotels themselves.
- Insufficient-data exclusion: 2 FM hotels excluded from 2023 occupancy/RevPAR indexes.
- Comparable set excludes large capex / damage hotels, possibly masking problem assets.

### Key financial observations
- HFH is a **pure pass-through royalty entity**: 99% net margin, nearly all cash distributed up to Hilton parent.
- Revenue CAGR 2022-2024 ~10.6% — steady royalty growth on mostly stable outlet count means **per-hotel royalty productivity is rising** (consistent with occupancy/rate increases in Item 19).
- **Related-party concentration**: $703.9M "other receivables due from Hilton affiliates" = 55% of total assets; equity is partially offset by $(261,959) due from Hilton affiliates within members' equity.
- **Franchise contracts intangible** grew from $238.5M → $392.7M (+$154.2M, +65% YoY). Cash flow shows $85,304K acquisition of franchise contract intangible assets in 2024 — signals significant new contract acquisition activity.
- Leverage: liabilities $692.9M / equity $577M ≈ 1.2x. Moderate on paper, but equity is depressed by ongoing distributions.
- Standalone credit independence of HFH is limited — it depends on the parent ecosystem for cash management.
