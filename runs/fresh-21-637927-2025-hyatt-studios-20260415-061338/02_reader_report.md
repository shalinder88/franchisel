# Reader Report — Hyatt Studios FDD (637927-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand**: Hyatt Studios — upper-midscale extended stay hotel concept launched by Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. (Delaware LLC), subsidiary of Hyatt Hotels Corporation
2. **System size**: Zero operating outlets as of December 31, 2024; 45 franchise agreements signed but none open; 2 projected openings in next fiscal year
3. **Entity structure**: Franchisor is Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C.; parent is Hyatt Hotels Corporation (publicly traded); key affiliates include Hyatt Corporation (operations/services), Rosemont (purchasing), and multiple international franchise affiliates
4. **Franchise began**: May 2023 — very early stage brand (Special Risk #2: "Short Operating History")
5. **Investment range**: $12,188,106 to $21,863,907 (excluding real estate); Development Rights Agreement: $100,000 to $350,000
6. **Fee burden**: 5% royalty + 3% commercial services fee + 2% World of Hyatt loyalty assessment + 1.35% digital acquisition fee = ~11.35% of Gross Rooms Revenue in ongoing percentage fees, plus numerous fixed/variable charges
7. **Item 19**: NO financial performance representations — franchisor explicitly declines
8. **Financial health of parent**: Hyatt Hotels Corp reported net income of $1,296M in 2024 on total revenues of $6,648M; total assets $13,324M; total equity $3,826M; parent unconditionally guarantees franchisor obligations
9. **Special risks**: (a) Out-of-state dispute resolution in Illinois, (b) short operating history / early stage, (c) unregistered primary trademark ("Hyatt Studios" application pending since April 2023)
10. **Territory**: Non-exclusive "Area of Protection" with AOP Term typically ending 3 years after hotel opening; after AOP Term expires, no territorial protection whatsoever
11. **Term/renewal**: 20-year initial term; successor franchise for 10 years on then-current terms (which may be "materially different")
12. **Contract burden**: Liquidated damages on termination (greater of $4,000/room or up to 36 months of 8% revenue); 150% multiplier for Consequential Termination
13. **Noncompete**: No post-term noncompete; during term, franchisee/management company may not be a "Competing Brand Owner"
14. **State addenda**: 14 states with additional disclosures and riders

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Area of Protection (AOP)**: Negotiated before signing; defined by geographic references or radius; could be as small as one block in urban areas or up to 5-mile radius in suburban areas
- **AOP Term**: Begins on FA effective date, typically ends 3 years after Hotel opening
- **During AOP Term**: Franchisor/affiliates won't open another Brand Hotel in AOP, with one major exception: if franchisor acquires a group of 4+ hotels, any in AOP can be converted to Brand Hotels
- **After AOP Term expires**: NO territorial protection whatsoever; franchisor can authorize Brand Hotels within former AOP
- **Existing operating Brand Hotels**: May receive NO Area of Protection at all
- **No exclusive territory**: Franchisee explicitly told to expect competition from other Hyatt brands in their area
- **No ROFR**: No options or rights of first refusal for additional franchises
- **Sales solicitation**: No restrictions on where any party may solicit
- **Internet**: Franchisor/affiliates may use Internet and other channels in AOP without compensation

### Item 13 — Trademarks

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
- No material patents
- Copyright protection claimed for Copyrighted Materials (not registered with US Copyright Office)
- Extensive Confidential Information protections
- Guest Information is franchisor's property; franchisor has unlimited access to PMS data

- Post-termination: franchisee may use only Guest Information generated at Hotel during guests' stays

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
- Management company must be approved; franchisee may self-manage if approved
- Competing Brand Owner may not serve as management company
- Core Management must devote all working time to Hotel; may not work at another lodging facility concurrently
- Revenue manager certification required within 90 days; may serve up to 20 Brand Hotels
- Guaranty required: owners of Controlling Ownership Interest must personally guaranty ALL obligations
- Guarantor Monetary Threshold: net worth minimum and liquidity minimum (set per-deal), increases annually by CPI
- GM vacancy must be filled within 6 months; interim GM must complete GMU

### Item 16 — Restrictions on Sales
- Must offer all products/services franchisor authorizes; may not sell unauthorized items
- Franchisee sets room rates subject to System Standards compliance
- Must honor "best rate guarantee"
- May not charge guest more than rate specified when reservation was made
- No restrictions on customer demographics

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 20 years (from 1st of month Hotel opens)
- **Successor franchise**: 10 years on then-current terms — **may be materially different** including fees
- **Renewal conditions**: substantial compliance during term, currently fully complying, meet then-current new franchisee standards, passing QA scores during previous 3 years, rights to maintain possession for at least 10 years
- **Renewal obligations**: renovate/remodel/expand Hotel and FF&E, sign general release (if state law allows), pay PIP fee

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: Cannot terminate FA or DRA except as law allows; may terminate Central Services Agreement on material breach (10-day cure); may terminate Hyatt Studios Services Schedule on 90 days' notice (after 21 months)
- **By franchisor without cause**: Cannot terminate FA or DRA without cause
- **Curable defaults**: 24 hours (health/safety threat); 10 days (insurance/payment); 30 days (taxes, development deadlines, provider payments, other defaults); 60 days (Guarantor Monetary Threshold)
- **Non-curable defaults**: Bankruptcy, failure to discharge judgments >$100K, cease operations, unauthorized IP activity, unauthorized transfer, felony conviction, false books, unauthorized disclosure, law violations, repeated defaults

### Transfer
- No transfer of FA, DRA, Hotel/assets, or Controlling Ownership Interest without franchisor approval
- Permitted without approval: mortgage to lender, estate planning transfers, non-controlling interest transfers (with conditions)
- Transfer conditions: transferee qualifies, transferee signs then-current agreement (may be materially different), you sign release, you pay amounts owed, transferee agrees to renovate
- Transfer fee: change of ownership application fee ($100,000)
- No right of first refusal or option to purchase
- Death/disability: representative must transfer to approved transferee within 6 months

### Noncompete
- **During term**: No explicit covenant not to compete, but franchisee/management company may not be a Competing Brand Owner (entity owning/managing brand with 5+ hotels that competes with Brand Hotels)
- **Post-term**: **No noncompete** — Item 17(r) states "Not applicable"

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: Mandatory for all disputes; location within 10 miles of franchisor's principal address (currently Chicago, IL)
- **Choice of forum**: Subject to arbitration, litigation in Illinois
- **Choice of law**: Illinois law (subject to Federal Arbitration Act and state overrides)
- **Jury trial waiver**: Implied through mandatory arbitration
- **Limitation of actions**: Not specifically stated in Item 17

### Liquidated Damages
- Greater of: (a) $4,000 × number of rooms; or (b) lesser of 36 months or remaining months × (Average Monthly Revenue × 8%)
- Average Monthly Revenue: based on preceding 12 months GRR if 36+ months open; system average if pre-opening; greater of system average or actual if <36 months open
- **Consequential Termination**: 150% of above — triggered by transfer to Competitor or 3+ Hyatt agreements terminated for default
