## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: Zero operating outlets as of December 31, 2024. Forty-five franchise agreements signed but no hotels yet open. Two projected openings in the next fiscal year (both in Alabama). This is a pre-revenue franchise system — entirely theoretical unit economics.

2. **Entity structure**: Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. (Delaware LLC), a subsidiary of Hyatt Hotels Corporation (NYSE: H). The franchisor converted from Hyatt Franchise Corporation under Delaware law. Neither the franchisor nor any affiliate has ever operated a Hyatt Studios hotel. The parent has operated hotels since 1957 and manages 25+ lodging brands.

3. **Ownership history**: Hyatt Franchising began offering Hyatt Studios franchises in May 2023. The franchisor also offers franchises for Hyatt Regency, Hyatt Centric, Unbound Collection, Caption by Hyatt, Destination by Hyatt, and JdV by Hyatt. Separate entities franchise Hyatt Place and Hyatt House.

4. **Investment range**: $12,188,106 to $21,863,907 for a new 90-130 room hotel, excluding land and building costs. The largest component is construction at $9.87M to $16.20M. Amounts paid directly to the franchisor or affiliates range from $119,267 to $680,202.

5. **Fee burden**: The aggregate ongoing percentage-based fee burden is approximately 11.35% of Gross Rooms Revenue: 5% royalty + 3% commercial services + 2% World of Hyatt loyalty + 1.35% digital acquisition fee. Plus mandatory fixed monthly charges of $2,000-$12,500+ and the Hyatt Studios Services subscription ($1,900-$2,950/month, mandatory for first 2 years). A 4% of GRR capital expenditure reserve is also required.

6. **Item 19**: The franchisor makes NO financial performance representations whatsoever. For an investment of $12M-$22M (plus land), the prospective franchisee receives zero data on expected revenue, occupancy, ADR, or profitability.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk indicators**: (a) Unregistered primary trademark; (b) zero operating history; (c) no FPR disclosed; (d) territory protection expires after just 3 years. These collectively place this offering in a higher-risk category.

8. **Financial health of parent**: Hyatt Hotels Corporation reported $6.65B in total revenue and $1.30B net income in 2024 (elevated by $1.25B in real estate gains). Total assets $13.3B; total equity $3.8B; long-term debt $3.3B. The parent issued $1B in new Senior Notes in March 2025 to fund the Playa Hotels & Resorts acquisition. Deloitte & Touche LLP issued a clean audit opinion. The parent unconditionally guarantees franchisor obligations.

9. **Growth trajectory**: 45 franchise agreements signed across 20 states but zero openings — the pipeline is building but has not yet converted to operating hotels. Only 2 openings projected in the next fiscal year.

10. **Territory**: Non-exclusive "Area of Protection" that typically expires 3 years after the hotel opening. After that, the franchisor can place competing Brand Hotels anywhere, including next door to the franchisee's hotel.

11. **Renewal**: 20-year initial term; 10-year successor franchise on then-current terms that "may be materially different" including fees.

12. **Disputes**: Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, Illinois. One pending AAA arbitration (Imperial Hotels, unpaid royalties).

13. **Special risks**: Three state-mandated risk disclosures: out-of-state dispute resolution (Illinois), short operating history, and unregistered primary trademark.

14. **State addenda**: 14 states with specific additional disclosures and franchise agreement riders.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

Approximately 95% of all purchases and leases to establish and operate the hotel must come from the franchisor, designated suppliers, or according to System Standards. This is one of the highest sourcing percentages in the franchise industry.

Required sources:
- Opera PMS from designated vendor
- CRS, GDS, and ADS from franchisor-designated systems
- All Mandatory Services exclusively from Hyatt Group
- Cyber endpoint protection and web filtering from designated tools
- Quality assurance inspections from designated provider
- Travel agent commission services from designated provider

To request approval of an alternative supplier, the franchisee must submit a written request with samples; the franchisor typically responds within 30 days and may charge up to $20,000 in testing fees. The franchisor can revoke approvals.

The Hyatt Group may receive rebates from suppliers but commits to either forwarding them, applying them to System Services Costs, or using them for the benefit of the Hotel System. In fiscal 2024, no such rebates were received from Brand Hotel franchisee purchases (because there were no operating Brand Hotels).

### Training Program

The mandatory initial training program has 8 parts totaling approximately 305-391 classroom hours and 96-118 OJT hours:

1. **Owner Briefing**: 8 hours (managing owner/senior ops officer)
2. **Franchise Manager University (FMU)**: 15-17 hours (management company)
3. **Owner/Operator Orientation**: 16 hours
4. **General Manager University (GMU)**: 53-58 hours (most intensive individual program)
5. **Operations Training**: 47-79 classroom + 40-76 OJT hours
6. **Sales/Events/Revenue Management**: 136.5 hours (single largest training block)
7. **Other Personnel Training**: 17-34 classroom + 15-36 OJT hours
8. **Pre-Opening Brand Training**: 2-3 trainers on-site for 7+ nights

Replacement training is required for all Core Management changes. The GM vacancy must be filled within 6 months.

### Technology Stack and Costs

Required systems: PMS (Opera), Market Place Solution, Revenue Management, Events/Lead Gen (Envision), Key Lock, PBX/Phones, In-Room Entertainment, HSIA, Music, Digital Surveillance.

Initial technology investment: $350,000-$450,000. Ongoing PMS subscription: $3.90/room/month (pass-through). The franchisor has unlimited independent access to all Technology System data. No contract limits the frequency or cost of future upgrades.

### Marketing Fund / Commercial Services

There is no separate marketing fund. Marketing is funded through the 3% Commercial Services Fee, which covers brand-wide marketing, advertising, public relations, guest satisfaction programs, centralized reservation services, revenue management technology, group sales support, property management system services, digital product technology, and other technology services.

The Hyatt Group is not required to spend any amount in the franchisee's market area, nor to prepare any accounting of how funds are spent. The Hyatt Group will not use these funds principally to solicit new franchise sales.

No local/regional advertising cooperatives currently exist, though the franchisor may form them. If cooperatives are formed, participation is optional.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition

The "Area of Protection" is negotiated before signing and typically defined by geographic references (streets, rivers) or as a circle with the hotel at its center. The AOP size varies widely: as small as one block in urban areas to about a 5-mile radius in suburban areas.

### AOP Term and Expiration

The AOP Term begins on the franchise agreement effective date and typically ends 3 years after the hotel opening. This is an unusually short protection period. After the AOP Term expires, the franchisee has **no territorial protection whatsoever** — the franchisor can authorize other Brand Hotels anywhere, including immediately adjacent to the franchisee's hotel.

### Carve-Outs and Exceptions

Even during the AOP Term, the franchisor has a significant exception: if it acquires a group of at least 4 hotels (any brand), it can convert any acquired hotels within the AOP to Brand Hotels. This acquisition exception effectively allows the franchisor to place a competitor inside the protected territory at any time.

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights

- No restrictions on where any party may solicit customers
- Franchisor and affiliates may use Internet, catalog, telemarketing, or other channels in the AOP without compensation
- Franchisee may use other channels outside AOP only with approval
- The only reservation system the hotel may use is the CRS (plus approved GDS/ADS)
- Other Hyatt brands (Hyatt Place, Hyatt House, Hyatt Regency, etc.) may operate in the AOP now and in the future
- No right of first refusal for additional franchises

### Encroachment Risk Assessment

The territory protection in this FDD is among the weakest available. The 3-year AOP Term means that by year 4 of operation, the franchisee has zero protection against a competing Brand Hotel opening nearby. The acquisition exception further weakens even the 3-year protection period.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

- **Initial term**: 20 years from first day of the month the hotel opens
- **Successor franchise**: 10 years on then-current terms
- **"Materially different" risk**: The successor franchise agreement may contain materially different terms, including different fee structures. This is explicitly stated multiple times.
- **Renewal conditions**: Substantial compliance during term, currently fully complying, meet new franchisee standards, passing QA scores for 3 years, 10-year possession rights, renovate/remodel, sign release, pay PIP fee.

### Transfer Provisions

- All transfers of the franchise agreement, hotel/assets, or Controlling Ownership Interest require franchisor approval
- Transfer fee: Change of ownership application fee of $100,000
- Transferee must meet current qualifications and may be required to sign then-current agreement (materially different terms)
- Franchisee must sign release upon transfer (if state law allows)
- Franchisor has no right of first refusal or option to purchase
- Permitted without approval: mortgage to lender, estate planning transfers, non-controlling interest transfers (with conditions)

### Termination and Death/Disability

- Death or incompetence of Controlling Ownership Interest holder: representative must transfer to approved transferee within 6 months
- No franchisor termination without cause
- Curable defaults: 24 hours (health/safety), 10 days (insurance/payment), 30 days (most defaults), 60 days (guarantor threshold)
- Non-curable defaults: bankruptcy, judgments >$100K, cease operations, unauthorized transfer, felony, false books, repeated defaults

### Noncompete Restrictions

- **During term**: No explicit noncompete covenant, but franchisee and management company may not be a "Competing Brand Owner" (owning/managing a brand with 5+ competing hotels)
- **Post-term**: No noncompete restriction at all — Item 17(r) states "Not applicable"

### Dispute Resolution

- **Mandatory arbitration**: All disputes must be arbitrated at a location within 10 miles of the franchisor's principal business address (currently Chicago, Illinois)
- **Governing law**: Illinois law (subject to Federal Arbitration Act and state franchise law overrides)
- **Litigation venue**: Illinois (subject to arbitration obligation)

### Litigation History

One pending case: Hyatt Franchising v. Imperial Hotels, LLC et al., AAA 2024. The franchisor brought a claim for unpaid royalty fees against a group of individuals. This case involves a Hyatt Place/House franchisee, not a Hyatt Studios franchisee.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

The franchisor **does not make any financial performance representations**. The FDD explicitly states:

> "We do not make any representations about a franchisee's future financial performance or the past financial performance of company-owned or franchised outlets. We also do not authorize our employees or representatives to make any such representations either orally or in writing."

For a total investment of $12.2M to $21.9M (excluding land), the absence of any Item 19 data is a material gap for prospective franchisees. There are no operating hotels to reference, no revenue data, no occupancy data, no ADR, no RevPAR, and no expense data.

The franchisor directs anyone receiving unauthorized financial performance information to contact Dan Hansen (Head of Americas Development and Global Head, Hyatt Studios) and to report it to the FTC and state agencies.

### Key State-Specific Provisions

- **California**: Liquidated damages clause may be unenforceable (Civil Code §1671); Illinois governing law may not be enforceable; spousal liability warning (community property state); general release must comply with Franchise Investment Law
- **Michigan**: Extensive statutory protections including good cause termination requirement, fair market value compensation on non-renewal, prohibition on out-of-state arbitration (which franchisor says it will enforce despite the law)
- **Minnesota**: 90 days' notice of termination with 60 days to cure; 180 days' notice of non-renewal; prohibits waiver of jury trial, liquidated damages, or termination penalties (to extent law requires)
- **North Dakota**: Liquidated damages deemed "unfair, unjust and inequitable" by Commissioner (but parties agree to enforce to extent law allows); releases may not apply to North Dakota Franchise Investment Law claims; arbitration location may be modified
- **Virginia**: Termination without "reasonable cause" may be unlawful under Virginia Retail Franchising Act

Riders to the Franchise Agreement, Development Rights Agreement, and Confidentiality Agreement exist for multiple states, modifying provisions on arbitration location, governing law, liquidated damages, general releases, and termination.

### Risk Profile Summary
This is a **high-risk, pre-revenue franchise offering** backed by a financially strong parent company. The absence of operating outlets, no Item 19 financial data, an unregistered primary trademark, and time-limited territory protection (3 years) are significant risk factors. The unconditional parent guarantee and the Hyatt brand platform provide countervailing strength. A prospective franchisee should carefully evaluate whether the Hyatt Studios concept will achieve competitive positioning in the extended stay segment before committing $12M-$22M+ in capital.
