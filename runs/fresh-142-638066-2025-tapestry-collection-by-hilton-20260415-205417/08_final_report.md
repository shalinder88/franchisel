# Final Diligence Report — Tapestry Collection by Hilton
## Filing 638066-2025 | Issuance Date: March 30, 2025

---

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System Size and Growth**: Tapestry Collection by Hilton operates 117 franchised hotels as of December 31, 2024, up from 63 at the end of 2022 — an 86% increase over two years. The system is 100% franchised with zero company-owned outlets. There are 70 signed franchise agreements for hotels not yet opened and 23 projected openings in the next fiscal year. The brand has experienced zero terminations, non-renewals, or reacquisitions across all three reporting years (2022–2024).

2. **Entity Structure**: The franchisor is Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware limited liability company formed in September 2007. Its parent is Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., a Delaware corporation, which is in turn wholly owned by Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). The franchisor entity covers all Hilton brand franchises in the United States — Tapestry is one of approximately 20 brands offered under the Hilton umbrella.

3. **Ownership History**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC became the Tapestry franchisor on December 1, 2016. Hilton Worldwide's lineage traces back to 1946 through various corporate reorganizations (Hilton Hotels Corporation → Hilton Worldwide, Inc. → Park Hotels & Resorts spin-off in 2017). Tapestry has no predecessor franchisor.

4. **Investment Range**: The total investment for a typical 250-room Tapestry hotel ranges from $3,898,359 to $129,454,079, excluding real property. The extreme width of this range reflects the difference between a conversion of an existing hotel (low end) and new full-service construction with spa and restaurant (high end).

5. **Fee Burden**: Monthly Royalty Fee is 5% of Gross Rooms Revenue. Monthly Program Fee is 4% GRR (may increase up to +1% over the franchise term). Hilton Honors guest reward program costs 4.3% of eligible folio. Combined with technology fees, distribution charges, and quality assurance assessments, the total fee load can approach or exceed 15% of room revenue.

6. **Item 19**: The franchisor does not make any financial performance representations. This means prospective franchisees receive no system-level revenue, cost, occupancy, ADR, RevPAR, or profitability data from the franchisor.

7. **Bottom-Quartile Risk**: Cannot be assessed due to absence of Item 19 data. However, the zero-termination track record suggests the system is not aggressively culling underperformers — or that the brand is young enough that performance has not yet triggered exits.

8. **Financial Health**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC reported net income of $1.488 billion in 2024 on total revenue of $1.502 billion — an operating margin exceeding 99%. The entity is essentially a royalty collection vehicle. Total assets were $1.27 billion. Clean audit opinion from Cherry Bekaert LLP (March 18, 2025). However, all assets and franchise contracts are pledged as collateral for certain Hilton parent debt obligations maturing 2025–2033.

9. **Growth Trajectory**: Rapid and sustained. The brand added 54 hotels in three years (2022–2024) and has 70 in the pipeline. California (23), Florida (10), and New York (9) are the largest state concentrations.

10. **Territory**: Non-exclusive. No default protected territory. A Restricted Area Provision may be negotiated but is time-limited and subject to significant carve-outs (gaming, timeshare, acquisitions of 4+ hotels, other Hilton brands, Strategic Partnerships).

11. **Renewal**: The franchise agreement is non-renewable. Franchisees have no right to renew or extend. Re-licensing is at the franchisor's sole discretion and may involve materially different terms.

12. **Disputes**: Five pending actions — four antitrust class actions involving Hilton Worldwide (IDeaS, Amadeus, CoStar/STR revenue management software allegations) and one franchise breach suit. Six concluded actions including settlements with Texas ($2.1M) and Nebraska ($300K) over mandatory guest fee disclosure.

13. **Special Risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Virginia or New York). Michigan addendum voids certain unfair franchise provisions. Governing law is New York.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Hilton Franchise Holding LLC is the franchisor of record for all Hilton brand franchises in the United States. The entity was formed in September 2007 and began Tapestry franchise operations on December 1, 2016. It is a wholly owned subsidiary within the Hilton Worldwide corporate family, with its principal office in McLean, Virginia.

The Tapestry Collection by Hilton is positioned as a "soft brand" or "collection" brand — each hotel retains its own unique identity, name, and character while operating under the Tapestry umbrella and participating in the Hilton reservation, loyalty, and distribution ecosystem. This is distinct from standardized prototype brands like Hampton or Tru, which require adherence to a uniform design template.

Key affiliates providing services to franchisees include:
- Hilton Reservations Worldwide, L.L.C. (reservation services, Addison, TX)
- Hilton Supply Management LLC (HSM, procurement)
- Hilton Honors Worldwide LLC (loyalty program)
- Hilton Systems Solutions, LLC (HSS, IT systems)

The franchisor also offers optional eforea spa franchises (2 in operation at franchised hotels as of year-end 2024) and optional Restaurant Brand licenses under the StiR Creative Collective program (none yet in operation).

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Franchise Application Fee: $100,000 for New Development/Conversion (250 rooms) + $400 per room over 250. Change of Ownership: $150,000. Re-licensing: $100,000. In practice, 2024 fees ranged $25,000 to $100,000 for New Development/Conversion/COO and $0 to $10,000 for Re-licensing, reflecting negotiated reductions.
- PIP Fee: $10,000 (for Conversion/COO/Re-licensing)
- OnQ Computer System: $103,500 to $273,500
- Other Initial Start-Up Fees: $6,500 (GRO, Digital Floor Plan, Hilton Opening Transition Tool)
- Eforea Spa Initial Fee: $47,500 (optional)

- Procurement Services: 4% to 10% of project cost (if using HSM)

**Ongoing Fees:**

The royalty fee was modified in 24 instances during 2024, indicating some negotiating flexibility for multi-unit or experienced operators.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions and Approved Supplier Process

Franchisees must comply with Hilton's Standards (compiled in the Manual) for all purchases. Key required systems and products include:
- OnQ property management system (proprietary, via HSS — sole source)
- Guest Internet Access ("StayConnected" — preferred providers)
- GRO revenue optimization system (IDeaS-based)
- Delphi sales and events system (Amadeus Hospitality)
- Connected Room system
- Digital Floor Plan
- Digital Key system
- Exterior signage from licensed vendors

For FF&E and general supplies, franchisees may purchase from any source meeting Standards, but Hilton may in the future require approved suppliers or specific brands/models. The supplier approval process typically takes 60 to 90 days.

HSM (Hilton Supply Management) negotiates bulk purchasing arrangements. In fiscal year 2024:
- Administrative fees from franchisee purchases: $38,697,912
- Rebates and allowances: $19,614,010
- Direct sales revenue: $8,763,692

Tapestry-specific requirements include approved Identity Consultant, Food & Beverage Consultant, Lighting Consultant, and Audio Consultant — reflecting the lifestyle/soft-brand positioning where each hotel has a distinct design identity.

Required purchases represent approximately 15% to 20% of establishment cost and 2% to 5% of operating expenses.

### Training Program Detail

The training program includes 13 distinct programs. Key requirements:
- Brand Conference: annually, varies in location, $2,500/attendee (GM and Director of Sales must attend)
- GM/Commercial/Sales Leader Training: up to 18 hours virtual, up to $1,200, within 90 days of start
- HPMS Training: 1-5 hours online, no fee, must complete before opening
- OnQ Rate & Inventory/GRO: 5 hours online, 80% certification score required
- Hilton Core Sales Skills: 24 hours virtual, up to $600, within 90 days (Foundations track for <2yr experience, Accelerated for 2+ years)
- Owner Orientation: 16 hours virtual, no fee, required for new-to-brand owners 12-18 months before opening
- New to Hospitality Owner Education: 20 hours virtual, up to $2,500, for owners without hospitality experience
- Tapestry Orientation: 0 to 32 hours on-site, no fee, all staff within 30 days of opening

Instructors generally have 2 to 5 years experience. Training may be modified, and franchisees pay travel/lodging for attendees and potentially trainers.

### Technology Stack and Costs

Initial technology investment: $286,250 to $562,595 (OnQ + Guest Internet + Connected Room + Delphi + other)

Monthly technology burden:
- OnQ Connectivity: $400–$600
- Hardware/Software Maintenance: $2,422–$6,815
- Connected Room: $980–$1,395
- Guest Internet Circuit: $1,950–$4,900
- Guest Internet Service: $1,003–$2,000
- Total estimated monthly: $6,755–$15,710 ($81,060–$188,520 annually)

Refresh cycles: OnQ every 3 years, Guest Internet Access every 4 years.

### Marketing Fund Spending and Audit Status

The Monthly Program Fee (4% GRR) funds system-wide and network-wide programs. The franchisor has sole control over spending. Key allocations include:
- Reservation Service systems and support
- Brand directories and internet sites
- Advertising, promotion, publicity, PR, market research
- Quality assurance programs
- Technology programs
- Administrative overhead

The Program Fee does not constitute a trust or "advertising fund" and the franchisor is not a fiduciary. There is no obligation to make proportionate expenditures for any individual hotel. No independent audit of Program Fee spending is disclosed.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition and Carve-Outs

The standard Franchise Agreement provides a **non-exclusive license** at a specified location with no default protected territory. Franchisees may face competition from other franchisees, affiliate-owned hotels, other distribution channels, or competitive brands controlled by Hilton.

A **Restricted Area Provision** may be negotiated, typically for New Development or Conversion (not usually for Change of Ownership or Re-licensing). Key characteristics:
- Boundaries vary by hotel, delineated by various methods (cities, streets, radius)
- Restrictive Period is shorter than franchise term, usually tied to years from Application approval
- May reduce geographically after an agreed period
- Historically extended for full term but franchisor states it does not intend to do so in the future

**Carve-outs** that render the Restricted Area Provision ineffective for:
1. Hotels already open, under construction, or approved as of the Effective Date
2. Hotels under brands other than Tapestry
3. Hotels opening after Restrictive Period expiration
4. Gaming-oriented facilities
5. Shared ownership/timeshare properties
6. Hotels acquired as part of a transaction involving 4+ hotels
7. Strategic Partnerships (SLH, AutoCamp, Evermore Resort, Field Station)

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights

The franchisor has broad rights to:
- Operate, franchise, or license any other brand anywhere, including within the Restricted Area
- Enter into Strategic Partnerships with third-party lodging companies
- Use the System, Brand, and Marks in Other Businesses
- Modify the System by adding, altering, or deleting elements

Franchisees may not:
- Register or own domain names containing Hilton marks
- Cross-market with non-System/non-Network hotels without consent
- Use customer lists without prior approval

There is no mechanism for resolving conflicts between a franchisee's hotel and other Network Hotels.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

- **New Construction**: 23 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion**: 10 to 20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership**: Remaining term or as approved
- **Non-renewable**: The franchise agreement explicitly confers "absolutely no rights of license renewal or extension whatsoever." Re-licensing requires a new application, new fees, new (potentially materially different) franchise agreement, and is entirely at the franchisor's sole discretion.

This is a significant contractual burden: franchisees invest millions in a branded hotel but have no guarantee of continued operation under the brand after the initial term expires.

### Transfer Provisions

**Permitted Transfers** (no change of control): Publicly traded equity interests, small private transfers where transferee will hold <50%, and institutional fund restructuring require no notice or consent. Family/affiliate/death transfers and private transfers where >50% has changed hands since the Effective Date require 60 days notice, consent, and a $5,500 processing fee.

**Change of Ownership Transfers**: Require 60 days notice, full application process, $150,000 COO fee, new franchise agreement (then-current form), PIP compliance, background checks. Proposed transferee must not be a Competitor or Sanctioned Person. SBA-financed transfers require fee escrow at closing.

### Termination and Death/Disability

Franchisee cannot voluntarily terminate before expiration — doing so is a material breach triggering liquidated damages.

The franchisor may terminate for curable defaults (payment, construction deadlines, insurance, other defaults — with 10 to 30 days to cure) or non-curable defaults (repeated non-compliance, 3 defaults in 12 months, bankruptcy, loss of possession, 5 consecutive days non-operation, contesting marks, becoming a Competitor, etc.).

On death, the franchise may transfer to immediate family members without consent, provided they meet all requirements within one year.

### Noncompete Restrictions

**During term**: Franchisee and affiliates may not own, license, or franchise a Competing Brand. However, they may be a franchisee of a Competing Brand, or manage a Competing Brand hotel (unless exclusive manager). A "Competing Brand" is defined at the franchisor's sole business judgment.

**Post-term**: No noncompete restriction.

### Dispute Resolution

**Venue**: U.S. District Court, Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria); if no federal jurisdiction, state court in Fairfax County, VA or New York, NY. The franchisor may elect to bring action where the hotel is located.

**Governing law**: New York, without recourse to choice-of-law principles.

**Arbitration**: Not applicable, except in Puerto Rico (where disputes are settled by AAA/JAMS arbitration with venue in Fairfax County, VA and seat in New York, NY).

**Waiver of jury trial**: Not explicitly stated in the Item 17 summary, but typical in Hilton franchise agreements.

### Litigation History

Five pending actions — notably four antitrust class actions alleging Hilton Worldwide participated in improper data exchange through revenue management software providers (IDeaS, Amadeus, CoStar/STR) leading to higher room rates. These are industry-wide suits naming multiple hotel companies. Hilton states it intends to vigorously defend.

Six concluded actions, most notably settlements with Texas ($2.1M) and Nebraska ($300K) over mandatory guest fee disclosure practices.

Zero collection suits against franchisees in 2024.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

**None.** The franchisor explicitly states:

> "We do not make any representations about a franchisee's future financial performance or the past financial performance of company-owned or franchised outlets."

No revenue data, no cost data, no occupancy, ADR, RevPAR, EBITDA, or any other financial metrics are disclosed. The franchisor notes that if a prospective franchisee receives any financial performance information from any source other than the FDD, they should report it to senior management, the FTC, and state regulatory agencies.

This is the most significant limitation of this FDD from a prospective franchisee's perspective. Without Item 19 data, the economic viability of a Tapestry franchise must be evaluated entirely through independent research, industry benchmarks (STR reports), and conversations with existing franchisees (whose ability to communicate may be limited by confidentiality provisions noted in Item 20).

4. **Asset Composition**: The largest non-cash asset is Franchise Contracts ($392.7M), which jumped from $238.5M in 2023 due to the $85M Graduate brand acquisition in May 2024.

5. **Revenue Trend**: Total revenue grew 22% from $1.23B (2022) to $1.50B (2024), driven by franchise royalty fee growth.

6. **Operating Margin**: >99% — the entity has virtually no operating expenses because all services are provided by Hilton affiliates under a no-compensation operator agreement.

7. **Termination fee spike**: Termination fees jumped from $2.4M (2023) to $18.3M (2024), a 7.5x increase. This may reflect the Graduate brand acquisition or increased early terminations across the Hilton portfolio.
