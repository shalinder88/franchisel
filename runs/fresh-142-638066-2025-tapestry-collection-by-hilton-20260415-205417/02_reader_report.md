# Reader Report — Tapestry Collection by Hilton (638066-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand**: Tapestry Collection by Hilton — a "soft brand" collection within the Hilton Worldwide portfolio (Brand code: UP). Launched December 1, 2016. Part of the lifestyle/full-service tier.
2. **Franchisor**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 2007. Parent chain: Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. → Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT).
3. **System Size**: 117 franchised hotels at year-end 2024, up from 63 at year-end 2022 — a rapid growth trajectory (+86% in 2 years). Zero company-owned outlets. Zero terminations in any of the three reporting years (2022–2024). 70 signed agreements not yet opened; 23 projected openings in next fiscal year.
4. **Total Investment**: $3,898,359 to $129,454,079 for a typical 250-room hotel (excluding real property). The extreme width of this range reflects the spread between conversion of an existing hotel (low end) and new full-service development with spa and restaurant (high end).
5. **Initial Fee**: $100,000 (New Development/Conversion, 250 rooms) + $400/room over 250. Change of Ownership: $150,000. Re-licensing: $100,000. In practice, 2024 fees ranged $25,000 to $100,000 for new/conversion and $0 to $10,000 for re-licensing.
6. **Ongoing Fee Burden**: Royalty 5% of Gross Rooms Revenue + Program Fee 4% GRR = **9% base load**. Hilton Honors 4.3% of eligible folio. Hilton Advance 1.35% of eligible digital direct revenue. Plus numerous distribution, technology, and quality assurance fees.
7. **Item 19**: No financial performance representations made. No FPR data available.
8. **Franchise Term**: New Construction: 23 years from Effective Date. Conversion: 10 to 20 years from Opening Date. **Non-renewable** — franchisees have no right to renew or extend. Re-licensing is entirely at franchisor's sole discretion and may come with materially different terms.
9. **Territory**: Non-exclusive license at a specified location. No default protected territory. Restricted Area Provision may be negotiated but is time-limited (Restrictive Period shorter than Term) and has significant carve-outs (gaming, timeshare, acquisitions of 4+ hotels, other brands).
10. **Dispute Resolution**: Venue is Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria) or Fairfax County, VA, or New York, NY. Governing law: New York. No arbitration clause except Puerto Rico.
11. **Special Risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (VA, fallback NY). Michigan addendum voids certain unfair provisions.
12. **Litigation**: Multiple pending class actions involving Hilton Worldwide re: antitrust/price-fixing allegations (IDeaS, Amadeus, CoStar/STR). Several concluded matters (resort fee disputes with TX, NE, HI).
13. **Noncompete**: During term — franchisee may not own/license/franchise a Competing Brand. Post-term — no noncompete restriction.
14. **Financial Health (Item 21)**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC reported net income of $1.488B (2024), $1.372B (2023), $1.233B (2022). Total assets $1.27B. Clean audit opinion (Cherry Bekaert, 3/18/2025). Distributions to parent: $1.5B (2024). Entity guarantees certain Hilton debt.

## Pass E: Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction**: 23 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion**: 10–20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership**: remaining term or as approved
- **Non-renewable**: No right to renew or extend. Re-licensing is at franchisor's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms.

### Termination by Franchisee
- Not authorized before expiration. Unilateral termination = material breach → liquidated damages.

### Termination by Franchisor — Without Cause
- Condemnation: may terminate, no liquidated damages
- Casualty: if Hotel closes, franchisee may repair/rebuild within 18 months; if not, franchisor may terminate

### Termination by Franchisor — Curable Defaults
- Payment default: at least 10 days to cure
- Construction deadline: at least 10 days to cure
- Insurance: at least 10 days to cure
- Other defaults: at least 30 days to cure
- Interim remedies available during default (suspension from Reservation Service, directory removal, software disabling, fee revocation)

### Termination by Franchisor — Non-Curable Defaults
- Repeated non-compliance (same issue within 24 months)
- 3 notices of material default in 12 months
- Bankruptcy/insolvency
- Loss of possession
- Failure to operate for 5 consecutive days
- Contesting System ownership/Marks
- Conduct adversely affecting reputation
- Concealing revenue/false records
- Unauthorized transfer
- Becoming a Sanctioned Person
- Becoming a Competitor
- Breach by guarantor
- Public health/safety threat
- Termination of other Hotel Agreements

### Transfer
- **Permitted Transfers** (no change of control): public equity, small private transfers (<50%), institutional fund restructuring — no notice/consent needed. Family/affiliate/death transfers — 60 days notice + consent + $5,500 fee.
- **Change of Ownership Transfers**: 60 days notice, full application process, $150,000 COO fee, new franchise agreement (then-current form), PIP, background checks, no transfer to Competitor or Sanctioned Person.
- No right of first refusal by franchisor.
- No option to purchase.

### Death or Disability
- Transfer on death to immediate family member without consent, if family member meets requirements within 1 year.

### Noncompete
- **During term**: May not own/license/franchise a Competing Brand. May be franchisee of one, may manage one (unless exclusive manager).
- **Post-term**: No noncompete.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue**: U.S. District Court, Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria), or Fairfax County, VA, or NY, NY. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Governing law**: New York
- **Arbitration**: None except Puerto Rico
- **Modification**: Only in writing signed by officer
- **Integration/Merger**: Only signed agreements enforceable

### Liquidated Damages
- Pre-opening termination: System's Average Monthly Royalty × 60
- Post-opening (before 2nd anniversary): Greater of Hotel's or System's average monthly royalty × 60
- Post-opening (after 2nd anniversary, before last 60 months): Hotel's Average Monthly Royalty × 60
- Last 60 months: Hotel's Average Monthly Royalty × remaining months
- Unauthorized opening: $5,000/day

### Unresolved Items
- U1: The "+1" and "+3" entries in Item 20, Table 3 under "Ceased Operations – Other Reasons" for 2022 and 2024 appear to be positive numbers, which is unusual for a "ceased operations" column. This may indicate reclassifications or additions rather than closures, but the FDD does not explain these entries.
