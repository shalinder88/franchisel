## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and heritage**: DQ Grill & Chill is part of the broader Dairy Queen system with over 7,700 locations globally. The US direct-licensed G&C system stood at 1,971 units (including 2 company-owned) as of December 31, 2024. An additional 567 subfranchised G&C/Brazier units operate under territory operators, and 568 Texas DQ restaurants operate under a separate concept.

2. **Entity structure**: ADQ is a Delaware corporation (incorporated 1962), wholly owned by IDQ, which is wholly owned by Berkshire Hathaway, Inc. Key affiliates include USCI (supply chain), DQF (financing services), DQGC (gift cards), DQC (Canada), and DQTR (operates 2 training restaurants in Minnesota).

3. **Ownership/governance history**: Troy A. Bader has served as CEO/President since January 2018. Arthur P. D'Elia joined as COO (US and Canada) in March 2025, previously serving as Domino's EVP International. Daniel J. Kropp shifted from COO to Chief Supply Chain and Development Officer in March 2025.

4. **Investment range**: $1,516,200 – $2,543,050 for a single new DQ Grill & Chill restaurant, excluding land ($250K–$800K if purchasing). The two prototypical buildings are Next Gen Core 34 (1,938 sq ft, seats 34) and Next Gen Core 46 (2,208 sq ft, seats 46).

5. **Fee burden**: Total ongoing fees of 9%–10% of Gross Sales (4% royalty + 5%–6% sales promotion fee). Initial franchise fee $45,000. Renewal fee $22,500. Transfer fee $6,000 (escalating). Termination fee is 2× last 12 months' royalty.

6. **Item 19 highlights**: Average gross sales of $1,427,281 across 321 qualifying restaurants in 2024 (median $1,355,422). Average manageable profit of 28.92% for 55 restaurants with usable P&Ls. Low-end gross sales were $583,730.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: A restaurant at the bottom of the distribution ($583K gross sales) paying the full 9–10% fee load ($52K–$58K) plus occupancy, insurance, debt service, and other fixed costs would likely operate at a loss. Even at the median ($1.36M), after deducting royalties (~$54K), sales promotion fees (~$68K–$81K), and typical occupancy/insurance, the owner's cash return may be modest relative to the $1.5M+ investment.

8. **Financial health**: IDQ is highly profitable — $97.9M net income on $257.6M revenue in 2024 (38% net margin). Berkshire Hathaway extracts substantial dividends ($110M in both 2023 and 2024, exceeding net income). Total stockholder's equity was $80.3M with a retained deficit of $68.4M. IDQ unconditionally guarantees ADQ's obligations.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Net growth has decelerated dramatically: +13 (2022), +2 (2023), +2 (2024). Openings declined from 38 to 30 while terminations remained near 25–39 per year. The subfranchised system is essentially flat, and the Texas DQ system is shrinking (-11 in 2024).

10. **Territory**: No exclusive territory. Site-only franchise. ADQ retains broad competition rights via any channel.

11. **Renewal risk**: Renewal requires signing the then-current franchise agreement, which "may differ materially, including higher or additional fees." This is a significant re-pricing risk at renewal.

12. **Litigation**: Four pending cases including a significant transfer-related dispute (Project Lonestar). ADQ's new COO D'Elia is named in Domino's securities litigation, though ADQ is not a party.

13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Minneapolis, MN) flagged on cover page.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
American Dairy Queen Corporation, incorporated in Delaware in 1962, operates as a franchise administration entity. It does not directly operate any restaurants. Its affiliate DQTR operates two DQ Grill & Chill training restaurants in Minnesota. The franchise structure offers single-unit (Street and Captive-venue) and multi-unit (MultiTRA) development options. Conversion programs exist for legacy Dairy Queen/Brazier and non-system food stores.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Initial franchise fee: $45,000 ($10K non-refundable deposit; balance refundable in 4 limited circumstances)
- MultiTRA trade area reservation: $22,500 per unit (non-refundable; credited against franchise fee)
- MTRA assessment: $200/person

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Continuing license fee (royalty): 4% of Gross Sales
- Sales promotion program fee: 5%–6% of Gross Sales (ADQ determines rate; 90 days notice)
- **Combined ongoing fee load: 9%–10% of Gross Sales**

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Transfer fee: $6,000 (increases $500 every 5 years starting 2030)
- Renewal fee: $22,500
- Termination fee: 2× last 12 months' royalty (with prorated alternatives)
- Audit costs: Full audit expenses + 2 years of follow-up audits if ≥3% Gross Sales understatement
- Interest on past due: 18% per annum
- Late fees: $50 per delinquent report/payment
- Training cancellation: $100–$1,000 depending on timing and phase
- Training materials: $150–$500 periodic
- Gift card program: 3% of redemptions (~$200/year)
- Costs and attorneys' fees for enforcement: variable

**Conversion/Relocation Adjustments:**
Conversion franchisees enjoy reduced royalty and sales promotion fee rates that phase up over 5–10 years to standard levels. Relocation franchisees carry over existing rates for years 1–5, mid-point for years 6–10, then standard rates apply.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
ADQ maintains tight control over the supply chain. Franchisees must use only ADQ-approved products meeting ADQ specifications. ADQ has the right to designate a single supplier for soft drinks, third-party brands, LTO products, equipment/EPOS, and any product where ADQ receives no vendor fee.

Designated sole suppliers include ParTech (EPOS), Data Central (back office), Fiserv (payment processing), Verifone (encryption), ValueLink (gift cards), Olo (mobile ordering), Punchh (loyalty), Acumera (firewall), and Cineplex (digital menu boards). The alternate supplier approval process takes 90–180 days minimum and may be significantly longer.

ADQ estimates that required purchases represent 65%–85% of establishment cost and 30%–50% of operating cost.

### Supply Chain Economics
USCI (wholly owned by IDQ) manages the supply chain with a permanently capped maximum average margin of 1.5% (since 2025). This commitment is binding on any future buyer of USCI. USCI authorized warehouses pay up to 0.5% of gross sales to USCI. IDQ and affiliates receive vendor payments of 0%–10% of supplier sales to franchisees.

In 2024, IDQ derived $40.3M from product sales, marketing kits, and supplier service fees (16% of $257.6M total revenue). Advertising fund vendor payments totaled ~$3.1M.

An independent accounting firm annually reviews USCI performance measures, shared with the Supply Chain Advisory Council (franchisee representatives).

### Training Program
Three phases totaling 277 hours (32 classroom + 245 OJT):
- Phase 1 (Product & Equipment): 121 OJT hours, ~2.5 weeks at certified DQ location
- Phase 2 (Service/Management/Financial): 122 OJT hours, ~2.5 weeks at certified DQ location
- Phase 3 (People, PRIDE & Profit): 32 classroom hours, 4 days at Minneapolis HQ

Three required attendees: designated manager + 2 assistant managers. Cost: $3,600/person (included in franchise fee for first 3 if full fee paid). MTRA assessment ($200/person) and SERVSAFE certification ($200–$400/person) also required.

### Technology Stack and Costs
- EPOS system (ParTech): $25,500–$33,000 initial
- Monthly software (ParBrink, Data Central, Olo, Punchh): $408–$481
- Olo per-transaction fee: 0.25% per order
- Hardware warranty: $72–$150/month
- Credit card processing (Fiserv): ~2%–5% per transaction + TransArmor $19.95/month
- Verifone encryption: ~$10/terminal/month
- Acumera firewall: ~$51/month
- PCI compliance: $200–$2,000 initial, up to $100/month ongoing
- No contractual limitation on upgrade frequency or cost
- ADQ has unlimited access to all EPOS data

### Marketing Fund
Sales promotion fees fund national (NMF), regional (DMA), and local activities. ADQ has sole discretion over spending allocation. 2024 spending: 66.8% media placement, 19.7% production, 4.3% admin, 9.1% other. ADQ takes a 7% management fee on sales promotion payments (not above 3% of gross sales). Fund is audited annually by independent accounting firm. MAC (Marketing Advisory Council) conducts its own review.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Territory Definition
DQ Grill & Chill franchises are **site-only** with no exclusive territory. The franchise agreement grants only the right to operate at a single authorized location. Franchisees receive no territorial protection whatsoever.

### Franchisor Competition Rights
ADQ and affiliates retain the right to:
- Operate and franchise competing businesses under any trademarks at any location except the specific authorized location
- Sell products via internet, catalog, telemarketing, social media, mobile apps, third-party platforms, and metaverse without compensation to franchisees
- Establish DQ Treat, Texas DQ, Orange Julius, or any other concept adjacent to existing locations
- License others to operate under DQ trademarks anywhere

### MultiTRA Trade Areas
Multi-unit franchisees receive designated trade areas but these are non-exclusive. ADQ retains rights to place units within trade areas in: shopping centers ≥500K sqft, transportation terminals, campus facilities, sports venues, retail buildings ≥150K sqft, institutional locations, airports, and pre-existing commitments.

### Channel Restrictions
Franchisees may sell only from their authorized location. No rights to sell via any other channel including internet, catalog, or other distribution methods unless specifically authorized by ADQ.

### Legacy Territory Operators
11 territory operators (standard and urban) still operate with subfranchising rights. These operators are NOT bound by ADQ site clearance guidelines and may grant franchises near existing units at their discretion.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- Initial term: 20 years (new restaurant); lesser of 20 years or remaining transferor term (transfer)
- Renewal: One additional term, shorter of 10 years or remaining lease term
- **"Materially different" risk**: Renewal requires signing the then-current franchise agreement, which "may differ materially, including higher or additional fees." This is a significant re-pricing mechanism.
- Renewal conditions: Written notice, modernization to current standards, good standing, no history of substantial noncompliance, $22,500 renewal fee, general release, location approved for renewal

### Transfer Provisions
- All transfers require ADQ consent (not unreasonably withheld if all conditions met)
- Conditions: Transferee qualifies, $6,000 transfer fee paid, all amounts current, release signed, transferee signs then-current agreement (may have materially different terms), modernization completed
- ADQ has right of first refusal to match any purchase offer
- MultiTRA is NOT transferable

### Termination and Death/Disability
- ADQ may terminate for cause with cure periods: 24 hours (trademark/health/safety), 10 days (late payments/reports), 30 days (other curable)
- Non-curable defaults: loss of premises, failure to reopen, abandonment, insolvency, conviction, intentional underreporting, 3rd default in 12 months, OFAC designation
- Franchisee may terminate for ADQ material breach (30 days notice, 60 days effective)
- Death/disability: Must comply with all transfer requirements
- Termination fee: 2× last 12 months' royalty (material financial penalty)

### Noncompete
- **During term**: No involvement in any QSR serving hamburgers (without alcohol) or business with >10% revenue from frozen treats
- **Post-term**: 500 meters from authorized location for 1 year (Street); same building/venue (Captive-venue)
- State overrides: CA (may be unenforceable), ND (may be unenforceable), WA (void below $100K employee earnings/$250K contractor earnings)

### Dispute Resolution
- Primary: Arbitration in Minneapolis, MN (except certain claims)
- Litigation forum: Federal District Court for District of MN or Hennepin County
- Governing law: Law of state where authorized location is situated
- State overrides: IL (venue outside IL void except arbitration), MD (claims may be brought in MD), MN (litigation stays in MN), ND (arbitration at nearest AAA office), RI (venue/law provisions void re RI Act claims), WA (arbitration in WA or mutual agreement; litigation in WA not precluded)

### Litigation History
**Pending (4 cases):**
1. Project Lonestar v. ADQ (N.D. Texas, Feb 2025): Breach of contract, interference, and fraud related to franchise transfer process. ADQ has counterclaims for unpaid fees.
2. D'Elia Domino's securities class action (E.D. Michigan): ADQ's COO named as defendant from prior Domino's role. ADQ is not a party.
3. ADQ v. Estate of Ridl (MN, Jul 2025): Franchise termination for abandonment after franchisee death.
4. Oshlo-Wilkinson v. ADQ (Iowa, Nov 2025): Territory operator transfer rights dispute.

**Concluded (4 cases):**
- Johnson/Food Ventures: Encroachment claim settled with reduced royalty + $25K from ADQ
- Universal Investment: Wisconsin dealership/termination settled with territory relinquishment + $425K from ADQ
- M&M Petroleum: Wrongful termination settled with mutual release
- White Enterprise: Marketing fund dispute settled with franchisee receiving full NMF benefits at 2.5%

### Critical Caveats
1. **Manageable profit excludes major costs**: Occupancy, insurance, royalties (4%), sales promotion fees (5–6%), legal, accounting, and admin costs are NOT deducted. The true net margin after all costs is substantially lower.
2. **2024 P&L sample is extremely small**: Only 55 of 321 qualifying restaurants (17%) had usable P&Ls. The 285 excluded may skew toward lower-performing units (less likely to submit P&Ls). This creates potential survivorship/selection bias.
3. **Schedule B 2024 gross sales average ($1,648,666) is higher than Schedule A ($1,427,281)**: This suggests the 55 restaurants with usable P&Ls are biased toward higher-performing locations.
4. **Bottom-quartile scenario**: At the low end of Schedule A ($583K gross), even with median manageable profit margins, the dollar profit before royalties/fees would be only ~$169K. After deducting ~$52K–$58K in royalties/sales promotion fees, occupancy, insurance, and debt service on $1.5M+ investment, the operator would likely lose money.
