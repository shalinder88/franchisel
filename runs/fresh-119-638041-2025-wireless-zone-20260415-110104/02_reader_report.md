# Reader Report — Wireless Zone LLC (638041-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Wireless Zone LLC** is a Connecticut LLC (founded 1988 as Automotive Technologies, Inc.) headquartered in Fishers, IN. Wholly owned subsidiary of PYITE, LLC → Round Room, LLC.
2. Franchises retail wireless stores selling smartphones, tablets, watches, accessories, wireless home internet under the Wireless Zone® mark. Has been franchising since 1989.
3. **745 franchised stores** at end of 2024 across 30+ states. Zero company-owned WZ stores. Affiliate TCC operates 515 competing stores under a different mark.
4. **Total initial investment:** $201,875–$532,600. Initial franchise fee: $25,000 standard ($1,000 for existing franchisees or conversions).
5. **Royalty structure:** Sliding scale on Gross Profit — 22% on first $5,000/month declining to 9% on GP above $100,000/month. Deducted from commissions before payout.
6. **Item 19 FPR (annual 2024, 703 stores):** Median gross revenue $1,725,481; median gross profit $502,319; weighted average gross margin ~29%.
7. **Bottom quintile risk:** Q5 avg gross profit only $243,825/year; ranges down to $36,967. Some stores posted negative gross profit.
8. **Massive 2023 expansion:** +273 stores (TCC storefront conversions). 2024 organic growth slowed to +25 net.
9. **Territory:** 2-mile radius (1-mile in cities ≥350k). Not exclusive — TCC can compete freely in protected territory.
10. **Term:** 7 years with one 7-year renewal. Renewal requires signing then-current agreement (which may be materially different).
11. **Dispute resolution:** Mediation at neutral site, arbitration within 15 miles of Indianapolis, litigation in Indiana courts. Indiana law governs.
12. **Financial health:** Net income $34.9M (2024). Parent Round Room carries $239.6M term debt maturing 2027. WZ LLC distributes more than it earns — accumulated deficit of $46.8M.
13. **Provider dependency:** Single wireless service provider (undisclosed by name, but implied to be Verizon based on industry context). 100% of commissions receivable from one provider.
14. **Non-compete:** 2-year post-term, 10-mile radius around store or any existing WZ/affiliate store.
15. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution; mandatory Foundation contribution; step-in rights; IP infringement liquidated damages ($1,000/day).

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Supplier Restrictions
- **Single Provider model:** Franchisor selects one wireless carrier as exclusive Provider for all franchisees. Franchisees cannot sell services from unauthorized providers.
- **Sole-source items:** POS Environment, digital signage, PCI compliance, signs, fixtures, displays, traffic counters, inventory — all from franchisor or approved suppliers only.
- **Equipment inventory:** Franchisor is approved but not sole supplier; can purchase equivalent from own supplier.
- **Accessory purchasing program:** Optional but exclusive — favorable pricing in exchange for exclusivity. Franchisor received $88,192 in supplier rebates from this program in 2024.
- **Revenue from franchisee purchases:** $994.7M in 2024 (77% of total revenue of $1.29B). Technology infrastructure: $962,564 (0.07%).
- **Source-restricted purchases:** ~70%–90% of initial investment; ~70%–85% of ongoing purchases.
- **Foundation contributions:** $646,625 mandatory in 2024.
- **Supplier approval process:** 30-day review. No obligation to approve alternative suppliers for sole-source items.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Protected Territory:** 2-mile radius (1-mile in cities ≥350,000 population). Shopping mall locations: the mall itself is the territory.
- **Exclusions:** Convention centers, enclosed malls within territory (unless mall IS the territory).
- **NOT exclusive:** Franchisee may face competition from other WZ franchisees, franchisor-owned outlets, TCC stores, other channels, and competitive brands controlled by affiliates.
- **Franchisor protection:** Will not grant another WZ franchise physically within Protected Territory (if franchisee is in compliance) — but extensive carve-outs and alternative channel rights remain.
- **Alternative channels:** Franchisor retains full rights for internet, e-commerce, mail order, telemarketing, big box, grocery, theme parks, airports, stadiums — no compensation to franchisee.
- **TCC competition:** TCC stores may freely solicit/accept orders in franchisee's Protected Territory.
- **Overlap:** Adjacent franchisees' Protected Territories may overlap.
- **Minimum performance:** Monthly sales minimums set by franchisor and Provider. Failure for 3 consecutive months = termination right.
- **No right to additional franchises** within Protected Territory.

### Item 13 — Trademarks

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term & Renewal
- **Initial term:** 7 years
- **Renewal:** One additional 7-year term
- **Renewal conditions:** Notice, no material default, no money owed, renovations, $1,000 renewal fee, sign general release, sign **then-current franchise agreement** (may contain materially different terms), receive Provider approval
- **Holdover:** If actively pursuing renewal but term expires, agreement may continue up to 180 days ($500/month holdover fee in addition to all other fees)

### Termination
- **By franchisee:** No provision
- **Without cause:** If Provider withdraws sub-agent approval
- **Curable defaults (30-day cure):** Failure to pay, file reports, comply operationally, maintain minimum activations, provide correct identifying info
- **Non-curable (immediate):** Bankruptcy, unapproved transfer, abandonment, felony conviction, impairment of goodwill (including sharing payments with other franchisees), confidentiality breach, underreporting royalties, false books, lease loss, repeated defaults, fraud, failure to agree on location in 180 days, anti-terrorism violation, cross-default with other WZ agreements, revoking EFT authorization

### Transfer
- No transfer without prior written consent
- Right of first refusal: 30 days on same terms as bona fide offer
- Transfer fee: up to $5,000 (currently $1,000); 50% discount for multi-store acquisitions
- Transferee must sign new 7-year agreement, complete training, purchase initial inventory from franchisor
- Convenience transfers to entity: single-purpose entity required, owners guarantee

### Death/Disability
- Franchisor may assume Store operation
- Option to purchase for 180 days at agreed or appraised value
- Heirs must transfer within 12 months (subject to transfer conditions)

### Post-Termination Purchase Option
- Franchisor may purchase assets at: Gross Profits for trailing 12 months + FMV of tangible assets

### Non-Competition
- **During term:** No involvement in competing wireless/wireline business
- **Post-term:** 2 years, 10-mile radius around store or any existing WZ/affiliate store
- **Provider restriction:** No discussions/negotiations with any Provider for 1 year post-term
- **Liquidated damages:** $100,000 per violation + attorneys' fees

### Dispute Resolution
- **Mediation first:** Minimum 4 hours, neutral metro area ≥250,000 pop, not within 200 miles of either party's office
- **Arbitration:** Within 15 miles of Indianapolis, IN
- **Litigation (if permitted):** State or federal court in Indiana
- **Governing law:** Indiana
- **Franchisor exceptions:** Not required to mediate for trademark impairment, abandonment, confidentiality violations, non-compete violations
- **Modification:** Only by written agreement, except franchisor may change Operations Manual/system standards; modifications accepted by 80% of stores are binding on all
