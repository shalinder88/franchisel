## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Wireless Zone LLC** is a Connecticut LLC founded in 1988, franchising since 1989 under the Wireless Zone® mark. The company is a wholly owned subsidiary of PYITE, LLC, which is in turn wholly owned by Round Room, LLC. Round Room also controls The Cellular Connection (TCC), which operates 515 company-owned stores selling substantially similar wireless products and services.

2. **System size:** 745 franchised stores at year-end 2024 across 30+ states, with zero company-owned Wireless Zone stores. The franchisor does not currently operate any locations, though it may do so in the future.

3. **Explosive 2023 growth:** The system grew from 447 to 720 stores in 2023 (+273), driven primarily by conversion of 277 TCC storefronts to Wireless Zone franchisees. 2024 organic growth was far more modest at +25 net (42 opened, 17 terminated).

4. **Total initial investment:** $201,875–$532,600, including $109,500–$281,000 payable to the franchisor or its affiliate. The standard initial franchise fee is $25,000 but practically all new grants in 2024 were at $0–$1,000 (existing franchisee or conversion rates).

5. **Fee burden:** Royalties are calculated on Gross Profit (not gross revenue) using a sliding scale from 22% on the first $5,000/month down to 9% on GP above $100,000/month. Royalties are deducted from Provider commissions before the franchisee receives any payout. Additional monthly technology/platform fees total approximately $383–$423/month (software license $103, WSS support $100, ReBiz $180, plus optional video/messaging).

6. **Item 19 FPR (703 stores, full-year 2024):** Median annual gross revenue $1,725,481; median annual gross profit $502,319; weighted average gross margin ~29%. Bottom quintile (141 stores) averaged only $243,825 in gross profit with the lowest store at $36,967. The FPR does not include rent, payroll, operating expenses, or royalties — actual franchisee take-home is substantially lower.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** With median gross profit of ~$502K and a blended royalty of perhaps 12–15% (estimated from the sliding scale), a median franchisee might net ~$425K–$440K in GP after royalties, before rent ($16.5K–$125K), payroll, insurance, and other operating costs. A Q5 store at $243K GP is highly likely operating at a net loss.

8. **Financial health of franchisor:** Net income of $34.9M on $1.29B revenue (2024). However, distributions to parent ($39.4M) exceeded net income, driving accumulated deficit to $46.8M. The parent Round Room carries $239.6M in term debt maturing September 2027, secured by substantially all borrowers' assets including WZ LLC's.

9. **Growth trajectory:** 48–83 new franchised stores projected for 2025, with 34 agreements signed but not yet opened. TCC plans 20–30 new company-owned stores.

10. **Territory:** 2-mile radius (1-mile in cities ≥350,000). Not exclusive — TCC affiliate can freely compete within the territory. Extensive alternative channel and encroachment rights reserved by franchisor.

11. **Renewal risk:** 7-year term with one 7-year renewal. Renewal requires signing then-current agreement, which may contain "materially different terms."

12. **Disputes:** Mediation at neutral location, arbitration within 15 miles of Indianapolis, litigation in Indiana. Indiana law governs. Out-of-state dispute resolution is identified as a "special risk" on the cover page.

13. **Provider concentration:** The entire system depends on a single wireless service provider (not named in FDD). All commissions receivable come from this one provider. This is the single most consequential risk factor.

14. **Litigation history:** Two concluded lawsuits (2015–2016) involving royalty calculation disputes with franchisees, settled for significant payments ($48,440/location in one case, $1.25M in another). No pending litigation.

15. **Non-compete:** 2-year post-term restriction within 10-mile radius of the store or any existing WZ/affiliate store. $100,000 liquidated damages per violation.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

The franchise system operates on a single-Provider model. The franchisor selects one wireless communications provider as the exclusive carrier for all franchisees. Franchisees may not sell services from any other provider. The franchisor is the sole or primary approved supplier for:

- All POS Environment products, services, and installation
- Digital signage and monitor equipment
- Signs, fixtures, displays, kiosks
- Traffic counter systems
- PCI compliance services
- Credit card processing (approved processor required)
- Wireless and wireline communications services (via Provider)

For equipment inventory (devices), the franchisor is an approved but not sole supplier — franchisees may purchase from other sources of equal or better quality. An optional exclusive accessory purchasing program offers favorable pricing in exchange for purchasing exclusivity (franchisor received $88,192 in supplier rebates from this program in 2024).

Source-restricted purchases constitute approximately 70%–90% of initial investment and 70%–85% of ongoing purchases. In 2024, the franchisor received $994.7M in revenue from franchisee device and accessory purchases (77% of total $1.29B revenue) and $962,564 from technology infrastructure purchases.

### Training Program

- **Format:** Virtual (mandatory new franchisee training)
- **Duration:** Minimum 80 hours
- **Deadline:** Must complete within 120 days of signing Franchise Agreement or face termination
- **Cost:** No initial training fee for one attendee; travel/lodging/meals at franchisee's expense for in-person programs
- **Ongoing:** Periodic mandatory meetings and training sessions; no-show fee $500–$2,000
- **Training director:** Miguel Chavez (since February 2025; >5 years wireless retail experience)
- **Waiver:** May be waived entirely for existing WZ franchisees

### Technology Stack

Initial setup: 2 desktop POS workstations + 4 mobile POS tablets + 2 credit card readers + printer + network/security equipment ($7,000–$9,000).

Monthly costs: Software license ($103), WSS tech support ($100–$160), ReBiz traffic counter ($180), digital video/radio ($20–$40), automated messaging ($30–$50). Total recurring technology burden: approximately $433–$533/month.

Hardware must be updated/replaced every 3 years or as directed. All upgrades at franchisee expense. The franchisor has full independent access to all POS data, sales information, and customer data. Annual PCI compliance certification required.

### Marketing Fund / Advertising

There is no mandatory franchisee-funded advertising or marketing fund. The franchisor spends cooperative funds received from the Provider in its sole discretion, including on branding, advertising, and promotional activities. The franchisor determines cost, media, content, format, timing, and allocation with no obligation to ensure proportionate benefit to any franchisee.

Franchisees must aggressively promote locally within their Protected Territory. All marketing materials require prior written approval. No independent websites, social media accounts, domain names, or digital presence permitted — only franchisor-maintained platforms.

Franchisees must participate in civic organizations and support all philanthropic programs the franchisor mandates, including the Foundation ($0.25 per activation/upgrade). Total mandatory Foundation contributions by all franchisees in 2024: $646,625.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory

The Protected Territory is a 2-mile radius around the original store location. In cities with population ≥350,000 (per U.S. Census), it shrinks to 1-mile radius. For enclosed shopping mall locations, the territory is the mall itself.

**Explicit exclusions from Protected Territory:**
- Exhibition, convention, and conference halls/centers
- Enclosed shopping malls (whether existing or future) within the geographic description, unless the mall IS the territory
- Non-traditional locations

**Franchisee will NOT receive an exclusive territory.** The FDD explicitly states: "You may face competition from other franchisees, from outlets that we own, or from other channels of distribution or competitive brands that we control."

### Encroachment Rights Reserved by Franchisor

The franchisor reserves comprehensive encroachment rights:

1. **TCC competition:** The affiliate TCC operates 515+ stores that may freely solicit and accept orders anywhere, including inside the franchisee's Protected Territory. Neither WZ LLC nor TCC is restricted from expanding geographically.

2. **Alternative channels:** Wholesale, mail order, catalog, Internet, e-commerce, telemarketing, toll-free numbers, and sales through department stores, big box stores, grocery stores, theme parks, airports, stadiums — all using Wireless Zone® marks or other marks, with no compensation to franchisees.

3. **Other providers and agents:** The Provider and other agents of the Provider may operate competitive stores in the territory (without using the WZ name).

4. **Franchisor's own operations:** The franchisor reserves the right to operate or license retail stores offering similar products within or outside the territory.

5. **Cross-territory orders:** The franchisor may accept orders from inside the franchisee's territory without payment. The franchisee may accept orders from outside the territory without payment to another franchisee, but may NOT market outside the territory.

### Minimum Performance

The franchisor establishes minimum monthly sales and performance standards including minimum new activations and total boxes. These are published in the Operations Manual (not in the FDD). Failure to meet minimums for 3 consecutive months triggers termination rights.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

- **Initial term:** 7 years
- **Renewal:** One additional term of 7 years
- **Renewal conditions:** Written notice; no material default; no money owed; renovations to current standards; $1,000 renewal fee; sign general release; sign then-current franchise agreement; receive Provider approval to continue as sub-agent
- **"Materially different" risk:** The renewal agreement "may contain terms and conditions materially different from those in your current franchise agreement, such as different fee requirements and territorial rights." This is a significant renewal risk.
- **Holdover provision:** If actively pursuing renewal but term expires, agreement may continue up to 180 days at $500/month holdover fee (in addition to all other fees). Not credited against renewal fee.

### Transfer Provisions

- No transfer without prior written consent
- Conditions: pay all amounts owed; relinquish demo lines; establish charge-back reserve; sign release; transferee must be qualified; transferee pays transfer fee (up to $5,000, currently $1,000); transferee signs new 7-year agreement; transferee must purchase initial inventory from franchisor; store may need renovation
- Right of first refusal: 30 days on same terms as bona fide offer
- Multi-store discount: 50% on transfer fee
- Convenience transfer to entity: single-purpose entity required, owners must guarantee

### Termination and Death/Disability

- **By franchisee:** No provision for unilateral termination
- **Without cause by franchisor:** If Provider withdraws sub-agent approval
- **Curable defaults (30-day cure):** Failure to pay, file reports, comply operationally, maintain minimum activations, provide correct identifying information
- **Non-curable (immediate termination):** Bankruptcy/insolvency, unapproved transfer, abandonment, felony conviction, impairment of goodwill (including sharing payments with other franchisees), confidentiality breach, underreporting royalties, false books, lease loss/eviction, repeated defaults, fraud, failure to agree on location within 180 days, anti-terrorism violation, cross-default, revoking EFT authorization
- **Death/disability:** Franchisor may assume store operation. 180-day purchase option at agreed or appraised value. Heirs must transfer within 12 months.
- **Post-termination purchase option:** Gross Profits for trailing 12 months + FMV of tangible assets

### Non-Compete Restrictions

**During term:** No ownership interest or involvement in any business offering wireless or wireline communications products/services and/or entertainment and security products/services materially similar to any part of the System. No discussions, negotiations, or agreements with any Provider.

**Post-term:** 2 years, within 10-mile radius of the store or any then-existing WZ or affiliate store offering similar products. No Provider discussions/negotiations for 1 year.

**State overrides:** Indiana limits post-term non-compete to 3 years max and exclusive area per IC 23-2-2.7-1(9). California may void the post-term non-compete entirely under B&P Code §16600. North Dakota considers such covenants generally unenforceable.

**Liquidated damages:** $100,000 per violation plus attorneys' fees.

### Dispute Resolution

1. **Mediation first:** Minimum 4 hours, at neutral metro area ≥250,000 population, not within 200 miles of either party's principal office. Mediator must have 10+ years franchise experience. Costs shared equally.
2. **Arbitration:** If mediation fails, binding arbitration within 15 miles of Indianapolis, Indiana.
3. **Litigation:** If permitted, in Indiana state or federal court.
4. **Governing law:** Indiana law.
5. **Franchisor exceptions:** Not required to mediate for: improper transfer, abandonment, trademark impairment, goodwill impairment/fraud, confidentiality violations, non-compete violations. May seek injunctive relief in any forum.
6. **No set-offs:** Franchisee may not withhold amounts owed regardless of claims.
7. **80% modification rule:** Any franchise agreement modification accepted by 80% of stores is binding on all franchisees.

**State overrides:** Indiana prohibits choice of forum outside Indiana (IC 23-2-2.7-1(10)). Multiple states override various provisions per Exhibit I addenda.

### Litigation History

Two concluded lawsuits (both settled in 2016):
1. **Family Wireless #1 (2015):** 32 franchisees challenged royalty calculations on Provider commissions. Settled for avg $48,440/location plus revised multi-store royalty reduction program.
2. **Tel Group (2016):** 2 franchisees (25 stores) with similar claims plus renewal rights dispute. Settled for $1,250,000 plus revised incentive program for 10 years.

Both cases arose from the transition to the current gross profit royalty model. No pending litigation.

5. **Sample bias.** 42 stores opened during 2024 and 17 that closed are excluded. Closed-store exclusion may bias results upward.
6. **Provider not named.** Commission structures, residual programs, and performance metrics are entirely dependent on a single unnamed provider's terms.
