# Reader Report — Cupbop Franchise, LLC (638043-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Concept:** Korean BBQ rice cups, salads, chicken wings, potstickers — fast-casual restaurant model, strip malls and free-standing locations, 1,200-2,000 sq ft.
2. **Franchisor entity:** Cupbop Franchise, LLC (Utah LLC, formed Nov 2014, franchising since March 2017). Wholly owned subsidiary of Cupbop Co. since Jan 2021.
3. **Ownership structure:** Two additional parents — Gold Light Holdings, LLC (UT) and MAK Holdings LLC (NV). Founder Junghun Song is CEO of both Cupbop Co. and the franchisor.
4. **System size (end of 2024):** 59 total outlets — 30 franchised, 29 company-owned. Net +5 in 2024 (vs. +9 in 2023, +16 in 2022). Growth rate decelerating.
5. **Investment range:** $296,400–$664,400 total; initial franchise fee $40,000 ($30,000 for additional units).
6. **Fee burden:** 6% royalty + 2% ad fund (can rise to 4.5%) + up to 1% local advertising + up to 2% cooperative = theoretical max 13.5% of Gross Sales (current effective ~9%).
7. **Item 19 disclosed:** Revenue-only FPR. System-wide average Gross Revenue $728,821 (median $710,250) across 50 qualifying restaurants. Franchise-only average $658,208 (median $669,260) across 24 units.
8. **Supplier control:** Proprietary sauces, marinades, proteins, and branded paper products MUST be purchased from Cupbop Co. (parent). Special risk #3 flagged.
9. **Territory:** Protected territory granted around each location (no minimum size guaranteed). Non-Traditional Venues (stadiums, arenas, airports, etc.) carved out — franchisor can operate inside your territory.
10. **Minimum sales requirement:** Beginning Year 2, must maintain 50% of trailing 12-month national average. Failure over 2-year period = default.
11. **Noncompete:** 2 years post-term, 20-mile radius from any Cupbop Restaurant.
12. **Dispute resolution:** Mediation then arbitration in Salt Lake County, Utah. Utah law governs. State addenda override for 13+ states.
13. **Litigation:** None disclosed (Item 3). Bankruptcy: None (Item 4).
14. **Financial health of franchisor:** Revenue $2,027,743 (2024), net income $1,025,658. Distributions of $1,896,607 significantly exceeded net income, leaving member's equity at only $21,352.
15. **Confidentiality clause:** Yes — franchisees in past 3 years required to sign agreements restricting ability to speak about their experience.

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8-11)

### Item 11 — Assistance, Advertising, Computer Systems, Training

**Pre-opening assistance:** Site selection criteria (accept/reject within 30 days), lease approval, specifications/layouts, food products/suppliers, Brand Standards Manual access, grand opening guidelines, training.

**Post-opening assistance:** On-site representative (24 hrs for 1st restaurant, 16 hrs for 2nd, 8 hrs for 3rd+), advice/recommendations, additional training on request, ongoing manual updates.

**Site selection:** 6-9 months from signing to opening. Must find site within 120 days. Must open within 16 months or termination. Franchisor does NOT own/lease premises to you.

**Advertising:**
- Local advertising: minimum 0.5% of monthly Gross Sales (may increase to 1%)
- Advertising fund: up to 4.5%, currently 2% — auto-debit
- Company-owned restaurants contribute at same rate
- 2024 ad fund spending: 31% production, 29% placement, 40% administrative
- Annual statement available on written request; NO independent audit required
- Cooperative advertising: up to 2%, not currently assessed
- Cap: total cumulative advertising burden cannot exceed 6.5% of Gross Sales
- No franchisee advertising councils currently (may form in future)

**Computer systems:**
- Required: POS system, computer, printers, high-speed internet
- POS cost: $2,500-$5,000
- Annual POS maintenance: $7,200-$12,000
- Accounting software: QuickBooks Online ($200/year current)
- Franchisor has unlimited access to POS data
- Must share all security access codes

**Training program:**
- 14-day initial training: 20.5 hrs classroom + 95 hrs on-the-job = 115.5 total hours
- Location: Draper, Utah office and/or Cupbop store locations
- Trainers: Gina Silveira (3 months Cupbop experience, 13 years industry), JongBin Lim (4 years Cupbop, 21 years industry)
- 2 people trained free; additional at $1,500/person
- Replacement GM must complete training within 90 days
- Ongoing training: at franchisor's discretion, times/locations they designate

**Operations Manual:** 108 pages. Covers: introduction/brand philosophy, franchise relationship, operational brand standards, food truck standards, menu/recipes, etc.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Protected territory:** Yes, around authorized location. Size varies based on population density; NO minimum guaranteed.
- **Encroachment risk (HIGH):**
  - Franchisor expressly reserves right to operate Non-Traditional Venues INSIDE your protected territory (stadiums, arenas, airports, hospitals, universities, military bases, corporate campuses, convenience stores, etc.)
  - Cupbop Co. already operates concessions at Vivint Smart Home Arena, Rice-Eccles Stadium, LaVell Edwards Stadium
  - Online delivery orders placed by customers within your territory may be fulfilled by franchisor, affiliates, or another franchisee
  - Franchisor reserves internet, catalog, wholesale, supermarket, telemarketing channels without compensation
- **Minimum sales performance:** 50% of trailing 12-month national average, starting Year 2. Failure over 2-year period = default and potential termination.
- **Catering:** Allowed within protected territory and immediately adjacent non-claimed areas (with prior written approval)
- **No channel rights beyond premises:** May only offer products at authorized location (no other channels without approval)
- **Relocation:** Requires approval + $10,000 fee + meet current specs + sign release + de-identify old premises

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term:** 10 years from opening date
- **Renewal:** One 10-year successor term
- **Renewal requirements:** Notice 180-220 days before expiration; maintain premises; remodel to current standards; pay $10,000 successor fee; sign NEW agreement that MAY BE MATERIALLY DIFFERENT
- **"Materially different" risk:** YES — successor agreement can have materially different terms

### Termination
- **By franchisee:** Only if franchisor breaches and fails to cure, or as allowed by state law
- **By franchisor without cause:** Not permitted
- **By franchisor with cause — curable defaults:** 3 days (health/safety violations), 10 days (monetary defaults, insurance), 30 days (monetary to third parties, operational defaults), 90 days (relocate if lose premises)
- **Noncurable defaults:** Failure to secure site within 120 days; failure to open within 16 months; opening without approval; training failure; abandonment; unapproved transfers; material misrepresentations; felony conviction; dishonest/unethical conduct; unauthorized use of confidential info; tax failures; understating Gross Sales; recruiting violations; repeated defaults; bankruptcy; anti-terrorism violation; cross-default

### Transfer
- Requires prior written consent (not unreasonably withheld)
- Transfer fee: $10,000
- Conditions: full compliance, terms in writing, transferee assumes obligations, signs then-current agreement, completes training, acknowledges FDD receipt, release required
- Franchisor has right of first refusal to match any offer
- Death/disability: 180 days to appoint qualified representative or transfer

### Noncompete
- **During term:** Cannot own interest in or perform services for competing business
- **Post-term:** 2 years at premises OR within 20 miles of ANY Cupbop Restaurant (existing, operating, or under construction at termination/expiration)

### Dispute Resolution
- Governing law: Utah
- Mediation first: Salt Lake County, Utah
- Arbitration: binding, Salt Lake County, Utah (Area Development Agreement)
- Litigation venue: Salt Lake County, Utah
- Subject to state law overrides (13+ states)

### Post-Termination Obligations
- Pay all outstanding amounts + possible liquidated damages
- De-identify premises
- Assign telephone numbers
- Return confidential information
