# Final Diligence Report — Anytime Fitness FDD (638153-2025)
## Anytime Fitness Franchisor LLC — Franchise Disclosure Document
### Filing ID: 638153 | Issuance: March 31, 2025, as amended April 24, 2025

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand and concept**: Anytime Fitness is a 24-hour convenience fitness center franchise offering personal and group training, nutrition coaching, recovery services, and digital health tools. The brand has been franchising since 2002 (originally through predecessor AFLLC) and is one of the largest fitness franchise systems in the United States.

2. **Entity structure**: The franchisor is Anytime Fitness Franchisor LLC, a Delaware LLC formed in October 2021 as part of a whole-business securitization transaction. The entity sits within a multi-layered corporate structure under Purpose Brands Holdings LLC (jointly owned by Anytime Worldwide LLC and Ultimate Fitness Holdings LLC). The predecessor AFLLC now serves as manager providing day-to-day services under a management agreement. The securitization created bankruptcy-remote special-purpose entities (SEB SPV Guarantor → SEB Funding → SEB Systems → franchising entities).

3. **Ownership history**: Founded by Charles Runyon and Dave Mortensen (both still on the Board of Governors). November 2021 securitization transferred all U.S. franchise agreements to new entity. April 2, 2024 — OTF Franchisor (Orangetheory, 1,283 studios) became an indirect subsidiary of Purpose Brands, significantly expanding the corporate family. CEO Thomas Leverton joined November 2024 from Pritzker Private Capital; interim CFO Benjamin Selden joined April 2025.

4. **System size**: 2,290 franchised + 11 company-owned = 2,301 total outlets at end of 2024. System has been slowly shrinking: net -17 (2022), -20 (2023), -9 (2024). The deceleration in shrinkage is notable — openings have held steady around 46-49/year while terminations have declined from 45 to 33. There are 179 signed-but-not-opened agreements and 48 projected new openings.

5. **Investment range**: $458,826–$907,607 for a 4,000–6,300 sq ft center built from vanilla shell.

6. **Fee burden**: Fixed Monthly Fee of $820/month (royalty), $600/month ad fund, $799/month technology fee (with 10% annual escalator), plus $600–$1,000/month local marketing spend. The Monthly Fee can be replaced with a percentage royalty of up to 8% of Gross Revenue on just 30 days' notice — a critical kill switch that could dramatically increase ongoing costs.

7. **Item 19 performance**: Average total revenue $442,686 across 1,656 Coaching Suite centers. Median $394,973. Bottom quartile average just $231,962 — challenging economics given the fixed fee burden (~$32K+/year in franchise fees alone). Company-owned EBITDA averages $144,002 (27.21% margin) but bottom-third EBITDA is only $65,101 — below a reasonable owner's draw.

8. **Financial health**: AFLLC consolidated shows $111M assets vs $1.06B liabilities (member's deficit of $949M). This is structural — driven by $955.6M in securitization debt — not operational distress. The business generates $127M operating cash flow and $113M net income. However, the guarantor entity (SFG) holds only $5M in cash, which is explicitly flagged as a special risk.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Declining unit count offset by rising revenue per unit. The 2024 acquisition of Orangetheory under the same corporate umbrella introduces both scale advantages and potential brand conflict within territories.

10. **Territory**: Protected within up to 3-mile radius (max 30,000 population), but NOT exclusive. Orangetheory, Basecamp, and Bar Method can all operate within your territory.

11. **Renewal risk**: 6-year initial term, single 5-year renewal. Renewal requires signing new agreement with potentially materially different terms, $7,500 fee, general release, and facility upgrade.

12. **Disputes**: Arbitration in Minnesota; mediation at mediator-selected location. Governing law Minnesota. Multiple state addenda override these provisions.

13. **Special risks**: Six flagged risks including spousal liability, mandatory minimums, supplier control, out-of-state dispute resolution, guarantor financial condition, and unopened franchises.

14. **Gag clause**: Present. Current and former franchisees have signed provisions restricting their ability to speak openly.

15. **Transfers**: 207 transfers in 2024 (high liquidity for a mature system).

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

The franchisor is a special-purpose entity within a securitization structure. This means franchise royalties and fees flow through the securitization waterfall before reaching the parent company. The management agreement with AFLLC means that the entity you contract with (Anytime Fitness Franchisor LLC) does not directly employ the people providing support — they are AFLLC employees managed under the management agreement. This creates a structural separation between your contractual counterparty and the operational team.

The Purpose Brands umbrella now includes five fitness-related brands: Anytime Fitness (2,290 franchised), Orangetheory (1,283), The Bar Method (73), Basecamp (19+4), and Waxing the City (151). While this provides scale, it also means your franchising entity shares corporate attention with ~1,600+ other franchised locations.

### Fee Stack

**Initial fees**: The standard $42,500 franchise fee reflects a mature system with established pricing tiers. Veteran discount of 10%, existing franchisee discount of ~12%, and loyalty program (Club Purple/Platinum) discounts of up to 47% create meaningful differentiation. The nonrefundable nature of all fees, combined with the 12-month opening deadline, creates urgency.

**Ongoing fee burden for a typical center (annual)**:
- Monthly Fee (royalty): $9,840 ($820 × 12)
- General Advertising Fund: $7,200 ($600 × 12)
- Base Technology Fee: $9,588 ($799 × 12) — increasing ~10% annually
- Local Marketing Spend: $7,200–$12,000 (depending on market tier)
- Total recurring mandatory: ~$33,828–$38,628/year

At the system average revenue of $442,686, total recurring mandatory fees represent approximately 7.6–8.7% of gross revenue. This is relatively low for a franchise system — but the 8% royalty switch option would more than double the royalty component, bringing total fees to roughly 16-18% of revenue.

The 10% annual escalator on the technology fee is particularly significant. At $799/month in Year 1, this becomes $879 in Year 2, $967 in Year 3, and approximately $1,287 by Year 6 (end of initial term). Over the full 6-year term, cumulative technology fees alone would total approximately $72,000.

**Event-driven fees**: The $9,999 transfer fee (post-opening) and $7,500 renewal fee are moderate. The $25,000 pre-opening transfer fee is substantial. The $10,000 per-center liquidated damages for ADA failure, combined with nonrefundable development fees, makes multi-unit commitment high-stakes.

### Initial Investment

The $458K–$908K range spans a wide margin, driven primarily by leasehold improvements ($94K–$430K) and fitness equipment ($138K–$156K). The technology package ($30K–$36K) is a captive affiliate purchase with no alternatives. The 3-month working capital cushion ($46K–$48K) assumes a lean startup — franchisees should budget more conservatively given the system's own Item 19 data showing some centers generating very low revenue.

Key cost anchors from system data: Average base rent $21.31/sq ft (so a 5,000 sq ft center = ~$107K/year base rent), average CAM $5.47/sq ft (~$27K/year). Tenant improvement allowances averaging $21/sq ft offset some build-out costs.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting

### Supplier Restrictions

Anytime Fitness operates with heavy supplier control. Over 90% of pre-opening purchases and ~70% of ongoing expenses must meet franchisor specifications. The mandatory vendor list is extensive:

- **Technology**: ProVision (affiliate) — no alternative. $30K–$37K upfront + $799/month ongoing
- **Billing/payments**: Single mandatory vendor with rebates to franchisor
- **Fitness incentive programs**: Healthy Contributions (affiliate) — mandatory
- **Grand opening**: Preferred vendor required
- **Uniforms, surety bond, architecture**: Designated vendors

The affiliate revenue numbers are revealing: ProVision alone generated $29M from franchisees in 2024; Healthy Contributions generated $8M; the franchisor itself generated $32.1M from required goods/services (24% of its $133.8M revenue). Combined, affiliate and franchisor revenue from captive supply channels approaches $69M — a significant portion of the system's economics flowing back to the corporate family beyond standard royalties.

Vendor rebates of up to 15% on franchisee purchases represent additional franchisor revenue that is not characterized as a fee.

### Training Program

The 63-hour initial training program (39 classroom + 24 OTJ) covers a reasonable range of topics with the Coaching Suite comprising a substantial portion (5+6 hours). Training is provided at no charge; franchisee bears travel costs to Woodbury, MN.

The ongoing training requirements are notable: 1,200 continuing engagement credits annually (with $1/credit penalty for shortfall), biennial conference attendance mandatory for a Principal Owner ($499–$749 fee), and various optional programs. The $4,000 on-site relaunch training fee for franchisees buying existing clubs is an additional onboarding cost.

### Technology Stack

The technology ecosystem is entirely proprietary and captive:
- Club Operating Software (ProVision)
- Club Management Software (designated vendor)
- Access control / door readers
- Security / surveillance (CCTV)
- Fitness scanning / monitoring (Evolt)
- Sound system
- Email hosting
- Coaching Suite / SmartCoaching platform
- AF Mobile Application

The franchisor has "independent access to the information in your computer and iPad" with "no limits on our right to access this information." This gives the franchisor complete visibility into all member data, financial transactions, and operational metrics.

### Marketing Fund

The $600/month advertising fund contribution feeds a national/regional fund where 71% goes to paid media, 13% to administration, and 10% to digital platform tools. The fund is not audited. There is no advertising council of franchisees. The franchisor has no obligation to spend in any particular territory. An annual accounting is available on request. Four local cooperatives exist (Denver, Madison, Phoenix, Des Moines) but participation is voluntary.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

The territory protection in this franchise is narrow. While the franchisor cannot place another Anytime Fitness center within your protected territory (up to 3-mile radius, max 30,000 population), it retains extensive rights:

1. **Affiliate brands**: Orangetheory, Basecamp Fitness, The Bar Method, and Waxing the City can all operate within your territory. With the April 2024 acquisition of OTF (1,283 studios), this is not theoretical — there are likely already markets where Orangetheory and Anytime Fitness centers compete for the same customers.

2. **Digital channels**: The franchisor and affiliates can sell products and services (apparel, nutrition counseling, supplements, virtual fitness) to your members through any channel including internet, catalog, and direct marketing — within your territory, without compensation. Meanwhile, you are prohibited from offering any products or services via internet, livestreaming, or app-based delivery without express approval.

3. **Renewal territory risk**: Upon renewal, the franchisor may "recalculate the population in your market and reserve the right to modify your protected territory in accordance with our then-current guidelines." This means your territory could shrink at renewal.

4. **TBD territories**: In some cases the franchisor assigns a "TBD" territory, which essentially gives no initial protection — the franchisee searches for a site in any unprotected area, and the franchisor may offer the site to an existing nearby franchisee first.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The 6-year initial term is shorter than many franchise systems (which commonly offer 10–20 year terms). With a single 5-year renewal, the maximum franchise horizon is 11 years. Given the $459K–$908K investment, this requires aggressive payback expectations.

Renewal requires signing a new franchise agreement "which may contain materially different terms and conditions than your original Franchise Agreement." This is a significant risk — the royalty structure, territory, fees, operational requirements, and any other terms could change at renewal. The $7,500 renewal fee and mandatory general release (subject to state law) add to the renewal burden.

### Transfer

The transfer market appears active (207 transfers in 2024), suggesting reasonable liquidity. The $9,999 transfer fee is moderate. However, the transferee must sign a new agreement on then-current terms, which may differ from the selling franchisee's terms — this could affect valuation. The franchisor's right of first refusal allows it to match any purchase offer.

The post-termination purchase option (book value of assets) gives the franchisor the ability to acquire your business at depreciated asset value upon termination or non-renewal.

### Termination and Default

The cross-default provision is aggressive: default under any franchise agreement, area development agreement, or agreement with any affiliate can trigger termination of all agreements. This means a dispute with ProVision over a technology issue could theoretically cascade into franchise termination.

The non-curable default triggers include 3 material breaches within 12 months (pattern default) and withholding franchisor access to accounting/financial systems — both of which could be weaponized in a deteriorating relationship.

### Noncompete

The during-term noncompete is among the broadest possible: "no involvement in any fitness center, exercise facility, health club, gym or business which offers exercise classes, personal training, fitness equipment, group training or nutrition or recovery services (including as creditor or landlord), wherever located." The "wherever located" scope and the inclusion of "creditor or landlord" roles make this extremely restrictive.

Post-term, the 2-year duration within the territory + 10-mile radius of any Anytime Fitness center (5 miles in metro areas >50,000) is substantial given the system's ~2,300 U.S. locations. For a franchisee in a major metropolitan area, this could effectively bar them from the fitness industry for 2 years.

### Dispute Resolution

Mediation → Arbitration (Minneapolis) → Litigation (Minnesota courts). The out-of-state venue is one of the six flagged special risks. State addenda modify these provisions for California, Illinois, Maryland, Minnesota, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Virginia, Washington, and Wisconsin.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Section I: Coaching Suite Centers (1,656 of 2,289 system centers)

This is the most relevant cohort for a new franchisee. Key observations:

- **Average total revenue of $442,686** masks wide dispersion: Q4 average is $742K while Q1 average is $232K — a 3.2x spread.
- **Only 39% of centers achieve or exceed the system average**, meaning the majority underperform the headline number.
- **Personal training revenue** ($74K average, $50K median) represents approximately 13% of total revenue — a meaningful but not dominant component. The SmartCoaching subset shows much higher PT revenue ($120K avg), suggesting that franchisees who fully implement the coaching tools capture meaningfully more PT revenue.
- **Pay-per-visit revenue** ($30K average) represents employer/insurer-funded visits — a relatively stable but capped revenue stream.
- **Monthly membership count** averages 677 (median 604). The bottom quartile averages 422 members — at typical membership pricing, this suggests centers with thin membership economics.

Revenue excludes one-time enrollment fees and vending income, so actual gross revenue is somewhat higher.

### Section II: SmartCoaching Centers (297 centers)

Centers using all five SmartCoaching tools show a 20% revenue premium over the broader Coaching Suite population ($531,750 vs $442,686). This premium is driven primarily by personal training revenue ($120K vs $74K). The implication is that full adoption of the technology ecosystem correlates with significantly higher revenue — though causation cannot be assumed (higher-performing operators may self-select into full tool adoption).

### Section III: Company-Owned P&L (11 centers)

This is the only section disclosing expenses, providing a unit economics framework:

- **Revenue**: $529,223 average (higher than franchised average, reflecting SmartCoaching usage and Illinois/Minnesota locations)
- **Rent/CAM**: $113,199 (21.4% of revenue) — the single largest expense
- **PT Expenses**: $105,211 (19.9%) — high, reflecting coach/trainer compensation for the PT revenue stream
- **Total operating expenses**: $340,048 (64.3%)
- **NOI before manager salary**: $189,175 (35.7% margin)
- **After manager salary ($45K)**: EBITDA $144,002 (27.2%)

The bottom-third EBITDA of $65,101 is concerning. After factoring in debt service on a typical SBA loan for the initial investment, an owner-operator at this level would have minimal take-home income. The median EBITDA of $92,240 is more reasonable but still modest relative to the $459K–$908K investment.

**Cohort comparability**: The company-owned centers pay the same $820/month ($9,840/year) royalty as franchisees. No cohort comparability issue.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### Trajectory

The system has been in gentle decline for three years: -17 (2022), -20 (2023), -9 (2024). The deceleration is positive — terminations declined from 45 to 33 while openings held at 46-49. If the 48 projected openings and declining attrition trend continue, the system may return to growth in 2025.

### Transfers

The transfer volume of 204–243 per year represents roughly 9% of the system annually. This is healthy transfer market activity and suggests reasonable resale demand. Wisconsin leads with 28 transfers in 2024, followed by Minnesota (12), California (13), Florida (12), and Texas (11).

### Terminations and Non-Renewals

Terminations declined from 45 (2022) to 33 (2024). Non-renewals declined from 25 (2023) to 21 (2024). Zero reacquisitions or ceased-operations in all three years — the franchisor does not appear to be actively reclaiming units.

### Pipeline

179 franchise agreements signed but not opened, with 48 projected openings. This is a substantial pipeline relative to the 46 units opened in 2024. Key pipeline states: California (21 signed), Florida (23), Texas (19), North Carolina (12), Michigan (12). The "unopened franchises" special risk flag references this pipeline.

### Gag Clause

The FDD explicitly states: "During the last 3 years current and former franchisees have signed provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with us or our predecessor." Prospective franchisees should be aware that conversations with former franchisees may be limited.

### Franchisee Association

AFFA (Independent Association of Anytime Fitness Franchisees) is listed with contact information. This independent association may provide perspective beyond what the franchisor's selected Franchise Advisory Council offers.

## H. Item 21 — Financial Statements

### SEB Franchising Guarantor LLC (Guarantor)

This is the entity that guarantees the franchisor's obligations under franchise agreements. It is a special-purpose shell company holding exactly $5,000,000 in cash — its sole asset. It has no operating revenue, no employees, no operations. Its entire purpose is to maintain a $5M guarantee pool and meet state net worth requirements.

The auditor (PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP) issued an unqualified opinion. There is no going-concern language. However, the FDD's Special Risk #5 explicitly states: "The Franchisor's guarantor's financial condition as reflected in its financial statements calls into question the Franchisor's financial ability to provide services and support to you."

For a system with 2,300+ outlets and ~$133M in franchisee-facing revenue, a $5M guarantee provides minimal protection. If the franchisor entity were to become unable to perform its obligations, the guarantor's $5M would be distributed among thousands of franchisees — yielding trivial per-franchisee recovery.

### Anytime Fitness LLC and Subsidiaries (Consolidated)

This entity operates the actual business, manages all franchisee relationships, and holds the securitization debt.

**Balance Sheet (December 31, 2024)**:
- Total assets: $111.1M
- Cash and equivalents: $9.6M + restricted cash $6.6M
- Accounts receivable: $18.9M
- Software development costs: $27.4M (net) — largest non-current asset
- Goodwill: $127K (minimal)
- Total liabilities: $1,060.1M
- Long-term debt: $955.6M (securitization notes — Series 2021-1 and 2024-1)
- Deferred revenue: $77.2M total ($17.4M current + $59.8M long-term)
- Member's deficit: ($949.0M)

The deep negative equity is a structural artifact of the securitization. The business was restructured to extract value through debt financing, with the franchise royalty streams serving as the primary collateral. This is not unusual for franchise system securitizations (similar structures exist at Domino's, Dunkin', etc.) but it does mean the entity has minimal equity cushion.

**Income Statement (2024)**:
- Total revenue: $317.0M (up from $184.1M in 2023 — largely due to OTF acquisition in April 2024)
- Revenue breakdown: Franchise royalties $145.2M (46%), Vendor rebates $56.0M (18%), Sales $78.2M (25%), Franchise fees $20.1M (6%), Ad fund $16.4M (5%)
- Gross profit: $273.7M (86.3% margin)
- Income from operations: $167.5M
- Interest expense: ($53.3M) — the cost of the securitization debt
- Net income: $113.1M

**Cash Flow (2024)**:
- Operating cash flow: $126.8M
- Capital expenditures: ($0.6M)
- Software development: ($6.8M)
- New debt issuance: $524.0M (Series 2024-1 Notes)
- Debt repayment: ($29.0M)
- Distributions to member: ($599.1M) — note this massive distribution in connection with the 2024 refinancing/OTF acquisition
- Cash paid for interest: $52.5M

**Key observations**:
1. The business generates strong operating cash flow ($127M) relative to its fixed cost obligations
2. The $599M distribution in 2024 dwarfs the $113M net income — this was funded by the $524M new debt issuance (Series 2024-1)
3. Deferred revenue of $77.2M represents pre-collected franchise fees not yet recognized — a significant future revenue cushion
4. The OTF acquisition dramatically changed the consolidated entity's scale (revenue nearly doubled)

### Key Material Overrides

**California**: General release may not be required; noncompete may not be enforceable; arbitration venue may not be enforceable; punitive damages/jury trial waivers may not be enforceable.

**Maryland**: Surety bond required by state; general release does not apply to Maryland Franchise Law claims; may sue in Maryland for Maryland law claims; 3-year statute of limitations.

**Washington**: Surety bond required; Washington Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails; arbitration/mediation must be in Washington or mutually agreed location; general release void except in negotiated post-agreement settlements with independent counsel; transfer fees limited to actual costs; statute of limitations restrictions may not be enforceable.

**Minnesota**: Minnesota Franchise Act provisions may supersede agreement.

**Illinois**: Illinois Franchise Disclosure Act provisions may supersede agreement.

**Michigan**: Specific protections against unfair provisions (termination only for good cause, ROFR limitations, fair market value compensation on non-renewal).

**North Dakota**: Various North Dakota Century Code provisions supersede agreement terms.
