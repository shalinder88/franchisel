# Reader Report — Hilton Hotels & Resorts FDD (638058-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 2007, wholly owned subsidiary of Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., which is ultimately owned by Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT).
2. **Brand position:** Upper upscale full-service hotels. Hilton brand has operated continuously since 1946 (originally as Hilton Hotels Corporation).
3. **System size:** 187 franchised hotels in the US at end of 2024 (down from 189 at start of 2024). Zero company-owned hotels. 11 franchise agreements signed but hotels not yet opened. 3 projected new openings in next fiscal year.
4. **Total investment:** $49,685,442 to $204,323,235 for a typical 300-room new-development hotel, excluding real property. This is among the highest investment levels in all franchise systems.
5. **Fee burden:** 5% royalty on GRR + 4% program fee + 3% F&B fee + 0.45–0.75% OnQ FBPP + 4.3% Hilton Honors = approximately 16.75–17.05% of gross rooms revenue before distribution/sales fees. Additional fees for quality assurance failures, connected room, Delphi, and numerous sales programs.
6. **Item 19:** FPR provided. Average room rate $209.86 (2024), median $188.04. Average occupancy 69.5%. Only 24.8% of franchisee-managed hotels met or exceeded average room rate. RevPAR Index average 104.7 (above fair share). No cost build-up or EBITDA disclosed.
7. **Term & renewal:** 23 years (new construction), 10–20 years (conversion). NO renewal right. Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms.
8. **Territory:** Non-exclusive by default. Restricted Area Provision may be negotiated but is not standard; even if granted, has numerous carve-outs and a Restrictive Period shorter than the franchise term.
9. **Financial health:** Franchisor generated $1.502B revenue in 2024 (up from $1.377B in 2023). Net income $1.488B. Distributed $1.5B to parent. Total assets $1.27B. Strong financial position but entity is a pass-through for Hilton parent.
10. **Litigation:** 5 pending class actions related to alleged antitrust/price-fixing through IDeaS/STR/Amadeus software. 2 settled state AG actions on mandatory guest fee disclosure (Texas $2.1M, Nebraska $300K). No franchisee collection suits in 2024.
11. **Gag clause:** During last 3 fiscal years, some current and former franchisees have signed provisions restricting their ability to speak about their experience.
12. **Key risk:** No renewal right combined with massive capital investment ($50M–$204M) creates significant end-of-term risk. Liquidated damages for post-opening termination are 60× average monthly royalty fees.

## Pass E: Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term & Renewal
- **New Construction:** 23 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10–20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership:** remaining term or as approved
- **NO renewal right.** Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion; may require signing new agreement with materially different terms

### Termination
- **By franchisee:** Not authorized. Unilateral termination = material breach → liquidated damages or actual damages
- **By franchisor without cause:** On condemnation or casualty (no liquidated damages if franchisee doesn't compete at site for 3 years)
- **By franchisor with cause — curable defaults:**
  - Payment default: 10+ days cure
  - Construction/opening deadline failure: 10+ days cure
  - Insurance failure: 10+ days cure
  - Other FA/Manual/Standard failures: 30+ days cure
  - Interim remedies available during cure: suspension from Reservation Service, directory removal, system disablement, revocation of financial accommodations
- **Non-curable defaults (immediate termination):**
  - Repeated material breach within 24 months
  - 3 notices of default in 12 months
  - Insolvency/bankruptcy
  - Loss of possession of hotel
  - Failure to operate for 5+ consecutive days
  - Contesting franchisor's ownership of System/Marks
  - Conduct adversely affecting reputation
  - False books/records/fraud
  - Unauthorized transfer
  - Sanctioned Person
  - Adverse gaming disclosure
  - Guarantor breach
  - Public health/safety threat
  - Becoming a Competitor

### Transfer
- **Permitted Transfers** (no change of control): public equity, <50% private equity, institutional fund transfers — some without notice/consent, others with 60-day notice + $5,500 fee
- **Change of Ownership Transfer:** 60-day notice, no default, pay all amounts due, transferee must meet current standards, sign current form FA, pay $150,000 COO application fee, agree to PIP upgrades
- **Right of first offer** (not first refusal): 30-day window on unsolicited bona fide offers
- Mortgage/pledge allowed without consent if sole borrower and hotel-only collateral; otherwise consent required

### Non-Competition
- **During term:** May not own/be licensor/franchisor of competing brand. May own minority interest, be franchisee of competitor, or manage competitor property.
- **Post-term:** NONE. No post-term noncompete.

### Dispute Resolution
- **No arbitration** (except in Puerto Rico)
- **Forum:** US District Court, Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria); if no federal jurisdiction, Fairfax County VA or New York, NY. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Governing law:** New York (without conflicts principles)
- **No jury trial waiver stated in Item 17** (but state addenda reference it)

### Unresolveds
1. **No renewal right:** Franchise is expressly non-renewable. Re-licensing at sole discretion with potentially materially different terms. Given $50M–$204M investment, this is a material structural risk.
2. **Territory:** No standard protected territory. Even negotiated Restricted Areas have significant carve-outs and time limits shorter than the term.

7. **Item 19 limitation:** No cost data, EBITDA, or profitability metrics provided. Only top-line performance (room rate, occupancy, indices) disclosed.
