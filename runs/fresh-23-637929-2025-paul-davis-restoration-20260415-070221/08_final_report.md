## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and trajectory**: Paul Davis Restoration, Inc. (PDRI) operates 266 franchised outlets across the United States as of December 31, 2024, with zero company-owned units. The system has grown steadily — from 211 units in early 2022 to 266 at year-end 2024, adding 55 net franchises in three years. An additional 26 new openings are projected for the next fiscal year, with 6 agreements already signed but not yet open.

2. **Entity structure and ownership**: PDRI is a Florida corporation (incorporated 1967). The parent chain runs through FSB Holdings, Inc. (DE) → FS Brands, Inc. (DE) → FirstService CAM Holdings, Inc. (DE) → FirstService Corporation (Ontario, Canada — publicly traded). Notable affiliate presence: PDO Holdings, Inc. holds ≥80% of 22 PDR franchises (20 US, 2 Canada); PDR Holdings, LLC holds ≤50.1% of 31 US franchises. All 53 operate under franchise agreements and are counted as "franchised."

3. **Ownership history**: PDRI has been franchising since January 1970. The name changed from "Paul W. Davis Systems, Inc." to "Paul Davis Restoration, Inc." on February 2, 2000. No predecessors. The FirstService Corporation ownership chain provides financial backing and cross-brand synergies (sister brands include California Closets, CertaPro Painters, Floorcoverings International, Pillar To Post).

4. **Investment range**: Total initial investment of $298,800 to $804,900, including a franchise fee of $65,000 to $208,000 (calculated at $0.26 per person of territory population, max 800,000). PDRI recommends maintaining minimum working capital of $500,000 at all times. The high end contemplates higher-cost-of-living states and urban markets.

5. **Fee burden**: The ongoing fee structure is among the more complex in franchising. The 4% royalty is the baseline, supplemented by a minimum royalty escalating to $6.00/person by year 9, a Strategic Marketing Plan fee of $500/month plus 0.75% of invoiced sales, guarantee fund contributions, indemnity fund fees, and a substantial technology stack (RMS $525/month, Xactimate up to $149/license/month, MS 365, QuickBooks, call center fees, etc.). A franchisee can easily face $1,500–$2,500+ per month in non-royalty recurring fees.

6. **Item 19 highlights**: For franchises operating 2+ years, median gross sales were $3,976,094 and average was $6,006,779 on total system reported sales of $1,099,240,532 across 183 reporting units. Only 30% of franchisees achieved or exceeded the average — the system has significant positive skew, with a few high performers (highest: $55.5M) pulling the average well above the median. No cost or profitability data is disclosed.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: The lowest-reporting territory generated only $83,873 in 2024, and several mature franchises reported under $1M. For franchises under 2 years, median was $1,006,593 with a low of $6,899. New franchisees face a real risk of extended ramp-up periods.

8. **Financial health (parent)**: FS Brands, Inc. reported $850.4M revenue and $82.7M operating income in 2024, up from $782.2M/$67.8M in 2023. No external debt. Cash of $82.5M. PwC clean opinion. $30M dividend paid in 2024. However, these are consolidated parent financials — PDRI's standalone performance is not separately disclosed.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Strong and accelerating. Net +13 (2022), +21 (2023), +21 (2024). 28 new openings in 2024 against 6 terminations and 1 non-renewal. Projected 26 new openings for the next year.

10. **Territory**: Non-exclusive. This is a significant consideration — PDRI explicitly states franchisees "will not receive an exclusive territory" and reserves broad rights for national accounts, alternative distribution, affiliate competition, and M&A.

11. **Renewal**: 5-year term renewable for 5 years, but renewal requires signing the then-current FA which "may have materially different terms and conditions." Combined with minimum sales thresholds for renewal, this creates meaningful renewal risk.

12. **Disputes and litigation**: Clean litigation record (only 1 concluded case from 2013, settled with $20K contribution). Dispute resolution is binding arbitration under PDRI's internal system — unusual in that arbitrators are franchisees, not a neutral third-party panel.

13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state arbitration and mandatory personal guaranty for all owners.

PDRI has national accounts with Sherwin Williams, Lowe's, Aramsco, Jon-Don, and Home Depot — usage is voluntary. In 2024, these vendors paid rebates directly to franchisees: Lowe's ($427K), Aramsco ($169K), Jon-Don ($107K), Sherwin Williams ($75K), Home Depot ($67K). PDRI also receives vendor rebates of 0.5%–5% on franchisee purchases.

PDRI derived $5,827,294 (9.33%) of its $62,442,294 total revenue from required franchisee purchases/leases in 2024.

Required purchases represent 75%–85% of establishment costs and 30%–40% of ongoing operations.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition and Carve-Outs

Territories are defined by zip code boundaries with typical populations of 500,000–800,000. PDRI uses ESRI/ARC GIS for population determination. Territory boundaries may change with zip code revisions (outside PDRI's control), but PDRI otherwise cannot alter territory during the term.

**Critical: The territory is not exclusive.** PDRI explicitly states: "You will not receive an exclusive territory." This is a major risk factor.

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights

PDRI reserves the right to:
- Enter into national/regional account programs with insurance companies, TPAs, and property managers and assign work to other franchisees in your territory if you don't participate, refuse claims, or don't qualify
- Allow Paul Davis Emergency Services franchisees to operate in your territory
- Operate Paul Davis National, which may service the entire US
- Use alternative distribution channels including the Internet under different trademarks
- Grant similar franchises outside your territory using PDRI trademarks
- Merge with/acquire competitors and continue competitive operations, including identifying competitor outlets as PDRI affiliates

You have no options, rights of first refusal, or similar rights to acquire additional territories. Your office must be in your territory.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

- Initial term: 5 years (short for a franchise of this investment level)
- Renewal: One additional 5-year term
- **Renewal requires signing the then-current FA, which "may have materially different terms and conditions"** — this is a significant risk
- Renewal conditions: 90–180 day notice; equipment to current standards; compliance throughout term; minimum sales for each of prior 2 years (greater of national median or $6.00 × population); minimum royalty compliance; $5,000 fee; execute within 90 days of presentation

### Transfer Provisions

- PDRI must approve all transfers (same criteria as new franchisees)
- Transfer fee: $10,000 (majority) or $5,000 (minority)
- Resale fee: 2.5% of sale price, capped at $40,000
- Optional broker/lead gen: $30,000–$50,000
- PDRI has right of first refusal on same terms as any third-party offer
- Transferee must sign new FA at current terms

### Termination and Death/Disability

- Franchisee termination: No provision (Item 17(d) "not applicable" — except NY addendum allows termination "upon any grounds available by law")
- Franchisor with cause: 15 days to cure for payment defaults, supplier issues, Operations Manual non-compliance, licensing failures
- Non-curable defaults (immediate): Failure to begin operations, material GS misrepresentation, insolvency, unapproved transfer, felony, 3+ defaults in 12 months, abandonment (including 2+ months no reported sales), confidential info disclosure, non-renewal
- On termination: PDRI has right to purchase franchise at fair market value
- Death/disability: Heir can inherit if approval conditions met

### Non-Competition Restrictions

- During term: Cannot participate in competing business, disrupt PDRI/affiliate employment, or divert business
- Post-term: **2 years within own territory AND other PDRI territories**; cannot contact former suppliers/customers for competitive purposes
- Note: California addendum states post-term non-compete may not be enforceable under CA law

### Dispute Resolution

- **Binding arbitration** under PDRI's internal arbitration system
- Arbitrators are franchisees — this is an unusual structure (peer arbitration)
- Forum selected by the Arbitration Committee (not by parties)
- Florida law governs (subject to state overrides)
- Subject to state law, disputes may not be litigated in court

**State overrides on dispute resolution**: MN — litigation cannot be required outside MN; WA — arbitration must be in WA or mutually agreed; IL — venue outside IL void but arbitration outside IL permitted; ND — arbitration venue outside ND may not be enforceable.

### Litigation History

Minimal. One concluded case: Smith et al. v. Paul Davis (Cook County, IL 2013) — negligence claim regarding Histoplasma capsulatum contamination at a franchisee job site. Settled November 2015 by franchisee's insurer (RockHill), with PDRI contributing $20,000 toward full releases, no admission of wrongdoing. No pending litigation.
