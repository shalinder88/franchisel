# Reader Report — Papa Johns FDD (638092-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Papa John's Franchising, LLC** is a KY LLC (organized Nov 6, 2020), wholly owned subsidiary of Papa John's International, Inc. (PJI, Delaware corp). PJI transferred all US franchise agreements to PJF on Feb 22, 2021.
2. **System size at end of 2024:** 3,291 total US outlets (2,752 franchised + 539 company-owned). Net growth of +71 outlets in 2024. 98 outlets are majority-owned JVs counted as company-owned.
3. **Three franchise programs:** Standard (10-year term, 5% royalty), Non-Traditional (5-year term, 5% royalty), Small-Town (5-year term, 6% royalty). Development Agreement required for all operators.
4. **Total initial investment:** Standard/Small-Town: $261,165–$853,365; Non-Traditional: $111,000–$403,915. Initial franchise fee: $5,000–$25,000 (standard), $5,000 (NT/ST).
5. **Fee burden is heavy:** 5% royalty + 6% marketing fund + 2% digital fee = 13% of Net Sales for standard restaurants (before any cooperative or local advertising). Small-Town pays 6% royalty with marketing fund included.
6. **Item 19:** Average Net Royalty Sales for all 2,878 standard restaurants: $1,157,331 (median $1,101,782). Franchised-only average: $1,122,575 (median $1,068,262). Bottom 25% franchise average: $697,195.
7. **Financial health:** PJF total assets $37.4M, total equity $20.2M. Net income: $108M (2024), $133.5M (2023), $127.7M (2022). Revenue is 99% royalty fees. All cash swept to PJUSA; PJF holds zero cash.
8. **Growth trajectory:** Franchised outlets grew from 2,564 (2022) to 2,752 (2024), +188 net over 3 years. Company-owned declined from 600 (2022) to 539 (2024), -61 net. Texas saw massive refranchising (90 sold in 2022). Wisconsin refranchised (9 reacquired, 15 sold back in 2023-2024).
9. **Territory:** 1.5-mile radius (standard), 0.5-mile in dense urban areas. Non-exclusive — franchisor can operate other brands, other channels, NT restaurants within territory. Online order routing not tied to territory.
10. **Noncompete:** During term: no competing business anywhere. Post-term: 2 years within 10 miles of restaurant or any other Papa Johns.
11. **Renewal risk:** Must sign then-current form of FA — may contain "materially different" terms. Requires remodel, general release, completion of training, renewal fee ($4,000 standard, $1,000 NT/ST).
12. **Dispute resolution:** Arbitration in Louisville, KY. Litigation venue: Jefferson County, KY. Kentucky law applies. State addenda override for IL, MD, MN, NY, ND, RI, WA.
13. **Liquidated damages:** Average royalty for 12 months × 24 = approximately 2 years of royalty on termination for franchisee breach.
14. **Shaquille O'Neal endorsement:** $3.5M/year cash + expenses + $100K foundation donation. Also owns ~30% of a 9-restaurant JV in Atlanta.
15. **Gag clause: Yes.** Some current and former franchisees signed confidentiality provisions restricting ability to speak openly, in context of settlement agreements.

## Pass B: Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — Entity & History
Papa John's Franchising, LLC is a KY LLC organized Nov 6, 2020. Principal address: 2002 Papa John's Boulevard, Louisville, KY 40299. Direct, wholly owned subsidiary of PJI (Delaware corp). PJI was the franchisor from Nov 1991 to Feb 2021; PJF took over Feb 25, 2021. PJF does not operate restaurants directly — PJ USA, Inc. operates the company-owned restaurants. PJ Food Service, Inc. operates Quality Control Centers (dough production + food distribution). Capital Delivery, Ltd. provides financing. All affiliates share the same principal address.

Historical note: PJI acquired Perfect Pizza Holdings (UK) in 1999, sold it in 2006. Franchised one "Papiano's" restaurant in East Lansing, MI from 1998 to 2008.

Market: Quick service pizza delivery and carry-out. Competes with national chains, regional chains, local operators, grocery stores.

California AB 1228: Minimum wage for fast food employees increased to $20/hour (effective April 1, 2024). Fast-Food Council can set wages until Jan 1, 2029.

### Item 3 — Litigation
Two disclosed matters:
1. **Pach Food Services v. Papa John's USA (2015)** — Former franchisee alleged improper alteration of equipment lease and filing of liens. Settled April 2016 for $30,000.
2. **In Re Papa John's Antitrust Litigation (2019)** — Class action alleging no-poach provisions in franchise agreements violated Sherman Antitrust Act. Settlement reached April 2022; settlement amount recorded in Q1 2022 G&A expenses. Court approval pending as of FDD issuance. Papa Johns denies liability.
3. **I&R Foods v. PJI (2023)** — Former franchisee terminated for operating competing pizza business. Alleged breach, violation of CA Franchise Relations Act. Compelled to arbitration. Settled March 2025 for $10,000 plus mutual release.

- **$5.6M received from designated suppliers** due to franchisee transactions in 2024.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Development Area:** Mapped area appended to Development Agreement. Protected from other standard restaurants until the associated restaurant opens, then protection ends.
- **Territory (Franchise Agreement):** 1.5-mile radius (standard), 0.5-mile in dense urban areas (e.g., New York). Not exclusive.
- Franchisor reserves right to:
  - Operate/license Non-Traditional Restaurants within Territory
  - Sell products under Marks through any non-restaurant channel (catalog, Internet, telemarketing)
  - Operate/franchise other food businesses (not pizza delivery or primarily carry-out)
  - Direct online orders to any restaurant regardless of territory
- Franchisee may solicit/accept orders outside Territory if approved
- Online trade area defined by franchisor, may differ significantly from Territory, may change without compensation
- Territory does not change with population or sales volume
- No right of first refusal for additional restaurants

### Item 13 — Trademarks
Marks owned by PJI. 26+ federal registrations, 5 pending applications. Key marks: PAPA JOHN'S, PAPA JOHNS, PIZZA PAPA JOHN'S & Design, BETTER INGREDIENTS BETTER PIZZA, PAPA REWARDS, PAPADIAS, PAPA BITES. No currently effective material determinations or pending infringement/cancellation. Assignment from Papa John's Inc. (unrelated WI corp) in 1991. Marketing Fund licensed to use Marks.

Known users of similar marks: restaurants in Boca Raton FL, East Lansing MI, and 11 other locations.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
No material patents. Copyright claimed on Manuals, Proprietary Programs, advertising, website, videos. Designated Software is proprietary and confidential. Franchisee must return all software and data on termination. Franchisor represents no infringement of third-party rights.

### Item 15 — Participation
- Principal Operator required — must own 5% equity (or have 5% profit bonus + equity option within 12 months)
- Full time and best efforts to supervision
- Must complete all training
- Must be proficient in English
- If PO fails to qualify, franchisee in default
- For multi-unit: one PO for all restaurants (not per-restaurant)
- Entity franchisees: all owners must sign Owner Agreement (personal guarantee of all obligations)
- Relationship Agreement (Exhibit K-2): if signed, all affiliates and 10%+ owners sign confidentiality and noncompete

### Item 16 — Product Restrictions
- Must sell only approved products and services
- Must offer all menu items for carry-out and delivery (except some NT/ST)
- Cannot offer unapproved products/services
- Franchisor may change authorized products without limits
- May require specific payment forms
- NT restaurants may operate limited menu as determined by operations team

## Subsection: Items 9–16 Operative Burden Summaries

### Item 9 — Franchisee's Obligations
The franchisee must: select and lease/purchase approved sites; complete construction per specifications; obtain Information System; complete all training before opening; pay all fees (royalty, digital, marketing, cooperative); comply with all standards in Manuals; maintain insurance; submit to inspections and audits; participate in advertising programs; get approval for all transfers; comply with noncompete during and after term; resolve disputes by arbitration in KY.

### Item 10 — Financing
The franchisor controls: equipment lease terms (48 months, $50 buyout, no interest), security interest in leased equipment. The franchisee is forbidden from: pledging franchise rights for SBA or bank financing, altering leased equipment, failing to maintain equipment insurance.

### Item 11 — Training/Technology/Advertising
The franchisee must: complete 16+ weeks of management training, maintain $375/month software maintenance, use only approved Information System, accept all upgrades within 120 days, allow franchisor unlimited data access. The franchisor controls: all data ownership, software specifications, training requirements, advertising content and agency approval, Marketing Fund administration, Papa Rewards program.

### Item 12 — Territory
The franchisee receives: 1.5-mile radius (non-exclusive) protected from other standard restaurants only. The franchisor retains: right to operate NT restaurants in territory, sell through any non-restaurant channel, direct online orders to any restaurant, change online trade areas unilaterally.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
The franchisee may: use Marks during term per specifications. The franchisee must not: use Marks in corporate/trade names without approval, create derivative works. All goodwill accrues to franchisor.

### Item 14 — Patents/IP
The franchisee must: maintain confidentiality of all proprietary information, return all software and data on termination. The franchisor owns: all copyrighted works, all data generated by use of proprietary programs.

### Item 15 — Participation
Principal Operator must: own 5% equity, devote full time, complete training, be proficient in English. All entity owners must: sign Owner Agreement (personal guarantee).

### Item 16 — Products
The franchisee must: sell only approved products, offer all specified menu items, accept specified payment forms. The franchisee must not: offer unapproved products or services. The franchisor may: change authorized products without limits, set mandatory maximum prices for national promotions.
