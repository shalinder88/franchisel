# Full Diligence Report — JdV by Hyatt FDD (637924-2025)

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and structure:** JdV by Hyatt operates 18 Brand Hotels in North America as of December 31, 2024 — 6 franchised and 12 company-owned or managed by Hyatt affiliates. The brand was acquired through Hyatt's December 2018 purchase of Two Roads Hospitality and has been offered as a franchise since May 2019. This is a very young franchise system with a limited track record.

2. **Entity structure:** The franchisor is Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C., a Delaware limited liability company wholly owned by Hyatt Hotels Corporation (NYSE-listed). Hyatt Hotels Corporation provides an absolute and unconditional performance guarantee of the franchisor's obligations. Hyatt Corporation, a separate affiliate, administers the World of Hyatt program and provides most Mandatory and Non-Mandatory Services. The collective "Hyatt Group" includes Hyatt Corporation, the franchisor, Rosemont Project Management, and other subsidiaries.

3. **Ownership history:** Hyatt Franchise Corporation converted to LLC form as Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. under Delaware law. The JdV brand originated with Two Roads Hospitality, which Hyatt acquired in December 2018. The franchisor has no predecessors and no undisclosed business activities. Hyatt also franchises under Hyatt Regency, Hyatt Centric, Hyatt Place, Hyatt House, Destination by Hyatt, The Unbound Collection by Hyatt, Caption by Hyatt, and Hyatt Studios brands, plus international brands through separate affiliates.

4. **Investment range:** $42,040,926 to $142,134,233 (excluding real estate costs) for a new 150–300 room lifestyle resort hotel. This is among the highest initial investment ranges in franchising, reflecting the luxury/lifestyle hotel segment. Construction and improvements alone account for $30.9M to $113.4M.

5. **Fee burden:** The combined recurring fee structure includes a 7% royalty on Channel Booking GRR, 3.5% Commercial Services Fee on all GRR, 1.35% Digital Acquisition Fee on digital channel GRR, 4.5% World of Hyatt loyalty assessment on WOH member revenue, plus GDS reservation fees (~$8.50/reservation), mandatory contract costs ($1,500–$25,000/month), and other corporate services ($500–$15,000/month). The total ongoing percentage-based fees alone approach 16–17% of gross rooms revenue before adding per-transaction and fixed monthly charges.

6. **Item 19 highlights:** Three Financial Performance Representations are provided for 18 Covered Hotels during 2024. Franchised hotels averaged 56.1% occupancy, $195.80 ADR, and $109.83 RevPAR — significantly below the system average of 65.6%, $230.65, and $151.22 respectively. Owned/managed hotels outperformed franchised hotels by 60% on RevPAR ($176.21 vs. $109.83). The brand underperforms its competitive set on pricing metrics (Smith Travel RevPAR Index of 93.4 for all hotels).

7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** The lowest-performing franchised hotel had $68.02 RevPAR, 39.6% occupancy, and only $4 in World of Hyatt room nights — suggesting a severely underperforming or very recently affiliated property. The spread between best ($175.63 RevPAR) and worst ($68.02) franchised performance is a 2.6x multiple, indicating highly variable outcomes.

8. **Financial health:** Hyatt Hotels Corporation reported $6.648B total revenue, $1.296B net income (boosted by $1.245B in real estate sale gains), and $13.324B total assets in 2024. Long-term debt increased to $3.326B (from $2.305B). The franchisor entity (Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C.) has $159.3M total assets and $148.5M member's equity on an unaudited basis, with minimal cash ($112K).

9. **Growth trajectory:** Modest organic growth — net +1 outlet in 2024, with 5 franchise agreements signed but not yet open (in Alaska, Georgia, New York, Oregon, South Carolina). Two projected franchise openings in the next fiscal year. One termination in California in 2024.

10. **Territory:** Limited "Area of Protection" that expires 1–5 years after hotel opening. After the AOP Term, the franchisee has no territorial protection whatsoever. Even during the AOP Term, an exception allows franchisor to place new Brand Hotels in the area if acquired as part of a 4+ hotel portfolio.

11. **Renewal:** 10-year successor franchise on then-current terms, which "may be materially different" from the original agreement — an explicit warning repeated multiple times in the FDD.

12. **Disputes:** Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, Illinois. One litigation case in 2024 (unpaid royalties).

13. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution flagged on the Special Risks page.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

The franchise concept is a lifestyle resort hotel that operates under the franchisee's own tradename but affiliates all rooms with the "JdV by Hyatt" brand. The franchisor expects only entities (not individuals) to acquire franchises. Unique to this brand, the hotel features the franchisee's own brand identity (the "Tradename") alongside the JdV by Hyatt affiliation — the franchisor makes no representation about the validity of the Tradename.

Key affiliates involved in operations:
- **Hyatt Corporation:** Administers World of Hyatt program, provides most System Services
- **Rosemont Project Management, L.L.C.:** Optional purchasing and project management services
- **Multiple international franchise affiliates** for Canada, EAME, Latin America, Brazil, Asia Pacific

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Approximately 75% of total purchases to establish and 75% of purchases to operate the Hotel must come from the franchisor, designated or approved suppliers, or conform to System Standards. This is a high level of supplier control typical for full-service hotel brands.

**Designated/Required Suppliers:**
- PMS: Opera PMS (must also sign Hosting Services Agreement for remote hosting)
- POS: Designated vendor only
- CRS, GDS, ADS: Franchisor-designated systems (exclusive)
- Key lock, PBX/phones, sound system: Designated vendors
- Cyber endpoint protection and web filtering: Designated tools
- Quality assurance/inspection: Designated provider
- Travel agent commissions: Designated provider
- Avendra (purchasing company): Optional but may be impractical to source certain products elsewhere

**Approved Supplier Process:** Franchisee may use any supplier meeting System Standards for non-designated categories. Franchisor may designate specific brands/suppliers for Technology System components, fire/life safety systems, and any goods/services where uniformity is deemed beneficial.

**Rebates and Revenue:** FY 2024: affiliates collected ~$320,765 from Brand Hotel franchisees for products/services. Franchisees received $1,096,437 more in WOH reimbursements than paid in WOH fees. Hyatt Corporation received $1 in supplier rebates (offset against System Services Costs).

### Training Program Detail
Seven-part initial training program totaling approximately 300–400 hours:
1. Owner Briefing: 8 hours (2 days, Chicago or virtual)
2. Owner/Operator Orientation: 16 hours (2 days, quarterly)
3. GMU: 53–58 hours (self-paced + virtual + 3-day in-person)
4. Operations Training: 87–155 hours (classroom + OJT)
5. Sales/Events/Revenue Management: 136.5 hours
6. Other Personnel: 32–70 hours
7. Pre-Opening Brand Training: 2–3 trainers on-site for 7+ nights

Replacement training required within 90 days for all Core Management changes. GM certification via GMU assessment required even for experienced Hyatt GMs absent more than 1 year.

### Technology Stack and Costs
Required systems: PMS (Opera), POS, Revenue Management, Events/Lead Gen (Envision), Key Lock, PBX/Phones, In-Room Entertainment, High Speed Internet, Guest Computing, Hotel Music Services, Digital Surveillance, and Scent.

Initial technology cost: $1,508,950–$3,296,000
Ongoing: PMS/POS vendor $3.90/room/month, sound system $18.95/month, plus Technology System components included in Commercial Services Fee.

No contract limits on frequency or cost of upgrades/updates. Franchisor has independent, unlimited access to all data generated by the Technology System.

### Marketing Fund Spending
The Commercial Services Fee (3.5% of GRR) funds brand-wide marketing, advertising, PR, guest satisfaction, reservations, revenue management technology, sales support, PMS services, and digital infrastructure. The franchisor explicitly states it will NOT prepare periodic accounting of how these funds are spent. No franchisee advertising cooperatives exist. The franchisor need not spend any amount on advertising in the franchisee's market area.

### Capital Reserve
Franchisee must deposit 5% of total operating revenue monthly into a separate capital reserve account for approved capital expenditures and renovation obligations.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
The "Area of Protection" is negotiated before signing the Franchise Agreement. Size varies from a few blocks in densely populated urban areas to approximately a 3-mile radius in less populated areas. The "AOP Term" begins on the Franchise Agreement's effective date and typically ends 1 to 5 years after the Hotel's opening date.

**Critical limitation:** The AOP Term is temporary. After it expires, the franchisee has NO territorial rights or protection whatsoever.

### Carve-Outs and Exceptions
Even during the AOP Term, there is one major exception: if the franchisor or any affiliate acquires ownership of or rights to operate/manage/franchise/license a group of at least 4 hotels, one or more of which are in the Area of Protection, the franchisor has unrestricted rights to convert those hotels to Brand Hotels — even if the other acquired hotels are not converted.

For hotels already operating under JdV by Hyatt when a new franchise agreement is signed, the franchisor may choose not to provide any Area of Protection at all.

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights
- No restrictions on where any party may solicit customers or accept orders
- Franchisor may use Internet, catalog, telemarketing, direct marketing in AOP without compensation
- Other Hyatt brands (Hyatt Place, Hyatt House, Hyatt Regency, etc.) may freely operate within AOP at any time
- Franchisee may not use Internet or other methods for reservations except through CRS
- Franchisee may advertise outside AOP only with approval

### Encroachment Risk Assessment: **HIGH**
The combination of a temporary AOP (1–5 years), the portfolio acquisition exception, and the complete freedom for other Hyatt brands creates one of the most limited territorial protections in hotel franchising. A prospective franchisee investing $42M–$142M should understand that within 1–5 years of opening, the franchisor could place competing JdV by Hyatt hotels adjacent to their property.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term:** 20 years from the first day of the month in which the Hotel opens
- **Successor franchise:** 10 years on then-current terms
- **"Materially different" risk:** The FDD repeatedly states that the successor franchise agreement's terms "may be materially different" from the current agreement. This means fees, obligations, territorial protections, and other key terms could all change at renewal.
- **Renewal conditions:** Substantial compliance during term, currently in full compliance, meet then-current new franchisee standards, passing QA scores for previous 3 years, rights to maintain hotel possession for at least 10 years, renovate/remodel hotel and FF&E, pay PIP fee, sign general releases (if state law allows)

### Transfer Provisions
- All transfers of the Franchise Agreement, the Hotel or substantially all assets, or Controlling Ownership Interests require franchisor approval
- Non-Controlling Ownership transfers allowed without approval if transferee is not a Competing Brand Owner or sanctioned person
- Transfer conditions include: transferee qualifies, all amounts paid, training completed, transferee may need to sign then-current FA (materially different), renovation commitment, capital structure and debt service assessment
- No separate transfer fee, but transferee pays the then-current Application Fee
- No public offerings permitted for franchisee or controlling owner

### Termination and Death/Disability
- Franchisee may not terminate the FA except as law allows
- Franchisor may not terminate without cause
- Curable defaults: 24 hours (health/safety), 10 days (payment/documents), 30 days (most other defaults), 60 days (Guarantor Monetary Threshold)
- Non-curable defaults: Bankruptcy, undischarged $100K+ judgments, ceasing operations, IP challenges, unauthorized transfers, felonies, adverse conduct, false books, confidentiality breaches, repeated defaults
- Death/disability: Representative must transfer Controlling Ownership Interest to approved transferee within 6 months

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term:** No covenant not to compete, but neither franchisee nor management company may be a "Competing Brand Owner" (entity owning/franchising/managing a competing brand with 5+ hotels worldwide)
- **Post-term:** No noncompete — franchisee is free to operate any business after termination

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration:** Mandatory for all disputes at a location within 10 miles of franchisor's principal address (currently Chicago, IL)
- **Governing law:** Illinois (subject to Federal Arbitration Act and state-specific overrides)
- **Forum:** Illinois courts for non-arbitrable matters
- **Special Risk:** Out-of-state dispute resolution flagged on the FDD's Special Risks page

### Litigation History
One case in fiscal year 2024: Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. v. Imperial Hotels, LLC et al. (AAA 2024) — unpaid royalty fees. No other litigation required to be disclosed. No pattern of enforcement litigation.

|--------|-------------------|----------------------|------------|

| Avg ADR | $195.80 | $247.20 | $230.65 |
| Avg RevPAR | $109.83 | $176.21 | $151.22 |

**Franchised statistics:**
- Occupancy range: 39.6%–85.9% (median 53.1%, only 2 of 6 exceeded average)

The franchised-vs-owned/managed performance gap is striking: owned/managed hotels generate 60% higher RevPAR. This gap is unexplained in the FDD and represents a significant concern for prospective franchisees.

### Reservation Channel Mix
Direct channels (Hyatt.com, voice reservations, electronic rooming lists) contribute 58.4% of revenue system-wide. Franchised hotels have a higher direct mix (62.8%) than owned/managed (56.8%).

## J. Unresolveds

1. **Franchised vs. owned/managed performance gap** — Franchised hotels average $109.83 RevPAR vs. $176.21 for owned/managed (38% lower). The FDD does not explain this gap. Possible factors include newer franchised properties, less experienced management, different markets, or selection bias in the sample. Severity: **HIGH** — material to investment decision.

2. **One franchised hotel WOH outlier** — One of 6 franchised hotels had only 2.1% WOH revenue, 0.1% WOH occupied room nights, and 4 WOH room nights. This is dramatically different from the group. Nature of this property is unclear. Severity: **MEDIUM** — affects FPR 3 interpretation.

3. **Goodwill impairment reporting unit** — The $110M goodwill impairment in Hyatt Hotels Corporation's 2024 financials was in a management/franchising segment reporting unit. Whether this relates to the JdV brand specifically is not disclosed. Severity: **LOW** — parent-level issue, not brand-specific.

4. **Franchise agreement assets ($144.8M)** — The franchisor entity's largest asset category is "franchise agreement assets constituting payments to customers" — the nature of these payments (likely key money, development incentives, or contribution commitments) is not fully explained. Severity: **LOW** — balance sheet item, not directly material to franchisee obligations.

5. **Exhibit E location** — Initially unlocated but resolved via retry to pages 291–294. Severity: **RESOLVED**.

## K. Contradictions

1. **No contradictions identified** between FDD sections, between text and tables, or in timing. The FDD is internally consistent.

2. **Tension (not contradiction):** Item 12 describes an "Area of Protection" but also explicitly states that "you will not receive an exclusive territory" and that after the AOP Term, "you will have no territorial rights or protection." The simultaneous use of "protection" language and disclaimer of exclusivity creates a potentially misleading impression.

3. **Tension (not contradiction):** The FDD cover page states the total investment includes "$279,406 to $1,119,942 that must be paid to the franchisor or affiliate," but the full Item 7 table contains many more line items payable to "Us" or "Hyatt Corporation" that are categorized differently. The cover page figure represents a specific subset of fees directly payable to franchisor/affiliate.
