# Reader Report — JdV by Hyatt FDD (637924-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand:** JdV by Hyatt (lifestyle resort and hospitality affiliation); franchisor is Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C., a Delaware LLC; parent is Hyatt Hotels Corporation (publicly traded).
2. **System size:** 18 Brand Hotels operating in North America as of Dec 31, 2024 (6 franchised, 12 company-owned/managed). 5 franchise agreements signed but not yet open. Small, growing brand.
3. **Acquisition of brand:** Hyatt acquired Two Roads Hospitality in December 2018; began offering JdV by Hyatt franchises in May 2019.
4. **Total investment:** $42,040,926 to $142,134,233 (excluding real estate) for a new 150–300 room resort hotel. Extremely high capital requirements.
5. **Fee burden:** Royalty 7% of Gross Rooms Revenue from Channel Bookings; Commercial Services Fee 3.5% of GRR; Digital Acquisition Fee 1.35% of GRR from digital channels; World of Hyatt assessment 4.5% of eligible revenue from members (2.25% for on-property enrollments). Combined recurring fees approximately 16–17% of revenue before additional mandatory charges.
6. **Item 19:** FPR disclosed for 18 Covered Hotels. Franchised hotels averaged 56.1% occupancy, $195.80 ADR, $109.83 RevPAR — significantly underperforming Owned/Managed hotels (71.3% occupancy, $247.20 ADR, $176.21 RevPAR).
7. **Growth trajectory:** Modest growth — net +1 total outlet in 2024, +2 projected franchise openings in next fiscal year. 1 termination (California) in 2024.
8. **Territory:** Limited "Area of Protection" during AOP Term (1–5 years post-opening), after which no territorial protection. Major encroachment risk.
9. **Renewal:** 10-year successor term on then-current agreement terms (which "may be materially different"). Must renovate, sign release if state law allows.
10. **Dispute resolution:** Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, Illinois; Illinois law governs.
11. **Noncompete:** No post-term noncompete. During term, neither franchisee nor management company may be a "Competing Brand Owner."
12. **Financial health (parent):** Hyatt Hotels Corporation — total revenue $6.648B (2024), net income $1.296B. Total assets $13.324B, total equity $3.826B. $110M goodwill impairment charge in 2024 on management/franchising segment.
13. **Financial health (franchisor):** Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. — unaudited balance sheet only. Total assets $159.3M, member's equity $148.5M. Most assets are franchise agreement intangibles ($5.7M) and payments to customers ($144.8M).
14. **Litigation:** One case in 2024 — Hyatt Franchising v. Imperial Hotels (unpaid royalty fees). No other litigation required to be disclosed.
15. **No bankruptcy** for franchisor or its principals.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Area of Protection:** Negotiated before signing; size varies (few blocks in urban to ~3-mile radius in suburban)
- **AOP Term:** Typically 1–5 years after hotel opening date — then ALL territorial protection expires
- **Exception during AOP:** If franchisor/affiliate acquires ≥4 hotels (even outside brand), can convert acquired hotels within AOP to Brand Hotels
- **After AOP Term:** Complete loss of exclusivity. Franchisor and affiliates may open unlimited competing Brand Hotels within former AOP
- **No exclusive territory** — explicit contractual language
- **No options, rights of first refusal, or rights to acquire additional franchises**
- **No restrictions on customer solicitation** for any party
- **Franchisor may use any channel** (Internet, direct marketing, telemarketing) to sell in AOP without compensation to franchisee
- **Other Hyatt brands** (Hyatt Place, Hyatt House, Hyatt Regency, etc.) may freely operate within AOP at any time

**Risk assessment: HIGH encroachment risk** — temporary AOP of only 1–5 years, after which franchisee has no territorial protection whatsoever. Plus, exception for acquisitions of 4+ hotel portfolios means even during AOP, a competitor acquisition could bring a new JdV into the area.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term:** 20 years from first day of month hotel opens
- **Successor franchise:** 10 years on then-current terms (which "may be materially different")
- **Renewal requirements:** Substantial compliance during term, currently in full compliance, meet then-current standards for new franchisees, passing QA scores previous 3 years, rights to maintain possession ≥10 years, renovate/remodel hotel, pay PIP fee, sign general releases (if state law allows)
- **"Materially different" risk:** Explicitly flagged — successor franchise agreement terms may differ significantly from current

### Termination
- **By franchisee:** Not permitted under FA except as law allows. May terminate Central Services Agreement on 10-day cured breach; Hosting Services Agreement on 30-day cured breach or 6 months without cause
- **By franchisor without cause:** Not permitted under FA. May terminate Central Services Agreement on 90 days notice; Hosting Services Agreement on 30 days notice
- **By franchisor with cause:**
  - Curable: 24 hours for health/safety threats; 10 days for payment/document failures; 30 days for taxes, development deadlines, provider payments, other defaults; 60 days for Guarantor Monetary Threshold failure
  - Non-curable: Bankruptcy, $100K+ undischarged judgments, ceasing operations, contesting IP ownership, unauthorized transfer, felony conviction, adverse conduct, false books, confidentiality breach, trade restriction violation, repeated defaults

### Transfer
- No transfers of FA, hotel, assets, or Controlling Ownership Interests without approval
- Non-Controlling transfers allowed without approval if transferee/owners not Competing Brand Owners or sanctioned persons
- Transfer conditions: transferee qualifies, all amounts paid, training completed, may require signing then-current FA (materially different terms possible), guarantors sign release, renovation commitment
- Death/disability: representative must transfer to approved transferee within 6 months

### Right of First Offer
- Only applies if Exhibit C to FA is signed (strategic markets only)

### Noncompete
- **During term:** No covenant not to compete, but neither franchisee nor management company may be Competing Brand Owner
- **Post-term:** No noncompete whatsoever

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration:** Mandatory for all disputes, at location within 10 miles of franchisor's principal address (currently Chicago, IL)
- **Choice of forum:** Illinois (subject to arbitration and state law)
- **Choice of law:** Illinois law (except Federal Arbitration Act)

### Liquidated Damages
- Complex formula: min(60, months remaining) × (Average Monthly Revenue × 3.5% + Average Monthly Channel Revenue × 7%)
- 150% multiplier for Consequential Termination (transfer to Competitor or 3+ related terminations)
- Casualty termination: fee equals lesser of formula damages or Net Recovery (insurance proceeds less lender payments less unreturned capital)
