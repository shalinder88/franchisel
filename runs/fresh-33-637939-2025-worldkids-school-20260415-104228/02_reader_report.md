# Reader Report — WorldKids School (637939-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand:** WorldKids School — bilingual (Spanish/Mandarin) immersion learning centers for children aged 2 months to 6 years.
2. **Franchisor:** WKAF LLC, a Washington State LLC formed July 18, 2024. Very early-stage franchisor — less than 1 year of franchising history.
3. **Affiliate:** WorldKids Academy, LLC, operating since January 2015 in Seattle, WA. Runs 8 company-owned outlets (as of Dec 31, 2024) — all in Washington State.
4. **Leadership:** Single officer — Nicole Chaudry, CEO since inception (July 2024), also CEO of affiliate since 2015. 10 years industry experience.
5. **Initial franchise fee:** $80,000 (non-refundable). ADA development fee: $40,000 per additional outlet.
6. **Investment range:** Small (1K-3K sqft): $195K-$291.5K; Medium (3K-6K sqft): $270K-$436.5K; Large (6K+ sqft): $435K-$591.5K.
7. **Ongoing fees:** Royalty 6% of Gross Revenue; National Ad Fund 2% (starts after 12 months operational); Local Advertising greater of $2,000/mo or 1.5%.
8. **Item 19:** FPR provided — 8 company-owned outlets reported for 2024, 6 for 2023. Top performer: Georgetown/SODO ($3.98M gross, 12,765 sqft). Smallest: Everett ($72.5K, opened Sept 2024). Revenue is gross only — no expense/cost/EBITDA disclosure.
9. **Item 20:** Zero franchised outlets in 2022-2024. 8 company-owned (affiliate) outlets at end of 2024. 1 projected new franchised outlet in WA.
10. **Financial health:** CRITICAL CONCERN — WKAF LLC has total assets of $97 (cash) as of Dec 31, 2024. Zero revenue, zero expenses, $97 members' equity. The franchisor is essentially a shell entity. FDD itself flags this: "financial condition calls into question the Franchisor's financial ability to provide services."
11. **Territory:** Exclusive, typically 5-mile radius, no performance contingency. Franchisor reserves internet/direct marketing channels.
12. **Contract term:** 10-year initial, 10-year renewals (materially different terms possible). Noncompete: 2 years, 25 miles post-term.
13. **Dispute resolution:** Mediation/arbitration in Washington State (subject to state-specific overrides). Washington law governs.
14. **State addenda:** Extensive — California, Hawaii, Illinois, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Washington, Wisconsin.
15. **Key risk:** Early-stage franchisor with negligible capitalization, sole-proprietor leadership, no existing franchisees, and disclosed financial inability to provide support.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Item 17 — Renewal, Termination, Transfer, Dispute Resolution (pp.37-44)

**Term and Renewal:**
- Initial term: 10 years.
- Successive 10-year terms available if: full compliance, capital expenditures for system uniformity, all monetary obligations satisfied, not in default, timely notice of intent to renew, execute then-current FA (**may contain materially different terms**), meet current qualifications, complete training requirements, sign general release (subject to state law), pay $5,000 renewal fee.

**Termination by Franchisee:**
- Mutual agreement, non-renewal, sale per FA terms, or any ground permitted by law.

**Termination by Franchisor — Non-Curable Defaults (immediate):**
- Failure to timely open; failure to complete training; loss of required licenses >5 business days; material misrepresentation; felony conviction; adverse behavior after notice; unauthorized use of trade secrets; failure to have personnel sign NDAs; abandonment (5+ consecutive days); unauthorized transfer; failure to maintain Designated Manager after death/disability; understatement of amounts due >2% on 2+ occasions; insolvency; misuse of Marks; 2+ failures to submit reports/pay fees in 12 months; 2+ health/safety violations; taking actions reserved to franchisor; failure to comply with law after notice; repeated breaches; cross-default with other franchisor agreements.

**Termination by Franchisor — Curable Defaults:**
- General: 30-day cure.
- Insurance: 10-day cure.
- Payment: 5-day cure.

**Post-Termination Obligations:**
- Cease operations, cease using marks/trade secrets/system, cancel assumed names, pay all sums owed, return Operations Manual, assign phone/fax numbers, comply with noncompete.

**Noncompete:**
- During term: Prohibited from owning or working for competitive business; prohibited from diverting business/customers.
- Post-term: 2 years, 25-mile radius of any other franchised or franchisor-owned outlet. No solicitation of customers/business associates.
- Subject to state law overrides.

**Transfer:**
- Franchisor may assign without restriction.
- Franchisee transfer requires prior written consent. Conditions: no ROFR exercise, all obligations paid, general release signed, transferee meets standards, transferee signs then-current FA, copy of transfer documents, $10,000 transfer fee, transferee personally bound, third-party consents obtained, seller signs noncompete, transferee completes training.
- Transfer to controlled entity: Permitted without fee if franchisee retains control.
- ROFR: Franchisor may match any offer for business or ownership interest.
- Franchisor has option to purchase business assets at book value within 30 days of termination/expiration.

**Death/Disability:**
- Representative must transfer interest within 180 days or franchisor may terminate.

**Dispute Resolution:**
- Choice of law: Washington State (subject to applicable state law).
- Forum: Franchisor headquarters (Washington State) for mediation, arbitration, litigation (subject to applicable state law).
- Mandatory mediation and arbitration.
- Jury waiver.
- Class action waiver.
- Limitation of damages (no punitive/exemplary).
- Limitation of actions.
- Prior notice of claims required.
- Prevailing party attorneys' fees.
