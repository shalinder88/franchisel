## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: Caption by Hyatt is an extremely nascent brand. As of December 31, 2024, the entire system comprises 2 operating hotels — 1 franchised (Nashville, TN, opened 2024) and 1 company-owned (Chattanooga, TN, opened 2022). Three additional franchise agreements are signed but not yet open (2 in California, 1 in Tennessee). Zero projected new openings in the next fiscal year.

2. **Entity structure**: Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. (Delaware LLC), a subsidiary of Hyatt Hotels Corporation (NYSE: H). Hyatt Hotels Corp absolutely and unconditionally guarantees performance. The franchisor is a single-purpose entity with $159.3M total assets (unaudited), backed by a parent with $13.3B in total assets.

3. **Ownership history**: Hyatt Franchising converted from Hyatt Franchise Corporation (Delaware). Hyatt Hotels Corporation is the ultimate parent. Caption by Hyatt franchising began September 2019. Multiple sister franchise entities exist for Hyatt Place, Hyatt House, Hyatt Centric, Hyatt Regency, and international operations.

4. **Investment range**: $28,378,455 to $87,964,018 (excluding real estate). This is among the highest investment ranges in the franchise hotel industry. Construction alone is $21.9M–$67.5M. Hotels are 125–300 guest rooms in urban primary/secondary markets.

5. **Fee burden**: The total ongoing percentage-based fee load on Gross Rooms Revenue is approximately 8.5% (5% royalty + 3.5% commercial services) before accounting for World of Hyatt (4% of eligible loyalty revenue), Digital Acquisition Fee (1.35% of digital channel GRR), GDS fees (~$8.50/reservation), and mandatory contract allocations ($1,500–$7,500/month). The all-in fee load is substantial.

6. **Item 19**: No financial performance representations. The franchisor makes no claims about past or future financial performance. Given the brand has only 2 operating hotels worldwide, limited data exists.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: This is a pre-scale brand. There is no meaningful operating history to assess franchisee outcomes. The single franchised hotel opened in 2024. A prospective franchisee is essentially a pioneer investor.

8. **Financial health**: Parent Hyatt Hotels Corporation is financially strong — $1.296B net income (2024), $633M operating cash flow, $1.011B cash on hand. Guarantee of Performance in place. However, 2024 income was boosted by $1.245B in real estate sale gains and offset by $213M in asset impairments.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Minimal. Net system growth was +1 in 2022 (company-owned), +0 in 2023, +1 in 2024 (first franchised unit). No projected openings in next fiscal year despite 3 signed agreements.

10. **Territory**: Area of Protection typically expires 3 years after opening. After that, fully nonexclusive. Franchisor can open competing Brand Hotels and other Hyatt Network Hotels without restriction.

11. **Renewal risk**: Successor franchise on then-current terms which "may be materially different." Franchise Agreement explicitly warns about this multiple times.

12. **Disputes**: Arbitration in Chicago, Illinois. Illinois governing law. One litigation case in 2024 (unpaid royalties against a different brand's franchisee).

13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution, emerging brand with minimal track record, very high investment, no FPR data, territory protection limited to 3 years.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. is a Delaware LLC that converted from Hyatt Franchise Corporation. Its sole member is (indirectly) Hyatt Hotels Corporation. The franchisor has offered Caption by Hyatt franchises since September 2019. Only entities (not individuals) are expected to acquire franchises.

Key affiliates providing services:
- **Hyatt Corporation**: Administers World of Hyatt loyalty program, provides most Mandatory and Non-Mandatory Services.
- **Rosemont Project Management, L.L.C.**: Optional purchasing and project management services.
- Together with the franchisor, these form the "Hyatt Group."

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
The application fee for a new development is $95,000 (plus $500/room over 200). Change of ownership is $150,000. Additional initial fees include PIP preparation ($10,000), operator approval ($10,000–$20,000), IT Project Management ($58,300+), comfort letters ($2,500 each), pre-opening training ($15,175–$34,300 plus expenses), and optional services. The FDD reports actual total initial fees paid in fiscal 2024 ranged from $129,750 to $208,320.

**Ongoing Fees (percentage-based):**
- Royalty: 5% of GRR (may be reduced first 1–3 years for new construction)
- Commercial Services Fee: 3.5% of GRR (covers marketing, reservations, revenue management tech, sales, PMS, digital tech)
- World of Hyatt: 4% of eligible loyalty revenue (2% for on-property enrollment)
- Digital Acquisition Fee: 1.35% of GRR through digital channels

**Ongoing Fees (fixed/variable):**
- GDS reservations: ~$8.50/reservation
- Mandatory contracts: $1,500–$7,500/month
- Other corporate services: $500–$5,000/month
- QA inspections: $1,500–$2,000/year
- Training tools: $3.64/room/year

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Transfer: No separate fee but must pay $150,000 change-of-ownership application fee
- Liquidated damages: Up to 36 months × 8.5% of average monthly GRR (150% for Consequential Termination)
- Late fee: $225 plus 1.5%/month
- De-identification failure: $5,000/day

### Initial Investment
The total investment for a 125–300 room new-construction Caption by Hyatt hotel ranges from $28,378,455 to $87,964,018, excluding real estate costs. The dominant cost driver is construction/improvements ($21.9M–$67.5M), followed by FF&E ($3.4M–$12M), technology ($638K–$778K), and architecture/design ($538K–$1.95M).

Additional notable costs: Liquor license ($30K–$400K, with "quota" markets potentially exceeding the high end), pre-opening marketing ($62.5K–$300K), and 3 months of additional working capital ($360K–$675K).

The franchisor requires a monthly FF&E reserve deposit: 3% of GRR in year 1, 4% in year 2, and 5% thereafter. This is a franchisor-mandated capital reserve, not optional.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Approximately 95% of establishment costs and 95% of operating costs must come from designated/approved suppliers or per System Standards. The franchisor and its affiliates are the only approved suppliers for pre-opening design/construction services, IT Project Management, PMS hosting, and several technology systems.

The Opera PMS is mandatory at $3.90/room/month for support. Franchisees must use designated vendors for PBX/phones, key locks, and sound systems. Cyber endpoint protection and web filtering tools must come from designated suppliers.

If a franchisee wants to use an unapproved supplier, a written request and up to $20,000 testing fee may be required, with a typical 30-day review period.

### Training Program Detail
Training is extensive — 8 mandatory programs totaling approximately 305–422 classroom hours and 56–118 on-the-job hours:

- **Owner Briefing**: 2 days (8 hours), Chicago or virtual
- **FMU**: 15–17 hours, online/virtual (for new management companies)
- **Owner/Operator Orientation**: 2 days (16 hours), Chicago or virtual
- **GMU**: 53–58 hours over multiple formats (eLearning, virtual, in-person workshop in Chicago)
- **Operations Training**: 47–79 classroom + 40–76 OJT hours
- **Sales/Events/Revenue Management**: 136.5 hours virtual/eLearning
- **Other Personnel**: 17–34 classroom + 15–36 OJT hours
- **Pre-Opening Brand Training**: 2–3 trainers on-site for minimum 7 nights

Training costs to franchisor: $15,175–$34,300 plus expenses ($3,600–$7,405). Attendee travel/living costs: $20,085–$27,810. Optional additional training: up to $250,000.

### Technology Stack
Required systems: Property Management (Opera PMS), Point of Sale, Revenue Management, Events/Lead Gen (Envision), Key Lock, PBX/Phones, In-Room Entertainment, High Speed Internet, Guest Computing, Hotel Music, Digital Surveillance, and Scent. Estimated cost: $638,600–$777,650. No limits on frequency or cost of future upgrades.

### Marketing Fund
No separate advertising fund. Marketing is covered by the 3.5% Commercial Services Fee. No periodic accounting of how funds are spent. Franchisor need not spend any amount in the Hotel's market area. No franchisee advertising councils.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
The Area of Protection (AOP) is defined before signing by geographic references or a circle radius. Urban AOPs may be as small as a few blocks; suburban up to ~3 miles from the entrance.

**Critical limitation**: The AOP Term typically expires 3 years after the hotel's opening date. After expiration, the franchisee has zero territorial protection. The franchisor and affiliates may open competing Brand Hotels and other Hyatt Network Hotels within the former AOP without restriction.

Even during the AOP Term, there is an acquisition exception: if the franchisor acquires a group of 4+ hotels, it may convert acquired hotels within the AOP to Brand Hotels.

### Channel Restrictions
The franchisor may use Internet, telemarketing, and other direct marketing to make sales in the AOP under Proprietary Marks without compensating the franchisee. The franchisee may market outside the AOP with approval. CRS is the only method to accept reservations.

### Encroachment Risk
High. The franchisee should expect to find Hyatt Network Hotels of other brands in the AOP at any time. The Hyatt portfolio includes 25+ brands. The FDD explicitly warns that conflicts between franchisees will be handled as the franchisor "deems appropriate."

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
**Initial term**: 20 years from the first day of the month the hotel opens. This is a long term appropriate for a high-capital hotel franchise.

**Renewal**: A 10-year successor franchise is available if the franchisee substantially complied during the term, currently complies, meets then-current standards, has passing QA scores for the previous 3 years, and has rights to maintain possession for 10+ years. The successor agreement may be "materially different" including different fees.

### Transfer Provisions
All transfers of the Franchise Agreement, Hotel, assets, or Controlling Ownership Interests require approval. The transferee may need to sign the then-current franchise agreement (which may be materially different). The transfer "fee" is the current change-of-ownership application fee ($150,000). The transferor and guarantors must sign a termination and general release (if state law allows).

Exceptions: Mortgages to lenders, estate planning transfers, and non-Controlling Ownership Interest transfers (with 30-day notice) do not require approval.

Right of First Offer: Only applies if Exhibit C is signed, and only for "strategic markets."

### Termination and Death/Disability
The franchisor cannot terminate without cause. Curable defaults have cure periods of 24 hours (health/safety) to 60 days (Guarantor Monetary Threshold). Non-curable defaults include bankruptcy, ceasing operations, unauthorized transfers, felony, false books, and repeated defaults.

Upon death or disability of a controlling owner, the representative has 6 months to transfer to an approved party.

### Noncompete
**During term**: No covenant not to compete per se, but neither the franchisee nor management company may be a Competing Brand Owner (entity owning/franchising/managing a brand with 5+ hotels globally that competes with Brand Hotels).

**Post-term**: No post-term noncompete.

### Dispute Resolution
All disputes must be arbitrated within 10 miles of the franchisor's principal business address (currently Chicago, IL). Illinois law governs (except federal law). The Michigan notice in the FDD states that the franchisor intends to enforce the arbitration clause despite Michigan law potentially voiding out-of-state arbitration requirements.

### Litigation History
One case: Hyatt Franchising v. Imperial Hotels, LLC et al. (AAA 2024) — unpaid royalty fees. This was against a different Hyatt brand's franchisee, not Caption by Hyatt.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

**No FPR.** The franchisor explicitly states: "We do not make any representations about a franchisee's future financial performance or the past financial performance of company-owned or franchised outlets."

This is significant given the investment range of $28.4M–$88M. With only 2 operating hotels in the entire system (1 franchised, 1 company-owned), a prospective franchisee has extremely limited financial data to assess viability. The only franchised hotel opened in 2024 in Nashville, Tennessee.

Any unauthorized financial performance representations should be reported to Dan Hansen, Head of Americas Development, at (312) 750-1234, the FTC, and state agencies.
