## Pass A — Executive Snapshot

1. **System scale**: 3,229 total US outlets at end of 2025 (3,134 franchised + 95 company-owned), net growth of +52 units in 2025 (down from +101 in 2024 and +130 in 2023). Growth is decelerating.
2. **Entity structure**: PLK Inc. (MN corp., formed 1992 as America's Favorite Chicken Company) is an indirect subsidiary of RBILP/RBI. 3G Restaurant Brands Holdings (Cayman Islands LP) owns ~22% combined voting power of RBI. First restaurant opened 1972 in New Orleans.
3. **Ownership history**: Acquired by Orange, Inc. (merged into PLK) in March 2017 as part of RBI portfolio. RBI also owns Burger King, Tim Hortons, and Firehouse Subs.
4. **Investment range**: Free-standing $1,222,045–$3,923,245; In-line $504,545–$1,968,245 (excludes real estate).
5. **Fee burden**: 5% royalty + 4.6%–5.0% advertising contribution (Ad Fund Test Program) + 0.5%–1.75% co-op + $200/mo + 1% digital sales (consumer ordering tech fee) + $720/yr training platform + $1,000/yr PLK Foundation + rent (if PLK property).
6. **Item 19 highlights**: Free-standing consolidated average sales $1,893,880 (median $1,788,529). Average EBITDA for free-standing franchised at $2.0M–$2.5M sales level: 14.9% ($331,499). At >$2.5M level: 17.9% ($547,990). Bottom quartile (<$1.5M): only 3.5% EBITDA ($41,234).
7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: 729 of 2,338 free-standing restaurants (31.2%) operate below $1.5M annual sales with EBITDA of only 3.5% — substantial risk at lower volume levels.
8. **Financial health (RBI)**: Total assets $25.6B, long-term debt $13.25B, net income from continuing operations $1.201B (2025). PLK segment: $800M total revenue, $250M adjusted operating income.
9. **Growth trajectory**: Net franchised outlet growth: +130 (2023), +44 (2024), +55 (2025). New openings declining (165→136→104). 14 terminations in 2025 (first material terminations in 3 years). 104 projected new franchised openings for 2026.
10. **Territory**: Protected Area is lesser of 1-mile radius or 50,000 population — relatively narrow. No exclusivity under TRA/MTRA. Alternative Venues excluded from protection.
11. **Renewal**: 20-year initial term + 10-year renewal + optional supplemental 10-year renewal. Must sign then-current form which "may contain terms and conditions materially different from your original Franchise Agreement."
12. **Disputes**: Litigation in Florida (Southern District or 11th Judicial Court Miami-Dade). Florida law applies. No arbitration.
13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution mandated in Florida.
14. **Gag clause**: Present. "In some instances, current and former franchisees sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with Popeyes." Tied to settlement, workout, and certain amendment agreements.

### Item 1 — Franchisor Identity and Structure

PLK Inc. was formed July 27, 1992 as America's Favorite Chicken Company, renamed AFC Enterprises Inc. (1996), then Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen Inc. (2014). The first Popeyes Restaurant opened in 1972 in New Orleans; franchising began in 1976. The brand has been transitioning from "Popeyes Chicken & Biscuits" to "Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen" since 2009.

## Pass C — Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Technology (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Supplier Restrictions

PLK exercises extensive supplier control. Approximately 95% of all purchases (both establishment and ongoing) must be from approved or designated suppliers.

**Trade Secret Products** (sole/designated suppliers only): Food, beverages, ingredients, spices, batter, seasonings, mixes, sauces, chemicals, cleaning supplies. National beverage strategy with sole beverage suppliers (Coca-Cola/KDPR).

**Proprietary Products** (approved manufacturers): Food, beverages, equipment, uniforms, signs, menu boards, paper goods, packaging bearing Popeyes marks.

**Supplier Approval Process**: Written request → evaluation under current criteria → facility inspection + product testing → 90 days (simple items) to 6 months (complex food formulas). Franchisee or supplier pays testing costs (up to $5,000). PLK can revoke approval at any time in sole discretion.

**Revenue from Franchisee Purchases**: $13.7 million in FY2025 (~1.7% of PLK's total revenues of ~$799M). Sources include rebates, flat fee payments, below-market products/services. ~4.8% of consolidated Ad Fund revenue comes from supplier contributions.

**Beverage Agreements**: Coca-Cola Company and Dr. Pepper/Snapple Group provide funding based on fountain beverage gallons purchased.

**SMS Membership**: By signing Franchise Agreement, franchisee becomes member of Supply Management Services, Inc. (SMS) purchasing cooperative.

**Financial Reporting Obligations**: Monthly P&L entry (required categories), quarterly unaudited financial statement (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow).

## Pass D — Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory

**TRA/MTRA**: No exclusivity. Target Areas are non-exclusive. PLK can establish other restaurants, sell branded products through any channel, license others in same areas.

**Franchise Agreement (Protected Area)**: Lesser of (a) 1-mile radius around restaurant, or (b) area encompassing 50,000 population (residential + workplace combined). Exclusions: existing restaurants, closed restaurants that may reopen within 3 years, previously granted franchise agreements, Alternative Venues. PLK can periodically reduce Protected Area for population shifts (but always ≥50,000 population if <1 mile).

**Alternative Venues** (excluded from all territory protections): transportation facilities, toll road plazas, educational facilities, enclosed shopping malls, institutional feeding facilities, government institutions, military bases, casinos, amusement/theme parks, stadiums/convention centers, delivery facilities (ghost kitchens).

**Development Agreement**: Grants a Territory with specific Target Locations. Limited grant — PLK can still operate elsewhere in Territory outside Target Locations. Protected Areas of existing franchisees excluded from Territory.

**Channel Competition**: PLK reserves right to sell POPEYES-branded products through grocery stores, convenience stores, internet, direct marketing in any area including Protected Area. Franchisee receives no compensation for alternative distribution channel sales. Franchisee cannot use alternative distribution channels.

**RBI Brand Overlap**: Burger King, Tim Hortons, and Firehouse Subs restaurants may be located in same market. Menus overlap on some items (sandwiches, fries, desserts, beverages). PLK management attempts to resolve cross-brand conflicts but is not responsible for resolution.

### Item 13 — Trademarks

Principal registered marks: POPEYES, POPEYES LOUISIANA KITCHEN, LOVE THAT CHICKEN, POPPY (character and designs). All on Principal Register with USPTO. No material determinations, infringement, or cancellation proceedings pending. Some registrations exclude Lake Geneva, Wisconsin.

Web/social media: Cannot operate website or social media without PLK written consent. PLK can designate form/content, require hyperlinks, charge fees for development/hosting. Cannot use Proprietary Marks in domain names, email addresses, or on social media platforms.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information

Two registered copyrights: POPPY (chicken character, VA2364861) and PATTERN ICON (VA2372797). Manual and materials claimed as trade secret/copyright. **AI restriction**: Franchisee prohibited from using confidential information for machine learning, AI model training, or generative AI input without prior approval. Must opt out of AI provider training on PLK data.

### Item 15 — Participation Requirements

**Managing Director**: Must have full-time control of day-to-day operations; primary residence within reasonable driving distance; cannot engage in any other substantial business; cannot own/operate competing chicken business; must complete all PTP modules.

**Managing Owner**: Must have ≥10% ownership interest; authority to bind franchisee; sign Managing Owner Certification (includes 2-year noncompete).

**Managing Director Ownership**: If not the Managing Owner, collectively all Managing Directors must have ≥2.5% ownership interest.

**Staffing**: Certified Manager on-site at all times. Additional supervisor required at 8th restaurant and every 7–10 restaurants thereafter. Must meet with PLK representatives in person at designated locations (including Miami HQ) on request.

**Guarantee**: Officers, directors, and all holders of ≥10% interest must jointly and severally guarantee all obligations.

### Item 16 — Product Restrictions

Must operate solely as Popeyes Restaurant. Must offer all and only authorized products. PLK can set maximum prices for certain designated menu items. Franchisee sets own prices otherwise. PLK can change menu items, ingredients, specifications without limit. Delivery only through approved third-party providers (e.g., Uber Eats, Grubhub) and PLK digital platforms.

## Pass E — Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Franchise Agreement Terms

**Term**: 20 years from commencement of operations. Alternative venues: 1–20 years.

**Renewal**: One 10-year renewal term + optional "Supplemental Renewal Term" of 10 years (at 50% of then-current franchise fee). Requirements: notice, satisfaction of all monetary obligations, compliance, general release, refurbishment/renovation/modernization, payment of renewal fee (pro-rated then-current franchise fee), compliance with all operational requirements for all restaurants, no pending/threatened litigation, meet then-current financial ratios. **Must sign then-current form of Franchise Agreement, which may contain terms and conditions materially different from original, including higher royalty fees and/or advertising contributions.**

**Termination by Franchisee**: Not available.

**Curable Defaults** (Franchise Agreement): 30 days for most defaults; 10 days for non-payment.

**Non-Curable Defaults** (Franchise Agreement): Insolvency/bankruptcy; failure to open by deadline; failure to stay open; criminal convictions; health/safety threats; failure to close after verbal notice for health/safety violation; failure to meet financial ratios; failure to meet transfer requirements; noncompete breach; confidentiality breach; false books/records/reports; default under other agreements (including Development Agreement); repeated failed assessment scores; failure to permit inspections; sale of unapproved products; conduct adversely affecting reputation/goodwill.

**Development Agreement Termination — Non-Curable**: Transfer; failure to meet Cumulative Opening Target after 60-day cure; failure to meet franchise approval or opening deadline; duplication of System; confidentiality breach; noncompete breach; insolvency; opening without franchise/site approval; challenging Proprietary Marks; false information; breach of financial covenants; operations failing to score in top 50% of peer category; grade of "D" or "F" in operational metrics; conduct adversely affecting reputation; breach of any PLK affiliate agreement.

**Transfer**: Requires prior written consent. Conditions: payment of debts, noncompete compliance, general release, qualified transferee, new franchise agreement and guaranty, restaurant remodeling, transferee training, transfer fee ($7,500). PLK has ROFR to match any offer. Death/disability: 12 months to assign (extendable to 18 months).

**Noncompete**: During term and for 2 years after: cannot own/operate QSR specializing in chicken within 10 miles of any Popeyes Restaurant owned. Officers, directors, 10%+ owners, Managing Director all bound.

**Dispute Resolution**: Litigation (no arbitration). Venue: US District Court for Southern District of Florida, or 11th Judicial Court in Miami-Dade County, Florida. Florida law applies. Subject to state law overrides in Illinois and Washington.

### Lease/Sublease Terms

**Term**: 20 years (may vary for non-traditional locations).
**Renewal**: No right of renewal. If successor franchise granted, may receive new lease.
**Curable Defaults**: 10 days for non-payment of rent; 30 days for reports, understatement of sales, unauthorized transfer, loss of premises, failure to restore.
**Non-Curable**: Bankruptcy, false statements, unauthorized assignment, multiple defaults, franchise agreement default.
