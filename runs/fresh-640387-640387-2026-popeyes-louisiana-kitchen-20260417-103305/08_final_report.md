## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System scale and structure**: Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen is one of the largest quick-service chicken restaurant systems in the United States, with 3,229 US outlets (3,134 franchised, 95 company-owned) and 2,112 international locations as of December 31, 2025. The brand is a household name, established in New Orleans in 1972 and franchising since 1976.

2. **Entity and ownership**: Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen, Inc. is a Minnesota corporation, originally formed in 1992 as America's Favorite Chicken Company. It is an indirect subsidiary of Restaurant Brands International Inc. (RBI), a Canadian-listed company that also owns Burger King, Tim Hortons, and Firehouse Subs. 3G Restaurant Brands Holdings (Cayman Islands LP) holds approximately 22% of RBI's combined voting power.

3. **Investment range**: A new free-standing restaurant requires $1,222,045 to $3,923,245 (excluding real estate), with the building component alone ranging from $700,000 to $1,600,000. An in-line format ranges from $504,545 to $1,968,245. These are substantial commitments, and PLK offers no direct financing or refunds.

4. **Fee burden**: The total ongoing fee burden is among the highest in QSR franchising: 5% royalty + 4.6%–5.0% advertising contribution (with potential escalation to 5.50%) + 0.5%–1.75% co-op + ~$200/month consumer ordering technology fee + 1% of digital sales + $720/year training platform + $1,000/year PLK Foundation. For a restaurant doing $1.9M in sales, the royalty alone is ~$95,000/year, and total franchisor-directed fees approach 12%+ of gross sales.

5. **Item 19 financial performance**: The FDD provides unusually detailed financial performance data across five tables. The consolidated free-standing average is $1,893,880 in annual sales. However, 31% of free-standing restaurants (729 units) fall below $1.5M in sales with an average EBITDA of only 3.5% ($41,234) — barely above breakeven. At the $2.0M–$2.5M level, EBITDA averages 14.9% ($331,499). The system shows a strong correlation between operational quality (measured by tier) and financial performance.

6. **Growth trajectory**: Net US growth has decelerated: +130 (2023), +101 (2024), +52 (2025). New franchised openings dropped from 165 to 104 over three years. The system saw its first material terminations (14) in 2025, and transfers nearly doubled from 125 to 229.

7. **Territory protection**: The Protected Area is relatively narrow — the lesser of a 1-mile radius or 50,000 population. Alternative Venues (ghost kitchens, airports, malls, military bases, etc.) are excluded from all protection. PLK reserves the right to sell branded products through retail and digital channels with no franchisee compensation.

8. **Renewal risk**: The 20-year initial term is followed by a 10-year renewal (plus optional supplemental 10-year). However, renewal requires signing PLK's then-current form of Franchise Agreement, which "may contain terms and conditions materially different from your original Franchise Agreement, including higher royalty fees and/or advertising contributions."

9. **Litigation and disputes**: Seven pending cases, including direct actions against PLK (Syed CAD$6M, ADP Direct Poultry CAD$35M + $1M punitive, Mun class action). Multi-jurisdictional no-poach settlements with 13+ states. All disputes must be litigated in Florida.

10. **Special risk**: The FDD flags out-of-state dispute resolution (Florida) as a special risk for certain states. This is a material burden for franchisees outside Florida.

11. **Gag clause**: Present. Some current and former franchisees have signed confidentiality provisions as part of settlement, workout, or amendment agreements. This limits prospective franchisees' ability to conduct thorough due diligence.

12. **Financial health of guarantor**: RBI has $25.6B in total assets but carries $13.25B in long-term debt (2.57x debt-to-equity). Intangible assets and goodwill represent 68% of total assets. The company is highly leveraged, though it generated $1.2B in net income from continuing operations and maintains ~$2.4B in total liquidity.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen, Inc. has been the franchisor of the Popeyes system since 1992. The company is ultimately controlled by RBI through its parent structure. The franchise relationship is documented through multiple agreements: a Target Reservation Agreement (TRA) or Multiple Target Reservation Agreement (MTRA) for site selection, a Development Agreement for multi-unit developers, and a Franchise Agreement for each individual restaurant. If PLK controls the property, a separate Lease/Sublease Agreement also applies.

The franchisor does not select sites for franchisees (except when leasing/subleasing PLK-controlled property). Franchisees select sites subject to PLK acceptance. The typical time from site approval to opening is 12–24 months.

### Fee Stack

The fee structure is multi-layered and represents a significant ongoing burden:

**Royalty**: 5% of Gross Sales, paid weekly via electronic funds transfer. This is the standard rate; the Veterans program reduces it to 2% for six months, and non-traditional locations may negotiate rates from 2%–8%. Actual royalties paid in FY2025 ranged from 2% to 10% of Gross Sales.

**Advertising Contribution**: The base rate is 4.6% of Gross Sales, but the Ad Fund Test Program (mandatory for all new franchisees and renewals) starts at 5.0%. If PLK determines that the 4-Wall EBITDA increased sufficiently after testing at 5.0%, the rate may escalate to 5.25% (as early as May 2026), and then to 5.50% (as early as May 2027). If a test fails, the rate reverts to the last successful level, with a floor of 4.6% (subject to a majority-vote override that could maintain 5.0%). This creates uncertainty for prospective franchisees.

**Co-Op Contributions**: 0.5%–1.75% of Gross Sales additionally, depending on the DMA. The co-op contribution can be increased once per year by a 2/3 vote, and cannot exceed 2% without unanimous consent.

**Consumer Ordering Technology Fee**: $200/month fixed plus 1% of Digital Sales (mobile app, website, kiosk, white label delivery, third-party delivery, loyalty), capped at $6,500/year per restaurant. As digital ordering grows, this fee will approach its cap for many operators.

**Other Recurring**: POP I.T. ($1,980/yr), Training Platform ($720/yr), PLK Foundation ($1,000/yr), Sitewise ($4,545/yr for qualifying franchisees), and various event-driven fees.

**Rent** (if on PLK property): A complex formula with base rent of 10% of capitalized costs (PLK-owned) or 125% of PLK's rent plus 10% of costs (PLK-leased), escalating 12% every five years. Percentage rent kicks in at 8.5% of monthly Gross Sales up to $133,333.33 and 10% above that.

For a restaurant doing $1.9M in annual sales on PLK property, the combined franchisor-directed fees (royalty + advertising + co-op + tech fee + rent) could exceed 20% of Gross Sales before any operating costs.

**Ad Fund Spending (FY2025)**: The advertising fund spent 3% on media production, 71% on traditional and digital media placement, 7% on agency fees, 2% on point-of-purchase materials, and 16% on other expenses including research. None of the Ad Fund is used for franchise solicitation.

### Initial Investment

The initial investment for a free-standing restaurant ranges from $1,222,045 to $3,923,245, excluding real estate. The largest components are the building ($700K–$1.6M), FF&E/signage/technology ($340K–$865K), site work ($40K–$800K), and soft costs ($10K–$420K). The initial franchise fee is a fixed $50,000.

For an in-line restaurant, the total is $504,545 to $1,968,245, with the building component reduced to $100K–$800K and no site work typically required.

Restaurant size ranges from 1,600 to 3,500 square feet. Free-standing locations require 28,000–38,000 square feet of land with 25–30 parking spaces. No part of the initial investment is refundable.

## C. Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Technology

### Supplier Restrictions and Supply Chain

PLK exercises extensive supply chain control. Approximately 95% of all purchases must be from approved or designated suppliers. The supply chain has three tiers:

1. **Trade Secret Products** (sole/designated suppliers only): Food, beverages, ingredients, spices, batter, seasonings, mixes, sauces, chemicals, cleaning supplies. National beverage strategy mandates specific sole beverage suppliers (Coca-Cola, Dr. Pepper/Snapple Group).

2. **Proprietary Products** (approved manufacturers): Food, equipment, uniforms, signs, menu boards, paper goods, packaging bearing Popeyes marks.

3. **Approved Suppliers** (evaluation required): All other items must come from suppliers meeting PLK's standards for quality, capacity, and system efficiency.

The supplier approval process takes 90 days for simple items and up to 6 months for complex food formulas. Franchisees or suppliers pay testing costs up to $5,000. PLK can revoke approval at any time in its sole discretion.

PLK derived $13.7 million in revenue from franchisee purchases in FY2025 (1.7% of total PLK revenues of ~$799M). These come as rebates, flat fees, or below-market products/services. An additional 4.8% of Ad Fund revenue comes from supplier contributions.

All franchisees must join Supply Management Services, Inc. (SMS), a purchasing cooperative that negotiates vendor terms for the system.

### Training Program

The Popeyes Training Program (PTP) is a comprehensive 6–10 week program totaling 115 hours of on-the-job training across six weekly modules:

- Weeks 1–2: Team member production and service, ServSafe certification (35 hours)
- Weeks 3–4: Shift manager production, service, and certification (40 hours)
- Weeks 5–6: RGM training, shadow shift running, supervised shift running, and certification (40 hours)

The fee is $5,000 per participant. First restaurants must train a minimum of 5 management employees; additional restaurants require 3–4. Training must occur at a Certified Training Restaurant under a Certified Training Manager.

Ongoing requirements include maintaining at least 1 RGM and 3 shift managers as Popeyes Certified Managers at all times, with current ServSafe Food Safety Certification. Replacement enrollment within 30 days of any manager departure.

### Technology Stack

The technology requirements are substantial:
- POS System: vendor-approved, $3,000–$6,500/year maintenance
- BOH System: $2,000–$10,000 (server) or cloud subscription, $3,000–$6,500/year
- Kiosk Package: $17,000–$26,000 initial, $1,000–$1,200/year per screen
- Outdoor Digital Menu Boards: $25,000–$70,000, $400–$500/year per lane
- Indoor Digital Menu Boards: $9,000–$20,000, ~$425/year
- Computer System: $800–$3,000
- Training Tablet + Headphones: $500–$1,000
- Managed Firewall: $0–$1,500 initial, ~$3,000/year

PLK can independently poll all POS/BOH data without limitation. There are no contractual limits on the frequency or cost of required technology upgrades.

### Marketing Fund and Advertising

The Ad Fund is nationally administered by PLK. Company-owned restaurants contribute at 4.6% or 5.0%. PLK uses national advertising agencies and various media formats (TV, radio, print, digital, social, mobile, POP). Co-ops may be established at the DMA level, governed by prescribed bylaws. PLK has the power to dissolve or merge co-ops. The Marketing Advisory Council (PIFA-based) is informal and advisory only — PLK can discontinue it at any time.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory

The Protected Area under the Franchise Agreement is defined as the lesser of (a) a 1-mile radius around the restaurant, or (b) an area encompassing 50,000 people (residential and workplace combined). This is relatively narrow for a QSR concept — in densely populated urban areas, the Protected Area could be just a few city blocks.

Critical exclusions from the Protected Area:
- Existing Popeyes Restaurants
- Closed restaurants that may reopen within 3 years
- Previously granted franchise agreements
- All Alternative Venues (ghost kitchens, airports, malls, military bases, educational facilities, hospitals, casinos, stadiums, convention centers, amusement parks)

PLK can periodically reduce the Protected Area for population shifts, though it must always encompass at least 50,000 people if the radius is less than 1 mile.

Under the TRA and MTRA, there is no exclusivity whatsoever. The Development Agreement provides limited protection for specific Target Locations within a Territory, but PLK retains broad rights to operate elsewhere in the Territory.

### Channel Competition

PLK reserves the right to sell POPEYES-branded products through grocery stores, convenience stores, internet, and other direct marketing channels anywhere, including within the Protected Area. Franchisees receive no compensation for such sales and are prohibited from using alternative distribution channels themselves.

### Sister Brand Competition

Burger King, Tim Hortons, and Firehouse Subs restaurants may be located in the same market. All four brands offer some overlapping menu items (sandwiches, fries, desserts, beverages). PLK management "will attempt to resolve" cross-brand conflicts but is not responsible for resolution.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The initial franchise term is 20 years from the date the restaurant opens. At renewal, the franchisee may receive a 10-year renewal term, plus an optional "Supplemental Renewal Term" of 10 additional years.

**Renewal conditions** are extensive: notice, satisfaction of all monetary obligations, compliance with all agreements, execution of a general release of all claims against PLK, refurbishment/renovation/modernization of the restaurant, payment of the renewal fee (pro-rated then-current initial franchise fee), compliance with all operational requirements across all restaurants, no pending or threatened litigation, and meeting PLK's then-current financial ratios.

**Materially different terms risk**: The franchisee must sign PLK's then-current form of Franchise Agreement at renewal, which may contain terms and conditions "materially different from your original Franchise Agreement, including higher royalty fees and/or advertising contributions and different requirements for your Managing Director." This is a significant risk for long-term planning.

### Transfer

All transfers require PLK's prior written consent. Conditions include: payment of all debts, noncompete compliance, general release, qualified transferee approved by PLK, execution of a new franchise agreement and guaranty, restaurant remodeling, training of transferee's personnel, and payment of a $7,500 transfer fee ($1,000 deposit non-refundable if transfer fails).

PLK has a right of first refusal (ROFR) to match any bona fide offer.

In the event of death or disability, the franchisee's interest must be assigned to an approved transferee within 12 months, extendable to 18 months if the heir cannot immediately satisfy transfer conditions.

### Termination

**Curable defaults**: 30 days to cure most defaults; 10 days for non-payment. **Non-curable defaults** (immediate termination): insolvency/bankruptcy, failure to open by deadline, failure to stay open, criminal convictions, threats to health and safety, failure to meet financial ratios, transfer violations, noncompete breach, confidentiality breach, false books/records, repeated failed assessment scores, sale of unapproved products, adverse conduct affecting reputation.

The Development Agreement has additional non-curable defaults including failure to score in the top 50% of peer category and receipt of grade "D" or "F" in operational metrics.

### Noncompete

During the term and for 2 years after termination/expiration/transfer: the franchisee, officers, directors, 10%+ owners, and Managing Director cannot own, operate, or have any interest in a quick-service restaurant specializing in chicken within 10 miles of any Popeyes Restaurant owned.

### Dispute Resolution

All disputes must be litigated (no arbitration) in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Florida, or if that court lacks jurisdiction, the 11th Judicial Court in Miami-Dade County, Florida. Florida law applies. State addenda modify this for Illinois and Washington (subject to state law).

### Litigation History

The FDD discloses 7 pending cases, 4 franchisor-initiated enforcement suits, 3 concluded cases, and multi-jurisdictional no-poach settlements with 13+ states.

Notable pending cases:
- **Syed v. PLK (Ontario)**: CAD$6M sought over franchise closures and alleged unfair actions
- **ADP Direct Poultry v. PLK (Ontario)**: CAD$35M + $1M punitive over supplier termination dispute
- **Mun v. PLK (S.D. Fla.)**: Putative class action over website cookie tracking/privacy
- **Pinnacle Foods v. PLK**: Development agreement breach claim (plaintiff in bankruptcy)

The franchisor-initiated suits are primarily trademark enforcement and post-termination obligation enforcement against former franchisees.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### System Trajectory

The US system grew from 2,946 total outlets at the start of 2023 to 3,229 at the end of 2025 — a gain of 283 over three years. However, the growth rate has decelerated significantly:

| Year | Franchised Opened | Franchised Net | Total Net |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2023 | 165 | +130 | +130 |
| 2024 | 136 | +44 | +101 |
| 2025 | 104 | +55 | +52 |

The 2024 dip in franchised net growth was driven by PLK reacquiring 58 franchised units (primarily in Mississippi: 32, Tennessee: 17, Louisiana: 5). These became company-owned restaurants, so total system net growth was +101.

In 2025, the system experienced its first material terminations in the three-year period: 14 franchised restaurants were terminated. Georgia was the most impacted state, losing 14 net units (6 terminations + 12 ceased operations — other reasons). North Carolina lost 7 net units (3 terminations + 5 ceased).

### Transfer Activity

Transfers nearly doubled: 128 (2023), 125 (2024), 229 (2025). The largest transfer states in 2025 were Illinois (38), California (26), Ohio (23), Michigan (21), and Arizona (16). This acceleration could indicate portfolio consolidation, financial stress among smaller operators, or market maturation.

### Projected Openings

PLK projects 104 new franchised outlets and 0 company-owned outlets for the next fiscal year. Top projected states: Texas (15), Pennsylvania (10), California (9), New York (7).

### Gag Clause

**The gag clause is present.** The FDD states: "In some instances, current and former franchisees sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with Popeyes." These are tied to "settlement agreements, workout agreements and certain amendments to franchise and development agreements." This materially limits a prospective franchisee's ability to conduct due diligence by speaking with current and former operators.

### Franchisee Association

PIFA (Popeyes International Franchisee Association, Inc.) is sponsored and endorsed by PLK. Chairman: David Damato. Executive Director: Jennifer Palmer. Incorporated in Louisiana.
