Pending litigation:

       Khalid Zia Syed, 14111109 Canada Inc., 14111290 Canada Inc., 14111168 Canada Inc, and
141111231 Canada Inc. v. Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen Inc., Rob Manuel, Tariq Shah, Shah & Shah
Lawyers, Khurram Hafeez, Shazia Qureshi, Re/Max Real Estate Centre Inc. and Javid Ali, (File No. CV-
00729046-0000), Ontario Superior Court of Justice , filed on October 23, 2024.

         On October 23, 2024, Plaintiffs, Khalid Zia Syed and four entities he controlled which operated
four Popeyes restaurants in Toronto (The “Toronto Restaurants”), filed a claim against us and other
Defendants. The claim relates to the sale of shares of the franchisee entities that operated the Toronto
Restaurants to Defendants Khurram Hafeez and Shazia Qureshi and the subsequent closure of three of the
four Toronto Restaurants, two of which were permanently closed and the third of which was closed and
subsequently re-opened by a new franchisee following termination of the franchise agreement and lease
(the “Claim”). In addition to us and Rob Manual (GM, PLK Canada), Defendants include Plaintiffs’ co-
owners of the Toronto Restaurants, the lawyer and law firm which acted for Plaintiffs in relation to the sales
and the real estate agent and agency that allegedly acted for both Plaintiffs and the purchasers in the
transactions. While the causes of action primarily relate to the conduct of the other Defendants in connection
with the share sales and subsequent operation and closure of the three restaurants, Plaintiffs allege that PLK
and Manuel without sufficient justification required the closure of one of the Toronto Restaurants following
expiry of a limited license agreement, insisted that Defendant Shazia Qureshi purchase two of the Toronto
Restaurants, unreasonably refused potential purchasers of Plaintiffs’ restaurants, and otherwise engaged in
unfair and discriminatory actions. Plaintiffs seek a declaration that the shares of the franchisee entities were
fraudulently conveyed, damages in amounts ranging from CAD$0.25 million to CAD$6 million against the
non-PLK Defendants and damages of CAD $6 million against us and Manuel.

        Pinnacle Foods of California, LLC v. Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen, Inc., (Case No. 2OSTCV49914),
Superior Court of the State of California for the County of Los Angeles, filed on December 31, 2020
removed on March 5, 2021 to the United States District Court for the Central District of California. (case
no. 2;21-cv-02050) and transferred to the United States District Court for the Southern District of Florida
(case no. 1:21-cv-21555).

         Plaintiff filed a complaint against Popeyes alleging breach of Development Agreement, breach of
the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing for Popeyes’ alleged failure to evaluate and approve
sites under the Development Agreement and for Popeyes’ alleged termination of the Development
Agreement, violation of Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 20020, and violation of Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 17200.
The claims arise from Plaintiff’s belief that Popeyes improperly refused to approve development locations
and that Popeyes improperly terminated the Development Agreement for the Bakersfield and Fresno, CA
markets. Plaintiff ultimately filed an amended complaint alleging breach of contract for PLK’s alleged
refusal to consider sites submitted by Plaintiff and for breach of the implied covenant of good faith and fair
dealing, alleging that PLK improperly utilized its expansion policy, to which Popeyes filed a response. On
April 20, 2023, both parties filed motions for summary judgment. On April 24, 2023, Plaintiff agreed to
pay Popeyes $59,000 in connection with the fees and costs Popeyes incurred in connection with its defense
of a spoliation motion previously filed by Plaintiff. Summary judgment briefing has closed. Plaintiff also
filed a motion in limine which Popeyes opposed, and Popeyes filed a motion to have facts asserted by
Popeyes in support of its motion for summary judgment deemed admitted, which has been opposed by
Plaintiff. The above-described motion for summary judgment and related motions remain pending. On
April 22, 2024, Plaintiff filed a voluntary petition for relief under chapter 11 of the Bankruptcy Code in the
United States District Court for the Eastern District of California, Case No. 24-11015 to 24-11017 (the
“Bankruptcy Case”). On October 30, 2024, the Bankruptcy Court granted the parties relief from the
automatic stay to conclude the litigation. The Bankruptcy Case is currently still pending.
