# Reader Report — Embassy Suites Hotels (638053-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed Sep 2007, subsidiary of Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. (itself a subsidiary of Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc., NYSE: HLT).
2. **Brand:** Embassy Suites by Hilton — upper upscale all-suite hotel segment, operating since 1984 through predecessors.
3. **System size:** 258 Embassy Suites hotels in the US as of 12/31/2024 (237 franchised, ~21 company-managed but 0 company-owned); 220 designated as "Comparable Hotels."
4. **Investment range (176-suite new development):** $50,082,670 to $82,170,820 excluding real property, insurance, interest.
5. **Royalty ramp (new/conv):** 3.5% → 4.5% → 5.5% of Gross Rooms Revenue; 5.5% flat for COO/re-licensing. Program fee: 4% GRR (can increase up to +1% over term).
6. **Item 19 highlights:** Avg Room Rate $184.12 (2024), median $171.32; Avg Occupancy 74.4% (median 75.3%); RevPAR Index 121.7 (beating comp sets by ~22%); only 30.9% of franchisee-managed hotels exceeded system average room rate.
7. **Growth trajectory:** Slow but steady net +1 franchised hotel per year (2022–2024); 29 signed-not-opened, 3 projected openings in next FY.
8. **Term and renewal:** 23 years (new construction), 10–20 years (conversion). **NO renewal right.** Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms.
9. **Territorial protection:** Non-exclusive license is the standard. Optional Restricted Area Provision available (usually shorter than term, with extensive carve-outs).
10. **Disputes:** Venue in Eastern District of Virginia or Fairfax County/NYC. Governing law: New York. No arbitration (except Puerto Rico).
11. **Special risks:** (a) Out-of-state dispute resolution (VA/NY); (b) Principal mark "Embassy" NOT federally registered.
12. **Financial health (Hilton FH LLC):** Revenue $1.502B (2024); net income $1.488B; distributions to parent $1.5B. Essentially a pass-through entity for Hilton franchise royalties.
13. **Key red flags:** No renewal right; extensive fee stack beyond royalty; unregistered principal mark; confidentiality clause on former franchisees; 3 terminations in 2024.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Standard FA:** Non-exclusive license. **No protected territory.**
- **Optional Restricted Area Provision:** Must be negotiated. Typically:
  - Restricted Area defined by local market, not standard formula
  - Restrictive Period shorter than term (tied to years from approval)
  - Extensive exclusions: pre-existing hotels, gaming, timeshare, 4+ hotel acquisitions, other Hilton brands
  - "Embassy Suites" brand only (not other Hilton brands)
  - May reduce in geographic scope over time
  - Historically extended for full term but "we do not intend to do so in the future"
- **Strategic Partnerships** are "Other Businesses" — not restricted by Restricted Area
- **No relocation permitted**
- **No options or ROFR for additional franchises**
- **Encroachment risk:** Hilton may establish other Network Hotels in or near your area; no conflict resolution mechanism

## Pass E: Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction:** 23 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10–20 years from Opening Date
- **COO:** Remaining term or such other term as approved
- **Eforea spa:** Expires with FA or earlier spa termination
- **NO RENEWAL RIGHT.** Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion; may require materially different terms, PIP, new fee structure.

### Termination
**By franchisee:** Not authorized. Unilateral termination = material breach → liquidated damages or actual damages.

**By franchisor without cause:**
- Condemnation: immediate termination, no liquidated damages
- Casualty: hotel must reopen within 18 months or franchisor may terminate; no LD if no competing hotel at site within 3 years

**By franchisor with cause (curable):** 10-day cure for payment/insurance/construction defaults; 30-day cure for other FA breaches.

**Non-curable defaults (immediate termination, 16 grounds):**
1. Repeat non-compliance within 24 months (even if cured)
2. Three notices of material default in 12 months
3. Insolvency/failure to pay debts
4. Bankruptcy filing
5. Loss of possession
6. Failure to operate 5+ consecutive days
7. Contesting System/Marks ownership
8. Conduct adversely affecting reputation
9. Concealing revenues/false records
10. Unauthorized transfer
11. Sanctioned Person status
12. Gaming license disclosure issues
13. Guarantor breach
14. Public health/safety threat
15. Becoming a Competitor
16. Termination of any Hotel Agreement

**Interim remedies on default:** Suspension from Reservation Service, directory removal, software disabling, revocation of financial accommodations.

### Transfer
**Permitted Transfers (no change of control):**
- Without notice: publicly traded equity, <50% private equity (no control change), institutional fund transfers
- With notice + consent + $5,500 fee: affiliate transfers, family/trust, death, >50% cumulative equity change

**Change of Ownership Transfers:**
- 60-day notice + full information package
- Not in default; all amounts paid through closing
- Transferee: must meet new-franchisee standards, submit COO Application, pay $200,000 fee, sign current-form FA, agree to PIP
- Not a Competitor or Sanctioned Person
- SBA financing: must escrow estimated fees

### Noncompete
- **During term:** May not own or be licensor/franchisor of competing brand; may own minority interest in Competitor; may be franchisee of Competitor or manage Competitor property
- **Post-term:** None

### Dispute Resolution
- **Governing law:** New York (without NY choice-of-law principles)
- **Venue:** US District Court, Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria); or state court in Fairfax County, VA or New York, NY; franchisor may sue where hotel is located
- **Arbitration:** None (except Puerto Rico — AAA/JAMS in Fairfax County, seat in NYC)
- **Jury waiver:** Not explicitly stated in Item 17 summary but likely in FA

### Modification
- Changes to FA: must be written and signed by both parties
- Franchisor CAN change Standards, Manual, and other materials unilaterally
