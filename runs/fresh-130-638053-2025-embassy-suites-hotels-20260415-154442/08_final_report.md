# Final Diligence Report — Embassy Suites by Hilton
## FDD Filing 638053-2025 | Issuance Date: March 30, 2025

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and brand position:** Embassy Suites by Hilton operates 258 hotels in the US (237 franchised, ~21 company-managed, 0 company-owned) as of December 31, 2024, competing in the upper upscale all-suite hotel segment. The brand has been operating since 1984 through predecessors and is one of approximately 20 brands in the Hilton Worldwide portfolio.

2. **Entity structure:** The franchisor is Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed in September 2007. Its parent is Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., which is itself a subsidiary of Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). The franchisor entity is essentially a franchise royalty pass-through, collecting $1.5 billion in annual revenue and distributing nearly all of it to its Hilton parent.

3. **Ownership history:** The brand has been continuously offered since 1984, first through Promus Hotels, Inc. (1984–2007), then Embassy Suites Franchise LLC (2007–2015), and finally the current franchisor since March 2015. All predecessor franchise agreements were assigned to the current entity or affiliates.

4. **Investment range:** The total investment for a newly constructed 176-suite Embassy Suites Hotel is $50,082,670 to $82,170,820, excluding real property, market study, insurance, and interest. Construction alone accounts for $36 million to $55 million.

5. **Fee burden:** The royalty ramps from 3.5% to 5.5% of Gross Rooms Revenue over two years for new development/conversion (5.5% flat for change-of-ownership). On top of this, franchisees pay a 4% Monthly Program Fee, 4.3% Hilton Honors fee on eligible guest folios, and extensive technology, distribution, and quality assurance fees. The all-in percentage burden on room revenue exceeds 13% before accounting for technology fees and per-transaction charges.

6. **Item 19 highlights:** Average room rate of $184.12 (2024) with median of $171.32. Average occupancy of 74.4%. The brand significantly outperforms competitive sets with a RevPAR Index of 121.7 (beating competitors by ~22%). However, only 30.9% of franchisee-managed hotels exceed the system average room rate, indicating performance is concentrated among a minority of properties.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** The wide performance range (room rates from $109.57 to $375.87; occupancy from 47.9% to 95.6%; RevPAR Index from 47.1 to 206.7) means bottom-quartile properties face substantially weaker economics. With no cost or profit data disclosed, a prospective franchisee cannot evaluate bottom-quartile profitability from this FDD alone.

8. **Financial health:** The franchisor entity had total revenues of $1.502 billion in 2024 (up 9.1% YoY), net income of $1.488 billion, and distributed $1.5 billion to Hilton affiliates. The entity is fully solvent with $577 million in member's equity, though it holds minimal cash ($317K) and relies on intercompany receivables.

9. **Growth trajectory:** Slow but steady: net +1 hotel per year in each of 2022, 2023, and 2024. Twenty-nine franchise agreements are signed but hotels not yet opened, with 3 projected to open in the next fiscal year. This is a mature, stable system rather than a high-growth one.

10. **Territory:** The standard Franchise Agreement provides NO protected territory. An optional Restricted Area Provision may be negotiated but comes with extensive carve-outs and is typically shorter than the full term.

11. **Renewal:** There is NO renewal right. Re-licensing is at the franchisor's sole discretion and may involve materially different terms.

12. **Disputes:** Venue is Eastern District of Virginia or Fairfax County, VA / New York, NY. Governing law is New York. No arbitration except in Puerto Rico.

13. **Special risks:** (a) The principal mark "Embassy" is NOT federally registered, meaning it has fewer legal protections; (b) out-of-state dispute resolution may increase costs and reduce settlement leverage.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Hilton Franchise Holding LLC is the franchisor for Embassy Suites hotels in the 50 US states, territories, and DC. The entity was formed in September 2007 as a Delaware LLC. It does not directly employ the people who provide franchise services — those are employees of Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. or its affiliates, acting on the franchisor's behalf.

The key service-providing affiliates are:
- **Hilton Reservations Worldwide** (reservations and customer care)
- **Hilton Supply Management (HSM)** (procurement, distribution, purchasing negotiation)
- **Hilton Honors Worldwide** (loyalty program)
- **Hilton Systems Solutions (HSS)** (IT systems, OnQ, HITS Agreement)

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions and Approved Supplier Process

The franchisor requires purchase from designated sources for:
- **OnQ software** (proprietary, from HSS only)
- **Trademarked items** (approved suppliers only)
- **Exterior signage** (licensed vendors only)
- **Eforea spa products** (from HSM, if operating eforea spa)

For all other FF&E, supplies, and equipment, franchisees may purchase from any source meeting Brand Standards. HSM operates as a voluntary group purchasing organization for most items, negotiating bulk pricing and charging 4%–10% procurement fees on top of product costs.

HSM revenue from franchisee-related activities in 2024:
- Rebates/allowances: $19,614,010
- Administrative fees: $38,697,912
- Goods/services sales: $8,763,692

The approval process for alternative suppliers or products typically takes 60–90 days. There is no material benefit (such as license renewal) granted for using designated suppliers.

Hilton's affiliate holds a non-controlling equity interest in Groups360 LLC, a third-party group booking platform.

### Training Program Detail

Thirteen training programs are specified, with the most significant being:
- **Brand Conference:** Annual, varies in location, $2,500/attendee (GM and Director of Sales required)
- **GM/Commercial/Sales Leader Training:** Up to 18 hours virtual, up to $1,200, within 90 days of start
- **Hilton Core Sales Skills:** 24 hours virtual, up to $600, 6-week or 2-week program depending on experience
- **Owner Orientation:** 16 hours virtual, no fee, required for new franchisees 12–18 months before opening
- **New to Hospitality:** 20 hours virtual, up to $2,500, for first-time hospitality operators

Most operational training programs (HPMS, OnQ, Hilton Honors, ADA, compliance, Make a Difference) carry no separate fee. Total direct training costs are modest relative to the overall investment.

### Technology Stack and Costs

The required technology infrastructure is substantial:
- **OnQ (PMS/reservations/revenue management):** $70.6K–$151.2K initial; $1,735–$4,128/month maintenance; must refresh every 3 years
- **Guest Internet Access (StayConnected):** $84.5K–$123.5K initial; $1,950–$4,900/month circuit + $744–$1,408/month service; refresh every 4 years
- **Connected Room:** $40.2K–$48K initial; $721–$1,062/month maintenance
- **GRO (revenue optimization via IDeaS):** $3,800 setup; no ongoing fee currently
- **Delphi (sales & events):** $900–$41K setup; $858/user/year
- **Digital Floor Plan:** $2,500 one-time
- **Digital Key:** No separate charge (part of OnQ)

Estimated annual technology costs (post-opening): $50,000–$90,000+ depending on hotel size and configuration.

### Marketing Fund Spending and Audit Status

The Monthly Program Fee (4% of GRR) is not a trust or dedicated advertising fund. The franchisor has sole discretion over how funds are spent. The fee covers:
- Reservation Service development and maintenance
- Brand directories and internet sites
- Advertising, promotion, publicity, PR, market research
- Quality assurance programs
- Technology programs
- Administrative overhead

The franchisor is not obligated to make expenditures proportionate to any individual franchisee's payments. No advisory council is currently active. There is no independent audit requirement for Program Fee spending.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition

The standard Franchise Agreement contains **NO protected territory**. You receive a non-exclusive license to operate at a specified location only.

An optional **Restricted Area Provision** may be negotiated on a case-by-case basis. When granted, it typically:
- Covers an area around the hotel defined by market conditions (not a standard formula)
- Lasts for a **Restrictive Period shorter than the franchise term** (tied to years from approval)
- May reduce in geographic scope over time
- Historically has been extended for the full term, but the franchisor states it does **not intend to do so in the future**

### Carve-Outs and Exclusions

Even with a Restricted Area, the franchisor retains the right to:
- Operate pre-existing hotels within the area
- License gaming-oriented or timeshare properties under the brand
- Acquire chains of 4+ hotels and operate them within the area under any brand
- Operate hotels under any OTHER Hilton brand (Hampton, DoubleTree, Hilton Garden Inn, etc.) within the area
- Enter into Strategic Partnerships whose participants may operate within the area

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights

- Franchisee must use the Hilton Reservation Service exclusively for outgoing referrals
- May not own or maintain any websites/domains related to the hotel or brand without approval
- May not cross-market with non-Network hotels without consent
- Must participate in all required loyalty, discount, and promotional programs
- Franchisor may require participation in new distribution programs at any time

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

| Type | Term |
|------|------|
| New Construction | 23 years from Effective Date |
| Conversion | 10–20 years from Opening Date |
| Change of Ownership | Remaining term or as approved |
| Eforea Spa | Expires with FA or earlier |

**There is NO renewal right.** If you wish to continue operating after the term expires, you must apply for re-licensing at the franchisor's sole discretion. Re-licensing:
- Requires signing a new franchise agreement (which may have "materially different terms")
- Requires compliance with any PIP and performance conditions
- Requires payment of a $100,000 re-licensing application fee
- Is not guaranteed in any way

This is a significant risk for a franchisee investing $50M–$82M+ in a property. The 23-year new construction term provides reasonable amortization, but the lack of any renewal right means the franchisee has no assurance of continued brand affiliation at term end.

### Transfer Provisions

**Permitted Transfers** (no change of control) are relatively straightforward:
- Publicly traded equity, small private equity stakes, and institutional fund transfers can proceed without notice
- Affiliate, family, trust, and death transfers require 60-day notice, consent, and a $5,500 fee

**Change of Ownership Transfers** are more restrictive:
- $200,000 application fee
- 60-day notice with full information package
- Transferee must meet all new-franchisee standards
- Transferee must sign the then-current franchise agreement form
- Transferee must agree to PIP upgrades
- All amounts due must be paid through closing
- Any pending litigation must be resolved or adequately secured

The franchisor has no right of first refusal to purchase but must be notified if the franchisee markets equity interests.

### Termination and Death/Disability

The franchisor can terminate with cause for:
- **Curable defaults** (10-day cure for payment/insurance; 30-day cure for other breaches)
- **Non-curable defaults** (16 grounds for immediate termination, including repeat non-compliance, 3 notices in 12 months, bankruptcy, loss of possession, 5+ days cessation, contesting marks, reputation harm, false records, unauthorized transfer, Sanctioned Person status, Competitor status)

**Interim remedies** during default include suspension from Reservation Service, directory removal, software disabling, and revocation of financial accommodations.

On death of a franchisee or equity owner, the interest may transfer to an immediate family member or entity formed by family members, provided they meet approval requirements within one year.

### Noncompete Restrictions

**During term:** You may not own or be the licensor/franchisor of a competing hotel brand. However, you MAY:
- Own a minority interest in a Competitor
- Be a franchisee of a Competitor
- Manage a property of a Competitor

**Post-term:** No noncompete restrictions.

### Dispute Resolution

- **Governing law:** New York (without NY choice-of-law principles)
- **Venue:** US District Court, Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria); or state court in Fairfax County, VA or New York, NY
- **Franchisor advantage:** May sue where hotel is located
- **Arbitration:** None (except Puerto Rico: AAA/JAMS in Fairfax County, seat in NYC)
- **Mediation:** Not required

Note: Multiple states (California, Michigan, Illinois, Minnesota, etc.) have addenda that may override venue, choice-of-law, or other provisions to the extent required by state franchise law.

### Litigation History

**Pending (5 actions):**
- Four antitrust class actions alleging improper data sharing through revenue management software providers (IDeaS, STR/CoStar, Amadeus) to set room rates. These are industry-wide matters affecting multiple hotel chains.
- One breach of contract action (AAAA Property Partners) arising from franchise termination.

**Concluded (6 actions):**
- Texas AG: $2.1 million settlement on mandatory guest fee disclosure (January 2025)
- Nebraska AG: $300,000 settlement on same issue (February 2024)
- Portland Hotel Ownership: $30M counterclaim settled with mutual releases (July 2022)
- San Pedro Inn: wrongful termination claim settled, franchise terminated without damages (September 2020)
- Soule: Hawaii resort fee class action settled for $178,000 (August 2015)
- 1970 Sherman Act consent decree (historical, anti-competitive supplier practices)

No collection suits were filed against franchisees in 2024.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Pass-through entity:** HFH operates as a royalty collection vehicle for Hilton Worldwide. Operating expenses are negligible ($11.4M on $1.5B revenue = 0.76% expense ratio). Nearly all income flows through to the parent via distributions.

2. **Revenue growth:** Total revenue grew 9.1% YoY ($1.376B → $1.502B), driven primarily by royalty fee growth ($1.331B → $1.434B, +7.8%). This reflects both rate increases across the system and the small net addition of hotels.

3. **Termination fee spike:** Termination fees surged from $2.4M (2023) to $18.3M (2024), a 7.5x increase. This corresponds to the 3 terminations in 2024 (vs. 0 in prior years) and suggests significant liquidated damage collections.

4. **Distributions exceed net income:** In 2024, Hilton distributed $1.5B against $1.488B of net income, drawing slightly from retained earnings. In 2022, distributions of $1.55B exceeded net income of $1.233B by $317M.

5. **Minimal cash:** The entity holds only $317K in cash, relying entirely on the intercompany receivable structure with Hilton affiliates ($703.9M current receivable from affiliates).

6. **Franchise contract intangible growth:** The franchise contracts (net) asset grew from $238.5M to $392.7M, reflecting a $85.3M acquisition of franchise contract intangible assets in 2024. This may relate to the purchase of franchise contracts from another entity or brand portfolio reorganization.

7. **Credit loss provision increasing:** Allowance for credit losses on AR grew from $7.3M to $10.4M, and the provision charge increased from $4.4M to $6.2M, suggesting some deterioration in franchisee payment performance.

8. **Deferred revenue growth:** Total deferred revenues (current + non-current) grew from $524.2M to $558.9M, representing upfront fees that will be recognized over franchise terms.
