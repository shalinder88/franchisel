# 02 — Reader Report
## McDonald's USA, LLC — FDD (638437-2025)
## Issuance: May 1, 2025, as amended February 1, 2026

---

## A. Executive Snapshot

McDonald's USA, LLC is a Delaware LLC, wholly-owned subsidiary of McDonald's Corporation. The FDD covers the granting of franchises to operate quick-service restaurants under the McDonald's System in the United States. As of December 31, 2024, the system comprised 13,559 total outlets: 12,887 franchised and 672 company-owned. Approximately 95% of U.S. restaurants are franchised to independent operators; ~5% are operated by wholly-owned McOpCo subsidiaries.

The franchise model is distinctive: McDonald's controls site selection, owns or leases the real estate, constructs the building, and then sublease/franchises the completed unit to the operator. The franchisee has no role in site selection. The traditional franchise term is 20 years, but there is no contractual right to renew or extend — new terms are offered at McDonald's sole discretion under the New Term Policy (Exhibit K).

Total initial investment for a traditional restaurant ranges from $1,471,000 to $2,728,000 (STO/STR: $1,014,500–$1,793,500; Satellites: $525,000–$1,193,500). The initial franchise fee is $45,000 for traditional, $22,500 for STO/STR, and $0–$500 for Satellites. Royalty is 4% or 5% of Gross Sales depending on timing and circumstances, with the 5% rate applying to new restaurants, McOpCo sales, and right-of-first-refusal transactions from January 1, 2024 onward.

The FDD includes an Item 19 financial performance representation. The average annual sales volume for traditional franchised restaurants open at least 1 year was $3,966,000 in 2024 (median $3,797,000). Three pro forma statements at $3M, $3.2M, and $3.4M sales levels show operating income before occupancy costs of $734,000 (24.5%), $812,000 (25.4%), and $879,000 (25.9%) respectively.

Financial statements are audited by Ernst & Young LLP (report dated March 14, 2025). McDonald's USA, LLC reported total revenues of $10.63 billion in 2024 and net income of $3.46 billion.

## B. Pass 1 — Front Matter, Formats, Fee Stack, Initial Investment

**Front matter.** The cover page identifies three format types: traditional, Satellite (including Walmart), and STO/STR. Special risks (p.4) highlight out-of-state dispute resolution in Illinois. Michigan-specific franchise law provisions are reproduced on pages 5–6.

**Formats.** McDonald's offers franchises in five configurations: (1) Traditional full-menu freestanding restaurants (20-year term); (2) Satellite locations in Walmart, airports, malls, hospitals, universities (term varies); (3) Small Town Oil (STO) — full-menu restaurants sharing space with fuel station/convenience stores (10-year term); (4) Small Town Retail (STR) — anchoring small retail centers in rural communities (10-year term); (5) Business Facilities Lease (BFL) — special 3-year arrangements with conditional option to purchase.

**Fee stack.** The recurring fee structure is complex:
- Royalty: 4% or 5% of Gross Sales (see note 2, p.21, for which rate applies).
- Rent: Combination of Monthly Base Rent + Pass Thru Rent + Percentage Rent. For traditional restaurants opening on or after January 14, 2026, Percentage Rent is generally 6%–23% of Gross Sales, with escalating rates reaching a minimum of 11.50% by year 8.
- Advertising: Minimum 4% of Gross Sales annually. OPNAD contribution rate currently 2.25% of sales. Local cooperatives set their own rates.
- Technology fees: Extensive list of annual fees totaling approximately $11,000+ per year (Sesame POS $1,133, Global Mobile App $664, McDelivery POS $620, Kiosk $558, HHOT $50, eProduction $47, Back Office $545, Payments/Fraud $740, Employee Engagement $385, PDW Reporting $167, McD Connect $250, Restaurant Employee Experience Survey $62, Deployment/Support $2,529, Network Management $1,134, Hardware/Data $951, RFM $690, Microsoft License $707, Pricing Engine $264, GRNT $185, Edge $435, Store Mail $56.40).
- One-time technology fees: Sesame $2,600, Kiosk $1,500, HHOT $500, DMB 2.0 $264, Edge $120.
- Audit fee: Cost of audit if understatement ≥2% of Gross Sales.
- Satellite annual fee: $500–$2,500.
- Relocation contribution: $50,000.

**Effective rent range.** For franchised restaurants in 2024, the effective rent percentage ranged from 0.00% to 33.33% (Item 19 note 3).

**Co-investment policy.** McDonald's offers optional co-investment in building/site improvements for reductions in Percentage Rent and Monthly Base Rent. Two options exist for new traditional restaurants opened on/after January 14, 2026, with co-investment floors of 11.50% and 12.50% respectively. Cost is generally not less than $30,000 per 0.25% ("Quarter") reduction.

**Initial investment (Item 7).** The traditional range of $1,471,000–$2,728,000 includes: franchise fee $45,000; rent (3 months) $0–$313,000; signs/seating/equipment/decor $1,100,000–$1,785,000; opening inventory $20,000–$39,000; miscellaneous $54,000–$66,000; travel $2,000–$41,000; additional funds (3 months) $250,000–$439,000. Store Systems cost $150,000–$250,000. Of 31 McOpCo transactions in 2024, 9 exceeded the high end of the initial investment range, one by as much as $2,035,816.

## C. Pass 2 — Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Reporting, Technology

**Supplier control (Item 8).** McDonald's does not generally sell food/supplies directly to franchisees, but requires use of approved suppliers only. Detailed food specifications are confidential. Suppliers must meet quality, production, integrity, and financial standards. McDonald's may negotiate favorable terms with suppliers on behalf of the system. Approximately 90%–95% of establishment purchases and 55%–65% of ongoing operating purchases must come from McDonald's or approved sources. In 2024, McDonald's and its predecessor received $39,008,767 in loan guarantee service fees, cashless incentives, and beverage supplier rebates. Total revenue from sale/lease of real estate and services to franchisees was ~$7.21 billion (68% of total revenue).

**Operations (Item 11).** McDonald's selects the site, negotiates purchase/lease, constructs/prepares the premises, and delivers the completed unit to the franchisee. Franchisees install signs, equipment, and fixtures at their own cost. McDonald's provides periodic consultation via operations consultants, printed reports, seminars, newsletters, emails, and online resources. The O&T Manual contains required operations procedures, inventory control, bookkeeping, business practices, and management policies.

**Training (Item 11).** The Candidate Development Program includes self-study modules, coaching, facilitated courses, and equipment training. Core training spans from Crew Development through Restaurant Leader, culminating in the Leading Great Restaurants Class at Hamburger University in Chicago (32 hours over 4 days). Total training hours include substantial in-restaurant time. Training takes 6 months to 2 years to complete. McDonald's bears HU overhead costs; franchisees pay travel/living. Franchisees must complete all training before signing the Franchise Agreement. No compensation during training. Instructor experience averages 15+ years. Head of L&D is Bethany Tate Cornell, SVP and Chief Learning & Development Officer since November 2020.

**Reporting.** Franchisees must submit monthly financial statements electronically through the Franchisee Financial System (FFS), including consolidated balance sheet, G&A expense statement, consolidated debt summary, and individual restaurant P&L statements.

**Technology (Item 11, Item 6).** Store Systems cost $150,000–$250,000, including POS (Sesame), Cashless System 3.0, indoor/outdoor digital menu boards, kiosks, table locators, hardware, and software. Sesame is proprietary to McDonald's predecessor. McDonald's has independent access to transaction-level data with no contractual limits. Gift Card System is managed by P2W, Inc. NFP (independent non-profit with board including McDonald's employees and franchisees). There are no contractual limitations on frequency or cost of hardware/software upgrades.

## D. Pass 3 — Territory, Channels, Encroachment

**Territory (Item 12).** The franchise is a limited grant at a specific address only. There is no exclusive territory, no protected area, and no right to exclude or impose conditions on future McDonald's development. McDonald's may establish other outlets that compete with the franchisee's location. McDonald's reserves the right to use the Marks in any channel of distribution (e.g., McCafe coffee sold in supermarkets and via e-commerce). Internal development policies are not part of the Franchise Agreement and do not create contract rights.

**Encroachment risk.** The FDD explicitly states that other McDonald's restaurants may affect the franchisee's sales and customer trading patterns, and that these patterns do not represent any continuing entitlement. This is a significant encroachment exposure.

## E. Pass 4 — Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

**Term.** Traditional: generally 20 years. STO/STR: generally 10 years. Satellite: varies. BFL: generally 3 years (with conditional option to purchase after year 1 for up to 20-year extension).

**Renewal.** There is no right to renew or extend. The New Term Policy (Exhibit K, effective January 1, 2025) is not part of the Franchise Agreement and is subject to change at McDonald's sole discretion. If a new term is not offered, the franchisee may sell during the remaining term, and a qualified buyer may receive a new-term agreement.

**Transfer.** Requires McDonald's approval. Right of first refusal (Section 15(c)) allows McDonald's to match any offer for the franchise. Conditions include: transferee qualifies; royalty increases to current rate; transferee assumes full liability; transferor remains personally liable for remaining term; no current breach.

**Termination.** Only for cause. Non-curable defaults include: failure to maintain cleanliness/standards; bankruptcy; non-payment within 30 days; unsatisfied judgment >$5,000 for >30 days; loss of possession; violation of franchise restrictions; unauthorized products; unauthorized transfer; denying McDonald's access; misrepresentation; conduct damaging reputation; felony conviction; intentional sales under-reporting; repeated breaches.

**Post-termination.** 18-month noncompete within 10 miles. Must sell furniture/fixtures/signs/equipment to McDonald's at fair market value (no intangible asset payment). Must return manuals and cease using McDonald's System/trademarks.

**Noncompete.** During term: no involvement in competing business. After termination/expiration: no competing business for 18 months within 10 miles.

**Dispute resolution.** Choice of law: Illinois. No mandatory arbitration or mediation provision in the Franchise Agreement. However, as common practice, McDonald's will often agree to mediation. No choice-of-forum provision specified.

**Death/disability.** May be assigned to approved purchaser or qualified spouse/heirs/nearest blood relative. McDonald's will work with spouse for up to 18 months to attempt qualification.

**Financing (Item 10).** McDonald's predecessor may guarantee loans from Bank of America, N.A. for remodeling, acquisitions, BFL option exercise. Rate: Term SOFR + 0.10% + 3.00% (APR 7.42% as of March 17, 2025). Terms 3–5 years, 7-year amortization. Guarantee fee 1.50% of average outstanding balance. Personal guarantee from franchisee and spouse required. Default on loan = default under Franchise Agreement. Franchisee must sign Operator Assistance Program Agreement (waiving all claims against McDonald's).

## F. Pass 5 — Items 18–23, Exhibits, Unresolveds

**Item 18 — Public figures.** McDonald's does not use any public figure to promote the franchise.

**Item 19 — Financial performance.** Comprehensive disclosure covering ~12,572 traditional restaurants open ≥1 year. Key metrics for all traditional restaurants: average sales $4,002,000, median $3,838,000, range $914,000–$19,680,000. For franchised-only: average $3,966,000, median $3,797,000. For McOpCo: average $4,793,000, median $4,612,000. Pro forma statements are based on 11,332 independent franchisee traditional restaurants. Operating income before occupancy costs: $734K at $3M sales (24.5%), $812K at $3.2M (25.4%), $879K at $3.4M (25.9%). ~76% of restaurants exceeded $734K OIBOC, ~69% exceeded $812K, ~62% exceeded $879K. Royalties were 4% during the covered period. Note: occupancy costs are excluded because of wide rent variation (effective rent 0%–33.33%).

**Item 20 — Outlets.** Net system growth of +102 units in 2024 (franchised +115, company-owned -13). 167 franchised openings, 29 terminations, 15 non-renewals, 8 reacquired by franchisor. 843 transfers between franchisees in 2024. Projected openings for next fiscal year: 181 franchised + 14 company-owned = 195 total. Franchisee organizations: 6 McDonald's-endorsed (NFLA, AMOA, MHOA, NBMOA, WON, OPN) plus 1 independent (NOA).

**Item 21 — Financial statements.** Audited by Ernst & Young LLP. Consolidated balance sheets as of December 31, 2024 and 2023. Statements of income, members' equity, and cash flows for years ended December 31, 2022–2024. Total assets $22.195 billion. Total revenues $10.631 billion (2024). Net income $3.462 billion (2024). Members' equity $10.222 billion. Net property and equipment $13.080 billion.

**Item 22 — Contracts.** Exhibits B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, J, M.

**Item 23 — Receipts.** Two copies (pp.388–389).

**Litigation (Item 3).** Six pending cases disclosed, including: (1) Deslandes class action re: no-poach/anti-solicitation clause (since 2017, survived Supreme Court denial of certiorari); (2) Turner — similar no-poach allegations; (3) Crawford — racial discrimination by 77 former Black franchisees (severed into two related cases); (4) Kytch — ice cream machine device dispute (trade libel, tortious interference); (5) Michell — Hispanic franchisee discrimination (Connecticut); (6) Williams — E. coli class action from Quarter Pounder outbreak; (7) Le — McRib deceptive marketing. Also extensive additional labor/joint-employer lawsuits referenced. Concluded cases include multiple settlements: Lentini ($22M), Byrd ($6.5M), Washington ($33.5M), Ochoa ($3.75M), Quijano ($6.3M), AA&S Food ($15.8M), Tavarua ($15.6M), Kim ($10K), among others.

**Bankruptcy (Item 4).** None to disclose.

**Exhibits.** 20 exhibits (A through T). Three separate franchise agreement forms (Traditional, Satellite, Walmart). The Operator's Lease (Exhibit G, 33 pages) is incorporated into each Franchise Agreement. Exhibit N (Loan Documents) is extensive at 35 pages, including the Unlimited Guaranty. Exhibit R (Franchisee List) spans ~144 pages. Exhibit T covers state-specific addenda for CA, HI, MD, MN, ND, WA, plus state effective dates.

**State addenda (Exhibit T).** California: Fast Food Act (AB 1228) disclosures. Hawaii: filing notice. Maryland: release limitations, bankruptcy enforceability, forum rights under MD Franchise Law. Minnesota: trademark protection commitment, 90-day termination/60-day cure/180-day non-renewal notice requirements. North Dakota: non-compete unenforceability notice. Washington: Assurance of Discontinuance re: no-poach clause, plus consistency with WA Franchise Investment Protection Act.
