## Brand Overview

McDonald's USA, LLC (Delaware LLC, wholly-owned subsidiary of McDonald's Corporation) is the world's largest quick-service restaurant franchisor in the United States. As of December 31, 2024, the system comprises 13,559 total outlets (12,887 franchised, 672 company-owned), with ~95% operated by independent franchisees. The franchise dates to 1955 when McDonald's Corporation began granting franchises.

## Franchise Model Summary

McDonald's operates a distinctive real-estate-driven franchise model. The company selects, acquires or leases, and develops each restaurant site, then licenses the completed unit to the franchisee via a Franchise Agreement and Operator's Lease. Franchisees have no role in site selection and no ownership interest in the real estate.

**Formats offered:**
- Traditional: 20-year term, full-menu, $1.47M–$2.73M total investment
- STO (Small Town Oil): 10-year term, full-menu, shared with fuel station, $1.01M–$1.79M
- STR (Small Town Retail): 10-year term, rural retail center, $1.01M–$1.79M
- Satellite: Variable term, Walmart/airports/malls/hospitals, $525K–$1.19M
- BFL: 3-year with purchase option, special-purpose

## Fee Architecture

The fee structure layers royalty, rent, advertising, and technology fees:

| Fee | Rate/Amount |
|-----|-------------|
| Royalty | 4% or 5% of Gross Sales (5% for new restaurants from Jan 2024) |
| Percentage Rent (new traditional) | 6%–23% of Gross Sales, escalating to min 11.50% by year 8 |
| Monthly Base Rent | Based on McDonald's investment with finance factor |
| Advertising minimum | 4% of Gross Sales annually |
| OPNAD | Currently 2.25% of sales |
| Technology fees (annual, mandatory) | ~$11,000+ |
| Technology fees (one-time) | ~$5,000 |

Effective rent for franchised restaurants in 2024 ranged from 0.00% to 33.33% of Gross Sales. The total occupancy burden (rent + royalty) is substantial and highly variable by location.

## Contract Highlights and Risk Factors

1. **No renewal right.** The New Term Policy (Exhibit K) is discretionary, not contractual.
2. **No exclusive territory.** McDonald's may develop competing outlets at any time.
3. **Real estate control.** McDonald's owns/leases all sites. Franchisee cannot retain location after termination/expiration.
4. **Right of first refusal.** McDonald's can match any sale offer.
5. **Noncompete.** 18 months, 10-mile radius post-termination.
6. **Choice of law.** Illinois.
7. **Financing.** McDonald's Corporation guarantees Bank of America loans (APR 7.42%), but loan default = Franchise Agreement default, and Operator Assistance Program Agreement requires waiving all claims against McDonald's.
8. **Gag clauses.** Some franchisees restricted from speaking about experience.
9. **Technology burden.** No contractual limits on upgrade frequency or cost. McDonald's has independent, unlimited access to transaction-level data.

## Litigation Risk

Significant pending litigation including racial discrimination (Crawford — 77 former Black franchisees), no-poach class actions (Deslandes, Turner — survived Supreme Court), E. coli class action (Williams), and various employment/joint-employer lawsuits. Notable concluded settlements include Washington ($33.5M), Lentini ($22M), Tavarua ($15.6M), AA&S ($15.8M).

## State Addenda Summary

California: Fast Food Act compliance. Maryland: franchise law protections. Minnesota: 90-day termination / 180-day non-renewal notice. North Dakota: non-compete likely unenforceable. Washington: no-poach clause removed per Attorney General AOD.
