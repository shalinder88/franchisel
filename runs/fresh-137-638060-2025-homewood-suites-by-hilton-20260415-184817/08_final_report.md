# Final Diligence Report: Homewood Suites by Hilton
## FDD Filing 638060-2025 | Issuance Date: March 30, 2025

---

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and structure:** 518 franchised hotels in the US at year-end 2024, with zero company-owned properties. The system grew by +7 net units in 2024, +3 in 2023, and +9 in 2022. There are 142 franchise agreements signed but not yet open, with 17 projected new openings in the next fiscal year. This is a mature, steadily growing extended-stay brand.

2. **Entity structure:** The franchisor is Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed in September 2007. Its parent is Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., a Delaware corporation, which in turn is a subsidiary of Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). Hilton Worldwide is the ultimate parent, a public company. The franchisor is a disregarded entity for US tax purposes and operates as a conduit for franchise fees flowing to the Hilton parent structure.

3. **Ownership history:** Homewood Suites hotels have been franchised since 1988, first by Promus Hotels' predecessors, then by Promus Hotels Inc. (1988–2007), then by Homewood Suites Franchise LLC (2007–2015), and finally by the current franchisor since March 30, 2015. Hilton Hotels Corporation (later Hilton Worldwide Inc., then Park Hotels & Resorts Inc.) was the ultimate parent from 1999 to 2017, when the current Hilton Worldwide structure was established through a spin-off.

4. **Initial investment:** $20,824,955 to $32,522,797 for a newly constructed 131-suite hotel, excluding real property, market studies, insurance, and interest. Of this, up to $267,019 is paid to the franchisor or its affiliates. The single largest component is construction and leasehold improvements ($14.5M–$22.0M).

5. **Fee burden:** The ongoing fee structure is substantial. At steady state (year 3+), franchisees pay a Monthly Royalty Fee of 5.5% of Gross Rooms Revenue (GRR) plus a Monthly Program Fee of 3.5% GRR, for a core burden of 9.0% of GRR. Adding the mandatory Hilton Honors program fee (2.5% of eligible guest folio), the effective ongoing fee rate approaches 11.5% of GRR before accounting for technology fees, distribution charges, quality assurance fees, and numerous other program fees detailed in Item 6.

6. **Item 19 highlights:** For 2024, Comparable Hotels (437 of 518) reported an average room rate of $160.03, average occupancy of 79.4%, and an average RevPAR Index of 123.0 (meaning the brand outperforms its competitive sets by 23% on average on a revenue-per-available-room basis). No EBITDA, cost build-up, or profit/loss data is disclosed.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** While not explicitly reported in quartile format, only 35.1% of franchisee-managed hotels met or exceeded the average room rate in 2024 (the median was substantially lower at $150.86 vs. average $160.03, indicating right-skew). Room rate range spans from $68.00 to $308.08, a 4.5× spread. Occupancy ranged from 50.5% to 97.1%.

8. **Financial health of franchisor:** The franchisor reported $1.502 billion in revenue and $1.488 billion in net income for 2024, with an expense ratio of just 0.76%. However, this entity is not a standalone operation — it is a pass-through that distributes $1.5 billion annually to Hilton affiliates, holds only $317K in cash, and has all assets pledged as collateral for Hilton's corporate debt. The franchisee's relationship is effectively with Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. and its financial strength, not with this LLC.

9. **Growth trajectory:** Positive and stable. The system has grown from 499 to 518 hotels over 2022–2024. The pipeline of 142 signed-but-not-open agreements suggests continued expansion. Termination rate is very low (0.97% in 2024).

10. **Territory:** No protected territory by default. Restricted Area Provisions may be negotiated but come with extensive carve-outs covering pre-existing hotels, all other Hilton brands, gaming/timeshare properties, and any acquisition of 4+ hotel chains.

11. **Renewal:** The franchise agreement is non-renewable. There is no right to renew or extend. Re-licensing is at the franchisor's sole discretion and may require materially different terms and compliance with a new Property Improvement Plan.

12. **Disputes and litigation:** Multiple pending antitrust class actions against Hilton Worldwide alleging Sherman Act violations through participation in data-sharing arrangements with IDeaS, STR (CoStar), and Amadeus that allegedly facilitated room rate-fixing. These cases are in early stages.

13. **Special risks:** (1) Dispute resolution requires Virginia venue with New York governing law; (2) the principal mark "Homewood" lacks federal registration — if challenged, franchisees may need to rebrand at their own expense.

14. **Confidentiality clauses:** "During the last 3 fiscal years, some current and former franchisees have signed provisions restricting their ability to speak about their experience with us." This limits a prospective franchisee's ability to conduct due diligence by speaking with former franchisees.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
All franchisees must build, design, furnish, equip, and supply their hotels per the Standards compiled in the Manual. Required purchases represent approximately 15-20% of establishment cost and 2-5% of operating expenses.

**Sole source/mandatory:**
- OnQ proprietary software from HSS (no alternative exists)
- Exterior signage from franchisor-licensed vendors
- Participation in Hilton Honors program
- Use of Reservation Service

**Optional but recommended:**
- HSM procurement services (4-10% procurement fee on project cost)
- In 2024, HSM earned $19.6M in rebates/allowances, $8.8M in goods/services revenue, and $38.7M in administrative fees across all Hilton brands
- Hilton and affiliates collectively earned $1.1 billion from franchisee purchases in 2024

**FRCM Program:** Franchisor may impose fixed renovation cycles requiring periodic upgrades to maintain Standards, at franchisee's expense, with no cap on frequency or cost.

### Training Program
14 training programs are specified, ranging from Brand Conference (varies, annually required for GM and DOS) to HPMS Training (1-5 hours, online, no fee). Fees range from $0 to $2,500 per attendee per program. Key mandatory programs:
- GM/Commercial/Sales Leader Training: up to 18 hours virtual, $1,200, within 90 days of start
- Hilton Core Sales Skills: 24 hours virtual, $600, within 90 days
- Revenue Management @ Work: 16 hours, $600, within 120 days
- Owner Orientation: 16 hours virtual, no fee, 12-18 months pre-opening
- New to Hospitality Owner Education: 20 hours virtual, up to $2,500

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
**The standard franchise agreement provides NO protected territory.** Franchisees receive a non-exclusive license to operate at a specified location. They may face competition from other franchisees, from hotels owned by Hilton affiliates, and from other Hilton brands.

### Restricted Area Provision
The franchisor may agree to a Restricted Area Provision for new development or conversion (but not normally for change of ownership or re-licensing). If granted:
- The Restricted Area boundaries vary by hotel and market circumstances
- The Restrictive Period is shorter than the franchise term (usually tied to years from application approval)
- The restriction only prevents another Homewood Suites by Hilton hotel within the Restricted Area during the Restrictive Period

### Carve-outs from Restricted Area
Even when a Restricted Area is granted, it does NOT protect against:
- Pre-existing hotels already in the system or approved for development
- Any replacement hotel for an existing hotel
- Hotels under ANY other brand (including other Hilton brands)
- Gaming-oriented hotels
- Timeshare/vacation ownership properties
- Any acquisition of a chain of 4+ hotels (Hilton can acquire competitors and operate them in your area)
- Hotels that will open after the Restrictive Period expires

### Channel Restrictions
- May not register or maintain any websites/domains relating to the Brand without approval
- Must use Reservation Service as sole outgoing reservation channel
- Must participate in Hilton Honors and all required programs
- May not engage in cross-marketing with non-Network hotels without consent
- Franchisor and affiliates may operate competing brands anywhere, including within any Restricted Area
- No conflict resolution mechanism exists

### Strategic Partnerships
Hilton has Strategic Partnerships with Small Luxury Hotels of the World, Autocamp, Evermore Resort, and Field Station. These are "Other Businesses" under the FA and are excluded from territorial protections. Guests can make reservations through Hilton's system at these properties.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction:** 22 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10–20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership:** Remaining term or as agreed
- **The franchise agreement is NON-RENEWABLE.** There is no right to renew or extend. Re-licensing requires a new application ($100,000 fee), the franchisor's sole-discretion approval, and may involve materially different terms, new PIP requirements, and the then-current form of franchise agreement. This is a significant "materially different" risk.

### Transfer Provisions
- **Permitted Transfers** (no change of control): publicly traded equity transfers, minority private equity transfers, transfers to affiliates/family/trusts — some require 60-day notice, consent, and a $5,500 processing fee
- **Change of Ownership Transfers:** require 60-day notice, $200,000 application fee, full compliance with then-current requirements, new franchise agreement, and possible PIP
- **No right of first refusal** by franchisor; **no option to purchase**
- Transferee may not be a Competitor or Sanctioned Person

### Termination and Death/Disability
- **By franchisee:** Not authorized — constitutes material breach plus liquidated damages
- **By franchisor with cause:** Payment defaults (10-day cure), construction delays (10-day), insurance failure (10-day), other defaults (30-day cure)
- **Immediate termination (no cure):** Repeated same non-compliance in 24 months; 3 defaults in 12 months; insolvency; loss of possession 5+ days; contesting marks; conduct harmful to brand reputation; fraud; unauthorized transfer; becoming a Competitor; guarantor breach; public health threat
- **Death:** Transfer to immediate family without consent; family must meet all requirements within 1 year

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term:** Cannot own or be licensor/franchisor of a competing brand; CAN be a franchisee or manager of a competitor and can hold minority interests
- **Post-term:** NO post-term noncompete covenant

### Dispute Resolution
- **Governing law:** New York (without NY conflict-of-law principles)
- **Venue:** US District Court, Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria); or state courts in Fairfax County, VA, or New York, NY. Franchisor may sue where the hotel is located.
- **Arbitration:** Only in Puerto Rico
- **State overrides (per Exhibit J-1):**
  - Michigan: Out-of-state litigation prohibited
  - Minnesota: Out-of-state litigation and liquidated damages prohibited; jury trial waiver prohibited
  - North Dakota: ALL liquidated damages deleted; out-of-state venue deleted; jury trial waiver deleted
  - Rhode Island: Out-of-state venue/governing law void for RI Act claims
  - Washington: Arbitration/litigation must be in WA for WA-purchased franchises

### Litigation History
**Pending (5 cases):** Four antitrust class actions alleging hotel room rate-fixing through data-sharing intermediaries (IDeaS, STR/CoStar, Amadeus); one franchise termination dispute.

**Concluded (5 cases):** Texas AG settlement ($2.1M) and Nebraska AG settlement ($300K) over mandatory guest fee disclosure practices; Hawaii class action ($178K + vouchers) over resort fee disclosure; two franchise disputes settled without damages.

### Key Financial Observations
1. **Asset-light model:** $1.27B in assets generates $1.49B in net income — an ROA exceeding 117%.
2. **Pass-through entity:** Expenses are only 0.76% of revenue. The entity exists primarily to collect franchise fees and pass them upstream.
3. **Massive intercompany receivable:** $703.9M "other receivables due from Hilton affiliates" represents amounts owed by the parent structure under the centralized cash management system — not controlled by this entity.
4. **Distributions exceed net income:** $1.5B distributed in 2024 vs. $1.488B net income. In 2022, $1.55B was distributed against $1.233B net income, actively drawing down retained earnings.
5. **Minimal cash:** Only $317K on hand at year-end 2024 — this entity is not a standalone going concern.
6. **All assets pledged:** Per Note 7, all assets and franchise contracts are pledged as collateral for Hilton's debt obligations maturing 2025–2033.
7. **Franchise contract intangibles:** Increased from $238.5M to $392.7M in 2024, primarily due to the ~$85M acquisition of Graduate brand franchise contracts.
8. **Revenue growth:** Franchise royalty fees grew 7.8% in 2024 and 12.4% in 2023.
9. **Termination fee spike:** $18.3M in termination fees in 2024 vs. $2.4M in 2023, consistent with 5 terminations that year.
10. **Disregarded entity:** For US tax purposes, all taxable income flows through to Hilton — no separate tax provision.
