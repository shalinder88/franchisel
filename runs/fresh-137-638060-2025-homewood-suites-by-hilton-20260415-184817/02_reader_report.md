# Reader Report: Homewood Suites by Hilton FDD (638060-2025)

## Pass A — Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand:** Homewood Suites by Hilton — upper midscale extended-stay hotel brand featuring suites with separate kitchens and living rooms, targeting guests staying 5+ consecutive nights.
2. **Franchisor entity:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 2007; became franchisor of Homewood Suites in the US on March 30, 2015.
3. **Parent chain:** Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. → Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). Blackstone Group's Jonathan D. Gray serves as Board Chairman.
4. **System size:** 518 franchised hotels at year-end 2024, up from 499 at year-end 2022 (+19 net). Zero company-owned hotels. 142 franchise agreements signed but not yet open; 17 projected new openings in next fiscal year.
5. **Initial investment:** $20,824,955–$32,522,797 for a newly constructed 131-suite hotel, excluding real property, market studies, and insurance.
6. **Franchise application fee:** $100,000 (new development/conversion); $200,000 (change of ownership); $100,000 (re-licensing).
7. **Ongoing fee burden:** Monthly Royalty Fee of 3.5%/4.5%/5.5% GRR (years 1/2/3+ for new development); Monthly Program Fee of 3.5% GRR; plus Hilton Honors at 2.5% of eligible folio, plus numerous tech, distribution, and quality assurance fees.
8. **Item 19 highlights:** Average room rate $160.03 (2024); average occupancy 79.4%; RevPAR Index average 123.0 (outperforming competitive set). No EBITDA or cost build-up disclosed.
9. **Financial health:** $1.488B net income in 2024 on $1.502B revenue; distributions to Hilton affiliates of $1.5B. Total assets $1.27B; member's equity $577M.
10. **Growth trajectory:** Positive — net +7 hotels in 2024, +3 in 2023, +9 in 2022. Very low termination rate (5 in 2024 out of 518).
11. **Territory:** Non-exclusive; no protected territory by default, though Restricted Area Provisions may be negotiated. Massive carve-outs even when granted.
12. **Renewal:** No renewal right. Franchise is non-renewable. Re-licensing is at franchisor's sole discretion and may involve materially different terms.
13. **Disputes:** Virginia venue, New York law. Multiple pending antitrust class actions against Hilton Worldwide (IDeaS/STR data-sharing allegations).
14. **Special risks:** (1) Out-of-state dispute resolution; (2) "Homewood" mark lacks federal registration.

## Pass D — Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

- No options, ROFR, or similar rights for additional franchises

## Pass E — Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction:** 22 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10-20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership:** remaining term or as agreed
- **NO renewal or extension right.** Franchise agreement is non-renewable.
- Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion; may require materially different terms and PIP conditions

### Termination
- **By franchisee:** Not authorized. Unilateral termination = material breach + liquidated damages
- **By franchisor without cause:** Condemnation; casualty requiring 18+ month closure
- **By franchisor with cause (curable):** payment defaults (10-day cure), failure to open on time (10-day), insurance failure (10-day), other franchise agreement breaches (30-day cure)
- **By franchisor with cause (non-curable, immediate):** repeated same non-compliance in 24 months; 3 default notices in 12 months; insolvency/bankruptcy; loss of possession for 5+ days; contesting marks; conduct adversely reflecting on brand; fraud; unauthorized transfer; becoming a Competitor; breach by guarantor; public health threat

### Transfer
- **Permitted Transfers** (no change of control): publicly traded equity, minority private equity, transfers to affiliates/family/trusts, transfers on death — some require 60-day notice + consent + $5,500 fee
- **Change of Ownership Transfer:** 60-day notice; $200,000 application fee; transferee must meet all current requirements; new franchise agreement at then-current terms; PIP may be required
- **No right of first refusal, no option to purchase**
- Death: transfer to immediate family without consent, but family must meet requirements within 1 year

### Non-Competition
- **During term:** Cannot own or be licensor/franchisor of competing brand (but may be franchisee or manager of competitor, or hold minority interest)
- **Post-term:** No post-term non-compete

### Dispute Resolution
- **Choice of law:** New York (without NY conflict-of-law principles)
- **Venue:** US District Court, Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria), or state court in Fairfax County, VA, or New York, NY. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Arbitration:** Only in Puerto Rico (AAA/JAMS, Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Fairfax County venue, New York seat)
- **No mediation requirement in standard agreement**
