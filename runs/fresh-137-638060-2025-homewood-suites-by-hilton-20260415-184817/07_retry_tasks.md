## R1 — Connected Room Fee Verification
**Status:** EXECUTED
**Severity:** MEDIUM
**Finding:** The Item 6 table states "$564 to $860 per guest room per month" but Item 11 (p. 54) clarifies: "currently costs between $564 and $860 per month ($6,768 to $10,320 per year)." The annualized figures confirm this is a **per-hotel monthly fee**, not per-room. The Item 6 table header is misleading — "per guest room per month" likely refers to the fact that the cost varies based on the number of guest rooms at the hotel, not that each room incurs a separate charge.
**Resolution:** Connected Room maintenance = $564–$860/month per hotel ($6,768–$10,320/year). Canonical updated.

## R2 — Exhibit J-1 State Addenda (Full)
**Status:** EXECUTED
**Severity:** MEDIUM
**Finding:** Complete state addenda extracted from pp. 272-279. States with addenda:

1. **California:** Liquidated damages may be unenforceable (Civil Code 1671); termination/non-renewal governed by Bus. & Prof. Code 20000-20043; venue limitation may not be enforceable; general release does not waive rights under Franchise Investment Law.
2. **Hawaii:** General release does not relieve franchisor from liability under Hawaii franchise law; 7-day disclosure requirement.
3. **Illinois:** Termination/renewal rights affected by Illinois law (815 ILCS 705/19-20); governing law/venue/jury waiver provisions do not waive Illinois Franchise Disclosure Act rights.
4. **Maryland:** State law may supersede FA on termination/renewal; bankruptcy termination may not be enforceable; may sue in Maryland for MD Franchise Law claims (3-year statute); no waiver of MD Franchise Law rights via release.
5. **Michigan:** Prohibits unfair provisions including: termination without good cause (30-day cure required), refusal to renew without fair compensation (if term <5 years), out-of-state arbitration/litigation, refusal to permit transfer without good cause.
6. **Minnesota:** 90-day termination notice (60-day cure) and 180-day non-renewal notice required; liquidated damages prohibited; out-of-state litigation prohibited; jury trial waiver prohibited; general release does not relieve MN franchise law liability.
7. **New York:** Franchisor assignment must be to willing/able assignee; NY law governs NY Franchise Law causes of action; general release in renewal/transfer may not be enforceable; injunctive relief requires proper proofs.
8. **North Dakota:** All liquidated damages provisions deleted; North Dakota law supersedes; out-of-state venue deleted; jury trial waiver deleted; general release does not relieve ND franchise law liability.
9. **Rhode Island:** Bankruptcy termination may not be enforceable; release does not waive RI Franchise Investment Act rights; out-of-state venue/governing law provisions void for RI Act claims.
10. **Virginia:** Unlawful to cancel franchise without reasonable cause per VA Retail Franchising Act.
11. **Washington:** State law may supersede FA on termination/renewal; release waiver only effective after negotiated settlement with independent counsel; WA law prevails in conflicts; transfer fees limited to reasonable costs; arbitration/litigation in WA for WA-purchased franchises; noncompete void if employee earns <$100K or independent contractor earns <$250K; no-poach restrictions void.

## R3 — Financial Statement Notes
**Status:** EXECUTED
**Severity:** LOW
**Finding:** Key items from Notes to Financial Statements (pp. 133-137):

- **Note 1 (Organization):** Entity is franchisor for all Hilton brands in US + territories + Thailand (for post-Oct 2007 contracts). Licenses IP from affiliate royalty-free.
- **Note 2 (Accounting):** Revenue from franchise royalty fees = variable consideration (% of GRR); sales/COO fees = fixed, recognized over contract term; termination fees recognized when received.
- **Note 3 (Acquisition):** In May 2024, acquired Graduate brand franchise contracts (~$85M intangible asset, 15-year amortization). This explains the $85.3M increase in franchise contract intangibles.
- **Note 4 (Franchise Contracts):** Net carrying value $392.7M at Dec 31, 2024. Amortization of contract acquisition costs was $16.1M (2024), $12.9M (2023), $12.0M (2022) — recognized as reduction to royalty fees.
- **Note 6 (Income Taxes):** Entity is a disregarded entity for US tax purposes — all taxable income/expense flows through to Hilton. Only foreign (territorial) taxes apply.
- **Note 7 (Commitments):** All assets and franchise contracts pledged as collateral for Hilton's debt obligations (maturing 2025-2033). No guarantee liability recorded.
- **Note 8 (Related Party):** Intercompany balances result from Hilton's centralized cash management (not controlled by the Company). $1.5B settled in 2024 with simultaneous $1.5B distribution to parent. Operator agreement with Hilton affiliate — no compensation paid in 2024, 2023, or 2022. Graduate acquisition paid by affiliate, recorded as short-term note (settled Feb 2024).
- **Note 9 (Subsequent Events):** Evaluated through March 18, 2024 (note: appears to be a typo — should be March 18, 2025).
