## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: 23 total outlets as of December 31, 2024 — 17 franchised and 6 affiliate-owned (KDKC, Inc.). Small niche system operating since 2002 in the B2B cookie gift/appreciation market.

2. **Entity structure**: Cookie Advantage, Inc. is an Oklahoma corporation formed October 15, 2001 by Duane and Kim Carns. The Carns family also owns KDKC, Inc., which operates the affiliate outlets and serves as sole approved supplier for packaging and custom software. CAI is a pure franchisor — it does not directly operate any outlets.

3. **Ownership history**: Family-founded and family-operated for 23+ years. No changes in ownership, no predecessors, no parent entities. Duane Carns (President) and Kim Carns (VP/Secretary/Treasurer) are the only disclosed executives.

4. **Investment range**: $92,550 to $171,250 total. $37,400 to franchisor/affiliate. The wide range ($78,700 spread) is primarily driven by equipment costs ($24,950 to $80,000 for used vs. new).

5. **Fee burden**: 6% royalty with $800/month minimum after month 12 + $100/month technology fee. No advertising fund. Transfer fee $2,900. Renewal fee $2,900. CPI-adjusted fixed fees.

6. **Item 19 highlights**: Average franchised outlet gross revenue $369,522 (2024). Median $260,925. Heavy right-skew — only 4 of 17 (24%) met or exceeded the average. Range from $50,198 to $1,231,278. No cost data disclosed. Revenue-only FPR.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: The lowest-performing outlet ($50,198 gross revenue) faces an effective royalty rate of ~19% due to the $800/month minimum. At this revenue level, after the technology fee ($1,200/year), the franchisee's total franchisor-owed fees alone would be approximately $10,800/year — over 21% of gross revenue before any operating costs.

8. **Financial health**: Stockholders' equity $78,724 (Dec 31, 2024). Total assets $148,196. Net income $23,630. Clean audit opinion. Virginia addendum explicitly notes investment range exceeds stockholders' equity. The franchisor's equity base is thin relative to the franchisee's required investment.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Zero new franchise openings in three years (2022-2024). Two franchisees reacquired in 2024 (Adcock — Oklahoma and Texas outlets). Affiliate-owned outlets grew from 3 to 6 over the same period. The franchise side of the system is contracting while the affiliate side is expanding. Five new franchised outlets projected for 2025 — ambitious given the recent trajectory.

10. **Territory**: Protected but not exclusive. Franchisor retains broad rights to internet sales, national accounts, alternative channels, and M&A. Population-based (up to 1.5M). No sales quota to maintain territory.

11. **Renewal**: One 5-year successor term only (total possible relationship: 10 years). Must sign then-current agreement which "may differ materially." Renewal fee $2,900.

12. **Dispute resolution**: Mediation then binding arbitration, both in Bixby, Oklahoma. Oklahoma law. Jury trial and punitive damages waived. 1-year statute of limitations. Multiple states override these provisions via addenda.

13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution, spousal guaranty liability, mandatory minimum royalty payments. These are flagged on the FDD's special risks page.

14. **Litigation/Bankruptcy**: None. Clean record.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Cookie Advantage, Inc. operates as a pure franchisor. All operational outlets are run by either independent franchisees (17) or the affiliate KDKC, Inc. (6). The corporate structure creates an unusual dynamic: the franchisor (CAI) collects franchise fees and royalties, then pays approximately 89% of its total revenue to the affiliate (KDKC) as an "Administrative fee" for management services, office space, production facilities, and day-to-day operations. In substance, CAI functions as a licensing entity while KDKC performs the operational functions of a franchisor.

The Carns family owns both entities, and the entities share a principal business address. This related-party structure means that the franchise system's true operating economics are largely captured at the KDKC level, not the CAI level. A prospective franchisee should understand that CAI's financial statements reflect only the licensing/franchising function, with the bulk of economic activity flowing through the affiliate.

### Fee Stack

**Initial fees**:
- Initial Franchise Fee: $34,900 (lump sum, non-refundable, uniform)
- Initial Packaging: $2,500 (to affiliate KDKC, non-refundable)
- Total initial payment to franchisor/affiliate: $37,400
- Note: No IFFs were collected in FY2024, which is consistent with zero new franchise openings.
- Fee deferral required in Illinois, Maryland, North Dakota, and South Dakota.

**Ongoing fees**:
- Royalty: 6% of Gross Revenue per month (first 12 months); greater of $800/month or 6% per month (after month 12). Due by the 15th.
- Technology Fee: $100/month (current), due with royalty. Covers Cookie Advantage Business Software, website hosting. May increase.
- Total ongoing minimum to franchisor: $10,800/year ($9,600 royalty + $1,200 technology) after month 12.

**Event-driven fees** (from Item 6, 19 fee types total):
- Transfer: $2,900 ($1,000 non-refundable deposit). Waived for entity/family transfers.
- Renewal: $2,900.
- Unauthorized Advertising: $500/occurrence.
- Audit: $1,000-$15,000 estimated, plus understated amounts plus 1.5%/month interest.
- Additional Training: $250/day + expenses.
- Late Payment: 1.5%/month or max by law.
- NSF/Returned Check: $100/occurrence.
- Late Report: $100/month + interest.
- Management Fee: $500/day + costs (if franchisor manages due to breach/absence/death).
- Broker Fees: actual cost on transfers.
- Payment Service Fee: up to 4% for credit card payments.
- Conference Fee: then-current (not currently charged).
- Manual Replacement: $500.
- Supplier/Product Evaluation: $100-$500.
- Customer Issue Resolution: reasonable costs.
- Indemnification: varies.
- Professional Fees: varies.

**Fee adjustment mechanism**: Fixed-dollar fees subject to CPI adjustment if CPI increases more than 5% since last adjustment. Maximum one adjustment per year. 60 days written notice.

**Gross Revenue definition**: Broadly defined — includes all revenues from all sales, gift certificate redemption value, business interruption insurance proceeds. Excludes: taxes, customer refunds, valid discounts. Does NOT exclude credit card fees, returned checks, or reserves for bad accounts.

### Initial Investment Breakdown

The total investment of $92,550 to $171,250 breaks down into:
- **To franchisor/affiliate** (21-40% of total): $37,400 (IFF + packaging)
- **Equipment** (27-47% of total): $24,950-$80,000. This is the largest variable component. Includes commercial oven, depositors, refrigerator, mixer, racks, and related bakery equipment. Used equipment at low end, new at high end.
- **Real estate-related** (5-5% of total): $4,400-$9,000 (lease + deposits + improvements). Based on Tulsa, OK market for 1,000-1,200 sq ft retail strip mall location.
- **Working capital** (11-12% of total): $10,000-$20,000 (additional funds + miscellaneous).
- **Other** (16-14% of total): inventory, computer, insurance, professional fees, advertising, training travel.

No financing offered by franchisor. No note, lease, or obligation guarantees.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
Territory is defined by geographic area and population characteristics, with franchisor using "commercially reasonable efforts" to grant only one franchise per area with up to 1.5 million people. Territory boundaries are delineated by physical description and/or map in Attachment 1 to the Franchise Agreement.

### Carve-outs and Reserved Rights
The territory is protected but NOT exclusive. The franchisor retains broad reserved rights:
1. Own/franchise outside territory regardless of proximity (even if it affects your business).
2. Sell via alternative channels (TV, mail order, catalog, wholesale, internet) within or outside territory.
3. Use different marks within territory.
4. Purchase/merge with competing businesses (newly acquired businesses in territory won't operate under Cookie Advantage marks).
5. Service national accounts within territory.
6. Implement multi-area marketing programs.
7. License technology to non-franchisees inside and outside territory.
8. Engage in any business activities not expressly prohibited by FA.

**Internet**: Exclusively reserved by franchisor. Franchisee may not independently market on internet or conduct e-commerce.

### National Accounts
Franchisor retains all rights to national accounts (customers with business in 2+ territories or regional/national chains with 3+ locations). If a national account is in franchisee's territory and doesn't specifically request CAI or another franchisee, franchisee has 5-day right of first refusal. Franchisee receives no compensation if franchisor or another party services national account in their territory.

### Channel Restrictions
Franchisee cannot sell outside territory. Cannot use internet, catalog, telemarketing, or other direct marketing to sell outside territory. May only solicit within territory boundaries.

### Territory Continuation
Not dependent on sales volume, market penetration, or other contingency (only FA compliance). No right to acquire additional franchises. No ROFR on existing franchise sales.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 5 years from Effective Date.
- **Renewal**: One 5-year successor term. Total possible relationship: 10 years.
- **Renewal conditions**: (a) Timely written notice; (b) compliance with current FA; (c) sign then-current franchise agreement and ancillary documents — "provisions of which may differ materially from those contained in the Franchise Agreement"; (d) sign release (if law allows); (e) update appearance and Equipment to current new-franchisee standards; (f) pay $2,900 renewal fee.
- **Materially different terms risk**: Explicitly stated. This means renewal is not guaranteed on current terms.

### Transfer Provisions
- Transfer requires prior written approval for: FA, business, any right to profits/losses/capital appreciation, substantially all assets, any ownership interest.
- Transfer fee: $2,900 ($1,000 non-refundable deposit). Waived for entity/family transfers.
- 17 transfer conditions including: current compliance, all amounts paid, transferee qualifies and not a competitor, training completed, lease transferable, sign current FA (may differ materially), sign release, guaranty, ROFR offered, upgrade/remodel with deposit, post-transfer noncompete for seller, reimburse transfer costs including broker fees.
- ROFR: Franchisor may match any offer for the business or ownership interest.
- Purchase option on termination: Franchisor may purchase business within 30 days after termination.

### Termination and Death/Disability
- **Curable defaults**: Health/safety (3 days), monetary (10 days), standards/operations (20 days).
- **Non-curable defaults**: 15 categories including material misrepresentation, 3+ NSF checks/year, failure to open on time, abandonment, unapproved transfer, felony, failure to maintain insurance, unauthorized disclosure, bankruptcy, repeated defaults (even if cured), 3+ report failures in 12 months.
- **Post-termination obligations**: Pay all amounts through scheduled expiration (accelerated royalties); de-identification; return Confidential Information; deliver customer files; comply with noncompete. This is notable — royalties are owed for the remainder of the term even after termination.
- **Death/disability**: Representative must transfer within 6 months. Must appoint approved manager or franchisor may assume management.

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term**: No diverting business; no ownership interest in or services for competitive business anywhere.
- **Post-term**: No competitive business within 50-mile radius from (i) franchisee's business, (ii) franchisee's territory, and (iii) all other Cookie Advantage businesses, for 2 years. Tolling provision extends the restricted period by any violation duration. Post-term scope is quite broad — the 50-mile radius from "all other Cookie Advantage businesses" effectively creates a nationwide restriction given the system's geographic spread across 13+ states.
- **State overrides**: California (may not be enforceable), Indiana (modified to comply with IC 23-2-2.7-1(9)), Michigan (limited by franchise law), Minnesota (subject to MN law), North Dakota (subject to ND Century Code 9-08-06), Washington (void if employee earnings under $100K or independent contractor under $250K annualized per RCW 49.62).

### Dispute Resolution
- **Mediation**: Non-binding, in Bixby, Oklahoma. Must be concluded within 60 days. No arbitration until mediation termination date passes.
- **Arbitration**: Binding, AAA or franchisor-selected service, in Bixby, Oklahoma. Federal Rules discovery available. Costs shared; prevailing party recovers actual costs. Each party must file compulsory counterclaims or they are forever barred.
- **Exception**: Marks/Confidential Information disputes — franchisor may seek immediate injunctive relief.
- **Forum**: Bixby, Oklahoma (subject to state law overrides).
- **Governing law**: Oklahoma (subject to state law overrides).
- **Waivers**: Jury trial waived. Punitive/exemplary damages waived.
- **Statute of limitations**: 1 year from discovery or when should have been discovered.
- **State overrides**: Illinois (venue outside IL void for litigation), Indiana (franchise law disputes in Indiana), Michigan (no arbitration/litigation outside Michigan), Minnesota (no litigation outside MN; no jury waiver; no liquidated damages), North Dakota (dispute resolution/choice of law may not be enforceable), Rhode Island (venue/law restrictions void for RI Franchise Investment Act claims), Washington (arbitration/mediation in WA or mutually agreed location).

### Litigation History
No litigation required to be disclosed (Item 3). Clean record.

### Distribution Analysis
- The average ($369,522) is 42% above the median ($260,925), indicating significant right-skew from one or more high-performing outliers.

- The range from lowest ($50,198) to highest ($1,231,278) is a 24.5x spread.

### Cost Build-up and EBITDA
**Not disclosed.** The FPR explicitly states: "The financial performance representations above do not reflect the costs of sales, operating expenses, or other costs or expenses that must be deduced from the gross revenue or gross sales figures to obtain your net income or profit."

No COGS, labor costs, rent, supplies, or any operating expense data is provided. No EBITDA or margin data. This is a revenue-only FPR.

### Critical Caveats
1. **Revenue only**: No cost, margin, or profitability data. Franchisees and former franchisees are cited as potential sources of cost information.
2. **Population**: All 17 franchised outlets included — no exclusions. This is a positive (no cherry-picking).
3. **Timing**: Calendar year 2024 (matches fiscal year).
4. **Unaudited**: "The numbers have not been audited but we have no reason to doubt their accuracy."
5. **Substantiation**: "Written substantiation for the financial performance representation will be made available to prospective franchisees upon reasonable request."
6. **No company-owned data**: The 6 affiliate-owned outlets are not included.
7. **Minimum royalty impact**: At the lowest revenue level ($50,198), the $800/month minimum royalty ($9,600/year) represents a 19.1% effective rate — over 3x the stated 6% rate. Adding the $1,200 technology fee brings franchisor-owed obligations to $10,800/year or 21.5% of gross revenue.
