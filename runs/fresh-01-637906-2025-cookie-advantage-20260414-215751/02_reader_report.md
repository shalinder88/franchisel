# Reader Report: Cookie Advantage, Inc. — 637906-2025

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: 17 franchised outlets + 6 affiliate-owned outlets = 23 total as of December 31, 2024. Small, mature system with no new franchise openings in 2022-2024. Two reacquisitions by franchisor in 2024. Projecting 5 new franchised outlets in 2025.
2. **Entity structure**: Cookie Advantage, Inc. (Oklahoma corporation, formed October 15, 2001). One affiliate, KDKC, Inc., operates 6 company-type outlets and is the sole approved supplier of packaging and custom software. Principals Duane Carns (President) and Kim Carns (VP/Secretary/Treasurer) are co-founders and co-owners of both entities.
3. **Business model**: B2B cookie gift/appreciation marketing — franchisees sell cookie delivery programs to businesses, bake and ship via USPS from approved retail bakery locations (1,000-1,200 sq ft).
4. **Investment range**: $92,550 to $171,250 total. Initial franchise fee $34,900. Packaging from affiliate $2,500.
5. **Ongoing fee burden**: 6% royalty on gross revenue (minimum $800/month after month 12), $100/month technology fee, no advertising fund.
6. **Item 19**: FPR provided. Average franchised outlet gross revenue $369,522 (2024). Median $260,925. Range $50,198 to $1,231,278. Only 4 of 17 (24%) met or exceeded average. Heavy right-skew.
7. **Financial health**: Total stockholders' equity $78,724 (Dec 31, 2024). Net income $23,630 (2024). Clean audit opinion from Morse & Co. (Tulsa, OK). Virginia addendum notes investment range exceeds stockholders' equity. S-corp — no income tax provision. $69,472 in advances from affiliate as sole liability.
8. **Growth trajectory**: Zero new franchise openings in three years (2022-2024). Two franchisees reacquired in 2024 (one Oklahoma, one Texas). Net loss of 2 franchised outlets. Affiliate grew from 3 to 6 outlets. System is contracting on the franchise side.
9. **Territory**: Protected but not exclusive. Franchisor reserves internet, national accounts, alternative distribution channels, and right to operate outside territory. Population-based up to 1.5M. No sales quota to maintain territory.
10. **Renewal**: One 5-year successor term only. Must sign then-current agreement which "may differ materially." Renewal fee $2,900.
11. **Noncompete**: During term — no competitive business anywhere. Post-term — 50-mile radius from business, territory, and all other Cookie Advantage businesses for 2 years.
12. **Dispute resolution**: Mediation then binding arbitration in Bixby, Oklahoma. Oklahoma law applies. Jury trial waiver.
13. **Special risks flagged**: Out-of-state dispute resolution, spousal liability (guaranty), mandatory minimum royalty payments.
14. **Litigation/Bankruptcy**: None required to be disclosed (Items 3 and 4).

## Pass E: Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 5 years.
- **Renewal**: One 5-year successor term.
- **Renewal conditions**: Timely notice; compliance with current FA; sign then-current FA (may differ materially); update Equipment to new standards; pay $2,900 renewal fee; sign release (if law allows).
- **Material difference risk**: "You may be asked to sign a contract with materially different terms."

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: If franchisor materially fails to comply and doesn't cure within 30 days after written notice, franchisee may terminate effective 30 days after additional written notice.
- **By franchisor without cause**: Not applicable.
- **By franchisor with cause — curable defaults**: 3 days (health/safety), 10 days (monetary), 20 days (standards/operations).
- **Non-curable defaults**: Material misrepresentation; 3+ NSF checks/year; failure to open on time; failure to use required supplies; failure to complete training; abandonment; unapproved transfers; felony conviction; failure to maintain insurance; unauthorized behavior; unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information; failure to pay taxes; repeated defaults (even if cured); bankruptcy; termination of other agreement with franchisor; failure to submit reports 3 times in 12 months.

### Post-Termination Obligations
- Pay all outstanding amounts including balance of royalties through scheduled expiration.
- Complete de-identification (signs, Marks, trade dress).
- Return Confidential Information.
- Deliver complete customer files.
- Comply with noncompete.
- Notify telephone company; transfer/forward numbers at franchisor's direction.
- Refund to customers entitled to refunds.

### Transfer
- No transfer without prior written approval.
- Transfer fee: $2,900 ($1,000 non-refundable deposit).
- Waived for entity/family member transfers.
- Conditions: current compliance; all amounts paid; transferee qualifies and not a competitor; training completed; lease transferable; sign current FA; sign release; guaranty; ROFR offered; reimburse transfer costs including broker fees.
- Transferee must upgrade/remodel and deposit estimated cost with franchisor.
- Post-transfer noncompete applies to seller.
- **ROFR**: Franchisor may match any offer.
- **Purchase option on termination**: Franchisor may purchase business within 30 days after termination.

### Death/Disability
- Representative must transfer interest within 6 months.
- Must appoint manager who completes training, or franchisor may assume management.

### Noncompete
- **During term**: No diverting business; no interest in competitive business anywhere.
- **Post-term**: 2 years, 50-mile radius from (i) franchisee's business, (ii) franchisee's territory, and (iii) all other Cookie Advantage businesses. Tolling provision extends restricted period by duration of any violation.

### Dispute Resolution
- Mediation first (non-binding, in Bixby, OK, within 60 days).
- Binding arbitration (AAA or franchisor-selected, in Bixby, OK). Federal Rules discovery available. Costs shared equally; prevailing party recovers actual costs.
- Exception: Marks/Confidential Information disputes — franchisor may seek injunctive relief.
- **Choice of forum**: Bixby, Oklahoma (subject to state law).
- **Choice of law**: Oklahoma (subject to state law).
- Jury trial waiver.
- Punitive damages waiver.
- 1-year statute of limitations for claims.

### Not Directly Surfaced / Unresolveds
1. **Missouri 2023 data anomaly**: Table 3 shows Missouri 2022 and 2024 data but uses "2021" instead of "2023" for the middle row. This appears to be a typographical error in the FDD. The data row shows 1/0/0/0/0/0/1, consistent with a stable outlet. Severity: low.

This is the dominant cost line and represents a related-party transaction where virtually all franchise revenue is transferred to the affiliate. The economic substance of this arrangement is unclear — is CAI merely a shell entity with all operations performed by KDKC? Severity: medium.
5. **Minimum royalty impact on low performers**: The lowest-grossing outlet ($50,198) would face an effective royalty rate significantly above 6% due to the $800/month minimum ($9,600/year = 19.1% effective rate). Severity: medium.
