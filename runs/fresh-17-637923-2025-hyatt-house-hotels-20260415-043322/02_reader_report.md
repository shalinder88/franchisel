# Reader Report — Hyatt House Hotels FDD (637923-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: Hyatt House Franchising, L.L.C. (Kansas LLC), subsidiary of Hyatt Hotels Corporation (NYSE: H). Formed as SF Hotel Company, L.P.; converted through Summerfield Suites → Hyatt Summerfield Suites → Hyatt House (rebranded September 2011).
2. **System size**: 118 Brand Hotels at end of 2024 (109 franchised, 9 company-owned/managed). 58 franchise agreements signed but not yet opened. Net +2 franchised in 2024.
3. **Concept**: Upscale, extended-stay hotels offering studio, 1-bedroom, and 2-bedroom suites with complimentary breakfast, WiFi, fitness center, pool, meeting space. Primary and secondary markets.
4. **Investment range**: $20,168,455 to $57,134,018 (excluding real estate). Sized for 90-200 guest rooms. $158,770 to $884,817 paid to franchisor/affiliates.
5. **Fee burden**: Royalty 5% of GRR + Commercial Services Fee 3.5% of GRR + World of Hyatt 4% of eligible revenue + Digital Acquisition Fee 1.35% of GRR through digital channels + GDS ~$8.50/reservation + mandatory contracts $1,500-$7,500/month + other corporate services $500-$5,000/month.
6. **Item 19**: FPR provided. 115 Covered Hotels. Average occupancy 75.0%, ADR $169.63, RevPAR $127.19 (all). Franchised: 75.1% occ, $167.22 ADR, $125.55 RevPAR. Smith Travel indices above 100 across all metrics. WoH drives 60% of revenue, 51.3% of room nights. Revenue-only FPR — no cost/profit data disclosed.
7. **Financial health (parent)**: Hyatt Hotels Corporation — 2024 revenue $6.65B, net income $1.30B ($12.65/share diluted), total assets $13.3B, stockholders' equity $3.55B, total debt $3.81B, cash $1.01B. $110M goodwill impairment in management/franchising segment.
8. **Franchise term**: 20 years from opening. Successor franchise 10 years on then-current terms (which may be materially different).
9. **Territory**: Area of Protection with AOP Term (typically 3 years post-opening). After AOP Term expires, no territorial protection. Exception during AOP for acquisitions of 4+ hotel groups.
10. **Dispute resolution**: Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, Illinois. Illinois governing law.
11. **Noncompete**: No post-term noncompete. During term, franchisee/management company may not be a "Competing Brand Owner."
12. **Liquidated damages**: Lesser of 36 months or remaining term × 8.5% (5% royalty + 3.5% commercial services) of Average Monthly Revenue. Consequential termination: 150% multiplier. $5,000/day for failure to de-identify.
13. **Growth trajectory**: Steady growth. 108→110→116→118 total outlets over 2022-2024. Low attrition (2 terminations in 2024). 58 signed but not opened shows strong pipeline.
14. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Illinois). Michigan franchise law provisions. System Standards comprise 3,615 pages — significant operational control.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Area of Protection**: Negotiated before signing. Defined by geographic references or radius. Urban: as small as a few blocks. Suburban: up to ~3 miles from entrance.
- **AOP Term**: Starts at FA effective date, typically ends 3 years after Hotel's opening date.
- **Protection during AOP**: Neither franchisor nor affiliates will open or authorize another Brand Hotel with physical premises within AOP. One exception: acquisition of 4+ hotel groups.
- **After AOP Term**: No territorial protection whatsoever. Franchisor and affiliates may open Brand Hotels and other Hyatt Network Hotels within the former AOP.
- **If acquiring existing Brand Hotel**: May receive no Area of Protection at all.
- **No exclusive territory** — explicitly stated. Competition from other Hyatt brands (Hyatt Place, Hyatt Regency, etc.) is expected within AOP.
- **No options, rights of first refusal, or similar rights** to acquire additional franchises.
- **No sales volume/market penetration contingency** for territory continuation.
- **Internet/other channels**: No restrictions on where anyone may solicit customers. Franchisor may sell via Internet/catalog/telemarketing in AOP without compensation.

**Risk assessment**: The 3-year AOP Term is unusually short for a 20-year franchise term. After Year 3, the franchisee has zero protection against another Hyatt House opening nearby. The carve-out for 4+ hotel acquisitions further weakens even the initial 3-year protection.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
- Must use franchisor-approved management company or be approved as self-operator.

- Core Management must devote all business time to Hotel. All members of Core Management must be at a single hotel — no split duties.
- Revenue manager may serve no more than 20 Brand Hotels; must be certified within 90 days.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on What Franchisee May Sell
- Must offer all products/services periodically authorized; may not sell unauthorized products/services.
- No restrictions on customer base.
- Mixed-Use Site restrictions: no pawnshops, gentlemen's clubs, pornography, package liquor stores, marijuana dispensaries, gambling without consent.
- Bars within 300 feet: consent required (not unreasonably withheld).
- Room rates: franchisee sets rates per System Standards. Must honor "best rate guarantee." Cannot charge more than reservations center quoted.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 20 years from 1st day of month of Hotel opening.
- **Successor franchise**: 10 years if substantially complied during term, currently in full compliance, meets then-current new franchisee standards, passing QA scores during previous 3 years, has rights to maintain Hotel possession for at least 10 years.
- **Renewal requirements**: Renovate/remodel Hotel and FF&E, sign then-current form of agreement **(which may be materially different)**, pay PIP fee, sign general releases (if state law allows).
- **"Materially different" risk**: Explicitly stated twice. Successor franchise terms including fees may be materially different.

### Termination
- **Franchisee termination**: Cannot terminate Franchise Agreement except as law allows. Can terminate Central Services Agreement upon material breach (10 days' cure). Can terminate Subscription and Hosting Agreement upon material breach (30 days' cure) or without cause on 6 months' notice.
- **Franchisor termination without cause**: Cannot terminate FA without cause.
- **Franchisor termination with cause (curable)**: 24 hours for health/safety; 10 days for insurance/payments; 30 days for most other defaults; 60 days for Guarantor Monetary Threshold failure.
- **Non-curable defaults**: Bankruptcy, judgment >$100K undischarged, cease operations, loss of possession, contest IP ownership, unauthorized transfer, felony conviction, adverse conduct, false books, CI breach, law violations, repeated defaults.

### Transfer
- No transfers without franchisor approval.
- Conditions: transferee qualifies, all fees/amounts paid, training completed, at our option sign new FA (which may be materially different), sign release (if state law allows), agree to renovate, capital structure approval, remain liable, stop association.
- Non-Controlling Ownership Interest transfers: allowed without approval if transferee is not Competing Brand Owner, not sanctioned person, doesn't create Controlling Interest, and notice within 30 days.
- **Right of first offer**: Only if Exhibit C signed (for strategic markets only).

### Death or Disability
Representative must transfer Controlling Ownership Interest to approved transferee within 6 months.

### Noncompete
- **During term**: No traditional non-compete covenant. Instead, franchisee and management company may not be a "Competing Brand Owner."
- **Post-term**: No post-term non-compete. This is favorable.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: Mandatory for all disputes. Location: within 10 miles of franchisor's principal business address (currently Chicago, IL).
- **Choice of forum**: Illinois (subject to arbitration).
- **Choice of law**: Illinois law (subject to FAA and state law).

### Liquidated Damages
- **Formula**: Lesser of 36 months or remaining term × (5% + 3.5%) × Average Monthly Revenue
- **Consequential termination** (transfer to Competitor or 3+ FA defaults): 150% multiplier
- **Casualty termination with Termination Agreement**: No liquidated damages, but if new select-service hotel built within AOP within 24 months: $5,000 × guest rooms.
- **Failure to de-identify**: $5,000/day + costs
