## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and trajectory**: 118 Brand Hotels at end of 2024 (109 franchised, 9 company-owned/managed) across the United States and Canada. Steady growth from 108 (2022) to 118 (2024). Net +2 franchised outlets in 2024 (4 opened, 2 terminated). Very low attrition. 58 franchise agreements signed but not yet opened — a robust development pipeline with the largest concentrations in California (14), Florida (8), New York (5), Michigan (5), and New Jersey (4).

2. **Entity structure and ownership**: Hyatt House Franchising, L.L.C. (Kansas LLC) is a subsidiary of Hyatt Hotels Corporation (NYSE: H), a publicly traded global hospitality company. The franchisor entity itself is small ($50.5M total assets, unaudited) — it functions as a licensing vehicle. Hyatt Hotels Corporation provides an absolute and unconditional guarantee of the franchisor's obligations to franchisees. The corporate parent has been in the hotel business since 1957 and currently operates 25+ lodging brands globally.

3. **Investment range**: $20,168,455 to $57,134,018 for a new-construction 90-200 room upscale extended-stay hotel, excluding real estate. This is a capital-intensive franchise. The largest cost component is construction ($15.75M-$45M). Amount paid to franchisor/affiliates: $158,770 to $884,817.

4. **Fee burden**: The effective mandatory ongoing fee load is one of the highest in the hotel franchise space. Royalty (5% of GRR) plus Commercial Services Fee (3.5% of GRR) plus World of Hyatt (4% of eligible revenue) plus Digital Acquisition Fee (1.35% of digital channel GRR) totals approximately 13-15% of Gross Rooms Revenue before any non-mandatory services, GDS fees, mandatory contracts, or other corporate services. The FF&E reserve (3%→4%→5% of GRR) adds further burden. Non-mandatory services can add another $3,000-$14,000+ per month.

5. **Item 19 highlights**: FPR provided with three discrete financial performance representations covering 115 Covered Hotels. Average RevPAR of $127.19 for all Covered Hotels; $125.55 for the 106 Franchised Covered Hotels. Smith Travel indices above 100 across all metrics (occupancy, rate, RevPAR), indicating the brand captures more than its fair share of the competitive set. World of Hyatt loyalty program drives 60% of systemwide revenue and 51.3% of room nights — a powerful demand generator but also a significant dependency. Revenue-only FPR with no cost or profit data disclosed.

6. **Bottom-quartile risk**: The lowest-performing franchised hotel had just 27.5% occupancy and RevPAR of $30.13 — extreme underperformance. While the median ($112.53 RevPAR) is healthy, the range suggests that location and market selection are critical determinants of success. Only 40% of all Covered Hotels exceeded the average RevPAR.

7. **Financial health (parent)**: Hyatt Hotels Corporation reported 2024 revenues of $6.65B, net income of $1.30B, total assets of $13.3B, and stockholders' equity of $3.55B. The $1.30B net income was boosted by $1.25B in gains on real estate sales (asset-light strategy). The company repurchased $1.19B in stock and has $3.81B in total debt. A $110M goodwill impairment was taken in the management/franchising segment. The company is financially strong but undergoing a strategic shift from asset ownership to asset-light fee-based income.

8. **Growth trajectory**: Consistent growth. 10 new franchised outlets opened in 2023 (best year), 4 in 2024. Only 2-3 terminations per year. 58 signed-but-not-opened agreements represent ~53% growth potential relative to the current 109 franchised outlets. Only 1 projected to open in the next fiscal year, suggesting most pipeline projects are multi-year developments.

9. **Territory**: The Area of Protection typically expires 3 years after opening — unusually short for a 20-year franchise term. After the AOP Term, the franchisee has no territorial protection whatsoever. A carve-out during the AOP Term permits the franchisor to place acquired hotel groups within the AOP.

10. **Renewal**: 10-year successor franchise available, but on then-current terms which may be materially different. General release required (where state law allows). Renovation/remodeling required.

11. **Disputes**: Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, Illinois. Illinois governing law. Out-of-state dispute resolution is identified as a Special Risk on the FDD cover page.

12. **Special risks**: The 3,615-page System Standards represent extraordinary operational control. The loyalty program dependency (60% of revenue) means leaving the Hyatt system would be extremely costly in terms of lost demand.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Hyatt House Franchising, L.L.C. was formed in Kansas as SF Hotel Company, L.P. and evolved through several name changes — Summerfield Hotel Company → Summerfield Hotel Company, L.L.C. → Hyatt House Franchising, L.L.C. (September 2011). The brand evolved from Summerfield Suites → Summerfield Suites by Wyndham → Hyatt Summerfield Suites → Hyatt House. Select Hotels Group, L.L.C. acquired the ownership interests in January 2006.

The franchisor entity's direct parent is Hyatt House Hotel Holding Company, L.L.C. The ultimate parent is Hyatt Hotels Corporation. Other parents include Hyatt Corporation and Select Hotels Group, all sharing the same Chicago address. The franchisor entity itself has $50.5M in total assets and $41.0M in member's equity (unaudited) — it is essentially a licensing shell, with the parent's guarantee providing the financial substance.

Key affiliate Hyatt Corporation administers the World of Hyatt loyalty program and provides the majority of Mandatory and Non-Mandatory Services. Rosemont Project Management, L.L.C. provides optional purchasing and project management services for FF&E.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Application fee: $75,000 (new development, 90-200 rooms) or $150,000 (change of ownership). Refundable less $7,500 if withdrawn/denied before approval; non-refundable after approval.
- PIP preparation fee: $10,000 (conversions only)
- Operator approval: $10,000 for 2 participants + up to $10,000 re-entry
- IT Project Management: $58,300 (up to 250 rooms) + $6,695-$11,330 in expenses
- Training: $15,175-$34,300 in fees + $3,600-$7,405 in expense reimbursements
- Optional services (Rosemont purchasing, advisory, revenue management, field marketing): $0-$548,482

2024 actual range: $64,750 to $214,450.

**Ongoing Fees (Mandatory):**
- Royalty: 5% of Gross Rooms Revenue (monthly). Possible reduction for years 1-3 of new construction.
- Commercial Services Fee: 3.5% of GRR (monthly). Covers marketing, GPGS reservations, revenue management technology, group sales, PMS services, digital products, and other technology.
- World of Hyatt assessment: 4% of eligible revenue (2% for on-property enrollments). Independently audited.
- Digital Acquisition Fee: 1.35% of GRR from Digital Channels (Hyatt.com, branded apps).
- GDS and reservation services: ~$8.50 per reservation (at cost, no markup).
- Mandatory contracts: $1,500-$7,500/month (Avendra, credit card, music, telecom).
- Other corporate services: $500-$5,000/month (group sales promos, evaluations, QA, security, training, upsell).
- PMS subscription: $6.65/room/month.
- Training tools: $3.64/guest room/year.

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- QA inspection: $1,500-$2,000/year; $2,000 for re-inspection
- FIP fees: $5,000 (Stage 1), $15,000 (Stage 2), $20,000 (Stage 3) per 6-month stage
- BPP fees: $45,000/year (if Hotel remains in non-compliance)
- Additional training: $950/trainer/day + expenses
- GM University (replacement): $2,700/new GM
- Owner convention: $2,000-$5,000/attendee
- Leadership summit: $3,000-$6,000/attendee (~every 4 years)
- Transfer: $150,000 change of ownership application fee
- New supplier testing: $20,000
- Liquidated damages upon termination: up to 36 months × 8.5% of Average Monthly Revenue (150% for Consequential Termination)

**Non-Mandatory Services (Optional):**
- Field Marketing: $996-$2,747/month
- Revenue Management: $1,000-$3,500/month (may become mandatory)
- Central Sales: $1,750-$7,600/month
- GPGS Add-On: $3.50-$16/interaction
- Digital Ancillary: $1,250/month + 5% commission
- HSF sales booking: 1%-6% of revenue

**FF&E Reserve**: 3% of GRR (year 1) → 4% (year 2) → 5% (year 3+). Deposited monthly into separate account.

### Initial Investment

The total estimated initial investment of $20.17M-$57.13M (excluding real estate) covers a new 90-200 room hotel. The dominant cost is construction at $15.75M-$45M. Technology systems alone cost $638,600-$777,650. The wide range reflects variation in hotel size, location, and market. The estimate does not include land, rent, or finance charges.

No direct financing from the franchisor. Ad hoc financial assistance possible but no formal program, criteria, or obligation.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Approximately 95% of all purchases and leases to both establish and operate the Hotel must come from the franchisor, designated/approved suppliers, or comply with System Standards. This is an extremely high level of control. The Hotel must use Opera PMS (designated vendor), the CRS/GDS/ADS systems, designated cyber endpoint protection, and designated quality assurance inspection services.

If the franchisee or management company lacks in-house IT support, a qualified third-party technology consultant is required. F&B Operations require an approved F&B Operations Plan and any F&B Operator must be approved.

New supplier approval takes typically 30 days and costs $20,000 for testing.

During 2024, Hyatt Corporation received $129,144 in supplier rebates based on franchisee purchases, but used the entire amount to offset System Services Costs. Notably, franchised Brand Hotels received $14,396,149 more in loyalty program reimbursements than they paid in loyalty program fees — a material net benefit from the World of Hyatt program.

### Training Program Detail
The initial training program has 8 mandatory parts totaling approximately 300-420+ hours:
- Owner Briefing: 8 hours (2 days, Chicago or virtual)
- FMU (new operators): 15-17 hours (eLearning + virtual)
- Owner/Operator Orientation: 16 hours (2 days)
- GMU: 53-58 hours (eLearning + virtual + 3 days in-person at Chicago)
- Operations: 47-79 classroom + 40-76 OJT hours
- Sales/Events/Revenue Management: 136.5 hours
- Other Personnel: 17-34 classroom + 15-36 OJT hours
- Required Other Personnel: 12-29 classroom + 1-6 OJT hours
- Pre-Opening Brand Training: Minimum 7 nights on-site

Replacement training for new Core Management is mandatory. Annual retraining required for human trafficking awareness and global privacy. eCornell partnership for optional continuing education.

### Technology Stack and Costs
Required systems: Property Management (Opera PMS), POS (Simphony), Revenue Management, Envision (Events/Lead Gen), Key Lock, PBX/Phones, In-Room Entertainment, High Speed Internet, Guest Computing, Hotel Music, Digital Surveillance, and Scent. Initial cost: $638,600-$777,650. Ongoing PMS subscription: $6.65/room/month. Sound system: $18.95/month. No limits on frequency or cost of required upgrades.

The franchisor has independent, unlimited access to all Technology System data.

### Marketing Fund and Advertising
There is no separate advertising fund. Marketing, advertising, and promotional programs are covered under the Commercial Services Fee (3.5% of GRR). The Hyatt Group corporate personnel and advertising agencies develop programs. The franchisor will not prepare any periodic accounting of how funds are spent. There is no obligation to spend any amount in the franchisee's market area.

Local/regional advertising cooperatives may be formed but participation is optional. No franchisee advertising councils currently exist.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
The "Area of Protection" (AOP) is negotiated before signing the Franchise Agreement. In urban areas, it may be as small as a few blocks. In suburban areas, it may extend up to about 3 miles from the Hotel's entrance. The AOP's boundaries are typically defined by geographic references (streets, rivers) or as a circle.

### AOP Term and Post-Term Rights
The AOP Term starts at the Franchise Agreement's effective date and typically ends 3 years after the Hotel's opening date. After the AOP Term expires, the franchisee has **no territorial protection whatsoever**. The franchisor and its affiliates may open Brand Hotels and other Hyatt Network Hotels within the former AOP.

This is one of the most notable provisions in this FDD. For a 20-year franchise with a $20M-$57M investment, the territory protection expires after only 3 years of operation. For the remaining 17 years of the franchise term, the franchisee has no protection against a new Hyatt House opening nearby.

### Exceptions During AOP Term
Even during the AOP Term, the franchisor may convert hotels acquired as part of a 4+ hotel group into Brand Hotels within the AOP.

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights
There are no restrictions on where anyone may solicit customers. The franchisor may use Internet, catalog sales, telemarketing, or other direct marketing to sell in the AOP without compensating the franchisee. Other Hyatt brands (Hyatt Place, Hyatt Regency, etc.) may also be placed within the AOP at any time.

The franchisee has no options, rights of first refusal, or similar rights to acquire additional franchises.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 20 years from the first day of the month in which the Hotel opens.
- **Successor franchise**: 10-year term available if the franchisee meets conditions (substantial compliance, current compliance, passing QA scores for 3 years, meets new franchisee standards, has 10+ years of Hotel possession rights).
- **"Materially different" terms**: The FDD explicitly states twice that renewal requires signing the then-current form of franchise agreement, "which may be materially different." This means fees, territorial protections, and other terms could change substantially at renewal.
- **General release**: Required at renewal (where state law allows).
- **Renovation/remodeling**: Required at renewal.

### Transfer Provisions
All transfers of the Franchise Agreement, Hotel, or Controlling Ownership Interests require franchisor approval. Conditions include: transferee qualifies, all fees paid, training completed, sign new FA or assume existing (may be materially different), sign release, agree to renovate, capital structure approval. The franchisee remains liable after transfer.

Non-Controlling Ownership Interest transfers are permitted without approval if the transferee is not a Competing Brand Owner, not a sanctioned person, the transfer doesn't create a Controlling Interest, and the franchisor is notified within 30 days.

Transfer fee: $150,000 change of ownership application fee (refundable less $7,500 if disapproved).

Right of first offer: Only for strategic markets, only if Exhibit C to the FA is signed.

### Termination and Death/Disability
The franchisor may not terminate without cause. Cure periods range from 24 hours (health/safety) to 60 days (Guarantor Monetary Threshold). Non-curable defaults include bankruptcy, felony conviction, cease operations, unauthorized transfer, false books, and repeated defaults.

Death or disability: representative must transfer Controlling Ownership Interest within 6 months.

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term**: No traditional non-compete covenant. However, neither the franchisee nor the management company may be a "Competing Brand Owner" (a brand with 5+ hotels worldwide with ADR ≥60% of Brand Hotel ADR).
- **Post-term**: No post-term non-compete. This is favorable to franchisees.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: Mandatory for all disputes, at a location within 10 miles of the franchisor's principal business address (currently Chicago, IL).
- **Choice of forum**: Illinois (subject to arbitration).
- **Governing law**: Illinois law (subject to FAA and state law).
- **Special Risk**: Out-of-state dispute resolution is identified on the FDD cover page as a risk.

### Litigation History
One case in the last fiscal year: Hyatt House Franchising v. Energy Corridor Hotels LLC (AAA 2024) — unpaid royalty fees. This is a remarkably clean litigation profile for a system of 118 hotels. No other litigation required to be disclosed.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

The FDD provides three FPRs plus a channel mix analysis covering calendar year 2024 for 115 "Covered Hotels" that operated during all of 2024.

### FPR 1: Occupancy Rate, ADR, and RevPAR

**All Covered Hotels (115):**
- Average Occupancy: 75.0% (high 95.2%, low 27.5%, median 75.2%)
- Average ADR: $169.63 (high $291.74, low $92.55, median $158.49)
- Average RevPAR: $127.19 (high $257.24, low $30.13, median $115.46)
- 40% of all Covered Hotels exceeded average RevPAR

**Franchised Covered Hotels (106):**
- Average Occupancy: 75.1% (median 75.3%, 53.8% exceeded average)
- Average ADR: $167.22 (median $152.78, 38.7% exceeded average)
- Average RevPAR: $125.55 (median $112.53, 38.7% exceeded average)

**Urban Hotels (30):**
- Average Occupancy: 76.0% (median 77.7%)
- Average ADR: $190.91 (median $178.34)
- Average RevPAR: $145.13 (median $129.92)

### FPR 2: Smith Travel Indices
All indices exceed 100, meaning Hyatt House hotels capture more than their fair share relative to competitive sets:
- All Covered Hotels: Occupancy Index 106.9, Rate Index 102.6, RevPAR Index 109.9
- Franchised Covered Hotels: Occupancy Index 107.0, Rate Index 101.8, RevPAR Index 109.3

The strongest outperformance is in occupancy. Rate performance is closer to par, suggesting the brand's competitive advantage is in demand generation (driven by the loyalty program) rather than pricing power.

### FPR 3: World of Hyatt Contribution
- All Covered Hotels: 60.0% of revenue, 51.3% of room nights from WoH members
- Franchised Covered Hotels: 58.8% of revenue, 50.2% of room nights
- Range: 24.1% to 92.7% of revenue

The loyalty program is the single most significant demand driver. Hotels with high WoH penetration tend to outperform. This creates a powerful but also dependency-creating relationship with the Hyatt ecosystem.

### Channel Mix
- Direct Channels (Hyatt.com, GPGS, rooming list): 59.9% of GRR
- Indirect Channels (GDS, OTAs): 40.1%

Direct channels are the majority, which is favorable for cost management (lower distribution fees). The Digital Acquisition Fee (1.35%) applies to the digital channel subset.

### Stay Length Distribution
- 1-4 nights: 63.5%
- 5-11 nights: 18.0%
- 12-29 nights: 8.5%
- 30+ nights: 10.0%

The brand positions as extended-stay, but the majority of stays are short-term. Only 18.5% of stays are 12+ nights.

### Critical Caveats
- Revenue-only representations — no cost, expense, or profit data
- Unaudited internal financial data
- Average room count is 145 (range 91-264)
- Some Covered Hotels converted from other brands (existing customer base)
- Results depend on size, age, location, market, management
- The lowest-performing hotel had RevPAR of just $30.13 — extreme underperformance suggesting certain locations/markets are not viable
