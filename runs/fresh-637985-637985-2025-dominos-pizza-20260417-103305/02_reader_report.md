# Reader Report — Domino's Pizza Franchising LLC (637985-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **System**: Domino's Pizza is a mature, publicly-traded (DPI, NYSE) pizza delivery/carry-out franchise system founded in 1963, with 7,068 total stores (6,751 franchised Traditional + 25 franchised Non-Traditional + 292 company-owned) as of December 29, 2024.
2. **Entity Structure**: Franchisor is Domino's Pizza Franchising LLC (Delaware LLC), a wholly-owned subsidiary of Domino's Pizza Master Issuer LLC, itself an indirect subsidiary of Domino's Pizza LLC (Michigan LLC) and ultimately Domino's Pizza, Inc. (Delaware corp, publicly traded). Complex securitization structure from 2007.
3. **Ownership**: DPI is publicly traded. Thomas Monaghan founded 1963, sold controlling interest to Bain Capital in 1998; IPO in 2004.
4. **Investment Range**: Traditional Store: $156,450–$743,500; Non-Traditional Store: $107,450–$709,500.
5. **Fee Burden**: Royalty 5.5% of Royalty Sales; Advertising Fund 4%; local cooperatives 1–4% additional (max total advertising 9%). Technology transaction fee $0.375/digital order. Annual tech costs ~$7,000/store.
6. **Item 19**: AWUS (2024) franchised $26,467, company-owned $26,120, combined $26,269. EBITDA by tier: <$15K AWUS = 1.7%, $15–20K = 6.7%, $20–25K = 10.1%, $25–30K = 12.1%, $30K+ = 14.7%.
7. **Bottom-Quartile Risk**: 490 stores had AWUS <$15,000 with only 1.7% EBITDA margin; 1,135 stores in $15–20K range with 6.7% EBITDA. Combined, ~24.5% of reporting stores under $20K AWUS.
8. **Financial Health**: DPF had total assets of $17.4M, net income $462.6M (2024). DPL had total revenues of $4.71B, net income $584.2M, but member's deficit of $(3.95)B and long-term debt of $3.83B — typical of leveraged recapitalization structure.
9. **Growth Trajectory**: Steady net growth in Traditional franchised stores: +216 (2022), +162 (2023), +151 (2024). Development agreements grew dramatically from 168 to 323 (2022–2024).
10. **Territory**: Traditional Store gets ~1 mile radius area of primary responsibility (½ mile in dense areas); Non-Traditional gets only the premises. No exclusive territory. Franchisor may sell under different brands in your territory.
11. **Renewal**: 10-year term, 10-year renewal. Must sign then-current franchise agreement which "may contain other terms materially different from the terms of your original franchise agreement."
12. **Disputes**: No arbitration or mediation required. No choice of forum clause. Choice of law = state where Store is located.
13. **Litigation**: Active securities class action (Rhode Island v. DPI, Weiner, Reddy) re: misleading net store growth guidance. Three derivative lawsuits. Joint employment lawsuits from franchisee employees.
14. **Special Risks**: No state-specific special risks listed. Michigan Franchise Investment Law provisions apply as front matter.
15. **No Gag Clause**: Domino's explicitly states: "We have not entered into agreements with confidentiality clauses that would prevent franchisees from discussing their experience with Domino's."

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Supplier Restrictions
Domino's exercises tight supply chain control. DPD is the primary supplier of food, equipment, and technology. DPD is the only approved supplier of PULSE software, fresh dough (screen and pan), and thin crust. Three approved pre-mix manufacturers exist. One approved supplier for gluten-free crust. Getronics is the sole approved PULSE hardware vendor. Franchisees must use approved suppliers for all food ingredients, packaging, uniforms, and supplies. DPD's 2024 revenue from franchisee purchases was $2.6B (55.7% of DPL's $4.7B total revenues). An optional profit-sharing plan exists for franchisees who commit to 10-year purchases from DPD. DPD also collects $141M annually in technology, online ordering, credit card, help desk and miscellaneous fees from franchisees (3% of DPL revenue). Coca-Cola is the designated beverage standard through 2030. Franchisees must participate in the Gift Card Program. Insurance: $1.5M per occurrence minimum for general liability and auto liability.

### Item 9 — Franchisee's Obligations
Standard cross-reference table covering 24 obligation categories from site selection through dispute resolution. All key obligations reference the Franchise Agreement and Development Agreement.

### Item 10 — Financing
Domino's does not offer direct or indirect financing and does not guarantee any franchisee obligations.

### Item 11 — Assistance, Advertising, Computer Systems, Training

**Pre-opening assistance:** Site approval, site plan approval, equipment/fixture standards, training programs, operating guidance.

**Ongoing assistance:** Operating information, problem identification from reports/inspections, advertising programs, pricing guidance (franchisor may set max/min prices), additional training, transfer approval, technology requirements, Operating Manual.

**Advertising Fund:** DNAF administers. 2024 spending: $558.8M total — 85.2% media placement, 7.1% administrative, 2.2% production, 5.5% other. Annual audit by independent CPA. Company stores contribute on same basis as franchisees. DMAC (8 franchisees + 2+ corporate reps) provides marketing advice. Roll-Up provision allows franchisor to apply up to 2% of local co-op contributions to national fund. Currently in effect since January 2010.

**Computer Systems:** Domino's PULSE is mandatory for all U.S. stores. Includes order tracking, driver dispatch, time clock, cash control, reporting, customer database. Hardware cost: $15,000–$25,000. Annual maintenance/support: not to exceed $7,000. Brand Technology modifications capped at 1.5% of cumulative Royalty Sales (10-year lookback). GPS technology platform (Driver App) is mandatory. Additional Order Systems may become mandatory.

**Training:** Controlling Person must complete 12 consecutive months as store GM (single store) or supervisor (multi-unit). Must complete Franchise Management School tracks: Admissions (~45–75 hours), Undergrad (3 days in Ann Arbor), Pre-Grad (~45–75 hours in market), Grad School (2 days in Ann Arbor). Training fees up to $1,250 per session. Supplemental training up to $500/class. Franchisees must train all their own managers/team members. Training oversight: Ella Meier, Director of Training & Activation (with Domino's since June 2013).

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
**Development Agreement:** Development area on a map. Franchisor and affiliates won't operate or grant another franchise within the area during the DA term. Enclosed malls, stadiums, airports, theme parks, convention centers excluded. DA can be terminated for failure to meet requirements.

**Traditional Franchise:** Area of primary responsibility, generally 1-mile radius (½ mile in dense areas). Franchisor won't overlap another store's area of primary responsibility. Delivery area defined by franchisor, adjustable at franchisor's sole discretion. Critical: no exclusive territory. Franchisor may sell under different brands within your area. Continuation does not depend on sales volume.

**Non-Traditional Franchise:** Area of primary responsibility is the premises only. No delivery area protection. Same non-exclusivity as Traditional.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
All marks owned by DP IP Holder LLC (subsidiary of DPMI), licensed to DPF under a 99-year license. Primary marks: "Domino's", "Domino's Pizza", "Domino Design", "Tile Logo" — all registered on the Principal Register of the USPTO. No material litigation or challenges. Non-exclusive license to franchisees.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
Multiple design and utility patents (delivery vehicle, pizza tracker UI, food assembly line). Copyright protection on manuals, training materials, software. Dough, sauce, and cheese are proprietary — sources may be restricted.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
Controlling Person must devote full time to Domino's business. No outside business without written approval (may be withheld in sole judgment). Each additional store must have on-premises trained manager. Controlling Person must own 51%+. All shareholders/members/partners must personally guarantee the FA and agree to non-compete.

### Item 16 — Restrictions
Must sell all required products; cannot sell unapproved products. Delivery service area may be adjusted by franchisor. Must offer delivery during approved hours within delivery area.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- Initial term: 10 years (both Traditional and Non-Traditional)
- Renewal: 10 years, subject to: not in material default, substantial compliance, sign then-current FA (may be "materially different"), maintain/secure Store premises, complete refurbishing or relocate per then-current standards
- **Materially different risk**: Explicitly disclosed that renewal requires signing "then current form of franchise agreement which may contain other terms materially different from the terms of your original franchise agreement"

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: If DPF breaches and doesn't cure after 30-day written notice
- **By franchisor without cause**: Not permitted
- **Cause — no cure**: Material misrepresentation, failure to open within 6 months, bankruptcy, abandonment, child labor violations, loss of Store, felony conviction, intentional underreporting, unauthorized assignment, restrictive covenant violation, improper disposal after death/disability, repeated violations, health/safety threat, failure to re-open within 6 months, anti-terrorism violations
- **Cause — curable**: 48 hours (insurance), 7 days (marks, safety, food quality, health/sanitation), 10 days (payment), 30 days (all other)

### Transfer
- Franchisor must approve; transferee must meet new-franchisee standards
- Conditions: full compliance, transferee qualifies, not operating competing pizza business, signs current FA, refurbishes at request, completes training, pays $1,500 transfer fee
- Rider to Purchase Agreement required
- Mutual termination and release at time of transfer (subject to state law)
- Right of first refusal to match any offer

### Death/Disability
Personal representative must submit transfer proposal within 120 days of death or permanent disability. Must transfer to approved party.

### Post-Termination Purchase Option
Franchisor may purchase for amount based on percentage of Royalty Sales during prior 12 months, less certain deductions. If open <12 months, documented cost. May also purchase real property at appraised value and commissary at net book value.

### Non-Competition
- **During term**: No involvement in any similar business, anywhere
- **Post-term**: No interest in competing business for 1 year, at Store premises or within 10 miles

### Dispute Resolution
- No arbitration or mediation required
- No choice of forum
- Choice of law: state where Store is located
- Modification: no modifications to FA; Operating Manual subject to change
- Integration: FA is binding; does not disclaim FDD representations; subject to state law
