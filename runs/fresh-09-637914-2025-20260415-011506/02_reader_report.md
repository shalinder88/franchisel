# Reader Report — Iron Valley Real Estate, LLC (637914-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Iron Valley Real Estate, LLC** is a Pennsylvania LLC organized May 21, 2018, franchising residential and commercial real estate brokerage offices under the "Iron Valley Real Estate" and "IVRE" brands.
2. **System size as of 12/31/2024:** 44 franchised outlets + 8 company-owned offices = 52 total, operating across DE, FL, MD, NC, PA, and VA. Net +9 franchised in 2024. Zero terminations in 2024.
3. **Investment range:** $58,500 to $206,500 total, including $5,000 to $20,000 initial franchise fee.
4. **Fee structure is per-transaction, not revenue-based:** Royalty is a flat $150 per transaction side. Marketing fee is $250/month flat. Agent website fee is $16.96/agent/month.
5. **No financial performance representations (Item 19).** The FDD contains the standard no-FPR statement.
6. **Financial condition flagged as a special risk.** The cover page explicitly states: "The franchisor's financial condition... calls into question the financial ability to provide services and support to you."
7. **Members' equity was negative** through 2022 ($-60,544) and 2023 ($-54,062), turned positive in 2024 ($116,710). Revenue growing strongly: $1.02M (2022) → $1.37M (2023) → $1.76M (2024).
8. **No exclusive territory.** Single-outlet franchisees get no territorial protection. ADRSA provides a non-exclusive development area (100-200 mile radius) for multi-unit operators.
9. **Minimum agent count requirements:** 10 agents by 6 months, 20 by year 1, 30 by year 2, 24 minimum thereafter. Failure can lead to termination.
10. **Mandatory minimum payments regardless of sales** — flagged as special risk.
11. **5-year initial term.** Renewal available for additional 5-year terms, but renewal franchise agreement "may have materially different terms and conditions."
12. **Dispute resolution:** Meeting → mediation → arbitration (JAMS) in Philadelphia County, PA. PA law governs.
13. **State addenda override key provisions** for CA, MD, MI, VA, WA, and WI. Maryland and Virginia require fee deferral due to financial condition. Wisconsin addendum contains a likely template error (references "Realty ONE Group Affiliates, Inc.").
14. **Trademarks owned by affiliate Kaydence Holdings, LLC** under a 25-year license.
15. **Litigation:** One Virginia unregistered-sale settlement (2019, resolved) and one default judgment against former franchisee Always Be Closing LLC ($71,609 + $17,268 fees, 2024).

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **No exclusive territory.** Franchisee may face competition from other franchisees, company outlets, and other channels.
- Single-outlet FA grants rights for one specific location only.
- Relocation allowed with written consent (90-day notice, 20 business day response).
- **ADRSA territory:** Non-exclusive development area defined as radius from first outlet:
  - Initial: 100 miles
  - After 2 additional outlets: 150 miles
  - After 5 additional outlets: 200 miles
- ADRSA term: 5 years, auto-renews for 1-year terms unless 60-day opt-out.
- Royalty Share benefit continues 12 months after ADRSA expiration for open outlets.
- **Agent count requirements:** 10 (6 months), 20 (year 1), 30 (year 2), 24 (thereafter). First failure = warning + performance improvement program. Second failure = termination with 30-day cure.
- No restrictions on soliciting/accepting orders outside outlet area, but no wholesale, Internet, or non-retail sales.
- Franchisor may establish company outlets near (but not adjacent to) franchisee.
- Franchisor reserves right to develop other residential RE concepts under different brands.
- Franchisor may sell ancillary services (mortgage, title, escrow) under the Brand through any channel.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- Initial term: **5 years** from effective date
- Renewal: Additional 5-year terms. Must give 180-day written notice before term end. Must sign **then-current FA** (which "may have materially different terms and conditions"), remodel if necessary, and pay renewal fee ($5,000; $2,500 if fully compliant).
- ADRSA: 5-year initial term, auto-renews for 1-year terms unless 60-day opt-out.

### Termination
- **By franchisee:** If franchisor is in material breach beyond cure periods.
- **By franchisor without cause:** Not applicable.
- **By franchisor with cause:**
  - Curable defaults: 30-day cure period for monetary and other curable defaults
  - Non-curable defaults: bankruptcy/insolvency, abandonment, material misrepresentation, felony conviction, repeated noncompliance, imminent public safety danger, impaired financial condition lasting 30+ days, failure to comply with law within 30 days
- Cannot terminate FA solely because ADRSA is terminated.

### Transfer
- Requires franchisor approval (not unreasonably withheld)
- Transfer fee: $5,000 (no fee for transfer to entity solely owned by franchisee)
- Conditions: new franchisee qualifies, purchase agreement approved, training arranged, release signed, current FA signed by new franchisee
- Franchisor response: within 60 days
- Right of first refusal: Franchisor can match any legitimate offer (exceptions for SBA-guaranteed transfers and less-than-100% equity transfers)

### Death/Disability
- Must assign within 270 days
- Franchisor may place interim manager ($750/day + expenses)

### Noncompete
- **During term:** No involvement in competing business anywhere
- **Post-term:** No use of trade secrets, operational/promotional methods

### Dispute Resolution
- Step 1: In-person meeting at franchisor HQ (without counsel) within 5 business days
- Step 2: Mediation with experienced mediator (10+ years franchise law/business)
- Step 3: Arbitration by JAMS (Streamlined rules if <$250K, Comprehensive if ≥$250K)
- Forum: Philadelphia County, PA (arbitration); PA courts (litigation)
- Governing law: PA law; Federal Arbitration Act for arbitration
- Excluded from arbitration: failure to pay sums due, immediate termination triggers

### Other
- No modification of FA (except Operations Manual)
- Integration/merger clause with anti-waiver carve-out for state franchise law claims
- Franchisor may assign without restriction (to willing and able assignee)

### State Addenda Summary
**States with addenda:** California, Maryland, Michigan, New York, Virginia, Washington, Wisconsin

Key overrides:
- **California:** Post-term noncompete may not be enforceable; interest rate capped (10% or 5%+Fed); cure periods extended 30→60 days; CA franchise law governs; general release excludes CA franchise law claims
- **Maryland:** May sue in MD for MD law claims; 3-year SOL; general release excludes MD law; **fee deferral required** until pre-opening obligations complete (due to financial condition)
- **Michigan:** Good cause termination only; fair market value on non-renewal (certain conditions); no out-of-state arbitration; transfer refusal only for good cause
- **New York:** Standard disclosure additions
- **Virginia:** Reasonable cause termination required; **fee deferral required** until pre-opening obligations complete
- **Washington:** WA law prevails in conflicts; arbitration in WA or agreed location; release excludes WA Act rights; noncompete limits ($100K employees, $250K ICs); no-poach; **fee deferral** until open for business
- **Wisconsin:** 90-day notice for termination/cancellation/non-renewal; 60-day cure; WI Fair Dealership Law supersedes conflicting provisions. **NOTE: Wisconsin addendum erroneously references "Realty ONE Group Affiliates, Inc." instead of Iron Valley Real Estate, LLC.**

### Unresolveds

1. **Wisconsin addendum template error (severity: medium):** The Wisconsin appendix (p.159) states "Realty ONE Group Affiliates, Inc. must give you 90 days written notice..." — this is clearly a template error. The addendum should reference Iron Valley Real Estate, LLC. Source: PDF page 159.

2. **Outlet count discrepancy (severity: low):** Note 7 of the 2024/2023 financial statements (p.113) states "46 operating locations franchised by IVREL" as of December 31, 2024, while Item 20 Table 3 shows 44 franchised outlets at end of 2024. The discrepancy may reflect different counting methods or timing. Source: PDF pages 45-46 vs. 113.

3. **Financial condition flagged as special risk (severity: high):** The cover page (p.10) explicitly warns that "the franchisor's financial condition... calls into question the financial ability to provide services and support to you." Members' equity was negative through 2023. Multiple states (MD, VA, WA) require fee deferral as a result. Source: PDF page 10.

4. **Marketing fund not audited (severity: medium):** Marketing/Promotion Fees are deposited into the franchisor's operating account (not a separate fund) and are not audited. Franchisor retains 15% administrative fee. Only an unaudited statement is available upon written request. Source: PDF pages 28-29.

5. **Renewal "materially different terms" risk (severity: medium):** The renewal franchise agreement may have materially different terms and conditions than the original. This is explicitly disclosed. Source: PDF page 40.
