## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Iron Valley Real Estate, LLC** is a Pennsylvania LLC (organized May 2018) that franchises residential and commercial real estate brokerage offices under the "Iron Valley Real Estate" and "IVRE" brands. The concept was originated by affiliated entity Annville Group, LLC in Lebanon, Pennsylvania in 2013.

2. **System size as of December 31, 2024:** 44 franchised outlets and 8 company-owned offices (52 total) across six states (DE, FL, MD, NC, PA, VA). The system added 9 franchised outlets in 2024 (net), with zero terminations. Franchised outlets have doubled from 22 (end 2021) to 44 (end 2024).

3. **Entity structure:** Four founding/managing partners lead the franchisor: Robert Cleapor (CEO), Lisa Loser (COO), Corey Kaplan (Chief Agent Engagement Officer), and Adam S. Gamble (Founder). No parent entity. Trademarks are owned by affiliate Kaydence Holdings, LLC under a 25-year license.

4. **Investment range:** $58,500 to $206,500 total initial investment, including $5,000 to $20,000 paid to the franchisor as the initial franchise fee.

5. **Fee model is per-transaction, not revenue-based:** Royalty is $150 per transaction side (flat fee per closed deal side). Marketing fee is $250/month (flat). Agent website fee is $16.96/agent/month. This is unusual in franchise systems — it means the royalty burden does not scale with commission rates, only with transaction volume.

6. **No financial performance representations (Item 19).** Prospective franchisees receive no earnings data from the franchisor.

7. **Financial health — special risk flagged:** The FDD cover page explicitly warns that "the franchisor's financial condition... calls into question the financial ability to provide services and support to you." Members' equity was negative through 2023 ($-54,062) but turned positive in 2024 ($116,710). Revenue has grown 73% over two years ($1.02M → $1.76M). Maryland, Virginia, and Washington require fee deferral due to financial condition concerns.

8. **Growth trajectory:** Strong and accelerating. +8 franchised (2022), +5 (2023), +9 (2024). Projected 9 new franchised in 2025 (including expansion to NJ, NY, SC — new states). Zero company-outlet growth strategy.

9. **No exclusive territory.** Franchisees receive rights to operate at one specific location only. ADRSA (available after 5 outlets) provides non-exclusive development area within a 100-200 mile radius.

10. **Renewal risk:** 5-year initial term. Renewal franchise agreement "may have materially different terms and conditions." This is the single most significant contract burden for existing franchisees.

11. **Dispute resolution:** Mandatory arbitration (JAMS) in Philadelphia County, PA. Pennsylvania law governs. Seven state addenda override or limit this for CA, MD, MI, NY, VA, WA, and WI.

12. **Minimum agent count is a kill-switch:** 10 agents by 6 months, 20 by year 1, 30 by year 2, 24 ongoing. Failure can trigger termination with 30-day cure. This is a material performance obligation.

13. **Litigation minimal:** One Virginia regulatory settlement (2019, $8K total cost, resolved). One default judgment against former franchisee ($71.6K + fees, 2024). No pattern of franchisor-initiated litigation or regulatory action.

14. **Wisconsin addendum contains a template error:** References "Realty ONE Group Affiliates, Inc." instead of Iron Valley Real Estate, LLC. This is a drafting error that could create ambiguity for Wisconsin franchisees.

15. **No gag clause.** No franchisees have signed confidentiality agreements restricting discussion of their franchise experience.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Iron Valley Real Estate, LLC is a four-partner PA LLC with no parent entity. The concept was developed by Annville Group, LLC beginning in 2013. The franchisor began offering franchises in September 2018. The franchisor does not directly operate real estate offices; company-owned offices are operated by affiliated entities.

Agent for service of process: Adam S. Gamble (Hershey, PA) and designated state officials in 14 registration states.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees (Item 5):**
- First outlet in county: $20,000
- Additional outlet in same county: $5,000
- Additional outlet in new county (pre-3/10/2022): $10,000
- Additional outlet in new county (3/11/2022–3/11/2025): $15,000
- Veteran discount: $5,000 off standard fee
- ADRSA Territory Fee: $100 (non-refundable)

**Event-Driven Fees:**

| Fee | Amount | Trigger |

| Indemnification | Variable | IP violations, torts |

All CPI-adjustable fees are noted with "CPI-adjusted" in the Item 6 table footnotes.

The per-transaction royalty model is distinctive. At $150 per transaction side, a franchisee closing 200 transaction sides per year would pay $30,000 in royalties. For a traditional 6% gross commission on a $300,000 property, the $150 royalty represents approximately 0.83% of gross commission revenue per side — significantly below the 5-8% royalty rates common in real estate franchise systems. However, this fee is fixed regardless of property value.

### Initial Investment (Item 7)

Total range: **$58,500 to $206,500**

The largest variable components are:
- Office setup/leasehold improvements ($15K–$65K)
- Real property lease ($9K–$30K for 3 months + deposit)
- Equipment/furniture ($8K–$15K)
- Signage ($3K–$18K)

Many franchisees convert existing real estate offices, which significantly reduces build-out costs. Outlet size ranges from 1,500 to 3,000 square feet. The franchisor does not finance any portion of the investment.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
There is **no exclusive territory.** The franchise agreement grants rights to operate at one specific consented location only. Franchisees may face competition from other franchisees, company-owned outlets, and other channels.

### ADRSA Development Area
After opening 5 franchised outlets, franchisees may sign an Area Development and Royalty Share Agreement providing a non-exclusive development area:
- Initial radius: 100 miles from first outlet
- After 2 additional outlets: 150 miles
- After 5 additional outlets: 200 miles
- ADRSA term: 5 years, auto-renewing for 1-year terms (60-day opt-out)
- Royalty Share benefit continues 12 months after ADRSA expiration

### Channel Restrictions
- No wholesale sales, Internet sales, catalog/mail order, or non-retail distribution
- May solicit/accept orders outside outlet area (but only through retail methods)
- Franchisor may sell ancillary services (mortgage, title, escrow) under the Brand through any channel, including Internet and telemarketing
- Franchisor reserves right to develop other RE concepts under different brands

### Encroachment
- Company-owned outlets may be established "near but never adjacent to" franchisee outlets
- No right of first refusal for nearby territories under standard FA
- ADRSA provides non-exclusive development rights but does not prevent franchisor competition

### Agent Count Requirements (Kill-Switch)
| Timeline | Minimum Agents |
|----------|---------------|
| 6 months after Opening | 10 |
| Year 1 anniversary | 20 |
| Year 2 anniversary | 30 |
| Ongoing thereafter | 24 |

First failure: Warning + performance improvement program. Second failure: Default notice with 30-day cure period, then termination. This is a material performance obligation and a genuine kill-switch for franchisees who struggle with agent recruitment.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term:** 5 years from effective date
- **Renewal:** Additional 5-year terms; requires 180-day written notice before term end
- **Renewal conditions:** Must sign then-current FA, remodel if necessary, pay renewal fee ($5,000; $2,500 if fully compliant)
- **CRITICAL:** "The Renewal Franchise Agreement may have materially different terms and conditions than your original Franchise Agreement." This means the franchisor can substantially change the deal at renewal, and the franchisee's only alternative is non-renewal.

### Transfer Provisions
- Requires franchisor approval (not unreasonably withheld)
- Transfer fee: $5,000 (no fee for transfer to entity solely owned by franchisee)
- Conditions: new franchisee qualifies, purchase agreement approved, training arranged, release signed, current FA signed by new franchisee
- Response time: 60 days after receipt of all documentation
- Right of first refusal: Franchisor can match any legitimate offer (exceptions for SBA-guaranteed and partial equity transfers)

### Termination and Death/Disability
- Franchisor can only terminate with cause (material default)
- Curable defaults: 30-day cure period
- Non-curable defaults: bankruptcy, abandonment, material misrepresentation, felony, repeated noncompliance, imminent public safety danger, impaired financial condition (30+ days)
- No termination without cause
- Death/disability: Must assign within 270 days; interim manager at $750/day + expenses

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term:** No involvement in competing business anywhere (unrestricted geography)
- **Post-term:** No use of trade secrets, including operational/sales/promotional methods and techniques. No geographic or temporal limitation specified — this is a trade secrets protection, not a traditional noncompete covenant.
- State overrides: California (may not be enforceable), Washington (income thresholds apply), Michigan (good cause requirements)

### Dispute Resolution
- Step 1: In-person meeting at franchisor HQ (without counsel) within 5 business days
- Step 2: Mediation with 10+ year franchise law/business experience mediator
- Step 3: Arbitration by JAMS (Streamlined if <$250K; Comprehensive if ≥$250K)
- Forum: Philadelphia County, PA (arbitration); PA courts (litigation)
- Governing law: Pennsylvania; Federal Arbitration Act for arbitration
- Excluded from mediation/arbitration: failure to pay sums due, immediate termination triggers
- State overrides: Michigan (no out-of-state arbitration), Washington (arbitration in WA or agreed location), California (CA law governs), Wisconsin (WI Fair Dealership Law prevails)

### Litigation History (Item 3)
Two matters:
1. **Virginia unregistered sale (2019):** Inadvertent sale without registration. Settlement: $6,000 penalties + $2,000 investigation costs. Rescission offered but declined by franchisee. Case dismissed December 2019.
2. **Always Be Closing LLC (2024):** Breach and defamation complaint in M.D. Pa. Default judgment: $71,609 + interest + $17,268 attorney fees. Judgment domesticated in Florida; collections ongoing.

The litigation profile is minimal. No pattern of aggressive franchisor litigation. The Virginia matter was self-reported and resolved cooperatively.

## I. State Addenda Summary

Seven states have addenda: **California, Maryland, Michigan, New York, Virginia, Washington, Wisconsin.**

### California (pp. 148-149)
- Post-term noncompete may not be enforceable under California law
- Interest rate capped at 10% APR (or 5% + Fed rate, whichever higher) — vs. 18% in standard FA
- Cure periods extended from 30 to 60 days
- CA Franchise Investment Law and CA Franchise Relations Act govern (overriding PA choice of law)
- General release excludes CA franchise law claims
- Liquidated damages clause enforceability noted

### Maryland (pp. 150-151)
- May sue in Maryland for MD Franchise Law claims (overriding PA forum)
- 3-year statute of limitations for MD claims
- General release does not apply to MD Franchise Law liability
- **Fee deferral required:** "All initial fees and payments owed by franchisees shall be deferred until the franchisor completes its pre-opening obligations under the franchise agreement." This is a financial condition-driven requirement imposed by the Maryland Securities Commissioner.

### Michigan (pp. 152-153)
- Termination only for good cause with 30-day cure
- Fair market value compensation required on non-renewal (under certain conditions: term <5 years + post-term business restriction)
- No out-of-state arbitration or litigation
- Transfer refusal only for good cause
- Anti-waiver protections (no release, assignment, novation that deprives franchisee of Act rights)

### New York (p. 154)
- Standard NY disclosure addendum with negative litigation/insolvency/order representations

### Virginia (p. 156)
- Reasonable cause required for cancellation (Virginia Retail Franchising Act)
- **Fee deferral required** until pre-opening obligations complete

### Washington (pp. 157-158)
- WA Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails in conflicts
- Arbitration/mediation in WA or mutually agreed location (overriding PA forum)
- General release excludes WA Act rights
- Post-term noncompete limitations: employees earning <$100K/year and ICs earning <$250K/year are exempt
- No-poach prohibition: franchisor cannot restrict franchisee from hiring other franchisees' or franchisor's employees
- **Fee deferral:** No initial franchise fees until franchisee has received all pre-opening obligations and is open for business

### Wisconsin (p. 159)
- WI Fair Dealership Law: 90-day notice required for termination/cancellation/non-renewal/substantial change
- 60-day cure period (vs. 30 days in standard FA)
- WFDL supersedes conflicting FA provisions
- **TEMPLATE ERROR:** The addendum states "Realty ONE Group Affiliates, Inc. must give you 90 days written notice" — this should reference Iron Valley Real Estate, LLC. This is a drafting error carried over from a template.
