## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: TeamLogic IT operates 311 franchised outlets across the United States (including 1 in Canada and 1 in Puerto Rico) as of December 31, 2024, managed by 192 distinct franchisee entities. There are zero company-owned outlets and the franchisor does not operate any businesses of the type being franchised.

2. **Entity structure and ownership**: TeamLogic, Inc. is a California corporation incorporated September 1, 2004, headquartered at 26722 Plaza, Mission Viejo, CA 92691. It is 100% owned by Franchise Services, Inc. (CA corp), which is wholly owned by KOAH, Inc. (DE corp). FSI also owns Sir Speedy (129 centers), PIP Printing (52 centers), Signal Graphics (4 centers), and Summit Marketing Communications (ad agency).

3. **Ownership history**: No predecessors. The Lowe family has controlled the parent company since 1996. Don F. Lowe transitioned from CEO to Chairman in January 2025; his son Richard Lowe assumed CEO role. Dan Shapero has been President since February 2018.

4. **Investment range**: Total initial investment of $109,490 to $144,742, including a $40,000–$49,500 initial franchise fee (VetFran discount available). This is a relatively low-investment franchise in the technology services space, with additional funds of $60,540–$78,092 covering 10–12 months of startup operations.

5. **Fee burden**: The ongoing fee stack is material: 7% royalty (with $1,000/month minimum after year 1), 1.2% advertising fund (min $200/month after year 1), mandatory $2,500/month local marketing spend, plus RMM software fees ($3–$6/agent/month), and technology/software fees (~$500–$650/month). The effective minimum monthly obligation after year 1 exceeds $4,000 before any variable fees.

6. **Item 19 highlights**: 161 qualified franchisees (operating 24+ months) generated $204 million in total network Gross Revenue in 2024. The first quartile averaged $3.34 million, but this is heavily skewed by multi-territory operators (27 of 41 Q1 franchisees run multiple businesses). The overall system median is estimated around $680,000. The fourth quartile averaged just $210,000, and the bottom performer reported only $13,813 in annual revenue. No cost data is disclosed.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: The Q4 median of $222,300 represents annual gross revenue before all expenses. Against the mandatory fee floor of ~$48,000/year (CFF minimums + Ad Fund + local marketing) plus technology fees and operating costs, bottom-quartile franchisees face significant financial pressure.

8. **Financial health of franchisor**: TeamLogic, Inc. is financially strong. Total 2024 revenue was $20.3 million, net income $8.1 million. The franchisor generated $7.9 million in operating cash flow and distributed $7.8 million to its parent. Total assets $6.5 million, liabilities $1.5 million, equity $5.0 million. Clean audit from CliftonLarsonAllen.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Net outlet growth of +23 (2022), +16 (2023), and +29 (2024). Attrition is very low — zero terminations in any of the three reported years, and total closures of only 5–6 per year. 44 new outlets projected for 2025.

10. **Territory**: Protected but not exclusive. Based on 1,500–2,000 target businesses in geographic area. Other franchisees may service clients in your territory. Minimum sales performance required ($2,500/week for 12 consecutive weeks after year 1) or franchisor may terminate.

11. **Renewal risk**: 10-year initial term with 10-year renewal available, but franchisee must sign the "then-current" franchise agreement which may contain "materially different" terms and conditions. $2,000 renewal fee.

12. **Disputes**: Arbitration in Orange County, California. California law governs. Multi-state addendum provides overrides for 14 registration states on venue, choice of law, non-compete, and other provisions.

13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (California), mandatory spousal guarantee, sales performance minimums with termination consequence, and mandatory minimum fee payments regardless of revenue.

14. **Litigation**: One resolved matter — CA DFPI consent order (January 2024) for 4 franchise sales through unregistered brokers between December 2017 and February 2023. $8,000 administrative penalty paid. No material litigation.

15. **Managed services revenue**: The franchisor derives $6.8 million (37% of total revenue) from mandatory RMM software purchases by franchisees, with a gross margin of $5.04 million (32% of total gross margin). This is a significant profit center for the franchisor derived from franchisee-mandatory purchases.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

TeamLogic, Inc. franchises the TeamLogic IT brand for outsourced IT managed services to SMBs. The franchisor is a California corporation owned by Franchise Services, Inc. (itself owned by KOAH, Inc.). The parent company manages multiple franchise brands (Sir Speedy, PIP, Signal Graphics) plus an internal advertising agency (Summit Marketing Communications). All entities share the same Mission Viejo headquarters. The company began franchising in January 2005 and has grown steadily to 311 locations over 20 years.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees**:
- Standard initial franchise fee: $49,500 (non-refundable, fully earned)
- VetFran discount: $40,000
- Conversion franchise: $49,500
- Additional/multiple franchise: $49,500 each
- Fee includes training for 2 people (transportation, 1 hotel room, some meals)

**Ongoing Fees — Royalty**:
- 7% of Gross Sales, collected monthly via EFT
- No minimums for first 12 months
- Starting month 13: greater of 7% or $1,000/month minimum until end of term
- "Gross Sales" includes all services, labor for installation, managed services, Core and Non-Core Services. Excludes product (hardware/software) sales and sales tax.

**Ongoing Fees — Advertising**:
- Advertising Fund: Greater of 1.2% of gross sales or $200/month. None required in first 12 months.
- Local marketing: $2,500/month minimum for entire contract term (in addition to Ad Fund)
- Advertising Cooperative Association: $0–$500/month (optional; no ACA currently exists)

**Ongoing Fees — Technology & Managed Services**:
- RMM agents: $3–$6/agent/month (volume-based); must purchase from franchisor; minimum 3 agents
- PSA tools (Autotask): $175–$225/month
- Anti-virus: $1–$2/month
- Office productivity suite: $35/month
- Email service: $24/user/month
- QuickBooks Online: $100–$150/month
- Process automation: $150/month
- Multi-factor authentication: $0–$30/month

**Event-Driven Fees**:
- Transfer fee: $10,000 (waived for family transfers where family member actively participated)
- Renewal fee: $2,000
- Audit fee: $0–$3,500 (only if >2% sales understatement in any 2-month period)
- Additional training: $1,000/person (beyond 2 included)
- Late payment: $250 + 1.5%/month or max legal rate
- Failure to report sales: $250/week
- Insufficient funds: $250
- Collection costs: actual costs including attorney fees
- Custom domain names: $0–$40/year

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
- **Hardware**: May be purchased from any source.
- **RMM software agents**: Must be purchased from franchisor. This is the single most significant supplier restriction and generates $6.8 million annually for the franchisor (37% of total revenue). Volume pricing ranges from $6 to $3 per agent per month.
- **PSA software (Autotask)**: Fees payable to franchisor. Third-party developed.
- **Other software**: May be purchased from any source, but specific platforms (QuickBooks Online, approved anti-virus, etc.) are required.
- **Supplier approval process**: Franchisor evaluates proposed suppliers within 90 days; no fee to request approval.
- **No officer ownership interest** in any supplier (per Item 8 disclosure).

### Training
- **Pre-opening**: 44 hours online/webinar/phone consultation (business setup, marketing prep, PSA training, recruiting, Profit Soup)
- **Classroom (Mission Viejo, CA)**: Up to 6 days
  - Sales & Marketing Management: 18.75 hours
  - Technology Management: 9 hours
  - Employee Management: 3.75 hours
  - Business & Financial Management: 16.5 hours
  - Security Training: 2 hours
- **Post-opening**: 61 hours OJT (Autotask implementation, technician onboarding, field visit, sales continuation)
- **Total**: ~155 hours across pre-opening, classroom, and post-opening
- Must be completed within 2 months of signing franchise agreement
- Training covers 2 people (included in franchise fee)
- Supervised by Lee Dye, SVP Franchise Support and Training (with company since 2018)
- If franchisor determines franchisee not qualified after training, may terminate franchise (refund less direct costs)
- No mandatory additional training beyond initial training at this time

### Technology Stack and Costs
Required equipment configuration: 3 laptops + 3 monitors, smartphones for owner + each technician, phone system with forwarding/voicemail capability. Equipment cost: $6,750–$10,600; annual upgrade/maintenance: $0–$3,000.

Monthly technology fees: PSA ($175–$225), AV ($1–$2), Office ($35), Email ($24/user), QuickBooks ($100–$150), Process Automation ($150), MFA ($0–$30), RMM agents ($3–$6 each, min 3). Estimated total technology spend: $500–$650+/month base before scaling for additional users/agents.

Franchisor has electronic access to "most operational aspects" of the business through PSA software including client identity, appointments, and invoicing. No contractual limits on this access.

Information Security Operations Guide compliance is mandatory and may be modified over time.

### Marketing Fund Spending
2024 Ad Fund allocation: Lead Nurturing 38%, National Marketing Support 15%, Website/Email/Domain 13%, Media 12%, Content 12%, Search & Display 6%, Production/Creative 3%, Other 1%. Franchisor does NOT contribute to Ad Fund. Created by affiliate Summit Marketing Communications. 100% of Ad Fund revenue allocated to Network Advertising Program; up to 15% may be used for administrative expenses. Unaudited annual financial statement provided. OMAC advisory council (4 franchisees, advisory only).

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
TeamLogic IT provides a "protected territory" but explicitly states: "You will not receive an exclusive territory." The territory is defined by the number of businesses (1,500–2,000 target businesses of the type expected to be potential TLI clients) in a geographic area determined by the franchisor using a business list provider.

Within the protected territory, the franchisor will not open another TeamLogic IT location or establish a company-owned outlet during the term. However, other franchisees may service clients within your territory, and you may face competition from other franchisees.

The territory cannot be modified without the franchisee's consent.

### Minimum Sales Performance
You must maintain Gross Sales greater than $2,500/week for 12 consecutive weeks (except during the first 12 months). Failure to meet this threshold may result in termination. This is highlighted as a special risk on the state cover page.

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights
- No restrictions on franchisee's use of internet, catalog, telemarketing, or other direct marketing outside territory.
- Franchisor has "no present intention" to market TLI products/services via alternative distribution (including internet) within your territory, but reserves the right to do so without compensation.
- Franchisor and affiliates retain the right to market products/services not sold through TLI businesses within or outside your territory.

### Acquisition/Encroachment Risk
The franchisor or an affiliate may acquire or be acquired by a competing chain. If an acquired brand already has a location in your territory, the franchisor reserves the right to maintain that location but will not open additional locations of the other brand in your territory.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 10 years from the date the franchise agreement is executed by the franchisor.
- **Renewal**: Available for additional 10-year terms.
- **Renewal conditions**: (1) Give 6 months advance written notice of intent to renew; (2) not be in default; (3) sign the then-current franchise agreement, which "may contain terms and conditions materially different from your original contract"; (4) satisfy current qualifications and training requirements; (5) pay $2,000 renewal fee.
- **"Materially different" risk**: This is explicitly disclosed and represents a meaningful risk. The franchisee has no guarantee that renewal terms will be comparable to original terms.

### Transfer Provisions
- Any transfer of the franchise, assets, or ownership change requires franchisor approval.
- Conditions: transferor in good standing; transferee meets current qualifications; all amounts paid in full; transferee completes training; purchase agreement approved by franchisor; $10,000 transfer fee paid; release signed by transferor; new franchise agreement signed by transferee.
- **Right of First Refusal**: Franchisor can match any offer for the franchisee's business.
- Family transfers: $10,000 fee waived for immediate family members who actively participated in operations.

### Termination and Death/Disability
- **Franchisee termination**: Under any grounds permitted by law.
- **Franchisor termination with cause — curable defaults**: 20 days to cure material breach or monetary default; site not acquired in 90 days; loss of possession or abandon premises for more than 7 days; 3 defaults within any 12-month period even if each was cured.
- **Franchisor termination with cause — non-curable**: Filing bankruptcy; committing a felony; maintaining false books; committing fraud or material misrepresentation on application; conducting business in a manner likely to impair franchisor's reputation.
- **Death/disability**: Franchise may be assigned to an approved buyer who must complete training.

### Non-Competition
- **During term**: No competing business for 1 year within 25 miles of former business or of another TeamLogic IT business. Non-solicitation of clients for 1 year. Subject to state law.
- **Post-term (termination/expiration)**: No competing business for 2 years within 25 miles of former business or of another TeamLogic IT business. Non-solicitation of clients for 2 years. Subject to state law.
- **California override**: Non-competes upon termination or expiration are "generally unenforceable in the State of California."
- **North Dakota**: Restrictive covenants subject to Section 9-08, N.D.C.C.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Method**: Arbitration (except for certain claims).
- **Venue**: Orange County, California.
- **Governing law**: California law applies generally; Section 10.3(g) (non-compete) is governed by law of the state where the franchised business is located.
- **Jury trial waiver**: Yes (deleted for North Dakota per multi-state addendum).
- **Multi-state addendum overrides**: 14 states (CA, HI, IL, IN, MD, MI, MN, NY, ND, RI, SD, VA, WA, WI) have specific overrides on venue, choice of law, non-compete enforceability, liquidated damages, and other provisions.

### Litigation History
One disclosed matter: California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation Consent Order dated January 11, 2024. TeamLogic sold 4 franchises between December 2017 and February 2023 through franchise brokers who were not properly registered as salespersons. Administrative penalty: $8,000 paid January 16, 2024. Status: resolved. No other litigation required to be disclosed.

### Multi-Territory Concentration (Critical Caveat)
The Q1 average of $3.34 million is heavily influenced by multi-territory operators:
- Q1: 27 of 41 operate multiple businesses; 16 operate 3+
- Q2: 8 of 40 operate multiple businesses; 4 operate 3+
- Q3: 6 of 40 operate multiple businesses; 1 operates 3+
- Q4: 3 of 40 operate multiple businesses; 0 operate 3+

A first-time, single-territory franchisee should not expect Q1 performance. Single-territory performance is more likely represented by Q2–Q3 after the business matures (24+ months).

### Definition of Gross Revenue
All revenue from all services — hourly, managed, product resale, project services, commissions, agency revenue, reseller revenue, cloud services, technical support, break/fix, training, consulting, and other. Includes one-time and recurring. Excludes freight and sales tax.

### Cost Build-Up and EBITDA
**Not disclosed.** The FDD provides no data on costs, expenses, margins, or profitability at the franchisee level. Prospective franchisees have no basis to estimate net income from the FDD alone and should conduct independent financial analysis.

### Critical Caveats
1. The franchise takes 2+ years to build a statistically representative client base — first 24 months excluded from FPR.
2. Conversion franchisees (existing IT businesses converting to TLI) start with an active client base and may have significantly different early-period revenue profiles.
3. Longer-operating and multi-territory franchisees "typically realize higher (in some cases significantly higher) Gross Revenue."
4. No certified public accountant has audited the FPR figures.
5. "Your individual results may differ. There is no assurance that you'll sell as much."

### Key Financial Observations

**Revenue growth**: 15.6% annually for both 2022–2023 and 2023–2024. Strong and consistent.

**Gross margin**: 86.8% (2024). Extremely high, reflecting the software/license revenue model.

**Managed services as revenue driver**: $6.8M (33.5% of total revenue) from mandatory RMM and technology purchases. Cost of sales ($2.68M) is primarily the cost of third-party software licenses. Gross margin on managed services: ~$5M ($5.04M per Item 8).

**Management fees to parent**: $3.31M (2024) covers rent ($280K), payroll, and administrative support from Franchise Services, Inc. This is 16.3% of total revenue — a significant related-party cost. Also includes $70K/year for printing from parent and $7K from Summit Marketing.

**Distributions**: $5.3M (2022), $5.4M (2023), $7.8M (2024). Distributions nearly equal net income each year. The franchisor retains only modest excess cash — this is typical for QSSS pass-through structures where tax obligations flow to the parent.

**Leverage**: Minimal. Total debt-to-equity ratio of 0.30x. Only long-term liability is $521K due to affiliates plus $41K deferred revenue.

**Asset composition**: Asset-light model. Total assets of $6.5M with $1.9M cash, $1.6M receivables, and $2.1M deferred franchise costs (commissions).

**Allowance for credit losses**: $154K (2024), up from $91K (2023) and $10K (2022). Growing but still modest relative to $1.8M gross receivables.

**Cash position**: $1.9M cash including $269K restricted for advertising fund. Adequate for operations though thin relative to distribution levels.

**Tax status**: QSSS — Qualified Subchapter S Subsidiary. No federal income tax at entity level. Only state and foreign taxes ($114K in 2024).
