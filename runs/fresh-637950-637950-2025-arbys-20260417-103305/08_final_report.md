## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Arby's Franchisor, LLC** is a special purpose Delaware LLC created in November 2015 as part of a $789.9 million securitization transaction. The franchisor entity holds all franchise agreements, development agreements, and the Arby's intellectual property license. It is managed day-to-day by Arby's Restaurant Group, Inc. (ARG) under a management agreement.

2. **Inspire Brands, Inc.** is the ultimate parent company, a global multi-brand restaurant platform. Through control by private equity funds managed by Roark Capital Management, LLC, Inspire's portfolio includes Arby's, Buffalo Wild Wings, Dunkin', Baskin-Robbins, Jimmy John's, Sonic, plus affiliations with GoTo Foods (Auntie Anne's, Carvel, Cinnabon, Jamba, McAlister's, Moe's, Schlotzsky's), Subway, CKE (Carl's Jr., Hardee's), Driven Holdings (Meineke, Maaco, CARSTAR, Take 5), ServiceMaster brands, Nothing Bundt Cakes, Primrose Schools, Massage Envy, Mathnasium, i9 Sports, School of Rock, and others.

3. **System size (12/29/2024):** 3,365 U.S. Arby's restaurants — 2,286 franchised, 1,079 company-owned. Additionally, 231 franchised international units. The system is mature, with an average franchised restaurant age of 25 years.

4. **Investment range:** $861,950–$2,451,000 for a free-standing leased Arby's in the "Inspire" image design, or $644,950–$1,374,000 for a non-free-standing location. This includes $11,250–$73,400 in development/franchise fees and training costs payable to the franchisor.

5. **Fee burden:** Standard 4% royalty on Gross Sales (6.2% for Non-Traditional), plus 3.22%–5.00% AFA advertising dues, plus local market and cooperative advertising to reach a minimum aggregate of 5.2%. The NRO incentive program dramatically reduces the first 5 years to 0.5%–2.0% royalty and 75%–50% advertising discounts.

6. **Item 19 AUV:** $1,269,097 overall average for 2,219 franchised Traditional Restaurants. Median $1,193,215. Bottom quartile AUV $794,630. Top quartile AUV $1,856,562. Revenue-only — no cost, expense, or profitability data disclosed.

7. **Growth trajectory:** The system is contracting. Net -30 franchised units in 2024 (33 opened but 29 non-renewed, 34 ceased other). Net -18 company-owned. Total system shrank by 48 units in 2024. Only 13 projected new franchised openings for the current fiscal year.

8. **Financial health:** The Franchisor LLC is a financially simple SPV generating $191.7M revenue and $156.5M net income in 2024. ARG is more complex: $1.69B total revenue, $91.3M net income but $884.7M in long-term debt and a stockholder's deficit of $(227.3M).

9. **Renewal risk:** Successor franchise will be on "the standard form FA then current as of 12 months prior to expiration" and "may have materially different terms and conditions." Requires full remodel to then-current image and General Release of all claims.

10. **Dispute resolution:** All litigation must be filed in Atlanta, GA (the franchisor's principal office). Georgia law governs. No arbitration or mediation required.

11. **Non-compete:** Narrow scope — limited to businesses offering roast beef sandwiches within the Protected Area, during term + 12 months. Applies to 15%+ equity holders.

12. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution identified on the FDD Special Risks page.

13. **Gag clause:** Confirmed. The FDD discloses that "current and former franchisees sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience."

14. **Securitization structure:** The $789.9M in notes (anticipated repayment July 2027, legal final July 2050) means the franchise agreements are pledged as collateral. If the securitization notes are not repaid by the anticipated repayment date, additional interest accrues.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Arby's Franchisor, LLC operates as a special purpose vehicle within Inspire Brands. The entity exists primarily to hold franchise-related assets as part of the 2015 securitization. ARG manages all operational aspects under a management agreement, paying a base annual fee of $10.4M plus incremental amounts tied to Retained Collections, with 2% annual escalators. For FY2024, the management fee was $31.3M.

The franchise relationship is documented through two primary agreements: (1) the Development Agreement (signed first, covering territory and development schedule), and (2) the Franchise Agreement (signed for each individual restaurant). Franchisees must sign a DA regardless of whether they plan to develop one or multiple units.

Two restaurant formats are offered: Traditional (full-menu, typically with drive-thru, 2,400–3,000 sq ft) and Non-Traditional (limited menu, smaller, captive-audience locations like airports, military bases, hospitals). Multi-Brand Locations are possible with Buffalo Wild Wings, Dunkin', Baskin-Robbins, Jimmy John's, and Sonic.

### Fee Stack — Initial Fees

The standard initial fee package for a Traditional Restaurant is $12,500 (development fee) + $37,500 (franchise fee) = $50,000 total. Non-Traditional Restaurants have no development fee but an $18,750 franchise fee.

The NRO (New Restaurant Opening) incentive program, available through March 31, 2026, waives the franchise fee entirely and charges only the $12,500 development fee. This is the primary development incentive for new Traditional Restaurant construction.

Additional incentive programs include:
- **Drive-Thru Only (DTO):** $25,000 rebate for each new DTO restaurant opened by December 31, 2027.
- **VetFran:** $10,000 royalty credit per restaurant (up to $100,000) for honorably discharged veterans.
- **Pioneer Incentive:** $50,000 royalty credit per unit (first 2) for the first developer in CT, DC, HI, MA, NH, RI, or VT.
- **Relocation Incentive:** Waived development fee, franchise fee, and reduced royalty ramp for relocations.

Training fees are $2,100 per attendee for the 7-week Management Training Program. The first 4 attendees (3 at the first restaurant, 1 at the second) are free. Trainees pay their own transportation, room, and board.

### Fee Stack — Ongoing Fees

**Royalty:** 4% of Gross Sales for Traditional, 6.2% for Non-Traditional. Under the NRO program, the effective royalty ramps over 5 years: 0.5% (Year 1), 1.0% (Years 2-4), 2.0% (Year 5), then 4.0% from Year 6 onward. A safety valve exists: if a restaurant under NRO generates less than $1.3M in trailing-twelve-month Gross Sales at year 4, and the franchisee demonstrates good-faith efforts, the 2% rate extends through Year 10.

**Advertising & Marketing Service Fee:** Set by the AFA Board of Directors, currently 3.22%–5.00% of Gross Sales on a tiered basis. The minimum aggregate advertising expenditure is 5.2% of Gross Sales, encompassing the AFA fee, local market advertising, and local cooperative area advertising. AFA spent 2024 funds: 77.7% on national media, 15.6% on creative/content, 4.8% on merchandising, 0.8% on consumer insights, 1.1% on G&A. None used for franchise sales solicitation.

**Technology fees:** The optional Arby's Order Ahead Platform carries a $50/month flat fee plus 4% per transaction (35¢ minimum, $1.25 maximum) on order-ahead sales and 3% on third-party delivery orders. The Learning Management System costs $59.22/year/restaurant.

**Transfer fee:** $17,500 for the first restaurant; $2,500 if the transferor is already an Arby's franchisee.

**Renewal fee:** 10% of the then-current standard franchise fee for Traditional; the full Non-Traditional franchise fee for NT.

**Other fees:** Audit costs (if >3% deficiency found), supplier approval costs ($2,500–$10,000), interest up to 18%/year on overdue amounts, indemnity, and insurance.

### Initial Investment

The free-standing leased investment ranges from $861,950 to $2,451,000. The largest single component is building costs ($400,000–$850,000), followed by equipment ($225,000–$325,000). Working capital for the first 3 months is estimated at $33,000–$100,000. The franchisor does not offer financing.

For non-free-standing locations, the range narrows to $644,950–$1,374,000, primarily because site costs and landscaping are much lower. The Inspire image exterior design is mandatory for all new construction and remodels.

## C. Supplier Control, Operations, Technology, Training

### Supplier Restrictions

Approximately 90% of all purchases and leases to establish and operate a restaurant must come from approved suppliers and distributors. ARCOP, a non-profit supply chain cooperative, negotiates pricing and is funded by upcharges on key items (processed potato products, roast beef, frozen breads). While ARCOP participation is technically not mandatory, all domestic franchisees currently participate.

IRB (an Inspire affiliate) is the sole approved supplier for the Arby's Order Ahead Platform. Beverage suppliers Coca-Cola and Dr Pepper provide funding for marketing, the Arby's Foundation, and the annual convention.

Franchisees may request approval of alternative suppliers, but the process takes approximately 90 days and the proposed supplier may be charged $2,500–$10,000 for evaluation costs. The franchisor can refuse approval at its sole discretion and may revoke approval at any time.

ARG earned $13.9M in rental revenue from 95 franchised properties in FY2024 (net $5.5M after costs). IRB earned $4.9M from the Order Ahead Platform, which it states covers program costs without being a profit center.

### Training

The Management Training Program is a 7-week, 336-hour entirely on-the-job program at a Nationally Certified Training Restaurant. It consists of three phases: TMTP (2 weeks, team member skills), SMTP (3 weeks, shift management), and MTP (2 weeks, critical thinking and leadership). All phases require 90% pass rates on knowledge checks.

For the first two restaurants, franchisees must maintain 3 certified managers each (6 total). For subsequent restaurants, 1 certified manager per unit. The manual is 658 pages covering operations (306), operating systems (105), cleaning/sanitation (80), safety (124), and business practices (43).

### Technology

The required POS system is PAR Brink, costing $32,000–$55,000 with $5,000–$10,000 annual maintenance. The franchisor has unlimited, independent access to POS data. Franchisees are solely responsible for PCI compliance and technology maintenance.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

The Development Agreement grants territorial protection during its term — the franchisor will not open or franchise new Arby's restaurants within the defined Territory. Territories are typically defined by radius or governmental boundaries but exclude institutional locations (malls, colleges, hospitals, military bases, airports, casinos, arenas).

The Franchise Agreement provides a more limited "Protected Area" — typically a 1-mile radius for free-standing restaurants, with the same institutional exclusions. Non-Traditional restaurants receive no Protected Area whatsoever.

The franchise is explicitly non-exclusive. The franchisor reserves the right to operate or franchise at non-traditional locations inside the Territory or Protected Area. The franchisor also reserves all alternative distribution channel rights (internet, catalogs, telemarketing) — and already sells consumer packaged goods (frozen fries, Arby's Horsey Sauce) at approximately 44,000 retail stores nationwide and online.

There is no right of first refusal for additional franchises under the FA, and no right to compensation if the franchisor exercises its reserved rights within the Protected Area.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

Traditional Restaurant franchise terms are up to 20 years. Non-Traditional terms are the shorter of 10 years or the lease term. Renewal to a "successor FA" requires substantial compliance, no default in the prior 12 months, remodel to the then-current Arby's image, completion of current training requirements, a General Release, and an administrative fee (10% of the then-current franchise fee, due 180 days before expiration).

Critically, the successor FA "will be the standard form FA then current as of 12 months prior to the expiration date" and "may have materially different terms and conditions." This is a significant risk — the franchise relationship could change substantially at renewal.

### Termination

The franchisor can terminate for a broad range of causes. Curable defaults generally allow 30 days to cure (or longer if state law requires), except monetary defaults (10 days) and casualty (180 days). Non-curable defaults triggering automatic or immediate termination include insolvency, bankruptcy, unauthorized transfer, loss of premises, criminal violations, false information, system duplication, non-compete violations, denial of inspection access, 2+ monetary defaults in 12 months, imminent health/safety danger, and abandonment.

### Transfer

All transfers require prior written approval. The transfer fee is $17,500 for the first unit ($2,500 for existing franchisees). The franchisor has a right of first refusal on bona fide transfers, but this is waived for "pre-qualified buyers" (persons who own 5+ Arby's restaurants through affiliates, are in compliance, and are not in litigation with Arby's). Family transfers of less than 50% control do not require consent.

### Non-Compete

The non-compete is narrowly scoped: during the franchise term plus 12 months post-termination, the franchisee (and 15%+ equity holders) cannot own, operate, or have more than a 15% interest in any business offering roast beef sandwiches for sale within the Protected Area. This is one of the narrower non-competes in the QSR space — it does not restrict competing in the broader restaurant category outside the Protected Area.

### Dispute Resolution

No arbitration or mediation is required. All litigation must be filed in federal or state court closest to the franchisor's principal office (currently Atlanta, GA). Georgia law governs.

State addenda override this for Minnesota (litigation cannot be required outside MN), Maryland (MD Franchise Law claims can be brought in MD), and North Dakota (various protections including forum restrictions, no liquidated damages, no jury trial waiver, no punitive damages waiver).

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

Arby's provides a revenue-only Item 19 for the 2024 fiscal year (January 1 – December 29, 2024). The FPR covers 2,219 franchised Traditional Restaurants that operated for the full year.

**Overall average unit volume (AUV): $1,269,097.** The median is $1,193,215, indicating the distribution is right-skewed (the average is pulled up by high-performing units).

The quartile breakdown reveals significant spread:
- **1st quartile (555 units):** AUV $1,856,562 (median $1,754,983, range $1,507,181–$3,957,746). Only 38.4% of units in this quartile exceeded the quartile average.
- **2nd quartile (555 units):** AUV $1,343,596 (median $1,339,208, range $1,193,215–$1,504,546). 48.5% above average.
- **3rd quartile (554 units):** AUV $1,081,262 (median $1,079,979, range $968,826–$1,192,753). 48.9% above average.
- **4th quartile (555 units):** AUV $794,630 (median $814,049, range $368,870–$968,870). 54.4% above average.

Travel plaza locations significantly outperform: AUV $1,564,424 (175 units) vs. $1,257,170 for free-standing (1,888 units) and $1,082,148 for other non-free-standing (156 units).

**Critical caveats:**
- Revenue only — no costs, expenses, or profitability data is disclosed. Prospective franchisees must independently investigate unit economics.
- The reporting population (2,219 units) excludes 33 partial-year openings, 15 units closed for remodel/casualty, 19 Non-Traditional units, 1 Multi-Brand unit, 63 that closed during 2024, and all 1,079 company-owned restaurants.
- Sales data comes from franchisee POS systems and submitted reports, not independently audited.
- Average restaurant age is 25 years — new units may not achieve comparable volumes.
- 2,204 of 2,219 units have drive-thru windows, which is a major revenue driver.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### Multi-Year Trajectory

The Arby's system has been contracting since 2023. After modest net growth in 2022 (+14 franchised, -6 company) and 2023 (+11 franchised, -13 company), the system saw material contraction in 2024:

- **Franchised:** 33 opened, 0 terminated, 29 non-renewed, 0 reacquired, 34 ceased other = net -30.
- **Company-owned:** 2 opened, 0 reacquired, 20 closed, 0 sold = net -18.
- **Total system:** net -48 units in 2024.

The projected pipeline is thin: only 8 FAs signed but not opened, and 13 projected new franchised outlets. No company-owned growth is projected.

### Transfer Activity

Transfers peaked in 2023 (87) and declined to 54 in 2024. Key transfer states in 2024: Oregon (19), Iowa (12), Kentucky (12).

### Non-Renewals

29 non-renewals in 2024 is a significant number, nearly matching the 33 new openings. Combined with 34 "ceased operations – other reasons," the total closures of 63 franchised units in 2024 far exceeded the 33 openings.

### Gag Clause

The FDD explicitly discloses: "In some instances, current and former franchisees sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with us. You may wish to speak with current and former franchisees, but be aware that not all such franchisees will be able to communicate with you." This is flagged as a business-risk item for prospective franchisees conducting due diligence.

## H. Item 21 — Financial Statements

### Arby's Franchisor, LLC (SPV)

**Auditor:** KPMG LLP, Atlanta. **Opinion:** Unqualified. **Date:** March 20, 2025. **Going concern:** None.

The Franchisor LLC is a simple pass-through entity. Its sole revenue comes from franchise fees and royalties ($115.0M from franchisees, $71.9M from affiliate-owned restaurants, $4.8M other). Total revenue was $191.7M in FY2024, down from $204.9M in FY2023 — reflecting the system contraction and lower comp-sales.

Costs are minimal: the management fee to ARG ($31.3M), G&A ($3.4M), and amortization ($435K). Operating income was $156.5M, and net income was $156.5M. The LLC is not subject to federal or state income taxes — all taxes flow through to ARG.

The balance sheet is dominated by intangible assets ($252.8M, mainly the $250M indefinite-lived trademark/trade name). Member's equity was $254.4M. Distributions to Member matched operating cash flow at $163.4M.

**Securitization notes:** The Issuer (Arby's Funding, LLC) has $789.9M in outstanding 2020 Class A-2 Senior Secured Fixed Rate Notes with an anticipated repayment date of July 2027 and legal final maturity of July 2050. A $300M variable funding facility has $293.1M remaining capacity.

The franchise agreements and intellectual property are pledged as collateral for these notes. This securitization structure means the franchise relationships are encumbered — a factor prospective franchisees should understand.

### ARG (Arby's Restaurant Group, Inc.)

**Auditor:** KPMG LLP, Atlanta. **Opinion:** Unqualified. **Date:** March 20, 2025. **Going concern:** None.

ARG is the operating entity with a much more complex financial profile.

**Revenue (FY2024):** $1.69B total. Company-owned restaurant sales were $1.43B (down from $1.55B in FY2023), franchise fees/royalties $115.2M, franchise advertising contributions $116.6M, other revenues $25.0M.

**Profitability:** Operating income was $158.4M (down from $221.2M in FY2023). The decline was driven by lower restaurant sales and franchise revenues. Interest expense was $43.4M. Income tax expense was $31.6M. Net income was $91.3M (vs. $146.7M in FY2023).

**Balance sheet:** Total assets $1.41B. Property & equipment $483.7M, goodwill $18.9M (stable), intangible assets $286.3M, operating lease assets $454.5M. Total liabilities $1.63B, including long-term debt $884.7M and operating lease liabilities $442.9M. Stockholder's deficit was $(227.3M) — ARG has a note receivable from parent of $(380M) on the equity side.

**Leverage:** The combination of $884.7M long-term debt, $442.9M operating lease liabilities, and a stockholder's deficit indicates significant financial leverage. However, operating cash flow was $146.3M, and interest was $43.4M, providing adequate coverage.

**Impairment charges:** $3.2M in FY2024 (down from $9.1M in FY2023 and $11.6M in FY2022), suggesting stabilization.

## I. State Addenda Summary

State addenda exist for **California, Hawaii, Maryland, Minnesota, North Dakota, and Washington**, plus a Michigan pre-body state notice and a multi-state no-waiver-of-disclaimer provision covering 13 states.

### California
Added compliance note regarding the Fast Food Act (AB 1228) covering minimum wages, working hours, and working conditions. Responsibility for compliance is solely on the franchisee.

### Hawaii
Standard Hawaii franchise investment law disclosure requirements.

### Maryland
- General Release at renewal/transfer does not apply to Maryland Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law liability.
- Termination upon insolvency may not be enforceable under federal bankruptcy law.
- Franchisee may bring Maryland Franchise Law claims in Maryland courts.
- 3-year limitations period for Maryland Franchise Law claims.

### Minnesota
- Arby's will protect franchisee's trademark rights per Minn. Stat. §80C.12.
- 90 days' notice of termination (60 days to cure); 180 days' notice of non-renewal.
- Litigation cannot be required outside Minnesota per Minn. Stat. 80C.21.
- Release at renewal/transfer does not apply to the extent prohibited by Minnesota law.
- Cannot require jury trial waiver, consent to liquidated damages/termination penalties.

### North Dakota
Comprehensive protection: restrictive covenants subject to N.D.C.C. §9-08-06; no out-of-state arbitration; no out-of-state forum requirement; no liquidated damages or termination penalties; North Dakota law governs; no jury trial waiver; no punitive/exemplary damages waiver; no general release on renewal; statute of limitations under North Dakota law applies.

### Washington
Assurance of Discontinuance (AOD) with Washington AG regarding no-poach provisions. ARG agreed to remove no-poach restrictions from franchise agreements.
