# Reader Report — 637950-2025-Arbys

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Arby's Franchisor, LLC** is a Delaware LLC organized July 2, 2015 as a special purpose entity in a securitization transaction. ARG (Arby's Restaurant Group, Inc.) manages the system under a management agreement.
2. **Inspire Brands, Inc.** is the ultimate parent company, a global multi-brand restaurant platform (Arby's, Buffalo Wild Wings, Dunkin', Baskin-Robbins, Jimmy John's, Sonic). Roark Capital Management controls Inspire.
3. **System size as of 12/29/2024:** 3,365 U.S. Arby's restaurants — 2,286 franchised, 1,079 company-owned. Plus 231 international franchised units.
4. **Investment range:** $861,950–$2,451,000 (free-standing) / $644,950–$1,374,000 (non-free-standing).
5. **Royalty:** 4% of Gross Sales for Traditional Restaurants, 6.2% for Non-Traditional. NRO incentive ramps from 0.5% in Year 1 up to 4% by Year 6.
6. **Advertising:** Minimum aggregate 5.2% of Gross Sales (AFA dues 3.22%–5.00% + local market + co-op).
7. **Item 19 AUV (all franchised Traditional):** $1,269,097 overall. 1st quartile $1,856,562; 4th quartile $794,630.
8. **Item 20 trajectory:** Net -30 franchised units in 2024 (33 opened, 29 non-renewals, 34 other closures). Company-owned also shrinking (-18 net in 2024).
9. **Financial health (Franchisor LLC):** $156.5M net income on $191.7M total revenue (2024). Simple SPV entity — virtually all revenue from franchise fees/royalties. $254.4M member's equity. Distributions matched operating cash flow.
10. **ARG financials:** $1.69B total revenue, $91.3M net income, $884.7M long-term debt, stockholder's deficit of $(227.3M). $789.9M in securitization notes outstanding.
11. **Franchise term:** Up to 20 years (Traditional), up to 10 years (Non-Traditional). Renewal to a "materially different" successor FA.
12. **Noncompete:** During term + 12 months post-term, restricted to businesses offering roast beef sandwiches within Protected Area. 15% ownership threshold.
13. **Special risk:** Out-of-state dispute resolution (Atlanta, GA).
14. **Gag clause:** Yes — "current and former franchisees sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience" (p.83).

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Technology (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Supplier Restrictions
- ~90% of purchases must come from approved suppliers/distributors.
- ARCOP (non-profit supply chain cooperative) negotiates pricing for all Arby's restaurants. Funded by upcharge on key items (potato products, roast beef, frozen breads).
- IRB (affiliate) provides the optional Arby's Order Ahead Platform — only approved supplier for that platform.
- Supplier approval process: ~90 days, $2,500–$10,000 evaluation cost charged to supplier.
- Beverage suppliers (Coca-Cola, Dr Pepper) provide funding for marketing, Arby's Foundation, and convention.
- ARG received $13.9M rental revenue from 95 franchised properties (net $5.5M). IRB received $4.9M from Order Ahead Platform (all used for platform costs).
- 10th anniversary refresh/upgrade required to match then-current exterior/interior image.
- Insurance: $1M commercial general liability minimum, A.M. Best "A-" / FSC "X" rated carrier.

### Item 11 — Franchisor's Assistance, Advertising, Computer Systems, Training

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Development Agreement** grants right to develop within a Territory during the DA term. Franchisor will not open or franchise new Arby's in the Territory during DA term.
- Territory defined by radius or governmental boundaries. Excludes: malls, colleges, hospitals, service plazas, military bases, theme parks, airports, casinos, arenas, sovereign nations.
- **Protected Area** under FA: Typically 1-mile radius for free-standing. No Protected Area for Non-Traditional.
- **Encroachment risk:** Franchisor and affiliates may operate at non-traditional locations inside Territory/Protected Area. Consumer packaged goods sold at ~44,000 retail stores + online.
- No exclusive territory. Competition from other franchisees, company-owned outlets, and Inspire Brands portfolio brands possible.
- No options/rights of first refusal for additional franchises under FA.
- Franchisor reserves all alternative distribution channel rights (internet, catalog, telemarketing).

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Traditional:** Up to 20 years (may be shorter if lease is shorter or acquiring existing unit).
- **Non-Traditional:** Shorter of 10 years or lease term.
- **Renewal:** Successor FA with "materially different terms." Requires: substantial compliance, no default in prior 12 months, remodel to then-current image, General Release (Exhibit F), training current, application 6–9 months before expiration, renewal fee (10% of then-current franchise fee for Traditional; full NT fee for NT).

### Termination
- **Curable defaults (30 days):** Non-compliance with Manual, operation detrimental to system.
- **Curable (10 days):** Non-payment of fees.
- **Curable (180 days):** Casualty rendering premises inoperable.
- **Non-curable (automatic):** Insolvency, bankruptcy, assignment for benefit of creditors, unauthorized transfer/assignment.
- **Non-curable (immediate on notice):** Loss of premises rights, criminal violation, false information, duplicating system in non-franchised outlet, violating non-compete/non-disclosure, denying inspection, 2+ monetary defaults in 12 months, imminent health/safety danger, abandonment.

### Transfer
- No transfer without prior written approval. Transfer fee: $17,500 (first unit); $2,500 (existing franchisee).
- Conditions: Transferee qualifies financially, good character, training completed, outstanding debts paid, General Release, corrections within 90 days.
- Right of first refusal on bona fide transfers (waived for "pre-qualified buyers" — own 5+ Arby's, in compliance, not in litigation with Arby's).
- Family transfers (<50% control) allowed without consent.

### Death/Disability
- DA terminates immediately on death of individual Developer. FA allows transfer to family/trust for <50% interest.

### Non-compete
- During term + 12 months post-term: no business offering roast beef sandwiches within Protected Area.
- Applies to franchisee, guarantors, partners, 15%+ equity holders.
- Scope: narrowly limited to roast beef sandwiches within Protected Area only.

### Dispute Resolution
- No arbitration or mediation required.
- Venue: Federal or state court closest to franchisor's principal office (currently Atlanta, GA). Franchisee must file in Atlanta; franchisor can file in Atlanta or franchisee's jurisdiction.
- Governing law: Georgia (without conflict of laws), except state franchise law applies only if jurisdictional requirements independently met.
- State overrides: Maryland (can bring MD Franchise Law claims in MD), Minnesota (litigation cannot be required outside MN per Minn. Stat. 80C.21), North Dakota (various protections per 51-19-09).
