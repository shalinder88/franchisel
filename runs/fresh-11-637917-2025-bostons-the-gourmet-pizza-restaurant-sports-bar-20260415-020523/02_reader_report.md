3. **Ownership**: Owned by Treliving Private Investments Ltd. (BC, Canada) and BPR GP, Inc. (Delaware). Ultimate parent: Treliving Family Holdings, Inc. Chairman: James Walter Treliving.
4. **System size (US)**: 24 franchised restaurants, 0 company-owned (as of December 31, 2024). Net +2 in 2024 (5 opened, 2 terminated). 4 franchise agreements signed but not yet opened.
5. **Canadian affiliate**: Boston Pizza Canada LP (BPCLP) operates 371 franchised locations — the US system is a small fraction of the overall brand.
6. **Mexico affiliate**: BPR Mexico has 23 franchised locations.

## B. Front Matter, Fees, and Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — The Franchisor
Boston Pizza Restaurants, LP is a Delaware limited partnership formed December 21, 2001. It has been offering franchises since formation. Its predecessor, BPR USA (a Delaware corporation), offered franchises from October 1995 to December 2001. BPR USA operates one company restaurant.

**Parents**: TPI (British Columbia), which is a wholly-owned subsidiary of JTHL, itself a subsidiary of TFHI. BPR GP, Inc. (Delaware) is owned by BPR USA, which is a wholly-owned subsidiary of TPI.

**Affiliates**:
- Boston Pizza International Inc. (BPI) — offered Canadian franchises 1983–2015, then transferred to BPCLP
- Boston Pizza Canada LP (BPCLP) — 371 franchised locations in Canada as of December 31, 2024
- BPR Mexico — 23 franchised locations in Mexico
- BPR (USA) Group Marketing Fund, Inc. — provides marketing/advertising services

The franchise concept is full-service casual dining combined with a sports bar, featuring gourmet pizza and pasta, alcohol service, large flat-screen TVs, flexible seating, outdoor patio, and an entertainment area (pool, darts). Restaurants are 5,000–7,000 sq ft.

Alcohol licensing is required. Dram Shop liability exposure. Multiple food service permits needed.

### Item 2 — Business Experience
- **James Walter Treliving** — Chairman since March 2023; President 2004–2023; CEO 2001–2004. Owner of BPI.
- **Jeff Melnick** — President since March 2023 (on temporary leave since January 2025). VP May 2018–March 2023. 40+ years restaurant industry experience.
- **Sergio Carvallo** — Interim President since January 2025. President of BPR Mexico since March 2022. VP of BPR Mexico March 2016–March 2022.
- **Jason Snavely** — Secretary and VP of Finance since March 2023. Previously at United Surgical Partners International and Tatum (consulting).
- **Mike Massie** — Director of Development, Design and Construction since March 2023.
- **Miguel Rodriguez** — Director of IT since January 2023.

### Item 3 — Litigation
Three disclosed actions, all resolved:
1. **Patel v. BPR** (2019): Franchisee sued for breach of oral agreements, fraud, negligent misrepresentation, TDTPA violations. Settled for $5,000 payment to Patel; all agreements terminated. Dismissed August 2019.
2. **BPR v. Kaushal** (2020): Franchisor sued ex-franchisee for $205,000 liquidated damages. Dismissed without prejudice.
3. **BPR v. BOSTCG/Patel** (2023): Franchisor sued ex-franchisee for $271,947 liquidated damages. Defendants counterclaimed breach of contract and "string-along fraud." Settled March 2024 for $85,000 payment to Boston's; fraud counterclaims abandoned.

## D. Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
**Franchise Territory**: Typically 1-mile radius around approved location. Protected in that franchisor will not establish or franchise another Boston's within the territory. However:
- **Not exclusive**: "You will not receive an exclusive territory. You may face competition from other franchisees, from outlets that we own, or from other channels of distribution or competitive brands that we control."
- Franchisor retains rights to use Marks via alternate channels (wholesale, internet, non-traditional locations) inside territory
- Delivery/catering may cross territory boundaries without compensation
- Franchisor may acquire competitive businesses within territory
- Territory may be modified on renewal
- No relocation without prior written approval

**Development Territory** (Area Development Agreement):
- Non-exclusive during compliance
- Size varies by demographics/population density
- Exclusivity lost on first failure to meet development schedule
- Second failure is material default — may terminate, reduce territory, or extend schedule

### Item 13 — Trademarks
Marks owned by TPI (Canada), licensed exclusively to BPR via Trademark License (5-year auto-renewing). 15+ USPTO registrations on Principal Register. If license terminates, TPI has agreed to license directly to franchisees. No adverse determinations, pending proceedings, or known infringements.

### Item 14 — Patents/Copyrights
No material patents. Common law and federal copyrights claimed on Operations Manual, advertising materials, recipes, product formulas. Broad confidentiality obligations on franchisees and employees.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
- Responsible Owner (individual or entity with ≥25% equity) must manage day-to-day operations, or designate a Franchise Manager approved by franchisor
- Both must complete initial training
- **Spousal guarantee**: All owners and their spouses must sign Franchise Owners Agreement guaranteeing franchisee's performance
- Franchise Manager and officers must sign System Protection Agreement
- All employees with access to confidential info must sign confidentiality agreement

### Item 16 — Restrictions on Sales
Must sell only authorized products/services. No independent internet sales, wholesale, or mail order. Franchisor may change required products at any time. No independent social media without prior written consent. Must honor systemwide promotions and gift cards.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 10 years
- **Renewal**: 2 successor terms of 5 years each (total potential 20 years)
- **Renewal fee**: $25,000
- **CRITICAL**: Successor agreement "may have materially different terms and conditions (including, e.g., higher royalty and advertising contributions)"
- Must be in good standing to renew

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: If franchisor is in material breach and fails to cure within 30 days of written notice
- **By franchisor without cause**: Not applicable
- **By franchisor with cause**: Section 24 violations, including cross-default on any other agreement (except ADA development schedule default only)
- **Curable defaults**: 30 days to cure (Section 24.3)
- **Non-curable defaults**: Sections 24.1 and 24.2

### Transfer
- All transfers require franchisor approval
- Transfer fee: $25,000
- Right of first refusal: 30 days to match any offer
- Option to purchase at fair market value on termination
- Death/disability: must transfer within 180 days; estate must apply within 120 days

### Non-Competition
- **During term**: No competing business, no >5% interest in competitor, no services for competitor — anywhere
- **Post-term**: 2 years, 10-mile radius from franchisee's location AND all other Boston's locations operating or under construction. If court finds too broad, fallback to 10-mile radius from franchisee's location only.
- Tolling provision: restricted period extended by time of violation

### Dispute Resolution
- Mediation and arbitration in Dallas, Texas
- Litigation (if applicable) in Dallas, Texas
- Texas law governs
- **Subject to applicable state law** — overridden by multiple state addenda

### Post-Termination Obligations
Complete de-identification, payment of all amounts due, return of confidential information and records.
