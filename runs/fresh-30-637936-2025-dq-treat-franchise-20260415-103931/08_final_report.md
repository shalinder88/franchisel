# 08 Final Diligence Report — DQ Treat Franchise (637936-2025)

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: 727 direct-licensed DQ Treat/DQ soft-serve outlets at year-end 2024 (zero company-owned). Plus 358 subfranchised territory operator outlets. Total system ~1,085 locations. The direct-licensed system has shrunk by 101 units over three years (828 → 727), a 12.2% decline.
2. **Entity structure**: Franchisor is American Dairy Queen Corporation (ADQ), a Delaware corporation (1962). Parent is International Dairy Queen, Inc. (IDQ). Ultimate parent is Berkshire Hathaway Inc. IDQ unconditionally guarantees all ADQ franchise obligations.
3. **Ownership history**: ADQ has had no predecessors in 10 years. Stable corporate lineage through IDQ to Berkshire Hathaway. Key leadership change in March 2025: Arthur D'Elia (former Domino's EVP) joined as COO; Daniel Kropp shifted to Chief Supply Chain & Development Officer.
4. **Investment range**: $549,100–$1,057,700 (Captive-venue) or $848,450–$1,604,700 (Street location), excluding land ($250K–$620K+ for Street). Total out-of-pocket for a Street franchise with land: approximately $1.1M–$2.2M.
5. **Fee burden**: 5% royalty + 5%–6% sales promotion fee = 10%–11% of Gross Sales in ongoing fees, before technology fees, insurance, and other obligations. Technology stack adds ~$550–$700/month in mandatory vendor fees plus ~2%–5% payment processing.
6. **Item 19**: No financial performance representations provided. ADQ does not disclose franchisee revenue, costs, or profitability. Buyer must conduct independent financial due diligence.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: System is shrinking. Zero new direct-licensed openings in 2024. Only 1 franchise agreement signed but not opened (New Mexico). The 3-year trend of 31→28→15 terminations suggests some stabilization but the system is in managed decline.
8. **Financial health**: IDQ reports strong profitability — $97.9M net income on $257.6M revenue (38% net margin). However, dividends to Berkshire ($110M) exceed net income, deepening the retained deficit to $(68.4M). Working capital is negative (current liabilities $207.5M vs current assets $132.1M). The deficit is a structural feature of Berkshire subsidiary capital management, not necessarily a solvency risk given Berkshire's backing.
9. **Growth trajectory**: Negative. System shrinking across all metrics. Subfranchised system also declining (-13 in 2024). No projected openings beyond 1 signed agreement.
10. **Territory**: No exclusive territory granted. Site-only franchise. Territory operators with older agreements can subfranchise near your location without following ADQ site clearance guidelines.
11. **Renewal**: Sign then-current agreement which "may differ materially, including higher or additional fees." This is a standard but material risk — your ongoing economics may change at renewal.
12. **Disputes**: One pending lawsuit (Project Lonestar, N.D. Texas, transfer-related). Three settled cases in reporting period. Mandatory arbitration in Minneapolis, MN (subject to state overrides).
13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Minneapolis). California Fast Food Act compliance obligations. Maryland fee deferral requirement. Gag clause exists — some franchisees restricted from speaking about ADQ experience.
14. **Berkshire guarantee**: IDQ's unconditional guarantee mitigates the risk that ADQ's separate financials are not disclosed.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
ADQ is a mature franchisor with a 64-year corporate history, backed by Berkshire Hathaway through IDQ. The franchise is offered as a single-unit license for two location types (Street and Captive-venue). There is no area development agreement or multi-unit obligation — each unit requires a separate franchise agreement.

The conversion program allows existing DQ soft-serve-only stores to convert to DQ Treat format with modified fee structures. The relocation policy permits qualifying Street franchisees to move within 2 miles.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Initial franchise fee: $25,000 ($10,000 non-refundable deposit)
- MTRA: $200/attempt
- SERVSAFE: $200–$400
- Training program: $3,600/person (or reduced for experienced ARD operators)

**Ongoing Fees (% of Gross Sales):**
- Continuing license fee (royalty): 5%
- Sales promotion program fee: 5%–6% (most products); up to 6% (OJ branded)
- **Total ongoing % fees: 10%–11% of Gross Sales**

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Transfer fee: $6,000 (escalating $500/5 years from 2030)
- Renewal fee: $1,000 × years of renewal term (max $10,000)
- Termination fee: 2× last 12 months CLF (on default termination)
- Audit costs: if understatement ≥3%
- Interest: 18% per annum on overdue amounts
- Late fees: $50/occurrence
- Training cancellation: $100–$1,000

**Technology Fees (monthly):**
- EPOS software (ParBrink/Olo/Punchh): $370–$413
- EPOS hardware warranty: $46–$118
- Credit card processing: 2%–5% per transaction
- TransArmor encryption: $19.95
- Verifone device encryption: ~$10/terminal
- Managed firewall (Acumera): $51
- Olo per-transaction: 0.25%
- Gift card fees: 3% of redemptions (~$200/year)

### Initial Investment Breakdown

The total initial investment varies significantly by location type:

**Captive-Venue: $549,100–$1,057,700** (excluding land, which is typically leased)
- Building/construction: $235K–$470K
- Equipment: $220K–$360K
- Additional funds (3 months): $41K–$115K
- All other: $53K–$113K

**Street: $848,450–$1,604,700** (excluding land at $250K–$620K+)
- Building/construction: $390K–$870K
- Equipment: $360K–$420K
- Additional funds (3 months): $41K–$155K
- All other: $57K–$160K

Local lending institutions typically require 20% equity on leasehold improvements and 25% on equipment. ADQ does not provide financing.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
ADQ maintains tight supplier control. All products must meet ADQ specifications and be purchased from ADQ-approved sources. ADQ can designate a single source for six critical product categories, including soft drinks, third-party brands, LTO items, equipment/EPOS, products where ADQ receives no vendor fee, and Orange Julius proprietary powders.

The supply chain is managed by USCI (IDQ subsidiary), which operates under a permanent margin commitment of 1.5% average for 2025 (reduced from historical highs of up to 8.5%). An independent accounting firm reviews USCI's performance annually, and the franchisee-elected SCAC has access to USCI's financial information.

In FY2024, IDQ derived $40.3M from the net sale of products, marketing kits, and supplier service fees (16% of IDQ's total $257.6M revenue). IDQ/affiliates receive 0%–10% of supplier sales to franchisees for various services.

ADQ estimates 80%–90% of establishment cost (excluding land) and 25%–35% of operating cost goes to ADQ-specified purchases. This is a high level of supplier control.

### Training Program
The training program is substantial: 174 total hours (32 classroom + 142 OJT) across 3 phases, plus the MTRA prerequisite and SERVSAFE certification. Phase 3 requires travel to Minneapolis, MN (4 days classroom). Training staff includes a Director, 7 field training consultants (8–40 years experience), and 20–40 restaurant training specialists.

Cost: $3,600/person for the full program + $200 MTRA + $200–$400 SERVSAFE + $2,600–$8,000 travel/living per person.

### Technology Stack
The technology burden is significant and growing. ADQ has designated sole-source vendors for all major technology components:
- **ParTech**: EPOS hardware/software ($20,800–$25,700 initial + $370–$413/month)
- **Fiserv**: Payment processing (2%–5% per transaction + $19.95/month)
- **Verifone**: Payment encryption (~$10/terminal/month + ~$80/device warranty)
- **Olo**: Mobile ordering (0.25% per transaction + included in monthly fee)
- **Punchh**: Loyalty platform (included in monthly fee)
- **Acumera**: Managed firewall ($51/month)
- **ValueLink/DQGC**: Gift cards (3% of redemptions)
- **Cineplex**: Digital menu boards (cost not specified)

Total mandatory technology spend: approximately $550–$700/month in fixed fees plus variable transaction fees.

### Marketing Fund Spending
FY2024 DQ sales promotion spending: 19.7% production, 66.8% media placement, 4.3% admin, 9.1% other. ADQ takes a 7% management fee on SPF payments up to 3% of gross sales. Spending is reviewed annually by an independent accounting firm. The MAC (Marketing Advisory Council) conducts its own annual review. ADQ has no fiduciary obligation to franchisees regarding fund spending and has sole discretion on allocation.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
**There is no protected territory.** ADQ grants only a site-only franchise — the right to operate at a single authorized location. No minimum area, no exclusive territory, no options for additional franchises, no rights of first refusal.

### Encroachment Risk
The encroachment risk is explicitly disclosed and significant:
- ADQ may establish new restaurants or stores near existing locations
- Territory operators (with older agreements) can subfranchise near your location without following ADQ's site clearance guidelines
- ADQ affiliates (DQ Grill & Chill, Orange Julius, etc.) may operate in your area under different marks
- ADQ makes "no commitment that ADQ will not establish new restaurants or stores in proximity to existing stores"

### Channel Restrictions
- Must sell only from your authorized location
- Must sell only ADQ-approved goods and services
- No alcohol, ATMs, video games, vending machines, lottery/gambling devices
- ADQ can set maximum, minimum, or other prices
- Must maintain smoke-free environment

### Relocation Policy
Qualifying Street franchisees may relocate within 2 miles if: (1) same location type; (2) ADQ consents; (3) meets construction timelines (30 days to start for captive, 180 for street; 90 days to open for captive, 120 for street). No initial franchise fee for relocations. Reduced CLF/SPF may apply during phase-in period.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 15 years
- **Renewal**: One additional term for the shorter of 15 years or remaining lease term
- **Materially different risk**: Renewal requires signing the then-current franchise agreement, which "may differ materially, including higher or additional fees." This means your royalty rate, advertising fee, transfer fee, and other terms could change at renewal. Pre-April 2007 agreements with 6% CLF: remain at 6% until modernization, then drop to 5%.
- **Renewal conditions**: 3–6 months advance notice, sign current form, modernize, good standing, right to premises, pay renewal fee, sign release, location approved.

### Transfer Provisions
- All transfers require ADQ's consent (not unreasonably withheld)
- Transfer fee: $6,000 (increasing $500 every 5 years from 2030)
- Transfer broadly defined to include the franchise, assets, revenues, ownership interests, or management agreements
- Transferee must sign then-current franchise agreement (which may differ materially)
- ADQ has right of first refusal to match any purchase offer
- In insolvency/bankruptcy: appraiser sets purchase price

### Termination and Death/Disability
- Franchisee may terminate for franchisor's material breach (30-day cure, effective 60 days after notice)
- Franchisor terminates with cause: cure periods of 24 hours (health/safety), 10 days (payments/reports), 30 days (other)
- Non-curable defaults: loss of premises, failure to reopen, abandonment, insolvency, conviction, intentional underreporting, 3rd default in 12 months, OFAC designation
- **Termination fee**: 2× last 12 months CLF — this is a significant liquidated damages provision
- Death/disability: all transfer requirements apply (no special accommodation)
- ADQ purchase option on termination/expiration: may purchase restaurant assets at appraised value

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term**: No involvement in QSR serving hamburgers (without alcohol) or any business with >10% revenue from ice cream, yogurt, frozen custard, fruit beverages, soft-serve, or frozen treats. This is broader than just DQ competitors.
- **Post-term**: 1 year after termination/expiration within 500 meters of authorized location (Street) or same building/venue (Captive-venue). Relatively narrow geographic scope.
- **State overrides**: California (may not be enforceable), North Dakota (limited), Washington (void if employee earns <$100K)

### Dispute Resolution
- Arbitration: mandatory for most disputes, in Minneapolis, MN (or mutually agreed place)
- Litigation: Federal District Court for District of MN or Hennepin County District Court
- Governing law: state where authorized location is situated
- Multiple state overrides exist (CA, IL, MN, ND, RI, WA) that may void out-of-state forum provisions

### Litigation History
One pending case: Project Lonestar, Inc. vs. ADQ (N.D. Texas, filed Feb 2025) — transfer-related dispute, breach of contract and fraud allegations. ADQ denies claims and has counterclaimed.

Three settled cases in reporting period, all resolved without material adverse findings against ADQ. No franchise relationship actions required to be disclosed in FY2024.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Leverage**: Negative working capital ($207.5M current liabilities vs $132.1M current assets). However, most current liabilities are non-debt: gift card liabilities ($102.7M) and committed advertising ($39.8M) are operating liabilities, not financial debt. No long-term debt on the balance sheet.

2. **Distributions**: Dividends to Berkshire exceed net income in 2024 ($110M vs $97.9M) and 2023 ($110M vs $93.9M). This deepens the retained deficit but is not unusual for wholly owned Berkshire subsidiaries.

3. **Related party**: $44.6M cash pooling receivable from BH Finance (Berkshire subsidiary). Interest income of $2.6M. This is essentially IDQ lending excess cash to Berkshire's centralized treasury — standard for Berkshire subsidiaries.

4. **Asset composition**: Primarily intangible — goodwill ($92.0M) and intangibles ($82.4M) represent 49% of total assets. Property and equipment is only $10.9M, reflecting the franchise model where franchisees own the physical assets.

5. **No going-concern**: Despite negative working capital and retained deficit, the auditor issued a clean opinion with no going-concern language. This is consistent with strong cash generation and Berkshire Hathaway backing.

### Material Overrides by State

**California** (p69-70):
- Liquidated damages clause may be unenforceable (Civil Code §1671)
- Post-term noncompete may not be enforceable
- Arbitration venue (Minneapolis) subject to CA law (B&P Code §20040.5)
- General release on renewal/transfer cannot waive Franchise Investment Law or Franchise Relations Act rights

**Hawaii** (p71):
- Filing notice required
- Anti-waiver provision

**Illinois** (p72):
- Illinois law governs franchise agreement
- Venue outside IL is void (except arbitration may be outside IL)
- Release/acknowledgments that violate IL Franchise Disclosure Act are void
- Note: ADQ (franchisor) separate financials not provided — only IDQ consolidated

**Maryland** (p73):
- **Initial fees deferred until franchisor completes pre-opening obligations and outlet opens** (financial assurance required by MD Securities Commissioner)
- Termination for bankruptcy may not be enforceable under federal law
- Claims under MD Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law may be brought in Maryland
- 3-year statute of limitations for franchise claims
- General release does not apply to MD franchise law liability

**Minnesota** (p74):
- No out-of-state litigation, no jury trial waiver, no consent to injunctive relief, no liquidated damages/termination penalties
- 90 days termination notice with 60 days cure; 180 days nonrenewal notice
- Transfer consent not unreasonably withheld
- General release cannot waive MN franchise law rights
- ADQ must undertake defense of trademark infringement claims

**North Dakota** (p75):
- **Termination fee provision deleted in its entirety**
- Noncompete enforceable only under certain conditions
- Arbitration at nearest AAA office (not Minneapolis)
- General release cannot waive ND franchise law rights
- Exemplary/punitive damages waiver and jury trial waiver may not be enforceable

**Rhode Island** (p76):
- Forum restriction outside RI void for RI Franchise Investment Act claims

**Virginia** (p77):
- Termination without reasonable cause is unlawful (VA Retail Franchising Act)
- Anti-waiver provision

**Washington** (p78-79):
- WA Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails in conflicts
- Arbitration/mediation must be in WA or mutually agreed place
- Release/waiver limited — cannot waive WA Act rights except in negotiated post-agreement settlement with independent counsel
- Transfer fees must reflect franchisor's reasonable estimated or actual costs
- **Noncompete void if employee earns <$100K/year** (adjusted for inflation)
- **No-poach restrictions void** — franchisor cannot restrict franchisee from soliciting/hiring employees of other franchisees or the franchisor

### States with Most Material Impact
- **North Dakota**: Termination fee entirely deleted
- **Maryland**: Fee deferral until opening
- **Washington**: Noncompete limited by income threshold; no-poach void; transfer fees must reflect actual costs
- **Minnesota**: Extensive protections — no liquidated damages, extended cure periods, no out-of-state litigation
- **California**: Noncompete and liquidated damages may be unenforceable
