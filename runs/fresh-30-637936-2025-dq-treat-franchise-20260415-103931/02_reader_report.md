# 02 Reader Report — DQ Treat Franchise (637936-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: American Dairy Queen Corporation (ADQ), Delaware corporation incorporated 1962, subsidiary of IDQ, which is wholly owned by Berkshire Hathaway Inc. Principal office: Bloomington, MN.
2. **Concept**: DQ Treat — quick-service retail selling DQ soft-serve, treat products, beverages, and limited approved food items. Two location types: "Street" (freestanding/strip mall) and "Captive-venue" (malls/airports/hospitals with 500K+ sqft).
3. **System size (direct-licensed)**: 727 outlets at end of 2024, down from 828 in 2022. Net shrinkage of 101 units over 3 years. Zero company-owned. Subfranchised territory operators add 358 more (down from 389 in 2022).
4. **Total investment**: $549,100–$1,057,700 (Captive-venue) or $848,450–$1,604,700 (Street), excluding land.
5. **Initial franchise fee**: $25,000 ($10,000 non-refundable deposit).
6. **Ongoing royalty**: 5% of Gross Sales (continuing license fee). Conversion and relocation franchisees may pay lower rates.
7. **Advertising/Sales promotion fee**: 5%–6% of Gross Sales on most products. Up to 6% on Orange Julius branded products.
8. **No FPR**: Item 19 contains no financial performance representations. ADQ does not disclose franchisee revenue, costs, or profitability.
9. **15-year initial term**, one renewal for the shorter of 15 years or remaining lease term. Renewal requires signing then-current agreement which may be "materially different."
10. **No exclusive territory**: Site-only franchise. No minimum area or territory. ADQ and affiliates may compete in your area.
11. **Financial health**: IDQ reports total assets $355.9M, total liabilities $275.6M, stockholder's equity $80.3M. Net income $97.9M (2024). But retained deficit of $(68.4M) due to dividend payouts exceeding earnings ($110M dividends vs $97.9M net income).
12. **Dispute resolution**: Mandatory arbitration in Minneapolis, MN (subject to state law overrides). Litigation venue: Federal District Court for District of Minnesota.
13. **Gag clause**: Some current and former franchisees signed confidentiality provisions restricting ability to speak about ADQ experience.
14. **Berkshire backing**: IDQ (Berkshire subsidiary) unconditionally guarantees ADQ's obligations to franchisees.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Item 17: Renewal, Termination, Transfer, Dispute Resolution (pages 53–56)

**Term & Renewal:**
- Initial term: 15 years (new franchise); lesser of 15 years or transferor's remaining term (transfer); renewal term from expiring agreement (renewal)
- One renewal for shorter of 15 years or remaining lease term
- Renewal conditions: 3–6 months advance notice; sign then-current agreement (may differ MATERIALLY, including higher fees); comply with modernization (Section 5.5); good standing with no history of substantial noncompliance; right to remain in possession; pay renewal fee; sign release; location approved
- **"Materially different" terms on renewal is a significant risk flag**

**Termination:**
- By franchisee: for material breach after 30-day notice to cure; effective 60 days after notice
- By franchisor with cause:
  - 24 hours to cure: defaults impairing goodwill or public health/safety
  - 10 days: failure to provide reports or pay amounts due
  - 30 days: all other curable defaults
- Non-curable defaults (immediate termination): loss of premises, failure to reopen, abandonment, insolvency, conviction, intentional underreporting, third default in 12 months, OFAC designation
- **Termination fee**: 2× last 12 months CLF (or prorated variants)

**Transfer:**
- All transfers require consent (not unreasonably withheld if requirements met)
- Transfer broadly defined: franchise, assets, revenues, ownership interest, management agreement
- Requirements: transferee meets qualifications, transfer fee ($6,000), amounts owed paid, compliance with agreement, all owners sign guarantee, release signed, training met, transferee signs then-current agreement (may be materially different), facility improvements completed
- Right of first refusal: ADQ can match any purchase offer (appraiser sets price in insolvency/bankruptcy)
- Purchase option on termination/expiration: ADQ may purchase or designate purchaser of restaurant assets

**Death/Disability:** Must comply with all transfer requirements.

**Noncompete:**
- During term: no involvement in any QSR serving hamburgers (without alcohol) OR any business generating >10% revenue from ice cream, yogurt, frozen custard, fruit beverages, soft-serve, or frozen treats ("Competitive Business")
- Post-term: 1 year after termination/expiration, within 500 meters of authorized location (Street) or same building/venue (Captive-venue)

**Dispute Resolution:**
- Arbitration: all disputes (except certain claims) must be arbitrated in Minneapolis, MN or mutually agreed place
- Litigation venue: Federal District Court for District of Minnesota or Hennepin County District Court, Minneapolis
- Choice of law: state where authorized location is situated
- Modification requires signed agreement by both parties (but ADQ can change menu, Operations Manual, and trademarks unilaterally)
