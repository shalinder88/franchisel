## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Entity**: We Are Crackin' LLC is an Oregon LLC formed December 29, 2016, wholly owned subsidiary of Elmer's Restaurants, Inc. (ERI). ERI has operated Elmer's Restaurants since 1960 from Eugene, Oregon.

2. **Three brand concepts**: Elmer's Breakfast Lunch Dinner® (full-service, 5,000–5,500 sq ft), Egg N' Joe® (breakfast/lunch, 3,500–4,000 sq ft), and Elmer's Kitchen Breakfast Brunch Mimosas® (brunch-forward, 3,500–4,000 sq ft).

3. **System size**: 25 total restaurants as of December 29, 2024 — 7 franchised Elmer's, 13 company-owned Elmer's, 3 company-owned Egg N' Joe (AZ), 2 company-owned Elmer's Kitchen (OR/ID). The system is heavily company-dominated with only 28% franchised.

4. **Investment range**: $1,146,750–$4,672,125 depending on brand concept. Initial franchise fee $40,000. No financing offered.

5. **Fee burden**: 4% royalty + 1% BPF (can increase to 2%) + up to 1% local marketing. Aggregate marketing cap 3%. Lost revenue damages clause (NPV of 5 years of royalties + BPF on termination) is a significant financial penalty.

6. **Item 19**: Gross Sales only — no cost, margin, or EBITDA data. Franchised Elmer's average $3.2M (2024), median $2.6M. 5 of 7 franchised units declined YoY. Elmer's Kitchen suffered ~30% revenue collapse. Egg N' Joe all declined.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: The smallest franchised Elmer's (Unit G) at $2.1M and the bottom of the company-owned range suggest a floor around $2.1M. Without cost data, profitability at these levels is unknowable from the FDD.

8. **Financial health**: Net income is strong ($1.1M in 2024), but distributions have exceeded net income in 2 of 3 years, eroding equity to just $201,153 — less than 18% of the minimum required franchisee investment. All company assets are pledged as collateral for parent company debts.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Contracting on the franchise side. Franchised count dropped from 14 (2022) to 7 (2024). Five units reacquired in 2024. Zero franchised openings projected for 2025. All 5 projected 2025 openings are company-owned.

10. **Territory**: No exclusive territory under the Franchise Agreement. ADA provides conditional Development Area protections with extensive franchisor carve-outs.

11. **Renewal**: 15-year term with one 10-year renewal. Renewal requires signing then-current FA "which may contain materially different terms," general release, and renewal fee.

12. **Disputes**: Mandatory arbitration in Eugene, OR. Oregon governing law. This out-of-state resolution requirement is flagged as Special Risk #1.

13. **Special risks**: (1) Out-of-state dispute resolution, (2) investment exceeds franchisor equity, (3) supplier control (90–97% of ongoing expenditures restricted).

14. **No litigation, no bankruptcy.**

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

We Are Crackin' LLC was formed in Oregon on December 29, 2016, as a wholly owned subsidiary of Elmer's Restaurants, Inc. (ERI), which has operated Elmer's restaurants since 1960. The franchise entity began offering franchises in June 2018, after receiving the franchise-related assets from the predecessor entity, Elmer's Franchise Systems, Inc. (EFS), which had offered franchises from 1982 to 2011. The trademarks are owned by a separate affiliate, We Own Breakfast, LLC (WOB), which licenses them to WAC under a 99-year agreement.

The franchisor primarily offers area development agreements for 4–10 restaurants rather than single-unit franchises. It "may choose not to enter into a Franchise Agreement with single unit operators."

Key management: Gerald Scott (President since May 2020; CEO of ERI since 2010), Michael Chamberlin (Manager; CFO of ERI since 2008), Jill Marie Ramos (Manager; VP of ERI since 2017).

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees**:
- Initial Franchise Fee: $40,000 (non-refundable)
- Development Fee: $20,000 × (restaurants − 1), typically $60,000–$180,000. Each subsequent FA gets $20,000 credit toward IFF.
- Branded Inventory: $1,500–$2,500
- Lease Addendum Review: up to $2,000

**Ongoing Fees**:
- Royalty: 4% of Gross Sales, weekly ACH
- Brand Promotion Fund: 1% of Gross Sales (can increase to 2%), weekly ACH
- Local Marketing: up to 1% of Gross Sales, monthly
- Marketing Cap: 3% aggregate (all marketing expenditures)
- Website Maintenance: not currently charged (est. $20/month if implemented)
- Technology Fee: not currently charged (est. $250/year if implemented)
- POS system monthly: $700–$1,000
- Various "not currently charged" fees subject to future implementation and CPI adjustment

**Event-Driven Fees**:
- Transfer Fee (FA): 50% of then-current IFF
- Transfer Fee (ADA): greater of 3% of purchase price or $20,000 (plus $2,500 non-refundable deposit, plus up to $3,000 expenses)
- Renewal Fee: 25% of then-current IFF
- Late Payment: up to 18% per annum + $100 per occurrence
- Inspection/Audit: actual costs (only for re-inspection or discrepancy >2%)
- Additional Training: $250/person/day (initial training for 4 at no cost)
- Non-Compliance Charge: up to $100 per failure
- **Lost Revenue Damages**: NPV of royalties + BPF for the lesser of 5 years or remaining term. Calculated on prior 12-month Gross Sales (or system average if <12 months). This is a significant termination penalty.

### Initial Investment

**Elmer's Restaurant** ($1,520,750–$4,672,125):
The dominant cost drivers are leasehold improvements ($700K–$2.2M) and FF&E ($500K–$1.5M). The building size of 5,000–5,500 square feet at $25–$60/sq ft/yr for rent places this as a mid-to-upper-tier restaurant build-out. Training expenses ($75K–$250K) are notably high, reflecting the 6-week training program plus pre-opening payroll ($45K–$112.5K). The low-end rent of $0 assumes the franchisee owns the building.

**Egg N' Joe / Elmer's Kitchen** ($1,146,750–$3,881,500):
A smaller-footprint concept (3,500–4,000 sq ft) with lower build-out costs but still substantial. Rent range $30K–$60K for 3 months reflects $27–$69/sq ft/yr.

**Area Development Agreement** ($60,000–$180,000):
Development fee only. Does not include per-restaurant costs.

No financing is offered directly or indirectly. No guarantee of obligations.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
The franchisor estimates that 50%–75% of the initial investment and 90%–97% of ongoing expenditures will be directed to purchases restricted by the franchisor. This is a very high level of supplier control. Specific designated suppliers include architects, kitchen designers, POS vendors, and suppliers for coffee, pancake mix, and waffle mix. Additional products require approved suppliers. The supplier approval process allows 60 days for review; silence is deemed denial. Testing fees up to $250 may be charged.

### Supply Chain Fees and Rebates
During fiscal year 2024, the franchisor (WAC) received no direct revenue from franchisee purchases. ERI received $23,284.18, all of which was passed through to vendors. However, rebates from approved suppliers totaled $93,847.91 (WAC) and $306,594.43 (ERI), for a combined $400,442.34 in rebate income. All rebates were contributed to the Brand Promotion Fund or annual conference. Specific rebate rates include per-gallon, per-pound, per-case, and percentage-of-sales arrangements.

Some designated vendors provide equipment at no cost: coffee vendor provides brewing equipment and maintenance, soft-drink vendor provides dispenser and maintenance, waffle mix vendor provides waffle irons and maintenance.

### Training Program
Initial training is approximately 6 weeks (43 classroom hours + 201 on-the-job hours) at Vancouver, WA or a designated location. Four Designated Managers must complete training: general manager, kitchen manager, and assistant manager/supervisor. If managers cannot complete training to franchisor's satisfaction, the FA may be terminated.

Grand opening training: free for 1st and 2nd restaurants (up to 2 weeks); $2,500–$25,000 for 3rd and subsequent restaurants.

Ongoing training: up to 5 days per year of required courses, plus annual conference and mid-year business meeting (up to 4 days). Franchisee pays all travel, lodging, and wages.

Training oversight: Susan Herzog (23 years experience) and John Peralta (29 years experience).

### Technology Stack
- POS system (designated vendor): $12,500–$40,000 to purchase + $700–$1,000/month maintenance
- Hosted software services: ~$250/year
- PC/internet/Wi-Fi maintenance: $800–$5,000/year
- Technology Fee: not currently charged (est. $250/year if implemented)
- Franchisor has independent access to POS system and all data; no contractual limitations on access
- Must upgrade hardware/software at franchisor's direction and expense

### Marketing Fund
The Brand Promotion Fund collects 1% of Gross Sales (can increase to 2%). In fiscal year 2024, spending was: 30.22% media placement, 20.28% administrative expenses, 16.92% sponsorships, 15.80% local restaurant marketing, 10.42% retained, 4.56% social media/eMarketing, 1.36% website maintenance, 0.44% gift card program. Zero percent was spent on franchisee solicitation or production.

The BPF is audited annually as part of WAC's audit. Company-owned restaurants contribute at the same or greater rate as franchisees. The franchisor owes no fiduciary obligation for BPF administration. 20.28% of BPF going to administrative expenses is notable.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition

**Under the Franchise Agreement**: No exclusive territory. The franchisee operates at a single approved location. The franchisor retains broad rights to establish competing restaurants anywhere, through any channel, under any name. The only protection is a right of first refusal if the franchisor plans to open or franchise within a 1-mile radius of the premises (and the franchisee is not in default).

**Under the Area Development Agreement**: A Development Area is defined (by cities, zip codes, counties, radius, etc.) with a minimum of 75,000–100,000 people per restaurant. During the ADA term, if the franchisee is in compliance, the franchisor will not own/operate, grant development rights, or grant franchises within the Development Area.

### Carve-Outs
The ADA protection has extensive carve-outs. The franchisor retains the right to:
1. Operate Special Venue Restaurants in the Development Area
2. Operate outside the Development Area without restriction
3. Operate under different trademarks (even identical products) in the Development Area
4. Use alternative distribution channels (internet, grocery stores) anywhere
5. Acquire or be acquired by Competitive Businesses
6. Exercise ROFR on franchise sales
7. All activities not expressly prohibited

### Channel Restrictions
Franchisees may not sell outside the restaurant (no internet, catalog, telemarketing, or direct marketing) without written consent. No carryout or delivery services unless expressly authorized in writing (temporary and revocable).

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- Initial term: 15 years
- Renewal: one additional 10-year term
- **Materially different terms risk**: Renewal requires signing the franchisor's then-current form of franchise agreement, "which may contain materially different terms and conditions than franchisee's original contract." This is a significant risk — after 15 years of investment, the franchisee may face substantially changed terms.
- Other renewal conditions: substantial compliance during term, full compliance at renewal date, remodel or secure approved substitute premises, sign general releases, pay renewal fee (25% of then-current IFF), provide written notice of intent.

### Transfer Provisions
- Broadly defined to include any transfer of ownership, control, management, or security interest.
- Franchisor approval required (not unreasonably withheld).
- Conditions include: all fees current, no violations in prior 60 days, transferee training, landlord consent, upgrade within 120 days, sign current FA, pay transfer fee, sign general release, franchisor approves price and financing terms with subordination to royalty obligations.
- Franchisor ROFR: 30 days to match any third-party offer.
- ADA transfer fee: greater of 4% of purchase price or $20,000, plus $2,500 non-refundable deposit and up to $3,000 expenses.

### Termination and Death/Disability
- Franchisor may not terminate without cause.
- Curable defaults: insurance (10 days), law violations (72 hours), payment (10 days), quality audit (15 days), other violations (30 days).
- Non-curable defaults: misrepresentation, site failure (180 days), opening failure, abandonment (3+ days), felony, Mark misuse, unauthorized transfer, bankruptcy, health/safety violations, 3+ defaults in 12 months.
- **Lost revenue damages**: On termination, franchisee owes NPV of royalties + BPF for 5 years or remaining term. This can be a six-figure penalty.
- Death/disability: transfer approval not unreasonably withheld if Managing Owner continues. If spouse not approved, 9 months to find alternate transferee.
- Franchisor purchase option: may purchase at fair market value upon Termination Event.

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term**: No involvement in any Competitive Business. No diversion of business. No duplication of System Standards.
- **Post-term**: 2 years, within 5-mile radius of the premises AND within 5-mile radius of any other Restaurant in the system.
- "Competitive Business" is broadly defined: any restaurant/food service with ≥10% of gross receipts from breakfast, or similar concept, menu, or business model to the franchisor's brands.
- State overrides: California (may not be enforceable), Washington (void for employees <$100K, contractors <$250K).

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: Mandatory, at a location within 50 miles of the franchisor's principal place of business (currently Eugene, OR). This is flagged as Special Risk #1.
- **Forum selection**: Federal or state court nearest to franchisor's principal place of business.
- **Governing law**: Oregon.
- State overrides: Illinois (venue outside IL void; IL law applies); Washington (arbitration/litigation in WA if purchased in WA; WA FIPA prevails).

### Litigation History
None. No litigation is required to be disclosed in Item 3.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Leverage/Distribution policy**: Distributions have exceeded net income in 2 of 3 years. In 2024, $1.4M distributed vs. $1.1M net income, reducing equity by $289,592. In 2022, $2.5M distributed vs. $1.25M net income, reducing equity by $1.27M. This aggressive distribution policy leaves the entity thinly capitalized.

2. **Asset composition**: 100% current assets (cash and receivables). No fixed assets, no goodwill, no intangible assets on the balance sheet. The company is a pure fee-collection entity.

3. **Assets pledged**: All company assets are pledged as collateral for "certain debts" of the Parent Company (ERI). The nature and quantum of these debts are not disclosed. This creates a risk that creditors of ERI could seize WAC's assets.

4. **Related party concentration**: 57% (2024), 48% (2023), 48% (2022) of advertising revenue comes from Parent Company-owned restaurant locations. As the system shifts from franchised to company-owned, the Parent Company increasingly pays advertising fees to itself through WAC.

5. **Unspent advertising funds**: $609,006 at year-end 2024, up from $478,498 in 2023. This represents unspent BPF contributions recorded as a liability.

6. **Uninsured deposits**: $601,700 (2024), $779,892 (2023), $308,662 (2022). The company exceeds FDIC insurance limits.

7. **No franchise sales in 2023 or 2024**: There was one franchise sale in fiscal 2022 ($40,000 deferred, recognized in 2023). The absence of new franchise sales in the most recent two years is consistent with the contraction in franchised units.
