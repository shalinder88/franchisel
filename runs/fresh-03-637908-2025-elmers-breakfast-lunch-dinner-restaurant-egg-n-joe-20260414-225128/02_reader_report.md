## Pass A — Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor entity**: We Are Crackin' LLC, an Oregon LLC formed December 29, 2016, wholly owned subsidiary of Elmer's Restaurants, Inc. (ERI). ERI has operated Elmer's Restaurants since 1960.
2. **Three brand concepts**: Elmer's Breakfast Lunch Dinner® (full-service, 5,000–5,500 sq ft), Egg N' Joe® (breakfast/lunch, 3,500–4,000 sq ft), Elmer's Kitchen Breakfast Brunch Mimosas® (brunch-forward, 3,500–4,000 sq ft).
3. **System size (as of Dec 29, 2024)**: 25 total restaurants — 7 franchised Elmer's + 13 company-owned Elmer's + 3 company-owned Egg N' Joe + 2 company-owned Elmer's Kitchen. System is heavily company-dominated.
4. **Initial investment**: Elmer's $1,520,750–$4,672,125; Egg N' Joe/Kitchen $1,146,750–$3,881,500. Initial franchise fee $40,000.
5. **Fee burden**: Royalty 4% of Gross Sales + BPF 1% (can increase to 2%) + local marketing up to 1%. Aggregate marketing cap 3%. Lost revenue damages clause (NPV of 5 years royalty+BPF on termination).
6. **Item 19**: Gross Sales only (no cost/profit data). Elmer's franchised range $2.1M–$6.7M (2024). Egg N' Joe $1.3M–$1.9M. Elmer's Kitchen showed sharp YoY decline (~30%). No EBITDA disclosure.
7. **Financial health warning**: Members' equity only $201,153 as of Dec 29, 2024 — dramatically less than the required franchisee investment ($1.1M–$4.7M). Distributions of $1.4M in 2024 exceeded net income of $1.1M, drawing down equity. Special risk #2 disclosed this.
8. **Franchisee attrition**: In 2024, 5 Elmer's franchisees were reacquired by the affiliate (all Southern Oregon Elmer's LLC) and 1 ceased operations. Franchised count dropped from 13 to 7. System is contracting on the franchise side.
9. **Territory**: No exclusive territory under the Franchise Agreement. ADA provides Development Area protections with extensive franchisor carve-outs (special venues, alternative channels, internet, acquisitions).
10. **Renewal risk**: 15-year term with one 10-year renewal. Must sign then-current FA which "may contain materially different terms." Must sign general release and pay 25% of then-current IFF.
11. **Dispute resolution**: Mandatory arbitration within 50 miles of Eugene, OR. Oregon governing law. Out-of-state dispute resolution flagged as Special Risk #1.
12. **Non-compete**: 2 years post-termination, 5-mile radius of premises and any other Restaurant. "Competitive Business" broadly defined (10%+ breakfast revenue).
13. **Confidentiality clause**: Franchisees have signed confidentiality clauses in last 3 fiscal years restricting ability to speak about their experience.
14. **No litigation, no bankruptcy.**

## Pass D — Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory

**Area Development Agreement**:
- No exclusive territory. May face competition from franchisees, company outlets, or other channels.
- Grants right to develop Restaurants within designated Development Area.
- Population minimum: 75,000–100,000 people per restaurant.
- During term (if in compliance): franchisor won't (1) own/operate, (2) grant development rights, or (3) grant franchises in the Development Area.
- **Extensive carve-outs**: Special Venue Restaurants (with separate consent); outside Development Area; different trade names; other distribution channels (internet, grocery); acquisitions of Competitive Businesses; being acquired by a Competitive Business; exercise of ROFR.
- ADA expires on last development date or last Development Period day.
- Failure to meet development schedule: franchisor may terminate ADA or reduce territorial protections.
- No options or rights to expand Development Area.

**Franchise Agreement**:
- No exclusive territory.
- Single approved location only; no relocation without consent.
- No sales outside restaurant (including internet, catalog, telemarketing) without written consent.
- No carryout/delivery unless expressly authorized.
- Franchisor retains broad rights to establish competing restaurants, distribute through any channel, acquire or be acquired by competitors.
- **Right of first refusal**: If franchisor plans to own/operate or franchise within 1-mile radius of premises (and franchisee is not in default).
- No other options or additional franchise rights.
- Franchisor does not currently operate or franchise under different trademarks with similar goods.

## Pass E — Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 15 years from Effective Date.
- **Renewal**: One additional 10-year term.
- **Renewal conditions**: Substantial compliance during term; full compliance on renewal date; maintain/remodel premises or secure approved substitute; sign then-current FA and ancillary documents ("which may contain materially different terms and conditions"); sign general releases; pay renewal fee (25% of then-current IFF); provide written notice of intent to renew.

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: If franchisor fails to materially comply and doesn't correct within 90 days of written notice (or if cure impossible, additional 90 days after notice of inability).
- **By franchisor without cause**: Not permitted.
- **By franchisor — curable defaults** (various cure periods):
  - Insurance failure: 10 days
  - Law violation: 72 hours
  - Payment failure: 10 days
  - Quality assurance audit failure: 15 days
  - Other FA/System Standard violations: 30 days
  - Breach of other agreements: applicable cure period
- **By franchisor — non-curable defaults**: Misrepresentation; failure to secure site in 180 days; failure to open on time; insufficient trained managers; abandonment (3+ days); felony conviction; adverse conduct affecting Marks; misuse of Marks; unauthorized transfer; loss of right to occupy premises; unauthorized disclosure of confidential information; failure to pay taxes; Gross Sales underestimation 3+ times; 3+ failures in 12 months or 2+ in 6 months; assignment for benefit of creditors/bankruptcy; anti-terrorism law violations; health/safety conditions; failure to pay third parties (including landlord).

### ADA Termination
- Curable defaults include: payment failure (5 days), development schedule non-compliance, financial document failure (10 days), cease exercising rights, insolvency events, judgment >$20,000, material misrepresentation, unauthorized transfer, felony, adverse conduct, repeated defaults.

### Post-Termination/Non-Renewal Obligations
- Pay all amounts due within 15 days, including lost revenue damages if applicable
- Close restaurant, cease using Marks
- Cancel fictitious names; deliver Mark materials within 30 days
- De-identify restaurant
- Cease using Online Presence, telephone numbers; transfer to franchisor
- Comply with de-identification and personal information disposal standards
- Give evidence of compliance within 30 days
- Cease using confidential information
- Comply with non-competition provisions

### Transfer
- **Definition**: Broadly defined — transfer, assignment, sale, gift, mortgage, pledge, security interest, loss of possession/control/management.
- **Franchisor approval**: Prior written approval required (not unreasonably withheld).
- **Conditions**: All fees current; no violations in 60 days before request through effective date; full info to franchisor; transferee not in Competitive Business; transferee completes training; landlord permits; transferee agrees to upgrade within 120 days; sign current FA; pay transfer fee (50% of IFF); sign general release; franchisor approves price/debt terms; subordination to royalty obligations.
- **ADA transfer conditions**: Similar plus $2,500 non-refundable deposit; transfer fee = greater of 4% of price or $20,000; $3,000 max for franchisor expenses.
- **Franchisor ROFR**: 30 days after receiving executed written offer.
- **Franchisor purchase option**: May purchase at fair market value upon Termination Event.
- **Franchisor assignment**: Without restriction.

### Death/Disability
- Approval not unreasonably withheld if Managing Owner continues.
- If spouse not approved, 9 months to find alternate transferee.

### Non-Competition
- **During term**: No involvement in Competitive Business; no diversion of business; no duplication of System Standards.
- **Post-term**: 2 years, 5-mile radius of premises AND 5-mile radius of any other Restaurant.
- "Competitive Business" = any restaurant/food service with ≥10% breakfast revenue, similar concept/menu, or that franchises/licenses same.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: Mandatory, within 50 miles of franchisor's principal place of business (currently Eugene, OR). Subject to applicable state law.
- **Forum selection**: Federal or state court nearest to franchisor's principal place of business (currently Eugene, OR). Subject to applicable state law.
- **Governing law**: Oregon (subject to applicable state law).
- **Modification**: Written only, signed by both parties.
- **Integration clause**: Only FA/ADA terms binding; representations outside FDD/FA may not be enforceable (subject to state law).
