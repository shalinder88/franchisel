## A. Executive snapshot

1. **Brand**: Ivybrook Academy — half-day preschool concept for ages ~1–6, emphasizing Reggio-Emilia-style curriculum, with optional private-kindergarten add-on. System is very small (41 total outlets end-2024) but has a large signed-but-unopened backlog (42 outlets).
2. **Franchisor entity**: Ivybrook Franchising, LLC, a North Carolina LLC formed March 26, 2015, HQ 9801 Suzanne Court, Weddington, NC 28173. Sole business since inception is franchising Ivybrook Academy.
3. **Ownership change (material)**: On **August 29, 2025**, private-equity-style aggregator **Crux I Ivybrook (Aggregator), LP** (Dallas, TX) acquired all membership interests in Ivybrook Franchising, LLC plus affiliates McWilliams Education Services, LLC and Catapult Industries, LLC, via a Delaware HoldCo/OpCo stack. Founders Drew and Jennifer McWilliams retain an interest via McWilliams IvyBrook Holdings, LLC (formed August 21, 2025).
4. **Investment range**: single-unit $540,700 – $869,860 (franchisor-captured $82,500); MUDA variant $575,700 – $929,860 (franchisor-captured $117,500). Construction/leasehold is the dominant swing factor at $277,300 – $518,860.
5. **Fee burden**: 7% royalty + 1% Brand Fund (rising to **2% in 2026**) + 1% local marketing minimum + $250/mo Technology Fee + up to 2% co-op ceiling, plus a dense set of compliance, audit, inspection, noncompliance ($1,000/week cap), transfer ($25,000 unauthorized), liquidated-damages, and holdover ($1,000/wk or 150% royalty) fees.
6. **Item 19 highlights**: Affiliate-owned Weddington NC school GR $1,487,947 / EBITDA $456,386 (31% margin) — a high outlier. Franchisee (2+ years) avg GR $858,259 / EBITDA $159,401 (19%); (3+ years) avg GR $967,875 / EBITDA $213,536 (22%).
7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: In the 2+ years pool (n=24), only 11 matched average GR (46%) and 11 matched average EBITDA (46%) — distribution is slightly left-skewed. Only 2 of 24 franchisees matched the affiliate's EBITDA.
8. **Financial health**: **Franchisor self-flags a Financial Condition Special Risk on the cover page**, pointing to Exhibit J. In this extraction run Exhibit J pages 168–197 are image-only and the balance sheet / income statement / audit opinion cannot be read from the text layer. Illinois's state addendum independently requires an **initial-fee deferral due to the franchisor's financial condition**, corroborating the Special Risk.
9. **Growth trajectory**: Franchised 17→26→33→40 (2021→2024) — roughly +7–9 per year. Company-owned flat at 1 (the Weddington school).
10. **Territory**: Lesser of 125,000 population or 3-mile radius. Exclusivity only against other Ivybrook Academy units under the Marks — franchisor retains broad reserved rights including solicitation into the Territory and online/alt-channel sales.
11. **Renewal**: 15-year initial term + two 10-year renewals, with explicit acknowledgement that the successor FA may be "materially different."
12. **Disputes**: Mediation and arbitration in Charlotte, NC; North Carolina governing law. Out-of-state DR is a state-highlighted Special Risk.
13. **Litigation**: None disclosed in Items 3 or 4.
14. **Special risks flagged on cover**: Out-of-State DR; Financial Condition; Supplier Control; Unopened Franchises.
15. **PE overlay to watch**: The Crux acquisition closed after the original issuance date but before the September 2025 amendment. The incoming aggregator structure has no track record with this brand, and future amendments — successor FA terms, Brand Fund increase to 2%, supplier consolidation — are the most likely sources of near-term franchisee burden.

## C. Supplier, operating, technology, reporting

**Supplier control**:
- Designated-supplier share = **70–90% of establishment purchases and 70–90% of ongoing purchases** (Item 8). Franchisor reserves unilateral right to become an exclusive supplier of anything.
- Mandatory channels: The Student Hub (affiliate Catapult), QuickBooks, franchisor-approved classroom furniture, signage, décor (franchisor sells), marketing-service supplier, architect and construction manager, approved branded-supply vendor.
- Supplier approval fee $500 per request; criteria not disclosed to franchisees.
- 2024 supplier rebates to franchisor ≈$33,821 (<2% of $2,171,363 total revenue). Catapult 2024 Student Hub revenue from franchisees ≈$36,000.
- Drew McWilliams is a licensed NC real estate agent; may receive referral fees.

**Operating burden**:
- CEO ≥10% ownership; ≥20 hours/week on-premises first year.
- School Administrator ≥40 hours/week on-premises.
- NAEYC membership + post-2-year accreditation required; NAREA membership required; BBB accreditation required.
- Brick-and-mortar only: no telemarketing, internet sales, mobile app, delivery, mail order.
- Minimum customer-evaluation score is a non-curable termination trigger, but the threshold is not disclosed.

**Training**:
- Pre-opening: 80 classroom hours (0 OJT). Phases: Startup/Real Estate 12 hrs; Marketing/Enrollment/Ops 24 hrs; Ivybrook University/School Setup 44 hrs. Held online + Charlotte, NC.
- Plus two weeks in-person at an approved training facility prior to opening.
- Up to 3 people trained under the $15,000 Training & Opening Support Fee; substitute Admin training $500/week.
- Trainers: Daniela McElhaney (VP Education & Curriculum) and Katherine Bell (VP Training).

**Technology**:
- Franchisor has **unilateral right to access any franchisee technology at any time without notice** (FA 12.6/12.7).
- Required hardware: office computers + iPads per classroom.
- Required software: The Student Hub (Catapult), QuickBooks.
- Annual optional tech support estimate $2,000–$3,000.
- Franchisor disclaims all liability for crashes/data loss.

**Marketing fund (Brand Fund)**:
- Started July 1, 2018. 1% of GR (to become 2% in 2026 per Item 6 remark).
- 2024 spend mix: Social Media Advertising 55%; Website 30%; Software Solutions 10%; Graphic Design 3%; Administrative 2%.
- **Not audited**. Franchisor-owned outlets not required to contribute on same basis. Brand Fund may borrow from franchisor. Surplus may be carried forward. Franchisor administers directly.

## D. Territory, competition, channels

- Territory = lesser of 125,000 population or 3-mile radius. Designated by franchisor at site acceptance; no contingency on sales volume; modifiable at renewal.
- Exclusivity is narrow: franchisor may not place another Ivybrook Academy School under the Marks in the Territory, but retains every other right — alternative channels, merchandise, solicitation into the Territory, operation of non-Ivybrook businesses inside the Territory, online sales (except Ivybrook School courses/curriculum which will not be sold online).
- Franchisee may NOT sell via mail order, phone, mobile app, internet, or any off-site channel, except approved off-site events.
- MUDA (Exhibit C) allows 2–3 outlets under an agreed schedule; no additional territorial protection beyond the per-unit Territory.

## E. Contract burden and legal mechanics

- **Term**: 15 years. **Renewals**: two 10-year terms, with renewal fee = greater of $10,000 or 10% of then-current initial franchise fee. Successor FA may be "materially different" — an explicit forward risk.
- **Termination by franchisee**: only for uncured franchisor material breach (30 days' notice + 90-day franchisor cure window).
- **Termination by franchisor, no-cause**: none.
- **Termination by franchisor, with cause**: extensive list of curable and non-curable defaults. Non-curable triggers include failure to open, failed training, material misrepresentation, felony or reputational conduct, Manual/trade-secret misuse, 3-day abandonment, unauthorized transfer, bankruptcy/insolvency, mark misuse, ≥2 reporting/fee failures in 12 months, ≥2 health/safety law violations, ≥2 under-report events >1%, license loss, governmental non-compliance letters, customer-evaluation score failures, uncurable defaults.
- **Transfer**: franchisor written consent required; ROFR; $10,000 transfer fee + $10,000 transfer-training fee; transferee signs then-current FA (may be materially different); general release required; $25,000 transfer damages for unauthorized transfer.
- **Death/disability**: interest must be transferred within 180 days to an approved transferee.
- **Noncompete during term**: owners + spouses/domestic partners prohibited from diverting business or working for a competitive business (subject to state law).
- **Post-term noncompete**: 2 years, within the greater of the Territory or 10 miles of the franchise location OR any other Ivybrook Academy Franchised Business (subject to state law).
- **Dispute resolution**: mediation then arbitration in Charlotte, NC; NC governing law. Integration/merger clause preserved. Litigation venue = Charlotte, NC (subject to state law overrides).
- **Litigation history**: none required.
- **State overrides**: Illinois, Minnesota, Virginia addenda in Exhibit B. Illinois additionally imposes an **initial-fee deferral** until franchisor has met pre-opening obligations, "due to our financial condition" — franchisor's own words.
