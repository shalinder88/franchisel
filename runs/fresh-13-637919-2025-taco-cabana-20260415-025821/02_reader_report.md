## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Entity**: Taco Cabana Franchising, Inc. is a Texas corporation formed January 17, 2023, wholly owned by Taco Cabana, Inc. (Delaware, est. 1991). The franchising entity does not operate any restaurants itself.
2. **System size**: 146 total units as of FY2024 end — 6 franchised (all in New Mexico, single franchisee), 140 company-owned (all in Texas). Net decline of 3 units in FY2024 (4 company closures, 1 company opening).
3. **Concept**: Fast casual Mexican restaurant, 2,500–3,200 sq ft, featuring tacos, fajitas, burritos, margaritas, etc.
4. **Investment range**: $1,266,500–$2,776,200 (single unit); $1,334,500–$2,684,000 (first unit under 5-unit DA).
5. **Fee burden**: Royalty 5% + Brand Fund 3% + Local Marketing 1% = 9% of Gross Sales, plus $1,840/month Technology Fee.
6. **Item 19**: 143 company units reporting. Average Gross Sales $1,490,385; Top 25% average $2,071,533; Median $1,451,946. COGS ~28.6%, Labor ~31.5%. No franchisee data included (6 franchisees provided no financial data).
7. **Franchise growth**: Zero franchise openings in 3 years (FY2022–2024). All 6 franchised units are legacy NM locations (Taco Cabana of New Mexico, Inc., Melvin & Mary Sloan).
8. **Financial health of franchisor entity**: Total assets $785,113; Revenue $895,112 (FY2024); Net income $713,397. Entity advanced $757K to related party in FY2024; subsequently advanced another $700K in Dec 2024/Jan 2025. Significant related-party cash drain.
9. **Term**: 10 years from opening, 1 successor term of 10 years. Renewal requires signing then-current form (terms "likely will differ materially").
10. **Territory**: Protected Area defined (urban: 4 blocks; suburban: 30K+ residents; rural: 1 mile radius). Significant carve-outs: ghost kitchens, captive market locations, Nick the Greek affiliates may operate in Protected Area.
11. **Dispute resolution**: Texas law; litigation only in Texas. No arbitration.
12. **Noncompete**: 2 years post-term, 10 miles from location. Competitive Business = 10%+ Tex-Mex/Mexican sales.
13. **Litigation/Bankruptcy**: None disclosed.
14. **Special risk**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Texas).
15. **Ownership**: CEO Anil Yadav also runs NTG Franchising (Nick the Greek) and Yadav Enterprises (multi-brand operator — Jack in the Box, Denny's, TGI Friday's). Concentration of management attention across multiple concepts.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 10 years from opening date
- **Renewal**: 1 successor term of 10 years
- **Renewal conditions**: Timely notice; substantial compliance; not in default; renovate to current standards; complete additional training; pay successor franchise fee (then-current IFF for new franchisees); sign then-current Franchise Agreement
- **MATERIALLY DIFFERENT TERMS RISK**: "You also must sign our then-current form of Franchise Agreement, the terms of which likely will differ materially from your original Franchise Agreement, including, without limitation, those relating to royalty fees and advertising obligations."

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: Not applicable (no voluntary termination right)
- **By franchisor without cause**: Not applicable
- **By franchisor with cause**: Upon default
- **Curable defaults**: 30 days to cure (10 days for monetary defaults)
- **Non-curable defaults**: Failure to complete ITP; failure to open by Opening Date; insolvency/bankruptcy; material breach of covenants; material misrepresentation; conviction/no contest plea; unauthorized transfer; cross-default; refuse audit; health/safety jeopardy; loss of right to operate at location; 3+ consecutive days or 5+ total days non-operation; 2+ defaults in 12 months
- **Franchisor option on default**: May elect to terminate Protected Area rights instead of full termination

### Transfer
- Broadly defined (sale, assignment, pledge, mortgage, etc.)
- Franchisor consent required (not unreasonably withheld)
- Conditions: compliance; transfer fee ($10,000); simultaneous transfer of related agreements; qualified transferee; sign assignment agreement; modernize/upgrade; general release; reasonable sales price; transferee must complete ITP
- **Right of first refusal**: 30 days after notice (does not apply to convenience-of-ownership or spousal/child transfers)
- **Option to purchase on termination/expiration**: 30 days to exercise; fair market value

### Death/Disability
- 3 months to apply for transfer consent; 6 months to dispose; otherwise franchisor may terminate

### Noncompetition
- **During term**: No interest in Competitive Business (10%+ Tex-Mex/Mexican food sales or similar trade dress)
- **Post-term**: 2 years, within 10 miles of Franchised Location or any other Taco Cabana Restaurant
- **Post-term also**: Cannot sell/lease the location to anyone intending to operate a Competitive Business for 2 years
- **Exception**: Pre-existing businesses listed in Exhibit A to the FA

### Dispute Resolution
- **Governing law**: Texas (subject to state law)
- **Forum**: Franchisee can only file suit where franchisor's principal offices are located (San Antonio, TX)
- **No arbitration or mediation required**
- **Jury trial waiver**: Not specifically noted in Item 17 table (would need FA review to confirm)

### Other Provisions
- No modification without signed agreement (but franchisor may modify the System)
- Integration/merger clause (subject to state law; not intended to disclaim FDD representations)
- Franchisor has unrestricted right to assign
