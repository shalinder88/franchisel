# Reader Report — Taco Bell Express / Taco Bell (Filing 637943)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: Taco Bell Franchisor, LLC, a post-securitization SPV (formed 2016). Operational manager is Taco Bell Corp. (TBC), a subsidiary of YUM! Brands, Inc. Securitization transactions occurred in 2016, 2018, 2021, with another planned for 2025.
2. **Concept**: Taco Bell Express — smaller-format quick-service Mexican food units (Express Units, Power Pumpers, In-Lines) situated in non-traditional locations (colleges, airports, gas stations, dual-brand facilities). Not the Traditional Taco Bell offering.
3. **System size**: 224 licensed Express Units + 14 company-owned (all co-branded KFC/TB operated by KFC) = 238 total at end of 2024. System is shrinking: net loss of 27 licensed units over 3 years (251→224).
4. **Investment range**: Express Units $287,350–$855,700; Power Pumpers $354,850–$770,700; Existing Units $152,250–$1,766,250+.
5. **Initial license fee**: $22,500 (Express); $22,500–$45,000 (Power Pumpers).
6. **Royalty**: 10% of Gross Sales — among the highest in QSR. No ad fund contribution required (licensees buy POP materials at $275/quarter).
7. **Item 19**: NO financial performance representation made. Zero FPR disclosure.
8. **Term**: 10 years (Express); 10–20 years (Power Pumpers); 9 months to 5 years (KT Units). No renewal rights; successor policy exists but can be modified/cancelled at any time.
9. **Territory**: Non-exclusive. No territorial protection whatsoever. Franchisor and YUM affiliates (KFC, Pizza Hut, HBG) can open anywhere.
10. **Litigation/Bankruptcy**: None disclosed in Items 3 and 4.
11. **Financial health**: Franchisor entity generates $727M+ revenue (2024), $729M net income. Virtually no operating expenses (SPV structure — all costs borne by TBC under management agreement). $708M distributed to member in 2024.
12. **Projected openings**: Only 8 new licensed units projected for next fiscal year (CA, FL, GA, HI, TX). No new company-owned projected.
13. **Key risk**: Shrinking system with high royalty rate, no FPR, no territory protection, and "materially different" successor terms risk.

## Pass E: Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Renewal/Successor
- **No renewal rights** under License Agreement or KT Successor Agreement.
- Successor policy exists but is subject to modification or cancellation at any time — purely discretionary.
- Successor requirements: operational/financial approval, Unit upgrade (offset/scrape-rebuild/major remodel), successor fee ($11,250 or ½ current initial fee), sign release.
- **MATERIALLY DIFFERENT TERMS**: Successor agreement may have materially different terms and conditions than original.
- Extension: discretionary, $250–$1,000/month extension fee.

### Termination
- **By licensee**: Treated as a DEFAULT. Triggers liquidated damages (greater of $100,000 or 11% of last 12 months Gross Sales).
- **By franchisor without cause**: Can terminate if key provisions declared invalid/unenforceable.
- **By franchisor with cause**: Any material breach. 30-day cure period for monetary/operational defaults.
- **Non-curable defaults**: Untrained manager, denial of access, unauthorized transfer, loss of possession, felony conviction, material misrepresentation, bankruptcy, pattern of repeated defaults.
- **Post-termination**: Stop using Trademarks/System, de-identify Unit, pay liquidated damages if applicable. Franchisor can require you to assign interest (sell premises or assign lease) and vacate.

### Transfer
- Requires prior written consent. Extensive conditions: no default, all amounts current, transferring licensee signs release, transfer fee paid, proposed transferee completes training and signs current License Agreement.
- **Transfer fees**: $7,500 per transfer (1-5 units); $1,500/unit (6+); Greater of standard or $150,000 for Relationship Agreement transfers.
- **Franchisor ROFR**: 30 days to match any third-party offer on any or all units.
- **5-year post-APA lock**: For 5 years after purchasing units from franchisor, cannot transfer those assets without consent (may be withheld in sole discretion). Franchisor has right of first offer.

### Death/Disability
- Heirs must notify within 120 days of election to continue. Franchisor approval required. If disapproved, 6 months to sell. If not sold, franchisor has option to purchase at fair market value.

### Non-Compete
- **During term**: No interest in Mexican-style food restaurant (except ≤10% publicly traded).
- **Post-term**: Same restriction for 1 year after termination due to breach, within 10-mile radius of Unit.
- **Relationship Agreement**: Broader — no interest in QSR, Mexican casual dining, or Mexican quick casual dining (during term and 1 year post).

### Dispute Resolution
- **No arbitration/mediation** under License Agreement.
- **Forum**: Federal or state courts in Orange County, California.
- **Governing law**: New York.
- **Cross-default**: Any breach of any agreement with franchisor/affiliates is deemed breach of all agreements.
- **Attorneys' fees**: Prevailing party.

### Key Kill Switches
1. No renewal rights — successor entirely discretionary and revocable.
2. Liquidated damages on voluntary termination ($100K minimum).
3. Cross-default across all agreements.
4. Materially different successor terms.
5. Franchisor ROFR on all transfers.
6. 5-year transfer lock on acquired units.
7. 95% FMV buyout option under Relationship Agreement.
