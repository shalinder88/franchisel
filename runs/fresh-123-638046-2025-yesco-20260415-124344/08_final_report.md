## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: 57 franchised + 42 company-owned = 99 total YESCO outlets as of December 31, 2024. The franchised network spans 24 US states and 4 Canadian provinces. Company-owned units (operated by affiliate YESCO LLC) have been stable at 42 since at least 2022.

2. **Entity structure**: YESCO Franchising LLC is a Utah LLC formed December 20, 2010, whose sole managing member is Young Electric Sign Company ("YESCO"), a Utah corporation in business since 1920. Four affiliates play key roles: YESCO LLC (operates company-owned outlets and the National Accounts Program), YESCO Financial Solutions LLC (franchisee financing), YESCO Administration LLC (owns the YESCO marks and software, holds a $7.47M non-interest-bearing loan from the franchisor), and YESCO Canada ULC (Canadian franchisor since July 2020).

3. **Ownership history**: No predecessors. The franchising entity was formed in 2010 and began offering franchises in March 2011. The Canadian franchise operations were sold to YESCO Canada ULC effective July 1, 2020. The parent company Young Electric Sign Company has operated since 1920 — over a century of sign industry experience.

4. **Investment range**: $65,000 to $432,200, with the franchise fee at $50,000 and vehicles ($0–$185,000) being the largest variable. YFS may finance up to 66.67% of the franchise fee at 4–8% above prime.

5. **Fee burden**: 6% royalty (minimum $1,000/month), 2% local advertising (may increase to 3%), up to 3% national advertising (currently not implemented), combined advertising cap 3%. National Lead Admin Fee up to 6%. The tiered royalty under the Early Renewal Program can reduce effective rates to 2% for high-revenue tiers.

6. **Item 19 highlights**: 50 of 57 franchised businesses included. Average Gross Revenue $1,033,005; median $776,958. Top quartile averaged $2.49M; bottom quartile $188K. System total: $51.9M. YESCO National Service contributed $7.97M (15.4%). No cost/expense data disclosed. Average GR per invoice: $1,425 (consistent across quartiles — revenue differences are volume-driven).

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: Bottom quartile average ($187,862) and median ($124,556) fall well below the annual Territory Sales Quota of $250,000. At least 12 of the 50 measured franchisees face territory reduction or termination risk. The average number of territories in the bottom quartile is 1.6 vs. 8.7 in the top quartile, suggesting multi-territory expansion is a major revenue driver.

8. **Financial health**: Franchisor reported net income of $1,702,798 on $4,002,944 in revenue (43% net margin) for 2024. Total assets $9.53M, member's equity $8.17M, total liabilities $1.36M. Clean audit opinion. However, $7.47M (78% of total assets) consists of a non-interest-bearing loan to affiliate YESCO Administration LLC with no disclosed repayment schedule, growing approximately $1.5M per year. Cash is declining slowly ($719K → $653K over 2 years) as operating cash flow ($1.54M) is largely transferred to the affiliate ($1.70M).

9. **Growth trajectory**: The franchised network has contracted slightly: 60 units (2022 start) → 57 (2024 end). In 2024: 2 opened (both Florida), 1 non-renewal (Tennessee), 1 ceased operations (New Hampshire territory combined with Oklahoma). The franchisor projects 5 new franchised units in 2025 (Florida, New York, Texas, Alberta, Quebec) with 2 additional already signed. If realized, this would represent the first net growth since 2021.

10. **Territory**: Non-exclusive but conditionally protected. Territory typically contains ~10,000 businesses. Protection lapses if Territory Sales Quota not met ($400K in first 24 months, $250K annually thereafter). Franchisor retains extensive reservation of rights including alternative channels (internet, wholesale, big box stores), acquisition of competitors, and franchising outside territory.

11. **Renewal**: 10-year initial term. Standard renewal offers two additional 5-year terms at $15,000 each. Early Renewal offers a 10-year extended term with tiered royalty benefits. Both require signing the then-current agreement, which explicitly may have "materially different terms and conditions." General release of claims is required.

12. **Disputes**: Utah venue, Utah governing law, 1-year limitation of action, jury trial waiver. Nine state addenda provide overrides: Illinois (IL venue/law), Maryland (MD court for MD claims), Minnesota (no venue outside MN, no jury waiver, trademark indemnification), North Dakota (comprehensive protections — no noncompete, no venue outside ND, no jury waiver, prevailing party fees), and others.

13. **Special risks**: Mandatory minimum payments (royalty $1K/month regardless of revenue), spousal guaranty (making spouse liable for all franchise obligations), sales performance requirement (TSQ), and out-of-state (Utah) dispute resolution.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

YESCO Franchising LLC is a pure-play franchisor — it does not operate sign/lighting businesses directly. Company-owned operations are conducted by affiliate YESCO LLC. The franchisor's revenue comes entirely from franchise fees ($796K in 2024) and royalties ($3.21M in 2024). The affiliate YESCO Administration LLC owns the intellectual property (marks, software) and is licensed to the franchisor. This creates a three-entity structure where (1) YESCO Administration owns IP, (2) YESCO Franchising is the franchisor, and (3) YESCO LLC operates company outlets and the National Accounts Program.

The managing member is Young Electric Sign Company, a century-old Utah corporation. This legacy provides brand credibility and industry infrastructure but also introduces related-party complexity — particularly the $7.47M loan to YESCO Administration LLC.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Franchise fee: $50,000 (lump sum at signing, non-refundable except 40% on training failure)
- Area development: $25,000–$50,000 for existing franchisees acquiring additional territories
- Grand opening advertising: $1,000 minimum

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Royalty: Greater of $1,000/month or 6% of Gross Revenue (auto-debited)
- Local advertising: 2% of Gross Revenue annually (can increase to 3% on 60 days notice)
- National advertising: Up to 3% when implemented (currently $0 for US operations)
- Combined advertising cap: 3% of Gross Revenue
- YESCO Software: Free for first 10 users, $100/user/month thereafter
- Data storage: $0.25/GB/month
- Website: $20/month (optional)
- Email: $150/user/year (currently free but may be charged)

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Transfer fee: Greater of $5,000 or 4% of last year's Gross Revenue
- Successor franchise fee: $15,000 per renewal term
- Audit fee: $3,000–$5,000 (triggered if Gross Revenue understated by 2%+)
- Late fees: 1.5%/month + $50 per late payment/report
- Insurance admin: 15% markup if franchisor procures on your behalf
- Additional training: $800/day + travel
- Management services: 5–10% of monthly Gross Revenue + 15% admin fee
- National Lead Admin Fee: Up to 6% of amounts from National Leads
- Territory encroachment: Disgorgement of revenue + admin costs

**Tiered Royalty (Early Renewal Program only):**

| Monthly GR per Agreement | Rate |
|--------------------------|------|
| $0–$41,666 | 6% |
| $41,667–$62,500 | 4% |
| $62,501+ | 2% |

Minimum: $1,000 per agreement per month. Must be in full compliance; non-compliance reverts to 6%.

### Initial Investment ($65,000–$432,200)

The investment range is wide due primarily to the vehicle component ($0–$185,000). A franchisee who already owns suitable vehicles and operates from a home office initially could invest as little as $65,000. The high end reflects a franchisee starting from scratch with multiple vehicles, leased commercial space, and full buildout.

Key components: Franchise fee ($50,000), vehicles ($0–$185,000), deposits ($0–$35,000), additional funds/3 months ($0–$50,000), insurance ($1,000–$20,000), rental improvements ($0–$20,000), inventory/tools ($8,500–$20,000).

The additional funds estimate ($0–$50,000 for 3 months) does not include owner salary or draw. Based on YESCO LLC's "over 90 years of experience operating similar businesses."

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Franchisees must use YESCO Software and designated enterprise email. The franchisor has the right to designate single-source suppliers for computer systems, signs, design professionals, media services, uniforms, and the office branding package. Alternative suppliers may be requested through a 30-day written approval process; a review fee may be charged.

Franchisor and affiliates may receive rebates of up to 10%+ of supplier sales to franchisees. In 2024, the franchisor received no such rebates, but YESCO LLC collected $334,306 in National Lead Admin Fees. One officer owns an interest in the parent company Young.

Estimated 70–85% of startup purchases and 20–60% of ongoing costs come from approved/specified sources. No purchasing cooperatives or buying groups are permitted.

### Training
The training program is substantial: 35–50 hours of online pre-training self-study, followed by 76–144 hours of classroom training and 17–48 hours of on-the-job training, totaling 128–242 hours. Training is conducted in Salt Lake City, Utah or Las Vegas, Nevada. Subjects cover orientation, administration/operations, marketing, strategic planning, sales, software (Servizio), technician basics, and general management/certification.

No tuition for the first 3 attendees. Franchisee bears all travel, lodging, and meal costs. Must complete 30 days before opening. Additional/refresher training at $800/day plus travel. Annual conference mandatory (1–2 days).

Principal instructor: Madeline Christopher (Training Development Director since July 2022, MS in Educational Psychology). Trade professionals and affiliate staff assist.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
Territory is non-exclusive per Section 2.2.1 of the Franchise Agreement. However, while the franchisee is in compliance with all obligations, the franchisor will not establish another YESCO business within the territory offering the same products/services under the Marks. Typical territory contains approximately 10,000 businesses, determined using 2012 US Census business density data (excluding certain non-relevant business categories). Smaller markets may have a minimum of 1,000 businesses.

Territory boundaries are fixed during the term — the franchisor cannot unilaterally modify them. However, the Territory Sales Quota provides an indirect mechanism for territory adjustment.

### Territory Sales Quota
- First 24 months: $400,000 in Gross Revenue
- Each 12-month period thereafter: $250,000
- Failure consequences: (1) reduce territory to an area franchisor determines you can appropriately service, (2) sell an additional franchise in your territory, or (3) terminate the Franchise Agreement

This is a significant risk given that the bottom quartile of franchisees (12 businesses) averaged only $187,862 in 2024, well below the $250,000 annual threshold.

### Channel Restrictions
- No internet channels permitted
- No advertising, solicitation, or sign patrols outside territory
- Limited exception: may accept outside leads if not in another assigned territory, under 3-hour one-way travel, no subcontractor needed
- No buying cooperatives allowed
- No separate website without express written permission

### Franchisor Reservation of Rights
The franchisor reserves extensive rights to operate or franchise in or out of the territory through alternative channels (internet, wholesale, big box stores, catalogs, mail order, television), acquire competing businesses, and license YESCO Software for non-sign businesses within the territory. These reservations significantly limit the practical exclusivity of the territory grant.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
The initial franchise term is 10 years. Two renewal paths exist:

**Standard Renewal**: Two additional 5-year terms at $15,000 each. YFS may finance at 3% interest for 36 months. Requirements: not in default, no money owed, sign then-current agreement and general release.

**Early Renewal**: Renew all existing agreements 120 days before first agreement expires. Each agreement gets a 10-year Extended Successor Term. Fee is $15,000 per agreement, financed at 0% interest. Includes the tiered royalty program.

**Critical risk**: Both paths require signing the then-current franchise agreement, which "may have materially different terms and conditions than your original contract." Territory boundaries may change. Royalties and fees adjusted to match current new-franchisee terms.

### Transfer Provisions
All transfers require franchisor approval (not unreasonably withheld if conditions met). Transfer fee: greater of $5,000 or 4% of last year's Gross Revenue. Conditions include: transferee qualified and trained; franchisee in full compliance with all debts paid; franchisee and principal owners execute general release; transition plan approved; transfer terms not harmful to business profitability. Right of first refusal: 30 days on same terms. Option to purchase at fair value before offering to third parties.

### Termination
The franchisor cannot terminate without cause. Curable defaults (30-day cure): failure to pay, submit reports, grant access, comply with operational requirements. Non-curable defaults (immediate on notice): bankruptcy, abandonment, felony conviction, unauthorized transfer, material misrepresentations, multiple defaults, falsification of records, failure to meet TSQ, noncompete violation.

### Death/Disability
Heirs can operate if approved by franchisor. Franchisor may (but is not required to) appoint interim manager at 5–10% of monthly Gross Revenue plus 15% admin fee.

### Noncompete
During term: franchisee and owners cannot operate a similar business. Post-term: 2 years within own territory or any other franchisee's territory. The geographic scope (any franchisee's territory) is notably broad. North Dakota addendum: generally unenforceable. Minnesota addendum: subject to state franchise law protections.

### Dispute Resolution
Pre-litigation: face-to-face meeting required. Non-binding mediation may occur before litigation. Venue: Salt Lake County, Utah. Governing law: Utah. 1-year limitation of action (Section 18.9). Jury trial waiver (Section 18.10).

**State overrides** — 9 states with addenda provide significant protections:
- **Illinois**: Must litigate in IL; IL governing law; limitation of action deleted
- **Indiana**: Jury trial waiver not enforceable; release cannot waive IN franchise law
- **Maryland**: MD federal court for MD franchise claims; 3-year SOL; release excludes MD law
- **Minnesota**: Cannot litigate outside MN; no jury waiver; no liquidated damages; trademark indemnification required; 90 days termination notice (60 cure) / 180 days non-renewal; 3-year SOL
- **New York**: Article 33 rights preserved; NASAA legend
- **North Dakota**: Most comprehensive — noncompete generally unenforceable; no venue outside ND; no jury waiver; no punitive damages waiver; no liquidated damages; ND law governs; ND SOL applies; prevailing party attorney fees; no release on renewal
- **Rhode Island**: Venue/law restrictions void for RI franchise claims
- **Virginia**: Reasonable cause required for termination
- **Wisconsin**: 90 days notice (60 cure); 10 days for nonpayment; WI Fair Dealership Law supersedes

### Litigation History
One prior action: Ontario Superior Court (2015) against a Canadian franchisee for breach of contract and noncompete violations. Counterclaim included misrepresentation and franchise act violations. YESCO obtained partial summary judgment (2017). Fully settled (2018). No current material litigation.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Overview
YESCO provides a detailed FPR covering 50 of 57 franchised businesses that were continuously operating during calendar year 2024. "Continuously operating" is defined as achieving monthly Gross Revenue of $1,000 or more in at least 10 of 12 months. Seven businesses were excluded: 2 opened after January 1, 2024 (the Florida openings), and 4 that did not meet the continuous operation threshold. Two Arkansas businesses with the same ownership are combined as one unit.

### Table 1 — Gross Revenue by Quartile (2024)

| Quartile | Count | Average | Median | Min | Max |
|----------|-------|---------|--------|-----|-----|
| Top | 12 | $2,486,985 | $1,973,614 | $1,436,094 | $4,464,693 |
| Second | 13 | $1,031,269 | $897,182 | $806,339 | $1,338,304 |

The spread is enormous — the highest-grossing franchise ($4.46M) generates 48x the revenue of the lowest ($92K). Only 19 of 50 businesses (38%) met or exceeded the overall average of $1.03M, indicating the average is skewed upward by high-performing multi-territory operators.

### Table 2 — National Service Revenue
YESCO National Service contributed $7,974,585 to franchised business revenue (15.4% of the $51,891,579 system total). This represents a significant and somewhat captive revenue stream channeled through the affiliate.

### Table 3 — Time in Business
Average time in business: 8.7 years (median 8.8). The top quartile averaged 10.8 years vs. 7.8 in the bottom quartile, suggesting that established businesses do better, as expected. However, the bottom quartile average of 7.8 years indicates that long tenure alone does not guarantee strong performance.

### Table 4 — Territories per Business
This is the most telling performance predictor. Top quartile businesses average 8.7 territories each; bottom quartile averages 1.6. Revenue appears strongly correlated with territory count — multi-territory expansion is the primary growth lever in this system. The median business holds 3.0 territories.

### Table 5 — Daily Leads
Top quartile: 104 average daily leads. Bottom quartile: 9. The sign patrol process (territory canvassing to identify signage needing repair) drives lead generation. Businesses with more territories generate dramatically more leads.

### Table 6 — Daily Sales Activities
Similar pattern: top quartile 130 daily activities vs. bottom quartile 10. Activity levels track closely with revenue.

### Table 7 — Gross Revenue per Invoice
Notably consistent across quartiles: $1,624 (top) vs. $1,357 (bottom). Average ticket size is not a differentiator — the business is volume-driven, not price-driven.

### Critical Caveats
- **No cost or expense data is disclosed.** This is the single most important limitation. Without COGS, labor costs, vehicle expenses, insurance costs, or overhead data, a prospective franchisee cannot estimate profitability from this FPR alone.
- Revenue figures are based on franchisee self-reporting and are not audited or verified by the franchisor.
- Canadian revenue is converted at Bank of Canada daily close rates.
- Expenses excluded from analysis: commissions, sign patrol/sales administration, office expenses (accounting, billing, postage), indirect operating costs (rent, utilities), truck operations (depreciation, fuel, repairs), advertising/marketing.

## J. Unresolveds

1. **Affiliate loan concentration risk** — Severity: HIGH
   The $7.47M non-interest-bearing loan to YESCO Administration LLC represents 78% of total assets and grows ~$1.5M/year. No repayment schedule or terms are disclosed beyond the note stating the affiliate "acts as an agent in paying the bills and collecting some receivables." If this affiliate becomes unable to repay, the franchisor's solvency would be materially threatened. Source: Exhibit F, Note 6 (p199).

2. **TSQ vs. bottom-quartile performance** — Severity: HIGH
   The annual TSQ of $250,000 exceeds the bottom quartile average ($187,862) and median ($124,556). At least 12 of 50 measured franchisees appear to be below or near the TSQ threshold. The FDD discloses that failure to meet TSQ can result in territory reduction, additional franchising in the territory, or termination. The disclosed consequence is severe but the extent of enforcement is unknown. Source: Items 12, 19.

3. **National Advertising Fund implementation uncertainty** — Severity: MEDIUM
   No US National Advertising Fund currently operates. The franchisor reserves the right to establish one at up to 3% of Gross Revenue on 30 days notice. This represents a potential future cost increase that is entirely at the franchisor's discretion and timing. Source: Items 6, 11.

4. **Servizio trademark not federally registered** — Severity: MEDIUM
   The Servizio service mark is unregistered. If challenged, franchisees may need to change at their own expense. The Servizio platform appears central to daily operations. Source: Item 13 (p38).

5. **Materially different terms on renewal** — Severity: MEDIUM
   Renewal requires signing the then-current franchise agreement which may have "materially different terms and conditions." Territory boundaries may change. This means a 10-year renewal decision is made without full knowledge of what the renewal terms will be. Source: Item 17 (p44).
