# Reader Report — YESCO Franchising LLC (638046-2025)

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System**: YESCO Franchising LLC operates a sign and lighting service/maintenance franchise system with 57 franchised + 42 company-owned = 99 total outlets as of Dec 31, 2024.
2. **Entity**: Utah LLC, formed Dec 20, 2010; managing member is Young Electric Sign Company (est. 1920). Affiliates: YESCO LLC, YFS (financing), YESCO Administration LLC (marks/IP), YESCO Canada ULC.
3. **Leadership**: Joshua M. Young (President since 2016), Samuel Fisher (SVP since 2021).
4. **Investment**: $65,000–$432,200 total; franchise fee $50,000 (area dev $25K–$50K).
5. **Fee burden**: 6% royalty (min $1,000/mo), local advertising 2% (can increase to 3%), national ad up to 3% (combined cap 3%), national lead admin up to 6%.
6. **Item 19**: FPR provided — 50 franchised businesses, avg Gross Revenue $1,033,005, median $776,958, range $92,304–$4,464,693. No cost/expense data disclosed.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: Bottom quartile avg GR $187,862 (median $124,556) — well below the $250,000 annual TSQ. These franchisees face territory reduction or termination risk.
8. **Financial health**: Franchisor profitable — net income $1,702,798 on $4,002,944 revenue (2024). Total assets $9.5M, member's equity $8.2M. Clean audit opinion. Major concern: $7.47M non-interest-bearing loan to affiliate YESCO Administration LLC.
9. **Growth**: Flat — 57 franchised at start and end of 2024 (+2 opened, -1 non-renewal, -1 ceased, +1 transfer). Projecting 5 new franchised in 2025.
10. **Territory**: Non-exclusive but protected (if compliant). TSQ $400K/24 months then $250K/year. Territory loss if TSQ not met.
11. **Renewal**: 10-year initial term; 2 additional 5-year (standard) or 10-year (early renewal) terms. Must sign then-current agreement — "materially different terms" language present.
12. **Noncompete**: 2 years post-termination, within own territory or any other franchisee's territory.
13. **Disputes**: Utah venue, Utah law, mediation before litigation, 1-year limitation of action, jury trial waiver. State addenda override for 9 states.
14. **Special risks**: Mandatory minimum payments, spousal guaranty, sales performance requirement, out-of-state (Utah) dispute resolution.

## E. Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 10 years
- **Standard Renewal**: 2 additional 5-year terms; $15,000 successor fee each; must sign then-current agreement (may be "materially different"); general release required; royalties/fees may be adjusted
- **Early Renewal**: Must renew 120 days before first agreement expires; 10-year Extended Successor Term; $15,000 successor fee per agreement; tiered royalty program; general release required

**"Materially different terms" risk**: Explicitly stated that renewal agreement may have materially different terms and conditions. Territory boundaries may change. Fees adjusted to match new franchisees.

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: Upon expiration of initial term
- **By franchisor without cause**: Not applicable
- **By franchisor with cause (curable)**: Failure to pay fees, submit reports, grant access, comply with operational requirements, repeated breaches. 30-day cure period.
- **By franchisor with cause (non-curable)**: Bankruptcy, abandonment, felony, unauthorized transfer, material misrepresentations, multiple defaults, falsification of records, failure to meet TSQ, noncompete violation. Effective upon notice.
- **Post-termination**: Pay all fees, cease use of marks/proprietary info, disassociate from system

### Transfer
- All transfers require franchisor approval (not unreasonably withheld if conditions met)
- Conditions: transferee qualified; franchisee in full compliance; all debts paid; reports submitted; transferee signs then-current agreement; training completed; transfer fee paid; general release from franchisee + principal owners; transition plan approved; terms not harmful to business profitability
- Right of first refusal: 30 days on same terms as bona fide offer
- Option to purchase: Fair value before offering to third party
- Death/disability: Heirs can operate if approved

### Noncompete
- **During term**: Franchisee and owners cannot operate similar business
- **Post-term**: 2 years, within own territory or territory of any other franchisee
- North Dakota addendum: generally unenforceable
- Minnesota addendum: subject to MN franchise law protections

### Dispute Resolution
- Face-to-face meeting required before litigation
- Non-binding mediation may be completed before litigation
- **Venue**: Salt Lake County, Utah
- **Governing law**: Utah
- **1-year limitation of action** (Section 18.9) — deleted by Illinois addendum; not enforceable under North Dakota addendum
- **Jury trial waiver** (Section 18.10) — not enforceable in Indiana or North Dakota
- State overrides: IL (must be in IL), MD (MD federal court for MD claims), MN (cannot be outside MN), ND (cannot be outside ND), RI (venue/law restrictions void for RI claims)

### Other
- Franchisor fully assignable — may merge, go public, be acquired by competitor
- Integration/merger clause present
- Modifications only by written agreement (except manuals, unilaterally modifiable)
