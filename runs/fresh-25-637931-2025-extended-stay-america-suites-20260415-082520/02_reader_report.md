# Reader Report — Extended Stay America Suites (637931-2025)

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System identity**: ESH Strategies Franchise LLC franchises Extended Stay America Suites hotels targeting self-sufficient, value-conscious extended-stay and transient guests. The franchisor has never owned or operated hotels itself — all operations run through affiliate ESA Management LLC.
2. **Ownership**: Equally owned by Starwood Capital Group (SCG) and Blackstone (BX) through intermediate holding entities. Delaware LLC formed September 2010.
3. **System size**: As of December 31, 2024, the system includes 107 franchised ESA Suites hotels (10,975 rooms) and 339 company-owned hotels. The total brand system was 446 hotels, down from 548 at start of 2024 (-102 net), driven by massive rebranding of owned hotels to ESA Select Suites.
4. **Investment range**: New construction (124 rooms): $9,174,346–$14,123,855 excluding real property. Conversion (100 rooms): $205,152–$3,945,554 excluding real property.
5. **Fee burden**: Royalty 5.5% + System Services 4.5% = **10.0% of Gross Room Revenue** ongoing, plus booking commissions, digital marketing commission (5% on paid digital channel GRR), and numerous technology fees.
6. **Item 19 highlights**: Franchised hotel average RevPAR $46.85, ADR $59.89, Occupancy 78.2%. Owned hotels significantly outperform: RevPAR $62.35, ADR $80.87. Extended stay occupancy ~61-63%. 30+ night stays represent 59.4% of consumed room nights.
7. **Financial health**: Franchisor entity reported $25.8M revenue, $1.5M net income in 2024 (improved from $2.2M loss in 2023). Total assets $19.2M, member's capital $8.2M. No going concern. Clean audit with emphasis of matter on related party transactions.
8. **Growth trajectory**: Franchised hotels grew from 93 (2022) to 107 (2024). Company-owned hotels contracted sharply from 532 (2022) to 339 (2024), mainly through rebranding to Select Suites. Only 1 projected new franchised opening.
9. **Territory**: Non-exclusive; territory MAY be granted but protects only against same brand (not Premier or Select). Extensive reserved rights allow franchisor to operate competing brands in territory.
10. **Contract term**: 20 years for new-build, no renewal right. Franchisee cannot extend.
11. **Noncompete**: No noncompete during or post-term — unusual and favorable.
12. **Disputes**: Arbitration in Charlotte, NC. North Carolina law. Jury trial waiver.
13. **Litigation risk**: Antitrust class action pending (IDeaS pricing algorithm). Prior class action (Brittian) settled for $100,000.
14. **Special risk**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (North Carolina).

## D. Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Not exclusive**; franchisor MAY grant a Territory based on financial/operational capability
- **Territory protection**: Only against same brand (ESA Suites). Does NOT protect against ESA Premier Suites, ESA Select Suites, Yotel, or future brands
- **Reserved rights** (extensive):
  - Operate Brand Hotels outside Territory
  - Operate Network Hotels (other brands) inside or outside Territory
  - Own/acquire/construct Brand Hotels inside Territory (but not open during term)
  - Market and advertise inside Territory
  - Acquire/merge with other hotel chains
  - Operate existing Brand Hotels in Territory at FA effective date (may renew, expand, replace)
  - Begin operations in Territory 6 months before FA expiration
  - Use all distribution channels (internet, catalog, telemarketing) inside Territory
- **No relocation right**
- **Development Agreement**: Similar territory protections with development milestones

## E. Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Term**: Generally 20 years for new-build; conversion term determined by franchisor based on age/condition/location
- **Renewal**: **No right to renew or extend** — a significant risk factor
- **Development Agreement**: Typically 2-6 years

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: Only upon fire/casualty exceeding coverage limits (subject to state law) or any grounds permitted by state law
- **By franchisor without cause**: Fire/casualty exceeding coverage (FA); HTA terminable on 10 days notice without cause
- **Curable defaults**: 30 days to cure (10 days for monetary defaults)
- **Non-curable defaults**: Insolvency/bankruptcy, cease operations, contest marks, safety threats, felony conviction, false records, 3 defaults in 12 months, 2 similar defaults in 6 months

### Transfer
- Franchisor must approve all transfers (except certain non-control changes)
- Conditions: satisfy all obligations, PIP fee, transferee meets criteria, new FA at then-current terms (may be materially different), general release required
- **Right of First Offer**: Only if Exhibit F signed (strategic markets only)
- Death/disability: Must transfer within required timeframe; failure may result in termination

### Noncompete
- **None during term; none post-term** — unusually favorable

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration** for most disputes (except indemnification, equitable relief, arbitral award enforcement)
- **Venue**: Charlotte, NC (franchisor HQ)
- **Choice of law**: North Carolina (subject to state addenda)
- **Jury trial waiver**

### Post-Termination Obligations
Cease all Brand use; de-identify hotel; return all materials; pay amounts owed within 10 days; pay liquidated damages within 10 days; allow franchisor inspection until de-identification complete.
