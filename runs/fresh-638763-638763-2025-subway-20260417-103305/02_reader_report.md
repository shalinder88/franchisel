# Reader Report — Subway FDD (638763-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: Doctor's Associates LLC, a Delaware LLC, operating since 1965 as the Subway sandwich chain franchisor. Converted from Florida LLC to Delaware LLC on May 29, 2024.
2. **Ownership**: Acquired by Roark Capital Management via Underground Purchaser LLC on April 30, 2024. Parent chain: UPL → SWSH → SSH → SUSH → Subway Funding Holdco → Subway Funding LLC → Doctor's Associates LLC.
3. **Securitization**: US Securitization Transaction closed June 20, 2024 — Series 2024-1 ($3.35B) and Series 2024-3 ($2.335B) fixed-rate senior secured notes. $93M variable-rate term notes and $400M revolving facility also issued. Company's assets pledged as collateral.
4. **System Size**: 19,502 US franchised outlets at end of 2024 (down from 21,147 at start of 2022). Zero company-owned outlets.
5. **Shrinking System**: Net loss of 631 outlets in 2024, 443 in 2023, 571 in 2022. Continued contraction of the US footprint.
6. **Investment Range**: $263,000–$630,000 (traditional); $227,000–$458,000 (non-traditional). Multi-unit development: $246,123–$604,245 per restaurant.
7. **Fee Burden**: 8% royalty on gross sales + 4.5% advertising fund + $75/mo restaurant technology fee + $57/mo POS hardware + $155/mo digital menu board lease + payment processing fees + 1.9% Sub Club loyalty fee + various other technology/compliance fees.
8. **No FPR**: Subway does not provide any financial performance representations in Item 19.
9. **Financial Health**: Total revenue $975.3M (2024), net income $397.0M (2024). Total assets $390.1M, member's equity $317.4M. Massive distributions of $683.3M in 2024 (post-acquisition extraction).
10. **Gag Clause**: Subway has signed confidentiality clauses with current or former franchisees restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience.
11. **Territory**: NO exclusive territory. No radius restrictions. Franchisor and affiliates retain unlimited right to compete. Roark portfolio brands may also compete.
12. **Term**: 20 years with right to renew for an additional 20 years if in full compliance.
13. **Disputes**: Arbitration required (AAA or ADRC), held in Connecticut. Florida law governs. Damages limitations apply.
14. **Litigation**: 59 actions + 34 franchisor-initiated actions disclosed (Exhibit L). Franchisees we filed actions against represent ~0.2% of global franchisees.
15. **Special Risk**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Connecticut).

## Pass B: Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — The Franchisor
Doctor's Associates LLC has been franchising Subway restaurants for over 50 years. The entity underwent significant structural changes in 2024: conversion to a Delaware LLC, acquisition by Roark Capital, and a massive US Securitization Transaction. The securitization resulted in DAL becoming a subsidiary of Subway Funding LLC, with all US trademarks and IP transferred to Subway US IP Holder LLC. DAL operates under a 99-year royalty-free IP license from SUIPH.

The company offered master franchise businesses internationally through affiliates SIP and SIBV in numerous countries. FWH (Franchise World Headquarters LLC) manages day-to-day operations as securitization manager.

Multiple affiliated programs exist through Roark's portfolio. Business Developers (independent contractors) assist with franchise sales and receive approximately half the initial franchise fee and up to one-third of royalties.

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Restrictions on Sources (pp. 59–64)
All food, equipment, beverages, and products must be purchased exclusively from approved distribution centers or sources. Franchisor considers approval criteria proprietary trade secrets and does not disclose them. Franchisees may not independently source products even if distribution center items are unavailable. Limited Time Offering items may be auto-shipped. Request for alternative supplier approval must be submitted in writing; approval timeline generally 2 weeks to 6 months. Franchisor receives rebates from vendors. Equipment and supplies vendors may have to pay application fees.

### Item 9 — Franchisee's Obligations (pp. 65–67)
Comprehensive obligation table cross-referencing FA, FAR, DLTR, CBLR, MUFA, and DA sections to FDD items. Key obligations: site selection (Items 1,6,7,8,11,12), pre-opening purchases (Items 7,8,10), fees (Items 5,6,7,10,17), compliance with standards/Operations Manual (Items 8,16,17), trademark protection (Items 6,13,14,17), restrictions on products (Items 8,16).

### Item 10 — Financing (pp. 68–70)
Financing available for:
- Leased space through designated affiliate (varies; security deposit $2,000–$12,000; rent $1,000–$6,000/month)
- Loans in connection with Subway restaurants (negotiable terms)
- Equipment leasing through Huntington Technology Finance (3-year initial term, 2-year renewal options; repossession on default)
- DAL guaranty of RTaaS payments to HP ($57/month; HP retains title to hardware)

Franchisees eligible for SBA Franchise Registry expedited processing. Apart from subleases and loans, franchisor does not offer direct financing. General release required as condition of loans. All equity holders must guarantee financing obligations.

### Item 11 — Franchisor's Assistance, Advertising, Computer Systems, Training (pp. 70–79)
**Pre-opening obligations:**
- Initial training at designated locations

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory (pp. 80–81)
**NO exclusive territory. NO radius restrictions. NO minimum/maximum population requirements.** This is one of the most aggressive territory provisions in franchising. Franchisor and affiliates retain unlimited rights to compete. Roark Capital portfolio companies (including other QSR brands) may also compete in the same areas. Franchisee may not exclude, control, or impose conditions on location of any Subway or other restaurant.

Franchisee may solicit and accept orders from restaurant location. May use internet couponing, billboards, POP materials only with prior written approval. If lease terminates without franchisee's fault, franchisor will grant relocation permission at franchisee's sole expense.

### Item 13 — Trademarks (pp. 81–83)
Marks owned by Subway US IP Holder LLC; licensed to DAL under 99-year IP License Agreement. Key marks: SUBWAY, SUBWAY & design, EAT FRESH, SUB CLUB, and various other marks. Franchisee may not use "Subway" in corporate names. Sign faces bearing Subway name remain SUIPH property. No disparagement clause applies (except private franchisee-only forums). Franchisee must assign all innovations to franchisor.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights (pp. 83–84)
No material patents. Copyright claims on various works (not all registered). Operations Manual is proprietary trade secret. All innovations by franchisee, employees, or contractors belong to franchisor.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate (pp. 84–85)
Restaurant must be under direct supervision of franchisee or trained full-time Designated Manager. Manager not required to own equity. Personal guaranty by principal owners required. 3-mile noncompete during term and 1 year post-term.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on Sales (pp. 85–86)
Must operate in strict compliance with Operations Manual. Premises only for Subway restaurant operation. Must offer all designated required goods. Marketwide Option Program (MOP) and Store Option Program (SOP) govern product decisions. Co-branding requires separate franchise agreement with third-party franchisor.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal (pp. 86–88)
- Standard term: 20 years
- Renewal: additional 20-year term if (1) complied with all material provisions, (2) maintain premises, (3) written notice 12–18 months before expiration, (4) satisfied all monetary obligations, (5) execute then-current franchise agreement + pay renewal fee ($3,750), (6) comply with then-current qualifications/training, (7) execute Renewal Addendum (Exhibit G-1), (8) execute general release
- School lunch: 5-year term, 5-year renewals, no renewal fee
- Satellite: co-terminus with Base Restaurant

### Termination (pp. 88–90)
- Franchisee termination: none (but may seek arbitration if franchisor defaults within 60 days)
- Non-traditional/school lunch: may stop after 30 days' notice
- Franchisor with cause: 10-day cure for payment defaults; 30-day cure for other defaults
- Non-curable defaults (immediate termination): 14 categories including failure to open/complete training, material misrepresentation, felony conviction, unauthorized disclosure of trade secrets, abandonment (2 business days), unauthorized transfer, underreporting >3% for 3+ weeks, insolvency/bankruptcy, trademark misuse, repeated failures (3 in 12 months or 2 same violation in 6 months), health/safety violations (72-hour cure), immediate health/safety concerns, failure to pay third parties, dishonest/unethical conduct

### Transfer (pp. 90–91)
- Franchisor must approve all transfers; will not unreasonably withhold
- Conditions: right of first refusal (30 days), buyer qualifies, transfer fee paid ($7,500 standard), buyer completes training, all money owed paid, not in default, general release signed, restaurant in full compliance, execute Transfer Addendum
- Reduced transfer fees: $3,200 standard to new/existing; $200 death/disability/divorce; $2,000 entity changes
- Satellite must transfer with Base Restaurant (except isolated exceptions)

### Death/Disability (p. 92)
Assignment to approved third party within 12 months. Subject to all transfer conditions.

### Noncompete (p. 92)
- During term: no direct or indirect association with competitive business ANYWHERE
- Post-term: 1 year, 3-mile radius from any Subway that operates or operated in prior year
- Violation: $15,000 per business + 8% of gross sales + injunction
- Non-traditional: noncompete limited to facility/school

### Dispute Resolution (pp. 92–93)
- Mandatory arbitration via AAA or ADRC (at discretion of filing party)
- Forum: Connecticut (franchisor's principal place of business)
- Governing law: Florida (subject to state law overrides)
- Damages limitations: no incidental, exemplary, contingent, punitive, or consequential damages (except where prohibited by law)
- Claims limited to franchisor only (not affiliates or individuals)
- Franchisee cannot bring litigation in court (except state law overrides)
- Probationary Case Management fee: $500; extension: $250; Interim Order: $250; Litigation Expense: 5% of gross sale consideration (max $5,000)
