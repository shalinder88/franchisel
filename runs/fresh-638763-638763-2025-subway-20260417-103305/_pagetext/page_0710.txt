Other Transactions with Affiliates
License Agreement
The Company retains the nonexclusive use of the recipes, formulas, food preparation procedures,
business methods, business forms, and business policies (the “System”) within the United States
through an Intellectual Property and System Transfer Agreement (the “License Agreement”) with
SIP. During 2022, Subway’s global transfer pricing policy was updated, and the Company became
the regional headquarters for the Americas. As a regional headquarters, the Company has the right
to sublicense the System in its respective region. License fee expense, included in “Operating
expenses, net” within the accompanying statements of income for the years ended December 31,
2024, 2023 and 2022, amounted to $245,181, $445,022 and $365,878, respectively. Regional
headquarters are invoiced for the administrative service costs relating to the services provided by
an affiliated entity under common control for its respective regions. This expense is recorded in
“Operating expenses, net” in the accompanying statements of income and is offset by the
compensation that the Company receives in the form of license fee income which amounted to
$36,687, $54,886, $32,988 for the years ended December 31, 2024, 2023 and 2022, respectively.
On June 20, 2024, SIP assigned its US rights in the Subway System and Marks to SUIPH.
Simultaneously, SIP and the Company terminated the License Agreement. SUIPH and the
Company entered into a Franchisor IP License Agreement granting the Company the right to use
the Subway System and Marks. The Franchisor IP License Agreement with SUIPH is a royalty free
license.
The License Agreement was subject to a guaranteed profit arrangement. The expense allocated to
the Company from SIP was adjusted so that the Company’s operating profit margin, as defined in
the transfer pricing study, was equal to 4% of a defined profit base, which includes specified
portions of “Total revenue”.
