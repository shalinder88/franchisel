Commitments and Contingencies
The Company records reserves for legal and other contingencies when information available to
the Company indicates that it is probable that a liability has been incurred and the amount of the
loss can be reasonably estimated. Predicting the outcomes of claims and litigation and estimating
the related costs and exposures involve substantial uncertainties that could cause actual costs to
vary materially from estimates. Legal costs incurred in connection with legal and other
contingencies are expensed as they are incurred.
The Company is a defendant in a number of lawsuits and arbitration matters (including claims),
some of which are for substantial amounts, arising in the ordinary course of business. However,
management believes that the resolution of these matters, both individually and in the aggregate,
will not have a material adverse impact on the Company’s financial position, results of operations
and cash flows.
