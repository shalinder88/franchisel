7) Jeffrey Offutt v. Doctor’s Associates, LLC (American Arbitration Association Case # 01-24-0008-8333). On
November 21, 2024, Jeffrey Offutt (“Claimant”), filed a demand for arbitration, seeking a declaration that his
expired 1995 Franchise Agreements for Restaurants 5727, 11852, 12161 and 13426 automatically extend to 2035;
that his expired 1996 Franchise Agreements for Restaurants 18589, 4398, 10655, 6045, 11262, 13656, 18563 and
17935 automatically extend to 2036; that his expired 1997 Franchise Agreements for Restaurants 4514 and 7468
automatically extend to 2037; and that his expired 1999 Franchise Agreement for Restaurant 21295 automatically
extends to 2039. All of the foregoing restaurants, with the exception of #4514, which claimant voluntarily closed,
remain open and operating.. Further, Claimant seeks a declaration that he is entitled to reasonable time after
judgment is issued in the arbitration to comply with “[DAL]’s reasonable and material requirements with may
have expired before the entry of the order requested in this action.” Claimant seeks a permanent injunction
forbidding DAL from terminating or failing to renew Claimant’s franchise agreements listed above in violation
of the declaratory judgment. Claimant also seeks damages under the Connecticut Unfair Trade Practices Act, and
attorneys’ fees and costs in connection with this demand. On December 23, 2024, the Respondent filed an Answer
denying the allegations, Affirmative Defenses and Counterclaims, claiming a breach of contract and seeking
declaratory relief, and a demand for Attorneys’ fees and costs. On January 17, 2025 Claimant filed an Answer
and Affirmative Defenses to Respondent’s Counterclaims. The Arbitration is pending.

Tribunal of Ontario against, notably, Subway Franchise Systems of Canada, ULC, claiming $80,000 in damages,
because the Subway® Franchisee’s employee allegedly made racist comments to the Complainant, and allegedly
acted in a discriminatory manner during the guest’s visit at the Subway® restaurant that violated the Ontario
Human Rights Code and infringed the Complainant’s rights. The Complainant alleges, notably, that she was
discriminated against on the grounds of her race, ancestry, place of origin, colour and ethnic origin and suffered

Systems of Canada, ULC filed a response to the complaint. Subway Franchise Systems of Canada, ULC will
continue to contest the complaint.
