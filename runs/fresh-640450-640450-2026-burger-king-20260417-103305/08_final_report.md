## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System Size & Scale**: Burger King operates 19,900 restaurants worldwide as of December 31, 2025, with 6,650 in the United States (5,518 franchised, 1,132 company-owned). The US system has contracted for three consecutive years: -264 net outlets in 2023, -77 in 2024, and -51 in 2025. The total US system has declined from 7,042 at the start of 2023 to 6,650 at the end of 2025, a reduction of 392 outlets (5.6%). However, much of the 2024 movement reflects the Carrols Restaurant Group acquisition in May 2024, which converted 1,023 franchised restaurants to company-owned status.

2. **Entity Structure**: Burger King Company LLC is a Florida limited liability company formed February 4, 2022, succeeding Burger King Corporation (dissolved December 2022). BKC is an indirect wholly-owned subsidiary of Restaurant Brands International Limited Partnership (RBILP, Ontario), whose general partner is Restaurant Brands International Inc. (RBI, Canadian corporation). 3G Restaurant Brands Holdings LP holds approximately 22% of RBI's combined voting power. RBI also owns Tim Hortons, Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen, and Firehouse Subs.

3. **Ownership History**: The Burger King brand was founded in 1954 as Burger King of Miami, Inc., incorporated in 1956 as South Florida Restaurants, Inc., and renamed Burger King Corporation in 1963. In August 2022, BK Corporation's assets and liabilities were transferred to BKC LLC in an internal reorganization. BK Corporation was dissolved in December 2022.

4. **Investment Range**: The total initial investment ranges from $348,400 (Indoor MRS low) to $3,320,600 (Traditional Freestanding high), excluding real estate purchase costs. A traditional freestanding restaurant requires $2.2M–$3.3M. Non-traditional facilities range from $348K to $1.9M depending on format.

5. **Fee Burden**: Standard royalty is 4.5% of monthly Gross Sales, with advertising contributions up to 4.5% (currently 4.5% as of January 1, 2025 for Fuel the Flame participants). Additional recurring fees include digital services ($110/month + 1% digital sales, capped $4,500/year), BK University support ($600/year), BK Foundation ($1,000/year), and potential Investment Spending (up to 2% of Gross Sales for DMA marketing). The total ongoing fee burden, excluding rent, can reach 11%+ of Gross Sales.

6. **Item 19 Performance**: Traditional restaurant average unit volume (AUV) was $1,707,974 consolidated ($1,692,549 for franchisee-owned), with a median of $1,610,633 ($1,593,606 franchisee-owned). Company-owned restaurants outperformed franchisees by approximately $87K in AUV. Bottom-quartile risk: 3.9% of franchisee-owned Traditional restaurants had annual sales below $700K, and 25.7% were below $1.1M. Remodel uplift averaged 12.8% across 1,031 restaurants (26% for Scrape & Rebuild).

7. **Financial Health**: RBI reported $9.4B total revenue, $2.2B income from operations, and $1.1B net income in FY2025. However, leverage is substantial: $13.3B long-term debt against $25.6B total assets. Net loss of $126M from discontinued operations (BK China/Pangaea Foods) was absorbed in 2025. KPMG issued a clean unqualified audit opinion. No going-concern qualification.

8. **Growth Trajectory**: The system is declining. Projected openings for the next fiscal year total only 40 (39 franchised + 1 company-owned) against a base of 6,650 outlets — a development rate of 0.6%. This compares to 83 franchised openings in 2025 and 63 in 2024. The brand's focus appears to be on remodeling and Carrols refranchising rather than net new development.

9. **Territory**: No exclusive territory under any agreement form. The Franchise Agreement, TRA, MTRA, and Development Agreement all explicitly state franchisees will not receive an exclusive territory and may face competition from other franchisees, company outlets, other distribution channels, and RBI's other brands.

10. **Renewal Risk**: No right of renewal. Franchisees may seek a successor Franchise Agreement, but it will be the then-current form, which "may have materially different terms." This is a significant contractual risk.

11. **Dispute Resolution**: Litigation venue is exclusively Miami-Dade County, Florida, with Florida law governing (subject to state overrides). No mandatory arbitration. Non-binding mediation required for development disputes only.

12. **Gag Clause**: The FDD discloses that "current and former franchisees signed provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with our predecessor."

13. **Special Programs**: Fuel the Flame (mandatory advertising program for new franchisees), Crown Your Career (employee-to-franchisee path with BKC financing), Carrols Refranchise (acquisition of former Carrols restaurants), Reclaim the Flame 2 (remodel incentive program), and 2024 Multi-Unit DIP (new development incentive).

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

BKC recognizes two franchise ownership types: Individual/Owner-Operator and Entity. Individual ownership requires the Operating Partner to own 50%+ equity and devote full time to on-premises operations. Entity ownership requires a Managing Owner to own 25%+ equity (currently required under Entity Guidelines). The Legacy Entity Franchise Agreement Addendum permits a Managing Director designation for legacy franchisees. A Corporate Addendum is available for publicly-traded entities.

The Crown Your Career Program requires 100% ownership of the franchisee entity by the Managing Owner until the later of the 5th anniversary of the Franchise Agreement or full repayment of the CYC Promissory Note.

### Fee Stack — Initial Fees

The standard franchise fee is $50,000 for a 20-year term, prorated for shorter terms with a minimum of $15,000. The fee is fully earned upon signing and is non-refundable. Application fees are $250 per individual applicant and $5,000 per Entity applicant. The training fee is $7,500 per trainee.

Under the TRA, a $5,000 deposit is required per restaurant opening; under the MTRA, $10,000 per restaurant multiplied by the number of committed openings. These are credited against the franchise fee but are generally non-refundable except in limited circumstances (e.g., BKC proposes development in your Target Area and you decline).

Under a Development Agreement, franchisees must prepay $50,000 per committed restaurant. After exhaustion of prepaid fees, the fee depends on FSS Development Grade: $25,000 for A/B grades, or the Base Fee (greater of $50,000 or then-current standard) for D/F grades.

### Fee Stack — Ongoing Fees

The ongoing fee burden is substantial and multi-layered:

**Royalty**: 4.5% of monthly Gross Sales (standard). Reduction programs exist for the 2024 Multi-Unit DIP (starting at 2.5%, ramping to 4.5% over 6 years), the Development Agreement (FSS grade-dependent reductions from Base Fee), and the RTF2 Remodel Program (franchisee-selected rate of 4.5%–6.0%). Penalty rates of 6.0%, 7.5%, or current rate +3.0% apply for remodel defaults.

**Advertising**: Up to 4.5% of monthly Gross Sales. Under the Fuel the Flame Program (mandatory for new franchisees), the rate is 4.5% through December 31, 2027. Extension through 2028 is conditional on average 4-Wall EBITDA reaching $230,000 or a 70% franchisee/company vote. If neither condition is met, the rate reverts to 4.0%. Various exceptions may maintain the 4.0% rate for certain restaurants.

**Rent**: If BKC owns the premises (BKL), minimum annual rent is typically 10% of capitalized site acquisition and construction costs, plus percentage rent of 8.5% of monthly Gross Sales up to $133,333.33 increasing to 10% above that threshold. If BKC leases the property, rent is 125% of BKC's rent plus 10% of capitalized costs. Rent escalates 12% every 5 years for BKC-owned properties.

**Technology Fees**: Digital services fee of $110/month per restaurant plus 1% of monthly digital sales, capped at $4,500/year. Network standardization $220–$450/month. POS data reporting $200–$1,200/year. Software maintenance/help desk $2,000–$4,000/year. BK University/support $600/year.

**Other Recurring**: BK Foundation $1,000/year per restaurant. Gift Card transaction fees ~1.8% of redeemed sales. Investment Spending (DMA marketing) up to 2% of Gross Sales if your DMA participates. Static menu board kit $200–$300/month if no outdoor digital menu boards.

**Event-Driven Fees**: Transfer fee $2,000 first restaurant + $500 each additional ($175 weekend/holiday surcharge). Training fee $7,500 for new franchisee on transfer. Entity/LLC conversion fees up to $5,000 + $1,000/restaurant. Follow-up walk-thru $1,500. Background check $145–$15,000. Sales transfer study $3,000–$8,000. Product testing up to $30,000/product. Resale fee $10,000/restaurant (Carrols).

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

BKC maintains extensive supplier control. Franchisees must obtain most goods and services from approved suppliers or distributors. BKC or its affiliates are the sole supplier of digital technology platforms (ordering, delivery, loyalty). Sole suppliers also exist for POS systems, network services, digital menu boards, gift card services, self-serve kiosks, cashless payments processing, quality assurance inspections, food, beverages, cleaning supplies, packaging, and certain equipment.

Purchases from approved suppliers represent approximately 65–90% of establishment costs and 30–60% of non-occupancy operating expenses. This is a high degree of supply chain control.

### Franchisor Revenue from Franchisee Purchases

In FY2025, BKC's revenue from required franchisee purchases and leases was approximately $231 million, representing 15.5% of BKC's total revenue of approximately $1.485 billion. Supplier rebates contributed 7.9% of the consolidated advertising fund revenue. Various suppliers make payments to BKC in the form of rebates, flat fees, or below-market goods/services.

### RSI Purchasing Cooperative

Restaurant Services Inc. (RSI) is an independent, not-for-profit purchasing cooperative that acts as the purchasing agent for most equipment, supplies, food, and other products. RSI has a 21-member Board: 18 franchisees (2 from each of 9 geographic districts), 1 minority franchisee representative, 1 BKC representative, and 1 independent director. RSI is supported by surcharges on certain products and issues patronage dividends.

### Training Program

The New Franchisee Training Program requires up to 340 hours: 20 hours classroom/online and 320 hours in-restaurant training. The fee is $7,500 per trainee. Training is held in Miami, FL or designated locations. ServSafe Food Safety certification is required before opening. Continuing training programs may be required at $7,500/person.

Employee training requirements include BK University Team Member Training for all team members and BKC Shift Certification for any person designated to run a shift.

### Technology Stack

The technology burden is substantial and multi-layered:

- **POS System**: $20,000–$25,000 purchase cost. Minimum 2 front counter + 2 drive-thru devices, interior/exterior digital menu boards, 4 kitchen display systems, self-serve kiosks. Must use approved vendors per global POS policy.
- **Network**: $220–$450/month per restaurant. Approved vendors only. Firewall, business Wi-Fi, free guest Wi-Fi required.
- **Digital Services**: $110/month + 1% digital sales (capped $4,500/year). Covers BK mobile app, website, third-party ordering platforms, ROYAL PERKS loyalty program.
- **Data Reporting**: $200–$1,200/year. BKC has right to review and access all POS records and reports via remote internet access. All transacted data transmitted to BKC.
- **Ongoing Software/Help Desk**: $2,000–$4,000/year.
- **Upgrade Obligation**: Franchisee must upgrade hardware/software on BKC request for PCI compliance or feature enhancements.

### Marketing Fund

The advertising fund is administered by BKC. FY2025 expenditure breakdown: Working media 80.0%, Production 3.3%, Agency fees 6.9%, Other expenses 9.8%. The Franchise Agreement requires at least 65% of annual contributions go to working media. The fund is audited annually with statements available on request.

The NFA's Franchise Advisory Council (FAC) serves in an advisory capacity with no decision-making authority. BKC has absolute discretion to establish amounts and types of media. The FAC has audit rights over the fund.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### No Protected Territory

This is one of the most aggressive encroachment postures in QSR franchising. Every form of agreement — the Franchise Agreement, TRA, MTRA, and Development Agreement — explicitly states: "You will not receive an exclusive territory. You may face competition from other franchisees, from outlets that we own, or from other channels of distribution or competitive brands that we control."

The only modest protection is under the TRA/MTRA: if BKC proposes to develop a restaurant at a site within your Target Area, you receive first option to develop it. If you decline, BKC or a third party can proceed and BKC can terminate your TRA/MTRA.

### Encroachment Risk Vectors

1. **Company-Owned Restaurants**: BKC operates 1,132 restaurants directly, including the Carrols portfolio. These may compete directly with franchised locations.
2. **Other Franchisees**: BKC retains sole discretion to grant additional franchises anywhere, including adjacent to your restaurant.
3. **Cross-Brand Competition**: Tim Hortons, Popeyes, and Firehouse Subs are RBI affiliates that may operate in the same markets. All four brands offer overlapping products (sandwiches, fries, beverages, desserts).
4. **Alternative Distribution Channels**: BKC reserves the right to sell BK-branded products through grocery stores, convenience stores, the Internet, or direct marketing.
5. **Military AAFES**: Army & Air Force Exchange Service has rights to develop restaurants on US military establishments.
6. **Future Acquisitions**: RBI "occasionally looks into acquiring chains of restaurants that sell products or provide services similar to those offered by BURGER KING Restaurants."

### Channel Restrictions on Franchisee

Franchisees cannot use BK marks on any website, social media, or domain name without BKC approval. All advertising must be pre-approved. BKC can restrict franchisee sales through alternative channels.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The standard Franchise Agreement term is 20 years for freestanding restaurants, potentially shorter for non-traditional locations. There is no right of renewal. Franchisees may apply for a successor Franchise Agreement, but must sign the then-current form, which "may have materially different terms." This creates significant renewal risk: after investing for 20 years, the successor agreement could impose substantially different fee structures, operating requirements, or restrictions.

Successor requirements include: successor application, general release, current-form Franchise Agreement, successor franchise fee, building upgrades, inspections, and signing a Successor Addendum. No successor option exists for Big-Box Program or Delivery Restaurant franchisees.

### Termination — Cure Periods

BKC's termination rights are comprehensive with tiered cure periods:
- **5 days**: Operational/quality/health/sanitation defaults, unapproved product sales, abandonment, cease to occupy premises, insurance failures.
- **10 days**: Non-payment of royalty or advertising fees.
- **30 days**: Late reports, unapproved equipment/uniforms/décor, maintenance failures, unsatisfied judgments, failure to restore after damage, legal non-compliance, Operating Partner/Managing Owner requirements.
- **60 days**: Failure to pay bills, other FA/lease provision failures.
- **180 days**: Failure to meet minimum financial criteria.

Non-curable defaults include: bankruptcy, inability to pay debts, felony/fraud/moral turpitude conviction, unfair competition, deleterious behavior, false/misleading statements, Gross Sales understatement, failure to close on health/safety issues, unauthorized transfer, and repeated breaches after notice.

The CYC program adds additional non-curable defaults: any dividend/distribution while the Promissory Note is outstanding, and failure to comply with CYC program agreements. The Carrols Refranchise Addendum adds cross-default provisions covering all agreements related to the refranchising transaction.

### Transfer

BKC consent is required for all transfers and may be withheld in sole discretion during the first 5 years under CYC or until remodel completion under Carrols Refranchise. Transfer fees are $2,000 for the first restaurant plus $500 for each additional in the same transaction, with a $175 surcharge for weekend/holiday transfers.

Transfer conditions include: buyer approval and training completion, all amounts current, transfer fees and training fees paid, general release by seller, restaurants inspected and brought to current image. The buyer must sign the then-current Franchise Agreement.

BKC has a right of first refusal to purchase any restaurant or interest at the same terms as a third-party offer. Post-termination, BKC has the option to purchase paper goods/menus at cost and equipment/fixtures/signs at fair market value. Under Carrols Refranchise, BKC can repurchase at depreciated value if remodels are not completed.

### Non-Compete

During the term: no ownership, operation, or interest in any hamburger business (except other BK Restaurants). Post-term: 1 year within 2-mile radius of your restaurant. Under CYC: Managing Owner cannot engage in any other business activities until the later of the 5th anniversary or loan repayment.

### Dispute Resolution

No mandatory arbitration. Non-binding mediation is required for development disputes only, with voluntary arbitration at the franchisee's election. All other disputes must be brought in the US District Court for the Southern District of Florida or state court in Miami-Dade County, Florida. Florida law governs, subject to state-specific overrides.

Under CYC loan agreements: waiver of right to jury trial, waiver of presentment/demand/protest, waiver of right to reduction or set-off.

### Remodel Uplift (Section B)

BKC provides unusually detailed remodel uplift data for 1,031 restaurants remodeled between January 2018 and December 2024:

- **Full Remodel** (964 restaurants): 11.9% average sales uplift, 10.2% median.
- **Scrape & Rebuild** (67 restaurants): 26.0% average sales uplift, 21.0% median — substantially higher but fewer locations.

**Critical caveats**: Sales figures do not reflect costs, operating expenses, or other deductions. The FDD explicitly states individual results may differ. Data relies on franchisee-reported Gross Sales.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### Multi-Year Trajectory

The Burger King US system has been in sustained contraction:

| Year | Total Start | Total End | Net Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2023 | 7,042 | 6,778 | -264 |
| 2024 | 6,778 | 6,701 | -77 |

Excluding this reclassification, the organic system contraction has been consistent but moderating: roughly 264, 77, and 51 net closures over the three-year period.

### Franchise Outlet Activity (2025)

In 2025, the franchise system experienced: 83 openings (up from 63 in 2024), 33 terminations (down from 49), 7 non-renewals (unchanged from 2024), 13 reacquired by franchisor (down dramatically from 1,061 in 2024), and 36 ceased operations for other reasons (down from 69). The net change for franchised outlets was only -6, a marked improvement from prior years.

### Company-Owned Activity (2025)

BKC sold 48 restaurants to franchisees in 2025 (refranchising from the Carrols portfolio), closed 14, and reacquired 13 from franchisees. The net change was -45, reducing company-owned count from 1,177 to 1,132.

### Projected Openings

For the next fiscal year, only 40 openings are projected: 39 franchised (led by Texas with 10) and 1 company-owned (Michigan). This is a very modest development pace for a system of 6,650 outlets.

### Transfers

Transfer activity remained stable at 242 in 2025, compared to 254 in 2024 and 245 in 2023.

### Gag Clause

The FDD explicitly discloses: "In some instances, during the last three fiscal years, current and former franchisees signed provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with our predecessor." This references BK Corporation (pre-2022). Whether BKC LLC has continued this practice is not explicitly stated.

### Ceased Operations

Exhibit O3 lists 331 franchisees who had outlets terminated, cancelled, not renewed, or otherwise ceased to do business in FY2025.
