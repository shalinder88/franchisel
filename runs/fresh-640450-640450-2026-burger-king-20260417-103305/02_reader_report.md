## Pass B — Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — Entity Structure and System Overview
Burger King Company LLC was formed February 4, 2022 as a Florida LLC. Its predecessor, Burger King Corporation, was incorporated in 1963 (originally founded 1954 as Burger King of Miami, Inc.). In August 2022, substantially all assets and liabilities of BK Corporation were transferred to BKC LLC in an internal reorganization. BK Corporation was dissolved December 2022.

BKC is a wholly-owned indirect subsidiary of Restaurant Brands International Limited Partnership (RBILP), an Ontario LP. RBILP's general partner is Restaurant Brands International Inc. (RBI), a Canadian corporation. 3G Restaurant Brands Holdings LP (Cayman Islands) owns approximately 22% of RBI's combined voting power.

RBI also indirectly owns Tim Hortons, Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen, and Firehouse Subs. These affiliate brands operate globally, with potential for cross-brand competition in the US market.

As of December 31, 2025, there were 19,900 Burger King Restaurants worldwide, of which 6,649 were in the United States (5,517 franchised, 1,132 company-owned as of that date).

Two franchise ownership types: Individual/Owner-Operator (Operating Partner must own 50%+ equity, full-time, on-premises) and Entity (Managing Owner must own 25%+ equity). Legacy Entity Addendum allows Managing Director delegation. Corporate Addendum available for publicly-traded entities.

Mandatory programs: Fuel the Flame advertising program for new franchisees. Crown Your Career Program for BKC employees purchasing restaurants.

### Item 5 — Initial Fees
- **Application fee**: $250 per individual applicant; $5,000 per Entity applicant. Non-refundable.
- **Franchise fee**: $50,000 for 20-year term (standard). Prorated for shorter terms, minimum $15,000. Fully earned and non-refundable upon signing.
- **TRA deposit**: $5,000 per restaurant. MTRA deposit: $10,000 × number of restaurant openings. Credited against franchise fee; may be forfeited.
- **Development Agreement**: Prepay $50,000 per committed restaurant. FSS-based fees for restaurants after exhaustion: $25,000 if A/B grade; Base Fee (greater of $50,000 or then-current standard) if D/F grade.
- **Training fee**: $7,500 per trainee. Non-refundable.
- **2024 Multi-Unit DIP**: Deposit = number of first-year and last-year restaurants × $50,000. Reduced royalty/ad schedules.
- **Carrols Refranchise**: 5% purchase price deposit. CYC: $7,500 deposit within 5 days of Term Sheet.
- **RTF2 Remodel**: Successor fee = $2,500/year × additional years purchased.

## Pass C — Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Supplier Restrictions
BKC establishes standards and specifications for most goods and services. Franchisees must obtain items from approved suppliers or distributors in most cases. BKC or its affiliates are sole suppliers of digital technology platforms (ordering, delivery, loyalty).

Sole suppliers exist for: POS, network services, digital menu boards, Gift Card services, self-serve kiosks, cashless payments processing, quality assurance inspections, food, beverages, cleaning supplies, packaging, and certain equipment.

Approved supplier purchases represent 65–90% of establishment costs and 30–60% of non-occupancy operating expenses.

Revenue from required franchisee purchases in FY2025: approximately $231 million (15.5% of BKC's total revenues of ~$1.485 billion). Supplier rebates contributed 7.9% of consolidated fund revenue.

RSI (Restaurant Services Inc.) is an independent, not-for-profit purchasing cooperative with a 21-member board (18 franchisees, 1 BKC rep, 1 minority franchisee rep, 1 independent director).

Supplier approval process: 90–160 days typical. Evaluation criteria include production quality, delivery capability, financial condition, food safety compliance, environmental responsibility, IT capabilities.

Insurance requirements: Commercial general liability $2M per occurrence ($2M–$10M aggregate based on restaurant count), auto liability $1M, property insurance at full replacement cost, boiler/machinery at full replacement cost, workers comp per law.

### Item 9 — Franchisee's Obligations
Comprehensive obligation table (a–z) covering: site selection, pre-opening, construction, training, opening, fees, compliance, trademarks, products/services, warranty, territory, purchases, maintenance/remodeling, insurance, advertising, indemnification, owner participation, records/reports, inspections/audits, transfer, renewal, post-termination, non-competition, dispute resolution, re-entry, and guarantees.

### Item 10 — Financing
BKC does not generally offer direct financing except:
1. **Real estate leases**: BKL (land and building) or BKG (land only) — rent based on capitalized costs + percentage rent.
2. **Crown Your Career Program**: BKC may finance all or portion of purchase price. Interest: Prime Rate + 1–4%. Term: up to 10 years. Secured by all restaurant assets. Personal guarantee required. Default under loan = default under Franchise Agreement.

No general guarantees of franchisee notes or leases.

### Item 11 — Training
**New Franchisee Training**: Up to 20 hours classroom/online + up to 320 hours in-restaurant (total ~340 hours). Fee: $7,500 per trainee. Covers Brand Orientation, BKC Shift Certification, BK University Team Member Training, Foundations of Shift Management. Training held in Miami, FL or designated locations. ServSafe certification required.

All training team members average 3+ years QSR/hospitality experience. Training program director: Greg Moscetti (joined June 2025, 15+ years operations/training experience).

Continuing training programs may be required; fee $7,500/person.

Employee training requirements: Team Members trained via BK University; shift runners must complete BKC Shift Certification.

**POS/Technology**:
- Standard POS: Min 2 front counter + 2 drive-thru devices, interior/exterior digital menu boards, 4 kitchen display systems, 6 receipt printers, back office computer. Cost: $20,000–$25,000.
- Network standardization: Approved vendors only. $220–$450/month per restaurant.
- Digital services fee: $110/month + 1% digital sales (capped $4,500/yr). Covers BK mobile app, website, third-party platforms, ROYAL PERKS loyalty.
- Free guest Wi-Fi required.
- POS data transmission to BKC required (including all transacted data).
- Software maintenance/help desk: $2,000–$4,000/year per restaurant.
- Self-serve kiosks required.

**Advertising Fund**:
FY2025 expenditure breakdown: Working media 80.0%, Production 3.3%, Agency fees 6.9%, Other 9.8%.
No less than 65% of annual contributions must go to working media (per Franchise Agreement).
Fund audited annually; statements available on request.

**MOD Manual**: Approximately 716 pages comprising Operations Manual, Restaurant Equipment Manual, RSI Red Book, Approved Brands List, AEL, BK University platform.

### Item 12 — Territory
**No exclusive territory** under any agreement form. TRA and MTRA define Target Areas for site search only — no exclusivity. BKC retains right to develop or license any location. Development Agreement grants a "Territory" but without exclusivity.

Franchisee may face competition from: other franchisees, company-owned outlets, other distribution channels, competitive brands controlled by RBI (Tim Hortons, Popeyes, Firehouse Subs).

Alternative distribution channels (Internet, grocery, convenience stores, direct marketing) reserved by BKC.

Military: AAFES has rights to develop restaurants on US military establishments.

Cross-brand competition: Tim Hortons, Popeyes, Firehouse Subs may operate in same markets. RBI management "attempts to resolve" conflicts — not guaranteed.

### Item 13 — Trademarks

### Item 14 — Patents/Proprietary

### Item 15 — Owner Participation
Individual: Operating Partner must own 50%+ equity, live near restaurant, devote full time, no other hamburger business interests.
Entity: Managing Owner must own 25%+ equity, devote full time, authority to bind franchisee.
Legacy Entity: Managing Director designation permitted (no equity requirement, must be full-time, on-premises).
CYC: Managing Owner must own 100% of franchisee entity until 5th anniversary or loan repayment (whichever later).

### Item 16 — Product Restrictions
Restaurant must be used solely for BK operation. Minimum hours/days per BKC specification. Only authorized products/services. Delivery required where aggregator operates. ROYAL PERKS loyalty mandatory. Maximum price control on designated items (e.g., Value Menu). No vending machines, ATMs, video games without authorization. Retail sales only (no redistribution/resale).

## Pass D — Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

**Territory protection**: None. This is one of the most aggressive encroachment postures in QSR franchising. Every franchise form — FA, TRA, MTRA, DA — explicitly states "you will not receive an exclusive territory" and warns of competition from franchisees, company outlets, other channels, and competitive RBI brands.

The only modest protection is that under TRA/MTRA, if BKC proposes to develop a restaurant at a site within your Target Area, you get first option to develop it. If you decline, BKC or a third party can develop at the site and can terminate your TRA/MTRA (refunding remaining deposit).

**Encroachment vectors**:
1. Company-owned restaurants in your market
2. Other franchisees developing nearby
3. Tim Hortons, Popeyes, Firehouse Subs operating in the same DMA (similar menus: sandwiches, fries, beverages, desserts)
4. Alternative distribution channels (grocery, Internet, direct marketing) under BK marks
5. AAFES military development rights
6. Potential future acquisitions of competing chains by RBI

**Channel restrictions on franchisee**: Must have all advertising approved. Cannot use BK marks on any website, social media, or domain name without approval.

## Pass E — Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Term**: 20 years for freestanding; may be less for non-traditional locations.
- **Renewal**: No right of renewal. Franchisee has option for successor Franchise Agreement if in compliance, property control obtained, and meets then-current successor requirements. Successor FA may have **materially different terms**.
- **Successor requirements**: Successor application + general release + current FA (materially different terms possible) + successor fee + building upgrades + inspections + sign Successor Addendum.
- **No successor option** for Big-Box Program or Delivery Restaurant franchisees.

### Termination
**Curable defaults** (with cure periods):
- 5 days: Operational/quality/health/sanitation standards; sale of unapproved products; abandonment; cease to occupy premises; insurance.
- 10 days: Non-payment of royalty/advertising.
- 30 days: Late reports; unapproved equipment/uniforms/décor; maintenance failures; unsatisfied judgment; failure to restore after damage; non-compliance with laws; Operating Partner/Managing Owner/Managing Director requirements.
- 60 days: Failure to pay bills; failure to comply with other FA/lease provisions.
- 180 days: Failure to meet minimum financial criteria.

**Non-curable defaults**: Bankruptcy; inability to pay debts; conviction of felony/fraud/moral turpitude; unfair competition; deleterious behavior; false/misleading statements; Gross Sales understatement; failure to close on health/safety issues; unauthorized transfer; repeated breaches after notice.

CYC additional non-curable: Any dividend/distribution while Promissory Note outstanding. Carrols Refranchise: Failure under any agreement related to refranchising transaction.

### Transfer
- BKC consent required (may be withheld in sole discretion during first 5 years under CYC; until remodel completion under Carrols Refranchise).
- Buyer must be approved, complete all training, all amounts current, transfer fees paid, general release by seller, restaurants inspected and brought to current image.
- Transfer fee: $2,000 first restaurant + $500 each additional (same transaction). $175 surcharge for weekend/holiday transfers.
- Right of first refusal: BKC can match any third-party offer to purchase restaurant or any interest.
- Post-termination option to purchase: Paper goods at cost, equipment/fixtures/signs at fair market value. Under Carrols Refranchise: option to repurchase at depreciated value if remodels not completed.

### Non-Compete
- **During term**: No ownership/operation/interest in any other hamburger business.
- **Post-term**: 1 year, within 2-mile radius of your restaurant.
- **CYC**: Managing Owner cannot engage in any other business until later of 5th anniversary or loan repayment.

### Dispute Resolution
- No mandatory arbitration (voluntary for development disputes at franchisee's election).
- Mandatory non-binding mediation for development disputes.
- **Venue**: US District Court for the Southern District of Florida, or state court in Miami-Dade County, FL.
- **Governing law**: Florida law (subject to state-specific overrides).
- Waiver of jury trial (under CYC loan agreements).

### Death/Disability
Heir must be approved or sell interest within 12 months. BKC has option to operate/manage restaurant during interim.
