## Pass A — Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: Pizza Hut, LLC, a Delaware LLC, successor to Pizza Hut, Inc. (merged 2016). Ultimate parent is YUM! Brands, Inc. Principal office in Plano, TX.
2. **Strategic uncertainty**: In November 2025, YUM announced it is exploring strategic options for the Pizza Hut brand, which may include a full or partial sale or separation. No assurance of outcome. This is a material risk factor.
3. **System size (Dec 29, 2025)**: 4,956 franchised + 75 company-owned = 5,031 total. Net decline of 206 units in FY2025. An additional 127 franchised restaurants ceased operations between Dec 30, 2025 and March 6, 2026.
4. **Restaurant concepts offered**: RBD (Restaurant-Based Delivery), Delco (Delivery/Carryout), DBR/FCD (Delivery Based/Fast Casual Delco). All must incorporate WingStreet.
5. **Investment range**: $670K–$2.13M depending on concept (Inline/Endcap Delco at low end, RBD/DBR at high end), excluding land.
6. **Fee burden**: 6% royalty (6.5% in certain circumstances) + 4.75% advertising fund + Digital Innovation Fee ($0.41/digital transaction) + Restaurant Technology Fee ($2,950/yr) + Dragontail KMX ($75/mo if DaaS). Total ongoing burden roughly 11.75%+ of gross sales before tech fees.
7. **Item 19 — FPR**: Average gross sales for all 4,767 mature franchised restaurants in FY2025 = $972,864 (median $929,232). Only 43.4% exceeded the average. Year-over-year decline from FY2024 average of $1,024,898.
8. **Territory**: No exclusive territory. Protected radius of 500 yards only. Delivery Area rights contingent on providing Adequate Delivery Service.
9. **Term**: 10-year initial term from opening date. Two 5-year renewal terms available. Renewal requires signing then-current form of agreement which may be materially different.
10. **Litigation**: One pending mass arbitration (80 claimants alleging Meta Pixel wiretapping violations in California).
11. **Bankruptcy**: None disclosed.
12. **Hut Forward Program**: Comprehensive agreement (Jan 5, 2026) between YUM, PHLLC, and IPHFHA to drive growth — includes YUM marketing investment, delivery strategy development, and new technology framework. Qualified franchisees may close up to 10% of existing restaurants.
13. **Contraction trajectory**: System lost 258 franchised units in FY2025, 86 in FY2024, and 2 in FY2023. Company-owned grew from 7 to 75 as PHLLC reacquired 54 units in FY2025. Net system shrinking significantly.
14. **Financial health (PH Guarantor)**: Net income $195.2M (2025), down from $226.8M (2024) and $231.8M (2023). Member's equity $69.1M. Cash swept to YUM: $193.4M in FY2025.
15. **Gag clause**: Present. "In some instances, current and former franchisees sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with us." (p. 58/PDF 66)

## Pass D — Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory

**NO EXCLUSIVE TERRITORY.**

**Protected Radius**: 500 yards around each restaurant Location. Within this radius, PHLLC will not develop or allow another franchisee/licensee to develop the SAME type of System Restaurant Concept. However, PHLLC may:
- Franchise/license ANY OTHER type of System Restaurant Concept within the Protected Radius
- Franchise/license ANY type outside the Protected Radius, even proximate
- Acquire/merge with competitors and operate within Protected Radius under any marks
- Sell Approved Products through ANY alternative distribution channel within Protected Radius (except same concept type through a System Restaurant)
- Operate at non-traditional locations within Protected Radius (sports arenas, airports, malls, universities, hospitals, military bases, etc.)

**Delivery Area**: Specified delivery zone per restaurant. Protected only if providing "Adequate Delivery Service" per Brand Standards. If inadequate delivery found:
- Written notice → 30 days to cure
- If uncured, PHLLC may redefine delivery area
- On second failure, may terminate franchise for that restaurant
- During cure period, PHLLC or others may furnish delivery in your area

**National/Regional/Institutional Accounts**: PHLLC alone controls. May require you to fulfill orders at PHLLC-negotiated prices.

**Relocation**: Requires prior written approval. Must pay relocation costs. New franchise fee if replacement doesn't open within 12 months.

**Other channels**: You may only sell from approved Location. No wholesale, no alternative distribution without written consent.

**Franchisee waiver**: You waive and release all claims related to PHLLC's competitive activities and promise never to join any legal action contesting these rights.

### Item 13 — Trademarks

Principal marks registered on USPTO Principal Register. Key registrations include PIZZA HUT (multiple registrations from 1962–2025), Pizza Hut Building Designs, Roof Logos, WINGSTREET. All affidavits current. No pending oppositions, cancellations, or material litigation.

PHLLC is sole and exclusive owner. You appoint PHLLC as attorney-in-fact for trademark claims. May modify/delete marks without compensation.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information

No material patents. Copyright claims on Brand Standards, Standards Library, Website, App, software, ads, promotional materials. Trade secrets include recipes, spice blends, Brand Standards, Computer System software. Strict confidentiality requirements — Qualified Operators and managers must sign Confidentiality Agreement (Appendix I-2); owners/equity holders must sign Confidentiality/Non-Competition Agreement (Appendix I-1).

## Pass E — Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal

- **Initial term**: 10 years from opening date per restaurant
- **Renewal**: Two consecutive 5-year renewal terms
- **Renewal conditions**: Timely notice; performance compliance; current on all payments to PHLLC, affiliates, landlord, suppliers; current on maintenance/asset upgrades/Brand Standards; possession of Location during renewal; satisfy qualification/training; sign general release; pay renewal fee ($12,500/restaurant, up to $25,000); sign then-current franchise agreement
- **MATERIALLY DIFFERENT RISK**: Renewal franchise agreement "may be materially different" from current FA. Only protections: no initial franchise fee, no Protected Radius change, royalty not greater than what imposed on similarly situated renewing franchisees.
- **No franchisee right to terminate**

### Termination

**With cure (30 days unless specified)**:
- Failure to open relocated restaurant within 12 months
- Failure to comply with FA or Brand Standards
- Failure to pay amounts owed (10-day cure for 2nd default in 12 months)
- Sale of unauthorized products
- Unresolved trade account delinquencies
- Misuse of Pizza Hut Marks (24-hour cure)
- Failure to provide Adequate Delivery Service
- Failure to satisfy/appeal judgment within 30 days
- Failure to construct/open as required
- Force Majeure extending >90 days (30-day notice)

**Without cure (immediate on notice)**:
- Failure to begin design/construction
- Unauthorized closure >15 consecutive days
- Cease operating
- Felony/fraud/moral turpitude conviction (you, QO, or related persons)
- Unauthorized transfer
- In-term non-compete or confidentiality violation
- Revenue concealment, false books, fraud
- Failure to maintain financial records
- Refusal to allow inspection/audit
- Misuse of withholdings, misappropriation
- Repeated curable defaults
- Failure to comply with law after notice
- Failure to maintain insurance
- Publicity/ridicule/derision or jeopardizing public safety
- Operating at unauthorized location
- Payment/trademark provisions declared invalid
- You or Related Person becomes a Restricted Person
- Bankruptcy/insolvency/receivership/dissolution/levy
- Cross-default under other agreements with PHLLC or affiliates

### Transfer

- **Definition**: Any absolute or conditional transfer of interest, including change of control, assignment, sale, pledge, encumbrance, sharing arrangement, sublicense
- **PHLLC consent required** except for Permitted Transfers (to existing owner, employee benefit, wholly-owned entity)
- **Transfer conditions**: Transferee must meet qualifications, complete Initial Training, sign then-current FA, sign guaranty, sign release. Transferor must cure defaults, satisfy obligations, sign termination agreement and release, furnish transfer documents, pay transfer fee ($2,500 + $250/restaurant). PHLLC must determine total transfer price won't jeopardize economic viability.
- **Right of first refusal**: PHLLC can match any offer for the interest being transferred
- **30 days advance notice required** for any transfer

### Death/Disability

- If approved succession plan exists, it controls
- Non-last-surviving owner: interest may pass to spouse, existing owner, or franchise entity
- Last surviving owner: interest passes to Estate; Estate has ≤6 months to transfer per Section 18.04
- During transition, Estate may operate under QO supervision; PHLLC may manage for Interim Operation Fee

### Non-Competition

**During term**: You and Related Persons may not be involved in any business producing/selling pizza, pasta, or food items similar to Approved Products (including WingStreet), or be a Competitor. May not sell/lease premises to a Competitor. "Competitor" = derives >10% revenue from similar products, or grants franchises for same, or offers confusingly similar products.

**Post-term (18 months)**: May not produce/sell similar products or assist a Competitor within (a) 25-mile radius of any Pizza Hut Restaurant, (b) any county with Pizza Hut Restaurants, or (c) within 10 miles of any U.S. System Restaurant location. For 2 years, may not sell/lease premises to a Competitor.

### Dispute Resolution

- **Mediation first**: National Franchise Mediation Program administered by CPR. Only for disputes specific to franchisee (not system-wide issues). Excludes trademark, confidential information, and money-owed disputes.
- **Venue**: Mediation in Plano, TX; litigation in Collin County, TX (or where principal place of business then located). PHLLC may bring certain actions in any court with jurisdiction.
- **Governing law**: Texas law (subject to state law overrides)
- **No arbitration** (unlike some YUM brands) — but note CA addendum mentions binding arbitration
- **Loser-pays** attorneys' fees
