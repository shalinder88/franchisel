## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand & Segment**: Graduate by Hilton is a lifestyle/boutique hotel brand targeting college-town markets. It was acquired by Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT) from Graduate IP LLC on May 24, 2024 for approximately $210 million (per public filings). The brand was previously independent; under Hilton, 36 existing "Project Alpha by Hilton" franchises were converted to the Graduate brand.

2. **Franchisor Entity**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 2007. This entity is the franchisor for ALL Hilton brands in the US (not just Graduate), which is significant for understanding the financial statements.

3. **Ownership Chain**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC → Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. → Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). Hilton is one of the world's largest hospitality companies with 18+ branded hotel systems.

4. **System Size**: 32 franchised Graduate hotels in 24 US states at end of 2024. Zero company-owned outlets. Zero terminations, non-renewals, or closures. All 32 were conversions from Project Alpha by Hilton, not organic Graduate developments. Two additional franchise agreements signed but not yet opened (both in Texas), with one projected opening in the next fiscal year.

5. **Investment Range**: $18,746,437 to $91,125,745 for a typical 150-room Graduate hotel, excluding real property, market studies, insurance, and interest. This is a premium full-service hotel investment. Hilton explicitly notes it does not yet have actual initial investment data for Graduate hotels developed in its system.

6. **Fee Burden**: The core ongoing fee burden exceeds 13% of Gross Rooms Revenue: 5% royalty + 4% program fee (can increase to 5% over the term) + 4.3% Hilton Honors + 1.35% Hilton Advance. Additional technology fees (OnQ maintenance $2,230–$6,630/month, connectivity $400–$600/month, Connected Room $630–$945/month) and numerous distribution/commission fees layer on top.

7. **Item 19**: NO financial performance representations are made. At a minimum $18.7 million investment level, this absence means prospective franchisees have no franchisor-disclosed data on revenue, costs, or profitability. The only avenue for financial due diligence is direct contact with the 34 existing franchisees listed in Exhibit A.

8. **Bottom-Quartile Risk**: The non-renewable franchise agreement (re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion, potentially under materially different terms) creates asymmetric contract risk. Combined with the non-exclusive territory, the extensive carve-outs from any negotiated Restricted Area, and the heavy fee burden, bottom-quartile operators face significant economic pressure with limited contractual recourse.

9. **Financial Health**: The franchisor entity is extremely profitable (99%+ operating margin, $1.49 billion net income in 2024), but this reflects ALL Hilton brands, not just Graduate. Nearly all cash is swept to the parent ($1.5 billion distributed in 2024, leaving only $317K cash). All franchisor assets and franchise contracts are pledged as collateral for Hilton parent debt maturing 2025–2033. The entity is a pass-through for Hilton's franchise revenue.

10. **Growth Trajectory**: The brand went from 0 to 32 hotels in a single year (2024) via brand conversion, not organic growth. With only 2 signed-not-opened and 1 projected new opening, near-term organic growth appears modest. The brand is in its earliest phase under Hilton's management.

11. **Territory**: Non-exclusive by default. A Restricted Area may be negotiated but is not standard, is typically shorter than the 23-year franchise term, and contains extensive carve-outs for other Hilton brands, acquisitions, gaming, timeshare, and chain transactions involving 4+ hotels.

12. **Renewal**: There is NO renewal right. At end of term, Hilton may offer re-licensing at its sole discretion, and the new agreement may have "materially different terms and conditions."

13. **Disputes**: Venue is Virginia or New York; governing law is New York. Multiple pending antitrust class actions allege Hilton participated in hotel room price-fixing through IDeaS/Amadeus/STR revenue management software.

14. **Special Risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Virginia/New York) is highlighted as a special risk. Michigan franchise investment law provisions are also prominently displayed.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Hilton Franchise Holding LLC is the single franchisor entity for all Hilton brands in the US. It was formed in 2007 and became the Graduate franchisor on May 28, 2024. The entity licenses intellectual property from Hilton International Holding LLC (affiliate) on a royalty-free basis and sublicenses it to franchisees. Key service affiliates include Hilton Reservations Worldwide (reservations), HSM (supply management), Hilton Honors Worldwide (loyalty program), and HSS (IT systems).

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Franchise Application Fee: $100,000 for new development/conversion (+ $400/room over 250); $150,000 for change of ownership; $100,000 for re-licensing
- PIP Fee: $10,000 (conversions/COO/re-licensing)
- OnQ Computer System: $118,350–$273,450
- Other startup fees: $6,500 (GRO, Digital Floor Plan, Opening Transition Tool)
- Training: $5,000–$15,000
- Opening Process Services: $20,000
- RMCC Pre-Opening: $4,059–$8,880 (required for expedited openings)
- Procurement services: 4%–10% of project cost (if using HSM)

**Ongoing Fees (core):**
- Monthly Royalty: 5% of Gross Rooms Revenue (non-modifiable)
- Monthly Program Fee: 4% of GRR (may increase up to +1% over term)
- Hilton Honors: 4.3% of total eligible guest folio (waived for on-property enrollments)
- Hilton Advance: 1.35% of eligible Digital Direct Revenue (max $30/stay)

**Ongoing Fees (technology):**
- OnQ Connectivity: $400–$600/month ($4,800–$7,200/year)
- Hardware/Software Maintenance: $2,230–$6,630/month ($26,760–$79,560/year)
- Connected Room Maintenance: $630–$945/month ($7,560–$11,340/year)
- OnQ Email: ~$24–$50/month per user
- Delphi: $858/user/year

**Ongoing Fees (distribution/commission):**
Numerous per-stay, per-room-night, and percentage-based fees for travel clubs, group partnerships, centralized payments, consortia, government programs, ResMax, and event booking platforms. These are layered and cumulative.

**Quality/Compliance Fees:**
- Brand Non-Compliance: $65–$145/room (capped $50K per 6-month period)
- Stay Score Failure: up to $300/room or 0.5% GRR annually
- Service Quality Improvement: $20,000–$50,000 per 6-month period
- Plans/Designs Late Fee: $8,000 every 90 days
- PIP/Renovation Failure: $16,000 every 6 months

**Liquidated Damages:**
- Pre-opening termination: $11,200 × approved rooms
- Post-opening (before 2nd anniversary): greater of (Avg Monthly Royalty × 60) or ($11,200 × rooms)
- Post-opening (after 2nd anniversary, before final 60 months): Avg Monthly Royalty × 60
- Final 60 months: Avg Monthly Royalty × months remaining

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Franchisees must build, furnish, and equip hotels to Hilton's Brand Standards. While most FF&E and supplies may be purchased from any source meeting Standards, several items require approved or sole-source suppliers:
- OnQ software: licensed exclusively from HSS (proprietary, no third-party market)
- Exterior signage: must purchase from Hilton-licensed vendors
- Logo/trademark items: must use approved suppliers
- Food and Beverage Consultant: must use a consultant approved by Hilton (unique to Graduate brand)

HSM (Hilton Supply Management) negotiates bulk pricing but is generally optional for Graduate (mandatory for Spark brand). In 2024, HSM collected $19.6M in rebates/allowances, $8.8M in direct sales, and $38.7M in administrative fees across all Hilton brands.

Hilton and affiliates (including Hilton Honors Worldwide) generated $1.1 billion in revenues from sales of goods, services, computer systems, and supplies to all-brand franchisees in 2024.

### Approved Supplier Process
Franchisees may request approval of alternative products or suppliers. Hilton reviews within 60–90 days. No fee charged for the review. No material benefit granted based on use of designated suppliers (franchise is non-renewable).

### Training Program
Hilton requires completion of multiple training programs before opening and on an ongoing basis:
- Brand Conference (annual, $2,500/attendee — GM and Director of Sales required)
- GM/Commercial/Sales Leader Training (up to 18 hours virtual, up to $1,200 — within 90 days of start)
- Hilton Core Sales Skills (24 hours virtual, up to $600)
- OnQ PMS, Rate & Inventory, Forecast Management, GRO, Hilton Honors (online, no separate fee)
- ADA Training, Annual Compliance Training (online)
- Owner Orientation (16 hours virtual), New to Hospitality Owner Education (20 hours virtual)
- Graduate Orientation (0–32 hours on-site)

Training is designed for business and operational functions of the hotel. Hilton emphasizes it provides training content but does not direct or supervise franchisee personnel.

### Technology Stack and Costs
- **OnQ**: Core property management system — reservations, revenue management, rate/inventory, forecasting, learning management. Hardware $118,350–$273,450; maintenance $2,230–$6,630/month. Refresh every 3 years.
- **HPMS (PEP)**: Replacement for OnQ PMS, being deployed. Not yet required for Graduate but anticipated to become mandatory. Developed with HotelKey, Inc.
- **Guest Internet Access (StayConnected)**: $72,033–$105,270 initial; circuit $1,950–$4,900/month; service $653–$1,200/month. Refresh every 4 years.
- **GRO**: Revenue optimization (IDeaS-based). Setup ~$2,500; $1,200–$1,750/month. Recommendations are advisory, not mandatory.
- **Delphi**: Cloud-based sales and events (Amadeus). $858/user/year.
- **Connected Room**: Streaming apps and Hilton Honors App control. Initial $34,850–$41,650; maintenance $630–$945/month.
- **Digital Key**: Within OnQ, no separate charge.

### Marketing Fund
The Monthly Program Fee (4% of GRR) funds: reservation systems, directories, websites, advertising, promotion, publicity, public relations, market research, quality assurance programs, technology programs, and associated administrative overhead. Hilton may create or modify programs and allocate funds to any regions or localities. No audited financial statement of the program fund is provided.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition and Carve-Outs
The standard Graduate franchise agreement provides a **non-exclusive license** at a specified location with **no protected territory**. However, Hilton may agree to negotiate a Restricted Area Provision for new developments and conversions (not typically for change of ownership or re-licensing). Key features of a negotiated Restricted Area:

- Boundaries vary by hotel and market; no standard formula
- Restrictive Period is shorter than the franchise term, usually tied to years from application approval
- Geographic scope may reduce after an agreed time period
- Hilton states it historically extended Restrictive Periods for the full term but "does not intend to do so in the future"

**Extensive carve-outs** apply even within a Restricted Area:
1. Pre-existing hotels (already open, under construction, or approved)
2. Hotels under other Hilton brands (DoubleTree, Hampton, Hilton Garden Inn, etc.)
3. Hotels not opening under Graduate until after Restrictive Period expires
4. Gaming-oriented hotels
5. Vacation/timeshare ownership properties
6. Any chain acquisition of 4+ hotels (regardless of location)
7. Strategic Partnerships (third-party distribution/marketing/loyalty relationships)

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights
- Franchisee may not register any domain names containing the Marks
- Must obtain written approval for third-party website listings
- Must participate in the Reservation Service (only outgoing reservation service permitted)
- Must participate in Hilton Honors and honor all discount/promotional programs
- May not charge Mandatory Guest Fees without franchisor consent

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction**: 23 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion**: 10–20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership**: Remaining term or such other term as approved
- **NO RENEWAL RIGHT**: The franchise agreement is non-renewable. Re-licensing is at Hilton's sole discretion and may be under "materially different terms and conditions." This is a critical risk factor — after investing $18.7M–$91.1M, the franchisee has no contractual assurance of continued operation beyond the initial term.

### Transfer Provisions
- **Permitted Transfers without consent**: Publicly traded equity interests; private equity <50% with no control change; institutional fund transfers with same asset manager
- **Permitted Transfers with consent ($5,500 fee)**: To affiliates, family members, trusts; death transfers; private equity >50% change
- **Change of Ownership Transfers ($150,000 fee)**: Proposed transferee must meet current business requirements, sign then-current franchise agreement (potentially materially different), agree to PIP upgrades
- **No right of first refusal or option to purchase**

### Termination and Death/Disability
- Franchisee may NOT terminate the franchise agreement before expiration — unilateral termination is a material breach triggering liquidated damages
- Franchisor may terminate without cause in limited circumstances (condemnation, casualty)
- Curable defaults require 10–30 day cure periods
- 16 categories of non-curable defaults allow immediate termination
- On death, franchise/equity interest may transfer to immediate family within 1 year without consent, provided family member meets requirements

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term**: Franchisee may not own or be licensor/franchisor of a competing brand. May own a minority interest in a competitor under certain circumstances. May be a franchisee or non-exclusive manager of a competitor. Must not allow any Competitor to use the franchisee's marks without Hilton approval.
- **Post-term**: NONE. There is no post-term noncompete, which is favorable to the franchisee.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue**: U.S. District Court, Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria); if no federal subject matter jurisdiction, state court in Fairfax County, Virginia, or New York, New York. Hilton may elect to sue where the hotel is located.
- **Governing Law**: New York law (without conflicts of law principles)
- **Arbitration**: Not required, except in Puerto Rico (AAA/JAMS, venue Fairfax County VA, seat New York NY)
- State addenda may override venue and governing law provisions (California, Illinois, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota)

### Litigation History
**Pending actions (5):**
- Three separate antitrust class actions alleging Hilton participated in room rate price-fixing through IDeaS revenue management software and Amadeus/STR data platforms. These are industry-wide matters involving multiple hotel chains.
- AAAA Property Partners v. Hilton — breach of contract from termination of a Hilton Garden Inn franchise.
- Portillo v. CoStar Group — class action alleging STR competitive data exchange led to higher room rates.

**Concluded actions (5):**
- Texas AG settlement (January 2025): $2.1 million + mandatory guest fee disclosure reforms
- Nebraska AG settlement (February 2024): $300,000 + disclosure reforms
- Portland Hotel Ownership — Curio brand franchise dispute, settled July 2022
- San Pedro Inn — Hampton franchise wrongful termination, settled September 2020
- Hawaii resort fee class action — settled August 2015, $178K + vouchers
- U.S. v. Hilton Hotels Corporation (1971) — Sherman Act final judgment (historical)

No collection suits against franchisees in 2024.
