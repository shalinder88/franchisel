# Reader Report — Graduate by Hilton (638055-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand**: Graduate by Hilton — lifestyle/boutique hotel brand acquired by Hilton Worldwide on May 24, 2024 from Graduate IP LLC. Brand is positioned in the college-town lifestyle segment.
2. **Franchisor**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC (Delaware LLC, formed 2007). Parent chain: Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. → Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT).
3. **System size**: 34 franchised hotels in the US as of December 31, 2024. All converted from the former "Project Alpha by Hilton" brand. Zero company-owned outlets. Zero terminations. Zero transfers. 2 signed but not yet opened (Texas). 1 projected new opening.
4. **Investment range**: $18,746,437 to $91,125,745 for a typical 150-room Graduate hotel, excluding real property, market studies, and insurance.
5. **Fee burden**: 5% royalty on Gross Rooms Revenue + 4% monthly program fee (can increase up to +1% over term) + 4.3% Hilton Honors fee on eligible folio + numerous technology, distribution, and quality assurance fees.
6. **Franchise application fee**: $100,000 (new development/conversion) + $400/room over 250; $150,000 for change of ownership; $100,000 for re-licensing.
7. **Item 19**: NO financial performance representations made. This is a significant absence for a lifestyle hotel brand at this investment level.
8. **Term**: 23 years for new construction; 10–20 years for conversions. NO renewal right — re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion under "materially different" terms.
9. **Territory**: Non-exclusive by default. Restricted Area may be negotiated but is not standard and is typically shorter than the franchise term. Extensive carve-outs for other Hilton brands, acquisitions, strategic partnerships.
10. **Dispute resolution**: Litigation in Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria) or Fairfax County, VA, or New York. New York law governs. No arbitration except in Puerto Rico.
11. **Financial health of franchisor**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC generated $1.50 billion in revenue in 2024 ($1.43B royalties + $49M sales/COO fees + $18M termination/other). Net income $1.49 billion. Distributed $1.5 billion to parent. Total assets $1.27 billion. Clean audit opinion from Cherry Bekaert LLP.
12. **No bankruptcy disclosed** (Item 4).
13. **Litigation**: Multiple pending class actions against Hilton Worldwide related to alleged price-fixing via IDeaS and STR revenue management software. Settled Texas and Nebraska AG actions related to mandatory guest fee disclosure practices.
14. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Virginia/New York). Michigan franchise law provisions highlighted.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Non-exclusive license** at specified location. No protected territory by default.
- Franchisor may negotiate a "Restricted Area Provision" — limited in duration (Restrictive Period typically shorter than full term), limited in scope (Graduate brand only).
- Extensive carve-outs: (a) pre-existing hotels; (b) other brands; (c) post-Restrictive Period; (d) gaming hotels; (e) vacation/timeshare ownership; (f) chain acquisitions of 4+ hotels.
- Franchisor and affiliates may own, operate, franchise any Other Business anywhere including within Restricted Area under non-Graduate brands.
- Strategic Partnerships (distribution/marketing/loyalty with third parties) are "Other Businesses" — no restriction.
- No mechanism for resolving conflicts between franchisee's hotel and Network Hotels.
- No relocation permitted. No options or rights of first refusal for additional franchises.
- Internet restrictions: franchisee may not register any domain names with Marks; must get approval for third-party site listings.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
- Principal Mark: "Graduate"
- Marks owned by Hilton International Holding LLC (affiliate), licensed royalty-free to franchisor.
- Key registrations: GRADUATE (Reg. 5104952, Dec 2016), GRADUATE HOTELS (Reg. 4668348, Jan 2015), GRADUATE HOTELS logo (Reg. 4867944, Dec 2015).
- Acquired from Graduate IP LLC on May 24, 2024.
- No material pending litigation or infringement proceedings.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
- No patents or registered copyrights material to franchise.
- Manual and all operational information is proprietary and confidential.
- Must implement procedures to prevent unauthorized disclosure, including NDAs with employees.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate in Operations
- Must provide qualified management — either owner-operator or approved Management Company.
- If owner operates directly, must complete training and get written approval.
- If Management Company, must get written approval; MC must have authority to perform all FA obligations.
- If MC resigns or is terminated, must retain replacement within 90 days.
- Owner may not be a "Competitor" (defined as owner/licensor/franchisor of a competing brand).
- Franchisees of competing brands or managers of competing brands (if not exclusive manager) are generally permitted.
- Required guaranty from individuals/entities based on financial review.
- No post-term non-competition requirement for owner or Management Company.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on Sales
- No customer restrictions.
- Must comply with Standards for types/levels of services and amenities.
- Must operate 24 hours/day, every day.
- May not change room count or make significant changes without approval.
- May not offer products/services without approval.
- Must participate in Reservation Service and Hilton Honors.
- Must honor all discount/promotional programs.
- May not charge Mandatory Guest Fee without consent.
- No gaming/casino without written permission.
- No timeshare/vacation ownership at or adjacent to hotel without written permission.
- May not share facilities with another lodging business without permission.
- No limit on franchisor's right to change the System.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction**: 23 years from Effective Date.
- **Conversion**: 10–20 years from Opening Date.
- **Change of Ownership**: remaining term or such other term as approved.
- **NO renewal right.** Franchise Agreement is non-renewable. Re-licensing is at franchisor's sole discretion and may require "materially different terms and conditions."

### Termination by Franchisee
- Not authorized. Unilateral termination is material breach → liquidated damages.

### Termination by Franchisor
**Without cause:**
- Condemnation: may terminate on notice, releases from liquidated damages.
- Casualty: if hotel closes, franchisee may rebuild (must reopen within 18 months). If elects not to rebuild, franchisor may terminate; released from liquidated damages if no competing hotel at site within 3 years.

**With cause — curable defaults (with notice + cure period):**
1. Failure to pay amounts due (10-day cure)
2. Failure to commence/complete hotel work or open on schedule (10-day cure)
3. Failure to maintain insurance (10-day cure)
4. Failure to comply with any curable provision (30-day cure)

**Interim remedies during default:** Suspension from Reservation Service, removal from directories, reservation diversion, software disabling, revocation of financial accommodations.

**With cause — non-curable (immediate termination):**
1. Same material breach recurring within 24 months
2. Three default notices in 12 months (regardless of cure)
3. Failure to pay debts / insolvency
4. Bankruptcy filing
5. Loss of possession of hotel/site
6. Failure to operate 5+ consecutive days (except Force Majeure)
7. Contesting franchisor's ownership of System/Marks
8. Conduct adversely affecting reputation/goodwill
9. Concealing revenues / false books
10. Unauthorized transfer
11. Becoming a Sanctioned Person
12. Adverse gaming license disclosure
13. Guarantor breach
14. Threat to public health/safety
15. Becoming a Competitor
16. Termination of other Hotel Agreements

### Transfer
**Permitted Transfers (no consent needed):**
- Publicly traded equity interests
- Private equity interests <50% with no change of control
- Institutional investment fund transfers if asset manager unchanged

**Permitted Transfers (consent needed, $5,500 fee):**
- Transfers to affiliates, family members, trusts
- Death transfers (to immediate family, within 1 year)
- Private equity >50% change since Effective Date

**Change of Ownership Transfers:**
- $150,000 application fee
- Proposed transferee must meet current business requirements
- Must sign then-current franchise agreement (potentially materially different)
- Must agree to PIP upgrades

**No right of first refusal.** No option to purchase.

### Noncompete
- **During term**: Owner may not own/be licensor/franchisor of a competing brand. May own minority interest in competitor under certain circumstances. May be a franchisee or non-exclusive manager of a competitor.
- **Post-term**: NONE. No post-term noncompete.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue**: U.S. District Court, Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria); or state court in Fairfax County, VA, or New York, NY. Franchisor may also sue where hotel is located.
- **Governing law**: New York law (without conflicts of law principles).
- **Arbitration**: None, except in Puerto Rico (AAA/JAMS, venue Fairfax County VA, seat New York).
