## Identified Gaps

1. **Exhibit D-1 (State Addenda to FA)**: Individual state overrides to the franchise agreement not fully enumerated. SEVERITY: Low — state addenda modify specific clauses and are relevant to individual franchisees in specific states, not to overall diligence.

2. **Exhibit J-1 (State Addenda to FDD)**: Only Hawaii, Illinois, and Maryland addenda partially captured. Other state addenda not individually extracted. SEVERITY: Low — same reasoning as above.

3. **Marketing fund spending audit**: FDD does not provide audited breakdown of Monthly Program Fee expenditures. This is a standard Hilton practice (no separate audit provided). SEVERITY: Medium — prospective franchisee cannot verify how 4% program fee is spent.

4. **Graduate-specific financial data**: Financial statements cover ALL Hilton brands. Graduate-specific revenue contribution, profitability, and operational metrics not separately disclosed. SEVERITY: Medium — makes it impossible to assess the Graduate brand's standalone financial health.

5. **Item 11 advertising section**: Detailed breakdown of advertising/marketing programs funded by Monthly Program Fee not fully extracted. The FDD provides only general categories. SEVERITY: Low — this is by design in the FDD.
