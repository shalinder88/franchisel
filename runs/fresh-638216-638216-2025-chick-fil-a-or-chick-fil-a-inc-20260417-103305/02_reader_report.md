## Pass A — Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** As of December 31, 2024, 3,109 domestic Chick-fil-A Restaurants — 2,629 franchised Operator restaurants, 55 company-owned, and 425 Licensed Units. System-wide sales (franchised/company restaurants only) approximated $22.75B in 2024.
2. **Entity structure:** Chick-fil-A, Inc. is a Georgia corporation. CFA Properties, Inc. (affiliate) owns all trademarks and patents, licensed to CFA for sublicensing. Key affiliates include CFA Supply (distribution), Bay Center Foods (lemon juice production), CFA Canada, CFA Puerto Rico, CFA UK, CFA Asia, CFA Servco (gift cards), and Truett's Group (legacy restaurants acquired Oct 2024).
3. **Ownership / leadership:** Cathy family-controlled. Dan T. Cathy — Chair of the Board. Andrew T. Cathy — CEO and Director since Nov 2021. Susannah W. Frost — President since Oct 2024. Donald "Bubba" Cathy — EVP and Director.
4. **Investment range:** $585,500 to $3,437,000 total initial investment. No initial license fee ($0).
5. **Fee burden:** 10% license fee on gross receipts (7% for transportation venues). No advertising fund contribution required. Additional training fee of $250–$400 per employee (first 2 free). Equipment handling fee of 8%.
6. **Item 19 highlights:** College/university units (312): median $897,932, avg $1,395,745. Hospital/business/airport units (97): median $3,152,764, avg $3,451,788.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** College/university units — ~10% had annual sales below $500,000. Lowest was $192,830. The 10% license fee makes low-volume locations unsustainable.
8. **Financial health:** Total assets $16.59B (2024). Net earnings $1.044B. Total revenues $9.063B. Audited by PwC, clean unqualified opinion. Strong cash position — $1.41B cash.
9. **Growth trajectory:** Licensed Units grew from 393 (start 2022) to 425 (end 2024) — net +32 over 3 years. Operator restaurants grew from 2,230 to 2,629 — net +399. Aggressive growth.
10. **Territory:** No exclusive or protected territory. CFA can open competing units anywhere, including at the same Licensed Site (just not the "exact same" location within it).
11. **Renewal:** No contractual right to renew. CFA may offer a new license at expiration in its sole discretion with potentially different terms and fees.
12. **Disputes:** Litigation in Georgia only (subject to state addenda). Georgia law governs.
13. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution — all litigation must occur in Georgia.
14. **Gag clause:** Licensees did NOT sign speech-restricting provisions. However, some Operators (separate program) DID sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly.

## Pass C — Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Restrictions on Sources of Products and Services

**Heavy supplier restrictions.** Licensees must purchase virtually all food, drink, non-food inventory, ingredients, food products, produce, mixes, wrappings, containers, marketing materials, and trademark-bearing items from CFA-approved suppliers. CFA can designate sole suppliers and may limit approved suppliers at any time.

**Proprietary products:**
- Proprietary seasoning — CFA receives ~$0.06/lb from poultry supplier
- Proprietary seasoned coater — purchased from CFA, markup varies
- Proprietary lemon juice — produced by affiliate Bay Center

**CFA Supply:** Affiliate distributor, may be sole distributor in its area. Pricing includes distribution fee and allowance for fuel/freight.

**Bay Center:** Affiliate producing lemon juice, may be sole supplier in its area.

**Warehouse goods:** CFA cost + ~2.5% for processing/storage. Licensees charged shipping from warehouse.

**Approved supplier revenue (2024):** Total consolidated revenues from Operator/Licensee purchases of proprietary items, leases, etc. = $1,778,691,073 (19.63% of total revenues of $9,062,620,436). CFA Supply received $1,996,943,030. Bay Center received $186,616,788.

**Purchase concentration:** ~50%–80% of operating purchases and ~30%–67% of establishment purchases from approved/designated suppliers.

National ad fund receives contributions from certain designated suppliers of up to ~33% of Operator purchases.

No buying or distribution cooperatives for Licensees.

### Item 11 — Franchisor's Assistance, Advertising, Computer Systems, Training

**Pre-opening assistance:**
- Training for 2 management-level employees per Licensed Site (Licensee Orientation + Operational Training)
- Operations/training/orientation manuals (electronic + loaner copy; $500 replacement)

**During operation:**
- Continuing manual access
- Additional advice/assistance at CFA's discretion
- Additional training at cost ($250–$400/employee)

**Advertising:** No advertising fees or fund contributions required for Licensees. Must honor CFA-issued coupons (may receive food/paper/distribution cost reimbursement). Must use only CFA-approved marketing materials.

**Hardware/Software:** CFA does not currently specify POS equipment for Licensees but reserves the right. Licensees choose their own POS system. CFA Restaurants typically use POS systems with support fees ranging $9,500–$20,000/year. CFA reserves right to require data transmission in future.

**Time to opening:** Must open within 360 days of site approval. Typical: 3–9 months.

**Training program:** Two components:
1. **In-Person Cultural Experience** (2 days at CFA Support Center, Atlanta GA) — 15 hours classroom + 5 hours on-job. Covers CFA brand/history/culture, roles, SERVE model, training resources, menu tasting.
2. **Virtual Licensee Training** (5 days via Zoom) — 15 hours classroom + ~10 hours on-job. Covers history/culture, quality assessments, back/front of house processes, critical success factors, action planning.

Certification valid 5 years; recertification required. Training conducted ~8 times/year (2 per quarter). Directed by Bill Dunphy (30+ years CFA experience).

First 2 employees per new site: free. Additional: $250 (2-day only) or $400 (2-day + 5-day).

## Pass D — Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
**No exclusive or protected territory.** Express and clear: "You will not receive an exclusive or protected territory, express or implied."

Licensed to operate at one specific Licensed Site location. No right to exclude, disapprove, or control other CFA restaurants. CFA can open other Licensed Units, Operator restaurants, or company-owned restaurants both within and outside the geographic/customer trading area. CFA can use Internet, catalog sales, telemarketing, and other channels.

**Only protection:** During the license term, CFA will not operate or grant others the right to operate at the "exact same" Licensed Site (or exact same retail area within a larger site like an airport).

No relocation without CFA written consent. Additional Licensed Units require CFA approval (not guaranteed). CFA may require new License Agreement and initial fee for additional units.

**Competition sources:**
- Other Licensed Units nearby
- Operator restaurants
- CFA-operated restaurants
- Truett's Group (18 Dwarf House/Truett's Grill restaurants)
- Little Blue Menu (delivery kitchen concept with virtual restaurants)
- Any future competitive business under a different name

### Item 13 — Trademarks
CFA Properties, Inc. owns all trademarks, licensed to CFA for sublicensing. Extensive trademark portfolio including: Chick-fil-A, Eat Mor Chikin, various cow designs, Chick-n-Strips, Chick-n-Minis, Cool Wrap, Icedream, Sunjoy, Chick-fil-A One, and many others. 80+ registered/pending marks listed.

No currently effective material PTO determinations, pending infringements, or known superior prior rights. CFA Properties-to-CFA license is 1-year auto-renewing.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
CFA Properties owns numerous utility patents including: charbroiler systems (3 patents), distributed computing for tax calculation, modular humidity control, automated food preparation (gantry frying), object detection-based projected content, capacitive touch surfaces, automated food coating, food transportation systems. Plus 8+ pending applications (automated condiment dispenser, mobile fryer, breading station, ML-based story generation).

Proprietary information includes seasoning, seasoned coater, operations manuals, training materials, market research data. Confidentiality requirements during term and 2 years post-termination (3 years for managers/supervisors).

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
Must use diligent efforts to advance CFA reputation. Must maintain at least 2 management-level employees per Licensed Site who have completed CFA training. Managers/supervisors must sign confidentiality agreements.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on What Franchisee May Sell
Must offer and sell only CFA-approved products. Must include all designated core menu items (Addendum B to License Agreement). Cannot use Licensed Unit premises for any other purpose. Any additional products require CFA written permission.

## Pass E — Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Term:** Determined by CFA prior to execution, based on licensing opportunity, site nature, lease duration, and past CFA experience. Not a fixed standard term.
- **Renewal:** **No contractual right to renew or extend.** CFA may, in its sole discretion, offer a new license agreement with potentially new terms, conditions, and fees. May require refurbishment/remodeling to current standards.

### Termination
- **By Licensee:** Upon CFA's material breach (30 days' cure opportunity) or material misrepresentation (10 days' notice).
- **By CFA without cause:** Not applicable.
- **By CFA with cause — curable defaults:** Breach of material duties; damage to goodwill; failure to comply with hours; continuance of operation materially impaired; loss of Licensed Site; failure to comply with government regulations; failure to comply with CFA standards.
- **By CFA with cause — non-curable defaults:** Material misrepresentation; felony conviction or crime of moral turpitude; conversion/embezzlement; misrepresentation to governmental authority or in securities; substantially all Licensed Units materially impaired; 5%+ understatement of gross receipts on 2+ occasions; insolvency/bankruptcy; operation frustrated by law, pandemic, acts of God, war, labor unions.

### Transfer
No transfer without CFA prior written approval. CFA has right but not obligation to approve. No right of first refusal or option to purchase.

### Noncompete
- **During term:** Cannot sell nationally/regionally branded chicken products or substantially similar products at Licensed Site. Cannot disclose trade secrets.
- **Post-termination:** 12 months post-expiration/termination at former Licensed Site — cannot sell nationally/regionally branded chicken products or substantially similar products. Trade secret protection is permanent; confidential information protection lasts 2 years post-termination.

### Death/Disability
Not applicable.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Forum:** Litigation in Atlanta, Georgia area (subject to state addenda).
- **Law:** Georgia law (subject to state addenda).
- **No arbitration or mediation required.**
- **Integration clause:** Only terms of License Agreement are binding (subject to state law).

### Unresolveds
1. **License term variability** — No standard term disclosed; "to be determined by Chick-fil-A" creates significant uncertainty. What is the typical range? [Severity: HIGH]
2. **Operator vs. Licensee economics** — Operators pay 12–15% base + 50% net profit; Licensees pay 10% of gross. No way to compare net economics without Operator FDD. [Severity: MEDIUM]
3. **CFA Supply geographic coverage** — CFA Supply served 1,107 locations in 2024 (of 3,109 total). Is it the sole distributor in areas it serves? What is the pricing markup? [Severity: MEDIUM]
4. **Gag clause ambiguity** — Licensees reportedly did not sign gag provisions, but Operators did. A prospective Licensee wanting to speak with current Operators may encounter restrictions. [Severity: MEDIUM]
5. **No renewal right** — Unlike most franchise systems, there is zero contractual entitlement to renewal. CFA can simply let the license expire. [Severity: HIGH]
