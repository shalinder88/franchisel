## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
Hyatt Place Franchising, L.L.C. is a single-purpose franchisor entity within the Hyatt Hotels Corporation family. It grants franchises only for Hyatt Place brand hotels and has no other business activities. The franchisor itself does not operate hotels — that function resides with Hyatt Corporation and its affiliates, who provide system services to franchisees through the "Hyatt Group" structure. This organizational separation means the franchise relationship is primarily financial: the franchisor collects fees and enforces standards, while affiliates deliver services.

Only entities (not individuals) may acquire franchises. Each franchisee must have a franchisor-approved management company or obtain approval to self-manage. Personal guaranties from Controlling Ownership Interest holders are mandatory.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
The total initial fees paid by franchisees in FY2024 ranged from $53,250 to $228,950. The key initial fee is the application fee: $75,000 for new development (plus $500/room over 150 rooms) or $150,000 for change of ownership. This fee is partially refundable (less $7,500) if the application is withdrawn before approval, but non-refundable after approval.

Additional initial fees include: PIP fee ($10,000 for conversions), operator approval ($10,000-$20,000), IT Project Management Services ($58,300 plus $6,695-$11,330 expenses for up to 250 rooms), training fees ($15,175-$34,300 plus expense reimbursement), comfort letter fees ($2,500 each), and various optional services (pre-opening purchasing $85K-$275K, additional advisory $0-$250K, alternative design review $5K-$30K).

**Ongoing Fees:**
The ongoing fee structure is layered and substantial:

- **Royalty:** 5% of Gross Rooms Revenue, payable monthly. May be reduced for years 1-3 for new construction (negotiable).
- **Commercial Services Fee:** 3.5% of GRR, covering marketing, reservations, revenue management technology, group sales, PMS services, digital technology, and other tech services. This is the franchisor's primary system services charge.
- **Digital Acquisition Fee:** 1.35% of GRR from digital channel bookings (Hyatt.com, mobile app). With 60%+ of bookings through direct channels, this fee applies to a majority of revenue.
- **World of Hyatt Assessment:** 4% of eligible WOH member revenue (reduced to 2% for on-property enrollments). With WOH driving 50.7% of system revenue, this is a material cost. However, in aggregate, franchisees received $68.88M more in WOH reimbursements than paid in fees in 2024.
- **GDS/Reservation Fees:** ~$8.50 per reservation from supplemental systems.
- **Mandatory Contracts:** $1,500-$7,500/month for credit card, music, telecom agreements.
- **Other Corporate Services:** $500-$5,000/month.
- **PMS Subscription:** $6.65/room/month.
- **QA/Compliance Fees:** $1,500-$2,000 annual inspection; FIP stages $5,000-$20,000 per 6-month stage; BPP $45,000/year if quality fails persist.

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Transfer: $150,000 change of ownership application fee
- Liquidated damages: Up to 36 months × (AMR × 8.5%); 150% for Consequential Termination
- De-identification failure: $5,000/day
- Late payments: $225 + 1.5%/month interest
- Audit underpayment: Cost of audit if 3%+ understatement

### Initial Investment
Total estimated initial investment: $17,918,455 to $52,134,018 (excluding real estate).

The investment is dominated by construction costs ($13.5M-$40M), making this a capital-intensive franchise requiring significant real estate development capability. Key other components include FF&E ($1.845M-$6M), technology systems ($638K-$778K), architecture/design ($315K-$1.2M), operating supplies ($414K-$1.32M), and liquor license ($30K-$400K depending on jurisdiction).

The estimate assumes a new-construction hotel with 90-200 guest rooms on a 1-3 acre site in a primary market. Conversion costs are not separately estimated but may be lower if the existing structure is in good condition.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Approximately 95% of establishment and operating purchases must come from the franchisor, designated suppliers, or according to System Standards. This is one of the highest supplier restriction levels in franchising and reflects the brand-standard-intensive nature of hotel operations.

Key supplier restrictions:
- **Opera PMS:** Mandatory, designated vendor only. Must sign Subscription and Hosting Services Agreement for franchisor-hosted PMS.
- **CRS/GDS/ADS:** Must participate in all authorized reservation systems. Best rate guarantee required.
- **F&B Operations:** Must submit F&B Operations Plan for approval, including any third-party restaurant operators.
- **Cyber security:** Must use designated endpoint protection and web filtering tools.
- **Technology consultant:** If no in-house IT support, must contract with qualified third-party.

Franchisees may request approval of alternative suppliers (typically 30-day response), but must pay testing fees up to $20,000.

### Training Program Detail
The 8-part mandatory training program is extensive:
- Owner Briefing: 8 hours (Chicago or virtual)
- FMU (for new management companies): 15-17 hours (virtual)
- Owner/Operator Orientation: 16 hours (Chicago or virtual, quarterly)
- GMU (for each new GM): 53-58 hours (eLearning + virtual + 3-day in-person workshop)
- Operations: 47-79 hours classroom + 40-76 hours OJT
- Sales/Events/Revenue Management: 136.5 hours
- Other Personnel: 17-34 hours classroom + 15-36 hours OJT
- Pre-Opening: 7+ nights on-site with 2-3 trainers

Total training investment is substantial, particularly for the general manager (53-58 hours over multiple modalities) and sales functions (136.5 hours). GM replacements must complete GMU within 6 months of hire.

### Technology Stack
Required systems: Opera PMS, POS, Revenue Management, Events/Lead Generation (Envision), Key Lock, PBX/Phones, In-Room Entertainment, High Speed Internet, Guest Computing, Music, Digital Surveillance, Scent. Estimated cost: $638,600-$777,650.

Ongoing technology costs include PMS vendor maintenance ($3.90/room/month), PMS hosting ($6.65/room/month), sound system ($18.95/month), plus technology costs embedded in the 3.5% Commercial Services Fee.

No contract limits the frequency or cost of required technology upgrades, creating an open-ended obligation.

### Marketing Fund Spending
There is no separate advertising fund. Marketing is funded through the 3.5% Commercial Services Fee, which covers brand-wide marketing, advertising, PR, guest satisfaction, and digital technology. The Hyatt Group need not spend any amount on advertising in the franchisee's market area. No periodic accounting of Commercial Services Fee spending is provided to franchisees. The Digital Acquisition Fee (1.35% on digital channel revenue) separately funds digital channel bookings.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
The Area of Protection is agreed pre-signing with boundaries defined by geographic references or radius. Urban areas may be as small as a few blocks; suburban areas up to approximately a 3-mile radius. The AOP Term begins on the Franchise Agreement effective date and typically ends 3 years after the Hotel's opening date.

### Critical Territorial Limitations
1. **AOP Term expiration:** After 3 years of operation, the franchisee has NO territorial protection whatsoever. The franchisor and its affiliates may open competing Hyatt Place hotels and other Hyatt Network Hotels anywhere, including within the former AOP.

2. **Acquisition exception:** Even during the AOP Term, if the franchisor acquires a group of 4+ hotels with one or more in the AOP, those can be converted to Brand Hotels regardless of the AOP protection.

3. **Other Hyatt brands:** Hyatt operates 25+ lodging brands. Other Hyatt brands (Hyatt House, Hyatt Regency, Hyatt Centric, etc.) may operate within the AOP at any time without restriction.

4. **No options for additional franchises.**

5. **Channel competition:** No restrictions on franchisor/other franchisees soliciting customers anywhere via any channel.

For a $18M-$52M investment, the 3-year territorial window followed by unlimited competition is a significant risk factor.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
Initial term is 20 years from the first day of the month the Hotel opens. Successor franchise is 10 years on then-current terms, which may be "materially different" including fees. This "materially different" language is a critical risk — after investing $18M-$52M and operating for 20 years, the franchisee faces the prospect of substantially different economic terms at renewal, with limited alternatives given the specialized nature of the hotel investment.

Renewal requires: substantial compliance during the term, current compliance, meeting then-current new franchisee standards, passing QA scores for the preceding 3 years, possessing the Hotel for 10+ additional years, renovating/remodeling the Hotel, paying the PIP fee, and signing a general release (where state law allows).

### Transfer Provisions
All transfers of the Franchise Agreement, Hotel assets, or Controlling Ownership Interests require franchisor approval. The transfer fee is the then-current change of ownership application fee ($150,000). Conditions include: transferee qualification, payment of all amounts owed, training completion, signing then-current agreement (which may be materially different), general release, agreement to renovate, and capital structure approval. The franchisor has a right of first offer only in strategic markets (Exhibit C).

Non-Controlling Ownership Interest transfers are permitted without approval subject to conditions (no Competing Brand Owner, no sanctioned persons, no creation of new Controlling Ownership Interest, 30-day notice).

### Termination and Death/Disability
The franchisee may not terminate the Franchise Agreement except as law allows. The franchisor may terminate only for cause, with cure periods ranging from 24 hours (health/safety) to 60 days (Guarantor Monetary Threshold). Non-curable defaults include bankruptcy, abandonment, unauthorized transfer, felony, false books, and repeated defaults.

Upon death or disability of a Controlling Ownership Interest holder, the representative must transfer to an approved transferee within 6 months.

### Noncompete
During the term, there is no formal covenant not to compete, but neither the franchisee nor its management company may be a "Competing Brand Owner" (entity that owns/franchises/manages a competing brand with 4+ hotels and ADR of at least 60% of Brand Hotel ADR).

Post-term: No noncompete covenant. This is favorable for the franchisee.

### Dispute Resolution
All disputes must be arbitrated within 10 miles of the franchisor's principal business address (currently Chicago, Illinois). Subject to arbitration, litigation must be in Illinois. Illinois law governs (subject to Federal Arbitration Act, federal law, and state-specific overrides in Exhibit L).

### Litigation History
No litigation disclosed in Item 3. Clean record.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Overview
Hyatt Place provides three FPRs covering all 348 Covered Hotels (323 franchised, 25 owned/managed) that operated during all of 2024. The system also breaks out 77 Urban Hotels (73 franchised, 4 owned/managed). No hotels were excluded from the Covered Hotels group.

### FPR 1: Occupancy, ADR, and RevPAR

**Franchised Covered Hotels (323 units):**
- Average Occupancy Rate: 72.0% (median: 72.2%)
- Average Daily Rate: $158.98 (median: $140.95)
- Average RevPAR: $114.45 (median: $101.75)

The $12.70 gap between average and median RevPAR ($114.45 vs. $101.75) indicates right-skew: a minority of high-performing hotels pull the average up. Only 117 of 323 franchised hotels (36.2%) exceeded the average RevPAR. This means nearly two-thirds of the system operates below the "average."

**Range analysis:** RevPAR ranges from a low of $43.29 to a high of $259.58 — a 6:1 spread. The low end represents hotels that are likely struggling to cover operating costs and debt service.

**Urban Hotels (77 total):**
Urban locations significantly outperform: 74.4% occupancy, $193.16 ADR, $143.69 RevPAR. The urban premium is approximately 27% in RevPAR.

### FPR 2: Smith Travel Indices
Hyatt Place outperforms its competitive set:
- Franchised RevPAR Index: 106.1 (capturing 6.1% more than fair share)
- Franchised Occupancy Index: 105.0
- Rate Index: 100.6

This indicates the brand has pricing power and guest preference relative to competitors — a positive signal for franchisees.

### FPR 3: World of Hyatt Program
The loyalty program is a major business driver:
- 50.7% of franchised revenue comes from World of Hyatt members
- 42.0% of room nights are WOH member stays
- Average 15,267 WOH-occupied room nights per hotel

This heavy loyalty dependence means the 4% WOH assessment, while costly, is associated with the business that fills rooms. In aggregate, franchisees received $68.88M more in loyalty reimbursements than they paid in fees in 2024.

### Channel Mix
60.2% of franchised GRR comes through Direct Channels (Hyatt.com, mobile app, contact centers), and 39.8% through Indirect Channels (GDS, OTAs). This is a favorable direct-to-indirect ratio for cost management.

### Critical Caveats
- **No cost data disclosed:** Franchisees cannot determine profitability, EBITDA, or cash flow from these FPRs. Independent cost investigation is essential.
- **Unaudited figures:** Except Smith Travel data, all figures are from internal records.
- **Converted hotels:** Some Covered Hotels converted from other brands with existing customer bases.
- **Average hotel age:** 10.8 years. New hotels may take years to reach system averages.
