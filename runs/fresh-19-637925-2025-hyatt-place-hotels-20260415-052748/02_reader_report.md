10. **No litigation, no bankruptcy** disclosed.

12. **Noncompete:** No post-term noncompete. During term, neither franchisee nor management company may be a Competing Brand Owner.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Area of Protection (AOP):** Boundaries agreed pre-signing, defined by geographic references or radius. Urban: few blocks. Suburban: up to ~3-mile radius.
- **AOP Term:** Typically 3 years after Hotel opening date. Begins on FA effective date.
- **During AOP Term:** Franchisor will not open/authorize another Brand Hotel in AOP, except: if franchisor acquires a group of 4+ hotels with one or more in the AOP, those can be converted to Brand Hotels.
- **After AOP Term:** NO territorial protection whatsoever. Franchisor and affiliates may open competing Brand Hotels and other Hyatt Network Hotels in the former AOP.
- **Existing Brand Hotels:** May receive NO Area of Protection at all.
- **Nonexclusive rights:** All rights nonexclusive in all respects outside AOP Term.
- **Channel competition:** No restrictions on where franchisor/other franchisees solicit customers. Internet/catalog/telemarketing all permitted.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term:** 20 years from first day of month Hotel opens.
- **Successor franchise:** 10 years on then-current terms (which may be **materially different** including fees).
- **Renewal conditions:** Substantially complied during term, currently complying, meet current standards for new franchisees, passing QA scores last 3 years, possess Hotel for 10+ more years, renovate/remodel, pay PIP fee, sign general release (if state law allows).
- **Materially different terms risk:** Explicitly stated that successor agreement terms may be materially different.

### Transfer
- Franchisor approval required for all transfers of FA, Hotel assets, or Controlling Ownership Interests.
- Non-Controlling Ownership Interest transfers allowed without approval (conditions: no Competing Brand Owner, no sanctioned persons, no creation of Controlling Ownership Interest, notify within 30 days).
- Transfer fee: current change of ownership application fee ($150,000), refundable less $7,500 if disapproved.
- **Right of first offer:** Only for strategic markets, requires signing Exhibit C.

### Termination
- **By franchisee:** May not terminate FA except as law allows. Can terminate Central Services Agreement on 10-day notice for material breach.
- **By franchisor without cause:** Not permitted for FA. Can terminate ancillary agreements with 30-90 days' notice.
- **Curable defaults:** 24 hours (health/safety threat), 10 days (failure to provide documents or pay), 30 days (other defaults), 60 days (Guarantor Monetary Threshold).
- **Non-curable defaults:** Bankruptcy, failure to discharge $100K+ judgments, ceasing operations, unauthorized transfer, felony conviction, false books, unauthorized use of Confidential Information, repeated defaults.
- **Liquidated damages:** Lesser of 36 months or remaining term × (AMR × 5% royalty + AMR × 3.5% system services). 150% multiplier for Consequential Termination (transfer to Competitor or 3+ defaults).
- **De-identification penalty:** $5,000/day for failure to remove Proprietary Marks after termination.

### Death/Disability
- Representative must transfer to approved transferee within 6 months.

### Noncompete
- **During term:** No formal covenant not to compete, but franchisee and management company may not be Competing Brand Owners.
- **Post-term:** NO noncompete covenant. Explicitly stated as "Not applicable."

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration:** Mandatory for all disputes, within 10 miles of franchisor's principal business address (currently Chicago, IL).
- **Choice of forum:** Subject to arbitration, litigation in Illinois.
- **Choice of law:** Illinois law (subject to Federal Arbitration Act, federal law, and state-specific overrides).

### Unresolveds
1. **Territory expiration risk (HIGH):** AOP Term expires 3 years after opening. After that, zero encroachment protection against other Hyatt Place hotels or other Hyatt Network Hotels. For a $18M-$52M investment, this is a significant risk.
2. **Materially different renewal terms (HIGH):** Successor franchise terms explicitly may be "materially different" including fees. No cap on how different.
3. **Unlimited System Standards changes (MEDIUM):** System Standards (3,614 pages) can be modified unilaterally. No limit on frequency or cost of required Technology System upgrades.
4. **Goodwill impairment (MEDIUM):** $110M goodwill impairment in 2024 on management/franchising reporting unit. Signals potential concerns about long-term value of the segment.
5. **Real estate gains inflate income (MEDIUM):** $1,245M in gains on real estate sales in 2024 accounts for most of the reported net income. Stripping out these gains and impairments, underlying operating income is substantially lower.
6. **Hyatt Group cost allocation opaqueness (MEDIUM):** System Services Costs allocated in "good faith" but franchisor has discretion on methodology. No independent audit of cost allocations required.
7. **F&B Operations Plan approval (LOW):** Franchisor must approve restaurant concepts and F&B operators, adding operational complexity and potential delays.
8. **Competing Brand Owner definition scope (LOW):** Definition of "Competing Brand" is broad (4+ hotels, 60% of Brand Hotel ADR), potentially limiting management company choices.

### Contradictions
1. **Item 18/19 page numbering:** TOC lists Item 18 at page 77 and Item 19 also at page 77. In practice, Item 18 is a single sentence ("No public figures") followed immediately by Item 19 on the same page. Not a material contradiction, but notable.
2. **Loyalty program economics:** Item 8 states franchisees received $68.88M more in WOH reimbursements than paid in fees, suggesting net benefit. But Item 6's 4% WOH assessment on eligible revenue is a significant ongoing cost. The net benefit likely varies dramatically by hotel.
