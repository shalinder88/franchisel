## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

The territory structure is aggressively franchisor-favorable. **No exclusive or non-exclusive territory is granted.** The franchisor explicitly retains rights to open company-owned or franchised JJ restaurants anywhere (except at the specific premises), establish competing brands, sell through any distribution channel (including Internet and national retail), and allow affiliated brands (Arby's, Dunkin', etc.) to compete freely. The delivery area is set by the franchisor and can be reduced at any time for any reason, with no compensation for lost sales.

The Development Agreement provides limited protection: a "non-exclusive" territory within which the developer has priority, but franchisor retains rights to alternative distribution channels and may terminate if the development schedule is not met.

This means a franchisee invests $366K–$733K with no protection against cannibalizing competition from the franchisor or its large network of affiliated brands (totaling 40,000+ restaurants under Inspire/Roark umbrella).

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- Initial term: 10 years from restaurant opening date
- One successor franchise for shorter of 10 years or lease term
- Renewal conditions: full compliance, timely notice, maintain/find premises, remodel to current standards (regardless of cost), pay $10,000 successor fee, satisfy training requirements, sign then-current FA and release
- **Then-current FA terms may be materially different** from existing terms

### Termination
- By franchisee: Only if franchisor breaches and doesn't cure after notice
- By franchisor without cause: Only during first 30 days of FA
- By franchisor with cause: Multiple curable and non-curable defaults
- Curable defaults: 24 hours (delivery stoppage), 3 days (health/safety), 10 days (monetary to franchisor, insurance), 30 days (operational, third-party monetary, other)
- Non-curable defaults: failure to secure site (12 months), failure to open (16 months), unapproved opening, training failure, abandonment, loss of premises, unapproved transfer, material misrepresentation, felony conviction, dishonest conduct, unauthorized confidential info disclosure, tax payment failure, gross sales understatement, repeated defaults (even if cured), assignment for creditors' benefit, anti-terrorism violation, cross-default (termination of any other FA)
- Cross-termination via Multi-Brand Addendum if Other Franchise Agreement terminated/expired

### Transfer
- No transfer until after restaurant opens
- No transfer without prior written consent
- Comprehensive conditions: transferee qualifications, all amounts current, no defaults in 60-day window, transferee not in competitive business, training completed, transferee acquires site rights, sign then-current FA, correct deficiencies, transfer fee paid, subordinate seller financing, de-identify, sign release
- Franchisor right of first refusal to match any offer
- Development Agreement rights not assignable

### Death or Disability
- Assignment to approved party within 9 months
- Franchisor may manage restaurant if no qualified manager

### Post-Termination
- De-identification, assign telephone numbers, return confidential information
- Franchisor may control de-identification process
- Liquidated damages may apply
- Franchisor option to purchase restaurant assets at fair market value and/or sublease premises

### Noncompete
- During term: No ownership or services in competitive business (>50% non-beverage revenue from submarine/deli/wrapped sandwiches) anywhere
- Post-term (2 years): Same restriction at premises, within 3 miles of premises, within 3 miles of any JJ restaurant existing as of FA signing, within 1 mile of any JJ restaurant existing or under construction at termination/expiration

### Dispute Resolution
- No arbitration or mediation
- Litigation in courts closest to franchisor's then-current principal address (currently Atlanta, GA)
- Georgia law governs
- Subject to state franchise law overrides
