## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System identity**: Jimmy John's Franchisor SPV, LLC is a special-purpose securitization vehicle formed in Delaware in June 2017 to hold all U.S. franchise agreements, trademarks, and related intellectual property for the Jimmy John's gourmet sandwich restaurant system. The Company has never directly operated a restaurant.

2. **Ownership**: The Company is an indirect wholly-owned subsidiary of Inspire Brands, Inc., one of the world's largest multi-brand restaurant platforms, which is in turn controlled by private equity funds managed by Roark Capital Management, LLC. Inspire encompasses Arby's, Buffalo Wild Wings, Dunkin', Baskin-Robbins, Sonic, Subway, and Dave's Hot Chicken, among others — collectively over 32,000 U.S. restaurants.

3. **System size**: 2,777 Jimmy John's restaurants in the United States as of December 28, 2025 (2,737 franchised, 40 company-owned), plus 37 international units. The system includes 38 multi-brand locations, primarily co-located with Dunkin' restaurants.

4. **Growth trajectory**: The system is accelerating growth — net +88 restaurants in fiscal 2025 (up from +45 in 2024 and +7 in 2023). This was driven by 123 new franchised openings in 2025, zero terminations, and only 13 non-renewals. The pipeline includes 75 signed franchise agreements not yet open plus 87 projected new franchised and 3 projected new company-owned units.

5. **Investment range**: A traditional Jimmy John's restaurant requires a total initial investment of $366,200 to $733,500, including real estate lease costs. A non-traditional location ranges from $206,200 to $686,000. The wide range is driven primarily by leasehold improvements ($115K–$310K for traditional) and FF&E including POS ($120K–$215K).

6. **Fee burden**: The total ongoing fee load is significant — 6% royalty + 4.5% advertising/development fund + up to 2% cooperative advertising + 0.5% local marketing = a potential 13% of gross sales before accounting for product purchase requirements from designated/exclusive suppliers. However, numerous incentive programs can materially reduce the effective royalty and fund contribution in the early years (e.g., Standard Incentive provides a $125,000 royalty credit; Strategic Incentive provides $175,000).

7. **Item 19 performance**: The average AUV for 2,581 qualifying franchised restaurants was $1,007,437 with a median of $955,639 in fiscal 2025. Drive-thru locations averaged $1,107,798 (17% premium over non-drive-thru at $943,539). The bottom quartile averaged $608,313 — representing significant downside risk with the full fee load. No cost, expense, or profitability data is disclosed.

8. **Bottom-quartile risk**: At the bottom-quartile median of $632,334, the 6% royalty alone is $37,940, the 4.5% fund is $28,455, and local marketing is $3,162 — totaling $69,557 in franchisor fees before any operating costs. At this revenue level, operating profitability is highly uncertain.

9. **Financial health**: The Company reported net income of $103.1 million in fiscal 2025 on revenue of $159.7 million, with operating cash flow of $131.3 million. KPMG issued an unqualified audit opinion with no going-concern language. However, distributions to the parent member ($134.4 million) exceeded net income, drawing down member's equity from $755.2 million to $723.9 million. The parent entity (JJ Issuer) carries $1.037 billion in securitization notes.

10. **Territory**: No exclusive or non-exclusive territory is granted — one of the most aggressive territory provisions among major franchise systems. The franchisor retains unrestricted rights to open competing outlets anywhere except at the franchisee's specific premises.

11. **Noncompete**: Post-term noncompete is 2 years within 3 miles of premises and any JJ restaurant existing at FA signing, plus 1 mile of any JJ restaurant at termination. The competitive business definition (>50% non-beverage revenue from submarine/deli sandwiches) is narrowly defined.

12. **Dispute resolution**: Litigation only (no arbitration), in courts closest to the franchisor's then-current principal business address (currently Atlanta, Georgia). Georgia law governs. This is identified as a special risk in the FDD.

13. **Renewal risk**: Successor franchise is available on "then-current terms, which may be materially different." This gives the franchisor significant leverage at renewal.

14. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Georgia).

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Jimmy John's Franchisor SPV, LLC was created as a special-purpose vehicle to facilitate a securitization transaction in July 2017. In this transaction, all existing U.S. franchise agreements and related intellectual property were transferred from Jimmy John's Franchise, LLC (JJF, the predecessor franchisor since 1993) to the SPV. JJF continues to provide day-to-day franchisee support under a management agreement, for which it receives management fees ($31.5 million in 2025).

The franchisor is part of a deeply layered corporate structure: SPV → Jimmy John's Funding, LLC (JJ Issuer) → Jimmy John's SPV Guarantor, LLC → JJBC, LLC → Jimmy John's LLC → Jimmy John's Holding Company LLC → IRB Holding Corp → Inspire Brands, Inc. The securitization structure means that the franchise royalties and fees flow through this chain and serve as collateral for $1.037 billion in securitization notes.

### Fee Stack

The Jimmy John's fee structure is built around a core 6% royalty on gross sales. This is a standard rate for the QSR sandwich segment. However, the total fee load is significantly higher when advertising and marketing contributions are included:

**Ongoing fees (standard rate):**
- Royalty: 6% of weekly gross sales
- Advertising & Development Fund: 4.5% of weekly gross sales (2.25% for non-traditional)
- Cooperative Advertising: up to 2% (not currently separately required)
- Local Marketing: 0.5% of weekly gross sales (mandatory)
- **Total: 11.0–13.0% of gross sales**

**Transfer fees** are tiered: $12,500 for a controlling interest transfer to a non-existing franchisee, $7,500 to an existing franchisee with 1+ year of operations, $2,500 to an immediate family member, and $0 for transfer to a controlled entity. Non-controlling interest transfers are $1,000.

**Liquidated damages** upon termination with cause: the lesser of 36 months or the remaining term, multiplied by the average monthly royalty over the prior 12 months. For a restaurant generating $1 million in annual gross sales (close to the system average), the average monthly royalty is $5,000, making the maximum liquidated damages $180,000.

**Incentive programs** are extensive and can materially reduce the effective fee burden in the early years:
- Standard Incentive (Standard Markets): $125,000 royalty credit + reduced fund rates (2%/3%/4.5% graduated)
- Strategic Incentive (Strategic Markets): $175,000 royalty credit + reduced fund rates (1%/2%/4.5% graduated)
- Early Opening Incentive: 0% royalty until required opening date (up to 6 months)
- VetFran Program: $10,000 royalty credit per restaurant (up to $100,000 total)
- Pioneer Incentive (AK, HI, ME, NH, RI, VT): $50,000 per unit for first 2 restaurants
- Drive-Thru Remodel/Relocation: Graduated 2%/3%/4%/6% schedule
- Multi-Brand Incentives: $42,500–$67,500 credits plus graduated royalty schedules for co-location with Dunkin'

### Initial Investment

The total estimated initial investment for a traditional Jimmy John's restaurant is $366,200 to $733,500. The largest variable cost components are:
- Leasehold improvements: $115,000 to $310,000 (the wide range reflects whether the landlord bears improvement costs and whether the location includes a drive-thru)
- FF&E including POS: $120,000 to $215,000
- Additional working capital (3 months): $50,000 to $75,000

For a non-traditional location (military base, university, airport, food court), the range is $206,200 to $686,000, with leasehold improvements as low as $31,500.

No financing is offered by the franchisor. The initial franchise fee ($35,000 for traditional; $17,500 for non-traditional) is fully earned when paid and non-refundable.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

The franchisor exercises virtually complete control over the franchisee's supply chain. Trade Secret Food Products (proprietary bread dough, meats, and other items) and Branded Products (items branded exclusively for the JJ system) must be purchased exclusively from the franchisor, designated affiliates, or specified exclusive sources. This covers the core menu ingredients.

Beyond proprietary products, the POS system must be purchased from the designated supplier (Signature Systems, Inc.), the online ordering system from the designated supplier (Olo), and the franchisor may designate suppliers for equipment, insurance brokerage, video security, and general contractors.

The franchisor and affiliates have the right to receive payments from suppliers (supplier rebates) and to use those amounts without restriction for any purposes they deem appropriate. This is a common but significant provision — it means the franchisor profits from the franchisee's supply chain without direct disclosure of the amounts.

No franchisee may become a supplier to the system. Officers of the franchisor have an indirect interest in Checkmate.com, Inc. (a technology supplier).

### Training Program

The training program is comprehensive and demanding:
- Initial training: 3–4 weeks at a designated facility or operating restaurant
- 4-week program plus apprenticeship for Operations Partner and certified managers
- Homework assignments and proficiency examinations at each stage
- First 2 trainees at no cost; additional trainees at $1,000/week
- Expelled trainees must wait 6 months and pay $4,000 for re-admission

Ongoing training obligations include up to 2 courses per year (max 10 business days) and an annual franchisee meeting (up to 4 days). The franchisor also offers an optional Regional Certified Training Store program (3–6 week train-the-trainer program).

Shift coverage requirements are strict: minimum 10 certified-manager-covered full shifts per week (14 for the first 12 months of a first restaurant). The Operations Partner must work at least 5 half-shifts per week.

### Technology Stack

The required technology stack includes:
- Back-office Windows computer: $800–$2,000
- Signature Systems POS: $25,000–$55,000 upfront + $250–$500/month
- Olo online ordering: $150 setup + $85/month
- Loyalty program equipment: $800–$1,200 + $100/month
- Network cabling to specifications

The franchisor has independent, unlimited access to all data generated by these systems. The franchisor may license proprietary software and charge upfront and ongoing fees.

### Advertising Fund

The Fund collects 4.5% of system gross sales. In fiscal 2025, spending was allocated: 50% media placement (TV, digital, social), 22% production, 28% administrative (including marketing team salaries, travel, and technology costs). The Fund is not a trust and the franchisor has no fiduciary obligation. An unaudited annual statement is available on written request. The Fund may borrow, invest surplus, and cover administrative overhead including salaries of personnel working on Fund business.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### No Protected Territory

This is one of the most aggressive territory provisions among major franchise systems. The FDD explicitly states: "You will not receive an exclusive territory. You also do not have any type of non-exclusive territory." The franchisor and its affiliates retain the right to:

1. Establish and operate, or allow others to operate, JJ restaurants at any location other than the franchisee's specific premises
2. Establish other businesses under the same or different marks, selling identical/similar/competitive products, through any channel including the Internet
3. Engage in all activities not expressly prohibited by the Franchise Agreement

The delivery area is set by the franchisor after the franchisee secures a site and "may at any time and for any or no reason change the delivery area, including reducing its size." If the delivery area is reduced, there is no compensation for lost sales, and the franchisee must "immediately change delivery practices."

### Affiliated Brand Competition

Through the Inspire Brands platform, franchisees face potential competition not only from other JJ locations but from 32,000+ affiliated restaurants including Subway (18,773 U.S. units — a direct sandwich competitor), Arby's, and others. The FDD states that all affiliated brands "may solicit and accept orders from customers near your business" and the franchisor has "no obligation to resolve any perceived conflicts."

### Development Agreement

The Development Agreement provides limited protection: a non-exclusive territory within which the developer has priority to develop a minimum of 3 restaurants. However, even this territory does not restrict the franchisor's alternative distribution channels and the franchisor may terminate the Development Agreement if the development schedule is not met.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The initial franchise term is 10 years from the restaurant's opening date. One successor franchise is available for the shorter of 10 years or the remaining lease term, on the franchisor's then-current terms, "which may be materially different." Renewal conditions include full compliance with all agreements, remodeling the restaurant to current brand standards "regardless of cost," paying a $10,000 successor franchise fee, completing any required training, and signing a general release (where state law permits).

The "materially different" renewal risk is significant. The franchisor could, at renewal, impose higher royalty rates, new technology requirements, costly remodel obligations, or other terms that fundamentally change the economics of the franchise.

### Termination

The franchisor may terminate the franchise for an extensive list of defaults. Notable non-curable defaults include:
- Failure to secure a site within 12 months
- Failure to open within 16 months
- Abandonment
- Material misrepresentation
- Felony conviction
- **Repeated defaults even if cured** — this means the franchisor can accumulate a history of cured defaults as grounds for termination
- **Cross-default**: if any other franchise agreement between the franchisor and franchisee (or affiliate) is terminated by the franchisor or the franchisor delivers a notice of default under any such agreement

The cross-default provision is particularly aggressive for multi-unit operators. A default on one unit could theoretically cascade to all units.

Cure periods are short: 24 hours for delivery service interruption, 3 days for health/safety violations, 10 days for monetary defaults, and 30 days for operational defaults.

### Transfer

No transfer is permitted until the restaurant opens. All transfers require prior written consent, and the franchisor retains a right of first refusal to match any offer. Development Agreement rights are not assignable at all.

Transfer conditions are comprehensive: transferee must meet all qualifications, all amounts must be current, no defaults in the 60-day window before and during transfer, transferee must not be in a competitive business, training must be completed, the transferee must acquire site rights, and the franchisee and all owners must sign a general release.

### Noncompete

During the term, the franchisee may not own or perform services for any competitive business (defined as a business deriving more than 50% of its non-beverage revenue from submarine, hero-type, deli-style, wrapped or rolled sandwiches) anywhere.

Post-term, the same restriction applies for 2 years at the restaurant premises, within 3 miles of the premises, within 3 miles of any JJ restaurant existing as of the date the franchisee signed the FA, or within 1 mile of any JJ restaurant existing or under construction as of the date the FA expires or is terminated. Given the system's 2,777 U.S. locations, this effectively precludes opening a competing sandwich shop in most major markets for 2 years.

### Dispute Resolution

Disputes must be resolved by litigation (no arbitration or mediation) in courts closest to the franchisor's then-current principal business address, which is currently Atlanta, Georgia. Georgia law governs. This is identified as a special risk in the FDD. State franchise law overrides may apply.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

The FDD provides historical AUV (Annual Unit Volume) data for 2,581 franchised Jimmy John's restaurants that operated for the full fiscal year 2025. The population excludes 123 restaurants opened during 2025, 12 closed for extended periods, 38 multi-brand locations, 33 that closed during the year, and all company-owned units.

### System-wide AUV

The overall average AUV was $1,007,437 with a median of $955,639. The highest-performing restaurant generated $3,046,579 while the lowest reported $159,965. Exactly 48.0% of restaurants (1,241 of 2,581) were at or above the average — the below-50% figure indicates a right-skewed distribution where high performers pull the average above the median.

### Quartile Distribution

The quartile analysis reveals significant performance dispersion:
- **1st Quartile** (top 645): Average $1,490,247, median $1,411,318 — only 35% above their quartile average
- **2nd Quartile** (645): Average $1,081,405, median $1,076,504 — 47.5% above
- **3rd Quartile** (645): Average $850,650, median $848,939 — 49.1% above
- **4th Quartile** (bottom 646): Average $608,313, median $632,334 — 60.5% above (inverted skew)

The gap between the 1st and 4th quartile average is $881,934 — nearly 2.5x. This is substantial and means the investment's risk profile varies enormously by location.

### Drive-Thru Premium

Drive-thru restaurants (972 units, 37.7% of the sample) averaged $1,107,798 — a 17.4% premium over non-drive-thru traditional locations ($943,539 average). This supports the franchisor's various drive-thru incentive programs and suggests that new franchisees should strongly consider drive-thru locations despite the higher initial investment.

### Critical Caveats

The FPR discloses only gross sales (AUV). No cost-of-sales, operating expenses, labor costs, or profitability metrics are provided. The FDD explicitly states: "These financial performance representations do not reflect the costs of sales, operating expenses or other costs or expenses that must be deducted from the AUV figures to obtain your net income or profit."

The data is not independently audited. AUVs are obtained from franchisee sales reports and POS system data. The franchisor offers written substantiation upon reasonable request.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### Multi-Year Trajectory

The Jimmy John's system has demonstrated accelerating growth over the three reported fiscal years:

| Year | Franchised Start | Franchised End | Net Change | Openings | Terminations | Non-Renewals | Ceased-Other |
|------|-----------------|----------------|------------|----------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| 2023 | 2,597 | 2,604 | +7 | 58 | 1 | 12 | 40 |
| 2024 | 2,604 | 2,647 | +43 | 88 | 1 | 13 | 29 |
| 2025 | 2,647 | 2,737 | +90 | 123 | 0 | 13 | 20 |

Several notable trends emerge:
- **Openings more than doubled** from 58 to 123 over three years, reflecting the robust incentive programs and Inspire Brands' development push
- **Terminations dropped to zero** in 2025 — extremely unusual for a system of this size and suggesting a very supportive approach to struggling franchisees
- **Non-renewals remain stable** at 12–13 per year, a modest level for 2,700+ units
- **"Ceased operations – other" declined** from 40 to 20, indicating fewer voluntary closures

Company-owned outlets declined slightly from 42 to 40 in 2025, with 2 closures (1 in Illinois, 1 in Michigan). Company-owned units are concentrated in Georgia (2), Illinois (8), Indiana (5), Michigan (18), and Wisconsin (7).

### Transfer Activity

Transfers declined from 205 in 2023 to 170 in 2024 to 124 in 2025. This downward trend could reflect improving unit economics (fewer owners wanting to exit), tightening transfer requirements, or a shift in market conditions. The largest transfer states in 2025 were Colorado (25), Georgia (23), Illinois (15), and Texas (9).

### Projected Pipeline

The pipeline is robust: 75 signed franchise agreements not yet open, 87 projected new franchised units, and 3 projected new company-owned units. This 165-unit pipeline represents approximately 6% of the existing system — a healthy development ratio.

### Confidentiality Clause (Gag Clause)

The FDD explicitly states: "During the last 3 fiscal years, no current or former franchisees have signed confidentiality clauses restricting them from discussing with you their experiences as a franchisee in the JIMMY JOHN'S® franchise system." **Gag clause flag: FALSE.** This is a positive indicator — prospective franchisees can freely contact current and former franchisees for candid feedback.

### Franchisee Advisory Council

A Franchisee Advisory Council exists, located at the franchisor's principal business address. Contact through Tim Asire, VP – Franchise Health. No other trademark-specific franchisee organizations are associated with the system.

## H. Item 21 — Financial Statements

### Auditor and Opinion

The financial statements were audited by KPMG LLP (Atlanta, Georgia), one of the Big Four accounting firms. The audit report, dated March 19, 2026, provides an **unqualified (clean) opinion** on the financial statements for all three fiscal years. There is **no going-concern language** in the auditor's report.

### Balance Sheet Analysis (December 28, 2025)

The Company's balance sheet is dominated by intangible assets ($724.0 million of $751.6 million total assets, or 96.3%). This reflects the securitization vehicle structure — the Company's primary assets are the trademark/trade name and franchise agreement rights transferred during the 2017 securitization.

**Intangible asset composition:**
- Trademark/trade name: $450.0 million gross, less $180.0 million in accumulated impairment charges = $270.0 million. This is an indefinite-lived asset tested annually for impairment using the relief-from-royalty method. No impairment was recorded in 2023–2025.
- Franchise agreements: $430.0 million gross, less $156.0 million accumulated amortization = $274.0 million. These are amortized on a straight-line basis over a 17-year weighted average useful life, producing $25.3 million in annual amortization expense.

The Company carries very little debt on its own balance sheet — total liabilities are only $27.8 million, primarily consisting of $24.6 million in deferred franchise fees (current and noncurrent combined). However, the parent entity (JJ Issuer) carries $1.037 billion in securitization notes secured by substantially all assets of the securitization entities including the Company.

Restricted cash ($20.9 million) is held to meet securitization reserve requirements. Accounts receivable ($6.3 million) represent amounts due from franchisees.

### Income Statement Analysis

Revenue has grown steadily: $152.9 million (2023) → $153.7 million (2024) → $159.7 million (2025), a 4.5% increase over two years. Revenue consists primarily of royalty fees (6% of franchisee gross sales) and franchise fees recognized over the contractual term.

Operating expenses are remarkably simple for a company of this size, consisting almost entirely of:
- Management fee to affiliate (JJF): $31.5 million — approximately 19.7% of revenue
- Amortization: $25.3 million — a non-cash charge related to franchise agreement intangibles
- Other operating loss: $0.6 million

This structure reflects the securitization vehicle design — the Company has no employees, no real property, and no direct operations. All operational activities are performed by JJF under the management agreement.

Income from operations was $102.4 million (2025), representing a 64.1% operating margin. Net income was $103.1 million after $0.7 million in interest income. This is not a realistic representation of system profitability, as the $1.037 billion in securitization debt service is borne at the parent level.

### Cash Flow Analysis

Operating cash flow was $131.3 million in 2025, driven by net income of $103.1 million plus $25.3 million in non-cash amortization, $0.7 million in credit loss provision, and $5.5 million in working capital movements (primarily growth in deferred revenue from new franchise fees).

All cash flow is distributed to the member (parent): $134.4 million in 2025, which exceeded net income by $31.4 million. This pattern of distributions exceeding net income is consistent across all three years (distributions of $125.7 million, $113.5 million, and $134.4 million vs. net income of $98.1 million, $97.6 million, and $103.1 million). The Company is being systematically de-capitalized, with member's equity declining from $798.8 million at January 1, 2023 to $723.9 million at December 28, 2025.

### Securitization Notes

The parent entity (JJ Issuer) has $1.037 billion in outstanding securitization notes:
- 2017-1 Class A-2-II: $288.8 million (anticipated repayment July 2027)
- 2022-1 Class A-2-I: $144.8 million (anticipated repayment April 2027)
- 2022-1 Class A-2-II: $434.3 million (anticipated repayment April 2029)
- 2022-1 Class A-2-III: $168.9 million (anticipated repayment April 2032)

Legal final maturities extend to 2047 (2017 notes) and 2052 (2022 notes). If not repaid by anticipated dates, additional interest accrues. The $50 million variable funding facility has no outstanding borrowings but $11.9 million in letters of credit.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Asset-light, cash-generative model**: The Company generates over $130 million in operating cash flow annually from a base of franchise agreements and trademarks.
2. **Distributions exceed earnings**: The systematic de-capitalization suggests the securitization structure prioritizes cash extraction. Member's equity has declined by $75 million over three years.
3. **Heavy reliance on intangibles**: 96% of total assets are intangible. If the trademark or franchise agreements were impaired (e.g., due to brand damage or system contraction), the Company's equity would be directly affected.
4. **Securitization debt overhang**: While not on the Company's balance sheet, the $1.037 billion in parent-level debt is secured by the Company's assets and could affect franchisees if the securitization defaults or is restructured.
5. **Management fee dependence**: 19.7% of revenue flows to JJF as a management fee. The FDD notes that "results of operations of the Company may not be indicative of those that would have been achieved had the Company operated on a stand-alone basis."
