The system consists solely of 3 company-owned alternative holistic wellness centers in Illinois, operated by the predecessor/affiliate Aum-Bio, Inc. (formed July 2, 2003). AumBio Franchising, LLC has been offering franchises since October 17, 2023, but has not yet successfully launched any franchised location.

2. **Entity structure:** AumBio Franchising, LLC is an Illinois LLC formed August 28, 2023. It has no parent company. Its sole predecessor/affiliate, Aum-Bio, Inc., owns the "AumBio" trademark and licenses it to the franchisor under a 25-year license agreement. The franchisor does not itself operate any wellness centers.

3. **Ownership:** Kamil Czubacki is the sole disclosed principal, serving as President since August 2023. He is also President of Aum-Bio, Inc. since 2010 and has 16 years of industry experience and 35 years with the affiliate. No other officers, directors, or key employees are disclosed in Item 2, indicating an extremely thin management team for a franchising operation.

4. **Investment range:** $159,320 to $267,620 for a single unit; $234,320 to $342,620 for a 3-unit multi-unit development agreement. The initial franchise fee is $50,000.

5. **Fee burden:** 6% royalty on gross revenues + 1% Brand Fund contribution + $100/month technology fee + minimum $1,500/month local marketing expenditure. The effective ongoing fee load is approximately 7% of revenue plus $1,600/month in fixed mandatory spending, before any event-driven fees.

6. **Item 19:** No financial performance representation is provided. The franchisor makes no earnings claims whatsoever.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk — Financial condition:** This franchisor has **zero revenue**, total assets of $0 (as of December 31, 2024), a bank overdraft of $9, cumulative net losses of $109, and members' equity in deficit at ($9). The Illinois Attorney General has imposed a fee deferral requirement due to the franchisor's financial condition. The FDD's own Special Risks section warns that the "Franchisor's financial condition as reflected in its financial statements calls into question the Franchisor's financial ability to provide services and support to you." This is a pre-revenue, pre-franchisee entity with essentially no financial resources.

8. **Financial health:** The franchisor entity is technically insolvent (bank overdraft exceeds assets). It was capitalized with a single $100 member contribution in 2023. No additional capital has been contributed. There is no evidence of credit facilities, lines of credit, or external financing.

11. **Renewal:** 10-year initial term with two 5-year renewals. Renewal requires signing the then-current franchise agreement, which may be "materially different" — a significant risk for franchisees who have built businesses under original terms.

12. **Dispute resolution:** Mandatory mediation and arbitration in Illinois under JAMS rules. Illinois law governs. This creates significant venue burden for out-of-state franchisees (flagged as a Special Risk).

13. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution, short operating history (development-stage company), and questionable financial condition. Virginia's addendum adds the explicit warning: "THIS IS A DEVELOPMENT STAGE COMPANY WHICH ENTAILS ADDITIONAL RISK OF FINANCIAL LOSS."

This is directly relevant to the core business model (alternative holistic wellness) and suggests regulatory risk around health claims associated with the THERA WELLNESS device.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

AumBio Franchising, LLC was formed in Illinois on August 28, 2023, solely for the purpose of franchising AumBio alternative holistic wellness centers. It does not operate any centers itself. The system concept involves providing "client wellness consultations and therapies" using proprietary equipment (THERA WELLNESS therapy devices and Oberon consultation devices) in locations of 700-2,000 square feet within shopping centers, business parks, and medical plazas.

The franchisor derives its right to the "AumBio" mark from a Trademark/Servicemark License Agreement with Aum-Bio, Inc. dated September 5, 2023. This creates a structural dependency: if the license is terminated (for material breach, cessation of franchising, or insolvency), franchisees have only 90 days to rebrand.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Initial Franchise Fee: $50,000 (uniform, nonrefundable, fully earned on signing)
- Multi-Unit Development Fee: $50,000 (1 unit), $90,000 (2 units), $125,000 (3 units)
- Illinois fee deferral: Fees deferred until franchisor meets initial obligations and franchisee opens for business — imposed by IL Attorney General

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Royalty: 6% of Gross Revenues, monthly by the 5th
- Brand Fund: 1% of Gross Revenue, monthly by the 5th
- Technology Fee: $100/month
- Local Marketing Minimum: $1,500/month

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Transfer Fee: 50% of then-current IFF (for transfers of >50% interest)
- Management Fee: $500/day (death/disability, subject to inflation adjustment)
- Supplier Evaluation Fee: $500
- Additional Training: $500/day/person plus per diem
- Audit Fee: Cost of audit + 18% interest on understatement (if >2%)
- Interest on Late Payments: 18% per annum
- Convention Non-Attendance: $1,000
- Lost Manual: $500
- Liquidated Damages: Royalty + Brand Fund for lesser of 2 years or remaining term
- On-Site Inspection Fee: Up to $5,000
- Prohibited Product Fine: $500/day
- Attorney's Fees: Actual fees and costs
- Indemnification: Actual attorney's fees and costs

The Gross Revenues definition is broad: all revenue whether cash or credit, including exchanges, barter, and insurance proceeds for lost profit. Only separately stated sales taxes are excluded.

### Initial Investment

The total estimated initial investment for a single unit ranges from $159,320 to $267,620. The largest single component is Furniture, Fixtures & Equipment at $63,500 to $120,000, which includes the required THERA WELLNESS therapy devices (minimum 2) and Oberon consultation device (minimum 1). The franchisor receives 10-15% on THERA WELLNESS purchases and 60% on Oberon purchases, making the equipment supply chain a significant revenue stream for the franchisor.

Other significant components include the $50,000 initial franchise fee, $5,000-$15,000 for market introduction, $6,000-$9,000 for three months' rent, and $20,000-$40,000 in additional working capital for the first three months. The estimates do not include owner's salary, royalty payments, Brand Fund contributions, or debt service.

For a 3-unit MUDA, the total investment is $234,320 to $342,620 (first unit investment plus $75,000 in additional development fees).

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

The franchisee must purchase certain equipment and supplies only from the franchisor, designated suppliers, or approved suppliers. The core required equipment is:
- Minimum 2 THERA WELLNESS therapy devices
- 1 Oberon consultation device

The THERA WELLNESS device is marketed in the U.S. as a "General Wellness Product" as defined by the FDA. It is NOT a medical device. Franchisees may NOT represent to customers or the public that they are making medical diagnoses or treating medical conditions — violation of this policy is grounds for termination of the franchise agreement.

The franchisor receives substantial markups on required equipment: 10-15% on THERA WELLNESS purchases and 60% on Oberon purchases. During fiscal year 2024, no revenue was derived from franchisee purchases (because there were no operating franchisees). The franchisor estimates that 50-70% of the initial investment (excluding IFF) and 5-20% of ongoing expenses (excluding royalties) will come from designated sources.

Franchisees may request approval of alternate suppliers. The process requires a written request, the supplier must demonstrate quality compliance, and the franchisor has 30 days to respond. A $500 evaluation fee applies. Approval may be revoked with written notice. No purchasing cooperatives exist.

### Training Program

Training totals 55 hours (10 classroom + 45 on-the-job) across three Illinois locations:
- Consultations (5 hours): Rolling Meadows Center
- Legal and Scheduling (3 hours): Chicago Center
- Operating System (7 hours): Rolling Meadows
- Device Training (40 hours): Oak Lawn Center

Additionally, franchisees must complete a mandatory virtual training program on the THERA WELLNESS device (no cost for initial training; advanced training optional at then-current cost).

Training is led by Kamil Czubacki. No tuition for up to 2 attendees; franchisee pays all travel, lodging, meals, and wages. Training must be completed at least 4 weeks before opening. Training classes are scheduled 3-6 times per year.

Additional training programs may be required: up to 4 per year, 1-2 days each, at $500/day/person. The franchisor provides 5 days of on-site assistance during the first week of operations at its own expense.

### Technology Stack

The franchisee must use all computer systems prescribed by the franchisor. The franchisor has **unlimited remote access to all information** in the computer system at any time, including sales, purchase orders, inventory, and expenditures.

Initial computer hardware/software cost: $3,000-$5,000. Maintenance up to $1,000/year. The $100/month technology fee includes the proprietary client management system license and website maintenance. Up to 3 system upgrades per year may be required. High-speed internet is required.

### Marketing Fund

The Brand Fund (1% of Gross Revenue) is administered with sole control by the franchisor. Company-owned units are NOT required to contribute. An unaudited annual statement is available on written request. During fiscal year 2024, no Brand Fund collections occurred (no franchisees). The franchisor may use the fund for local, regional, or national advertising, market research, and promotional activities. The fund is not a trust and the franchisor owes no fiduciary obligations. Expenditures need not be proportionate to geographic contributions.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory

The franchisee receives an exclusive territory determined after the approved location is identified, shown on Schedule A to the franchise agreement. The density limit is 1 center per 100,000 population. The territory definition is based on local demographics, population density, and competition.

**Territorial protections are strong relative to many franchise systems:**
- The franchisor may NOT establish competing centers within the franchisee's territory.
- The franchisor may NOT solicit or accept orders from customers inside the territory.
- The franchisor may NOT use internet, catalog, telemarketing, or other direct marketing to sell within the territory using the marks.
- Other franchisees and company outlets may serve customers who reside in the territory (walk-in trade) but may not establish centers there.

### Channel Restrictions

The franchisee may serve customers from inside or outside the territory but may not conduct direct marketing activities outside the protected territory. The franchisee cannot solicit business or provide services outside the territory (with limited exception for phone directory listings).

### Encroachment Risk

Encroachment risk is relatively low for the single-unit franchise agreement. However, under the Multi-Unit Development Agreement, the franchisor retains broader rights: (a) to sell products under different marks anywhere, (b) to operate outside the development area regardless of proximity, (c) to operate non-traditional sites within the development area, and (d) to acquire competing businesses within the development area.

### Relocation

Relocation requires prior written consent, which the franchisor may withhold in its sole discretion. All relocation costs are borne by the franchisee.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The initial franchise term is 10 years. The franchisee may renew for two additional 5-year terms if the franchisor is still franchising and the franchisee is in good standing. However, renewal requires signing the **then-current franchise agreement, which may be materially different** from the original agreement. This "materially different" risk is significant: a franchisee who has built a successful business over 10 years could face substantially changed terms at renewal.

Renewal also requires timely notice, a general release, payment of a fee, and signing all current ancillary documents.

### Transfer Provisions

Transfer requires the franchisor's prior written consent, which may not be unreasonably withheld. The transfer fee is 50% of the then-current initial franchise fee. Conditions include: transferee qualifies, all amounts paid, transferee not in competitive business, training completed, transferee signs then-current FA, release signed, guaranty signed, and non-compete agreed by the transferring franchisee.

No right of first refusal or option to purchase exists for the franchisor. On death or disability, the franchisee's representative must transfer the interest to an approved party within 6 months under the same conditions as any transfer.

### Termination and Death/Disability

The franchisee may terminate with 60 days' notice if the franchisor defaults and fails to cure. The franchisor may not terminate without cause. The franchisor may terminate with cause for numerous defaults, including non-curable defaults such as: misrepresentation, false books, abandonment, felony conviction, trademark misuse, repeated defaults (even if previously cured), fraud, bankruptcy/insolvency, or selling sound-wave equipment at wholesale/retail.

**Cross-default:** A default under one agreement with the franchisor may result in termination of all other agreements. This creates cascading risk for multi-unit developers.

**Liquidated damages:** If the franchisor terminates for default or the franchisee terminates without the right to do so, liquidated damages equal the royalty + Brand Fund contributions for the lesser of 2 years or the remaining franchise term. This is calculated as the average monthly royalty for the prior 12 months multiplied by 24 months.

### Noncompete Restrictions

**During term:** No involvement in any competing business anywhere in the United States. The noncompete applies to the franchisee, all stockholders/members/partners, the guarantor, and their respective spouses and children. This is an exceptionally broad geographic scope.

**Post-term:** For 2 years after termination/expiration/transfer, no involvement in a competing business within the territory, within any territory where an AumBio center is located at the time of signing, or within 50 miles of the franchisee's location. A 2-year non-solicitation covenant also applies to clients.

**State overrides:** Several states (North Dakota, Indiana, California) have addenda that may limit or void the post-term noncompete provisions.

### Dispute Resolution

All disputes (except for franchisor actions for monies owed, injunctive/extraordinary relief, or real estate matters) must be mediated and arbitrated in the city of the franchisor's headquarters (currently Rolling Meadows, IL) under JAMS Commercial Arbitration Rules. Illinois law governs (subject to state law overrides).

This creates significant venue burden for out-of-state franchisees, as flagged in the Special Risks section. Multiple state addenda (Minnesota, Indiana, Maryland, North Dakota) override the forum selection or governing law provisions for claims arising under their respective franchise laws.

### Litigation History

The sole disclosed litigation is the Illinois DFPR Agreed Order to Cease and Desist (Case No. 2016-3225, entered April 16, 2024) against Kamil Czubacki and Jerzy Czubacki d/b/a Aum Bio, Inc. The order requires maintaining websites free from unsupported health claims and clearly disclosing they are not medical professionals. This is directly relevant to the core business model.

**California:** Covenant not to compete extending beyond FA term might not be enforceable. Litigation venue outside CA might not be enforceable. Application of non-CA law might not be enforceable. Liquidated damages clause may be unenforceable under Civil Code §1671.

**Hawaii:** General release as condition of renewal/transfer shall exclude claims under Hawaii Franchise Investment Law. Termination upon bankruptcy may not be enforceable under federal bankruptcy law.

**Illinois:** Illinois law governs. Venue designation outside IL is void (but arbitration outside IL is permitted). Compliance waiver provisions are void. **Maryland:** General release may not exclude Maryland Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law claims. Litigation may be in Maryland for MD law claims. Claims must be brought within 3 years. Maryland law prevails in conflicts if required. Termination upon bankruptcy may not be enforceable.

**Minnesota:** Franchisor must reimburse mark defense costs. 90 days' notice of termination (60 days to cure), 180 days' notice of non-renewal. No general release of Minnesota Franchise Law claims. Litigation cannot be required outside Minnesota.

**New York:** Enhanced disclosures re: litigation, bankruptcy, regulatory orders. General release may not waive GBL Article 33 rights. Termination by franchisee on any grounds available by law.

**North Dakota:** Cancellation refund provisions per ND Century Code. No consent to termination/liquidated damages. No general release excluding ND law claims. ND statute of limitations applies. **Rhode Island:** Forum selection/venue outside RI void for RI Franchise Investment Act claims. General release void for RI FIA claims.

**Virginia:** "THIS IS A DEVELOPMENT STAGE COMPANY WHICH ENTAILS ADDITIONAL RISK OF FINANCIAL LOSS." Unlawful for franchisor to use undue influence to induce franchisee to surrender rights.

**Washington:** Initial franchise fee deferred until franchisor meets initial pre-opening obligations and franchisee opens. WA Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails in conflicts.
