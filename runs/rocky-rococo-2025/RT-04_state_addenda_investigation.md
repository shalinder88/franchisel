# RT-04: State Addenda Investigation

**Task**: Confirm whether state-specific addenda exist in the document.

## Findings

A full-text search of all 167 pages for "addend", "state specific", and "state-specific" found references on only 4 pages:

1. **Page 4** (What You Need to Know section): Boilerplate language states "you should check the State Specific Addenda. See the Table of Contents for the location of the State Specific Addenda."
2. **Pages 148–150**: The SBA Addendum to Franchise Agreement — this is a standard SBA financing addendum, not a state-specific addendum.

## Conclusion

**No state-specific addenda exist in this FDD.**

The reference on page 4 is standard FTC-mandated boilerplate language that appears in virtually all FDDs. Despite registration in 5 states (Illinois, Indiana, Michigan, Minnesota, Wisconsin), this FDD does not include state-specific addenda.

This is not necessarily an error — some smaller franchise systems, particularly those operating primarily in one state (Wisconsin in this case), may not have state-specific addenda if:
- Their franchise agreement already complies with the relationship laws of their registration states
- The state cover page (p.6) already notes that the FA's provisions are "subject to State Law" for applicable provisions (as seen in the Item 17 table)
- The Wisconsin governing law clause and AAA arbitration provisions are the primary compliance mechanisms

The franchise agreement itself contains multiple notations that certain provisions (noncompete, choice of forum, integration/merger) are "subject to State Law," which serves a similar function to formal state addenda.

**Severity**: Low to medium. The absence of formal state addenda means Illinois, Indiana, Michigan, and Minnesota franchise law protections are not explicitly surfaced in the FDD, but the "subject to State Law" qualifications throughout the Item 17 table provide partial coverage.

**Status**: Resolved — confirmed absent from document.
