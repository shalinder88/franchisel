# Reader Report — Rocky Rococo Pizza and Pasta (638785-2025)

**Franchisor**: Rococo Franchise Corporation
**Issuance Date**: April 24, 2025
**Auditor**: Vrakas, S.C. (Brookfield, WI)
**Fiscal Year End**: December 30, 2024 (52/53-week year ending last Monday in December)

---

## A. Executive Snapshot

Rocky Rococo is a regional Wisconsin-centric pizza and pasta quick-service franchise system with 31 total restaurants (24 franchised, 7 affiliate-owned) as of year-end 2024, operating primarily in Wisconsin with one unit in Minnesota. The franchisor, Rococo Franchise Corporation (RFC), was incorporated in 1995 and has offered franchises since that date. The system is growing modestly — net outlets went from 32 (2022) to 31 (2024), but franchised units grew from 18 to 24 over the same period as predecessor-franchised locations (under Rocky Rococo Corporation) were wound down from 7 to 0. The brand targets a 5-to-70 age demographic with dine-in, carry-out, and delivery service. Total investment ranges from $211,000 to $705,500 with a $25,000 initial franchise fee. The FDD is registered in IL, IN, MI, MN, and WI, all with April 24, 2025 effective dates.

RFC does not make financial performance representations (Item 19 is blank). The financial statements show a healthy and growing franchise entity — total revenues of $1,382,868 in FY2024 (up from $900,330 in FY2022), net income of $70,626 in FY2024, and $669,550 in cash. The system is family-controlled by the Hester family (Thomas R. Hester, Sharon R. Hester, Thomas R. Hester III, Amy R. Bartley) through multiple related entities.

## B. Pass 1 — Front Matter, Formats, Entry Structure, Fee Stack, Initial Investment

**Front matter**: The cover page identifies the concept as a restaurant specializing in pan-style pizza and pasta dishes. The document includes FTC-mandated consumer guidance, a "How to Use" page, and a "What You Need to Know" general franchising section. Special risks (p.5) highlight only one risk: out-of-state dispute resolution via American Arbitration Association at its office nearest to the franchisor's home office.

**Formats**: Rocky Rococo restaurants operate in enclosed malls with seating, enclosed mall food courts, strip-center malls, storefronts, free-standing buildings, and campus locations. Space requirements range from approximately 900 sq ft (enclosed mall) to 3,800 sq ft (free-standing). The FDD notes that the cost for free-standing buildings is "substantially higher" than projected and is not available from RFC.

**Entry structure**: A single-unit franchise model. Franchisees receive a specific territory around a single location. The Franchise Deposit Agreement secures the franchise while a site is found (180-day window). Multi-unit ownership exists in the system (Heyden Penkert operates 3 units; Stacey Agostino operates 2+ units; Darin Hogy/Morten Sunde operate 2 units) but no formal multi-unit development agreement is disclosed.

**Fee stack**:
- Initial franchise fee: $25,000 (first unit), $17,500 (second unit). Payable in two installments via Franchise Deposit Agreement structure.
- Royalty: 5% of gross sales weekly, with retroactive reduction to 3.5% if annual sales fall below $425,000 (52-week year) or $433,173 (53-week year).
- Marketing fund: up to 0.75% of gross sales weekly.
- Local advertising: up to 3.5% of gross sales (tiered by media type: 1.5% no electronic media, 2.5% radio, 3.5% TV). Local Ad Fund is not currently being collected.
- Transfer fee: 50% of then-current initial franchise fee.
- No renewal fee.
- Interest on late payments: maximum by law or 1.5% per month.

**Initial investment**: $211,000 to $705,500, broken down as:
- Initial franchise fee: $25,000
- Equipment/fixtures: $80,000–$300,000
- Leasehold improvements: $80,000–$325,000
- Opening inventory: $4,000–$12,000
- Security deposits: $0–$4,000
- Promotion: $5,000
- Pre-opening training: $1,000–$5,000
- Misc. (legal, permits): $1,000–$4,500
- Additional funds (3 months): $10,000–$20,000
- Insurance: $5,000

RFC estimates franchisees may need to put additional cash into the business during at least the first 3–9 months, and sometimes longer. RFC cannot estimate or promise when or whether positive cash flow or profits will be achieved. This is an owner-operated estimate and does not include salaries/benefits for employees or owner's draw.

## C. Pass 2 — Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

**Supplier control**: Minimal. RFC designates only a national soft drink supplier. Neither RFC nor its affiliates are approved suppliers or derive revenue from franchisee purchases. 100% of goods (except the designated soft drink) may be purchased from suppliers of the franchisee's choosing. RFC attempts to negotiate purchase arrangements for franchisees' benefit but derives no revenue from such negotiations. Pizza Rococo, Inc. (an affiliate) provides uniforms and miscellaneous items at no profit.

**Operating control**: Comprehensive. RFC specifies standards for food quality, taste, weight, dimensions, ingredients, hours of operation, delivery, maintenance, cleanliness, employee dress/appearance, use of marks, standard forms, identification, and advertising. The Operations Manual governs detailed operational procedures across 6 chapters (Operations, Equipment, Administration, Purchasing, Human Resources, Training). RFC reserves the right to change the system and designate additional required services. You may not operate any other business at the location without RFC's consent.

**Technology burden**: Moderate. RFC may specify computer hardware and POS systems (~$10,000 cost). During the franchise term, you may be required to upgrade hardware/software. RFC cannot estimate upgrade frequency or costs. You must establish a dedicated line for RFC to access the computer system. RFC does not currently have access to franchisee computer information (notable contradiction with the requirement for a dedicated access line). The cost of the computer system may not be fully amortizable over the remaining franchise term. You must install and use the system and transmit information to RFC.

**Reporting burden**: Standard. Franchisees must maintain full corporate records and reports, databases in the form specified by RFC. RFC has the right to audit all books and records at any time. If audit reveals gross sales deficiency exceeding 2% of reported sales, franchisee pays all audit costs. RFC reserves the right to terminate for submission of false reports or underreporting.

## D. Pass 3 — Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

**Territory structure**: Franchisees receive a 1-mile radius exclusive territory around their location. However, significant carveouts apply:
- Major regional shopping mall locations (500,000+ sq ft, 2+ department store anchors) do not receive 1-mile protection.
- A mall within a franchisee's 1-mile radius is not a violation.
- Central business district locations (in RFC's sole judgment) receive no 1-mile protection.
- Continuation of territory is not dependent on sales volume, market penetration, or any other contingency.
- Territory may not be altered except with written consent of both parties.

**Competition/encroachment**: RFC retains the right to operate and grant others the right to operate outside the territory. RFC may sell products and services under the marks or other marks through similar or dissimilar channels on any terms it deems appropriate. There are no restrictions on other franchisees soliciting customers inside your territory or on you soliciting outside your territory. The document explicitly states: "You will not receive an exclusive territory. You may face competition from other franchisees, from outlets that RFC owns, or from other channels of distribution or competitive brands that RFC controls." This creates tension with the 1-mile radius protection described earlier — the territory is protected from new same-brand units within 1 mile, but is not exclusive in any broader commercial sense.

**Channels**: RFC does not operate, franchise, or have present plans to operate a business under a different trademark selling similar goods. However, RFC has reserved the right to sell through dissimilar channels.

## E. Pass 4 — Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

**Term**: 15 years, with one 15-year renewal available if franchisee is in good standing.

**Renewal conditions**: Give notice, execute then-current franchise agreement (which may contain materially different terms), execute general release, remodel restaurant, attend and complete retraining. No renewal fee.

**Transfer**: Requires RFC approval (not unreasonably withheld). Transfer fee of 50% of then-current initial franchise fee. Conditions include: new franchisee qualifies, accounts current, training arranged, release signed, covenant not to compete signed, remodel, current agreement signed by new franchisee.

**Termination**: RFC cannot terminate without cause. Curable defaults (60 days to cure): failure to submit reports, bankruptcy, property attachment, acts impairing goodwill, failure to comply with agreements, failure to maintain insurance, etc. Non-payment of fees: 10 days to cure. Non-curable defaults: adjudged bankrupt, assignment for benefit of creditors, abandonment, criminal misconduct, material misrepresentation, loses possession, unauthorized assignment, unauthorized disclosure of confidential information, intentionally understates gross sales, repeatedly fails to comply.

**Post-termination obligations**: Complete de-identification, payment of amounts due, covenant not to compete.

**Noncompete (during term)**: No involvement in competing business anywhere. Subject to state law.

**Noncompete (post-term)**: Franchise Agreement: 1 year within 5 miles of designated area or 5 miles of any system location. Franchise Deposit Agreement: 1 year within 50 miles of designated area or 50 miles of any system location. Subject to state law.

**Dispute resolution**: Binding arbitration via American Arbitration Association at its office nearest RFC. Exception: the legality of the Wisconsin Franchise Investment Law and Wisconsin Fair Dealership Act is not subject to arbitration. Each party waives trial by jury. Claims must be brought within 1 year. Franchisee waives punitive/exemplary damages and money damages for unreasonable withholding of consent.

**Choice of law**: Wisconsin.

**Right of first refusal**: RFC can match any offer for the franchise business.

**Franchisor purchase option on termination**: RFC may elect within 60 days of termination/expiration to purchase all tangible assets at fair market value (or independent appraisal if parties disagree).

**Death/disability**: Estate must apply to continue or transfer within 6 months.

**Integration/merger**: Only terms of the Franchise Agreement and accompanying exhibits are enforceable, but nothing disclaims express representations in the FDD.

**Not directly surfaced or partially surfaced**:
- State-specific addenda: Referenced on state cover page (p.6) which says "check the State Specific Addenda" and "See the Table of Contents for the location of the State Specific Addenda." However, no state addenda are listed in the TOC and none appear as separate exhibits. This is an unresolved gap — the document references them but does not appear to include them, despite registration in 5 states.
- Item 19 detail: No financial performance data disclosed. This is by design (franchisor election) but limits diligence value.
- Item 11 advertising fund audit: The marketing fund is not audited (stated on p.28). Only unaudited statements available on request.
- Technology/digital agreements: No separate technology or digital services exhibit despite computer system requirements in Item 11.
- Insurance requirements detail: Referenced in Item 6 table and Item 7 notes but detailed insurance specifications likely reside in the Operations Manual rather than the FDD.

**Contradictions**:
- Territory: The document states franchisees receive a 1-mile radius exclusive area (p.38) but also explicitly states "You will not receive an exclusive territory" (p.39). These are reconcilable — the 1-mile protection prevents new same-brand units but is not exclusive in any broader commercial sense — but the juxtaposition creates potential confusion for franchisees.
- Computer access: Item 11 states "RFC does not have access to your computer information" (p.32) but also requires franchisees to "establish and maintain at the Franchised Business a dedicated line RFC may use to access the computer system" (p.31). The intended distinction may be between current access vs. the right to access, but the language creates tension.
- Noncompete radius: The Franchise Agreement specifies 5 miles post-term (pp.45–46), while the Franchise Deposit Agreement specifies 50 miles (pp.46, 157). This is a significant discrepancy — 10x difference in geographic scope depending on which agreement governs. The broader FDA noncompete may be unenforceable but exists as a disclosed burden.
