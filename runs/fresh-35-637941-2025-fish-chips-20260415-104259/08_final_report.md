## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: Extremely small — 5 total outlets at end of FY2024 (2 licensees in Nevada hotel/casino locations operated under IPH2 affiliate license agreements, plus 3 company-owned restaurants in New York, Orlando, and Washington D.C.). Zero actual franchise agreements signed as of the issuance date. Zero projected franchise or company openings for the next fiscal year.

2. **Entity structure**: GRNA FC Group LLC is a Delaware limited liability company formed on June 20, 2024. It is a wholly-owned subsidiary of GRNA Franchise Group LLC, which sits beneath GRNA LLC → GRNA MidCo LLC → GRNA Holdings Inc. Gordon Ramsay owns 49% of the ultimate parent, GRNA Holdings Inc.

3. **Ownership history**: The franchisor entity was funded with a $100,000 capital contribution from GRNA LLC on January 30, 2025 — less than one month before the FDD issuance date. The entity had not commenced operations as of January 31, 2025. The first Fish & Chips restaurant opened in 2017 in Las Vegas as a licensee operation, and the three company-owned locations opened in 2022, all under different affiliate entities.

4. **Investment range**: $1,503,000 to $2,410,000 for a single unit under the Franchise Agreement, which is substantial for a ~2,000 sq ft fast-casual restaurant. The investment is based on affiliate experience developing restaurants in New York, Orlando, and Washington D.C., which are high-cost metropolitan markets.

5. **Fee burden**: Total ongoing minimum of 6.5% of Restaurant Revenue (5% royalty + 0.5% brand fund + 1% local marketing), with a combined brand fund/local marketing cap of 4%. The royalty can be increased to 8% at the franchisor's sole option if the franchisee violates the Menu Item Price Policy.

6. **Item 19**: No financial performance representation made. Prospective franchisees have zero disclosed unit economics for a $1.5M+ investment.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: This franchise carries high bottom-quartile risk due to: (a) zero franchisee operating history, (b) no FPR, (c) $100K total assets on the franchisor's balance sheet, (d) aggressive liquidated damages provisions, and (e) a prototype business model untested in standalone franchise format.

8. **Financial health**: The franchisor entity has $100,000 in total assets (all cash), $100,000 in members' equity, zero liabilities, no revenue, and no operations. This is a shell entity funded solely for franchise registration purposes.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Flat to minimal. Net +1 outlet in FY2024 (one licensee opened in Reno). Zero projected openings. The system has been essentially static at 4–5 outlets for three years.

10. **Territory**: Designated Area determined by franchisor. Not an exclusive territory. Franchisor retains broad rights to operate in non-traditional venues, sell collateral products, operate other brands, and allow delivery/catering into the territory from outside locations.

11. **Renewal**: Four additional 5-year terms after 10-year initial term. Must sign then-current franchise agreement, which "may be materially different." General release required.

12. **Disputes**: Mediation then binding arbitration in Dallas, Texas. Texas law governs. All 13 state addenda override this to varying degrees.

13. **Special risks**: (1) Out-of-state dispute resolution (Texas); (2) Short operating history — franchisor at early stage of development.

14. **Litigation/bankruptcy**: None disclosed.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

GRNA FC Group LLC was formed as a Delaware LLC on June 20, 2024 for the specific purpose of franchising Fish & Chips restaurants. The entity sits within the Gordon Ramsay North America corporate structure, four levels below the ultimate parent GRNA Holdings Inc. The franchise rights derive from a sublicense chain: Gordon Ramsay (personally) → GRNA IP HoldCo LLC (exclusive license) → GRNA FC Group LLC (nonexclusive sublicense). The sublicense has a 10-year initial term with auto-renewal.

The Gordon Ramsay restaurant empire spans multiple brands and continents. Key U.S. affiliates include:
- **IPH2**: Licenses Ramsay's Kitchen, Fish & Chips, Gordon Ramsay Steak, Burger, Pub & Grill, and Food Market in U.S. hotels/casinos/resorts
- **GRNA Street Pizza Group LLC**: Street Pizza franchises (since 2025)
- **GRNA RK Group LLC**: Ramsay's Kitchen franchises (planned 2025)
- **HK Restaurant Ventures, LLC**: Hell's Kitchen licenses in U.S. hotels/casinos/resorts

The company-owned Fish & Chips restaurants are operated by separate single-purpose affiliates: GRNA FC of NY LLC (New York), GRNA ICON PARK LLC (Orlando), and GRNA FC of DC LLC (Washington D.C.).

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Initial franchise fee: $50,000 (uniform, non-refundable, due at signing)

**Restaurant Revenue Definition**: All actual receipts and revenue (including applicable taxes, service charges before any reductions), excluding taxes payable to government, gratuities, credit card fees, employee comps (3.5% annual cap), and discretionary gratuities paid to below-minimum-wage employees.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

The franchisor estimates that required purchases from approved suppliers will represent approximately **75% of the initial investment** and **90–95% of ongoing operating expenses**. This is an extremely high degree of supplier control.

Required purchases from approved suppliers include: fixtures, furniture, equipment, computer systems, decor, architect services, food products/ingredients, all beverages (fountain, bottled, alcoholic, non-alcoholic), uniforms and merchandise, advertising materials, packaging, paper products, insurance, food safety services, mystery shopper services, and all other goods/services as required.

Franchisees must use an **approved architect** for planning and design. General contractors may be selected by the franchisee subject to franchisor approval (which can be withdrawn with 48 hours' notice). Proposed leases must be submitted for franchisor consent.

**Steritech** is currently the sole approved vendor for food safety inspections ($262/quarter).

Franchisees must receive approval for any online ordering/delivery platforms, including aggregators like Grubhub, DoorDash, and Uber Eats.

**Revenue from purchases**: As of the issuance date, neither the franchisor nor any affiliate derives revenue from required purchases. However, the franchisor reserves the right to derive and retain rebates, commissions, and other consideration from supplier dealings without obligation to pass through to franchisees.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition and Carve-outs

The Franchise Agreement grants a "Designated Area" — **not an exclusive territory**. The exact size is determined by the franchisor based on: DMA, population, density, affluence, co-tenants, traffic generators, driving times, and topographical features.

The franchisor's territorial commitment is limited: it will not operate or grant another Fish & Chips restaurant in the Designated Area during the FA term, subject to full franchisee compliance and subject to the following broad reserved rights:

1. Advertise and promote the System within the Designated Area
2. Operate Fish & Chips or other brands anywhere outside the Designated Area (including adjacent locations)
3. Sell collateral products (packaged food, clothing, memorabilia) through any channel anywhere (grocery, catalogs, Internet, kiosks, food trucks, multi-brand facilities)
4. Operate any non-Fish & Chips restaurant or business inside the Designated Area
5. Operate Fish & Chips in "Reserved Areas" inside the Designated Area (airports, universities, military bases, hospitals, hotels, casinos, stadiums, arenas)
6. Deliver/cater from outside-territory restaurants to customers inside the Designated Area
7. Acquire or merge with businesses operating within the Designated Area

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights

Franchisees may not use alternative channels (satellite, temporary sites, mobile vehicles, carts, kiosks, catalogs, electronic media, social media, mobile apps, online ordering via aggregators) without prior written approval. The franchisor may require delivery, take-out, and catering services, and may restrict such services to the Designated Area — or may authorize others to deliver into the Designated Area.

Under the ADA, the Development Territory has essentially the same reserved rights and limitations.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

- **Initial term**: 10 years
- **Renewal**: Four additional 5-year terms (total potential: 30 years)
- **Renewal conditions**: Renewal requires signing the then-current franchise agreement, which **"may be materially different"** from the form attached to this FDD. This is a significant "materially different" risk. Additional conditions: 12–18 months written notice, good standing, right to possession, repair/upgrade to then-current standards, $12,500 renewal fee, qualification/training compliance, general release.

### Transfer Provisions

- Franchisor approval required (not unreasonably withheld)
- Full compliance with FA required
- Transferee must meet then-current requirements, provide business plan, agree to trained personnel
- Transferee executes then-current FA
- $12,500 transfer fee
- General release required from franchisee and all principals
- Franchisor has right of first refusal (can match any offer)
- For ADA transfers: must also transfer all FAs for all open restaurants; transfer fee = reimbursement of expenses

### Termination and Death/Disability

- **No termination by franchisee** (no exit clause)
- **No termination by franchisor without cause**
- **Termination for cause only** — cross-default between FA and ADA
- **Non-curable defaults** (immediate termination): material misrepresentation, failure to open by deadline, abandonment, felony conviction, unauthorized transfer, unauthorized use of Marks/Confidential Information, 3+ defaults in 12 months or 10+ during term (regardless of cure), termination of ADA or other FA, failure to pay taxes
- **Automatic termination**: bankruptcy, assignment for benefit of creditors, receiver/trustee appointment
- **Curable defaults** (30-day cure): failure to pay, inaccurate reporting, law compliance failure, failing Brand Audit, inspection deficiencies, lease non-compliance
- **Post-termination**: Cease operations, pay all amounts, pay liquidated damages, discontinue Marks/System, return Manuals, de-identify restaurant, assign phone/domain/email/social media, comply with noncompete
- **Franchisor purchase option**: Upon termination/expiration, right to purchase assets and/or assume lease
- **Death/disability**: 6 months to transfer controlling interest to approved third party

### Noncompete Restrictions

**During term**: No involvement with any "Competitive Business" — defined as (1) any casual dining restaurant or food service establishment that is the same as or substantially similar to Fish & Chips, including any in which British-style fish and chips accounts for 25% or more of menu items or gross sales, OR (2) any restaurant identified by or promoted in connection with a celebrity chef.

The celebrity chef clause is **unusually broad** — it effectively prohibits the franchisee from involvement with any celebrity-chef-branded restaurant during the term, regardless of cuisine type.

**Post-term**: For 2 years following expiration, termination, or transfer: no involvement in a Competitive Business within (1) the Designated Area, (2) within 3 miles of the Restaurant, or (3) within 3 miles of any other Restaurant operating or under construction.

**State overrides**: California (may be unenforceable), Indiana (modified per IC 23-2-2.7-1(9)), Minnesota (limited), Washington (salary thresholds per RCW 49.62).

### Dispute Resolution

- **Step 1**: Non-binding mediation (AAA, Dallas, Texas)
- **Step 2**: Binding arbitration (AAA, Dallas, Texas)
- **Step 3**: Litigation in state or federal courts encompassing Dallas, Texas
- **Governing law**: Texas (subject to applicable state law)
- **State overrides**: Multiple states require local venue and/or provide that state law supersedes

### Litigation History

No litigation required to be disclosed (Item 3). The franchisor has never been involved in material litigation, which is consistent with an entity that has existed for less than one year.
