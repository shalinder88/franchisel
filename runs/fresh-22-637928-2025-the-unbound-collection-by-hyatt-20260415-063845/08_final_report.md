# Final Diligence Report — The Unbound Collection by Hyatt
## Filing 637928-2025 | Issuance Date: March 26, 2025

---

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **The Unbound Collection by Hyatt** is Hyatt's luxury/upscale "soft brand" hotel affiliation, where each property retains its own unique tradename but affiliates with The Unbound Collection by Hyatt brand. Franchising since June 2016.

2. **Franchisor**: Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. (Delaware LLC), wholly owned by Hyatt Hotels Corporation (NYSE: H). Parent has $13.3B in total assets, $3.8B equity, and reported $1.3B net income in 2024 (boosted by $1.2B in real estate sale gains).

3. **System size**: 18 Brand Hotels as of December 31, 2024 — 10 franchised and 8 company-owned/managed. This is a small but steadily growing system: net +2 (2022), +3 (2023), +2 (2024). Four franchise agreements signed but not yet opened. Zero terminations, non-renewals, or closures across all three years.

4. **Investment**: $46.8M to $161.5M for a new-build 150-250 room hotel (excluding real estate). This is among the highest-investment franchise opportunities in the hospitality industry.

5. **Fee burden**: The effective all-in fee load is very high. Royalty at 7% of Gross Rooms Revenue from Channel Bookings, plus 3.5% Commercial Services Fee, plus 1.35% Digital Acquisition Fee, plus 4.5% World of Hyatt loyalty assessment on eligible WOH revenue. For a hotel where a significant portion of business comes through Hyatt channels and WOH members, the aggregate fee take can approach 16% of gross rooms revenue.

6. **Item 19**: FPR provided. Average RevPAR: $180.26 (all 17 Covered Hotels), $154.84 (9 franchised). Franchised hotels have a RevPAR Index of 92.5, meaning they underperform their Smith Travel competitive sets. Revenue-only disclosure — no cost or profit data.

7. **Territory**: Limited "Area of Protection" lasting only 1-5 years after opening, on a 20-year franchise term. After AOP Term expiration, no territorial protection whatsoever. Other Hyatt brands (25+) may operate within AOP at any time.

8. **Financial health**: Parent company is a publicly traded, investment-grade corporation with strong cash flow. Franchisor itself has $159M in total assets and $149M in member's equity. Hyatt Hotels Corporation provides absolute and unconditional guarantee of franchisor's obligations.

9. **Renewal risk**: Successor franchise is on then-current terms which "may be materially different" including fees. General release required. No floor or ceiling on changes.

10. **Dispute resolution**: Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, Illinois. Illinois law governs.

11. **Noncompete**: No post-term noncompete. During term, franchisee and management company may not be a "Competing Brand Owner."

12. **Litigation**: Minimal — one action (unpaid royalty fees). No bankruptcy.

13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Illinois). Small system size creates statistical fragility. Extreme performance variance among existing hotels.

14. **State addenda**: 7 states in Exhibit M (CA, HI, MD, MN, ND, RI, VA) plus Michigan in front matter.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. is a Delaware LLC that converted from Hyatt Franchise Corporation. It is wholly owned by Hyatt Hotels Corporation, a publicly traded company that also owns or controls Hyatt Corporation (the operating affiliate that administers World of Hyatt, provides Mandatory Services, and manages hotels), Rosemont Project Management (optional purchasing), and numerous international Hyatt franchise entities.

The Unbound Collection is a "soft brand" — each hotel retains its own unique tradename (owned by the franchisee) while affiliating with The Unbound Collection by Hyatt brand and other Proprietary Marks. This distinguishes it from "hard brand" franchises like Hyatt Regency or Hyatt Place where the hotel operates under the Hyatt brand name directly.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Application Fee: Greater of $100,000 or $400 per guest room. Partially refundable (less $7,500) if withdrawn/denied before approval; non-refundable after approval.
- Design Services Review: $75,000-$160,000.
- IT Project Management: $69,900 (up to 250 rooms) + $10,815-$17,510 expenses.
- Pre-Opening Training: $19,450-$46,650 tuition + $4,241-$9,900 expense reimbursement.
- Additional Pre-Opening Advisory: $0-$375,000 (may be required for conversions or inexperienced operators).
- 2024 actual initial fees paid: $154,220 to $216,250.

**Ongoing Fees — Franchise Agreement:**
- Royalty: 7% of Gross Rooms Revenue from Channel Bookings (monthly).
- World of Hyatt Assessment: 4.5% of eligible WOH revenue (2.25% for on-property enrollments).
- Quality Assurance: FIP fees $5,000-$20,000/stage (3 stages); BPP $45,000/year.
- QA Inspections: $1,500-$2,000/year; re-inspection $2,000.
- Training Tools: $9.01/room/year. GM University: $2,700/new GM.
- Owner Convention: $2,000-$5,000/attendee.

**Ongoing Fees — Mandatory Services (System Services Charges):**
- Commercial Services Fee: 3.5% of GRR (marketing, reservations, revenue management tech, sales, PMS, digital, other tech). No markup — cost recovery only.
- Digital Acquisition Fee: 1.35% of GRR through Digital Channels.
- GDS/Reservation Services: ~$8.50/reservation.
- Mandatory Contracts: $1,500-$25,000/month.
- Other Corporate Services: $500-$15,000/month.

**Optional/Non-Mandatory Services:**
- Field Marketing: $996-$2,747/month by tier.
- Revenue Management Services: $1,000-$3,500/month by tier.
- Central Sales Services: $3,850-$6,650/month (group sales) up to $18,350/month combined.
- GPGS Add-On: $3.50-$16/interaction.
- Digital Ancillary Services: $1,250/month + 5% commission.
- HSF Sales Team: 6% group (max $30K), 3% transient, 1% referrals.

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Transfer: no fee, but transferee pays Application Fee.
- Extension: $10,000 per request.
- Late Fee: $225 + 1.5%/month interest.
- Audit: cost of audit if 3%+ understatement.
- Liquidated Damages: up to 60 months x 10.5% of Average Monthly Revenue; 150% for Consequential Termination.
- De-identification failure: $5,000/day + costs.
- Insurance default: premiums + $200/room/year.

### Initial Investment

The total estimated initial investment ranges from $46,830,426 to $161,465,733 for a new-build 150-250 guest room hotel, excluding real estate. This is dominated by construction costs ($34.0M-$131.3M), FF&E ($5.9M-$13.0M), operating supplies ($2.2M-$4.3M), and technology ($1.5M-$3.3M).

Key notes:
- No finance charges, interest, or debt service included.
- Conversion costs vary widely depending on existing condition.
- 5% of total operating revenue must be deposited monthly into a capital reserve account for upgrades.
- Additional funds (3 months working capital): $650,000-$1,250,000.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Approximately 75% of purchases/leases to both establish and operate the Hotel must come from designated/approved suppliers or comply with System Standards. This is a high level of supplier control.

Key designated suppliers:
- Opera PMS (designated vendor, mandatory).
- PMS hosting (franchisor, mandatory via Hosting Services Agreement).
- CRS/GDS/ADS systems (mandatory participation).
- Cyber endpoint protection and web filtering tools (designated).
- Travel agent commission services (designated).
- QA inspection services (designated).
- Technology System components and fire/life safety systems (may be designated).

Avendra (products/services sourcing company) is available but optional. Rosemont (purchasing/project management) is optional.

In 2024, Hyatt Corporation received $7,917 in rebates from suppliers based on franchisee purchases, which was used entirely to offset System Services Costs. Brand Hotel franchisees received $2,208,810 MORE in loyalty program reimbursements than they paid in loyalty fees.

### F&B Operations
Franchisee must submit an F&B Operations Plan for approval. F&B Operators must be approved by franchisor. Material changes to F&B concept require prior written approval.

### Training Program Detail
Seven mandatory pre-opening training programs totaling approximately 290-404 hours of classroom training and 56-118 hours of on-the-job training:
1. Owner Briefing: 2 days / 8 hours (Chicago or virtual).
2. Owner/Operator Orientation: 2 days / 16 hours (quarterly, Chicago/hotel/virtual).
3. GM University: 53-58 hours total including eLearning, virtual, and 3-day in-person workshop.
4. Operations Training: 47-79 hours classroom + 40-76 hours OJT.
5. Sales/Events/Revenue Management: 136.5 hours (primarily virtual/eLearning).
6. Other Personnel: 17-34 hours classroom + 15-36 hours OJT (partially optional).
7. Required Other Personnel: 12-29 hours classroom + 1-6 hours OJT.
8. Pre-Opening Brand Training: 2-3 trainers on-site for minimum 7 nights.

Replacement GM training is mandatory ($2,700 per GM). Core Management vacancies must be filled within 6 months.

### Technology Stack and Costs
Initial Technology System cost: $1,508,950-$3,296,000. Required systems: PMS (Opera), POS, Revenue Management, Events (Envision), Key Lock, PBX/Phones, In-Room Entertainment, High Speed Internet, Guest Computing, Music Services, Digital Surveillance, Scent.

Ongoing PMS/POS vendor charge: $3.90/room/month. Music service: $18.95/month. No contractual limit on frequency or cost of required upgrades.

Franchisor has independent, unlimited access to all Technology System data.

### Marketing Fund
No separate advertising fund or cooperative. Brand-wide marketing is funded through the Commercial Services Fee (3.5% of GRR). Franchisor will not prepare periodic accounting of spending. No obligation to spend in franchisee's area. No franchisee advertising council. Marketing materials must comply with System Standards and may require prior approval.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
The "Area of Protection" is agreed before signing the Franchise Agreement. Size varies — from a few blocks in urban areas to 1-2 mile radius in less populated areas. The AOP provides protection only during the "AOP Term," which typically ends 1-5 years after the Hotel's opening date.

### Critical AOP Limitations
1. **AOP Term expiration**: After the AOP Term expires (potentially as early as 1 year post-opening), there is NO territorial protection. On a 20-year franchise term, this means the franchisee could have zero territorial protection for 15-19 years.
2. **Acquisition exception**: During AOP Term, if franchisor acquires a group of 4+ hotels, it may convert any of them within AOP to Brand Hotels.
3. **Other Hyatt brands**: 25+ Hyatt lodging brands (Hyatt Regency, Hyatt Place, Hyatt House, Hyatt Centric, Hyatt Studios, etc.) may operate within AOP at any time without restriction or compensation.
4. **Internet and marketing**: Franchisor may use Internet, catalog, telemarketing in AOP without compensating franchisee.

### Channel Restrictions
- Franchisee must use CRS, GDS, ADS as designated.
- Franchisee may not use any other reservation systems.
- Hotel-Specific Website may not accept reservations (only through approved link to Hotel System Website).
- Franchisee sets room rates subject to System Standards and best rate guarantee compliance.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- Initial term: 20 years from 1st day of opening month.
- Successor franchise: 10 years on then-current terms (which MAY BE MATERIALLY DIFFERENT).
- Renewal conditions include: substantial compliance, currently fully complying, then-current standards, passing QA scores for 3 years, 10+ years possession rights, renovate/remodel, sign general release.
- **"Materially different" risk**: The FDD explicitly states the successor franchise agreement may have materially different terms, including fees. There is no floor, ceiling, or negotiation right disclosed.

### Transfer Provisions
- All transfers of FA, Hotel assets, or Controlling Ownership Interests require franchisor approval.
- Non-Controlling Ownership Interest transfers permitted without approval if conditions met.
- Transferee must meet current qualifications, complete training, sign then-current agreement.
- General release required (if state law allows).
- No transfer fee, but transferee pays Application Fee ($100K+).
- Death/disability: transfer to approved transferee within 6 months.

### Termination and Death/Disability
- Franchisee may NOT terminate the FA except as law allows.
- Franchisor may NOT terminate without cause.
- Cure periods: 24 hours (health/safety), 10 days (payment/insurance), 30 days (general), 60 days (guarantor threshold).
- Non-curable: bankruptcy, $100K+ judgments, cease operations, IP contests, unauthorized transfers, felony, repeated defaults.
- Liquidated damages: up to 60 months x 10.5% of Average Monthly Revenue.
- Consequential Termination (transfer to Competitor or 3+ defaults): 150% multiplier.

### Noncompete
- During term: no formal noncompete covenant, but neither franchisee nor management company may be a "Competing Brand Owner" (owns/franchises/manages brand with 5+ hotels competing with Brand Hotels).
- Post-term: NO noncompete restrictions.

### Dispute Resolution
- Mandatory arbitration within 10 miles of franchisor's principal address (Chicago, IL).
- Subject to arbitration obligation, litigation in Illinois.
- Illinois law governs (subject to Federal Arbitration Act and state overrides).
- Waiver of punitive damages and jury trial (subject to state law overrides).

### Litigation History
One action in 2024: Hyatt Franchising v. Imperial Hotels, LLC et al. (AAA, unpaid royalty fees). No other required disclosures. No bankruptcy history.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### FPR Overview
Three FPRs provided plus reservation channel mix data, covering 17 "Covered Hotels" that operated during all of 2024. Nine are franchised, 8 are owned/managed. The FPRs are revenue-only — no cost, expense, or profit data is disclosed.

### First FPR: Occupancy, ADR, RevPAR

| Metric | Franchised (9) | Owned/Managed (8) | All (17) |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Avg Occupancy | 63.1% | 55.4% | 60.0% |
| Avg ADR | $245.47 | $391.53 | $300.61 |
| Avg RevPAR | $154.84 | $217.11 | $180.26 |

**Franchised hotel statistics:**
- Occupancy: High 85.9%, Low 7.9%, Median 66.7%. 7 of 9 (77.8%) exceeded the average.
- ADR: High $396.28, Low $152.22, Median $222.03. 4 of 9 (44.4%) exceeded the average.
- RevPAR: High $261.39, Low $17.78, Median $150.15. 5 of 9 (55.6%) exceeded the average.

**Critical observations:**
- The 7.9% occupancy and $17.78 RevPAR for the lowest-performing franchised hotel suggests a severely distressed or newly opened property. This single outlier significantly pulls down averages.
- Median franchised RevPAR ($150.15) is close to the average ($154.84), indicating the distribution is not extremely skewed once the outlier is accounted for.
- Owned/managed hotels significantly outperform franchised hotels in ADR ($391.53 vs $245.47) suggesting the company-owned portfolio skews toward higher-end markets.

### Second FPR: Smith Travel Research Indices

| Metric | Franchised (9) | Owned/Managed (8) | All (17) |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Avg Occ Index | 93.9 | 95.8 | 94.8 |
| Avg Rate Index | 97.4 | 100.3 | 98.8 |
| Avg RevPAR Index | 92.5 | 98.2 | 95.2 |

An index of 100 means a hotel captures its "fair share" relative to its competitive set. Franchised Brand Hotels average below 100 on all three metrics, indicating systematic underperformance versus competitive sets. The median franchised RevPAR Index is 93.3 — most franchised hotels are underperforming their comp sets.

### Third FPR: World of Hyatt Program Contribution

| Metric | Franchised (9) | Owned/Managed (8) | All (17) |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Avg % Revenue from WOH | 53.3% | 40.4% | 45.9% |
| Avg % WOH Room Nights | 48.3% | 55.5% | 47.2% |
| Avg # WOH Room Nights | 21,017 | 18,291 | 19,805 |

Over half of franchised hotel revenue comes from World of Hyatt members, demonstrating significant loyalty program value but also dependency on the Hyatt system for demand generation.

### Channel Mix
Franchised hotels: 60.7% Direct / 39.3% Indirect. Owned/managed: 69.2% / 30.8%.

Direct Channels include Hyatt.com, World of Hyatt app, and GPGS voice. Indirect includes GDS and OTA. Royalty applies only to Channel Bookings (which includes Direct and GDS but excludes OTA).

### Critical Caveats
- Revenue only — no cost or profit data.
- Small sample (17 total, 9 franchised) makes statistics fragile.
- Extreme variance: ADR range $152-$1,050; occupancy range 7.9%-85.9%.
- Average room count 175 with range 14-339 — properties are very diverse.
- Some were conversions with existing customer bases.
- Unaudited figures from internal records.
