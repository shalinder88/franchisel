The second sentence of Section 5.4(b) is hereby deleted in its 
entirety and replaced with the following: 
In addition, except for the Hotel System Website, a Hotel-
Specific Website (defined below), approved ADS, and as 
otherwise provided in this Section 5.4, Franchisee may not 
develop, maintain or authorize any other website, other online 
presence or other electronic medium that describes or in any 
other way promotes the Hotel or displays any of the 
Proprietary Marks, without the prior written approval of Hyatt. 
5.4(c) 
Websites and 
Electronic 
Media 
The following is added as a new Section 5.4(c): 
Franchisee may develop, maintain, or authorize a website 
featuring the Hotel to consumers (the “Hotel-Specific 
Website”) only with Hyatt’s approval and subject to the 
conditions in this Section 5.4.  The Hotel-Specific Website 
may not use the word “hyatt” or any similar word, or any of 
the Proprietary Marks, as part of its domain name or URL.  
The Hotel-Specific Website may not accept reservations for 
the Hotel (other than through an approved link to a Hotel 
System Website) or otherwise sell any products or services 
associated with the Hotel or any of the Proprietary Marks.  
Franchisee is solely responsible for obtaining all intellectual 
property rights to use any information and materials on the 
Hotel-Specific Website.  However, as between Hyatt and 
Franchisee, Hyatt owns all intellectual property rights and 
other rights in and to all Guest Information that visitors supply 
or the Hotel-Specific Website obtains.
