# Reader Report — The Unbound Collection by Hyatt (637928-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand**: The Unbound Collection by Hyatt — unique, upscale hotel affiliation where franchisee retains own tradename but affiliates with "The Unbound Collection by Hyatt" brand. Franchising since June 2016.
2. **Franchisor**: Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. (Delaware LLC), parent Hyatt Hotels Corporation (publicly traded, $13.3B total assets as of 12/31/2024).
3. **System size**: 18 total Brand Hotels as of 12/31/2024 (10 franchised, 8 company-owned/managed). Net +2 in 2024, +3 in 2023, +2 in 2022. Small but growing system.
4. **Investment range**: $46,830,426 to $161,465,733 for a new 150-250 guest room hotel (excluding real estate). This is an ultra-high-investment franchise.
5. **Fee burden**: 7% royalty on Gross Rooms Revenue from Channel Bookings + 3.5% Commercial Services Fee + 1.35% Digital Acquisition Fee + 4.5% World of Hyatt loyalty assessment on eligible revenue from WOH members. Effective all-in fee load is very high.
6. **Item 19**: FPR provided. 17 Covered Hotels in 2024. Average RevPAR $180.26 (all); $154.84 (franchised); $217.11 (owned/managed). Revenue-only disclosures; no cost or profit data.
7. **Territory**: Limited "Area of Protection" during AOP Term only (typically 1-5 years after opening). After AOP Term expires, no territorial protection whatsoever. Significant encroachment risk from other Hyatt brands.
8. **Term**: 20-year initial term; 10-year successor franchise on then-current terms (which may be materially different). General release required at renewal.
9. **Dispute resolution**: Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, Illinois. Illinois law governs (subject to state overrides).
10. **Noncompete**: No post-term noncompete. During term, neither franchisee nor management company may be a "Competing Brand Owner."
11. **Litigation**: One action in 2024 (Hyatt v. Imperial Hotels, unpaid royalty fees). No bankruptcy history.
12. **Financial health**: Parent Hyatt Hotels Corp: 2024 net income $1,296M ($12.65/share diluted), total assets $13.3B, total equity $3.8B. Franchisor-level: member's equity $148.5M, total assets $159.3M (unaudited).
13. **Liquidated damages**: Up to 60 months of remaining term x (3.5% + 7%) of Average Monthly Revenue. 150% multiplier for Consequential Termination (competitor transfer or serial defaults).
14. **State addenda**: 7 states in Exhibit M (CA, HI, MD, MN, ND, RI, VA) plus Michigan in front matter.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- "Area of Protection" agreed before signing FA. Boundaries defined by geographic references or radius.
- AOP Term: begins at FA effective date, typically ends 1-5 years after opening.
- During AOP Term: franchisor will not open another Brand Hotel in AOP, EXCEPT if acquiring a group of 4+ hotels.
- After AOP Term: NO territorial rights whatsoever. Franchisor and affiliates may open Brand Hotels anywhere.
- Existing operating Brand Hotels may receive no AOP at all when signing new FA.
- No exclusive territory. Competition from all Hyatt brands (25+ lodging brands) expected.
- No options, rights of first refusal, or similar rights for additional franchises.
- No performance contingencies for territorial rights.
- No restrictions on solicitation/customer acceptance areas.
- Franchisor may use Internet, catalog, telemarketing in AOP without compensation.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
- Key marks registered with PTO: "Hyatt" (1972), "World of Hyatt" (2017), "The Unbound Collection by Hyatt" (2017, word and logo).
- Marks owned by Hyatt Corporation, licensed to franchisor under perpetual Trademark License Agreement (1993, as amended).
- Hotel features own Tradename (owned by franchisee) as primary name, with Unbound Collection affiliation.
- No known infringements or material challenges.

### Item 14 — Patents/Copyrights/Proprietary Information
- No material patents.
- Copyright protection claimed for Copyrighted Materials (not registered).
- Guest Information is franchisor's property. Franchisor has unlimited access to Hotel PMS.
- Post-term: franchisee may use Hotel-generated Guest Information stored in PMS.
- All Innovations developed by/for franchisee belong to franchisor.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
- Must use approved management company OR be approved to self-manage.
- Management company may not be a "Competing Brand Owner" (5+ hotel brand competing with Brand Hotels).
- Core Management must work exclusively at Hotel.
- Revenue manager must be certified within 90 days; max 20 Brand Hotels per revenue manager.
- Personal guaranty required from Controlling Ownership Interest holders.
- Guarantor Monetary Threshold: net worth minimum and liquidity minimum (set per FA, increases annually by CPI).

### Item 16 — Restrictions on What Franchisee May Sell
- Must offer all products/services franchisor authorizes; may not sell unauthorized products.
- No restrictions on customers.
- Mixed-Use Site restrictions: no pawnshops, gentlemen's clubs, pornography, package liquor stores, marijuana dispensaries, gambling without consent; bars with 50%+ alcohol revenue within 300 feet need consent.
- Franchisee sets room rates subject to System Standards compliance and best rate guarantee.
- May not change number of guest rooms without consent.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- Initial term: 20 years from 1st day of opening month.
- Successor franchise: 10 years on then-current terms (which may be MATERIALLY DIFFERENT).
- Renewal conditions: substantial compliance during term, currently fully complying, meet then-current standards, passing QA scores for 3 prior years, possession rights for 10+ years, renovate/remodel, sign general release, pay PIP fee.
- Franchise Agreement term extends during casualty closure.

### Termination
- Franchisee may NOT terminate the FA (except as law allows).
- Franchisor may NOT terminate without cause.
- Curable defaults: 24 hours for public health/safety; 10 days for insurance/payment failures; 30 days for tax/development/compliance failures; 60 days for Guarantor Monetary Threshold.
- Non-curable defaults: bankruptcy, judgment over $100K, cease operations, contest IP, unauthorized transfer, felony conviction, adverse actions, false books, unauthorized disclosure, law violations, repeated defaults.

### Transfer
- No transfer of FA, Hotel assets, or Controlling Ownership Interests without approval.
- Non-Controlling Ownership Interest transfers permitted without approval if transferee not a competitor/sanctioned person.
- Transfer conditions: transferee qualifies, all amounts paid, transferee's personnel trained, sign then-current agreement (may be materially different), general release, agree to renovate, acceptable capital structure.
- Death/disability: representative must transfer to approved transferee within 6 months.

### Right of First Offer
- Franchisor has right of first offer ONLY if Exhibit C is signed. Required only for strategic markets.

### Noncompete
- During term: no formal noncompete covenant, but neither franchisee nor management company may be a Competing Brand Owner.
- Post-term: NO noncompete restrictions.

### Dispute Resolution
- Mandatory arbitration at location within 10 miles of franchisor's principal address (Chicago, IL).
- Subject to arbitration, litigation in Illinois.
- Illinois law governs (subject to federal law and state overrides).
- Liquidated damages: see Item 6 Note 5.

### Unresolveds
1. **Item 19 extreme outlier**: One franchised hotel has 7.9% occupancy and $17.78 RevPAR, suggesting a severe problem property. Nature of issue unknown.
2. **RevPAR Index below 100**: Franchised hotels average 92.5 RevPAR Index — systematically underperforming competitive sets. Cause not explained.

5. **Materially different renewal terms**: Successor franchise is on then-current terms which "may be materially different" — including fees. No floor or ceiling disclosed.
6. **Consequential Termination 150% multiplier**: If franchise terminates and Hotel transfers to a Competitor, liquidated damages increase to 150%. This creates significant exit burden.
7. **Competing Hyatt brands in AOP**: The 25+ Hyatt-affiliated lodging brands may operate within AOP during and after AOP Term without restriction. No impact compensation mechanism.
