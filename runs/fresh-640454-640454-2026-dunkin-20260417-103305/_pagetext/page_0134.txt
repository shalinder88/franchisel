INCOME TAXES
"Income tax expense" consists of current taxes related to foreign jurisdictions of $6.3 million, $6.1 million and $6.0 million
for 2025, 2024 and 2023, respectively.
The Company does not record a provision for income taxes for the limited liability companies that are treated as disregarded
entities for U.S. income tax purposes and for which there is no tax sharing agreement. As a result, the provision for income
taxes differed from the expense computed using the statutory federal income tax rate of 21% for 2025, 2024 and 2023.

The 2019 Class A-1 Senior Variable Funding Notes ("2019 Variable Funding Notes") and 2023 Class A-1 Senior Variable
Funding Notes ("2023 Variable Funding Notes") allow for the issuance of up to $150.0 million and $850.0 million,
respectively, of variable funding notes and certain other credit instruments, each including letters of credit of $100.0 million
in support of various Dunkin' subsidiary obligations. As of December 28, 2025, the Master Issuer had outstanding
borrowings of $88.8 million under the 2019 Variable Funding Notes leaving $61.2 million of remaining capacity for future
borrowings. As of December 28, 2025, the Master Issuer had no outstanding borrowings exclusive of $56.4 million of
outstanding letters of credit under the 2023 Variable Funding Notes leaving $793.6 million of remaining capacity for future
borrowings.
In November 2025, the Master Issuer issued $550.0 million 2025-1 Class A-2-I Senior Secured Fixed Rate Notes and
$350.0 million 2025-1 Class A-2-II Senior Secured Fixed Rate Notes (collectively, the "2025 Fixed Rate Notes"). Proceeds
from the 2025 Fixed Rate Notes were used to repay the remaining $376.0 million outstanding on the 2019-1 Class A-2-II
Senior Secured Fixed Rate Notes due May 2026, repay $481.6 million in drawn 2023 Variable Funding Notes balances and
pay related transaction fees and expenses. The remaining net proceeds were distributed to Inspire. No amendments or
terminations were made to the 2023 Variable Funding Notes in connection with the transaction.
Legal and Environmental Matters
The Company is involved in various litigation and claims incidental to its business. Although the outcome of these matters
cannot be predicted with certainty and some of these matters may be resolved unfavorably to the Company, based on
currently available information, including legal defenses available to the Company and its legal reserves and insurance
coverages, the Company does not believe that the outcome of these legal matters will have a material adverse effect on its
financial position, results of operations or cash flows.
