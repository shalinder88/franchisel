## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and growth:** Dunkin' operates 9,999 restaurants in the U.S. (9,963 franchised, 36 company-owned) as of December 28, 2025. This includes 8,744 single-branded Dunkin' restaurants, 36 at Multi-Brand Locations, and 1,219 Dunkin'/Baskin-Robbins Combo restaurants. The system added a net 281 standalone Dunkin' outlets in 2025 (8,499→8,780), accelerating from +202 in 2024 and +179 in 2023. An additional 4,242 Dunkin' restaurants operate internationally.

2. **Entity structure and ownership:** Dunkin' Donuts Franchising LLC is a Delaware LLC formed in 2006, wholly owned through a securitization structure (DB Master Finance LLC, 2015) under Inspire Brands, Inc., which is controlled by private equity funds managed by Roark Capital Management, LLC. The franchisor itself has never operated a Dunkin' Restaurant; all operational services are performed by Dunkin' Brands, Inc. under a management agreement.

3. **Ownership history:** The Dunkin' brand originated in 1954 with franchising since 1955. Inspire Brands acquired Dunkin' Brands Group in December 2020. Inspire also owns Arby's, Buffalo Wild Wings, Jimmy John's, Sonic, and Baskin-Robbins, and through Roark affiliation, Subway, Dave's Hot Chicken, and numerous other franchise brands.

4. **Investment range:** Freestanding $532,400–$1,832,500; Shopping Center $443,000–$1,333,500; Gas/C-Store $216,400–$1,065,500; SDO (Non-Traditional) $142,000–$862,500. Wide range reflects diverse format options from full drive-thru to kiosk.

5. **Fee burden:** CFF 5.9% of Gross Sales + CAF 5.0% + Loyalty 1.4% (declining to 1.2% in 2027) = approximately 12.3% of Gross Sales for standard locations (SDOs pay 2.5% CAF instead of 5.0%). Multiple incentive programs can significantly reduce initial CFF burden.

6. **Item 19 highlights:** Average AUV $1,372,069 across 7,010 franchised restaurants (median $1,297,694). Drive-thru restaurants averaged $1,547,899 vs. $1,124,559 without drive-thru. 78% of traditional restaurants exceeded $1M. No cost/expense data disclosed.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** The bottom quartile averaged $718,088 with the lowest AUV at $65,354. A franchisee at the bottom quartile median ($753,308) paying 12.3% in ongoing fees would remit approximately $92,657/year in royalty+ad+loyalty fees alone — a significant burden on a sub-$1M revenue unit.

8. **Financial health:** KPMG-audited, unqualified opinion, no going concern. Total revenue $1.398 billion; net income $732.9 million; operating income $739.0 million. The franchisor is extremely profitable. However, distributions to parent ($813.3M) consistently exceed net income, eroding member's equity from $1.582B (2022) to $1.331B (2025).

9. **Growth trajectory:** Strong and accelerating — 314 new franchised openings in 2025 with only 81 closures. 406 projected new franchised outlets for next fiscal year. The Combo format is declining (-50 net in 2025).

10. **Territory:** No exclusive territory under the Franchise Agreement. Limited protection only during Development Agreement term for multi-unit developers.

11. **Renewal:** 20-year initial term with 20-year renewal. Renewal requires signing the then-current franchise agreement (which may be materially different), mutual general release, and meeting stringent compliance conditions.

12. **Disputes:** Georgia law governs; venue in Atlanta. No arbitration required. Out-of-state dispute resolution flagged as a special risk.

13. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution only.

14. **Gag clause:** Present. Confidentiality agreements signed with some current/former franchisees as part of dispute settlements, restricting their ability to speak openly.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Dunkin' Donuts Franchising LLC operates within a securitization structure that creates a layer of complexity. DB Master Finance LLC issued secured notes backed by franchise royalties, meaning the royalty stream is pledged to noteholders. Dunkin' Brands, Inc., a parent company, performs all franchise management services under a management agreement and receives management fees. This structure means the franchisor entity is essentially a special purpose vehicle — it collects franchise fees and passes them through.

The Inspire Brands affiliation is significant. Roark Capital's portfolio includes more than 30 franchise brands, creating both scale advantages (technology, supply chain) and potential conflicts (multi-brand locations, cross-selling, management attention allocation). The franchisor has an indirect interest in at least one supplier (Checkmate.com) through Inspire Brands ownership.

### Fee Stack

The total ongoing fee burden for a standard Dunkin' restaurant is among the highest in QSR franchising:

**Continuing Franchise Fee (Royalty): 5.9% of Gross Sales.** This is the base royalty rate for all new restaurants. It is collected weekly via electronic transfer. The 5.9% rate is higher than many QSR competitors (McDonald's: ~4% base + rent; Subway: 8% but all-in). However, Dunkin' does not typically charge rent through the franchise agreement itself (though affiliate leases exist separately).

Several incentive programs can temporarily reduce the effective CFF:
- Standard Markets: $125,000 CFF credit applied against future royalty payments
- Strategic Markets: $225,000 CFF credit
- Pacific Northwest: $25,000 base + $50,000 one-time for first restaurant + $300,000 opening credit (2026–2027) or $250,000 (2028+) — this is an extremely generous incentive reflecting Dunkin's push into the Pacific Northwest
- Early Opening: 0% CFF from opening to required opening date (up to 6 months of royalty-free operation)
- VetFran: $10,000 per restaurant, up to $100,000

**Continuing Advertising Fee (CAF): 5.0% of Gross Sales (2.5% for SDOs).** This funds the Dunkin' Advertising and Sales Promotion Fund. Combined with the CFF, the base ongoing fee for standard locations is 10.9% of Gross Sales before the loyalty contribution. The Standard incentive reduces CAF to 2% in year 1, ramping to 5% by year 4. The Strategic incentive provides even more generous relief at 2.4% through year 5.

**Loyalty Program Contribution Payment: 1.4% of Loyalty Program Sales (declining to 1.2% from 2027).** "Loyalty Program Sales" means Gross Sales to loyalty members for in-store eligible products. This is effectively an additional royalty. The Loyalty Fund is segregated, audited annually, and reimburses franchisees at National Average Product Cost for redeemed items. The fund received an approximately $3M loan from the franchisor to cover initial shortfall. This is a relatively new fee layer.

**Technology costs are layered and recurring:** Monthly POS managed services ($55–$70/month), digital menu board services ($13–$17/panel/month), service desk ($570–$825/year), drive-thru monitoring ($400–$600/year). These add roughly $2,000–$3,000/year per restaurant in technology overhead beyond the initial capital investment.

**Transfer fees are tiered by sales and tenure:** For a Dunkin' restaurant operated 3+ years with trailing 12-month Gross Sales of $1.4M+, the transfer fee is $32,500 ($12,500 base + $20,000 tier). For Combo restaurants, the equivalent is $47,500 ($20,000 + $27,500). This is a meaningful cost on transfers.

### Initial Investment

The investment range varies dramatically by format:

**Freestanding ($532,400–$1,832,500):** The widest range, driven primarily by building costs ($180K–$600K) and site development ($13K–$350K). Equipment/fixtures ($189K–$300K) and technology ($65K–$118K) are relatively consistent. The low end assumes a build-to-suit lease where the landlord bears most development costs. Land purchase adds $100K–$1.2M+.

**Shopping Center/Storefront ($443,000–$1,333,500):** Lower building costs ($145.6K–$455K) due to shared walls and existing structures.

**Gas/C-Store ($216,400–$1,065,500):** Significantly lower due to smaller footprint (600–2,300 sqft), lower equipment requirements, and shared infrastructure.

**SDO Non-Traditional ($142,000–$862,500):** The lowest entry point, suitable for carts, kiosks, and host establishments.

The IFF itself ranges from $40,000 to $90,000 depending on DMA tier, with the highest fees in the densest markets (Boston, New York, Philadelphia at $90K) and lowest in smaller markets ($40K). This tiered pricing reflects market-by-market franchise value.

**Insurance requirements are substantial:** $2M per occurrence CGL, $1M EPL, all-risk property coverage including flood and earthquake. Inspire Brands and affiliates must be named as additional insureds.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

Dunkin' exercises tight supplier control — more than 95% of purchases and leases must come from approved or designated sources. The centerpiece is **National DCP, LLC (NDCP)**, a franchisee-owned cooperative designated as the exclusive purchasing and distribution supplier. Membership is mandatory ($2,500 fee), requires a personal guarantee, and obligates franchisees to purchase all food and non-food products through NDCP. NDCP passes savings through lower prices and patronage dividends.

In certain markets, franchisor has approved third-party suppliers for donut and bakery products, and franchisees must maintain an approved back-up supplier. Franchisees must use delivery vehicles meeting franchisor specifications.

The alternative supplier approval process is restrictive: written request required, criteria not disclosed, testing at franchisee expense ($1,000–$10,000), 180-day window (auto-denied if no response), franchisor can withdraw approval at any time.

In FY2025, franchisee payments generated $2M in Center fees to the franchisor and $132.6M to affiliates from sales/leases (predominantly real estate sublease payments).

### Training Program Detail

Initial training is 4–8 weeks depending on production platform and restaurant complexity. The first 2 trainees are included in the IFF for the first 5 restaurants; additional trainees cost $4,000 each. Cancellation fees apply ($1,000 for 8–14 days notice, $2,000 for ≤7 days). Training may be at a Dunkin' training facility, with additional costs if at another location.

The operations manual totals 1,235 pages covering: Menu Items (389pp), Food Safety (114pp), Equipment (108pp), Operational Excellence (195pp), Digital/Mobile/Technology (196pp), Drive-Thru (36pp), People (80pp), CML/FP (117pp). The Center is the online learning platform with annual subscription ($340/restaurant).

### Technology Stack and Costs

The technology requirements are extensive and represent both upfront capital and ongoing operating expense:

- **POS Systems:** 2–4 systems required per restaurant. Initial cost included in $65K–$118K technology budget. Monthly managed services $55–$70/month.
- **Digital Menu Boards:** Exterior drive-thru pedestal/canopy system ($13,500–$14,300 hardware + $3,600–$4,700 installation); interior Order Status Board ($1,600–$2,000 + $1,400–$1,825 installation); exterior presell panel ($8,200–$8,700 + $4,300–$4,800 installation). Monthly managed services $13–$17 per panel.
- **Service Desk:** Mandatory from approved provider; $570–$825/year per restaurant.
- **Drive-Thru Monitoring:** $2,500–$5,000 initial + $400–$600/year ongoing.

### Marketing Fund Spending and Audit

The CAF (5.0% of Gross Sales) funds the Dunkin' Advertising and Sales Promotion Fund. In FY2025, franchise advertising contributions totaled $597.8M and advertising expenses were exactly equal — the fund operates at breakeven with no franchisor profit extracted. The Loyalty Fund is separately audited by an independent third-party accountant annually.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition and Carve-Outs

**The Franchise Agreement provides no exclusive territory whatsoever** — not even a nonexclusive territory. The franchisor retains the right to operate or license others to operate Dunkin' Restaurants and other concepts at any location, and to use the Proprietary Marks in ways that compete with the franchisee's location. This is a significant encroachment risk.

The **Development Agreement** provides limited territorial protection within the Development Area during its term, but even this has carve-outs for: (i) franchises to the franchisee or controlled affiliates, (ii) existing restaurants or those under development, and (iii) SDO opportunities. Development Areas are "relatively limited in size and scope."

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights

Sales must be made only from the approved location — no Internet or electronic commerce without prior written authorization. The franchisor reserves the right to distribute (or license others to distribute) products through any channel, anywhere, in any form, regardless of proximity to the franchisee's location. Third-party delivery services are permitted but controlled by the franchisor.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The 20-year initial term is long by QSR standards. The 20-year renewal is also long. However, renewal is conditioned on signing the "then-current form of Franchise Agreement," which may be "materially different" from the original. This creates significant renewal risk — the franchisee may face substantially different terms at renewal. Additionally, renewal requires a mutual general release, meaning the franchisee must release all claims against the franchisor as a condition of continuing to operate.

The requirement that the franchisee not receive more than 3 notices of default in the prior 10-year period before issuing notice to renew means that even cured defaults can accumulate to block renewal.

**SDO locations have no renewal rights** — the franchise simply expires at the end of the term.

### Termination Triggers and Cure Periods

Termination for cause only. Cure periods are extremely short:
- 24 hours for health/sanitation/safety violations and ceasing operations without consent
- 7 days for failure to pay
- 30 days for all other defaults

Non-curable defaults include several situations that could arise from financial distress (bankruptcy, lease termination) and behavioral issues (fraud, felony conviction, intentional underreporting). The cross-default provision means a default under any agreement with the franchisor can trigger termination of all agreements.

Three or more notices of the "same or similar" defaults within 12 months constitutes a non-curable default, regardless of whether each individual default was timely cured.

### Transfer Conditions

Transfer is broadly defined to include virtually any change in ownership, control, or management. The franchisor has a 60-day right of first refusal for any sale. Post-termination, the franchisor has a 30-day option to purchase Operating Assets at fair market value less indebtedness.

For death or disability, the legal representative must propose a transfer within 12 months and complete the transfer within 18 months.

### Noncompete Scope

During the term, the noncompete is relatively narrow — it prohibits interest in any business deriving more than 20% of revenue from a "combination of coffee and/or baked goods." Post-term, the restriction extends 24 months and covers the Premises, a 5-mile radius, and any other Dunkin' Restaurant location. The 5-mile radius is moderate by industry standards, but the blanket prohibition on interest near "any other Dunkin' Restaurant" effectively prevents operation anywhere near the 9,999-unit system.

### Dispute Resolution

Georgia law governs all claims, and venue is exclusively in Atlanta, Georgia — subject to state law modifications. No arbitration or mediation is required. The out-of-state dispute resolution provision is flagged as a special risk. State-specific addenda in Exhibit E likely modify these provisions for several states.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

Dunkin' provides robust FPR data covering 7,010 of its 8,744 franchised restaurants (80.2% of the system). The exclusions are reasonable — newly opened restaurants, temporarily closed for remodel/casualty, Multi-Brand Locations, self-serve, and part-time/seasonal operations.

### Overall System Performance

The system-wide average AUV of $1,372,069 and median of $1,297,694 indicate a healthy franchise system. The 45% of restaurants exceeding the average (meaning the distribution skews right with high-volume outliers) is a common pattern.

**Quartile analysis reveals meaningful dispersion:**
- Top quartile: Average $2,154,341, median $2,041,189. These are strong-performing units.
- Second quartile: Average $1,489,962. Solid performance.
- Third quartile: Average $1,126,192. Adequate but margins may be thin given the fee burden.
- Bottom quartile: Average $718,088. At this level, with 12.3%+ in franchise fees alone, profitability is extremely challenging.

### Drive-Thru Premium

The drive-thru premium is significant: drive-thru traditional restaurants average $1,547,899 vs. $1,124,559 for non-drive-thru — a 37.6% premium. This strongly suggests drive-thru capability is critical for financial viability.

### Site Type Variation

Airport locations perform best (average $1,667,646) despite being non-traditional, reflecting captive-audience economics. Freestanding pad/building locations average $1,584,319. "Other" non-traditional locations average only $890,102 — a meaningful discount.

### Critical Caveats

**No cost data is disclosed.** The FPR explicitly states it "does not reflect the costs of sales, operating expenses or other costs or expenses that must be deducted from the AUV figures to obtain your net income or profit." This is a significant limitation. A $1.37M AUV restaurant with 12.3% franchise fees ($168K), 25-30% labor, 25-30% COGS, and occupancy costs may have limited operating profit.

The data is from POS systems and sales reports submitted by franchisees — not independently audited.

### Item 19 Cohort Comparability

The Item 19 data reflects the AUVs of restaurants paying various CFF rates (some under prior agreements with potentially different rates, some under incentive programs). The standard CFF for new franchisees is 5.9%. If the cohort includes restaurants paying lower legacy CFF rates, the economic comparability between the disclosed AUVs and a new franchisee's expected experience may differ — though this affects profitability rather than top-line AUV. No cohort comparability issue is identified in the AUV data itself since the Item 19 measures only sales, not costs.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### Multi-Year Trajectory

Dunkin's standalone system has shown consistent, accelerating growth:
- 2023: Net +179 (8,118→8,297)
- 2024: Net +202 (8,297→8,499)
- 2025: Net +281 (8,499→8,780)

The 2025 growth of 314 new franchised openings is particularly strong, against only 81 closures (2 terminations, 24 non-renewals, 55 ceased operations). The closure rate (81/8,744 = 0.9%) is very low by industry standards.

### Transfer Trends

Transfers increased from 137 (2024) to 241 (2025), potentially reflecting increased transaction activity as the system matures and shifts ownership. Large transfer concentrations in Massachusetts (25), New York (30), Florida (42), Connecticut (27), and New Hampshire (22) — the legacy Northeast core.

### Ceased Operations Declining

The most notable trend is the decline in "ceased operations — other reasons": 139 (2023) → 67 (2024) → 55 (2025). This 60% decline over two years suggests improving unit economics and/or more selective site approval. Terminations (2 in 2025) and non-renewals (24) are both low.

### Company-Owned Outlets

Only 36 company-owned restaurants, all in Ohio. Projected to add 10 more company-owned in Ohio — suggesting a testing or development function rather than a reacquisition strategy.

### Combo Restaurant Decline

The Combo format (Dunkin' + Baskin-Robbins) is declining: from 1,283 (end 2023) to 1,219 (end 2025), a net loss of 64 units. Only 12 new Combo openings in 2025 against 62 closures. The Combo concept appears to be unwinding in favor of standalone formats.

### Projected Pipeline

The projected pipeline is robust: 229 FAs signed but not open, 406 projected new franchised outlets in the next fiscal year, plus 26 new Combo outlets. If realized, this would represent the largest single-year expansion in recent history.

### Confidentiality Clause

The gag clause is a moderate risk factor. The FDD explicitly states that "some current and former franchisees sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience." This may limit a prospective franchisee's ability to conduct thorough due diligence through franchisee interviews.

## H. Item 21 — Financial Statements

### Auditor and Opinion

KPMG LLP (Atlanta, GA) issued an unqualified opinion on March 19, 2026, covering the financial statements of Dunkin' Donuts Franchising LLC as of December 28, 2025 and December 29, 2024, and for each of the three fiscal years in the period ended December 28, 2025. No going concern language.

### Balance Sheet Analysis

**Total assets of $1.572 billion** are dominated by intangible assets (franchise agreements) at $1.453 billion (92.5% of total assets). These intangibles are being amortized on a straight-line basis over 10–29 years, with annual amortization of $60.9 million. No impairment has been recorded. This asset base reflects the acquired franchise system value.

Accounts receivable increased significantly from $72.4M to $114.4M, a 58% increase, which bears monitoring but may reflect system growth and timing differences. Allowance for credit losses decreased from $5.5M to $4.8M.

The liability side is modest relative to assets. Total liabilities of $240.8M include deferred revenue of $195.5M ($9.3M current + $186.3M long-term) — representing IFFs being recognized over 20-year franchise terms. This is a substantial balance reflecting the system's continued development activity.

**Member's equity of $1.331 billion** has declined each year from $1.582B (2022) as distributions to parent consistently exceed net income. In 2025: $732.9M net income vs. $813.3M distributions = $(80.4M) equity reduction. This pattern — the parent extracting more than the entity earns — is structural in securitization vehicles and worth monitoring.

### Income Statement Analysis

**Total revenue of $1.398 billion** consists primarily of:
- Franchise fees and royalty revenues: $793.2M (56.7% of total)
- Franchise contributions for advertising: $597.8M (42.7%)
- Other revenues: $7.4M (0.5%)

The advertising contributions and expenses net to zero ($597.8M each), confirming the ad fund operates at breakeven.

**Operating income of $739.0M** represents a 52.8% operating margin (on total revenue) or a remarkable 93.2% margin on non-advertising revenue ($793.2M + $7.4M = $800.6M). The only significant operating cost is intangible amortization ($60.9M). Other operating costs are minimal ($653K in 2025, down from $2.8M in 2024).

Income tax expense is minimal ($6.3M) given the LLC pass-through structure — the entity pays taxes only on non-pass-through items.

**Revenue growth:** Franchise royalty revenue grew 4.8% ($756.6M → $793.2M) year-over-year, reflecting both system growth and same-store sales improvement.

### Cash Flow

Cash flows from operations ($813.3M) essentially equal distributions to parent ($813.3M), confirming this entity functions as a cash flow pass-through. There is no investment activity and no debt service — the securitization debt sits at the DB Master Finance level.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Leverage:** The franchisor entity itself has no debt. However, the parent structure (DB Master Finance) holds securitization debt backed by franchise royalties.

2. **Distributions exceeding income:** A structural pattern that has eroded equity by $251M over three years. While the entity remains solvent with $1.33B in equity, this trend bears monitoring.

3. **Related party structure:** The management agreement with Dunkin' Brands, Inc. creates related-party dynamics. Management fees are paid to DBI for performing franchise management services.

4. **Asset composition:** 92.5% intangible assets creates concentration risk, though franchise agreements are a proven income-generating asset.

5. **Deferred revenue:** $195.5M in deferred IFFs represents substantial advance fee collection, providing cash flow stability.

6. **No impairment:** No impairment charges on the $1.45B intangible asset base in any year — reflecting strong franchise value.
