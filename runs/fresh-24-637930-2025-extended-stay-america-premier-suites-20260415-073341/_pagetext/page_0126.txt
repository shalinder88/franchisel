EXHIBIT F 
 
RIGHT OF FIRST OFFER FOR STRATEGIC MARKETS 
Franchisor and Franchisee acknowledge that Franchisor’s right of first offer reflected in this 
Exhibit F applies to this Agreement. Franchisor has the unrestricted right to assign its right of first offer 
to an Affiliate or other third party to whom Franchisor has assigned its rights under this Agreement, who 
then will have Franchisor’s rights and obligations described in this Exhibit F.  
If Franchisee (or any of Franchisee’s Equity Owners) at any time during the Term determine 
to sell or transfer for consideration this Agreement, the Hotel or all or substantially all of its assets or a 
Controlling Equity Interest in Franchisee or its Controlling Equity Owner (except (i) transfers to or 
among Franchisee’s Equity Owners as of the Effective Date, (ii) transfers described in Section 14.7 of 
this Agreement, (iii) any bona fide, third party financing obtained by Franchisee, or (iv) an involuntary or 
forced sale, assignment, or transfer, such as a foreclosure sale, a deed in lieu of foreclosure transaction, or a 
sale ordered by a bankruptcy court or other court, which transactions are not subject to this Exhibit F or 
the rights of Franchisor in this Exhibit F), then Franchisee must first give Franchisor the opportunity to 
acquire those rights (the “Offered Rights”) by delivering written notice to Franchisor. Franchisee’s notice 
must contain a summary of the material terms and conditions of the proposed sale or transfer, including 
(without limitation) the proposed purchase price, the earnest money, the due diligence period, the closing 
date, and the terms of any financing Franchisee or its Affiliate will provide for the proposed purchase 
price (the “Offer Terms”). The Offer Terms must relate exclusively to the Offered Rights and not to any 
other assets or rights. 
 
Franchisor will then have thirty (30) days after receiving the Offer Terms to notify Franchisee in 
writing whether Franchisor elects to acquire the Offered Rights on the Offer Terms, provided that if 
Franchisor timely exercises the right of first offer, Franchisor and Franchisee will promptly execute a 
purchase and sale agreement (the “Purchase Agreement”), in form and substance reasonably acceptable to 
both parties, which will provide (1) Franchisor may substitute cash for any form of non-cash payment 
proposed in the Offer Terms (such as ownership interests in an entity) and may elect to pay the net present 
value of any payments to be made over time; (2) Franchisor must be afforded the opportunity to conduct 
customary due diligence on the Offered Rights within the due diligence period set forth in the Offer Terms; 
and (3) Franchisor must receive, and Franchisee or  Franchisee’s Equity Owners (as appropriate based on the 
proposed seller) agree to make, all customary representations, warranties, and indemnities in 
Franchisor’s purchase, including representations and warranties as to ownership and condition of and title 
to assets; liens and encumbrances on assets; validity of contracts and agreements; liabilities affecting the 
assets, contingent or otherwise; and indemnities for all actions, events, and conditions that existed or 
occurred in connection with the Hotel or Franchisee’s business before the closing. 
 
If Franchisor timely exercises the right of first offer, the closing will take place at a location 
selected by Franchisor on the closing date set forth in the Offer Terms. Franchisor and Franchisee will 
sign the Purchase Agreement and such other documents, including deeds, affidavits, transfers and 
assignments, and any other documents necessary or appropriate for the sale or transfer of the Offered Rights. 
Franchisee must satisfy all liens, mortgages, and/or encumbrances on the Hotel. Franchisor and Franchisee 
will pay closing costs in accordance with the Offer Terms and customary allocations of closing costs in the 
market in which the Hotel is located.

(30)-day period described above, then Franchisee thereafter may offer the Offered Rights on terms no more 
materially favorable to that party (the buyer) than the Offer Terms.  However, unless this Agreement
