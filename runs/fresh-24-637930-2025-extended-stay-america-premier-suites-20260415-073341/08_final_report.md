## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System Size & Growth**: 49 total Brand Hotels at year-end 2024 (32 company-owned, 17 franchised). Rapid growth from 25 hotels in 2022. Pipeline of 41 signed-but-not-opened franchise agreements with 12 projected new openings in next fiscal year. Zero terminations, non-renewals, or transfers to date.

2. **Entity Structure & Ownership**: ESH Strategies Franchise LLC is a Delaware LLC wholly owned through ESH Hospitality Strategies LLC by Eagle Strategies Holdings LLC, which is equally owned by Starwood Capital Group (SCG) and Blackstone Inc. (BX). The franchisor has never directly owned or operated a Brand Hotel — operations are through affiliate ESH Hospitality, Inc. (572 hotels across 3 ESA brands) and ESA Management LLC.

3. **Ownership History**: Formed September 2010. Extended Stay America, Inc. sold its interest to Eagle Strategies on June 16, 2021. Began offering ESA franchises in August 2017, launched Premier Suites brand in March 2021, added Select Suites brand in September 2022.

4. **Investment Range**: New construction (125 rooms): $10,058,979–$15,123,386 excluding land. Conversion (100 rooms): $322,659–$5,590,465 excluding land. Land cost not estimated due to "multiple variables." Prototype requires at least 2.0 acres.

5. **Fee Burden**: Royalty 5.5% + System Services 4.5% = 10.0% of Gross Room Revenue. Additional Digital Marketing Commission of 5% on paid-digital-channel GRR effective 2025. Agoda Marketing Fee of 0.6% on Agoda bookings. No renewal; 20-year term effectively locks in fee structure.

6. **Item 19 Highlights**: 41 Brand Hotels reported combined data. Average occupancy 77.4% (median 79.4%), average ADR $93.84 (median $93.16), average RevPAR $72.60 (median $72.34). Extended stay occupancy 54.8%. Nearly half of room nights are 30+ day stays. No cost, expense, or EBITDA data disclosed — prospective franchisees have no FDD-based visibility into profitability.

7. **Bottom-Quartile Risk**: With 49% of hotels at/exceeding the average ADR ($93.84) and RevPAR ($72.60), bottom-half hotels are operating with relatively thin revenue per room. Without cost data, profitability for below-median performers cannot be assessed from the FDD alone.

8. **Financial Health**: Franchisor entity turned net profitable in 2024 ($1.5M net income on $25.8M revenue, vs. $2.2M net loss in 2023). Total assets $19.2M, cash $9.2M, member's capital $8.2M. No going concern. Heavy related party transactions — $10.4M in costs reimbursed to ESA Management and other affiliates. LLC entity not subject to federal income tax.

9. **Growth Trajectory**: Aggressive franchise growth — from 0 franchised hotels in early 2022 to 17 at year-end 2024, with 41 more signed. This is a young franchise system. The brand was launched in 2021 and franchised only since 2022.

10. **Territory**: Non-exclusive. Territory may be granted but heavily carved out. Sister brands (ESA Suites, ESA Select Suites) can operate in your territory. Franchisor reserves right to own, build, and franchise other brand hotels inside territory.

11. **Renewal**: No renewal right. 20-year term with no right to extend or renew. At term end, franchisee must de-identify.

12. **Disputes**: Arbitration in Charlotte, NC. North Carolina law governs. Jury trial waived. State addenda override venue/law for 12 states.

13. **Litigation**: Two consolidated antitrust class actions alleging algorithmic price-fixing via IDeaS Revenue Solutions software. Company denies allegations and "intends to vigorously defend." Prior class action (guest refund practices) settled for $100K.

14. **Special Risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (North Carolina venue). No noncompete during or after term. Transfer requires general release and new agreement with potentially materially different terms.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

ESH Strategies Franchise LLC is the franchising entity within the Extended Stay America family. It does not operate hotels directly — all hotel operations are conducted by affiliates ESH Hospitality, Inc. (owner) and ESA Management LLC (manager). The trademarks are owned by yet another affiliate, ESH Strategies Branding LLC, and licensed to the franchisor via a Trademark and System License Agreement expiring December 31, 2030 (with franchisee rights surviving through FA term if license terminates).

The ownership chain runs through two major private equity firms — Starwood Capital Group and Blackstone — providing significant financial backing but also creating a complex multi-entity structure with extensive related party transactions.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Initial Franchise Fee: $50,000 (non-refundable; negotiable; prior year $40,000)
- Development Fee: $50,000 × number of hotels (minimum 2)
- Pre-Opening PIP Fee: $2,000 (credited to IFF)
- Extension Fee: $5,000 per 90-day extension
- Pre-Opening Training: $1,900/person (GM Cert) + $1,900/person (New Owner Orientation) + $2,500 (onsite training)
- Project Management Fee: $5,000 + third-party costs
- IT Certification Fee: $2,000–$5,000 (if using unapproved vendor)
- Construction Site Visit Fees: $6,000 (new build, 3 visits) or $2,000/visit (conversion)
- IT Site Visit Fees: $2,000/visit (up to 2)

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Royalty: 5.5% of Gross Room Revenue
- System Services Fee: 4.5% of Gross Room Revenue
- Digital Marketing Commission: 5% of paid-digital GRR (effective 2025)
- Agoda Marketing Fee: 0.6% of GRR per Agoda booking
- Booking Commissions: varies (OTA/GDS pass-throughs)
- PMS: $3.87/room/month ($2.20 license + $1.00 hosting + $0.67 support)
- AHLA Dues: $3.00/room/year (opt-out available)
- Annual Conference: $1,200/attendee (1 mandatory)
- QA Re-inspection: up to $1,000 + expenses
- Remedial Training: $4,000–$6,500
- Guest Complaint Fee: $150 + guest compensation (if not resolved in 72 hours)
- Interest: 1.5%/month on late payments

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Transfer Fee: then-current IFF
- Assumption Fee: $1,500–$5,000 (minority changes)
- Room Addition Fee: $500/room
- Liquidated Damages (pre-opening): $5,000/day for unauthorized opening; rooms × $3,000 for pre-opening termination
- Liquidated Damages (post-opening termination): 5 × annual royalty or pro-rated remaining term

**Total effective fee burden**: The base ongoing fee load is 10.0% of GRR. With the Digital Marketing Commission (5% on paid-digital-channel GRR) and booking commissions, the total effective fee burden could approach 12-14% of GRR depending on channel mix.

### Initial Investment Detail

**New Construction (125 rooms)**: $10,058,979–$15,123,386 excluding land. The largest component is improvements ($8.6M–$13.0M). FF&E adds $640K–$750K, technology $196K–$267K, and OS&E $150K–$230K. The $175K–$250K in additional funds covers only 3 months of operations.

**Conversion (100 rooms)**: $322,659–$5,590,465 excluding land. The wide range reflects variance between light-touch conversions of existing extended-stay properties (with kitchens) versus heavy renovations. The low-end assumes the property is already owned with existing insurance, licenses, and operational cash flow.

**Land not estimated**: The FDD explicitly states "given the multiple variables associated with the cost of real property, we do not have a valid means for estimation." The prototype requires at least 2.0 acres for a 125-room, 4-story building.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition and Carve-Outs
A territory may be granted based on the franchisee's financial and operational capability. If granted, the franchisor agrees not to open (or authorize opening) a Brand Hotel (Premier Suites only) within the territory during the term, provided no default.

**Critical carve-outs and reserved rights:**
1. Protection applies ONLY to the "Brand" (Extended Stay America Premier Suites). It does NOT prevent ESA Suites or ESA Select Suites hotels in the territory.
2. Franchisor may own, lease, construct, or renovate a Brand Hotel inside the territory — just not open it to the public during the term.
3. Network Hotels (different brands) may be placed anywhere inside the territory.
4. Existing Brand Hotels at the FA effective date continue and may be renewed, expanded, or replaced with a larger hotel.
5. Franchisor may enter the territory with a Brand Hotel starting 6 months before FA expiration.
6. Franchisor reserves the right to acquire or be acquired by another hotel chain/system, which could bring competing properties into the territory.

### Channel Restrictions
- Franchisee must exclusively use the franchisor's Reservation Service for hotel bookings.
- Franchisee cannot enter into own OTA agreements — must use franchisor's negotiated OTA contracts.
- Must participate in and honor all loyalty/discount/promotional programs.
- No digital or email marketing permitted without franchisor approval (currently blanket prohibition).
- May use catalog sales, telemarketing, and other direct marketing.

### Sister Brand Competition
The franchisor also franchises ESA Suites and ESA Select Suites brands. Additionally, affiliates may offer franchises under the Yotel brand and potentially the Field & Stream brand. All of these may operate in the franchisee's territory. Conflicts between the franchisee and these other brands are resolved entirely at the franchisor's discretion — there is no formal conflict resolution procedure.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Term**: 20 years for new-build properties. For conversions, the term is determined by the franchisor based on current age, condition, and location.
- **Renewal**: None. No right to renew or extend. This is a significant departure from many hotel franchise systems that offer at least one renewal term.
- **Development Agreement**: Typically 2-6 years depending on number of hotels.

### Transfer Provisions
- All transfers require franchisor consent.
- **Transfer fee**: Then-current initial franchise fee (currently $50,000).
- **Conditions**: All debts paid, PIP fee paid, general release signed by franchisee and owners, transferee submits new application, transferee signs then-current FA which **may contain materially different terms**, transferee must renovate to current Standards.
- **Assumption Fee**: $1,500–$5,000 for minority ownership changes that don't result in change of control.
- **Right of First Offer**: Only for "strategic markets" (Exhibit F, requires separate execution). Not a blanket right.

### Termination and Death/Disability
- **Curable defaults**: 30 days to cure (10 days for monetary).
- **Non-curable (immediate termination)**: Bankruptcy, cease operating under Brand, asset attachment, contesting marks, felony conviction, false records, adverse action to System, public safety threat, 3 defaults in 12 months, 2 similar failures in 6 months.
- **Liquidated damages**: Substantial. Pre-opening: rooms × $3,000. Post-opening varies by time remaining; for early terminations with >5 years remaining, the penalty is 5 × prior-year royalty (at full rates, even if temporary reductions were granted).
- **Death/disability**: Must transfer within required timeframe meeting all Section 14.3 conditions, or franchisor may terminate.
- **HTA**: Franchisor can terminate HTA at any time on 10 days' notice without cause.

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **None**. No noncompete during term or post-term. This is unusual and favorable for franchisees.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: All disputes except indemnification, equitable relief, and enforcement of awards.
- **Venue**: Charlotte, North Carolina (franchisor headquarters).
- **Governing Law**: North Carolina.
- **Jury Trial**: Waived.
- **State overrides**: 12 states have addenda that may modify venue, governing law, or other provisions (see Section I below).

### Litigation History
**Pending**: Two consolidated antitrust class actions (In re Extended Stay Hotel Antitrust Litigation, Case No. 24-cv-6324, N.D. Cal.) alleging Sherman Act violations through algorithmic price coordination via IDeaS Revenue Solutions software. Filed July/September 2024, transferred to N.D. Cal. December 2024. Briefing stayed pending court's decision in related Dai case. Company "denies the allegations and intends to vigorously defend."

This is a significant pending matter. If the plaintiffs prevail, it could affect revenue management practices across the ESA system and potentially expose franchisees to operational changes in how room rates are set.

**Prior**: Brittian v. ESA Management LLC (W.D.N.C.) — class action regarding guest refund practices. Settled July 2024 for $100,000 (individual claims dismissed with prejudice, class claims without prejudice).

### Critical Caveats
- **No cost or profitability data**: The FPR provides only top-line metrics (occupancy, ADR, RevPAR). No operating expenses, EBITDA, or net operating income is disclosed. Prospective franchisees cannot assess profitability from the FDD alone.
- **Combined data**: Owned and franchised hotels are combined. The 32 owned hotels (managed by affiliate ESA Management) may have different cost structures than independently-managed franchised hotels.
- **ADR relatively modest**: At $93.84 average ADR, this is a value-tier extended-stay offering. With a 10%+ fee load on GRR, the margin compression on below-median performers could be significant.
- **Small population**: 41 hotels is a small sample. Individual market conditions vary significantly.
- **Substantiation**: Written substantiation available on reasonable request.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Profitability Turnaround**: The franchisor entity swung from a $2.2M net loss in 2023 to $1.5M net income in 2024. Revenue grew 40% ($18.4M → $25.8M) driven by franchise system growth. However, $2.1M of 2024 "other revenue" came from contract termination fees, which are non-recurring.

2. **Related Party Dominance**: $6.6M in G&A cost reimbursements and $3.8M in system service cost reimbursements paid to ESA Management LLC — together totaling $10.4M, or 43% of total expenses. The emphasis of matter in the audit opinion specifically flags these related party transactions.

3. **Trademark License**: 5% of franchise royalties paid to ESH Strategies Branding LLC ($429K in 2024). This is a related-party cost that reduces franchisor profitability.

4. **Deferred Revenue**: $4.8M in contract liabilities, mostly non-current. These represent initial franchise fees being recognized over the 20-year term. Growing as the system signs new franchisees.

5. **Asset Composition**: Assets are primarily cash ($9.2M, 48% of total assets) and intangibles ($5.4M franchise agreements + $2.2M contract acquisition costs). No tangible property — this is a pure franchising entity.

6. **Due to Affiliates**: Declined from $4.8M to $2.8M, primarily driven by repayment of $1.6M to ESH REIT and reduction in amounts owed to ESA Management.

7. **Brand Launch Costs**: $1.6M accrued liability remains from the 2022 ESA Select Suites brand launch — payments to existing franchisees for area-of-protection exemptions, being satisfied through fee reductions.

8. **IT Equipment Leases**: Sales-type leases with franchisees for IT equipment (5-year term) fully maturing in 2025. Current balance $260K.
