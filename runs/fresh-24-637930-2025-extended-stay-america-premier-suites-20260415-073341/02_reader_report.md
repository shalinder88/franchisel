## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Entity & Ownership**: ESH Strategies Franchise LLC, a Delaware LLC, wholly owned subsidiary of ESH Hospitality Strategies LLC. Ultimate owners are Starwood Capital Group (SCG) and Blackstone Inc. (BX) through Eagle Strategies Holdings LLC.
2. **Brand History**: Began franchising ESA hotels in 2017; launched Premier Suites brand in March 2021 as an upgraded tier. Also offers ESA Suites and ESA Select Suites brands.
3. **System Size**: 49 total Brand Hotels at year-end 2024 (32 company-owned, 17 franchised). Growing rapidly — from 25 in 2022 to 49 in 2024. 41 additional franchise agreements signed but not yet opened.
4. **Investment Range**: New construction (125 rooms): $10.1M–$15.1M excluding land. Conversion (100 rooms): $323K–$5.6M excluding land.
5. **Fee Burden**: Royalty 5.5% + System Services 4.5% = 10.0% of Gross Room Revenue. Plus Digital Marketing Commission of 5% on paid-digital-channel GRR (starting 2025) and Agoda Marketing Fee of 0.6% on Agoda bookings.
6. **Item 19 Highlights**: 41 Brand Hotels reported. Average occupancy 77.4%, ADR $93.84, RevPAR $72.60. Extended stay occupancy 54.8%. No cost/expense or profitability data disclosed.
7. **Term & Renewal**: 20-year term (new build). No renewal right. No right to extend.
8. **Territory**: Non-exclusive. Territory may be granted but heavily reserved. Sister brands (ESA Suites, ESA Select Suites) may operate in your territory.
9. **Noncompete**: None during term or post-term.
10. **Dispute Resolution**: Arbitration in Charlotte, NC. NC law governs. Jury trial waiver.
11. **Litigation**: Two consolidated antitrust class actions alleging algorithmic price-fixing via IDeaS revenue management software. One prior class action (Brittian) settled for $100,000.
12. **Financial Health**: Franchisor entity turned net profitable in 2024 ($1.5M net income vs. $2.2M loss in 2023). Total assets $19.2M. Heavy related party transactions.
13. **Growth Trajectory**: Strong growth — 41 signed but unopened franchise agreements; 12 projected new franchised hotels in next fiscal year.
14. **Transfer**: Requires franchisor consent, then-current IFF as transfer fee, general release, new FA with potentially materially different terms.
15. **Special Risk**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (NC venue).

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **No exclusive territory** guaranteed. Territory may be granted based on financial/operational capability.
- If territory granted, franchisor will not (and will not authorize) opening a **Brand Hotel** (Premier Suites only) within territory during term, provided no default.
- **Critical carve-outs**:
  - Protection applies only to the "Brand" (Premier Suites). Does NOT prevent ESA Suites or ESA Select Suites hotels in territory.
  - Franchisor and affiliates may own, lease, construct, renovate Brand Hotels inside territory — just not open to public for business during term.
  - Franchisor may franchise/operate Network Hotels (different brands) anywhere in territory.
  - Existing Brand Hotels at FA effective date continue and may be renewed/expanded/replaced.
  - Franchisor may enter territory with Brand Hotels starting 6 months before FA expiration.
  - Franchisor reserves right to acquire/be acquired by other hotel chains.
- No right to relocate site.
- No option/first refusal for additional hotels (except Development Agreement).
- No restrictions on franchisor soliciting/accepting reservations from guests in territory.
- Franchisor may use internet, catalog, telemarketing in territory.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
- Marks owned by ESH Strategies Branding LLC and licensed to franchisor via Trademark and System License Agreement (expires 12/31/2030).
- If license agreement terminated, franchisee's right to use marks continues for FA term.

- No pending infringement/opposition proceedings.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
- Owner or Management Company must manage hotel (subject to franchisor approval).
- Franchisor can withdraw management company approval at any time.
- Franchisor can require Management Company if franchisee lacks experience.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on What Franchisee May Sell
- Must operate 24/7 per Standards.
- Cannot affiliate hotel with another franchise system, brand, or reservation service.

- Must honor all reservation service bookings with priority.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Term**: 20 years (new build). Conversion terms set by franchisor based on age/condition/location.
- **Renewal/Extension**: None. No right to renew or extend.
- **Development Agreement**: Typically 2-6 years.
- **Hotel Technology Agreement**: Initial 3-year term with automatic 1-year renewals, coterminous with FA.

### Termination
**By Franchisee**: Only if Hotel damaged by fire/casualty exceeding coverage limits, or grounds permitted by state law.

**By Franchisor Without Cause**: Hotel taken/condemned/destroyed. HTA terminable on 10 days' notice.

**By Franchisor With Cause (Curable)**: Most defaults — 30 days to cure (10 days for monetary).

**By Franchisor (Non-Curable/Immediate)**: Liquidation/dissolution, bankruptcy, cease operating under Brand, asset attachment, contesting marks, false records, felony conviction, adverse action to System, threat to public safety, 3 defaults in 12 months, 2 failures of similar obligations in 6 months.

### Transfer
- All transfers (contract, assets, site, ownership) require franchisor approval (except minority changes).
- Transfer fee: then-current IFF.
- Conditions: all debts paid, PIP fee paid, general release signed, transferee submits new application, transferee signs then-current FA (may have materially different terms), transferee must renovate to current Standards.
- Development Agreement transfer: similar conditions.
- HTA: consent withheld at sole and absolute discretion.

### Death/Disability
- Must transfer to third party (may include heirs) meeting Section 14.3 conditions.
- If transfer fails within required timeframe, franchisor may terminate.

### Right of First Offer
- Only for strategic markets (Exhibit F, must be signed).
- Applies to transfers of Hotel or controlling equity interest.

### Post-Termination Obligations
- Cease operating as Brand Hotel and using System/Marks.
- De-identify hotel (franchisor may inspect).
- Change phone listings, internet registrations, etc.
- Pay all amounts owed within 10 days.
- Pay liquidated damages within 10 days.
- Return Standards and confidential materials.

### Noncompete
- **None** — no noncompete during term or post-term.

### Modification
- FA: written agreement by both parties. Franchisor may unilaterally change Standards.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: All disputes except indemnification, equitable relief, enforcement of awards.
- **Venue**: Charlotte, North Carolina (franchisor HQ).
- **Governing Law**: North Carolina (subject to state addenda overrides).
- **Jury Trial**: Waived.
- **Integration**: Only FA/DA/HTA terms binding; nothing disclaims FDD representations.
