## Section A: Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and structure**: Great Clips operates the largest haircare franchise system in North America with 4,439 franchised salons as of December 31, 2024, and zero company-owned outlets. The system is 100% franchised. Great Clips, Inc. is a Minnesota C corporation (converted from S corporation January 1, 2019) incorporated in 1982. The company began franchising in 1983. It has no parents, predecessors, or affiliates. The average franchisee who has operated for 5+ years owns 8 salons (median 5).

2. **Ownership and leadership**: Chairman Ray Barton has been with the company since 1983, Vice Chair Rhoda Olsen since 1984, and CEO/President Robert Goggins since 2007. The leadership team of 34 named officers and directors is deeply experienced, with many having 10-25+ years of tenure. The company is privately held with stock repurchase agreements based on EBITDA multiples.

3. **Investment range**: The total initial investment for a single franchise is $187,800–$419,900, including a $20,000 initial franchise fee and $5,000 MDAF contribution. A typical salon is 900-1,200 sq ft in a shopping center. The Three Star Program offers three franchise agreements for $35,000 + $5,000 MDAF ($40,000 total vs. $75,000 for three separate agreements). The Master Development Agreement requires $4,000 per salon development fee plus $6,000 initial franchise fee per salon.

4. **Fee burden**: The ongoing fee burden is substantial. The 6% royalty and 5% ad fund contribution alone consume 11% of gross sales. Adding the recommended 1-3% local advertising, $250/month software license, $18.99/month network services, $750/year recruiting tech fee, $200/year training fee, local co-op dues (~$100/month), insurance, and various technology fees, the all-in ongoing cost burden approaches 14-16% of gross sales plus fixed monthly costs of approximately $450-500+.

5. **Item 19 performance**: Average total sales across 4,147 eligible salons were $399,179 (median $382,316) for the 2024 period. Of 2,175 reporting salons that provided expense data, average operating cash flow was $82,637 (19.99% of sales; median $75,851). However, 1,972 salons (47.5% of the eligible pool) did not report expense data, and these non-reporting salons skewed lower-performing. If included, Great Clips acknowledges the median would decrease by 2.9% and cash flow by "a larger percent." The bottom tier (<$250k sales, 9.01% of reporting salons) averaged negative cash flow of -$3,635.

6. **Bottom-quartile risk**: Salons in the <$250k range had average expenses exceeding 100% of sales (101.74%), producing negative cash flow. The lowest actual operating cash flow was -$116,986. The lowest total sales salon earned only $37,920 (closed for 8 of 12 months but remained "eligible"). There is meaningful downside risk for underperforming locations.

7. **Financial health of franchisor**: Great Clips is financially strong. Total assets of $135.2M, net earnings of $22.3M, total equity of $43.8M (up from $9.7M two years ago). Long-term debt declining ($14.8M from $26.6M in 2022). Strong operating cash flows of $17.6M. No going-concern risk. Clean unqualified audit opinion. The company operates without external equity financing and has been redeeming shareholder stock ($2.6M in 2024, $5.1M in 2023).

8. **Growth trajectory**: Net salon growth has been minimal — declining 20 in 2022, flat in 2023, and growing 12 in 2024. However, 965 signed franchise agreements remain unopened and 90 new outlets are projected for the next fiscal year. The system appears to be in a steady-state phase with incremental growth. Transfer volume is significant (207 in 2024), reflecting active secondary market activity.

9. **Territory and encroachment risk**: The 3/4-mile protected area is modest. Non-traditional location exclusions (airports, universities, big-box stores, military bases, etc.) create potential encroachment vectors within the protected area. Great Clips reserves broad rights to compete under different brands and to distribute branded products through alternative channels including Amazon.

10. **Renewal risk**: There is no right to renew. Renewal is at Great Clips' sole discretion. If renewed, the franchisee must sign the then-current franchise agreement, which may have "materially different terms and conditions." This is a significant contractual risk.

11. **Dispute resolution**: All disputes must be arbitrated in Minneapolis, MN, with litigation in Hennepin County, MN. This is a Special Risk flagged on the cover. State addenda may provide relief.

12. **Gag clause**: Great Clips has signed confidentiality clauses with current/former franchisees restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience. This is explicitly disclosed.

13. **No litigation, no bankruptcy**: Clean on both counts.

14. **AI/ML restriction**: A notable modern provision prohibits franchisees from using any confidential or proprietary information for machine learning, AI model training, or generative AI platforms without express written consent.

## Section B: Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Great Clips, Inc. is a Minnesota corporation incorporated July 12, 1982. It has no parents, predecessors, or affiliates. The company operates as a pure franchisor — it does not own or operate any salons. It licenses the operation of GREAT CLIPS salons offering haircare and personal grooming services and products.

Three franchise structures are available: (1) Single Franchise Agreement — standard single-unit license at $20,000 IFF; (2) Three Star Program Agreement — lease-signing incentive for three franchise agreements at $35,000 total program fee (vs. $60,000 for three separate IFFs), with a 24-month completion window; and (3) Master Development Agreement — commitment to open a minimum number of salons in an Exclusive DMA at $4,000 per salon development fee plus $6,000 IFF per salon.

Additional programs include the VetFran Program ($5,000 IFF rebate for qualified veterans, first salon only), the Deferral Program (IFF deferred for salons within the protected area of an existing salon), and the Expediter Program (up to $20,000 rebate for third-party site acquisition costs).

### Fee Stack

The ongoing fee burden is multi-layered and substantial:

**Percentage-based fees (on Gross Sales, biweekly via ACH)**:
- Continuing Franchise Fee (royalty): 6%
- Continuing Advertising Contribution to Ad Fund: 5%
- Subtotal: 11% of gross sales

**Fixed annual/monthly fees per salon**:
- Software License (ICS Styleware): $250/month ($3,000/year)
- Network & Hardware Managed Services: $18.99/month ($227.88/year)
- Recruiting Technology Fee: $750/year
- Training Fee: $200/year
- Local Co-op Dues: ~$100/month ($1,200/year average)
- Mobile Device Management: $54/year per iPad
- Router Maintenance: $312/year per router
- Payment Terminal Warranty: $59.76/year per terminal
- Heartland Merchant Protection: $5/month ($60/year)
- Insurance: $1,500-$3,000/year
- Subtotal fixed: approximately $7,400-$9,000+/year

**Event-driven fees**:
- Renewal Fee: $1,750/salon (CPI-adjusted)
- Assignment Fee: $1,500/salon (CPI-adjusted)
- Franchisee Program Fees: $345-$550/event
- Salon Upgrades: $20,000-$80,000 every 7-10 years
- Salon Ongoing Maintenance: $3,000-$9,000/year
- Late Charges: 14% per annum interest
- Audit/Inspection: Cost of audit if discrepancy ≥5% or underpayment ≥2%

For a salon generating $400,000 in gross sales (near the system average), the royalty is $24,000/year and the ad fund is $20,000/year, plus approximately $7,500+ in fixed fees, plus recommended local advertising of $4,000-$12,000. Total fee load: approximately $55,500-$63,500 (13.9%-15.9% of sales).

### Initial Investment

The total initial investment for a single franchise under a Franchise Agreement ranges from $187,800 to $419,900. The largest variable cost component is leasehold improvements ($70,000-$200,000), which depends on the condition of the space. Fixtures, signage, and furnishings add $40,000-$55,000. Grand opening advertising is a significant requirement at $20,000-$25,000. Additional working capital of $20,000-$60,000 is needed for the first 3-6 months (excluding owner's salary).

Great Clips does not offer direct or indirect financing. The company is registered on the Franchise Registry for streamlined SBA lending.

## Section C: Supplier Control, Operations, Technology, Training

### Supplier Restrictions

Franchisees must purchase approximately 90-95% of establishment goods and 80-85% of ongoing goods from Great Clips or designated suppliers. Key sole-source suppliers include ICS (POS software), Heartland (credit card processing), and Foundation Technologies (network/hardware). Great Clips sells lobby items, work stations, signage, and other salon fixtures directly.

Required haircare brands include MATRIX TOTAL RESULTS, AMERICAN CREW, BOSLEY MD, SEXY HAIR, TIGI BEDHEAD, and JOHN PAUL MITCHELL SYSTEMS. Great Clips derives a 6% royalty on exclusive brand haircare products sold through Salon Innovations, Inc. ($598,165 in 2024).

In 2024, Great Clips' total revenue from all sources was $115,242,065, of which $16,270,369 (14.1%) came from required purchases. Supplier appointment requires a 90-day approval window. Franchisees may request appointment of new suppliers but the cost of evaluation may be charged to the franchisee. Great Clips does not have purchasing cooperatives.

### Technology Stack

The technology infrastructure is tightly controlled:
- **POS**: ICS Styleware iPad ($250/month) — sole designated supplier
- **Online Check-In (Net Check-In)**: Customer-facing wait time and remote check-in
- **Styleware Vantage**: Real-time metrics app for franchisees
- **Salondata.com**: Online reporting tool
- **Hardware**: Apple iPads through Foundation Technologies (sole integrator)
- **Payment Processing**: Heartland via Ingenico Lane/5000 (transitioning to Verifone P630 by June 2026)
- **Network**: Foundation Technologies managed services ($18.99/month)

Great Clips has independent, unimpeded access to all salon sales, financial, marketing, customer, and operational data. All such information is proprietary to Great Clips. The franchisee receives only a limited license to use this data during the franchise term.

### Training Program

The QuickConnect Training Program consists of 49.56 hours of classroom training and 13 hours of on-the-job training:
- Online learning via GREAT CLIPS University (Modules 1-12)
- Building Your Legacy: 12.5 hours in-person in Minneapolis, MN (2 days)
- Great Clips Academy: 4.87 hours online + 3.5 hours live virtual
- LEADS for Management Staff: 5.52 hours online + 18 hours live virtual (6 × 3-hour sessions)
- In-salon training: 13 hours with existing franchisee

Training must be completed within 180 days of signing the franchise agreement. The annual training fee is $200/year/salon. Franchisees pay all wages, travel, and expenses for themselves and their staff. Refresher training may be required at renewal at Great Clips' discretion.

### Advertising Fund

The Ad Fund operates on a 5% contribution from all franchisees (biweekly). In 2024, spending breakdown was: 80.0% media, 2.7% production, 18.7% other (including 5% admin fee to Great Clips = $4,347,880). Total expenses were 101.8% of revenue, meaning the fund spent slightly more than it collected. The fund is audited annually. Franchisees may not develop their own advertising materials and are prohibited from independent internet advertising without permission. A Marketing and Advisory Review Council (MARC) provides franchisee input, but Great Clips retains all decision-making authority.

## Section D: Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory

The protected area is a 3/4-mile radius from the primary customer entrance (1/10 mile in densely populated areas). This is a relatively small territory for a high-density retail concept. The protected area does not constitute an exclusive territory.

### Non-Traditional Location Exclusions

Non-traditional locations are excluded from the protected area, creating encroachment risk. These include: military bases, airports/transportation terminals, college/university campuses, grocery/club/big-box stores, amusement/theme parks, casinos, and sporting/community/racing events. Great Clips may develop, license, or franchise these locations within a franchisee's protected area (unless covered by an Exclusive MDA).

### Channel Asymmetry

Great Clips and its affiliates may: (1) operate under different trademarks selling similar services/products at any location including the protected area; (2) distribute products including branded products through alternative channels (e.g., Amazon) at any location or over the Internet without obligation to franchisees. Meanwhile, franchisees are prohibited from using alternative distribution channels (Internet, catalog, telemarketing) without prior written consent. This creates a significant channel asymmetry favoring the franchisor.

### MDA Protection

The Master Development Agreement provides an Exclusive DMA during the term if the franchisee is in compliance with the development schedule. This is the strongest territorial protection available. However, MDA availability is "extremely limited."

## Section E: Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The initial franchise term is 10 years from the date of salon opening (or balance of term for acquired salons). There is no right to renew — renewal is at Great Clips' sole discretion. If renewed, the term is an additional 10 years. Renewal conditions include: compliance with the franchise agreement, upgrade to then-current salon standards, payment of $1,750 renewal fee (CPI-adjusted), possible re-attendance at training, and execution of a general release and the then-current franchise agreement.

The "materially different terms" risk is explicitly stated: the renewal franchise agreement may have materially different terms and conditions than the original. This means royalty rates, ad fund rates, technology requirements, and other obligations could all change at renewal. Given the 10-year term, a franchisee who invests $200,000-$400,000 in leasehold improvements faces renewal uncertainty.

### Termination

Great Clips may not terminate without cause. Curable defaults (non-payment: 7-day cure; all others: 30-day cure) include system standards violations, financial misrepresentation, and unauthorized products/services. Non-curable defaults include impairment of Marks/System, insolvency, assignment without consent, criminal conviction, material misuse of customer data, and material misrepresentation. Cross-default provisions mean a breach under any franchise agreement with Great Clips can trigger termination of all agreements.

Repeated breaches are non-curable: Great Clips may terminate on 30 days' written notice for "repeated breaches" or a material breach that "cannot be cured due to the nature of the breach." This gives the franchisor significant leverage.

### Transfer

All transfers require Great Clips' prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld). The assignment fee is $1,500 (CPI-adjusted). Assignees must meet current qualification guidelines, sign the new form franchise agreement (for the balance of the term), sign a general release and guaranty, and communicate in English. Great Clips has a 60-day right of first refusal on any proposed sale, with the ability to match on price and terms or cash equivalent. If the offer changes, a new 60-day ROFR period begins. The transaction must close within 120 days of Great Clips declining.

Upon death or disability, heirs/executors must apply for consent and pay the assignment fee. No assignment is permitted if the salon is not in operation (except when selling all salons and leaving the system entirely).

### Noncompete

The noncompete is broad. During the term, you and your spouse may not be involved in any haircare/grooming business, beauty/barber/cosmetology school, or hair product sales at any location or over the Internet. Post-term, the restriction narrows to 1 year within 5 miles of any GREAT CLIPS salon (not just yours — any in the system). "Retail Haircare Business" is broadly defined to include any business offering services on the GREAT CLIPS price board, related products, blow drying/styling, braiding, treatments, or perms.

### Dispute Resolution

All disputes must be arbitrated in Minneapolis, MN, with limited exceptions. All legal proceedings must be held in Hennepin County, MN. Governing law is the state where the salon is located (a franchisee-favorable provision). State addenda may override venue and arbitration requirements. A general release in favor of Great Clips is required upon termination or non-renewal.

### Litigation History

No litigation is required to be disclosed. This is a clean record for a system of 4,439+ salons.

## Section F: Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Table 1: All Eligible Salons (4,147 of 4,439)

Great Clips provides robust financial performance data. Table 1 covers 4,147 salons that were eligible to be open for the entire 2024 period. The 292 excluded salons were either not approved/authorized to be open for the full year or changed ownership during the year.

Average total sales were $399,179, with 44.78% of salons attaining or exceeding the average. The median was $382,316. Service sales ($388,522 average) represent 97.3% of revenue, with product sales ($10,657 average) contributing only 2.7%. The range is enormous: $37,920 (a salon closed for 8 months) to $1,099,873. 103 salons permanently closed in 2024; none had been open less than 12 months.

### Tables 2 & 3: Operating Cash Flow (2,175 Reporting Salons)

The operating cash flow data is based on 2,175 of the 4,147 eligible salons (52.5%) that provided sufficient expense data. The 1,972 non-reporting salons introduce significant survivorship/selection bias: 1,138 had total sales below the reporting salon median versus only 834 above. Great Clips acknowledges that if all non-reporting salons were included, the median total sales would decrease by 2.9% and net operating cash flow would decrease by "a larger percent."

Average total sales for reporting salons: $413,389. Average operating cash flow: $82,637 (19.99% of sales). Median operating cash flow: $75,851. Operating cash flow excludes income taxes, depreciation, amortization, and capital expenditure reserves — it is a pre-tax, pre-depreciation measure.

The sales range breakdown reveals a steep operating leverage curve:
- Salons below $250k (9.01%): Average negative cash flow of -$3,635
- Salons $250-300k (11.86%): Cash flow of $21,875 (7.90%)
- Salons $350-400k (15.72%): Cash flow of $69,565 (18.51%)
- Salons $500-600k (13.29%): Cash flow of $140,420 (25.77%)
- Salons above $600k (10.25%): Cash flow of $193,727 (27.98%)

The breakeven point appears to be approximately $250,000 in annual sales. Below that level, salons are operating at a loss on a cash-flow basis.

### Critical Caveats

1. **Non-reporting bias**: Nearly half the eligible salons did not report. The non-reporting pool skews lower. Actual system-wide economics are likely worse than the reported averages.
2. **Cash basis**: Most franchisees use cash vs. accrual accounting, which may create timing differences.
3. **Canadian conversion**: Canadian dollar amounts converted at 0.73 rate.
4. **Unaudited**: Neither Great Clips nor its auditors independently verified the POS data or franchisee financial statements.
5. **Operating cash flow definition**: Excludes taxes, depreciation, amortization, and capital reserves. True owner earnings would be lower.
6. **New salon performance**: "Newly opened salons tend to have average sales and cash flows significantly below" the reported averages, especially in markets with few existing salons.

## Section G: Item 20 — Outlet Data

### Multi-Year Trajectory

The Great Clips system has been essentially flat over three years: from 4,447 at the start of 2022 to 4,439 at the end of 2024 — a net decline of 8 salons. Openings of 116, 98, and 115 have been roughly offset by exits of 136, 98, and 103. The "ceased operations - other reasons" column (128, 89, 98) includes salons closed pending relocation, making it difficult to distinguish true permanent closures from strategic relocations. In 2024, 14 salons closed and subsequently relocated/reopened within the same fiscal year.

### Transfer Activity

Transfer volume has been significant: 325 in 2022, 164 in 2023, and 207 in 2024. This represents active secondary market trading. Indiana stands out with 82 transfers in 2022 and 37 in 2024, suggesting large multi-unit portfolio sales. In 3 of 207 transfers in 2024, Great Clips exercised its ROFR and simultaneously transferred the salons to a new owner.

### Terminations and Non-Renewals

Termination and non-renewal numbers are very low relative to system size: 4 terminations and 1 non-renewal in 2024 across 4,439 salons. This suggests Great Clips exercises termination sparingly. Zero salons were reacquired by the franchisor in any year.

### Projected Growth

965 franchise agreements have been signed but outlets not yet opened. 90 new franchised outlets are projected for the next fiscal year, with zero company-owned. The pipeline of signed-but-not-opened agreements (965) is substantial and suggests ongoing development momentum despite the flat system count.

### Gag Clause

Great Clips has signed confidentiality clauses with current or former franchisees during the last three fiscal years, "restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with Great Clips." Prospective franchisees should be aware that not all current/former franchisees will be able to communicate freely.

## Section H: Item 21 — Financial Statements

### Auditor and Opinion

The financial statements were audited by an independent auditor based in Minneapolis, MN (firm name not captured from text layer — letterhead appears image-only). The auditor issued an unqualified (clean) opinion on the financial statements for all three years ended December 31, 2024, 2023, and 2022. There is no going-concern language. The audit report is dated March 26, 2025.

### Balance Sheet Analysis

Total assets grew from $123.9M (2022) to $135.2M (2024), primarily driven by increased property and equipment. Cash and cash equivalents declined from $45.5M to $39.8M. Transportation equipment surged from $20.0M to $28.7M, suggesting a significant fleet investment (likely mobile salon units or training vehicles). Computer equipment and software decreased from $19.6M to $17.6M due to depreciation.

Operating lease right-of-use assets continued to decline ($8.5M → $6.0M → $4.2M), reflecting the winding down of training facility leases. Goodwill from two historical franchisee acquisitions was fully amortized in 2024 (original gross value $4.6M).

Total shareholders' equity improved dramatically from ($12.3M) deficit at end of 2021 to $9.7M in 2022 to $24.6M in 2023 to $43.8M in 2024 — a swing of $56.1M in three years, driven primarily by retained earnings growth.

Unearned initial franchise fees (current + long-term) totaled $21.2M at December 31, 2024, representing deferred revenue from franchise fees being amortized over 12 years.

### Income Statement Analysis

Total franchise revenue grew from $179.8M (2022) to $202.7M (2024), a 12.7% increase. Continuing franchise fee revenue (the royalty stream) grew from $100.3M to $112.8M, reflecting both system growth and per-salon revenue increases. The Ad Fund operates as a pass-through: contributions and expenses are equal ($87.5M in 2024).

Gross profit increased from $67.9M to $79.0M. G&A expenses grew from $42.0M to $50.8M (21% increase over two years), outpacing revenue growth (12.7%). Operating profit was essentially flat at $28.1M in 2024 vs. $28.2M in 2023 vs. $25.9M in 2022.

Net earnings were $22.3M in 2024, $19.9M in 2023, and $22.8M in 2022. The 2022 figure includes $6.0M in one-time items (ERC $3.985M + PPP forgiveness $2.017M); normalized 2022 net earnings were approximately $16.8M. On a normalized basis, net earnings have grown meaningfully.

### Cash Flow Analysis

Operating cash flows have been consistent at $17-18M annually across all three years. The major investing event in 2024 was $30.1M in capital expenditures (predominantly transportation equipment), partially offset by $12.4M in equipment sale proceeds. Long-term debt has been steadily reduced from $26.6M (2022) to $14.8M (2024), with $3.25M due in 2025, $3.75M in 2026, and $7.755M in 2027.

The company drew $5M on its $35M revolving line of credit in 2024 but repaid it within the year. The RLOC carries interest based on prime or SOFR plus applicable margin. All borrowings are collateralized by substantially all business assets. Debt covenants include restrictions on dividends, capital stock redemptions, and minimum interest/debt service coverage and leverage ratios.

### Key Financial Statement Notes

**Revenue recognition**: Continuing franchise fees recognized as underlying franchisee sales occur. Initial franchise fees deferred and amortized over 12 years. Ad Fund contributions recognized when underlying sales occur.

**Lease accounting (ASC 842)**: Operating lease ROU assets of $4.2M and lease liabilities of $5.4M at December 31, 2024. Weighted-average remaining lease term: 4.5 years. Discount rate: 1.9% (risk-free rate). Future lease payments total $5.72M through 2029.

**Deferred compensation**: Three plans: (1) traditional deferred comp (up to $100k deferral, WACC interest); (2) long-term incentive plan (3-year performance period, tied to salon growth and cash flows); (3) terminated additional plan (final payments in 2024-2025, $1.537M remaining). Total deferred compensation liability: $4.5M.

**Financing**: $35M RLOC ($0 drawn at year-end). Term note $8.0M at 5.93% SOFR+margin. Subordinated shareholder notes $6.75M at 8.5%. Total long-term debt principal payments: $14.755M through 2027.

**Commitments**: 249 lease guarantees with $8.994M combined annual rentals and $1.277M recorded liability. Ad Fund sponsorship commitments: NHL $3.8M/year, NCAA $9.982M-$10.346M/year, ESPN $1.0M-$930k/year (total $14.8M-$15.1M through 2026).

**Related parties**: Board members and their families own/operate 23 GREAT CLIPS salons, generating $647,000 in continuing franchise fee revenue in 2024.

**Income taxes**: 25% effective rate all years. Net deferred tax asset of $5.267M. No unrecognized tax benefits.

**Employee benefit plan**: Safe harbor 401(k) match: 100% of first 2% + 50% of next 4%. Employer matching contributions: $1.040M (2024).

### What A Prospective Franchisee Should Know
This is a mature, well-run franchise system with strong franchisor financials, robust FPR disclosure, and a massive salon network.
