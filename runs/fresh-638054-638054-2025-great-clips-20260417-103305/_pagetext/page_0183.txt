Note 6.       Financing Arrangements (Continued)
The terms of the Company’s credit agreements provide for various fees, including an unused commitment
fee and a prepayment fee. All of the amounts outstanding under the credit facilities are collateralized by
substantially all business assets of the Company. The terms of the credit facilities include various
covenants including, but not limited to, restrictions on the payment of dividends, limitations on the amount
of capital stock to be redeemed, minimum interest and debt service coverage ratios, maximum leverage
ratios and various other covenants. The credit agreements contain a default provision and subjective
acceleration clauses that permit the lender to accelerate the due date of outstanding balances under
certain circumstances including, but not limited to, a material adverse change in the business; however,
the agreement does not require the Company to maintain a lockbox and, accordingly, any borrowings
under the revolving lines of credit would be classified as long-term obligations in the balance sheets.

Effective January 1, 2019, the Company converted from an S corporation to a C corporation. In
preparation for this conversion, the Company amended its credit agreements with its banks and issued
subordinated notes payable to its shareholders.

Note 7.       Commitments and Contingencies
Lease guarantees: In connection with its franchising operations, the Company may execute an operating
lease location prior to obtaining a franchisee. The Company assigns its rights under the lease to the
franchisee when the franchisee begins operations; however, the Company remains contingently liable
under the lease agreement. At December 31, 2024, the Company was contingently liable under
249 leases that had combined annual rentals of approximately $8,994,000 during 2024 with expiration
dates that range from 2025 to 2035. The Company has recorded a liability for estimated potential
payments on these leases of approximately $1,277,000, $973,000 and $1,094,000 at December 31,
2024, 2023 and 2022, respectively, which is included in accrued liabilities on the balance sheets.
