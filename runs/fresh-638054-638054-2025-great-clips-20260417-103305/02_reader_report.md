## Pass B: Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — Franchisor Structure
Great Clips, Inc. is a Minnesota corporation incorporated July 12, 1982. It has no parents, predecessors, or affiliates. The company grants franchises for single GREAT CLIPS salons at designated locations, typically in shopping centers. The franchise system offers haircare and personal grooming services and products in a distinctive retail environment. Three franchise structures are offered:
- **Single Franchise Agreement** — standard single-unit license
- **Three Star Program Agreement** — lease-signing incentive for three franchise agreements with a 24-month term
- **Master Development Agreement (MDA)** — commitment to open minimum salons in an Exclusive DMA based on IMUP formula

The company began franchising in 1983. As of December 31, 2024, the system had 4,439 franchised salons and zero company-owned outlets. Average salons per franchisee (5+ years): 8; median: 5.

### Item 5 — Initial Franchise Fee
- **Single salon**: $20,000 ($10,000 partial payment for subsequent agreements; balance upon equipment order)
- **Three Star Program**: $35,000 non-refundable program fee + $5,000 MDAF = $40,000. Waives $20,000 IFF per salon and $5,000 MDAF per salon if leases signed within 24 months
- **MDA**: $4,000 per salon development fee + $6,000 IFF per franchise agreement
- **Initial Advertising Contribution**: $5,000 to MDAF (one-time)
- **Architecture fees**: $100–$3,800

- **Lease review fee**: $2,200–$2,500
- **VetFran Program**: $5,000 IFF rebate for qualified veterans (first salon only)
- **Deferral Program**: IFF deferred (not waived) for salons within protected area of existing salon
- **Expediter Program**: Up to $20,000 rebate for third-party site acquisition costs
- **Initial purchases (fixtures, signage, flooring, inventory)**: $48,700–$71,600

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Supplier Restrictions
Franchisees must purchase approximately 90-95% of establishment goods and 80-85% of ongoing goods from Great Clips or designated suppliers. Great Clips sells lobby items, sales area items, flex station items, signage directly. Key designated suppliers:
- **Salon Innovations, Inc.** — exclusive brand haircare products, paper goods
- **Ideal Printers, Inc.** — printed materials
- **Innovative Computer Software (ICS)** — POS software (sole supplier)
- **Heartland Payment Systems** — credit card processing (sole supplier)
- **Foundation Technologies, LLC** — network/hardware managed services (sole supplier)
- **Stored Value Systems, Inc.** — gift card processing

GC derives ~6% royalty on exclusive brand haircare products from Salon Innovations. GC had $16.27M in required purchase sales (14.1% of $115.2M total revenue, 2024). Supplier appointment: 90-day approval window; GC reserves right to revoke. No purchasing cooperatives. Franchisees/owners may not be designated as exclusive suppliers.

Required haircare brands: MATRIX TOTAL RESULTS, AMERICAN CREW, BOSLEY MD, SEXY HAIR, TIGI BEDHEAD, JOHN PAUL MITCHELL SYSTEMS.

Insurance minimums: $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate general liability; all-risk property coverage at replacement cost; workers' comp as required. Additional recommended: professional liability, hired/non-owned auto, umbrella, business income (12 months), employers liability, employment practices liability, cyber liability.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

The 3/4-mile protected area is a relatively small radius for a high-density retail haircare concept. Non-Traditional Location exclusions create significant encroachment risk — GC could open in airports, universities, big-box stores, etc. within the protected area. The MDA provides an exclusive DMA but only during compliance with the development schedule. GC and affiliates may operate under different trademarks selling similar services/products at any location including the protected area. GC may distribute branded products through Amazon and other alternative channels without obligation to franchisees. The franchisee is prohibited from internet/telemarketing solicitation without consent, while GC faces no such limitation. This creates material channel asymmetry. The AI/ML prohibition in Item 14 is a notable modern restriction preventing any franchisee use of proprietary data for AI applications.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 10 years from date of opening (or balance of term for acquired salon)
- **Renewal**: No right to renew. Renewal at GC's sole discretion. Conditions: notice, compliance, upgrade to current standards, $1,750 fee (CPI-adjusted), possibly re-attend training, sign then-current franchise agreement and general release
- **"Materially different" risk**: Explicitly stated — renewal agreement may have materially different terms and conditions
- **MDA term**: 10 years; renewal at GC's discretion with conditions
- **Three Star Program**: 24-month term, no extension

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: Only for good cause or upon grounds available by law
- **By GC without cause**: None
- **Curable defaults**: Non-payment (7-day cure); all other breaches (30-day cure). System standards violations, misrepresenting financials, unauthorized products/services. Cross-default: material breach of any franchise agreement with GC
- **Non-curable defaults**: Impairment of Marks/System, insolvency, assignment without consent, failure to cure lease default, material misuse of customer data, criminal conviction, material misrepresentation, unauthorized use of confidential information. Repeated breaches: 30-day written notice

### Transfer
- **Definition**: Any transfer/assignment of assets or rights or any ownership change altering percentage
- **GC consent required** for all assignments (not to be unreasonably withheld)
- **Conditions**: Assignee must be qualified, sign new franchise agreement (for balance of term), sign general release and guaranty, pay assignment fee ($1,500), be financially qualified, communicate in English, not have conflicting interests
- **Right of First Refusal**: GC has 60 days to match any bona fide offer (price and terms or cash equivalent). If offer changes, new 60-day ROFR. Transaction must close within 120 days of GC declining
- **Death/disability**: Heirs/executor must apply for consent and pay assignment fee
- **No assignment if salon not in operation** (except selling all salons/leaving system)
- **MDA**: Cannot assign without prior written consent; assignee must assume all franchise agreements in Exclusive DMA

### Noncompete
- **During term**: You and spouse — no involvement in any haircare/grooming/beauty school/hair product business at any location or internet
- **Post-term**: 1 year, within 5 miles of any GREAT CLIPS salon (not just yours)
- Scope: includes any Retail Haircare Business (services on GC price board, related products, blow drying/styling, braiding, treatments, perms)

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: All disputes must be arbitrated in Minneapolis, MN (with certain exceptions)
- **Venue**: All legal proceedings in Hennepin County, MN
- **Governing law**: State where the Authorized Location is located
- **State addenda may override venue/arbitration requirements**
- **General release required** on termination/non-renewal in favor of GC

### Post-Termination Obligations
Cease use of Marks, assign telephone number to GC, pay all sums owed, return manuals, de-identify location, execute general release, comply with noncompete.
