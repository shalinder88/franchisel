# Reader Report — 637918-2025-Burger-King

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: 6,701 U.S. BURGER KING Restaurants as of Dec 31, 2024 (5,524 franchised + 1,177 company-owned); 19,732 worldwide
2. **Entity structure**: Burger King Company LLC (FL LLC), formed Feb 2022 as part of internal reorganization from Burger King Corporation (dissolved Dec 2022); wholly-owned indirect subsidiary of RBILP/RBI
3. **Ownership history**: Founded 1954 as Burger King of Miami; BK Corporation 1963–2022; transferred to BKC LLC in Aug 2022; parent RBI also owns Tim Hortons, Popeyes, Firehouse Subs; 3G Restaurant Brands Holdings owns ~26% voting power of RBI
4. **Investment range**: Traditional freestanding $2,049,200–$4,705,600 (excl. real estate); Non-traditional as low as $348,400–$789,100 (Indoor MRS)
5. **Fee burden**: Royalty 4.5% of Gross Sales (standard); advertising up to 4.5% (currently 4.5%); digital services fee $110/mo + 1% digital sales (cap $4,500/yr); extensive incentive programs with stepped royalty reductions
6. **Item 19**: Traditional average sales $1,671,613 consolidated; franchisee-owned average $1,658,463; remodel uplift average 12.3% sales, 11.1% traffic
7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: Traditional franchisee-owned: 4.4% below $700K, 9.6% at $700K–$900K — meaningful tail risk
8. **Financial health**: RBI total revenues $8.4B (2024); net income $1,445M; total assets $24.6B; significant leverage ($13.5B long-term debt)
9. **Growth trajectory**: Net decline of -77 outlets in 2024 (but distorted by Carrols acquisition converting 1,023 franchised to company-owned); 48 projected new franchised + 2 new company-owned in next fiscal year
10. **Territory**: No exclusive territory; no protected territory under any agreement form
11. **Renewal**: No right of renewal; successor FA available if in compliance, but must sign then-current FA which "may have materially different terms"
12. **Disputes**: Litigation in Florida courts; Florida law governs (subject to state addenda)
13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (FL); mediation required for development disputes
14. **Gag clause**: YES — current and former franchisees signed provisions restricting ability to speak openly (p.126/FDD p.117)

## B. Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — The Franchisor
BKC is a Florida LLC formed Feb 4, 2022, principal office at 5707 Blue Lagoon Drive, Miami, FL 33126. It assumed all BK Corporation assets/liabilities in Aug 2022 as part of an internal reorganization. BK Corporation (predecessor, founded 1954) was dissolved Dec 2022. BKC has offered franchises since Sept 2022.

RBI (parent, Canadian corporation) also indirectly owns Tim Hortons, Popeyes, and Firehouse Subs. Affiliates operate BK internationally: BK Europe (6,065 franchised), BK APac (1,851), BKA IP (2,345), BKL IP (2,232), BK Canada (381). BK APac's affiliate acquired 1,188 restaurants in Feb 2025.

Carrols LLC is an affiliate that operates BK restaurants in the U.S. and may sell them under the Carrols Refranchise Program. RBI acquired Carrols Restaurant Group Inc. in May 2024, converting 1,023 franchised restaurants to company-owned.

Two ownership types: Individual/Owner-Operator (personal responsibility) and Entity (corporation/LLC/LP with Managing Owner holding at least 25% equity). Crown Your Career Program allows BKC employees to purchase restaurants after 1-3 years of working at the location.

### Item 5 — Initial Fees
- **Franchise fee**: $50,000 standard for 20-year term (prorated for shorter terms, minimum $15,000); nonrefundable when FA signed
- **Application fee**: $250/individual, $5,000/entity; nonrefundable
- **Training fee**: $7,500 per trainee; nonrefundable
- **TRA deposit**: $5,000 per restaurant
- **MTRA deposit**: $10,000 per restaurant × number of openings committed
- **Development Agreement**: Prepay $50,000 per restaurant committed; credited against franchise fees; FSS Development Grade determines fees for restaurants after prepaid amount exhausted ($25,000 for A/B grade, Base Fee for D/F)
- **Crown Your Career**: Purchase price varies by restaurant; determined at time of offer
- **Carrols Refranchise**: Purchase price varies; 5% deposit on Term Sheet; no franchise fee for purchased restaurants; tech installation up to $5,000/restaurant

## C. Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Technology (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Restrictions on Sources
65%–90% of establishment costs and 30%–60% of non-occupancy operating expenses from approved suppliers. BKC is sole supplier of technology platforms for ordering, delivery, and loyalty. Sole suppliers for: network services, digital menu boards, gift cards, cashless payments, quality inspections, food, beverages, cleaning supplies, packaging, certain equipment. Insurance requirements: $2M/$2M CGL (1-10 restaurants), $5M (11-50), $10M (50+); $1M auto; all-risk property.

### Item 9 — Franchisee's Obligations
Comprehensive cross-reference table covering site selection, pre-opening, construction, training, opening, fees, compliance with standards, trademark, noncompete, personal participation, records, inspections, transfer, renewal, advertising, indemnification, financial covenants, and dispute resolution.

### Item 10 — Financing
BKC does not offer direct financing except: (1) Real estate leases (BKL/BKG), (2) Crown Your Career Program financing at Prime + 1%–4%, secured by all restaurant assets, personal guaranty required, typically 10 years or less, no prepayment penalty. Default on any BK agreement triggers cross-default on loan.

### Item 11 — Training, Assistance, Computer Systems
**Training**: New franchisee training fee $7,500. Restaurant Management Program (classroom + in-restaurant), ServSafe certification required. Continuing training also $7,500 per person. Must implement BK University Team Member Training and Shift Certification programs.

**POS/Technology**: Minimum 2 front counter POS, 2 drive-thru POS, interior/exterior digital menu boards, 4 receipt printers, kiosks. Annual data reporting $200–$1,200/restaurant. Digital fee $110/mo + 1% digital sales (cap $4,500/yr). Royal Perks Loyalty Program mandatory.

**Advertising**: BKC administers the advertising fund. Contribution currently 4.5% of Gross Sales. Fund may use TV, radio, print, internet, social media, and other forms. BKC does not use fund money for advertising principally for the sale of franchises.

### Item 12 — Territory
No exclusive territory under any agreement form. BKC and affiliates may establish restaurants anywhere, including near your location. BKC reserves right to sell BK-branded products through alternative channels (grocery, internet, etc.). No compensation for competition.

Development Agreement grants a geographic "Territory" but you still do not receive exclusivity.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
BURGER KING, BK, WHOPPER, HAVE IT YOUR WAY, and other marks registered with USPTO. RBI owns the marks; BKC has license to use and sublicense. No current material litigation challenging the marks' validity.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights
No patents material to the franchise. BKC owns proprietary information in the MOD Manual, recipes, specifications, computer software, and websites.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
Operating Partner (Individual) or Managing Owner (Entity) must devote full time and best efforts. Managing Owner must own at least 25% of the entity. Managing Director (Legacy Entity) must be approved and responsible for day-to-day operations.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on What Franchisee May Sell
Must offer only BKC-approved menu items. Cannot sell non-approved products. BKC can change menu, add/remove items at any time. Must sell all standard menu items unless specific exception (limited menu for non-traditional).

## D. Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

No protected territory. No exclusive territory. BKC affiliates operate Tim Hortons, Popeyes, Firehouse Subs in same or nearby markets. BKC retains right to sell through all channels including internet, grocery, convenience stores. Development Agreement territory does not provide exclusivity. Military base rights granted to Army & Air Force Exchange Service.

Encroachment risk is HIGH: BKC explicitly reserves right to open or allow franchisees to open restaurants near existing locations. "Other BURGER KING Restaurants may compete with your Restaurant or may affect customer trading patterns."

## E. Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Items 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Term**: 20 years freestanding; shorter for non-traditional or limited property control
- **Successor**: No right of renewal; option for successor FA up to 20 years if compliant + property control + meet current successor requirements. **Materially different terms risk**: Must sign then-current FA which "may have materially different terms"
- **Successor fee**: $2,500/year for additional term; Reclaim the Flame 2 remodel can reduce

### Termination
**Curable defaults**: 5 days (health/safety, unapproved products, abandonment, insurance); 10 days (royalty/ad fee nonpayment); 30 days (reports, equipment, condition, management requirements); 60 days (bills, general noncompliance); 180 days (financial criteria)

**Non-curable defaults**: Bankruptcy, felony conviction, fraud, understatement of gross sales, unauthorized transfer, repeated breaches after notice, unfair competition, false statements. Cross-default with Reclaim the Flame and Crown Your Career programs.

### Transfer
Requires BKC consent. Buyer must be approved, complete training, all monies current, pay transfer fee ($2,000 first + $500 each additional) and training fee ($7,500). Must sign current FA (may differ substantially). General release by seller. BKC has right of first refusal.

Crown Your Career: BKC may withhold consent to transfer during first 5 years for any reason.
Carrols Refranchise: BKC may withhold consent until remodels completed.

### Death/Disability
Heir must be approved or sell interest within 12 months. BKC has option to operate/manage until resolved.

### Noncompete
**During term**: Cannot own/operate any hamburger business. Operating Partner/Managing Owner cannot engage in other business activities (Crown Your Career: until later of 5th anniversary or loan repayment).
**Post-term**: 1 year within 2 miles of restaurant, cannot own/operate hamburger business.

### Dispute Resolution
No mandatory arbitration. Mandatory non-binding mediation for development disputes. Voluntary arbitration at franchisee's election for development disputes. Delivery restaurant franchisees waive development dispute rights.

**Venue**: U.S. District Court, Southern District of Florida, or state court in Miami-Dade County (subject to state addenda).
**Governing law**: Florida (subject to state addenda).
