## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and footprint**: 92 franchised outlets + 2 company-owned = 94 total (end of 2024), across 25 states + DC. Net growth of +4 franchised in 2024 (10 opened, 6 terminated, 1 ceased). The system has grown from 50 qualifying outlets (2020) to 92 (2024), a 84% increase in four years.

2. **Entity structure**: Rytech Franchising, Inc. is a Georgia corporation (incorporated 1996), wholly owned by RyBrands, Inc. (FL). CEO William D. Ryan has controlled the company since inception. Three affiliates: Rytech Admin Services, LLC (administrative services to franchisees — $5.72M in revenue from franchisees in 2024), Rytech, Inc. (administers National Ad Fund), and Rytech North Florida, Inc. (company-owned FL operations).

3. **Investment range**: $166,500–$258,100 total initial investment. Home-based start permitted. Initial franchise fee $60,000–$85,000 (population-based). Full-size cargo van required ($26K–$60K, only monthly payments shown in table).

4. **Fee burden**: Ongoing royalty 8% + admin fee 11% + national ad fund 1% = **20% minimum of gross revenues** before local advertising (4% GR or $1,000/month minimum). The 11% admin fee to the CEO-controlled affiliate is the single largest ongoing fee. Additional technology fees (MICA, NetSuite, DocuSign, Rytech U) add roughly $5,000+/year.

5. **Item 19 (FPR)**: 2024 average gross revenue $731,214 (80 qualifying franchisees), median $467,724. Only 28% met or exceeded average. Range: $74,010–$4,975,620. Gross revenue only — no cost or profit data disclosed. The median has declined from $563,461 (2021) to $467,724 (2024) even as the system expanded.

6. **Bottom-quartile risk**: A franchisee at the median ($467,724 GR) would pay approximately $93,500 in fees alone (20% × $468K), plus $18,700+ in local advertising (4%), plus technology fees — leaving roughly $355K for all operating expenses, equipment, labor, and owner compensation. A bottom-quartile franchisee at ~$200K GR would face $40K+ in fees on revenue that likely cannot support an owner salary.

7. **Financial health**: Franchisor revenue $4.48M (2024), net income $1.21M. But distributions to parent ($1.265M) exceeded net income, creating an accumulated deficit of ($36,361). Cash declined from $1.02M to $792K. The franchisor is profitable but depleting retained earnings through excessive distributions.

8. **Growth trajectory**: Steady franchise unit growth (74→80→88→92 over 2022-2024). However, 2024 saw the highest termination count (6) in the three-year period. Projecting 9-14 new openings for next fiscal year.

9. **Territory**: Protected zip-code blocks (~400K population) but not exclusive. Multiple encroachment carve-outs for national accounts, catastrophic events, and alternative channels.

10. **Renewal risk**: 10-year initial term, 5-year renewals. Must sign then-current agreement which may have materially different terms. Renewal fee 15% of then-current initial franchise fee. General release required.

11. **Dispute resolution**: Georgia-centric (Cobb County). Mediation then binding arbitration. 9 state addenda modify these provisions.

12. **Litigation/bankruptcy**: None disclosed.

13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution; mandatory minimum payments.

14. **Noncompete**: 2-year post-term restriction with broad geographic reach encompassing own territory + 10 miles + all Rytech territories + 10 miles of each.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Rytech Franchising, Inc. is a Georgia corporation incorporated November 25, 1996, wholly owned by RyBrands, Inc. (Florida corporation, in business since January 1, 2020). The company has been offering franchises since December 1996. The franchisor operates from 1690 Roberts Blvd. NW, Suite 120, Kennesaw, Georgia 30144.

The organizational structure involves the franchisor (Rytech Franchising), its parent (RyBrands), and three affiliates. Notably, Rytech Admin Services, LLC is the mandatory administrative services provider for all franchisees, generating $5,721,199 in revenue from franchisees in 2024. CEO William D. Ryan is the majority shareholder of RyBrands, which wholly owns both the franchisor and Admin Services. This creates a structure where 11% of every franchisee's gross revenue flows to a CEO-controlled affiliate.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees**:
- Franchise fee: $60,000 (base for 300K–400K population territory)
- Additional population above 400K: $0.20/person (max territory 500K population, max fee ~$85K)
- Veterans discount: $10,000 off first franchise
- Expansion franchise: $60,000

**Ongoing Fees (recurring)**:
- Royalty: 8% of GR ($2,000/month minimum, waived first 12 months if in good standing)
- Administrative Fee: 11% of GR (to affiliate)
- National Ad Fund: 1% of GR (max 3%, 90 days notice)
- Local Advertising: $1,000/month or 4% of GR, whichever greater (may increase to 6%)
- MICA: $8/transaction + $199/month/location
- Rytech U: $115/quarter
- NetSuite accounting: $100/month (single unit) or $175/month (multi-unit)
- DocuSign: $20/month
- Wizer Security Training: $20/year/user

**Event-Driven Fees**:
- Renewal: 15% of then-current initial franchise fee
- Transfer: 15% of then-current initial franchise fee
- Late payment: $50 + 1.5%/month
- Non-compliance: $250/day
- Territory intrusion: up to 50% of violating revenue (or 75% for intrusion into another franchisee's territory)
- Supplier approval: actual cost (up to $2,500 + $150/hr)
- Additional training: $200/day per diem + expenses
- Lead fee: $10,000 (for referred leads)
- Rebuild fees: 4% royalty + 4% admin + 1% ad fund (reduced rates for repair work)

### Initial Investment Breakdown

The total initial investment ranges from $166,500 to $258,100 and covers the first three months of operation. The largest components are the initial franchise fee ($60K–$85K), initial equipment and supplies ($55K–$60K), and additional working capital ($25K–$50K). The estimate assumes a home-based start (no lease), owner-operated (no manager salary), and does not include the owner's draw.

The vehicle requirement ($26K–$60K total) is a significant additional outlay shown only as monthly payments ($2K–$4K) in the table. Insurance for three months ($4K–$15K) varies widely depending on state and coverage configuration. Pre-training certification courses (IICRC WRT/ASD, AMRT, Lead Paint, OSHA, Claims) cost $4,900–$7,000 plus travel.

None of the initial investment amounts are refundable.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory

Territories are granted in zip-code blocks with a minimum population of approximately 400,000. Territories are designated by specific zip codes. However, the FDD explicitly states: "You will not receive an exclusive territory." The protection is conditional:

**Territory preserved when**: Franchisee is in good standing, participating in assigned programs, and not refusing legitimate work.

**Territory overridden when**:
1. **National/regional accounts**: Franchisor may assign jobs to other franchisees if franchisee refuses, doesn't qualify, or isn't in good standing
2. **Catastrophic events**: Other franchisees/company personnel may enter territory during declared disasters
3. **Alternative channels**: Franchisor reserves internet and alternative distribution channel rights
4. **Different brands**: Franchisor may operate under different names offering different services within territory

### Channel Restrictions

- Franchisee may ONLY accept and perform work within own territory
- No internet, catalog, telemarketing, or direct marketing sales outside territory
- May request to work in bordering unassigned zip codes (revocable at any time)
- May market outside territory only to generate work within territory (and not in another franchisee's territory)

### Territorial Rights Preservation

Territorial rights are NOT dependent on sales volumes, market penetration, or other contingencies. However, failure to maintain "good standing" or refusal to service assigned claims can effectively erode territory protection. There is no right of first refusal for additional territories.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

- **Initial term**: 10 years
- **Renewal**: 5-year terms, subject to approval
- **Renewal conditions**: 180-day advance notice; substantial compliance; current renewal requirements met within 60 days; execute then-current franchise agreement + guaranty + confidentiality agreement; sign general release; pay 15% of then-current initial fee
- **Materially different terms risk**: The renewal agreement may differ materially from the original, including increased royalty fees and ad fund contributions. Territory boundaries will remain the same, and continuing royalty will not exceed that imposed on similarly situated renewing franchisees.

### Transfer Provisions

- Transfer requires franchisor approval (except to wholly-owned entity)
- Transfer fee: 15% of then-current initial franchise fee
- Conditions: transferee meets standards, not a competitor, signs current agreements, general release, completes training, all defaults cured
- Right of first refusal: franchisor can match any offer

### Termination and Death/Disability

- **Curable defaults** (with notice/cure period): failure to report, failure to train, failure to offer core services, failure to attend 2+ meetings/year, failure to perform background checks, failure to pay, failure to maintain insurance, failure to meet $500K sales by month 24
- **Non-curable defaults** (immediate/on notice): bankruptcy/insolvency, mark misuse, abandonment, illegal operation, material misrepresentation, unauthorized assignment, confidential info disclosure, repeated non-compliance, felony conviction, unauthorized territory intrusion
- **Death/disability**: 90 days for estate to assign to approved buyer or demonstrate heir qualification; additional 120 days if initial request denied

### Noncompete (During and Post-Term)

**During term**: No involvement in competing business; no diversion of carriers, customers, or employees; no hiring of carrier representatives or franchisor/affiliate/other franchisee employees.

**Post-term (2 years)**: No competing business within:
- Franchisee's territory
- 10 miles of the border of franchisee's territory
- Any Rytech franchised or company territory
- 10 miles of the border of any Rytech territory

This is an exceptionally broad noncompete. With 92 franchised + 2 company-owned locations across 25 states, the practical effect is to prohibit the former franchisee from operating a restoration business in much of the geographic areas where such businesses are commercially viable. Multiple state addenda limit or void the noncompete entirely.

### Dispute Resolution

- Step 1: Direct negotiations with franchisor's President
- Step 2: Non-binding mediation (concluded within 60 days)
- Step 3: Binding arbitration — except for known monetary amounts and injunctive relief
- Venue: Cobb County, Georgia
- Governing law: Georgia
- Subject to state law modifications (see state addenda)
- Waiver of punitive damages (in Guaranty)
- Waiver of class actions (in Guaranty)

### Litigation History

No litigation disclosed in Item 3. No bankruptcy disclosed in Item 4. This is a clean litigation and bankruptcy profile.

### Key Observations

1. **Average trending up, median trending down**: The 2024 average ($731K) is the second-highest in the series, but the median ($468K) is the lowest. This indicates growing concentration at the top — high performers are pulling the average up while the typical franchisee is generating less revenue.

2. **Only 28% at or above average in 2024**: Down from 36% in 2023 and 33% in 2022. The gap between average and median has widened significantly.

3. **Enormous range**: The top performer ($4.98M) earned 67x the bottom performer ($74K). This suggests very different market conditions, operator capabilities, or territory characteristics.

4. **No cost data**: The FDD provides gross revenue only. Given the 20%+ fee burden, a franchisee at the median ($468K) would pay ~$94K in ongoing fees alone, leaving ~$374K for all operating costs, labor, equipment, and owner compensation. Whether this is viable depends on the cost structure, which is not disclosed.

5. **Exclusion bias**: Franchisees open less than a full year and those who closed within the year are excluded. This means the worst-performing units (those that failed and closed) are systematically excluded from the data.

6. **No adjustments**: No geographic, catastrophic event, or open territory adjustments are made, meaning some reported figures may include one-time revenue from extraordinary events.

Written substantiation is available upon reasonable request.
