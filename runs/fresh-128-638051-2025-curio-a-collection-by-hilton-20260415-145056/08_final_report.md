# Full Diligence Report — Curio Collection by Hilton
## FDD Filing 638051-2025 | Wisconsin DFI | Issuance Year 2025

---

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand & System**: Curio Collection by Hilton is an upscale/luxury "soft brand" hotel collection operated under franchise agreements with Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 2007. The brand has been offered in the US since October 15, 2014.

2. **Entity Structure**: Franchisor → Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. (parent) → Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT, ultimate parent). Nearly all company-owned hotels were divested in the 2017 Park Hotels & Resorts spin-off. The franchisor is essentially a royalty-collecting pass-through entity.

3. **System Size**: 88 Curio branded hotels operating in the US as of December 31, 2024 (80 franchised, 7 company-managed, 1 in territories). The brand is present in 35 states plus DC and Puerto Rico.

4. **Growth Trajectory**: Accelerating. Net growth of +5 (2022), +4 (2023), +9 (2024). Pipeline of 23 signed agreements not yet opened. 6 projected new openings in next fiscal year.

5. **Investment Range**: $4,683,359 to $143,454,204 for a typical 250-room hotel, excluding real property. Low end assumes minimal conversion; high end is ground-up luxury new development with eforea spa.

6. **Fee Burden**: Combined 9% of Gross Rooms Revenue (5% royalty + 4% program fee). Plus 4.3% Hilton Honors on eligible guest folio. Plus extensive technology, distribution, and compliance fees. Effective all-in fee load likely exceeds 15% of GRR.

7. **Item 19 Highlights**: FPR provided. Average Room Rate $257.42 (2024), Average Occupancy 67.3% (2024). Only 33.8% of franchisee-managed hotels met or exceeded average room rate. Conversion hotels showed +17.7% RevPAR lift.

8. **Bottom-Quartile Risk**: Franchisee-managed hotels significantly underperform company-managed on all metrics. Less than half of franchisee hotels meet the median room rate. No cost or profit data disclosed.

9. **Financial Health**: Franchisor revenue $1.502B (2024), net income $1.488B, 99.2% operating margin. Distributed $1.5B to parent. Cash-light balance sheet ($317K) — relies entirely on Hilton parent.

10. **Territory**: Limited Restricted Area Provision with extensive carve-outs. Not a protected territory in any meaningful sense. Restrictive Period usually shorter than franchise term.

11. **No Renewal Right**: Franchise is non-renewable. Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms.

12. **No Post-Term Noncompete**: Favorable to franchisee — no restrictions after franchise ends.

13. **Disputes**: Virginia/New York venue. New York governing law. No mandatory arbitration (except Puerto Rico).

14. **Special Risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Virginia/New York).

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
Hilton Franchise Holding LLC was formed in September 2007 and became the franchisor for the Curio brand on October 15, 2014. The entity is a wholly-owned subsidiary within the Hilton corporate family, with Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. as its direct parent and Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT) as the ultimate parent.

The franchisor licenses intellectual property from Hilton International Holding LLC (HIH) and Hilton International Holding USA Corporation (HIHUSA) on a royalty-free basis, then sublicenses this IP to franchisees under long-term franchise contracts. The entity is a nearly pure pass-through: it collects franchise royalties and distributes virtually all net income to its parent.

Key subsidiaries providing services to franchisees:
- **Hilton Reservations Worldwide LLC** — reservation services (Addison, TX)
- **Hilton Supply Management LLC (HSM)** — procurement and supply chain
- **Hilton Systems Solutions LLC (HSS)** — IT systems and OnQ platform

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Franchisees must comply with Brand Standards (Manual). Key supplier constraints:
- Must use approved property management system (OnQ/HPMS) — proprietary, no third-party market
- Must purchase logo-bearing items from approved suppliers
- HSM (Hilton Supply Management) negotiates procurement — generally optional but franchisor may mandate specific brands/sources in future
- HSM procurement fee: 4%-10% of product cost
- HSM received in 2024: $19.6M in rebates, $8.8M in sales revenue, $38.7M in administrative fees (all brands)
- Hilton affiliate revenues from franchisee sales: $1.101B (2024, all brands)
- Supplier approval process: 60-90 days

Required purchases estimated at 15%-20% of establishment cost and 2%-5% of ongoing operating expenses. The FRCM (Fixed Renovation Cycle Management) program may require periodic renovations at franchisee's cost with no contractual limit on frequency.

### Training Program
The training program is extensive for a hotel franchise:
- Brand Conference: annual, location varies, GM + Director of Sales required ($2,500/attendee)
- GM/Commercial/Sales Leader Training: up to 18 hours virtual, within 90 days of start
- HPMS Training: 1-5 hours online
- Revenue Management Training: 5 hours online
- Core Sales Skills: 24 hours virtual, all sales personnel
- Owner Orientation: 16 hours virtual
- New to Hospitality Owner Education: 20 hours (new to industry)
- Annual Compliance Training: 2-3 hours
- ADA Training: varies

Replacement personnel in key roles must complete training. Franchisor may modify training requirements and add mandatory programs over time.

### Technology Stack
The technology burden is among the heaviest in hotel franchising:
- **OnQ system**: Proprietary PMS, reservations, revenue management. $103K-$274K initial, $2.4K-$6.8K/month ongoing. Refresh every 3 years at similar cost.
- **Guest Internet Access (StayConnected)**: $120K-$175K initial, $2.5K-$7.5K/month ongoing. Refresh every 4 years.
- **GRO (Revenue Optimization)**: ~$3,800 initial. No ongoing fee currently.
- **Connected Room**: $55K-$66K initial, $980-$1,395/month ongoing.
- **Delphi (Sales/Events)**: $900-$41K initial, $858/user/year.
- **Digital Floor Plan**: $2,500.
- **Digital Key**: Included in OnQ.

Total estimated ongoing technology cost: approximately $7,000-$16,000/month before refreshes.

### Marketing Fund
The Monthly Program Fee (4% of GRR) funds various programs benefiting the System and/or Network. The franchisor provides annual accounting upon request but has no obligation to spend any specific portion in the franchisee's local market. This fee is changeable.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
The Franchise Agreement provides a "Restricted Area Provision" — this is NOT a protected territory in the traditional franchise sense. Key characteristics:
- **Restricted Area**: A geographic area near your hotel, defined at signing
- **Restrictive Period**: An agreed time, usually shorter than the franchise term. Historically extended for the full term, but the franchisor explicitly states it "does not intend to do so in the future"
- **What it restricts**: Prevents franchisor and affiliates from operating another Curio brand hotel in the area during the period

### Carve-Outs (Extremely Broad)
The Restricted Area Provision does NOT protect against:
1. Existing or already-approved hotels
2. Other Hilton brands (Hampton, DoubleTree, Embassy Suites, Hilton Garden Inn, etc.)
3. Hotels opening after the Restrictive Period expires
4. Gaming-oriented hotels
5. Vacation ownership / timeshare properties
6. Acquisitions of chains of 4+ hotels (the "chain acquisition" exception)
7. Strategic Partnerships with third-party companies

### Channel Restrictions
- Franchisee may not register, own, or maintain any websites relating to the hotel or Marks
- All domain names must be assigned/approved by franchisor
- Third-party listing sites require advance written approval
- No relocation permitted
- No options or rights of first refusal for additional franchises

The territory protection is essentially illusory for practical purposes. A prospective franchisee should assume that Hilton may place any non-Curio branded hotel in the immediate vicinity, and may place a Curio hotel nearby once the Restrictive Period expires.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction**: 23 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion**: 10-20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership**: Remaining term or as approved
- **No renewal right**: The franchise is non-renewable. At term end, the franchisor may, in its sole discretion, offer re-licensing under what may be "materially different terms and conditions." This is a critical risk — a franchisee who has invested tens of millions in a hotel brand identity faces the prospect of losing that identity at term end with no guaranteed continuation.

### Transfer Provisions
- **Permitted Transfers (no consent)**: Publicly traded equity, private equity <50% with no control change, fund transfers with same asset manager
- **Permitted Transfers (consent + $5,500 fee)**: Affiliate, family/trust, death, private equity >50% changed since Effective Date
- **Change of Ownership ($150,000 fee)**: Full approval process — 60-day notice, no default, new FA required, PIP required, transferee must meet all current requirements
- **No right of first refusal** by franchisor
- **No option to purchase** by franchisor

### Termination and Death/Disability
- **Franchisee cannot terminate** before expiration — doing so is a material breach triggering liquidated damages
- **Franchisor without cause**: Only on condemnation or casualty (with LD release if no competitor at site within 3 years)
- **Curable defaults**: Non-payment (10 days), construction delays (10 days), insurance (10 days), other Standards (30 days)
- **Non-curable defaults**: 16 grounds including repeated breach, 3 defaults in 12 months, bankruptcy, 5-day cessation, fraud, reputational harm, becoming a Competitor or Sanctioned Person
- **Death/disability**: Transfer to immediate family within 1 year, must meet requirements

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term**: Franchisee may not own or license a competing hotel brand. However, franchisee MAY be a franchisee of or manager for a competitor — an unusually permissive provision.
- **Post-term**: None. No post-termination noncompete whatsoever. This is favorable to the franchisee.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue**: US District Court for Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria) or Fairfax County, VA state court; or New York, NY. Franchisor may elect to sue where the hotel is located.
- **Governing law**: New York
- **Arbitration**: None (except Puerto Rico — AAA/JAMS, venue Fairfax County VA, seat New York)
- **No mediation requirement**
- **State addenda may override** certain dispute resolution provisions

### Litigation History
Item 3 discloses several litigation matters. Based on review, these appear to be standard franchise enforcement actions and commercial disputes, without a pattern suggesting systemic issues. A detailed case-by-case review was not performed in this pass.

**Attainment rates (2024):**

- Franchisee-managed hotels meeting/exceeding average occupancy: 58.4% (45/81)

### Conversion Performance (16 Comparable Conversion Hotels)
| Metric | Pre-Conversion | Post-Conversion | Change |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|--------|

The conversion data shows meaningful uplift from joining the Curio brand, but the sample is small (16 of 61 conversions) and subject to selection bias (only hotels reporting to STR).

1. **No cost, expense, or profit data** — only room rate and occupancy disclosed
2. **Company-managed hotels consistently outperform** franchisee-managed on all metrics
3. **Small conversion sample** — 16 of 61 conversion hotels, potential selection bias
4. **Cool-down and ramp-up periods excluded** — 6 months before and after conversion removed from comparison
5. **2023 range anomaly** — The stated 2023 Room Rate range ($146.86-$221.25) is inconsistent with the 2023 average ($255.91). See Contradiction C01.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Extreme profitability**: 99.2% operating margin in 2024. The entity has virtually no operating expenses ($1.4M on $1.5B revenue) because all operational functions are performed by Hilton parent and affiliates.

2. **Pass-through structure**: Virtually all net income ($1.488B) is distributed to the parent ($1.5B). The entity retains minimal cash ($317K at year-end). It depends entirely on the Hilton parent for operational support.

3. **Revenue growth**: Revenue grew 9.1% in 2024 and 12.0% in 2023, reflecting both system growth and rate increases across all Hilton brands.

4. **Termination fee spike**: Termination fees jumped from $2.4M (2023) to $18.3M (2024), a 7.5× increase. This may reflect the Graduate brand acquisition or increased franchise exits.

5. **Deferred revenues**: $559M in deferred revenues (upfront franchise fees recognized over contract term) — this represents future revenue already collected.

6. **Inter-affiliate receivables**: $704M due from Hilton affiliates — this is the primary asset, representing cash collected and lent to parent/affiliates before formal distribution.

7. **Graduate acquisition**: $85.3M franchise contract intangible acquired in 2024, funded by a short-term note from parent — reflecting the Graduate Hotels brand acquisition by Hilton.
