# Reader Report — Curio Collection by Hilton (638051-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed Sept 2007; parent is Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., ultimate parent Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). Principal address: 7930 Jones Branch Drive, Suite 1100, McLean, VA 22102.
2. **Brand**: Curio Collection by Hilton — upscale/luxury "soft brand" collection. Franchisor has offered Curio in US since Oct 15, 2014.
3. **System size**: 88 Curio branded hotels operating in the US as of Dec 31, 2024 (80 franchised, 7 company-managed, 1 territory). Net growth: +5 (2022), +4 (2023), +9 (2024). Strong growth trajectory.
4. **Investment range**: $4,683,359 to $143,454,204 for a typical 250-room hotel (excluding real property). This is extremely high-capital — hotel construction/conversion.
5. **Fee burden**: 5% royalty on Gross Rooms Revenue + 4% Monthly Program Fee = **9% combined ongoing fee on GRR**. Plus Hilton Honors 4.3%, technology fees, and extensive distribution charges.
6. **Item 19**: FPR provided. Average Room Rate $257.42 (2024), Average Occupancy 67.3% (2024). Conversion hotels showed +8.5% ADR lift, +5.5pts occupancy lift, +17.7% RevPAR lift post-conversion. No cost/profit data disclosed.
7. **Term**: 23 years (new construction), 10-20 years (conversion). **No right to renew or extend** — re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion, potentially with materially different terms.
8. **Territory**: Limited Restricted Area Provision with extensive carve-outs — not a protected territory in meaningful sense. Restrictive Period usually shorter than franchise term.
9. **Dispute resolution**: Virginia courts (Eastern District, Alexandria, VA or Fairfax County); New York as fallback. New York law governs.
10. **No post-term noncompete** — unusual for hotel franchises and favorable to franchisee.
11. **Liquidated damages**: 60× average monthly royalty fees — very significant tail exposure.
12. **Financial health**: Franchisor revenue $1.502B (2024), net income $1.488B. Total assets $1.27B, member's equity $577M. $1.5B distributed to parent in 2024. Very profitable, essentially a pass-through royalty entity.
13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Virginia/New York).

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **No exclusive territory.** Franchise Agreement provides a "Restricted Area Provision" — NOT a protected territory in the traditional sense.
- Restricted Area: typically a geographic area near your hotel.
- Restrictive Period: agreed time, usually shorter than franchise term. Historically extended for full term but "we do not intend to do so in the future."
- **Major carve-outs**: (a) existing/approved hotels; (b) other Hilton brands (not Curio); (c) hotels opening after Restrictive Period; (d) gaming hotels; (e) vacation ownership/timeshare; (f) acquisitions of 4+ hotel chains.
- Strategic Partnerships may place competitor accommodations in your area, including within Restricted Area.
- No mechanism to resolve conflicts between your hotel and Network hotels — at Hilton's business judgment.
- No relocation permitted. No options or rights of first refusal for additional franchises.
- **Key risk**: The restricted area carve-outs are so broad that protection is essentially illusory for chain-scale acquisitions, multi-brand deployments, and vacation ownership.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
- Principal Mark: CURIO (Reg. #5100741, Dec 13, 2016). Held by Hilton International Holding USA Corporation.
- HILTON mark (Reg. #2478190). EFOREA marks. All licensed to franchisor.

- No known infringements or material proceedings.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Term**: New Construction: 23 years from Effective Date. Conversion: 10-20 years from Opening Date.
- **No renewal right.** You do not have the right to renew or extend.
- **Re-licensing**: At franchisor's sole discretion. May require materially different terms. Must comply with any PIP conditions.
- **"Materially different" risk**: This is a significant issue — at end of term, you have no assurance of continuity and may face a brand-new agreement with substantially different economics.

### Termination
- **By you**: Not authorized. Unilateral termination = material breach → Liquidated Damages.
- **By franchisor without cause**: On condemnation or casualty (with release from LD if no competitor hotel at site within 3 years).
- **By franchisor with cause (curable)**: Non-payment (10-day cure), construction delays (10-day cure), insurance failure (10-day cure), other Standards violations (30-day cure). May impose interim remedies during cure period (suspension from reservation system, removal from directories, software disabling, revocation of financial accommodations).
- **By franchisor with cause (non-curable)**: Repeated breach within 24 months, 3 notices in 12 months, bankruptcy/insolvency, loss of possession, 5-day operational cessation, contesting Marks, reputational harm, fraud, unauthorized transfer, becoming Sanctioned Person or Competitor, guarantor breach, public health threat, breach of other Hotel Agreements.

### Transfer
- **Permitted Transfers** (no consent needed): publicly traded equity, private equity <50% with no control change, fund transfers with same asset manager.
- **Permitted Transfers** (consent needed, $5,500 fee): affiliate, family/trust, death, private equity >50% changed since Effective Date.
- **Change of Ownership Transfers**: $150,000 fee, 60-day notice, no existing default, new FA required, PIP, transferee must meet current requirements. Must not be Competitor or Sanctioned Person.
- **No right of first refusal** by franchisor.
- **No option to purchase** by franchisor.
- **Death/disability**: Transfer to immediate family within 1 year, must meet requirements.

### Noncompete
- **During term**: You/Affiliates may not own or be a licensor/franchisor of a competing hotel brand. MAY be franchisee of competitor or manage competitor property. May own minority interest under certain circumstances.
- **Post-term**: NONE. No post-termination noncompete.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Forum**: US District Court for Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria) or Fairfax County, VA state court, or New York, NY. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Puerto Rico**: Arbitration (AAA/JAMS), venue Fairfax County VA, seat New York.
- **Governing law**: New York law.
- **No general mediation or arbitration requirement** (except Puerto Rico).

### Post-Termination Obligations
- Pay all sums including liquidated damages
- Cease operations as System Hotel, cease using Marks
- Return Manual and Proprietary Information
- Cancel all registrations, transfer domain names to franchisor
- De-identify hotel (remove signs, promotional materials)
