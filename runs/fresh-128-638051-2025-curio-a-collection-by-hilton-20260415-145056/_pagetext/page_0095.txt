Room Rate, Occupancy, and RevPAR Before and After Conversion 
 
Of the 88 Comparable Hotels identified above, 61 were Conversions. Of those 61 Conversions, 
all 61 began operating as Curio Brand hotels between 2014 and 2023 (the “Conversion 
Period”). Of the 61 Conversion hotels that began operating during the Conversion Period, 16 
were: (a) were open and operating in the US during calendar year 2024 (including those that were 
only open and operating for part of the year); and (b) reported business performance data to STR 
(“Comparable Conversion Hotels”). Of these 16 Comparable Conversion Hotels, 13 (81.3%) were 
Franchisee-Managed and 3 (18.8%) were Company-Managed.

As used above, the term “Cool-Down Period” means the 6-month period immediately before each 
Comparable Conversion Hotel opened and began operating as a Curio Brand hotel. The Cool-
Down Period is a reasonable approximation of the average time it typically takes for a pre-
Conversion hotel to prepare for a Conversion. The term “Ramp-Up Period” means the 6-month 
period immediately after each Comparable Conversion Hotel opened and began operating as a 
Curio Brand hotel. The Ramp-Up Period is excluded as a reasonable approximation of the 
average time it typically takes for a converted hotel to begin stabilizing in the market as a newly 
opened or reopened hotel under the Brand. “RevPAR” means the revenue per available room as 
reported by STR for the Pre-Conversion period and reported to us during the Post-Conversion 
period, which is calculated by dividing the total rooms revenue by the total number of rooms 
available.
