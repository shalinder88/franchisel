## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** 732 franchised outlets in the United States at end of 2024. Zero company-owned. Projected 76 new franchises in 2025. No international outlets disclosed (international operations exist through EOS OpCo subsidiaries).
2. **Entity structure:** EOS Worldwide Franchising, LLC (Delaware LLC, formed January 11, 2021) is a wholly-owned subsidiary of EOS HoldCo, LLC (Michigan). The operational affiliate, EOS Worldwide, LLC (EOS OpCo), owns all trademarks and IP under a 99-year license agreement. The founders (Gino Wickman and Don Tinney) sold majority interests in 2018 and now operate as franchisees.
3. **Ownership history:** Transitioned from a membership-based model to a franchise model in February 2021. Pre-existing EOS Implementers could sign franchise agreements and continue operating. This dual history makes Item 19 data more complex — some metrics include performance from the pre-franchise era.
4. **Investment range:** $61,045 to $150,660 total. The dominant cost is the $45,000 Boot Camp fee (30%–74% of total). Initial franchise fee is $5,000. Monthly Membership Fee of $1,395–$1,695 replaces traditional percentage-based royalty.
5. **Fee burden:** Flat monthly fees are favorable for high-revenue operators and unfavorable for low-revenue operators compared to percentage royalties. A Professional-tier franchisee earning the average $162,542/year pays $16,740/year in membership (10.3% effective rate). An Expert earning $704,649 pays $20,340 (2.9% effective rate).
6. **Item 19 highlights:** System-wide average session revenue $418,274; median $367,975. Revenue only — no cost or profit data. 35.24% of 3+ year franchisees achieved $400K within first 3 years. The highest earner generated $1,583,500 in annual session revenue.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** Professional-tier franchisees (111 of 468) averaged $162,542 with a low of $20,000 and only 5 session days annually for the least active. The $20,000 annual revenue against $16,740 in membership fees alone represents a severe loss scenario.
8. **Financial health:** FY2024 revenue $23.8M, net income $2.7M. Clean RSM audit. However, 96% of assets ($5.7M of $5.9M) are intercompany receivables. Cash dropped 82% to $233K. Operating cash flow was negative despite profitability. A $17.9M note purchase agreement encumbers all assets.
9. **Growth trajectory:** Net additions: +136 (2022), +137 (2023), +60 (2024). Growth is decelerating while attrition is accelerating.
10. **Territory:** No exclusive territory whatsoever. Franchisor retains unlimited rights to compete and distribute.
11. **Renewal risk:** 5-year term with two 3-year renewals. Must sign then-current FA which may contain "materially different terms" + general release.
12. **Dispute resolution:** Mediation/arbitration in Michigan; litigation in Delaware. Multiple state addenda override this.
13. **Special risks:** Rising attrition (49 voluntary exits in 2024), intercompany financial dependency, no cost data in FPR, and unlimited franchisor competition rights.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

The franchisor is an asset-light Delaware LLC that generates revenue primarily through membership fees, Boot Camp fees, and required product sales. It does not own real estate, equipment, or inventory. Its balance sheet is dominated by intercompany receivables from its parent/affiliate structure.

The franchise model is a sole-practitioner consultancy. Each franchisee operates independently as the sole EOS Implementer for their franchise. No physical location is required. The business model is fundamentally a licensed coaching practice with brand, methodology, training, and community infrastructure.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Initial Franchise Fee: $5,000 (nonrefundable)
- Boot Camp Fee: $45,000 (nonrefundable, paid at signing)
- Start-Up Inventory: $1,500–$3,200
- Total to franchisor at signing: $52,895–$58,780

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Monthly Membership Fee: $1,395 (Professional), $1,495 (Certified), $1,695 (Expert) — flat monthly, not percentage
- Practice Management+: $195–$495/mo (optional CRM upgrade)
- Annual membership cost: $16,740–$20,340

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Warm Leads Fee: 50% of initial session revenue (min $1,500) — this is a significant per-event fee
- Speaking Referral Fee: 25% of gross revenue
- Transfer Fee: $5,000
- QCE beyond included: $1,000 each
- Additional/Remedial Training: $995–$10,000

**Reserved Fees (not yet imposed):**
- Brand Development Fund: up to $100/mo ($1,200/year)
- Technology Fee: up to $100/mo ($1,200/year)
- Combined potential: $2,400/year additional burden with 30–60 days notice

### Initial Investment

The $61,045–$150,660 range is moderate for a service franchise. The wide spread reflects the $54,630 "Additional Funds" variable at the high end. The fixed portion ($61,045 low end) is dominated by the $50,000 franchise fee + Boot Camp combination.

No financing is offered. SBA Franchise Registry eligible. No real estate costs are required.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

The franchisor operates a controlled supply chain for core materials:
- **Required from franchisor:** Leadership Team Manuals, designated session materials, business cards (via EOS Online Store)
- **Approved suppliers only:** BenBella Books (books), KalBlue Group/Commercial Graphics (US marketing materials), international equivalents
- **Prohibition on reproduction:** Cannot copy, replicate, or translate any copyrighted materials

Revenue from required purchases was $3,309,031 in 2024 (13.9% of franchisor total revenue). Required purchases represent 3%–15% of ongoing operating costs for franchisees. The franchisor reserves unlimited rights to add new required suppliers.

### Training Program

- **Boot Camp:** 27 hours over 3 days. Detroit, MI or Denver, CO (international locations available). Quarterly schedule. $45,000 fee. Must complete within 6 months of signing. Failure to complete = termination right.
- **180 Day Sprint Coach:** Assigned coach for first 180 days post-signing
- **Online Platform (Dojo):** Ongoing access included in membership. Contains tools, worksheets, videos, courses.
- **QCEs:** Quarterly Collaborative Exchanges. 1 per quarter included in membership. Minimum attendance required for tier maintenance. In-person attendance may be required. Travel at franchisee expense.
- **Additional Training:** Optional workshops $995–$10,000. Remedial training may be mandated.

### Technology Stack

- Computer less than 3 years old with current browser (Chrome preferred)
- EOS One / Practice Management (CRM) — included in membership
- Practice Management+ — optional upgrade $195–$495/mo
- No proprietary POS, no required hardware beyond computer
- Technology Fee reserved but not yet imposed

### Marketing Fund

No Brand Development Fund exists. No local advertising cooperatives. Franchisor reserves right to establish both. If established, Brand Development Fund would be unaudited (annual unaudited statement available on request) and the franchisor would control all spending.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### No Exclusive Territory

This is a home-based consultancy with no geographic restrictions. The franchisor explicitly states:

- "You do not receive an exclusive territory."
- "You may face competition from other franchisees, from outlets that we own, or from other channels of distribution or competitive brands that we control."
- Franchisor retains "sole discretion to offer products and services under the Marks in the same manner as offered by the EOS Worldwide franchises or in any other manner."

### Channel Restrictions

Franchisor may sell through website, app, e-commerce, retail, wholesale, catalog, telemarketing. Franchisee receives no compensation for franchisor direct sales.

### Practical Implications

With 732 franchisees and no territorial limits, the market is increasingly saturated in key metros. Texas alone has 76 Implementers. The lack of territory protection means any franchisee's clients could be targeted by other Implementers or by the franchisor directly.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

- **Initial term:** 5 years
- **Renewal:** Two 3-year terms (total potential: 11 years)
- **Renewal conditions:** Good standing, current obligations paid, sign then-current FA, complete required training, execute general release
- **"Materially different" risk:** The then-current FA may contain materially different terms. This gives the franchisor unilateral ability to change the deal on renewal.

### Transfer Provisions

- Transfer fee: $5,000
- Transferee must complete Boot Camp at current rates and sign current FA
- General release required from transferor
- 90-day transition support required
- No right of first refusal by franchisor
- Option to purchase at book value on termination

### Termination

- Franchisee: 60 days' notice (extremely clean exit for a franchise)
- Franchisor: for cause only (both curable and non-curable defaults)
- Non-curable: felony, brand damage, unauthorized transfer, bankruptcy, failure to complete training, 3+ defaults, fraud
- Curable: general 30 days; payment 10 days; marks 5 business days; insurance 5 business days

### Death/Disability

Must transfer to new Implementer in franchisor-designated timeframe. Franchisor may step in and charge management fee.

### Noncompete

- **During term:** No competing business, no client diversion
- **Post-term (1 year):** Cannot solicit EOS clients or induce strategic partners. BUT may continue non-EOS services for existing clients.
- The post-term noncompete is narrower than many franchises — it restricts EOS client solicitation but not coaching generally.

### Dispute Resolution

- Mediation/arbitration: Michigan (at franchisor's discretion)
- Litigation: Delaware
- Governing law: Delaware
- This is a significant out-of-state burden for franchisees outside MI/DE

### Litigation History

None required to be disclosed in Item 3 or Item 4.

### Critical Caveats

1. **Revenue only — no cost, expense, or profit data.** The California addendum explicitly warns: "The financial performance representations do not reflect the costs of sales, operating expenses, or other costs or expenses that must be deducted from the gross revenue or gross sales figures to obtain your net income or profit."

4. **Mixed history.** Includes pre-franchise membership model data (back to 2008) for the 400K System table.
5. **Substantiation available** on reasonable request.

### Revenue Distribution Analysis

The mean ($418,274) exceeds the median ($367,975) by 14%, indicating right-skew (a minority of high earners pulling the average up). Only 41.88% of franchisees exceed the average — confirming the skew.

At the Professional tier, the $20,000 annual low represents a franchisee conducting just 5 session days at an average of ~$4,000/session. Against annual membership fees of $16,740, this franchisee would be losing money before any other expenses.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Asset-light, intercompany-heavy:** 96% of assets are "due from affiliates." The entity has no fixed assets, no inventory, no receivables from external parties. If the affiliate cannot repay, the entity is essentially insolvent.
2. **Cash drain despite profitability:** Net income was $2.7M but operating cash flow was negative $1.0M because $3.3M was lent to affiliates. The entity is funding its parent/affiliate structure from operating cash flow.
3. **Interest burden:** $1.17M annually on a note purchase agreement. The note has a balance of ~$17.9M (2024) secured by all company assets. The interest rate and maturity are not disclosed in the notes.
4. **Deferred revenue decline:** $2.3M→$1.8M. This represents Boot Camp fees collected but not yet recognized. The decline suggests fewer Boot Camp pre-payments are being collected relative to recognition, consistent with slowing new franchisee additions.
5. **Member's deficit:** Cumulative member distributions of $3.716M exceed contributed capital. Retained earnings of $7.86M more than cover this, resulting in positive equity of $4.1M.
6. **Related-party transactions:** All expenses are paid by the Affiliate and allocated to the Company under an expense sharing agreement. The intercompany balance is periodically netted for cash flow purposes. This structure means the entity's actual cost base is an allocation, not direct spending.

## I. State Addenda Summary

15 states have addenda to the FDD. Key material overrides by family:

### Forum Selection Overrides
- **Illinois:** Forum outside IL void for franchise disputes
- **Indiana:** Jurisdiction/venue outside IN deleted
- **Minnesota:** Litigation outside MN prohibited, no jury waiver, no liquidated damages
- **North Dakota:** Arbitration site must be agreeable to all parties, not remote from franchisee; forum changed to ND
- **Washington:** Arbitration/mediation in WA or mutually agreed location; litigation may be brought in WA
- **Rhode Island:** Franchisee may bring action in RI for state franchise law claims
- **Maryland:** Franchisee may sue in MD for state franchise law claims

### Governing Law Overrides
- **Illinois:** Illinois law governs
- **Indiana:** Indiana law supersedes conflicting provisions
- **North Dakota:** ND law supersedes conflicting provisions
- **Washington:** WA Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails on conflict

### Noncompete Limitations
- **California:** Post-term noncompete may not be enforceable under CA law
- **North Dakota:** Covenants restricting competition generally unenforceable
- **Washington:** Noncompete void unless earnings exceed $100K/year (employees) or $250K/year (contractors), adjusted annually for inflation

### General Release Restrictions
- **Maryland:** Release does not apply to MD Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law claims
- **Minnesota:** Franchisor prohibited from requiring general release (Rule 2860.4400(D))
- **Washington:** Release void except in negotiated settlement with independent counsel

### Termination Protections
- **Minnesota:** 90 days' notice of termination (60 days to cure); 180 days' notice of non-renewal
- **Virginia:** Unlawful to cancel without reasonable cause per VA Retail Franchising Act

### Damages
- **North Dakota:** Waiver of exemplary/punitive damages, jury trial, liquidated damages — all unfair and deleted
- **Washington:** Waiver of exemplary/punitive damages void; treble damages available under RCW 19.100.190

### Other Notable Overrides
- **California:** Interest rate capped at higher of 10% or 5% + Fed Reserve rate; FPR cost caveat required
- **Maryland:** Surety bond required; 3-year statute of limitations
- **Washington:** Indemnification limited (no obligation for indemnified party's negligence/willful misconduct); transfer fees limited to reasonable costs; buy-back without consent unlawful
