## Pass B: Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — The Franchisor

EOS Worldwide Franchising, LLC is a Delaware LLC formed January 11, 2021 as a wholly-owned subsidiary of EOS HoldCo, LLC (Michigan, formed November 13, 2008). The franchise system began offering franchises February 16, 2021, transitioning from a pre-existing membership model. The founder, Gino Wickman, and co-founder Don Tinney sold majority interests in EOS HoldCo in May 2018 and are now franchisees.

The principal operational affiliate is EOS Worldwide, LLC (EOS OpCo, Michigan, formed June 15, 2018), a wholly-owned subsidiary of EOS HoldCo. EOS OpCo owns all trademarks and intellectual property. International subsidiaries: EOSW International, Inc. (Michigan corp) and EOS Worldwide UK, LTD.

The franchisor does not own or operate any businesses of the type franchised. From time to time, certain full-time corporate employees maintain part-time Implementer businesses — these are individually owned and not treated as company-owned outlets.

The franchise is a home-based/remote consultancy providing entrepreneurial training and coaching using the Entrepreneurial Operating System® (EOS). Implementers deliver session-based services (Focus Days, Vision Building, Quarterly Pulsing, Annual Planning) to business clients. No physical location is required, though franchisees may rent session space.

Market: Competitive with independent consultants, coaching franchises, educational institutions, peer-to-peer organizations. Some seasonality (higher sessions November–February for annual planning).

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory

**Critical: No exclusive territory.** The franchise grants worldwide service rights but no territorial protection whatsoever. Key implications:

- Franchisor can grant unlimited additional franchises anywhere
- Franchisor retains right to offer competing services under the same marks
- Franchisor may sell through alternative channels (website, app, e-commerce, retail, catalog, telemarketing)
- No option or right of first refusal for additional franchises
- No restrictions on franchisee relocation (but must notify franchisor and obtain approval for community change)

**Tier system governs franchise rights more than territory:**
- Professional: entry level, $1,395/mo, limited service offerings
- Certified: requires 80+ lifetime session days, 30+/year, 8.75+ rating, $1,495/mo
- Expert: requires 500+ lifetime session days, EOS 400K System achievement, $1,695/mo
- Legacy: 10+ years, semi-retired, <30 sessions/year, $1,395/mo
- Emeritus: by invitation, distinguished contributors, $1,395/mo

**Performance requirements trigger termination risk:** Professional tier must maintain minimum 20 session days per 4 quarters, $3,000 minimum session fee, 8.75+ rating. Failure leads to "Cure" period (2 quarters), then potential remedial training, then potential termination.

### Item 13 — Trademarks

18+ registered marks owned by EOS OpCo, licensed to franchisor under 99-year license (since Feb 15, 2021). Key marks: EOS®, Entrepreneurial Operating System®, Traction®, EOS Implementer®, V/TO®, IDS®, Level 10 Meeting®, Focus Day®, Six Key Components®, QCE®.

Some marks not federally registered — franchisor acknowledges these "do not have many legal benefits and rights as a federally registered trademark."

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information

No patents. Copyrights claimed on Operations Manual, training materials, session materials. All franchisee-developed IP must be disclosed and is assigned to franchisor (work-for-hire). Client data is franchisor property.

**AI clause noted:** Franchise Agreement Section 8.6 includes "Artificial Intelligence Usage" provisions (in FA table of contents).

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate

Franchisee must be the sole Implementer. Must maintain 51%+ ownership of Operating Entity. Only the named Implementer may provide sessions to clients. Boot Camp must be completed before providing services.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on Sales

Must offer EOS "in its purest form." Cannot offer unauthorized products/services. "Item 16 review" process: franchisees must clear outside business opportunities that could conflict with EOS brand. Three rules: (1) don't damage the brand, (2) don't use EOS IP without permission, (3) ask before investing time or money in outside ventures.

Part-time operation is explicitly permitted — "one of the benefits of EOS."

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal

- **Initial term:** 5 years (ending last day of calendar month following 5-year anniversary)
- **Renewal:** Up to two 3-year terms
- **Renewal conditions:** (i) prior notice, (ii) maintenance/upgrades, (iii) good standing, (iv) all monetary obligations current, (v) sign then-current FA, (vi) meet current new franchisee requirements + training, (vii) execute general release, (viii) franchisor still offering franchises
- **"Materially different terms" risk:** The then-current FA may contain materially different terms and conditions. This is a significant renewal risk.
- **No renewal fee currently** (reserved right for future)

### Termination

- **By franchisee:** 60 days' written notice (or 10 days before Boot Camp registration). Must execute release, be in good standing, pay fees through termination date. No refunds.
- **By franchisor without cause:** Not permitted
- **Curable defaults (30 days unless shorter specified):** Late monthly payments (10 days), other late payments (10 days), unauthorized mark use (5 business days), insurance lapse (5 business days), third-party debts (30 days), minimum performance failure (30 days + remedial training)
- **Non-curable defaults (immediate):** Felony/moral turpitude conviction, misconduct harming brand, unauthorized transfer, bankruptcy/insolvency, failure to complete Boot Camp, repeated defaults (3+ in any term), fraud/misrepresentation, failure to commence operations, tax default

### Transfer

- Transfer fee: $5,000 (waived partially for death/disability/family transfers; waived for internal equity transfers with no Implementer change)
- Conditions: full compliance, transferee meets criteria + completes Boot Camp, transferee signs then-current FA, general release, franchisor approval (not unreasonably withheld), 90-day transition support
- No right of first refusal
- Franchisor option to purchase at book value (cost less depreciation) on termination

### Noncompete

- **During term:** Prohibited from operating/owning interest in competing business, diverting clients, interfering with franchisor business relationships
- **Post-term (1 year):** Prohibited from soliciting EOS clients, inducing strategic partners/referral sources, diverting business relationships. BUT: may continue with non-EOS services for existing clients where such services were provided during the franchise term.

### Dispute Resolution

- **Mediation/arbitration:** Michigan, at franchisor's discretion (Section 20.4–20.5)
- **Litigation venue:** Delaware (Section 20.2)
- **Choice of law:** Delaware (Section 20.1), except federal law where applicable (Lanham Act for trademarks, federal copyright law)

### Death/Disability

Must transfer to new approved Implementer in time designated. Franchisor may step in to manage and charge management fee.
