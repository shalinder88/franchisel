## A. Executive Snapshot

- **Total investment:** $348,400–$4,705,600 excluding real estate; franchise fee $50,000 standard (20-year term), prorated for shorter terms, minimum $15,000.
- **Royalty:** 4.5% of monthly Gross Sales (standard); numerous incentive programs can reduce to as low as 2.5% in Year 1 for new development.
- **Advertising contribution:** Up to 4.5% of Gross Sales (currently 4.5%), plus up to 2.0% Investment Spending (DMA-level), yielding a combined royalty+ad burden of up to 11.0% of Gross Sales before DMA spending.
- **Systemwide outlets (12/31/2024):** 6,701 total U.S. (5,524 franchised, 1,177 company-owned). Massive shift in 2024: RBI acquired Carrols Restaurant Group (1,023 units converted from franchised to company-owned in May 2024).
- **Net system shrinkage:** System lost 77 net outlets in 2024, 264 in 2023, and 62 in 2022. Franchised count dropped from 7,053 (start 2022) to 5,524 (end 2024), a loss of 1,529 franchised units in three years—much of it from the Carrols reacquisition.
- **Average Traditional franchise-owned sales:** $1,658,463; company-owned $1,730,667. Median franchise-owned $1,561,502. Non-traditional average: $1,321,324.
- **Remodel uplift:** 893 restaurants remodeled to Modern Image; average sales uplift 12.3% (Full remodel 11.4%, Scrape & Rebuild 26.1%). Uplift holds or improves at 3 years post-remodel (13.4% cumulative average).
- **No exclusive territory.** Target Areas are non-exclusive. BKC reserves all rights to develop, sell through alternate channels (grocery, internet, delivery), and operate competing BURGER KING restaurants nearby.
- **20-year term** standard. Successor franchise available if you meet conditions (including remodel). No bankruptcy disclosed. Major litigation includes the Carrols/CRG shareholder suit and antitrust no-poach class action.
- **Digital Services Fee:** $110/month + 1% digital sales (capped $4,500/year). Exhibit V (Digital App Services Agreement) is a significant technology and data-access burden.
- **Parent guarantor:** RBI guarantees franchisee obligations (or RBILP for CA, IL, MD, ND, RI, VA, WA residents). Audited by KPMG, unqualified opinion. RBI total assets $24.6B, long-term debt $13.5B, net income $1.445B (2024).
- **Multiple remodel and incentive programs:** Reclaim the Flame 2, Fuel the Flame, BKoT, Crown Your Career, Development Agreements with FSS-graded royalty reductions. Significant contractual complexity.
- **Carrols Refranchise Program:** RBI is actively refranchising the 1,177 company-owned restaurants acquired from Carrols.
- **Crown Your Career Program:** BKC employees can purchase restaurants with BKC-provided financing (promissory note + security agreement).

## B. Pass 1 — Front Matter, Formats, Entry Structure, Fee Stack, and Initial Investment

### Franchisor
Burger King Company LLC, a Florida LLC formed February 4, 2022. Indirect wholly-owned subsidiary of Restaurant Brands International Limited Partnership (RBILP), whose general partner is Restaurant Brands International Inc. (RBI), a Canadian corporation publicly traded on NYSE/TSX. RBI also owns Tim Hortons, Popeyes, and Firehouse Subs. Principal office: 5707 Blue Lagoon Drive, Miami, FL 33126.

Predecessor: BK Corporation (founded 1954, dissolved December 2022). Internal reorganization in August 2022 transferred all assets/liabilities to BKC.

### Concept
Quick-service hamburger restaurants operating under the BURGER KING marks and system. As of 12/31/2024: 19,732 BURGER KING restaurants worldwide, 6,701 in the U.S.

### Issuance Date
03/2025, as amended 01/2026.

### Formats
Two franchise agreement forms:
1. **Individual/Owner-Operator** (Exhibit D1): Individuals sign personally; Operating Partner designated.
2. **Entity** (Exhibit D2): Corporation, LP, LLP, or LLC can execute; Managing Owner required (minimum 25% equity), Guaranty (Exhibit D3) required. Corporate Addendum (Exhibit J1) available for parent entities with Managing Director.

Restaurant types include Traditional (free-standing with double drive-thru), Non-Traditional (in-line, food court, institutional, drive-thru only, mall, mobile, Big Box Retail), and Fuel Co-Branded (gas station attached).

### Development Structure
- **Target Reservation Agreement (TRA):** $5,000 deposit, non-exclusive Target Area to search for one site.
- **Multiple Target Reservation Agreement (MTRA):** $10,000 per committed opening, non-exclusive.
- **Development Agreement (Exhibit M1):** Multi-unit commitment with development schedule. Prepay $50,000 per committed restaurant. FSS-graded royalty reductions for A/B operators.

### Special-Risk Signals
Two disclosed:
1. Out-of-state dispute resolution (litigation only in Florida).
2. Non-binding mediation required before suit on development disputes.

Michigan Franchise Investment Act notice also included (pages 5–6).

## D. Pass 3 — Territory, Competition, Channels, and Encroachment

### Item 12

**No exclusive territory.** The TRA and MTRA define Target Areas for site search only—non-exclusive.

**Protected area limits:** None. BKC may establish and license other restaurants at any other locations, including near your restaurant. Other BURGER KING restaurants may compete with your restaurant.

**Reserved rights (extensive):**
- BKC and affiliates may sell BURGER KING branded products through grocery stores, convenience stores, internet, and other direct marketing channels.
- BKC may operate or license other competing restaurants.
- BKC's affiliates may operate other franchise concepts (Tim Hortons, Popeyes, Firehouse Subs).

**Internet/direct sales:** BKC reserves right to sell through internet and other channels.

**Non-traditional carveouts:** Non-traditional restaurants (institutional, airports, military, colleges, retail, turnpikes, food courts, drive-thru only, Big Box Retail, delivery/ghost kitchens) compete within the system.

**Affiliate competition:** RBI affiliates operate Tim Hortons, Popeyes, and Firehouse Subs—all quick-service competitors. RBI may operate third-party concepts in combination.

**Alternate channels / delivery / encroachment:** Delivery through BKC-approved third-party providers, BURGER KING mobile app and website. BKC controls the digital ordering channel. BKC may develop Fuel Co-Branded restaurants. No restriction on BKC placing restaurants in proximity to existing franchisees, though BKC will give existing franchisees the option to develop if BKC proposes to develop in their Target Area under the TRA/MTRA.

## E. Pass 4 — Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Item 17

The Item 17 summary table (Renewal, Termination, Transfer and Dispute Resolution) spans pages 82–97. Key provisions:

**Term:** 20 years standard. Non-traditional and shorter property-control terms may be shorter, minimum 6 years. Prorated fee for shorter terms.

**Renewal/Successor:** Available if you meet conditions including remodel to then-current standards, signing then-current form of franchise agreement (which may differ materially), compliance with all obligations, payment of successor franchise fee. BKC has discretion to decline.

**Transfer:** BKC approval required. Transfer fee: $2,000 + $500/additional restaurant. BKC has right of first refusal. Transferee must meet all BKC requirements. You must sign a general release.

**Default/Termination:**
- Immediate termination for bankruptcy, abandonment, health/safety violations, criminal conviction, fraud, misrepresentation.
- 30-day cure for most defaults.
- BKC may terminate for failure to meet remodel deadlines, repeated defaults, failure to meet development schedule.

**Post-term obligations:** Noncompete: 1 year, within 2 miles of the Premises (FA D1 Section 19) / 2 miles of any of your BK restaurants (DA Art. VII), for the sale of hamburgers. CYC enhancement: no ANY business until 5th anniversary or loan repayment (Managing Owner only, per Exhibit Z1). Perpetual: no use of BK System/marks/secrets (FA Section 12). De-identification within 30 days.

**Forum:** Litigation only in Florida. Mediation required for development disputes before suit.

**Governing law:** Florida.

**Liquidated damages:** Not directly surfaced in the Item 17 text read, but deferred remodel default royalty increases (to 6.0% or 7.5%) and Brand Damage Fee under Development Agreement function similarly.

**Guaranty (Exhibit D3):** Required for entity ownership. Owner(s) personally guarantee all Franchisee obligations.

**Release:** Required on transfer and successor.

**Remodel/Re-equip obligations:** Extensive. BKC may require remodel to then-current standards as a condition of successor franchise. Multiple remodel programs (RTF2, BKoT, Fuel the Flame) create layered remodel obligations with penalty royalty rates for non-compliance.
