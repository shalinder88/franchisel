#### 1. First International Fund Ltd. v. Burger King Corporation et al
- **Case No.:** CO-13-487734
- **Court:** Ontario Superior Court of Justice
- **Filed:** August 28, 2013
- **Parties:** First International Fund Ltd. (plaintiff, entity owned by Marc Vaturi of Triple Five International Group Ltd.) v. BK Corporation and BK Corporation's former master franchisee in Canada
- **Core Allegations:** FIF alleges that on January 28, 2013, before its incorporation, Vaturi reached a binding verbal agreement with BK Corporation representatives for BK Corporation to sell all shares of Burger King Restaurants Canada Inc. ("BKRC") to an entity to be designated by Vaturi. FIF alleges written documents were exchanged to memorialize the agreement but were not necessary to make the oral agreement binding. BK Corporation sold BKRC to another company (an affiliate of which was formerly its master franchisee in Canada) on April 22, 2013.
- **Relief Sought:** Specific performance (conveyance of shares of BKRC/Redberry Franchising Corp. to FIF), or in the alternative money damages in excess of $500 million.
- **Status:** On July 20, 2018, the action against BKRC was dismissed. Certain discoveries took place in March 2019. Examinations for discovery took place in March 2025. Case remains pending.
- **BKC Party Status:** BK Corporation is a party (through predecessor).
- **Commercial Relevance:** Dispute over sale of the Canadian master franchisee entity; potential $500M+ exposure.

#### 2. Arrington v. Burger King Worldwide, Inc. (Consolidated No-Poach Class Actions)
- **Case No.:** 18-24128-CV-MARTINEZ/AOR (consolidated)
- **Court:** U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Florida
- **Filed:** October-November 2018 (four separate complaints consolidated January 17, 2019)
- **Component Cases:**
  - Jarvis Arrington v. Burger King Worldwide and Burger King Corporation (1:18-cv-24128-JEM)
  - Monique Michel v. RBI, BK Worldwide, BK Corporation (1:18-cv-24304-JEM)
  - Geneva Blanchard and Tiffany Miller v. BK Corporation and BK Worldwide (1:18-cv-24576-SCOLA/TORRES)
  - Sandra Munster v. RBI, BK Worldwide, BK Corporation (1:18-cv-24623-RNS)
- **Core Allegations:** Plaintiffs allege Defendants violated Section 1 of the Sherman Antitrust Act by incorporating an employee no-solicitation and no-hiring clause in the standard franchise agreement.
- **Relief Sought:** Injunctive relief and damages for plaintiffs and the class.
- **Status:** Motion to dismiss granted March 24, 2020. Appellate court reversed and remanded August 31, 2022. Court denied BKC's renewed motion to dismiss April 9, 2025. Amended complaint filed April 30, 2025; BKC answered May 21, 2025. Court ordered mediation by April 3, 2026 (ordered December 22, 2025).
- **BKC Party Status:** BK Corporation and affiliates are defendants.
- **Commercial Relevance:** Class action antitrust challenge to no-poach clauses in franchise agreements; systemic franchise-agreement risk.

#### 3. Mister Crab s.r.l., Krusty s.r.l. and Gianni Ugolini v. Burger King Europe GmbH et al
- **Case No.:** 33924/2024
- **Court:** Milan Tribunal (Italy)
- **Filed:** 2024 (writ of summons received by BKE)
- **Parties:** Mister Crab s.r.l. (Italian franchisee) and its shareholders v. BKE, Burger King SEE (Switzerland) AG (master franchisee for Italy), and Burger King Restaurants Italia S.r.l.
- **Core Allegations:** Claimants allege that certain franchise agreement clauses -- particularly those requiring royalties and advertising contributions calculated as a percentage of gross sales -- should be declared void. They seek reimbursement of all royalties and advertising contributions paid over the years. They also allege that the franchisors' conduct has been "particularly rigid and not in good faith."
- **Relief Sought:** Declaration that royalty/advertising clauses are void; reimbursement of all such payments; relief for bad-faith conduct.
- **Status:** Initial hearing April 2, 2025; matter re-assigned to another division. New initial hearing scheduled January 27, 2026.
- **BKC Party Status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."
- **Commercial Relevance:** Challenge to the enforceability of percentage-of-gross-sales royalty structure in Italian franchise agreements.

#### 5. Jonathan Deschatelets v. Restaurant Brands International Inc. et al
- **Case No.:** 500-06-001306-246
- **Court:** Superior Court of Quebec
- **Filed:** April 19, 2024
- **Parties:** Consumer plaintiff v. RBI, RBILP, and The TDL Group Corp.
- **Core Allegations:** TDL sent an email to approximately 500,000 customers stating they won a boat worth CAD $64,000 as part of the "Roll Up To Win" promotion. A second corrective email was sent the same day attributing the first to a technical error. Plaintiff argues this constitutes a prohibited practice under the Consumer Protection Act and that TDL should honor the representations.
- **Relief Sought:** Specific performance (delivery of the boat/prizes to class members), or payment of equivalent damages, plus punitive damages of CAD $10,000 per class member.
- **Status:** Class certified June 27, 2025 (limited to Quebec residents). TDL is appealing class certification; appeal hearing anticipated March 2026.
- **BKC Party Status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."
- **Commercial Relevance:** Tim Hortons promotional error class action; potential mass damages exposure.

#### 6. Plymouth County Retirement Association et al v. Restaurant Brands International Inc. et al

- **Court:** Court of Chancery of the State of Delaware
- **Filed:** October 7, 2024

- **BKC Party Status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."
- **Commercial Relevance:** Shareholder litigation arising from RBI's acquisition of its largest franchisee (Carrols Restaurant Group).

#### 7. Daniel Pemberton v. Restaurant Brands International, Inc. & Restaurant Brands International US Services LLC
- **Case No.:** 4:25-cv-03647-JCS
- **Court:** U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California
- **Filed:** May 2, 2025
- **Core Allegations:** Class action alleging that www.bk.com secretly allowed third-party advertising and analytics firms (Google, Meta/Facebook, Microsoft Clarity, Snap, The Trade Desk, AdTheorent, and others) to place tracking cookies on visitors even after users opted out through the cookie-consent banner. Claims violations of California Invasion of Privacy Act (CIPA) wiretap and pen-register provisions, common law intrusion, trespass to chattels, fraud, unjust enrichment, and invasion of privacy.
- **Relief Sought:** Statutory, compensatory, and punitive damages; injunctive relief.
- **Status:** RBI filed motion to compel arbitration and motion to dismiss June 10, 2025. Court denied motion to compel arbitration September 5, 2025. Court partially granted motion to dismiss November 24, 2025 -- dismissed both CIPA claims as time-barred and trespass to chattels claim. Leave to amend granted; amended complaint due January 29, 2026. Remaining claims allowed to proceed. Case management conference set January 28, 2026.
- **BKC Party Status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."
- **Commercial Relevance:** Privacy/tracking class action targeting the Burger King website directly.

#### 8. ADP Direct Poultry Ltd. v. Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen, Inc., Restaurant Brands International Inc. et al
- **Case No.:** CV-25-00741914-0000
- **Court:** Ontario Superior Court of Justice
- **Filed:** May 29, 2025 (amended June 23, 2025; further amended November 19, 2025)
- **Parties:** ADP Direct Poultry Ltd. (former supplier) v. PLK, RBI, Restaurant Services Canada Inc., Amjad Farooq Inc., and others
- **Core Allegations:** Breach of contract, breach of duty of good faith, misrepresentations, conspiracy, negligence, loss of opportunity, promissory estoppel, estoppel by convention, breach of the Competition Act, and defamation. ADP contends that an unauthorized supplier (Amjad Farooq Inc.) sold uninspected and unsafe chicken to Ontario Popeyes restaurants with defendants' knowledge, and that defendants conspired to terminate ADP's supplier role in breach of an alleged oral supply agreement.
- **Relief Sought:** $35 million in damages; $1 million in aggravated and punitive damages; injunction preventing distribution of chicken from non-compliant suppliers; defamation-related damages including injunctive relief; costs.
- **Status:** ADP amended claim November 19, 2025 to add additional defendants. PLK and RBI served motion to strike and dismiss December 19, 2025.
- **BKC Party Status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."
- **Commercial Relevance:** Popeyes supply chain dispute; food safety allegations involving unauthorized supplier.

#### 9. Keswani and Sivertson v. PH Canada Company, Popeyes Louisiana Kitchen, Inc., Restaurant Brands International, Inc. et al (Drip Pricing Class Action)
- **Case No.:** VLC-S-S-254553
- **Court:** Supreme Court of British Columbia
- **Filed:** June 18, 2025
- **Parties:** Payal Keswani and Kendra Sivertson v. PLK, RBI, and 10 other fast-food companies/affiliates (Pizza Hut, Domino's, Little Caesar, Papa John's, PizzaPizza, Fresh Slice, BB.Q Chicken Canada)
- **Core Allegations:** Unlawful "drip pricing" on proprietary digital platforms -- displaying food prices without disclosing mandatory delivery fees until final stages of checkout, rendering initially advertised prices unattainable. Alleged breach of the Competition Act (Canada) and provincial consumer protection laws.
- **Relief Sought:** Damages, restitution, disgorgement of delivery and service fees, declaratory relief, and injunction. Proposed class: all Canadian consumers who paid such fees since June 18, 2023.
- **Status:** Filed. Class not yet certified.
- **BKC Party Status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."
- **Commercial Relevance:** Industry-wide digital ordering pricing practices challenge; affects Popeyes brand under RBI.

#### 10. Steve Hugi v. Restaurant Brands International US Services LLC and Restaurant Brands International Inc. (Firehouse Subs Cookie Tracking)
- **Case No.:** 1:25-cv-26046-DPG
- **Court:** U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Florida
- **Filed:** January 2, 2026
- **Core Allegations:** Plaintiff alleges the Firehouse Subs website (www.firehousesubs.com) deploys and transmits information to third-party platforms (Google, Meta) via non-essential cookies/trackers before the user can opt out, and continues doing so even after the user disables non-essential cookies. Claims violations of California Invasion of Privacy Act (CIPA) wiretap, pen register, and trap-and-trace provisions, Florida Security of Communications Act (FSCA), intrusion upon seclusion, fraud/deceit, and unjust enrichment.
- **Relief Sought:** Class certification (nationwide class, California and Florida subclasses), statutory damages under CIPA and FSCA, attorneys' fees, and other relief.
- **Status:** Filed January 2, 2026. Early stage.
- **BKC Party Status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."
- **Commercial Relevance:** Second privacy/tracking class action targeting an RBI brand website (Firehouse Subs); pattern with Pemberton/BK.com case.

#### 11. Liel Ohayon v. Starbucks Coffee Canada, Inc., The TDL Group Corp., Restaurant Brands International Inc. et al (Non-Dairy Surcharge Class Action)
- **Case No.:** 500-06-001351-242
- **Court:** Superior Court of Quebec
- **Filed:** December 30, 2024
- **Parties:** Liel Ohayon (vegan consumer) v. RBILP, RBI, TDL, Starbucks, Foodtastic/Second Cup, and other food/beverage companies
- **Core Allegations:** Price-gouging for non-dairy milk substitutes. Companies charged $0.50-$0.80 per drink surcharges for almond, soy, and oat milk despite these costing the same or less than regular milk at wholesale. Surcharges alleged to be disproportionate, exploitative, and unconscionable under Quebec's Consumer Protection Act and Civil Code of Quebec.
- **Relief Sought:** Reimbursement of surcharges, punitive damages, declaratory judgment that practices are abusive and illegal, changes in company practices.
- **Status:** Class has not been certified.
- **BKC Party Status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."
- **Commercial Relevance:** Tim Hortons-related (TDL is named); industry-wide consumer pricing challenge.

#### 12. Burger King Europe GmbH and Burger King Spain, S.L. v. Kasba Internacional S.L. and Alfonso Manuel Arroyo Sanchez
- **Case No.:** 1598/2023
- **Court:** Court of First Instance of Madrid (Spain)
- **Filed:** July 10, 2023
- **Parties:** BKE and Burger King Spain, S.L. ("BKS") v. Kasba Internacional S.L. ("Kasba", a legacy BK franchisee in Spain) and its owner Alfonso Manuel Arroyo Sanchez
- **Core Allegations (Claim):** Kasba disconnected its home delivery system from its POS system, understating gross sales by EUR 35,659.51 between January 1, 2022 and January 1, 2023. - **Status:** BKE/BKS filed response rejecting Kasba's allegations January 29, 2025. Pre-trial hearing scheduled March 17, 2027.
- **BKC Party Status:** BKE and its Spanish subsidiary are direct parties (franchisor-initiated).
- **Commercial Relevance:** Franchisee underreporting gross sales / POS manipulation; counterclaim for wrongful termination.

#### 16. Meals Catalunya Group v. Burger King Europe GmbH
- **Case No.:** 115/11
- **Court:** Civil Court of Pozuelo de Alarcon No. 4, Spain
- **Filed:** June 2011
- **Core Allegations:** Plaintiff alleged wrongful termination of franchise agreements for four restaurants in Catalunya, Spain. Claimed damages of EUR 8,500,000. BKE counterclaimed ~EUR 350,000 for post-termination royalties, advertising fund contributions, and trademark damages.
- **Outcome:** In June 2014, court found wrongful termination for 3 of 4 locations (Abrera, Villa Olimpica, Baricentro -- proper termination for Lerida). Awarded franchisee EUR 1,544,483 in damages and BKE EUR 180,562 in royalties plus EUR 600/day for continued trademark use. BKE deposited damage amount plus interest in February 2015. In September 2015, Plaintiff requested additional EUR 479,000 for legal costs and interest; BKE opposed. In March 2016, appeal court denied franchisee's appeal for higher damages but reduced BKE's royalty award for Lerida from EUR 180,562.23 to EUR 129,744.35.
- **Commercial Relevance:** Wrongful termination finding (3 of 4 locations); EUR 1.5M+ damages paid.

#### 18. Hapema Gastro AG v. Burger King Europe GmbH
- **Case No.:** HG180172O0
- **Court:** Handelsgericht Zurich (Commercial Court of Zurich, Switzerland)
- **Filed:** September 10, 2018
- **Core Allegations:** Hapema (franchisee of two BK restaurants in Basel) claimed BKE forced certain prices via coupons, causing lost profit.
- **Court Decision (December 7, 2021):** Court ordered BKE to pay CHF $270,000 damages and CHF $40,000 in court fees. Court declared BKE infringed the Swiss Cartel Act by fixing Hapema's prices and prohibited BKE from making price advertising within 15 km of Hapema's two restaurants. BKE's request to set off CHF $700,000 claim was rejected.
- **Outcome:** BKE appealed January 28, 2022. Same day, BKE entered settlement with Hapema and Swiss Master Franchisee BKCH. BKCH acquired shares in Hapema; parties agreed to settle, withdraw the appeal, and waive all claims.
- **Commercial Relevance:** Cartel Act / resale price maintenance finding against BKE; resolved by franchise buyout.
