## 1. Exhibit D3 -- Franchisee-Level Guaranty

### Guaranty present: YES

### Guarantor entity
The guarantor under Exhibit D3 is **the individual owner(s)** of the franchisee entity (or their spouses). This is a personal guaranty executed by one or more natural persons who hold an ownership interest in the franchisee or who are spouses of such persons. It is NOT a corporate-level parent guarantee.

### Scope of guaranty
- **Absolute, continuing, primary, and unconditional** guarantee of payment AND performance (not merely collection).
- Joint and several liability with the Franchisee and all other guarantors.
- Covers **all "Obligations"** defined as: all debts, liabilities, and obligations of Franchisee to BKC arising under any "BKC Agreements" (Franchise Agreement, Lease/Sublease, Crown Your Career Promissory Note if applicable, and all ancillary agreements), whether direct, indirect, absolute, contingent, matured or unmatured.
- Includes costs of collection, compromise, and enforcement including reasonable attorneys' fees.

### What is guaranteed
- Prompt payment of all Obligations.
- Personal liability for breach of each provision of all BKC Agreements, as if the guarantor were the franchisee, including:
  - Section 12 (Unfair Competition / non-compete)
  - Section 13 (Confidential Information)
  - Section 14 (Indemnification)
- Accuracy of Exhibit B to the Franchise Agreement (ownership disclosure).
- All amendments, modifications, extensions, and renewals of BKC Agreements.

### What is NOT guaranteed / Limitations
- **Transfer release (cash sale):** If guarantor transfers all interest for cash payment, liability terminates one year from transfer date, limited to accrued but unpaid royalties and advertising contributions during that period.
- **Transfer release (installment sale):** Liability terminates at the later of (i) one year from transfer or (ii) final installment payment; after first anniversary, liability limited to original purchase money debt amount.
- **Spousal transfer provisions:** Parallel release terms for spouses of transferring owners.
- The guaranty is irrevocable during the term and does not terminate upon death (binds the estate) or incapacity.

### Key waivers by guarantor
- Waiver of acceptance, demand, presentment, protest, notice of default, statute of limitations/laches.
- Waiver of subrogation and reimbursement rights against Franchisee.
- Waiver of trial by jury.
- Subordination of all debts owed by Franchisee to guarantor, to BKC's claims.

### Governing law and venue
- Florida law governs.
- U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Florida, or 11th Judicial Court in Miami-Dade County, Florida.

### Form details
- Exhibit D3, dated 03/2025.
- Template form with blanks for restaurant number, dates, and entity names.
- May be executed electronically per Uniform Electronic Transactions Act.

## 3. Structural Analysis

### Operating affiliate vs contractual obligor gap

| Layer | Entity | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Franchisor (contractual obligor) | Burger King Company LLC | Florida LLC; signs Franchise Agreement, Lease, etc. |
| Intermediate parent | Restaurant Brands International Limited Partnership (RBILP) | Ontario LP; indirect 100% owner of BKC |
| Ultimate parent | Restaurant Brands International Inc. (RBI) | Canadian corporation; sole general partner of RBILP; NYSE-listed |
| Operating entity | Carrols LLC (affiliate) | Operates BK company restaurants being refranchised |

**Key gap:** BKC (the franchisor) is a wholly-owned indirect subsidiary. It is an operating LLC, not the publicly traded entity. Without the Guarantee of Performance, a franchisee's contractual counterparty would be limited to BKC's own assets. The Guarantee of Performance bridges this by making either RBI or RBILP the backstop guarantor depending on franchise state.

### RBI vs RBILP guarantee assignment by state

| Guarantor | States |
|---|---|
| **RBILP** | California, Illinois, Maryland, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Virginia, Washington |
| **RBI** | All other states |

**Why the split:** These seven states have franchise registration requirements and/or specific regulatory provisions that require or favor the limited partnership entity (RBILP) as guarantor rather than the corporate parent (RBI). The RBILP guarantee is signed by RBI in its capacity as RBILP's general partner.

### Financial backing comparison

Both RBI and RBILP consolidate the same underlying operations. The RBI consolidated financial statements show:
- Total assets: $24,632M (Dec 31, 2024)
- Total equity: $4,843M
- Net income: $1,445M (2024)
- Long-term debt: $13,455M

RBILP's consolidated financials are substantially identical (same total assets, total revenues, net income) because RBILP is the operating partnership through which essentially all RBI activity flows. The primary difference is in equity structure: RBILP reports Class A common units and Partnership exchangeable units rather than common shares.

### What is NOT covered by the Guarantee of Performance

The Guarantee of Performance covers BKC's "duties and obligations" under the Franchise Agreement. It does NOT:
- Guarantee the franchisee's profitability or unit economics.
- Guarantee supply chain performance or third-party obligations.
- Create any direct contractual relationship between the franchisee and RBI/RBILP beyond the guarantee itself.
- Override the terms of the Franchise Agreement or expand BKC's obligations beyond what the Franchise Agreement provides.
