# Exhibit V: Digital Services Agreement

**Source:** Exhibit V, pages 942-956 (PDF index 941-955)
**Document title:** Order Form -- Digital Services Agreement (Effective Date: 1/1/26)
**Provider:** Burger King Company, LLC ("BKC")
**Governing agreement:** Order Form + General Terms and Conditions (Exhibit A to Order Form)

---

## 1. Fees

### Base fee structure
- **Fixed fee:** USD $110 per Restaurant per month
- **Variable fee:** 1% of total monthly Digital Sales per Restaurant
- **Annual cap:** USD $4,500 per Restaurant per calendar year

**CONFIRMED:** The $110/month + 1% digital sales + $4,500/year cap structure is explicitly stated in the Order Form under "Digital Services Fee."

### Definition of "Digital Sales"
Gross sales from Transactions processed through or facilitated by any Digital Platform, **excluding** transactions completed via (i) credit card token integrations and (ii) self-service kiosks.

### Definition of "Transaction"
Includes orders placed/paid through Digital Platforms (online pickup or delivery), digital offer/coupon redemptions at the restaurant (dine-in, pickup, drive-thru), and loyalty point accumulation via Digital Platforms.

### Non-deductibility
Franchisee may NOT deduct Digital Services Fees from the calculation of Gross Sales under the Franchise Contracts. Digital Services Fees are separate from and in addition to royalty obligations.

### Accounting separation
Digital Services Fees are accounted for separately by Provider and are NOT co-mingled with the U.S. Advertising Fund.

### Audit rights
The Franchisee Association has the right (not more than once annually, with reasonable notice) to audit Provider's fiscal year-end income/expenditures associated with Digital Services Fees for U.S. BK restaurants. Audit is at the Franchisee Association's sole cost. Only past two fiscal years' records are produced. Individual franchisees have no independent audit right, but if no Franchisee Association exists, franchisees owning 30%+ of U.S. franchised restaurants collectively may audit under the same terms.

## 2. Fee Adjustability / Unilateral Modification

**Yes, BKC can adjust fees unilaterally.**

On or after January 1, 2026 (but no more than once per calendar year), the Digital Services Fees are subject to adjustment by Provider (increase or decrease) to cover fees, costs, and expenses of Provider in providing, improving, and expanding the Cloud Services, **as determined by Provider in its discretion**.

There is no cap on the magnitude of adjustment, no franchisee consent requirement, and no arbitration mechanism disclosed. The only constraint is the once-per-year frequency limit.

---

## 3. Modifiability of Agreement Terms

BKC retains broad unilateral control over the Cloud Services:

- Provider may publish and update an "acceptable use policy" from time to time (Section 4.1)
- Provider controls which POS system versions, hardware specs, connectivity, and technical requirements are required, specified "from time to time" (Order Form, Obligations of Franchisee)
- Provider determines loyalty program strategy, terms, conditions, reward types/amounts, tiers, and methods of earning/redeeming in its discretion (Order Form, Cloud Services description)
- Provider chooses which Digital Platforms to integrate and which Delivery Applications to designate
- Provider controls which payment service providers are approved

**Formal amendment of the Agreement** itself requires written consent signed by both parties (Section 12.7). However, the broad discretionary powers above effectively allow BKC to reshape the service unilaterally without amending the Agreement.

---

## 4. Required Systems

Franchisee must maintain at its sole cost and expense:

1. **POS system** -- Provider-approved point-of-sale system with Provider-approved software versions, hardware specs, and connectivity requirements (upgrades at franchisee's cost; not included in Cloud Services)
2. **Internet connectivity** -- At all times at each Restaurant, including connectivity to POS and Delivery Application systems
3. **Menu database** -- Franchisee responsible for providing menu offering for each Digital Platform, coordinating with approved POS providers for new products/vouchers
4. **Payment service provider** -- Must contract directly with a Provider-approved payment service provider enabling multiple payment methods through each Digital Platform
5. **POS injection/upgrades** -- System upgrades to match hardware/software requirements, at Franchisee's sole cost

## 5. Data Access / Polling / Deletion / Hosting Rights

### BKC's data access (Section 4.5 of General Terms)
Provider, Provider's Affiliates, and Provider's designees may **at any time** have **full access**, both on-site and from a remote location, to **all** of Franchisee's computer data, equipment, and systems containing information, records, and reports required under the Franchise Contracts. Provider may use this data for evaluation of products/services, store performance, tests, EBITDA performance, and as an analytical tool to improve Cloud Services.

### Cloud Data ownership
All "Cloud Data" (defined extremely broadly -- see Section 1.3) is the exclusive property of Provider. This includes:
- All data obtained in connection with Cloud Services implementation, testing, usage, provision, maintenance, or operation
- Data obtained through any software accessible to Provider by/on behalf of Franchisee
- Data provided by Authorized Users or Site Visitors
- Data derived from Franchisee's use of Cloud Services
- Non-public data relating to Authorized Users, Site Visitors, or business operations
- Anonymous usage data, statistics, reports collected from usage of Cloud Services

### Menu Data
Menu Data is separately defined and is NOT classified as Cloud Data.

### Post-termination
Upon termination, Franchisee must (i) immediately discontinue all use of Cloud Services, (ii) delete Confidential Information from computer storage and all media, (iii) return or destroy all physical copies of Confidential Information, and (iv) pay all amounts due through termination date.

### IP Rights
All IP Rights in and arising under Cloud Services remain Provider's exclusive property. Franchisee irrevocably assigns any rights it may be deemed to have in modifications or corrections to Cloud Services.

### Franchisee feedback
Any Feedback provided by Franchisee becomes sole and exclusive property of Provider, with no obligation to compensate or utilize. Franchisee may not use its own Feedback in Restaurants without Provider's prior written consent.

## 6. Support Scope

The agreement does **not** include an explicit SLA or defined support scope. The Cloud Services are provided **"AS IS" and "WITH ALL FAULTS"** (Section 8.2). Provider:

- Makes no warranties of operability, reliability, functionality, accuracy, fitness for purpose, or error-free operation
- Does not warrant that Cloud Services will meet Franchisee's requirements or be uninterrupted
- Does not warrant that all errors will be corrected

Provider's aggregate liability for direct damages is capped at total fees paid in the 6 months prior to the claim. No consequential, indirect, special, incidental, or punitive damages are recoverable.

---

## 7. Suspension Rights

Provider reserves the right to **suspend Franchisee's access to Cloud Services** if Franchisee fails at any time to pay any amounts due (Section 11.6).

Key details:
- Suspension does NOT release Franchisee from payment obligations or other duties
- Provider is not liable to Franchisee or any third party for consequences of suspension resulting from non-payment
- No cure period is specified for suspension (contrast with termination, which requires 10-day cure for payment, 30-day cure for other breaches)

---

## 8. Franchisee Compliance Burdens

The Agreement places extensive compliance obligations on the Franchisee:

1. **Legal compliance** -- Ensuring all usage is compliant with all applicable laws, rules, and regulations
2. **Content accuracy** -- Ensuring accuracy, completeness, quality, integrity, legality, reliability, and appropriateness of all content uploaded (including Menu Data)
3. **Tax compliance** -- Determining, collecting, and remitting all Taxes; ensuring compliance with online marketplace seller laws including digital services taxes
4. **Confidentiality** -- Protecting all Confidential Information to at least the same degree as own confidential information; restricting access; returning/destroying upon termination
5. **No reverse engineering** -- May not decompile, reverse engineer, disassemble, or determine source code
6. **No sublicensing** -- May not market, sublicense, distribute, reproduce, rent, lease, or offer Cloud Services to third parties
7. **Indemnification** -- Must indemnify Provider against claims arising from: Franchisee's use with unapproved products, violations of the Agreement, misappropriation of IP/Confidential Information, inaccurate Menu Data, and third-party contractual interference claims

## 9. Payment / Auto-Debit Mechanics

### Invoice schedule
- On the **2nd Business Day** of each calendar month, Provider bills Franchisee for the prior month's Digital Services Fees
- Fees appear on Franchisee's **ePay accounts** on the **3rd Business Day**
- Fees must be paid by Franchisee via ePay accounts **on or before the 10th Business Day**

### Late fees
18% per annum (or max allowed by law, whichever is less) on outstanding past-due balances not subject to good-faith dispute.

### Non-refundability
All payments, once made, are **not refundable nor creditable** for any reason whatsoever.

### Indirect Taxes
Fees exclude Indirect Tax. If Indirect Tax applies (existing or future), Franchisee bears the full economic burden.

## 11. Termination and Renewal

### Initial Term
2 years from Effective Date (1/1/26).

### Auto-renewal
Automatically renews for successive **1-year periods** unless either party gives **60 days' written notice** before expiration of the then-current period.

### Termination for breach
- **Payment breach:** 10-day cure period after written notice
- **Other breach:** 30-day cure period after written notice

### Automatic termination triggers (no notice required from Provider)
- Termination/expiration/non-renewal of the underlying Franchise Contract for a Restaurant
- Insolvency or bankruptcy of Franchisee prior to full payment

### Provider-specific termination rights
- Provider may terminate upon occurrence of a "Default" or "Event of Default" under the Franchise Contract for a Restaurant (written notice to Franchisee)
- Provider may terminate if Franchisee becomes insolvent/enters bankruptcy

### Survival
Sections 1-7, 8.2, 9, 10, and 11.4-12 survive termination, along with all payment obligations.

### Assignment
- Franchisee: Cannot transfer, assign, or sublicense without Provider consent (which may be granted or denied in sole discretion, and may include additional fees)
- Provider: May transfer, assign, sublicense, or delegate in its sole discretion, including appointing subcontractors, without Franchisee consent

## 12. Notable Asymmetries and Risk Signals

1. **Provider discretion dominates:** Fee adjustments, platform choices, technical requirements, loyalty program design, and acceptable use policy are all at Provider's discretion.
2. **"AS IS" service:** No warranties on operability, reliability, or error correction, despite mandatory participation and mandatory fees.
3. **Full data access:** Provider has unrestricted remote/on-site access to all Franchisee computer data and systems at any time.
4. **Cloud Data ownership:** Extremely broad definition makes virtually all data generated through digital operations Provider's exclusive property.
5. **Feedback trap:** Any suggestions or feedback become Provider's property; Franchisee cannot use its own feedback without written consent.
6. **Suspension without cure:** Provider can suspend Cloud Services for non-payment with no specified cure window (unlike termination).
7. **Liability cap:** Provider's aggregate liability capped at 6 months of fees paid -- potentially a few thousand dollars per restaurant.
8. **Non-refundable fees:** All payments non-refundable for any reason.
9. **Assignment asymmetry:** Franchisee cannot assign; Provider can freely assign, subcontract, and delegate without consent.
