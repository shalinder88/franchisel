## 1. Executive Snapshot

- **Total initial investment:** $348,400--$4,705,600 excluding real estate; of which $57,750--$67,500 is payable to the franchisor or affiliates.
- **Franchise fee:** $50,000 standard (20-year term), prorated for shorter terms, minimum $15,000. Fully earned at signing.
- **Royalty:** 4.5% of monthly Gross Sales (standard). Incentive programs (DIP 2024, Development Agreement FSS-graded) can reduce to as low as 2.5% in Year 1 for new development.
- **Advertising contribution:** Up to 4.5% of Gross Sales (currently 4.5%), plus up to 2.0% Investment Spending (DMA-level), yielding a combined royalty+advertising burden of up to 11.0% of Gross Sales before DMA spending.
- **Digital Services Fee:** $110/month + 1% of digital sales, capped at $4,500/year per restaurant. BKC may adjust annually at its discretion with no cap on magnitude.
- **Average traditional franchise-owned sales:** $1,658,463 (median $1,561,502). Company-owned average $1,730,667. Non-traditional franchise-owned average $1,321,324.
- **Remodel uplift:** 893 restaurants remodeled to Modern Image; average sales uplift 12.3%. Scrape-and-rebuild uplift 26.1%. Uplift holds at 3+ years (13.4% cumulative average).
- **Systemwide U.S. outlets (12/31/2024):** 6,701 total (5,524 franchised, 1,177 company-owned). System lost 77 net outlets in 2024.
- **Carrols reacquisition:** RBI acquired Carrols Restaurant Group in May 2024, converting approximately 1,023 franchised units to company-owned. Refranchising is underway.
- **Franchised unit decline:** Franchised count fell from 7,053 (start 2022) to 5,524 (end 2024), a loss of 1,529 franchised units in three years, driven primarily by Carrols and net closures.
- **No exclusive territory.** Target Areas are non-exclusive. BKC reserves all rights to develop competing BURGER KING restaurants nearby and sell through alternate channels.
- **20-year term.** Successor franchise available if conditions are met, including remodel to then-current standards and signing the then-current franchise agreement.
- **No bankruptcy disclosed.** No public figures disclosed.
- **Litigation:** 21 matters disclosed (12 pending, 1 franchisor-initiated, 8 concluded). Key themes: antitrust no-poach class action (mediation ordered by April 2026), Carrols shareholder suit (trial February 2027), privacy/tracking class actions, international franchise disputes.
- **Parent guarantor financial strength:** RBI total assets $24.6B, long-term debt $13.5B, net income $1.445B (2024). KPMG unqualified opinion. Leverage approximately 2.8x total debt/equity. Intangibles + goodwill = $16.9B (69% of assets).
- **State addenda:** 11 states override base contract provisions for forum, governing law, release, noncompete, termination notice, and/or liquidated damages.
- **Contractual complexity is high.** Multiple remodel programs (RTF2, BKoT, Fuel the Flame), a new-franchisee financing program (Crown Your Career), a dedicated Digital App Services Agreement, and 50+ exhibit families create layered obligations.
- **Digital Services Agreement is significantly franchisor-favorable:** "AS IS" service, full data access, Cloud Data owned by BKC, suspension without cure period, 6-month liability cap, non-refundable fees, unilateral fee adjustment.

## 2. Pass 1 -- Front Matter, Formats, Entry Structure, Fee Stack, and Initial Investment

### Franchisor Identity

Burger King Company LLC, a Florida LLC formed February 4, 2022, is the successor to BK Corporation (founded 1954, dissolved December 2022) following an internal reorganization in August 2022. BKC is an indirect wholly-owned subsidiary of Restaurant Brands International Limited Partnership (RBILP), whose general partner is Restaurant Brands International Inc. (RBI), a Canadian corporation publicly traded on NYSE and TSX. RBI also owns Tim Hortons, Popeyes, and Firehouse Subs. Principal office: 5707 Blue Lagoon Drive, Miami, FL 33126. As of 12/31/2024, there were 19,732 BURGER KING restaurants worldwide and 6,701 in the U.S.

### Management (Item 2)

BKC is led by a 3-member Board of Directors: Thomas B. Curtis IV (President since February 2022; formerly COO of BK Corporation and EVP at Domino's), Vicente A. Tome (VP of Legal US & LAC since February 2022), and Jill Granat (General Counsel of both BKC and RBI since 2022/2014 respectively). ### Bankruptcy (Item 4)

No bankruptcy is required to be disclosed. Confirmed for BKC, its predecessor, parents, and all persons identified in Item 2.

### Franchise Formats

Two franchise agreement forms are offered: Individual/Owner-Operator (Exhibit D1, individuals sign personally with Operating Partner designated) and Entity (Exhibit D2, for corporations/LPs/LLCs with Managing Owner holding minimum 25% equity, requiring personal Guaranty via Exhibit D3). Three entry paths exist. The Target Reservation Agreement (TRA) requires a $5,000 deposit for a non-exclusive Target Area to search for one site. The Development Agreement (Exhibit M1) is a multi-unit commitment with a development schedule, requiring prepayment of $50,000 per committed restaurant and offering FSS-graded royalty reductions for A/B-grade operators.

### Special-Risk Signals

Two special risks are disclosed: (1) out-of-state dispute resolution requiring litigation only in Florida, and (2) non-binding mediation required before suit on development disputes. A Michigan Franchise Investment Act notice is also included.

## 3. Pass 2 -- Supplier Control, Operating Control, and Technology Burden

### Item 8 -- Supplier Restrictions

BKC establishes standards and specifications for most goods and services. Franchisees must obtain most items from approved suppliers or distributors. Double drive-thru is required for all facility types except food courts, in-lines, indoor MRS, Big Box Retail, and delivery facilities. Approved architects from BKC's list are required for certain programs. Ordering and delivery services must use BKC-approved third-party delivery providers and the BURGER KING app/website. All delivery providers must be BKC-approved. Participation in BKC-required loyalty programs via BKC-required technology platforms is mandatory. Approved-supplier restrictions are pervasive across food, beverages, paper products, equipment, signage, decor, cleaning supplies, POS systems, digital menu boards, network services, and technology platforms.

BKC and its affiliates derive revenue from franchisee purchases through rebates, incentives, or other consideration from suppliers. The specific revenue amounts from these arrangements were not quantified in the FDD, though BKC acknowledges their existence. RBI's consolidated 2024 supply chain sales were $2.708B with cost of sales of $2.180B. Franchise and property revenues were $2.919B.

### Quality-Control Obligations

BKC prescribes standards for food preparation, presentation, cleanliness, equipment maintenance, employee uniforms, and customer service. Third-party food safety and brand standards inspections are conducted; franchisees pay for re-inspection upon failure.

### Reporting and Bookkeeping Burden

Monthly ACH withdrawal covers royalties, advertising, and other fees. Gross Sales reporting is required. BKC retains audit rights with expense recovery if understatement exceeds 2%.

### Technology and Software Obligations

Franchisees must maintain a BKC-approved POS system (hardware and software), outdoor digital menu boards (or pay $200--$300/month for static menu board kit), the BURGER KING mobile app and website for delivery and ordering, the Digital Services Agreement (Exhibit V), mandatory loyalty program participation, BK University / eLearning platform ($700/year), and a Kitchen Management System (referenced in Operations Manual TOC). Service Desk provides centralized IT support at $750--$1,200/year.

### Training Structure

Training includes classroom and on-the-job components. Fees are $7,500 per trainee for new franchisees. BK University costs $700/year. Additional training courses may require materials/course fees up to $25,000.

### Digital Services Agreement (Exhibit V) -- Deep Read

The Digital App Services Agreement (effective 1/1/2026, pages 942--956) creates substantial technology and data obligations. The fee structure is $110/month per restaurant plus 1% of monthly Digital Sales, capped at $4,500/year per restaurant. Digital Sales are defined as gross sales from transactions processed through Digital Platforms, excluding credit card token integrations and self-service kiosks. The Digital Services Fee is non-deductible from Gross Sales and is separate from royalty obligations.

**Fee adjustability:** BKC may adjust fees annually at its discretion, with no cap on magnitude and no franchisee consent requirement. The only constraint is a once-per-year frequency limit.

**Service level:** Cloud Services are provided "AS IS" and "WITH ALL FAULTS." BKC makes no warranties of operability, reliability, functionality, accuracy, fitness for purpose, or error-free operation. BKC's aggregate liability for direct damages is capped at total fees paid in the 6 months prior to the claim. No consequential, indirect, or punitive damages are recoverable.

**Data access and ownership:** BKC, its affiliates, and designees may at any time have full access, on-site and remote, to all franchisee computer data, equipment, and systems. All "Cloud Data" -- defined extremely broadly to include all data obtained in connection with Cloud Services, data from authorized users and site visitors, derived data, and anonymous usage statistics -- is the exclusive property of BKC. Any feedback provided by franchisees becomes BKC's sole and exclusive property, and franchisees may not use their own feedback without written consent.

**Suspension rights:** BKC may suspend access to Cloud Services for non-payment with no specified cure window. Suspension does not release the franchisee from payment obligations.

**Term and renewal:** Initial term is 2 years from 1/1/2026, auto-renewing for 1-year periods unless 60 days' notice is given. Automatic termination occurs upon termination/expiration of the underlying franchise agreement.

**Assignment asymmetry:** Franchisees cannot assign without BKC consent (granted or denied in sole discretion). BKC may freely assign, subcontract, and delegate without franchisee consent.

**Notable risk signals:** (1) Provider discretion dominates fee adjustments, platform choices, technical requirements, and loyalty program design. (2) Full data access with no apparent limitation on purpose. (3) Extremely broad Cloud Data ownership. (4) 6-month liability cap amounts to potentially a few thousand dollars per restaurant. (5) Non-refundable fees regardless of cause. (6) Suspension without cure period, unlike termination which requires 10--30 day cure.

## 4. Pass 3 -- Territory, Competition, Channels, and Encroachment

### Item 12

**No exclusive territory.** The TRA and MTRA define Target Areas for site search only, and those Target Areas are non-exclusive. BKC may establish and license other BURGER KING restaurants at any other locations, including near the franchisee's restaurant. Other BURGER KING restaurants may compete directly with the franchisee's restaurant. There are no protected-area limits of any kind.

**Reserved rights are extensive.** BKC and its affiliates may sell BURGER KING branded products through grocery stores, convenience stores, the internet, and other direct marketing channels. BKC may operate or license other competing restaurants. BKC's affiliates may operate other franchise concepts (Tim Hortons, Popeyes, Firehouse Subs), all of which are quick-service competitors. RBI may operate third-party concepts in combination. BKC controls the digital ordering channel through the BURGER KING mobile app and website.

**Non-traditional carveouts:** Non-traditional restaurants (institutional, airports, military, colleges, retail, turnpikes, food courts, drive-thru only, Big Box Retail, delivery/ghost kitchens) compete within the system and are not restricted by proximity to traditional locations.

**Encroachment:** There is no restriction on BKC placing restaurants in proximity to existing franchisees. BKC will give existing franchisees the option to develop if BKC proposes to develop in their Target Area under TRA/MTRA, but this is a right of first offer, not a territorial protection. BKC may develop Fuel Co-Branded restaurants without restriction.

## 5. Pass 4 -- Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Item 17 -- Renewal, Termination, Transfer, and Dispute Resolution

The Item 17 summary table spans pages 82--97 (16 pages). Key provisions follow.

**Term:** 20 years standard. Non-traditional and shorter property-control terms may be shorter, with a minimum of 6 years. Prorated franchise fee for shorter terms.

**Renewal / Successor:** Available if the franchisee meets conditions including remodel to then-current standards, signing the then-current form of franchise agreement (which may differ materially from the original), compliance with all obligations, and payment of successor franchise fee. BKC has discretion to decline.

**Transfer:** BKC approval is required. Transfer fee is $2,000 + $500 per additional restaurant (weekend/holiday surcharge $175). BKC has a right of first refusal. The transferee must meet all BKC requirements. The franchisee must sign a general release.

**Default and Termination:** Immediate termination is permitted for bankruptcy, abandonment, health/safety violations, criminal conviction, fraud, or misrepresentation. A 30-day cure applies for most other defaults. BKC may also terminate for failure to meet remodel deadlines, repeated defaults, or failure to meet development schedule.

**Post-term obligations:** Three-tier noncompete: (1) Post-term (FA D1 Section 19): 1 year, 2 miles from the Premises, no hamburger business. DA variant: 2 miles from any of your BK restaurants. (2) CYC enhancement (Exhibit Z1): No ANY business activity until 5th anniversary of first BK FA or until loan repaid, whichever later (Managing Owner only). (3) Perpetual (FA Section 12): No use of BK System/marks/secrets in any other restaurant, ever. State overrides: CA likely unenforceable, MN prohibited, ND heavily restricted. De-identification must occur within 30 days.

**Forum and governing law:** Litigation only in Florida. Florida law governs. Mediation is required for development disputes before suit.

**Release:** Required on transfer and successor.

**Remodel obligations:** Extensive. BKC may require remodel to then-current standards as a condition of successor franchise. Multiple remodel programs (RTF2, BKoT, Fuel the Flame) create layered remodel obligations with penalty royalty rates for non-compliance (increases to 6.0% or 7.5%). The Brand Damage Fee under the Development Agreement functions as liquidated damages for development shortfalls.

### Guaranty (Exhibit D3) -- Deep Read

The Exhibit D3 Guaranty is a personal guaranty required for entity ownership. It is absolute, continuing, primary, and unconditional -- a guarantee of both payment and performance, not merely collection. Guarantors are jointly and severally liable with the franchisee and all other guarantors. The guaranty covers all "Obligations" defined to include all debts, liabilities, and obligations arising under the Franchise Agreement, Lease/Sublease, Crown Your Career Promissory Note (if applicable), and all ancillary agreements, whether direct, indirect, absolute, contingent, matured, or unmatured. It includes costs of collection and reasonable attorneys' fees.

Guarantors waive acceptance, demand, presentment, protest, notice of default, statute of limitations, subrogation and reimbursement rights against the franchisee, and trial by jury. All debts owed by the franchisee to the guarantor are subordinated to BKC's claims. The guaranty is irrevocable during the term and does not terminate upon death (binding the estate) or incapacity.

**Transfer release provisions:** If a guarantor transfers all interest for cash, liability terminates one year from transfer, limited to accrued but unpaid royalties and advertising. For installment sales, liability terminates at the later of one year or final installment.

### Guarantee of Performance (Parent-Level) -- Deep Read

A dual-guarantor model applies at the parent level. If the franchise is in California, Illinois, Maryland, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Virginia, or Washington, RBILP is the guarantor. For all other states, RBI is the guarantor. Both guarantees are absolute and unconditional, covering BKC's duties and obligations under the Franchise Agreement. They continue until all obligations are satisfied or liability is completely discharged. The guarantees do not cover franchisee profitability, supply chain performance, or third-party obligations, nor do they expand BKC's obligations beyond those in the Franchise Agreement. Both RBI and RBILP consolidate the same underlying operations, so the financial backing is substantively identical.

### State Addenda (Exhibit P) -- Deep Read

Eleven states have material overrides in Exhibit P (pages 706--801). The key overrides by theme are:

**Forum / venue must be in-state:** California, Illinois, Maryland, Minnesota, North Dakota, Rhode Island, South Dakota, and Washington all override the Florida forum clause for claims arising under their respective franchise laws.

**Governing law overridden from Florida:** Illinois, Minnesota, North Dakota, South Dakota, and Washington substitute their own state law.

**Release at renewal/transfer void or limited:** California, Hawaii, Maryland, Minnesota, North Dakota, New York, and Washington prevent BKC from requiring a release of statutory franchise-law claims as a condition of renewal or transfer. Washington imposes the strongest limitation, requiring independent counsel and post-closing negotiation.

**Noncompete void or limited:** California, North Dakota, South Dakota, and Washington likely void the post-term noncompete. Washington further limits enforcement against employees earning under $100K and independent contractors under $250K.

**Termination notice / cure enhanced:** Illinois requires 10--30 day cure. Minnesota requires 90 days' notice with 60 days to cure (among the longest statutory periods). South Dakota requires 30 days' notice with opportunity to cure. Virginia requires "reasonable cause" for cancellation.

**Liquidated damages / termination penalties void:** California (Development Agreement only), Minnesota, and North Dakota.

**Jury trial waiver void:** California, Minnesota, North Dakota, and Washington.

**Punitive / exemplary damages waiver void:** North Dakota and Washington. Washington provides for treble damages.

**Fair-value repurchase on termination:** Hawaii requires BKC to compensate franchisees at fair market value for inventory, supplies, equipment, and furnishings purchased from BKC or designated suppliers. If BKC refuses to renew in order to convert to company-owned, BKC must also compensate for loss of goodwill.

**Purchasing requirements:** Washington requires that approved-supplier prices be fair and reasonable.

**Other notable provisions:** New York requires that assignment by the franchisor be only to an assignee willing and financially able to assume obligations. Washington prohibits BKC from unilaterally modifying the franchise agreement during the term without franchisee consent and prohibits restricting franchisee communication with regulators.
