## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** 538 total outlets at year-end 2024 (537 franchised, 1 company-owned), operating across 40+ U.S. states and Puerto Rico. System is contracting: net losses of 12 (2022), 20 (2023), and 27 (2024) units, with accelerating non-renewals and zero new openings in 2024.

2. **Entity structure:** Regal Nails, Salon & Spa, LLC is a Nevada LLC formed September 30, 2005. No parent companies. Key affiliate is Alfalfa Nail Supply, Inc. (LA corp, 1995), the exclusive supplier of furniture, fixtures, and equipment, solely owned by CEO Quy T. Ton, who also has ownership interests in the franchisor. This cross-ownership structure creates a significant related-party dynamic.

3. **Ownership and leadership:** CEO Quy T. Ton has led the franchise since its founding and has been in the nail salon business since 1995 through predecessor Regal Nails, LLC. CFO David Anderson has served since 2005. COO Loan Nguyen since 2007. The leadership team has been stable for nearly two decades.

4. **Investment range:** $172,722–$720,666 total initial investment, with $156,500–$664,466 payable to franchisor or affiliates. The Snow White Package (turnkey salon buildout) ranges from $150,000–$545,000 depending on location type, area, and size.

5. **Fee burden:** Monthly Fee of $500–$15,000 (bundling franchise fee, base rent, and rental tax for party-tenant locations). Percentage Rent at Walmart locations: 6.5% of Gross Sales above the breakpoint. Insurance $1,000–$8,000/year. No ad fund currently but reserved right to impose $25–$500/month.

6. **Item 19:** No financial performance representations made. Prospective franchisees have no franchisor-provided revenue, cost, or profit data to evaluate the investment.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk indicators:** System shrinking; financial condition flagged on cover page as special risk; California DFPI imposed fee deferral due to capitalization concerns; Illinois addendum separately flags financial condition; no new openings in 2024; 21 non-renewals in 2024.

8. **Financial health:** Total revenue $24.8M (2024), down from $25.6M (2023). Net income $2.14M. $8.4M cash on hand. Total equity $14.3M against $48.4M liabilities (primarily $42.5M operating lease obligations). Franchisor is profitable and cash-generating but heavily leveraged through its sublease portfolio.

9. **Growth trajectory:** Negative. Zero new franchised outlets opened in 2024. 14 agreements signed but not yet opened. Zero projected new outlets in the next fiscal year. The system has lost 59 net units over three years.

10. **Territory:** Non-exclusive. Limited to the four walls of the salon. Franchisor reserves right to open in the same shopping center.

11. **Renewal:** Possible successor terms of 2–5 years but no automatic right. Must sign then-current franchise agreement that may have "materially different" terms. General release required (except where prohibited by state law).

12. **Disputes:** Litigation (no arbitration), venue in Baton Rouge, Louisiana. Waiver of jury trial and punitive damages (deleted in some states). Governing law: Louisiana (overridden in several states).

13. **Special risks (cover page):** Out-of-state dispute resolution, supplier control, financial condition concerns, mandatory minimum payments regardless of sales.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Regal Nails, Salon & Spa, LLC was formed in Nevada on September 30, 2005, with its principal office at 5150 Florida Boulevard, Baton Rouge, Louisiana. The company has offered Regal Nails Salon & Spa franchises since April 1, 2006. On January 1, 2011, it also acquired the franchisor rights for Regal Nails & Spa outlets from former affiliate Regal Nails Select, LLC.

The franchisor has no parent companies. Its predecessor, Regal Nails, LLC (Louisiana, 1996), transferred 227 franchise agreements to the current entity between 2006 and 2008. Key affiliates include Regal Nails, Canada, ULC (wholly-owned subsidiary for Canadian franchises) and Alfalfa Nail Supply, Inc., the exclusive supplier for salon furniture, fixtures, and equipment. CEO Quy T. Ton is the sole owner of Alfalfa and has ownership interests in both the franchisor and Regal Nails, LLC.

The business model is distinctive: salons are primarily located inside Walmart stores (approximately 99% of locations), operating under a master lease agreement between Regal and Walmart (the 2009 MLA). The franchisor leases space from Walmart and subleases to franchisees. This creates a three-party structure where the franchisee's continued operation depends on both the franchisor and Walmart.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Application Fee: $250 (non-refundable)
- Initial Franchise Fee: $25,000 (lump sum on signing)
- Confirmation Fee: $45,000 (within 10 days of landlord site confirmation)
- Snow White Package: $150,000–$545,000 (turnkey salon including furniture, fixtures, signs, equipment, décor, initial inventory)

Snow White Package pricing varies by location type:
- Walmart Most Areas: $150,000–$250,000
- Walmart High Cost Areas: $200,000–$300,000
- Non-Walmart Most Areas: $250,000
- Non-Walmart High Cost Areas: $300,000

High Cost Areas include AK, AZ, CA, CT, FL, HI, MA (within 65 miles of Boston), NH, NJ, NV, NY (within 85 miles of NYC), OR, RI, TX (within 45 miles of Houston or Dallas), VT, WA, and Puerto Rico.

Additional charges may apply: up to $30,000 for Existing Locations, $40,000 for government fees, $150,000 for additional construction, $20,000 for exclusive brand add-on, and $5,000 for landlord-required review costs. After 2025, an annual 8% increase applies. Monthly Fee may increase by 25% after 2025.

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Monthly Fee: $500–$15,000/month (bundled franchise fee, base rent, rental tax if party tenant)
- Percentage Rent (Walmart): 6.5% of Gross Sales above natural breakpoint
- Monthly Utility/CAM: $200–$1,200/month (if party tenant)
- CGL/PL Insurance: $1,000–$8,000/year (through approved group carrier)
- Insurance Handling: $50/year
- System Advertising Fund: Currently $0 (reserved right to impose $25–$500/month)
- POS Subscription: $14–$100/month (optional)

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Successor Franchise Fee: $500
- Transfer Fee: $1,000 ($500 to own entity; waived on death/disability)
- Expansion Fee: $2,500 plus expansion costs ($25K–$40K)
- Inspection Fines: $25–$500 per deficiency
- Failure to Open (Walmart Grand Opening): $10,000 + $300/day
- Liquidated Damages: All remaining Monthly Fees through end of term (discounted to PV)
- Holdover: 150% (with consent) or 300% (without consent) of Monthly Fee
- White Box Fee: $25,000 for failure to return salon in white box condition

### Initial Investment

Total estimated initial investment: $172,722–$720,666. The low end assumes a 400 sq ft salon with no supplemental purchases, no rental tax, and opening on the first of a calendar month. The high end assumes a 1,500 sq ft salon with $27,249 in supplemental furnishings and maximum pro-rata rent. Key components beyond the Snow White Package include: insurance ($1,000–$8,000), first three months' Monthly Fee ($2,000–$74,517), security deposit ($1,400–$32,400), merchant services hardware ($750–$1,500), training travel ($700–$3,000), and additional working capital ($12,000–$15,000 for 3 months).

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

The franchisor maintains heavy supplier control. The Snow White Package must be purchased from the franchisor, which sources all items from Alfalfa Nail Supply, Inc. After opening, all replacement furniture, fixtures, equipment, decorative items, and Regal-branded products must be purchased from Alfalfa. CEO Quy T. Ton is the sole owner of Alfalfa. The FDD states "we do not control Alfalfa, its product pricing or discounts" — a claim of independence that is difficult to reconcile with the sole ownership structure.

Approximately 80–85% of establishment costs and 65–80% of ongoing costs come from franchisor-approved suppliers or meet franchisor specifications. In FY2024, Alfalfa received $1,711,979 from direct franchisee purchases. The franchisor also receives: 5% on Regal-branded product sales from Alfalfa, 2.5% on mani/pedi pod sales, and 15% of net profit from the designated merchant services vendor.

Nail products and consumable supplies (other than Regal-branded) are recommended but not currently required to be purchased from approved suppliers. Single-use supplies may be purchased from any vendor.

### Training Program

The training program is a 3-day classroom course at the Baton Rouge, LA headquarters. It covers leadership, marketing, renewals/remodeling, merchant services, products/services, and insurance. Total classroom hours: approximately 18.5. There are zero on-the-job training hours. The primary (and, in practice, sole) instructor is CEO Quy T. Ton. Franchisees bear all travel, lodging, and meal costs ($700–$3,000 estimated).

Training must be completed before the Grand Opening. The schedule is bi-monthly except November and December. Franchisees with two insurance claims within three years must re-attend within 90 days. Proposed transferees and renewing franchisees must also complete training.

### Technology Stack

Required: Designated merchant services solution hardware ($750–$1,500) from a designated vendor. The franchisee must grant the franchisor "unlimited, independent access to all information and data" from the merchant services system. Optional (but franchisor reserves right to require): Point of Sale system software subscription ($14–$100/month). The franchisee is responsible for internet/data connectivity to enable remote access by the franchisor and landlord.

### Marketing Fund

No national advertising program, advertising fund, or franchisee advertising council currently exists. The franchisor reserves the right to establish: (1) a system advertising fund ($25–$500/month), (2) regional advertising cooperatives (minimum $10/month), and (3) franchisee advertising councils — all at the franchisor's sole discretion. All franchisee advertising using Regal marks requires advance written approval.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

The franchisee receives a **non-exclusive territory** defined as the four walls of the salon. For party-tenant locations, the franchisor commits not to license another Regal Nails in the same designated retailer store address, but explicitly reserves the right to open in the same shopping center. For non-party-tenant locations, the franchisor will not open in the same shopping center address but may open inside a big box retailer in the same shopping center.

The franchisee has no right to exclude, control, or impose conditions on the location of any present or future Regal Nails, Regal Nails Salon & Spa, or other brand businesses. Alfalfa may sell products anywhere, including in the franchisee's territory, via any channel including internet, catalog, and telemarketing, with no compensation to the franchisee.

There are no sales volume quotas, market penetration requirements, or performance metrics required by the franchisor for territory continuity. However, certain landlords (notably Walmart) may review sales volume and enforce performance covenants. Failure to meet Walmart's performance covenant can result in lease termination.

If the landlord remodels or relocates, the franchisee may move at their sole cost ($25,000–$50,000 for Walmart, potentially double for others). If the franchisee declines to move, or if the landlord does not offer space, the franchise agreement terminates with no liability to either party.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The initial franchise term is generally 5 years, coinciding with the lease term for party-tenant locations. There is no automatic right to renewal. A successor term of 2–5 years is possible, subject to: 180 days advance written notice of intent to renew; full compliance with all agreements; meeting then-current qualification and training standards; paying the Successor Franchise Fee ($500); modifying the salon to then-current standards; signing a general release (where not prohibited by state law); and signing a new franchise agreement.

**Critical risk: The renewal franchise agreement may have "materially different" terms and conditions than the original.** This means the economic structure, fee levels, operational requirements, and other terms could change substantially at renewal.

For Walmart locations, the initial lease term is 5 years with up to two consecutive 3-year mutual renewal options. Both Regal and Walmart must mutually agree to each renewal "in their respective discretion."

### Transfer Provisions

A "transfer" is defined as a conveyance of more than one-third interest. Transfer requires franchisor consent and involves: current on all obligations; transferee must qualify, submit application ($250), and complete training; $1,000 transfer fee; execution of bill of sale and assignment; general release. On death or disability, the franchise must be transferred within 6 months; no transfer fee is charged but the transferee must qualify. The franchisor has no right of first refusal and no option to purchase the franchisee's business.

### Termination

**Without cause:** The franchise agreement automatically terminates if the lease to the premises is terminated, without fault or liability to the franchisor. Walmart can terminate any individual location lease on 90 days' notice without cause — a significant existential risk.

**Curable defaults (14 days to cure):** Misuse of marks; default on payment; failure to meet COM standards; failure to operate continuously; lapsed insurance; failure to comply with laws; numerous operational violations.

**Non-curable defaults (immediate termination):** Failure to attend training; unauthorized transfer; bankruptcy; license revoked; unauthorized services; competing within 5 miles; felony conviction; abandonment; false representations; three material defaults in 12 months; failure to meet Walmart performance covenant; employment of unlicensed personnel.

### Noncompete

During the franchise term, the franchisee may not be involved in any similar business within the territory or within a 5-mile radius. After expiration, termination, or transfer, the same restriction applies for 1 year. The period is tolled for any noncompliance. California addendum notes the post-term noncompete may not be enforceable under CA law. North Dakota addendum states noncompetes are "generally considered unenforceable" in ND.

### Dispute Resolution

Disputes are resolved through **litigation** (not arbitration or mediation), with venue in **Baton Rouge, Louisiana** and **Louisiana governing law**. The franchise agreement includes a **jury trial waiver** and **punitive/exemplary damages waiver**, plus limitations on types and amounts of damages and periods to bring claims.

Walmart lease-related matters have venue in Benton County, Arkansas under Arkansas law. State addenda override several of these provisions: Illinois mandates Illinois law and venue; North Dakota deletes jury waiver, punitive waiver, and liquidated damages provisions; Maryland preserves franchisee rights under MD franchise law.

### Litigation History

Three collection actions against former franchisees (standard debt recovery, 2020–2024). Two historical public agency actions: Virginia (2007, $21,000 settlement for selling unregistered franchises) and California (2005, desist and refrain order for the same). No current material litigation.

### Key Financial Observations

**Leverage:** Total liabilities ($48.4M) vastly exceed equity ($14.3M), but this is primarily driven by $42.5M in operating lease obligations from the sublease portfolio. The lease liabilities are matched by $42.5M in ROU assets and offset by sublease income ($17.48M vs. lease cost $17.56M — near break-even).

**Distributions:** Members received $1.45M in distributions in 2024 (down from $2.38M in 2023). This represents 67% of net income, leaving some capital for retention.

**Related party lending:** Notes receivable to the owner total $2.57M ($3M note at 2.07%, $625K note at 3.02%). Notes to the President total $3.90M across 11 separate promissory notes at rates ranging from 1.02% to 4.73%. Combined, $6.47M in company capital is loaned to two insiders — a significant concentration representing 45% of total equity.

**Affiliate receivables:** $2.62M due from Alfalfa Nail Supply (non-current) and $735K from Regal Nails, LLC (predecessor). These have been on the books for years.

**Subsidiary disposals:** The company disposed of its UK subsidiary (RGEU Limited) and Mexico subsidiary (RNS & GV MEX) in 2024, recording total losses of $223K. This suggests international expansion has not been successful.

**Cash position:** Cash increased to $8.41M from $7.31M. Operating cash flow was $3.08M, well in excess of capital expenditures ($134K) and distributions ($1.45M).

**Contract liabilities:** $1.76M in deferred revenue (prospect deposits and security deposits treated as contract liabilities), suggesting some pipeline activity.
