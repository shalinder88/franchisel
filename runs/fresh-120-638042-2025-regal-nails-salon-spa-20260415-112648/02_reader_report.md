# Reader Report — Regal Nails, Salon & Spa, LLC (638042-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot (8–15 bullets)

1. **Brand:** Regal Nails, Salon & Spa — full-service nail salons primarily located inside Walmart stores
2. **Franchisor entity:** Regal Nails, Salon & Spa, LLC (NV LLC, formed Sep 2005); no parent companies
3. **Leadership:** CEO Quy T. Ton (founder, since 1995 via predecessor); CFO David Anderson; COO Loan Nguyen
4. **System size (YE 2024):** 537 franchised outlets + 1 company-owned = 538 total; net loss of 27 units in 2024 (564→537); system has been shrinking every year for 3 years
5. **No new openings in 2024;** 0 outlets opened, 4 terminations, 21 non-renewals, 2 ceased operations; 14 signed-but-not-opened agreements in pipeline
6. **No Item 19 FPR:** Franchisor makes no financial performance representations whatsoever
7. **Total initial investment:** $172,722–$720,666; of which $156,500–$664,466 goes to franchisor/affiliate
8. **Fee structure:** Monthly Fee $500–$15,000 (bundled franchise fee + base rent + rental tax if Regal is party tenant); Percentage Rent on Walmart locations 6.5% of gross sales above breakpoint
9. **Snow White Package:** $150,000–$545,000 turnkey salon buildout through Alfalfa Nail Supply (affiliate, owned by CEO Quy T. Ton)
10. **Special risks flagged on cover:** (1) Out-of-state dispute resolution (Louisiana), (2) Supplier control (must buy from franchisor/affiliates), (3) Financial condition concerns per Item 21, (4) Minimum payment obligations regardless of sales
11. **California DFPI fee deferral condition** imposed due to franchisor not demonstrating adequate capitalization
12. **Financial health:** Total revenue $24.8M (2024, down from $25.6M in 2023); Net income $2.14M; Total equity $14.3M; but total liabilities $48.4M (driven by $42.5M operating lease obligations)
13. **Territory:** Non-exclusive — limited to the four walls of your salon; franchisor can open in same shopping center
14. **Noncompete:** 5-mile radius during term + 1 year post-termination
15. **Renewal risk:** Must sign then-current franchise agreement on renewal — may have "materially different" terms

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term:** Generally 5 years (coincides with lease term for party-tenant locations)
- **Renewal:** No automatic right; possible 2–5 year successor term
- Walmart: mutual option for 2 additional 3-year terms
- Renewal conditions: 180 days advance written notice; compliance with all agreements; meet current standards/training; pay Successor Franchise Fee ($500); sign new Franchise Agreement (may have materially different terms); sign general release; modify salon to then-current standards
- **Materially different terms risk flagged** — renewal may require substantially different economics

### Termination
- **By franchisor without cause:** If lease to premises is terminated → franchise automatically terminates, no fault/liability to Regal
- **Walmart can terminate without cause** on 90 days' notice
- **Curable defaults (14 days to cure):** Misuse of mark; default on payment; failure to meet COM standards; failure to operate continuously; lapsed insurance; failure to comply with laws; numerous operational defaults
- **Non-curable defaults (immediate):** Failure to attend training; unauthorized transfer; bankruptcy; license revoked; unauthorized services; competing within 5 miles; felony conviction; abandonment; false representations; 3 material defaults in 12 months; failure to meet Walmart performance covenant; employment of unlicensed personnel
- **Franchisee termination:** Not expressly addressed; subject to state law

### Transfer
- Transfer = conveyance of >1/3 interest
- Subject to franchisor consent
- Conditions: current on all obligations; transferee qualifies and completes training; $1,000 transfer fee ($500 to own entity); sign bill of sale and assignment; execute general release
- Death/disability: transfer within 6 months; no transfer fee; transferee must qualify
- No right of first refusal by franchisor

### Post-Termination Obligations
- Return COM and all marked materials
- Vacate salon in "broom clean" and "white box" condition ($25,000 fee if not)
- Assign phone, web, social media, d/b/a to franchisor
- Pay all monies due
- Comply with noncompete covenants
- Abandoned property rule: anything left in salon deemed abandoned, franchisor disposes at will
- Holdover: 150% of Monthly Fee (with consent), 300% (without consent) plus legal costs

### Noncompete
- During term: No involvement in similar business in territory or within 5-mile radius
- Post-term: Same restriction for 1 year after expiration/termination/transfer
- Period tolled for noncompliance
- California addendum notes noncompete may not be enforceable

### Dispute Resolution
- Litigation (not arbitration or mediation)
- Forum: Baton Rouge, Louisiana
- Governing law: Louisiana
- Walmart lease matters: Benton County, Arkansas; Arkansas law
- Waiver of jury trial and punitive/exemplary damages
- Limitation on types and amount of damages and periods to bring claims
- State addenda may override

### Unresolveds
1. **Financial condition risk:** Special Risk #3 states franchisor's financial condition "calls into question" ability to provide services — yet auditor issued clean opinion. Tension not explicitly reconciled in FDD. CA DFPI fee deferral supports concern.
2. **Walmart lease renewal uncertainty:** Lease renewals require mutual agreement from both Regal and Walmart, in their "respective discretion." No assurance of renewal.
3. **Walmart 90-day termination without cause:** 2009 MLA allows Walmart to terminate any individual location lease on 90 days' notice without cause — existential risk to individual franchisees.
4. **Percentage-based rent transition:** Walmart "currently contemplating" monthly rent based on % of gross sales (up to 20%) with minimum base. If implemented, could materially change economics.
5. **Accelerating non-renewals:** 9→16→21 over three years — unclear if driven by Walmart decisions, franchisee decisions, or performance covenants.
6. **Related party lending volume:** $6.47M in notes to owner/president — material concentration of franchisee-generated cash flowing to related parties.
7. **Alfalfa pricing independence:** FDD states "we do not control Alfalfa, its product pricing or discounts" — yet CEO is sole owner of both entities. Effective independence is uncertain.
