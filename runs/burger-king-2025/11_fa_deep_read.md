# Franchise Agreement Deep Read: Exhibits D1 and D2

**Source:** PDF pages 185-232 (D1, Individual/Owner-Operator, 48 pages) and 233-274 (D2, Entity, 42 pages)
**Status:** Fully surfaced

---

## Structure

Both forms share the same 22-section architecture. D2 (Entity) is the more commonly used modern form. D1 (Individual) adds provisions for assignment to an operating company (LLC/corporation) and has the Operating Partner construct instead of Managing Owner. The substantive obligations are materially identical.

### Key Contract Data Page
Both forms include a fill-in Key Contract Data page setting: Restaurant #, Effective Date, Term dates, Initial Franchise Fee ($50,000 or custom), Royalty (4.5% or per Addendum), Advertising Contribution (4.5% or per Addendum), Managing Owner/Operating Partner, and Transfer Fees. This creates per-restaurant customization within the standard form.

### List of Attachments
Both forms include a checklist of ~20 possible addenda (Fuel the Flame, Crown Your Career, Carrols Refranchise, Non-Traditional, Delivery, DIP, BKoT, Replacement, Successor, Deferred Remodel, RTF Upgrade, 2024 Developer Incentive, Corporate Addendum, plus 6 state amendments). Each checked addendum is incorporated into the FA.

## Provisions Beyond What Item 17 Summarized

### Section 5 — Standards and Uniformity (the operational control section)

**MOD Manual (Sec. 5.A):** The MOD Manual is "deemed a part of this Agreement." Changes in standards, specifications, and procedures that BKC "in the good faith exercise of its judgment believes to be desirable and reasonably necessary" must be accepted and complied with. Material and information in the MOD Manual is confidential and proprietary. Franchisee may not print a copy without BKC's prior written approval.

**Current Image (Sec. 5.B.2):** During the 10th year of the Term, franchisee must remodel exterior to Current Image as of the 9th anniversary. If BKC and the Franchisee Advisory Council disagree on Current Image, the matter goes to binding arbitration by a sole arbitrator under CPR rules in Miami, FL. Failure to comply with Current Image is a default.

**Equipment (Sec. 5.D):**
- POS Systems must be BKC-approved. BKC may require upgrades or replacements. BKC may Poll (remotely extract) any information from POS at any time.
- Additional Ordering Systems: BKC may mandate centralized/technology-based ordering methods at any time. BKC owns all related IP and all data generated. BKC may charge upfront and/or ongoing fees.
- Additional Digital Systems: BKC may mandate technology for customer communication and data collection at any time. BKC owns all IP and all data generated. BKC may use this data to analyze trends, market products, reward customers, provide to third parties, and for any other purpose in its sole discretion. **All net profits from providing this data to third parties are BKC's sole property.**

**Menu and Service (Sec. 5.F):** 24-hour notification requirement for any Food Safety Incident (failing government score, closure, critical violation). BKC may direct actions in response.

**Hours of Operation (Sec. 5.G):** Minimum 6am-12am Monday-Saturday, 7am-12am Sunday, 52 weeks/year. May close Thanksgiving and/or Christmas if DMA majority elects to close.

**Right of Entry (Sec. 5.J):** BKC has unrestricted right to enter and inspect without prior notice at any time an employee is present.

**BK Foundation (Sec. 5.L):** Must purchase at least one $1,000 scholarship per restaurant per year. Charitable funds raised are held in trust — not property of franchisee.

### Section 9 — Royalty and Advertising

**Gross Sales Definition (Sec. 9.C):** All sums charged for goods, merchandise, or services sold at or from the restaurant, including all premiums unless exempted. Excludes: federal/state/county/city/excise taxes collected from customers, and cash received as payment in credit transactions already counted. If BKC approves sales away from the restaurant, those are included.

**Advertising Fund (Sec. 9.B):** BKC must spend at least 65% of total annual Advertising Contribution on Media (TV, radio, outdoor, print). Public relations capped at 0.5% of total annual Ad Contribution. Annual Ad Fund audit right for the Franchisee Association (at Association's cost, unless misappropriation or >3% discrepancy found). Franchisee has no independent audit right — only the Association does, or 30%+ of franchisees collectively if no Association exists.

**Investment Spending (Sec. 9.B.viii):** If 66.7%+ of franchised and company restaurants in the DMA execute binding ISP contracts, franchisee must execute on the same terms. Capped at 2% of Gross Sales. Term no longer than 1 year.

**No Set-Off (Sec. 9.H):** Royalty and Advertising must be paid in full free of any deductions or set-off whatsoever.

**Alternative Payment (Sec. 9.G):** BKC may require direct electronic funds transfers from franchisee's bank account. Franchisee may not terminate the authorization or close the account without establishing a substitute.

### Section 10 — Accounting and Audit

Monthly and YTD P&L in BKC-prescribed format. Quarterly balance sheets. All in GAAP. Due within 25 days of period end. Retain state sales tax returns and supporting data for 24 months. Annual financial statements reviewed by independent CPA. Understated Gross Sales >2% triggers franchisee pays full audit costs.

**Release of Financial Information (Sec. 10.D):** BKC may use, disclose, or publish financial information derived from franchisee's operations without identifying the specific franchisee, for any purpose BKC deems appropriate.

### Section 13 — Insurance and Indemnification

Insurance: as previously surfaced. Additionally: all insurance must be with A.M. Best A-(VIII) or S&P A- rated insurers. BKC must be named as loss payee with waiver of subrogation.

**Indemnity (Sec. 13.E):** Franchisee indemnifies BKC against all losses from restaurant operations unless resulting from BKC's negligence. Indemnification is not limited by insurance amount. Includes employment-related claims. Separate indemnity for any securities offerings by franchisee. If multi-franchisee claim, BKC may consolidate defense and charge pro-rata costs.

### Section 15 — Assignment and Transfer (D2 Entity specifics)

**BKC consent window:** 90 days for 10+ restaurant transactions, 60 days for smaller. BKC conditions include: transferee completes standard franchisee application and meets all BKC requirements; transferee may be required to sign BKC's then-current FA (royalty and ad rates remain as stated in existing FA, but all other terms may change); general release by seller; approval of sale terms re: sufficiency of post-debt-service cash flow.

**Continuing Liability (Sec. 15.H):** After transfer, transferor remains liable for royalty and advertising for: (1) if installment sale — longer of 12 months or duration of installment payments, capped at installment amount after first year; (2) if cash sale — 12 months. Installment notes must be subordinate to royalty/advertising and non-assignable.

**Right of Re-Entry (Sec. 15.I):** If BKC seeks to enforce continuing liability, the prior transferor may cure the default and reassume the franchise if: (1) was in good standing at time of transfer; (2) meets current approval criteria; (3) pays all past-due sums; (4) takes possession of all tangible property.

### Section 17 — Successor Franchise Option

Notice must be given during the **4th year before expiration**. BKC has 120 days to advise of ineligibility. Franchisee must have completed Current Image remodel not more than 3 years and not less than 3 months before expiration. Must meet then-current financial ratios. General release required. Successor FA may differ on royalty, advertising, ownership, and other terms.

### Section 18 — Default and Termination

Full default catalog in operative language (confirming Item 17 summary with additional detail):

- Sec. 18.A.(1): Health/sanitation — 5 days. Serious health/safety: immediate closure upon verbal notice, no cure.
- Sec. 18.A.(12): Non-payment of royalty/advertising — 10 days.
- Sec. 18.A.(20): Continued operation after expiration without successor FA — 30 days.
- Sec. 18.A.(28): Failure to meet minimum financial criteria per Financial Policy — 180 days.
- Sec. 18.A.(19): Understatement of Gross Sales — no cure.
- Sec. 18.A.(15): Unauthorized transfer — no cure.

**Effect of Termination (Sec. 18.B):** Upon termination: (1) cease trademarks/de-identify; (2) BKC option to purchase usable paper/containers/menus at cost, equipment/furniture/fixtures/signs at FMV; (3) payment of all amounts due; (4) royalties and advertising contributions for the remainder of the Term; (5) return MOD Manual; (6) restrictive covenant activates.

### Section 19 — Restrictive Covenant

During term: no interest in any hamburger business. Post-term: 1 year, within 2 miles. Guaranty by all Owners (Entity) or all named Franchisees (Individual).

### Section 21 — Miscellaneous

**Governing Law (Sec. 21.C):** Florida law. Forum: S.D. Florida or Miami-Dade County. **Jury Trial Waiver (Sec. 21.M):** Knowing and voluntary irrevocable waiver by both parties.

**Entire Agreement (Sec. 21.J/K):** FA, addenda, and TRA/MTRA constitute the entire agreement. Representations outside these documents and the FDD may not be enforceable.

## D1 vs. D2 Key Differences

| Feature | D1 (Individual) | D2 (Entity) |
|---|---|---|
| Franchisee | Named individuals | Corporation/LLC/LP |
| Key person | Operating Partner | Managing Owner |
| Equity requirement | 50% (Operating Partner) | 25%+ (Managing Owner per Entity Guidelines) |
| Assignment to entity | Sec. 15.D allows assignment to a corporation or LLC with BKC consent and conditions | Not applicable — already an entity |
| Non-voting equity | Up to 25% Non-Voting Stock/Units may be issued to family/key employees | Governed by Entity Guidelines |
| Processing Fee (on entity conversion) | $2,500 | N/A |
| Transfer Fee (entity conversion) | $500 | N/A |
| Section numbering | 22 sections + 2 exhibits | 22 sections + 2 exhibits |
| Page count | 48 pages (including entity conversion guidelines) | 42 pages |

The D1 form contains extensive provisions (Sec. 15.D, 8+ pages) governing assignment of the Individual FA to a corporation or LLC, including guidelines for corporate governing instruments, non-voting stock issuance, and key employee stock restrictions. These provisions are unique to D1 and not present in D2.

## Critical Findings Not in Item 17 Summary

1. **BKC owns all data** generated by POS Systems, Additional Ordering Systems, and Additional Digital Systems. Net profits from selling this data to third parties are BKC's sole property.
2. **MOD Manual changes** are deemed part of the FA and must be complied with. BKC's "good faith exercise of judgment" is the only constraint. No cost-impact analysis or franchisee consent required.
3. **65% Media floor** on advertising spending — the FA guarantees at least 65% goes to media.
4. **ISP binding majority** — if 66.7% of a DMA signs ISP contracts, all franchisees in that DMA are bound to the same terms.
5. **Release of financial information** — BKC can publish aggregated franchisee financial data for any purpose.
6. **4th-year successor notice** — must be given during the 4th year before expiration, not merely before expiration.
7. **Post-transfer continuing liability** is capped but can extend for the duration of installment payments.
8. **$1,000 BK Foundation scholarship** is a mandatory annual purchase per restaurant, not optional fundraising.
