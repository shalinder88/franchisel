## Executive Snapshot

- **System:** 6,701 US restaurants (5,524 franchised + 1,177 company-owned) as of 12/31/2024. 19,732 worldwide. The US system is contracting: net -77 in 2024, -264 in 2023, -62 in 2022. The 2024 data is dominated by RBI's May 2024 acquisition of Carrols Restaurant Group (1,023 franchised restaurants converted to company-owned).

- **Fee stack:** 9.0% combined mandatory floor (4.5% royalty + 4.5% advertising) before rent, digital services ($110/month + 1% digital sales, capped $4,500/yr), local marketing (up to 2.0%), BK University ($700/yr), service desk ($750-$1,200/yr), and Burger King Foundation ($1,000/yr).

- **Investment:** $2,049,200-$4,705,600 for traditional freestanding (excluding real estate). Non-traditional from $348,400 (Indoor MRS) to $1,942,800 (In-Line/End Cap).

- **Financial performance (Item 19):** Traditional franchisee-owned average Gross Sales $1,658,463; median $1,561,502. Company-owned average $1,730,667. Non-traditional average $1,321,324. No cost, income, or profit data disclosed.

- **Remodel pressure is the defining feature.** RTF2 requires Sizzle Image remodels by BKC-set deadlines (2026-2030). BKC cash contributions of $90K-$725K per restaurant depending on scope, royalty rate elected, and completion year — but only for FSS A/B operators. D/F operators get $0. Missed deadline triggers +3.0% royalty surcharge. Cross-default cascades to all development agreements and, for Carrols restaurants, every FA in the refranchise transaction.

- **Fuel the Flame** ties the 4.5% advertising rate to a systemwide 4-Wall EBITDA benchmark of $230,000 as of 12/31/2026. If not met, rate reverts to 4.0% unless franchisee supermajority votes to maintain. 4-Wall EBITDA is defined as restaurant-level EBITDA excluding above-restaurant G&A, with rent capped at 7.4% above a 2021 base year.

- **No exclusive territory.** Single-location rights only. BKC reserves all alternative distribution channels. Affiliate brands (Tim Hortons, Popeyes, Firehouse Subs) may operate in same markets.

- **Successor franchise is not a right.** Requires BKC approval, then-current FA (terms may materially differ), general release, remodel completion, and successor fee.

- **Post-term noncompete:** 1 year, 2 miles, no hamburger business. State addenda in CA, MN, and ND may void or limit this.

- **Litigation:** 10 pending matters, 2 with direct franchise impact — Arrington no-hire class action (mediation by April 2026) and Plymouth County Carrols acquisition fairness (trial February 2027). Concluded matters include a Swiss Cartel Act finding and a Spanish wrongful termination finding against BK Europe. No bankruptcy.

- **Guarantor:** RBI (or RBILP for CA/IL/MD/ND/RI/VA/WA). Consolidated financials: $24.6B total assets, $13.5B long-term debt, $1.445B net income, $1.503B operating cash flow (2024). KPMG audited, clean opinion.

- **Management:** President Thomas Curtis and Executive Chairman Patrick Doyle both ex-Domino's. Multiple late-2025 leadership changes (COO NA, VP Finance, VP Development, RVP West all appointed Nov-Dec 2025). CEO Joshua Kobza has been with RBI since 2013.

Wholly-owned indirect subsidiary of Restaurant Brands International Limited Partnership (RBILP), controlled by Restaurant Brands International Inc. (RBI). Principal place of business: 5707 Blue Lagoon Drive, Miami, FL 33126.

### Development Structure

### Initial Fees
Franchise fee: $50,000 (20 years); prorated minimum $15,000. Application: $250/individual, $5,000/entity. Training: $7,500/trainee. All nonrefundable except limited TRA/MTRA deposit refund scenarios.

Remodel penalty: +3.0% royalty surcharge on all Gross Sales for missed deadlines under RTF2, RTF1, or Midterm Remodel Forbearance, continuing until BKC verifies completion or FA expires.

### Rent (BKL Properties)
Where BKC controls real estate: minimum annual rent typically 10% of capitalized site costs (BKC-owned) or 125% of BKC's rent + 10% of costs (BKC-leased). Percentage rent: 8.5% up to $133,333.33/month, 10% above. 12% escalation every 5 years on BKC-owned properties. Default triggers full remaining-term rent liability.

## Pass 2 — Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology, and Training

### Supplier Control (Item 8)
Purchases from approved sources: 65-90% of establishment costs, 30-60% of non-occupancy operating costs. Sole-source items: network services, digital menu boards, Gift Card services, cashless payments, QA inspections, and BKC technology platforms (ordering/delivery/loyalty). RSI (Restaurant Services Inc.) is an independent purchasing cooperative with a 21-member Board (18 franchisees). BKC received approximately $227M in revenue from franchisee purchases/leases in 2024 (~16% of BKC's $1.427B total revenues). Supplier rebate contributions to the advertising fund: ~8.4% of consolidated fund revenue. BKC officers own publicly traded shares in General Mills, J&J, Kraft Heinz, and PepsiCo (all approved suppliers).

### Technology Burden
POS/kiosks: franchisee-purchased ($20K-$25K). Digital menu boards: sole-source, mandatory. Digital App Services Agreement (Exhibit V): mandatory, governs BK app/website/third-party ordering/delivery/loyalty platforms. Fee: $110/mo + 1% digital sales, capped $4,500/yr. BKC is sole supplier of these platforms. Royal Perks loyalty program mandatory. Delivery via BKC-approved aggregators mandatory in serviced areas.

### Training
Required before opening. Held in Miami or BKC-designated locations plus in-restaurant training. Fee: $7,500/trainee. ServSafe certification required. BK University: $700/yr ongoing. No formal training program table with Subject/Hours/Classroom columns was found in Item 11 — training described narratively only.

### Insurance
CGL: $2M/occurrence, aggregate tiered by portfolio ($2M for 1-10 restaurants, $5M for 11-50, $10M for 50+). Auto: $1M. Property: full replacement cost with 12-month business interruption. Boiler/Machinery: full replacement. Workers Comp: per law.

## Pass 3 — Territory, Competition, and Channels

### Territory (Item 12)
No exclusive territory at any level. Franchise Agreement: single-location rights only. Development Agreement: geographic Territory granted but not exclusive. TRA/MTRA: Target Areas for search only, no exclusivity. BKC and affiliates reserve all rights to alternative distribution channels (grocery, convenience, internet, direct marketing) — with no compensation to franchisees. Military development rights held by Army & Air Force Exchange Service.

### Affiliate Competition
Tim Hortons, Popeyes, and Firehouse Subs may operate in same markets. All four brands share some menu overlap (sandwiches, fries, beverages). BKC "not responsible for resolving" inter-brand conflicts. BKC and affiliates retain the right to establish or acquire additional brands selling similar products.

### Owner Participation (Item 15)
Individual/Owner-Operator: Operating Partner must own 50%+, live nearby, devote full time, no other restaurant business. Entity: Managing Owner must own 25%+, full time, nearby. Corporate Addendum: Managing Owner may be parent entity; Managing Director designated for day-to-day. CYC: Managing Owner must own 100%, no other business until 5th anniversary or loan repayment.

### Product Restrictions (Item 16)
Must offer all and only BKC-authorized products. Value Menu subject to BKC maximum price cap. Mandatory delivery, mandatory loyalty. No vending/ATMs without BKC approval. Retail sales only — no wholesale/redistribution.

## Pass 4 — Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term, Renewal, Transfer (Item 17)
**Term:** 20 years (freestanding traditional); shorter for non-traditional or limited property control.
**Successor (renewal):** Not a right. Option conditioned on compliance, BKC approval, then-current FA (materially different terms possible), general release, successor fee, building upgrades, inspections. No successor for Big-Box or Delivery Restaurants.
**Transfer:** BKC consent required. Buyer approved, training complete, all fees current, transfer fee ($2K first + $500/additional), new franchisee training ($7,500), general release by seller, current image compliance. ROFR: BKC/designee on same terms as third-party offer.

### Default and Termination
**Curable:** 5 days (health/safety/ops), 10 days (non-payment), 30 days (reports/equipment/maintenance/remodel/behavior), 60 days (other FA/lease provisions), 180 days (financial criteria).
**Non-curable:** Bankruptcy, felony conviction, fraud, Gross Sales understatement, unauthorized transfer, repeated breaches, delivery aggregator termination (Delivery Restaurants), RTF program default.
**Cross-defaults:** CYC: any BK FA default = default under all CYC agreements. Carrols Refranchise: any lease/agreement default in the transaction = default under this FA.
**Post-termination:** Cease marks, de-identify, pay remaining-term royalties and advertising, return MOD Manual.

### Noncompete
During term: no hamburger business interest. Post-term: 1 year, 2 miles. CYC: no other business until 5th anniversary or loan repayment. Corporate Addendum Contract Feeders: may operate competing businesses under limited conditions.

### Forum and Governing Law
Florida forum (Southern District or Miami-Dade County); Florida law. Both subject to state addenda overrides.

### State Addenda Overrides (Exhibit P — 9 states surfaced)

California adds an unaudited BKC balance sheet to Exhibit Q and caps late interest at 10%.
