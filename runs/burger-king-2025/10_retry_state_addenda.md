# RT-03: State Addenda — Operative Provisions for Key States

**Source:** Exhibit P FDD Addenda pages: CA (782-784), HI (785), IL (787), MD (788), MN (789-790), NY (791-793), ND (794-795), RI (796), WA (799-800)
**Status:** Fully surfaced for FDD-level addenda

## California (pages 782-784)

1. All proposed agreements must be delivered with the FDD.
2. FDD must be delivered before soliciting material modifications to existing franchises.
3. No securities association/exchange suspension orders against BKC or Item 1-2 persons.
4. **Post-term noncompete may not be enforceable** under California law. (FA Sec. 19, DA Sec. 7.5)
5. **Florida choice of law may not be enforceable** under California law. (FA Sec. 21.C, TRA/MTRA Art. 11.5, DA Sec. 15.4)
6. **Liquidated damages clause in Development Agreement (Sec. 5.7) may not be enforceable** under California law.
7. **Forum selection clause void** for claims arising under or relating to a franchise operating within California. (Cal. Franchise Relations Act Sec. 20040.5)
8. **General release cannot waive compliance** with California Franchise Investment Law or California Franchise Relations Act. To the extent required by such laws, franchisee shall not be required to execute a general release.
9. **Jury trial waiver may not be enforceable** under California law.
10. No questionnaire/acknowledgment may waive claims under state franchise law or disclaim reliance on franchisor statements.
11. **Item 21 amended: unaudited consolidated BKC balance sheet** as of 12/31/2024 is also included at Exhibit Q for California. (Separate from the RBI/RBILP audited statements.)
12. **Late payment interest capped at 10% annually** (California maximum).

## Hawaii (page 785)

1. Filing is not approval/recommendation/endorsement.
2. Unlawful to offer franchise without providing FDD at least 7 days before binding agreement or payment.
3. FDD is a summary only; refer to actual agreements for all rights/conditions/restrictions.

## Illinois (page 787)

1. **Termination/non-renewal affected by Illinois law** (815 ILCS 705/19 and 705/20).
2. **Waiver of compliance with Illinois Franchise Disclosure Act is void.** (Section 41)
3. **Forum selection clause void** for any provision designating jurisdiction outside Illinois, except that arbitration outside Illinois is permitted. (Section 4)
4. **Illinois law governs** (amends Item 17(w)).
5. No questionnaire/acknowledgment may waive claims under state franchise law or disclaim reliance.

---

## Maryland (page 788)

1. **Bankruptcy termination may not be enforceable** under US Bankruptcy Act, but BKC intends to enforce to the extent enforceable.
2. **General release at renewal, sale, or assignment shall not apply to liability under Maryland Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law.** (COMAR 02.02.08.16L)
3. **May sue in Maryland** for claims arising under Maryland Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law.
4. No questionnaire/acknowledgment may waive claims or disclaim reliance.
5. **Claims under Maryland law must be brought within 3 years** after grant of franchise.

## Minnesota (pages 789-790)

1. **BKC must protect franchisee's right to use BK Marks or indemnify** from loss arising from trademark claims. (Minn. Stat. 80C.12, subd. 1(g))
2. **90 days' notice of termination required (with 60 days to cure). 180 days' notice for non-renewal.** (Minn. Stat. 80C.14, subds. 3, 4, 5). This supersedes the FA's shorter cure periods.
3. **General release cannot waive claims under Minnesota Franchise Act** as a condition of renewal or assignment.
4. Transfer release shall not apply to claims under the Minnesota Franchise Act or its Rules.
5. **Forum selection clause outside Minnesota is unenforceable** (except exclusive arbitration clause). (Minn. Rule 2860.4400(J))
6. **Prohibited from requiring:** waiver of jury trial, liquidated damages, termination penalties, or judgment notes. (Minn. Stat. 80C.21 and Rule 2860.4400(J))
7. Nothing in the FDD or agreements can abrogate franchisee's rights under Minn. Stat. Chap. 80C.
8. No questionnaire/acknowledgment may waive claims or disclaim reliance.

## New York (pages 791-793)

1. **Cover page amendment:** Registration does not mean NY recommends the franchise. Franchisor may negotiate but cannot prevail upon less favorable terms than FDD.
2. **Item 3 amendments:** Confirms no pending felony/fraud/securities actions, no currently effective injunctions against Item 1-2 persons.
3. **Item 5 amendment:** Franchise fee constitutes general operating funds, used in BKC's discretion.
4. **Renewal/transfer general release:** All franchisee rights under NY GBL Article 33 remain in force. (Non-waiver provisions of GBL 687.4 and 687.5 satisfied.)
5. **Assignment by BKC:** No assignment except to an assignee willing and financially able to assume BKC's obligations.
6. **Forum/law:** Choice of law does not waive any right conferred by NY GBL Article 33.
7. No questionnaire/acknowledgment may waive claims under state franchise law or disclaim reliance.
8. **Receipts:** Must comply with Section 683(8) — FDD provided at earlier of first personal meeting or 10 business days before execution/payment.

## North Dakota (pages 794-795)

The North Dakota Insurance Commissioner has held the following **unfair, unjust, or inequitable** to ND franchisees:

1. **Restrictive covenants:** Subject to N.D.C.C. Section 9-08-06 (which heavily restricts noncompetes).
2. **Arbitration situs:** Cannot require remote arbitration location.
3. **Forum restrictions:** Cannot require consent to courts outside North Dakota.
4. **Liquidated damages and termination penalties:** Cannot require consent.
5. **Applicable laws:** Cannot specify governing law other than North Dakota.
6. **Jury trial waiver:** Cannot require consent.
7. **Waiver of exemplary/punitive damages:** Cannot require consent.
8. **General release:** Cannot require at renewal.
9. **Limitation of claims:** ND statute of limitations applies.
10. **Enforcement of agreement costs:** Prevailing party entitled to recover all costs including attorney's fees (overrides one-sided BKC provision).
11. No questionnaire/acknowledgment may waive claims or disclaim reliance.

## Rhode Island (page 796)

1. **Forum selection clause restricting venue to a forum outside Rhode Island is void.** (RI General Laws §19-28.1-14)
2. **Requiring application of laws of another state is void** for claims enforceable under RI Franchise Investment Act.

---

## Washington (pages 799-800)

1. **Conflict of laws:** Washington Franchise Investment Protection Act (RCW 19.100) prevails.
2. **Franchisee Bill of Rights** (RCW 19.100.180) may supersede FA provisions on termination and renewal.
3. **Arbitration/mediation/litigation situs:** Must be in Washington or mutually agreed location. Litigation for franchise sale claims or FIPA violations may be brought in Washington.
4. **General release void** except when executed pursuant to negotiated settlement after agreement in effect, with parties represented by independent counsel (RCW 19.100.220(2)). **Also void at renewal or transfer** except per same provision.
5. **Statute of limitations restrictions and jury trial waiver may not be enforceable.**
6. **Transfer fees:** Collectable only to the extent they reflect franchisor's reasonable estimated or actual costs.
7. **Termination by franchisee** permitted under any grounds allowed by state law.
8. **Buy-back provisions** permitting franchisor to repurchase during term without franchisee consent are **unlawful** (RCW 19.100.180(2)(j)) unless franchise terminated for good cause.

## Summary: Material Overrides by State

| Provision | CA | IL | MD | MN | NY | ND | RI | WA |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Post-term noncompete limited/void | Yes | - | - | Yes* | - | Yes | - | - |
| Florida forum void/unenforceable | Yes | Yes | Partial | Yes | Partial | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Florida governing law overridden | - | Yes | - | Yes | - | Yes | - | Yes |
| General release at renewal restricted | Yes | - | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | - | Yes |
| Jury trial waiver void | Maybe | - | - | Yes | - | Yes | - | Maybe |
| Liquidated damages/termination penalties void | Maybe | - | - | Yes | - | Yes | - | - |
| Termination notice enhanced | - | Yes* | - | 90/180 days | - | - | - | Yes* |
| Transfer fee limited to actual costs | - | - | - | - | - | - | - | Yes |
| BKC buy-back without consent unlawful | - | - | - | - | - | - | - | Yes |

*Illinois: termination/non-renewal affected by 815 ILCS 705/19-20.
*Minnesota: 90 days notice to terminate (60 to cure), 180 days for non-renewal — supersedes 5/10/30/60 day FA cure periods.
*North Dakota: Most comprehensive — effectively voids the noncompete, forum selection, governing law, jury waiver, liquidated damages, general release, and one-sided fee recovery provisions.
*Washington: Franchisee Bill of Rights may supersede FA on termination and renewal.
