## C. What Still Blocks Production Quality

Three items, in priority order:

### 1. Franchise Agreement operative text (D1/D2) — 90 pages
The core contract. Item 17 summaries are a proxy, not a substitute. The FA contains the actual Gross Sales definition, BKC's unilateral modification rights (MOD Manual can be changed at any time), the indemnification scope, the specific remodel trigger language, and the representations and warranties that define the franchisee's legal exposure. Until D1 and D2 are read, every contract-burden field in the canonical carries an implicit "per Item 17 summary" caveat.

### 2. Exhibit V — Digital App Services Agreement — 15 pages
BKC is the sole supplier of the technology platforms that enable ordering, delivery, and loyalty. The fee is confirmed but the operative terms — SLAs, data ownership, platform change rights, termination mechanics, indemnification for misappropriation claims — are unsurfaced. As digital sales grow as a share of total Gross Sales, this agreement's burden grows proportionally.

### 3. Exhibit O1 franchisee list extraction — 62 pages
5,524 franchised locations with franchisee names, addresses, and restaurant numbers. Multi-unit operator concentration, largest franchisees, and geographic clustering are foundational to any competitive intelligence or territory analysis. The data exists and is structured — it just hasn't been row-extracted.

**Estimated effort to close all three gaps:** ~167 pages of additional reading and structuring.
