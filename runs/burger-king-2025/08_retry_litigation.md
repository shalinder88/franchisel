## Item 3 — Litigation

### Pending Litigation (10 matters)

#### 1. First International Fund Ltd. v. Burger King Corporation et al
- **Forum:** Ontario Superior Court of Justice (Case # CO-13-487734)
- **Filed:** August 28, 2013
- **Parties:** FIF (entity of Marc Vaturi / Triple Five International Group) vs. BK Corporation and former Canadian master franchisee
- **Claims:** Alleged binding verbal agreement to purchase shares of BK Restaurants Canada Inc. (BKRC) for an entity to be designated by Vaturi. Seeks specific performance or damages exceeding $500 million.
- **Status:** Action against BKRC dismissed July 2018. Examinations for discovery took place March 2025. Active.
- **BKC party status:** Predecessor (BK Corporation) named as defendant.

#### 2. Arrington v. Burger King Worldwide, Inc. (Consolidated No-Hire/No-Solicitation Class Action)
- **Forum:** US District Court, Southern District of Florida (Case No. 18-24128)
- **Filed:** 2018 (four consolidated complaints)
- **Parties:** Jarvis Arrington et al. (former BK restaurant employees) vs. BK Corporation, BK Worldwide, RBI
- **Claims:** Violation of Section 1 of the Sherman Antitrust Act. Allege employee no-solicitation and no-hiring clause in standard form Franchise Agreement constituted illegal restraint. Seek injunctive relief and class damages.
- **Status:** Motion to Dismiss granted March 2020, reversed on appeal August 2022, remanded. BKC's second motion to dismiss denied April 2025. Amended complaint filed April 2025, BKC answered May 2025. Court ordered mediation by April 3, 2026. **Active — approaching mediation.**
- **Diligence note:** This is the most significant pending matter for franchise operations. A no-hire clause in the standard FA was the basis of the claim. Outcome could affect future FA terms and franchisee labor practices.

#### 3. Mister Crab s.r.l. et al. vs. Burger King Europe GmbH et al.
- **Forum:** Milan Tribunal (Case No. 33924/2024)
- **Claims:** Italian franchisee alleges royalty and advertising contribution clauses calculated as percentage of gross sales should be declared void. Seeks reimbursement plus alleges bad-faith conduct.
- **Status:** Re-assigned to new court division. Hearing scheduled January 27, 2026.
- **BKC party status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."

#### 4. Olympia Tile International Inc. vs. RBI et al.
- **Forum:** Ontario Court of Justice (CV-20-00648343)
- **Claims:** $3.5M + $500K punitive damages for breach of contract related to tiles purchased for Tim Hortons renovation program.
- **BKC party status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."

#### 5. Deschatelets v. RBI et al. (Tim Hortons Roll Up To Win Class Action)
- **Forum:** Superior Court of Quebec (500-06-001306-246)
- **Claims:** Tim Hortons sent email to ~500K customers saying they won a boat; later said it was a technical error. Seeks specific performance or damages plus CAD $10K punitive per class member.
- **Status:** Class certified June 2025 (limited to Quebec). TDL appealing; hearing anticipated March 2026.
- **BKC party status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."

#### 6. Plymouth County Retirement Association et al. v. RBI et al. (Carrols Acquisition)
- **Forum:** Court of Chancery, State of Delaware (C.A. No. 2024-1030)
- **Filed:** October 7, 2024
- **Parties:** Purported former shareholders of Carrols Restaurant Group vs. RBI and two individual CRG directors
- **Claims:** Breach of fiduciary duty by RBI as alleged controlling shareholder; unjust enrichment in Carrols acquisition. Allege RBI coerced CRG and directors had undisclosed conflicts.
- **Status:** Motion to dismiss denied July 2025. Trial set for February 1, 2027. **Active — headed to trial.**
- **BKC party status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."
- **Diligence note:** Directly related to the Carrols acquisition that converted 1,023 franchised restaurants to company-owned. Outcome could affect the refranchising program and purchase price fairness.

#### 7. Pemberton v. RBI (BK Website Privacy Class Action)
- **Forum:** US District Court, Northern District of California (4:25-cv-03647)
- **Filed:** May 2, 2025
- **Claims:** www.bk.com allegedly placed tracking cookies (Google, Meta, Microsoft Clarity, Snap, etc.) after users opted out. CIPA violations, common law privacy claims.
- **Status:** CIPA claims dismissed as time-barred; trespass claim dismissed. Remaining claims proceeding. Amended complaint due January 29, 2026.
- **BKC party status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."

#### 8. ADP Direct Poultry Ltd. v. PLK, RBI et al. (Popeyes Supplier Dispute)
- **Forum:** Ontario Superior Court of Justice (CV-25-00741914)
- **Claims:** $35M + $1M punitive. Former Popeyes Canada chicken supplier alleges breach of oral supply agreement, conspiracy, defamation. Claims unauthorized supplier sold uninspected chicken.
- **Status:** PLK and RBI filed motion to strike and dismiss December 2025.
- **BKC party status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."

#### 9. Keswani/Sivertson v. Popeyes et al. (Drip Pricing Class Action)
- **Forum:** Supreme Court of British Columbia (VLC-S-S-254553)
- **Filed:** June 18, 2025
- **Claims:** Drip pricing on digital ordering platforms — delivery fees allegedly undisclosed until checkout. 12 fast-food defendants including PLK and RBI. Seeks damages, restitution, injunction.
- **BKC party status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."

#### 10. Hugi v. RBI (Firehouse Subs Website Privacy Class Action)
- **Forum:** US District Court, Southern District of Florida (1:25-cv-26046)
- **Filed:** January 2, 2026
- **Claims:** www.firehousesubs.com deploying tracking cookies before user opt-out and continuing after opt-out. CIPA, FSCA, common law claims.
- **BKC party status:** "We are not a party to this litigation."

### Summary Assessment
- **2 matters with direct franchise operations impact:** Arrington (no-hire clause class action, approaching mediation) and Plymouth County (Carrols acquisition fairness, trial Feb 2027).
- **3 privacy/tech class actions** against RBI affiliates (website tracking cookies). BKC not directly named.
- **1 matter challenging royalty/advertising structure** (Italian franchisee claiming percentage-of-gross-sales clauses should be void).
- **1 concluded matter confirming price-fixing liability:** Swiss court found BKE violated Cartel Act via coupon pricing. Settled.
- **1 concluded matter confirming wrongful termination liability:** Spanish court found BKE wrongfully terminated 3 of 4 franchise agreements. Damages paid.
- BKC is directly named as a party in only 2 matters: the consolidated Arrington no-hire class action and the Chaus termination enforcement.
