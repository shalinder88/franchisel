# Exhibit V — Digital App Services Agreement Deep Read

**Source:** PDF pages 942-956 (Exhibit V, 15 pages)
**Status:** Fully surfaced

---

## Structure

The agreement consists of two parts:
1. **Order Form** (pages 943-947): provider/franchisee identification, term, cloud services description, fee schedule, audit rights, and restaurant list.
2. **General Terms and Conditions (Exhibit A)** (pages 948-956): definitions, license grant, IP rights, confidentiality, limitations of liability, indemnification, termination, and miscellaneous.

Effective Date: 1/1/2026. Provider is BKC (or a BKC entity).

---

## Term

- **Initial Term:** 2 years from Effective Date.
- **Auto-renewal:** Successive 1-year periods unless 60 days' written non-renewal notice.
- **Termination ties to FA:** Agreement terminates automatically for any restaurant upon termination, expiration, or non-renewal of the Franchise Contract for that restaurant.

---

## Cloud Services Description

The Cloud Services include:
1. **Digital ordering** via the BK App, BK Website, and Third Party Platforms (Google, UberEats, DoorDash, GrubHub, etc.) — integrated with each restaurant's POS.
2. **Online Pickup:** Order via Digital Platform, pick up in-restaurant.
3. **Delivery:** Order via Digital Platform, delivered from restaurant.
4. **Loyalty program management** (Royal Perks): strategy, terms, reward types, tiers, earning/redeeming methods, member databases, hardware/software management, data analysis, marketing campaigns, personalized offers.

UI/UX is the same across App and Website. App deployed on iOS and Android. Includes restaurant locator, menu function with nutritional info, and offer/coupon function.

## Digital Services Fee

| Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Fixed fee | $110 per restaurant per month |
| Variable fee | 1% of total monthly Digital Sales per restaurant |
| Annual cap | $4,500 per restaurant per calendar year |

**Digital Sales** = gross sales from Transactions processed through any Digital Platform, excluding credit card token integrations and self-service kiosks.

**Transaction** includes: orders placed/paid via Digital Platform for pickup or delivery; orders placed via Digital Platform but paid in-restaurant; digital offer usage in any channel (dine-in, pickup, drive-thru); loyalty point accumulation via any Digital Platform or alternative method.

**Key restrictions:**
- Fees are not deductible from Gross Sales calculation.
- Fees are in addition to (not in lieu of) royalties, advertising contributions, and all other FA obligations.
- Adjustable by Provider once per calendar year (increase or decrease) starting 1/1/2026, to cover Provider's costs of providing, improving, and expanding the Cloud Services, as determined by Provider in its discretion.

**Payment:** Billed on 2nd Business Day of each month for prior month. Appears on ePay on 3rd Business Day. Due by 10th Business Day.

## License and IP Rights

- **License type:** Subscription-based, non-transferable, non-exclusive. For use solely in connection with restaurant operations per Franchise Contracts.
- **Ownership:** Provider, its Affiliates, and licensors own all IP Rights in the Cloud Services. Franchisee has no ownership rights.
- **No reverse engineering:** Franchisee may not decompile, reverse engineer, disassemble, or attempt to determine source code.
- **Feedback:** Any feedback/suggestions become Provider's sole property.
- **Assignment:** Franchisee irrevocably assigns to Provider any rights it may be deemed to have in modifications to Cloud Services.

## Data Ownership

**Cloud Data** is broadly defined to include: all data obtained by Provider in connection with the Cloud Services; all data from Authorized Users and Site Visitors; all data derived from franchisee's use of Cloud Services; all anonymous usage data, statistics, and reports.

**Exclusion:** Menu Data (food/beverage listings provided by franchisee) is not Cloud Data.

**Access rights:** Provider, its Affiliates, and designees may have full access (on-site and remote) to all franchisee computer data, equipment, and systems at any time. Provider may use this data for product/service evaluation, store performance analysis, EBITDA performance assessment, and to improve Cloud Services.

## Termination

- **For breach:** 10 days to cure payment failures; 30 days to cure other breaches.
- **Insolvency:** Provider may terminate immediately upon franchisee insolvency or bankruptcy.
- **FA-linked:** Provider may terminate per-restaurant upon any FA "Default" or "Event of Default." Agreement automatically terminates per-restaurant upon FA termination/expiration/non-renewal.

Digital Services Fee revenues are accounted for separately (not co-mingled with Ad Fund). Franchisee Association has annual audit right (at Association's cost). ## Key Findings

1. **"AS IS" with no SLAs.** There are zero service level commitments — no uptime guarantee, no error correction timeline, no performance benchmarks. If the app goes down, Provider has no contractual obligation to fix it on any timeline.

2. **Liability cap is extremely low.** Maximum direct damages = 6 months of fees. At $4,500/year cap, that's a maximum recovery of $2,250 per restaurant. For a restaurant doing $500K+ in digital sales annually, this is functionally zero.

3. **Data ownership is comprehensive.** Provider owns all Cloud Data including anonymous usage statistics, customer behavior data, and derived analytics. Provider can use this data for any purpose including selling to third parties. This is consistent with FA Section 5.D.4 where BKC owns "all net profits" from third-party data sales.

4. **Fee adjustment is at Provider's discretion.** Once per year, Provider can increase (or decrease) the fee to cover its costs of "providing, improving, and expanding" the Cloud Services — with no ceiling beyond the annual $4,500 cap per restaurant. If Provider raises the fixed fee or variable rate, the $4,500 cap is the only constraint.

5. **Auto-renewal makes termination difficult.** The Agreement auto-renews in 1-year increments. The only non-renewal mechanism is 60 days' written notice before period expiration. But since the Agreement terminates automatically with the FA, this is largely academic — franchisees cannot meaningfully exit the Digital Services relationship while operating a BK restaurant.

6. **Broad franchisee indemnification.** Franchisee indemnifies Provider for claims related to inaccurate Menu Data, law violations, and third-party interference. Given that Provider auto-retrieves menu data from the POS, errors in the pipeline could create indemnity exposure for the franchisee.

7. **Digital Sales definition is expansive.** Includes not just app/web orders but also digital offer usage at POS (dine-in, drive-thru) and loyalty point accumulation. As digital engagement grows, the 1% variable fee applies to an increasingly large portion of total Gross Sales.
