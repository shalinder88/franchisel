## A. Executive Snapshot

- **System size:** 6,701 US restaurants as of 12/31/2024 (5,524 franchised + 1,177 company-owned). Worldwide: 19,732 locations across 120+ countries. The system is contracting domestically: net -77 in 2024, net -264 in 2023, net -62 in 2022.

- **Carrols acquisition dominates 2024 data.** On May 16, 2024, RBI acquired Carrols Restaurant Group, converting 1,023 franchised restaurants to company-owned. This makes 2024 outlet tables misleading without context — the "Reacquired by Franchisor" column (1,061 units in 2024) is almost entirely this single transaction, not routine reacquisitions.

- **Total initial investment:** $2,049,200 to $4,705,600 for a freestanding traditional restaurant, excluding real estate. Non-traditional formats start as low as $348,400 (Big-Box).

- **Ongoing fee stack is heavy.** Standard royalty is 4.5% of Gross Sales. Advertising contribution is also 4.5% (as of 1/1/2025). Combined mandatory franchise-level fees reach 9.0% of Gross Sales before rent, digital services fees, investment spending, or other assessments.

- **Remodel pressure is a defining feature of this system.** Multiple overlapping programs (Reclaim the Flame, RTF2, BKoT, Midterm Remodel Forbearance) create time-bound obligations with punitive consequences — a missed remodel deadline triggers a +3.0% royalty surcharge on all Gross Sales until completion or franchise expiration.

- **Average traditional franchisee-owned restaurant Gross Sales: $1,658,463.** Median: $1,561,502. 43% of traditional franchised locations met or exceeded the average. Company-owned traditional average was higher at $1,730,667.

- **Remodel uplift data is extensive.** 893 remodeled restaurants showed an average 12.3% sales uplift and 11.1% traffic uplift vs. control restaurants. Scrape & Rebuild remodels averaged 26.1% uplift vs. 11.4% for Full remodels.

- **No exclusive territory.** The Franchise Agreement grants rights at a single location only, with no protected area, no exclusivity, and explicit reservation of all alternative distribution channels (internet, retail, grocery, convenience).

- **Successor franchise (renewal) is not a right** — it is an option requiring franchisor approval, signing the then-current Franchise Agreement (which may have materially different terms), paying a successor franchise fee, completing building upgrades, and executing a general release.

- **Post-term noncompete:** 1 year within 2 miles. No hamburger business of any kind.

- **Guarantor is RBI (or RBILP for CA/IL/MD/ND/RI/VA/WA residents).** Financial statements are consolidated parent-level (RBI/RBILP), not BKC standalone. RBI total assets $24.6B, net income $1.445B (2024), long-term debt $13.5B.

- **Fuel the Flame advertising program** ties the 4.5% advertising rate to a systemwide 4-Wall EBITDA benchmark of $230,000 as of 12/31/2026 — if not met, rate reverts to 4.0% unless franchisees vote to maintain it.

- **Crown Your Career program** offers BKC/affiliate employees a path to franchise ownership with direct BKC financing (Prime + 1-4%), but imposes severe restrictions: no distributions while loan is outstanding, no other business activity for 5 years or until loan repayment, BKC option to repurchase at depreciated value.

- **Franchise Success System (FSS)** grades (A/B/D/F) drive development fee discounts, royalty reductions, and remodel cash contributions, creating a performance-linked fee structure where underperformers pay substantially more.

## B. Pass 1 — Front Matter, Formats, Entry Structure, Fee Stack, and Initial Investment

### Franchisor
Burger King Company LLC, a Florida LLC formed February 4, 2022. Successor to BK Corporation (founded 1954, dissolved December 2022). Part of an internal reorganization where all assets, liabilities, and franchise agreements were transferred. Principal place of business: 5707 Blue Lagoon Drive, Miami, FL 33126. Wholly-owned indirect subsidiary of Restaurant Brands International Limited Partnership (RBILP), itself controlled by Restaurant Brands International Inc. (RBI), a publicly traded Canadian corporation. RBI also owns Tim Hortons, Popeyes, and Firehouse Subs.

### Concept
Quick-service hamburger restaurants offering a limited menu of breakfast, lunch, and dinner products under the BURGER KING marks and system. As of 12/31/2024: 19,732 worldwide, 6,701 in the US.

### Issuance Date
03/2025, as amended 01/2026.

### Development Structure
Three primary entry paths:

1. **Target Reservation Agreement (TRA):** Search for one restaurant site in a defined Target Area. Deposit: $5,000. Term: typically 1–1.5 years. No exclusivity.

2. **Multiple Target Reservation Agreement (MTRA):** Search for multiple sites. Deposit: $10,000 per committed opening. Term: typically 2–3 years. No exclusivity. Required for the 2024 Multi-Unit DIP.

3. **Development Agreement:** Geographic Territory for multiple restaurants with a binding development schedule. Prepay $50,000 per committed restaurant. Franchise fees and royalty/advertising rates are tied to FSS Development Grade. Not exclusive — BKC retains the right to operate or franchise other restaurants in the Territory.

Additional entry paths: **Carrols Refranchise Program** (purchasing former Carrols-operated restaurants from BKC with remodel obligations) and **Crown Your Career Program** (BKC employee path to ownership with direct financing).

### Special-Risk Signals
Two state-mandated risks are highlighted on PDF page 4:
1. Out-of-State Dispute Resolution — all litigation must be in Florida.
2. Mediation for Development Disputes — non-binding mediation required before suit, which may delay court resolution.

The Michigan Franchise Investment Act notice (pages 5–6) voids certain unfair franchise provisions including termination without good cause and refusal to renew without fair compensation.

## C. Pass 2 — Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, and Reporting Burden

### Item 8 — Restrictions on Sources of Products and Services

BKC establishes standards and specifications for most goods and services used in development and operation. In most cases, items must be obtained from approved suppliers or distributors. Purchases from approved/specified sources represent approximately 65–90% of establishment costs and 30–60% of non-occupancy operating costs.

**Sole-source items:** Network services, digital menu boards, Gift Card services, cashless payments processing, quality assurance inspections, and the technology platforms for ordering/delivery/loyalty (BK app, website). BKC or affiliates are the sole supplier of these technology platforms.

**Double Drive Thru** is required for all facility types except food courts, in-lines, indoor MRS, Big Box, and delivery facilities (ghost kitchens).

**Supplier approval process:** 90–160 days. Suppliers must meet specifications, maintain standards, participate in QA programs, and pay testing/audit costs. BKC retains sole discretion to approve or terminate suppliers. Specifications are shared with suppliers but not disclosed to franchisees.

**Supplier rebates and contributions:** Various suppliers make payments to BKC based on franchisee purchases (rebates, flat fees, below-market products/services). In 2024, BKC contributed approximately 8.4% of consolidated advertising fund revenue from these supplier payments. BKC officers own publicly traded shares in General Mills, Johnson & Johnson, Kraft Heinz, and PepsiCo (all approved suppliers).

**RSI (Restaurant Services Inc.):** Not-for-profit independent purchasing cooperative serving as agent for equipment, supplies, food, and distribution. 21-member Board (18 franchisees, 1 BKC representative, 1 minority representative, 1 independent). Funded by surcharges on products and supplier allowances. Issues patronage dividends.

**BKC revenue from franchisee purchases/leases:** Approximately $227 million in 2024, representing ~16% of BKC's total revenues of ~$1.427 billion (from unaudited internal statements).

**Insurance requirements:** Commercial general liability ($2M per occurrence, aggregate varies by portfolio size: $2M for 1–10 restaurants, $5M for 11–50, $10M for 50+), auto liability ($1M), all-risk property (full replacement cost), boiler and machinery, workers comp. Business interruption coverage required with minimum 12-month indemnity period.

### Item 9 — Franchisee's Obligations
Standard cross-reference table linking 26 obligation categories to agreement sections. Notable obligations include: mandatory indemnification (Franchise Agmt. Sec. 13, 15), mandatory guarantee of obligations (Guaranty), and right of re-entry by BKC (Franchise Agmt. Sec. 15).

### Item 11 — Franchisor's Assistance, Advertising, Computer Systems, and Training

**Pre-opening assistance:** Site designation (geographic area only — BKC does not negotiate leases), Site Approval, standard architectural plans (downloadable, franchisee responsible for code adaptation), pre-opening training, on-site personnel at BKC's discretion, MOD Manual (on loan).

**Ongoing assistance:** Periodic inspections, marketing/operations counsel, update of standards and specifications, mandatory technology platforms for ordering/delivery/loyalty.

**Advertising:** BKC collects up to 4.5% of Gross Sales as an advertising contribution. In addition, franchisees may collectively agree to local Investment Spending of up to 2.0% of Gross Sales within their DMA. BKC administers the national advertising fund. There is no advertising council with binding authority over BKC's use of funds. BKC is not obligated to spend advertising dollars proportionally in any market.

**MOD Manual:** Approximately 639 pages. Provided on loan, may be in electronic form. Contains equipment specifications, supplier lists, operational standards. Franchisee may not copy or disclose.

**Computer/Technology:** Franchisees must buy their own POS and inventory control systems including freestanding kiosks. Digital menu boards required (static menu board kit charged monthly if not installed). Network services from sole-source provider. Digital App Services Agreement mandatory.

**Training program:** Required before opening. Held in Miami, FL or other BKC-designated locations. In-Restaurant Training at authorized Training Restaurants. Fee: $7,500 per trainee. ServSafe certification required. No formal training table with subject/hours/classroom breakdown was found in the disclosure; training is described narratively. The FDD references specific course requirements (BK University, ServSafe, Franchisee Onboarding) but does not provide a tabulated training schedule.

## D. Pass 3 — Territory, Competition, Channels, and Encroachment

### Item 12 — Territory

**Franchise Agreement:** Grants the right to operate at a specific location only. No territory, no exclusivity, no protected area, no protected customer base. Explicit disclosure: "You may face competition from other franchisees, from outlets that we own, or from other channels of distribution or competitive brands that we control."

**TRA/MTRA:** Define Target Area(s) for site search but grant no exclusivity. If BKC proposes to develop a restaurant in a Target Area for itself or another franchisee, the TRA/MTRA holder gets a right-of-first-offer to develop that restaurant, but if they decline (or fail to meet requirements), BKC can proceed and terminate the TRA/MTRA.

**Development Agreement:** Grants a geographic Territory, but this is explicitly not exclusive. The franchisee must develop restaurants per a binding schedule, but BKC may still develop or franchise other restaurants in the Territory. BKC retains sole discretion over future franchise rights in any market.

**Alternative Distribution Channels:** BKC and affiliates reserve the right to sell BURGER KING branded products through grocery stores, convenience stores, internet, direct marketing, or any other channel — within any territory granted to a franchisee. No compensation to the franchisee. Franchisees may not use BK marks on any website, social media, domain name, or email address.

**Relocation:** No right to relocate without BKC's prior written approval.

**Military:** Army & Air Force Exchange Service holds development rights on US military establishments.

**Affiliate competition:** Tim Hortons, Popeyes, and Firehouse Subs (all RBI-owned) may operate in the same markets as Burger King. All four brands currently offer some overlapping products (sandwiches, fries, desserts, beverages). If a conflict arises between a BK franchisee and a TH/Popeyes/FHS franchisee, BKC's management team will attempt to resolve it based on what it deems best for the affected systems — but BKC is "not responsible for resolving" such conflicts.

**Future brands:** BKC and affiliates retain the right to establish or acquire other franchise or company-operated systems selling similar products under different marks.

## E. Pass 4 — Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Item 17 — Renewal, Termination, Transfer, and Dispute Resolution

**Term:** 20 years for freestanding traditional. May be shorter for non-traditional or where property control is shorter.

**Successor (Renewal):** No right of renewal. Option to obtain a successor Franchise Agreement for up to 20 years, conditioned on: compliance with all BKC agreements, property control obtained, meeting then-current successor requirements, BKC approval of Successor Franchise Application, execution of a general release, signing the then-current form of Franchise Agreement (which may have materially different terms), payment of successor franchise fee, completion of building upgrades, and passing inspections. No successor right if Big-Box or Delivery Restaurant.

**Termination by franchisee:** No contractual right; only as permitted by applicable state law.

**Termination by BKC without cause:** Not applicable.

**Termination by BKC with cause — curable defaults:**
- 5 days: operational/quality/health/sanitation standards; sale of unapproved products; abandonment; insurance.
- 10 days: non-payment of royalty or advertising fees.
- 30 days: late reports; unapproved equipment/uniforms/decor; maintenance failures; failure to remodel; deleterious behavior; unsatisfied judgment; failure to restore after damage; failure to comply with Operating Partner/Managing Owner/Managing Director requirements.
- 60 days: failure to pay bills; failure to comply with any other Franchise Agreement, lease, or related agreement provision.
- 180 days: failure to meet minimum financial criteria per BKC's then-current financial policy.

**Termination by BKC with cause — non-curable defaults:** Bankruptcy; conviction of indictable offense (>1 year imprisonment) or fraud/dishonesty; unfair competition; false/misleading statements; understatement of Gross Sales; failure to close immediately for serious health/safety issues; unauthorized transfer; repeated breaches after notice; termination of delivery aggregator agreement (for Delivery Restaurants); event of default under RTF Master Program Agreement.

**Cross-default provisions:** Under Crown Your Career, a default under any BK franchise agreement constitutes a default under all program agreements. Under Carrols Refranchise, a default under any lease or any agreement related to any BK Restaurant in the refranchising transaction constitutes a default under the Franchise Agreement.

**Post-termination obligations:** Cease use of all trademarks; de-identify restaurant; pay all amounts due including royalties and advertising contributions for the remainder of the term; return MOD Manual and proprietary information.

**Transfer:** BKC consent required. Buyer must be approved, complete all training, all monies current, transfer fees paid ($2,000 + $500/additional), new franchisee training fee ($7,500), assignment of existing FA or signing of then-current FA (terms may substantially differ), general release by seller, restaurant inspected, all repair and maintenance performed, restaurant brought to current image. Under Crown Your Career, BKC may withhold consent to any transfer for any reason during the first 5 years. Under Carrols Refranchise, BKC may withhold consent for any reason until all required remodels are completed.

**Right of first refusal:** BKC and its designee have the right to purchase the restaurant (or any interest) on the same terms as any third-party offer.

**Option to purchase:** Upon termination or expiration, BKC has the option to purchase usable paper goods/containers/menus at franchisee's cost, and restaurant equipment/furniture/fixtures/signs at fair market value. Under Crown Your Career, BKC also has the option to purchase real estate premises at fair market value less outstanding debt. Under Carrols Refranchise, if required remodels are not completed, BKC may purchase all restaurants from the refranchising transaction at depreciated value.

**Death or disability:** Heir must be approved or sell within 12 months. BKC has the option to operate/manage the restaurant in the interim.

**Noncompete during term:** May not own, operate, or have any interest in any other hamburger business. May not use BK System, marks, or trade secrets outside BK operations. Crown Your Career adds: no other business activity until 5th anniversary or loan repayment (whichever is later).

**Noncompete after termination:** 1 year post-termination, within 2 miles of the restaurant — no ownership, operation, or interest in any hamburger business.

**Dispute resolution:** Mandatory non-binding mediation for development disputes only. No mandatory arbitration. Voluntary arbitration at franchisee's election for development disputes. Delivery Restaurant franchisees waive all development dispute rights.

**Choice of forum:** US District Court for the Southern District of Florida, or state court in Miami-Dade County, Florida (subject to state law).

**Choice of law:** Florida law (subject to state law).

**Modification:** Written instrument only.

**Integration clause:** Franchise Agreement, addenda, and TRA/MTRA constitute the entire agreement. Representations or promises outside these documents and the FDD may not be enforceable.

**Lease/Sublease (BKL):** 20-year term (may be shorter for non-traditional). Franchisee has no termination right. BKC may terminate for default (cross-defaulted with Franchise Agreement). No right of renewal — successor lease is contingent on completing remodels, signing current form BKL and FA, paying current fees/rent, and signing a release.

**Development Agreement:** Term is as agreed. Termination by BKC if development schedule defaults are not cured. Brand Damage Fee on termination equals remaining prepaid franchise fees. Noncompete: during term plus 1 year within the Territory.
