## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Domino's Pizza Franchising LLC** is a Delaware limited liability company that serves as the franchisor of Domino's Pizza stores in the United States. It is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Domino's Pizza Master Issuer LLC (DPMI), itself an indirect subsidiary of Domino's Pizza LLC (DPL) and Domino's Pizza, Inc. (DPI), which is publicly traded on the NYSE. The corporate structure is a securitization vehicle — DPF collects franchise royalties and fees, while DPL provides all actual services to franchisees under a management agreement.

2. **System size**: As of December 28, 2025, the U.S. system comprises 7,210 Traditional Stores (6,948 franchised, 262 company-owned) and 26 Non-Traditional Stores (all franchised), totaling 7,236 domestic outlets. Internationally, DP International Franchising has granted 14,956 franchises. There are 356 active Development Agreements.

3. **Investment range**: A Traditional Store requires an estimated initial investment of $231,450 to $743,500. A Non-Traditional Store requires $107,450 to $709,500. The largest component is leasehold improvements ($67,000–$350,000 Traditional).

4. **Fee burden**: Royalty of 5.5% of Royalty Sales plus 4% advertising fund contribution plus 1–4% cooperative advertising (max 9% combined advertising). Additional technology fees include $0.385 per digital order, PULSE license and maintenance costs, and various hardware fees. The total ongoing fee load is among the highest in QSR franchising.

5. **Item 19 highlights**: Five-year AWUS data shows 2024 combined average of $26,269/week (~$1.37M annualized). Franchised median was $25,160/week (~$1.31M). EBITDA tiers range from 1.7% (<$15K AWUS) to 14.7% (>$30K), with 490 stores in the bottom tier generating marginal returns.

6. **Bottom-quartile risk**: The 490 stores with AWUS below $15,000 had average EBITDA of only 1.7%, with 51.4% of those stores falling below even that level. At $15K AWUS ($780K annualized), a 1.7% EBITDA yields ~$13,260 — below typical owner's draw. This represents approximately 7.8% of the 6,262 stores reporting.

7. **Financial health**: DPF reported net income of $491.6M on revenue of $512.1M in FY2025. The entity is essentially a pass-through: total assets are only $17.5M, and $490.1M was distributed to the parent. DPF has guaranteed ~$5.25 billion in securitized notes issued by DPI subsidiaries. PwC issued an unqualified opinion with no going-concern language.

8. **Growth trajectory**: Net +167 Traditional stores in 2025 (214 opened vs. 17 closures). Projected 188 new franchised + 7 company-owned openings in the next fiscal year. DA activity is robust (356 active, up from 168 at end of 2022).

9. **Territory**: Not exclusive. Area of primary responsibility ~1 mile radius for Traditional stores. DPF can adjust delivery area at sole discretion. DPF may also operate different brands or distribution channels within the area.

10. **Renewal risk**: 10-year term, 10-year renewal — but must sign then-current FA which "may contain other terms materially different from the terms of your original franchise agreement." Refurbishment or relocation may be required.

11. **Litigation**: Active federal securities class action alleging false/misleading statements about net store growth guidance. Five derivative lawsuits. Joint employer claims. These pose reputational risk but do not directly affect franchise operations.

12. **Gag clause**: Explicitly absent. No confidentiality clauses restricting franchisee speech.

13. **Special risks**: No state-mandated special risks listed on the Special Risks page.

14. **Noncompete**: During term — no involvement in any similar business anywhere. Post-term — 1 year, within 10 miles of store. Washington state addendum limits enforceability based on earnings thresholds.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Domino's Pizza Franchising LLC was organized on March 2, 2007 as part of a securitization transaction. The entity is structured as a single-member LLC, treated as a disregarded entity for tax purposes. Its sole purpose is to serve as the franchisor of record for U.S. Domino's Pizza stores, collecting royalties and fees. All actual franchise support services — site selection, training, operations support, advertising — are provided by Domino's Pizza LLC (DPL) under a management agreement.

The IP is held by Domino's IP Holder LLC under a 99-year license to DPF and DPMI. Domino's Pizza Distribution LLC (DPD) serves as the primary food supplier. Domino's National Advertising Fund Inc. (DNAF) administers the advertising fund as a not-for-profit. This structure means the franchisor entity has minimal assets ($17.5M) while guaranteeing billions in securitized debt — a structure a prospective franchisee should understand thoroughly.

Franchise applicants must have been a Store general manager for at least 12 months (single store) or a supervisor for 12 months (multi-unit). A Controlling Person must own 51% or more and cannot have outside business involvement without written approval. These are among the more stringent qualification requirements in franchising.

### Fee Stack

**Percentage-Based Ongoing Fees:**
The royalty rate is 5.5% of weekly Royalty Sales, paid via electronic funds transfer by Thursday of each week. Royalty Sales are defined as total receipts from all sales of authorized products less taxes and approved discounts. The advertising fund contribution is 4% of Royalty Sales. In addition, cooperative advertising contributions of 1–4% may apply, with DPF retaining the right to mandate at least 2% to cooperatives. The maximum combined advertising burden is 9% of Royalty Sales. Adding royalty to the maximum advertising creates a potential 14.5% top-line fee burden before any technology or other fees.

Incentive programs may temporarily reduce royalty and/or advertising contributions. A $25,000 reservation fee applies if Development Agreement stores are not opened on schedule.

**Technology and System Fees:**
Technology fees are extensive and numerous. The PULSE point-of-sale system requires a $4,200 initial license fee to DPL plus annual enhancement fees of $819.25 (capped at 5% annual increases unless the Operations Advisory Council approves more). Third-party PULSE vendor fees are $1,357.04 annually. Help desk support costs $44 per call or $28 per chat. A connectivity fee of $1,200/year applies if broadband is available but not installed. The technology transaction fee is $0.385 per digital order. Credit card processing security fees are $0.0525 per transaction. SmartRecruiters hiring platform costs $432/year per store.

Hardware fees include server bundles ($4,025.20), carryout tracker bundles ($440.87), Meraki networking licensing ($280/year), customer-facing payment terminals ($709.92), and various other devices. The total PULSE hardware cost ranges from $15,000 to $25,000, with annual maintenance estimated at up to $7,000.

The Brand Technology addition cap of 1.5% of cumulative Royalty Sales (10-year lookback) provides some protection against unlimited mandatory technology spending, but this cap does not apply to PULSE system acquisition or renewal-related requirements.

**Other Fees:**
Transfer fee of $1,500 per transaction. Training fees up to $1,250 per session. Inspection costs variable. Audit expenses chargeable if understatement exceeds 2%. Customer Care billing of $20–$30 per complaint call. Environmental/EPR costs in Oregon and Colorado. Interest on late payments at 1.5% per month (2% above Libor for Alaska/Hawaii).

### Initial Investment

For a Traditional Store, the total estimated initial investment ranges from $231,450 to $743,500. The largest variable component is leasehold improvements ($67,000–$350,000), reflecting wide variation in location condition and landlord contribution. Furniture, fixtures, and equipment range from $105,000 to $145,000. Insurance costs $25,000–$75,000 annually (potentially higher in certain states). Three months of additional funds are estimated at $10,000–$73,000.

For a Non-Traditional Store, the range is $107,450–$709,500, with lower furniture/equipment costs ($62,000–$136,000) and lower insurance ($15,000–$50,000).

DPL from time to time sells company-owned stores to franchisees; in 2025, two such sales exceeded the Item 7 upper range. Delivery vehicles are not included in these estimates.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
DPF maintains extensive control over the supply chain. DPD (an affiliate) is the approved supplier of fresh dough, thin crust, and numerous food products. DPD generated $2.7 billion in revenue from franchisee sales in FY2025, representing 55.4% of DPL's total $4.9 billion revenue. DPL separately collected $155 million in online ordering, technology, credit card, help desk, and miscellaneous franchisee fees.

Coca-Cola beverages are the company standard, though franchisees may purchase from any supplier. DPD offers an optional profit-sharing plan where franchisees commit to 10-year exclusive purchasing in exchange for a share of DPD center profits.

Franchisees seeking to use non-approved items or suppliers face a 180-day evaluation period. The number of approved suppliers may be limited, and DPF/affiliates may be the only source for certain products.

### Training Program
Pre-opening training is mandatory. Franchise candidates must complete required classes at Ann Arbor, MI headquarters or approved training stores. Travel and lodging are at the franchisee's expense. Ella Meier, VP Operations Services (with DPL since 2013), oversees training. Franchisees bear responsibility for training all managers and team members. A 1-year window applies after training completion to acquire a franchise.

### Technology Stack
The PULSE point-of-sale system is mandatory for all U.S. stores and includes order management, driver dispatch, labor scheduling, cash control, reporting, commissary ordering, and customer database functions. The system requires specific hardware purchased from Getronics (the sole approved vendor). DPL provides software and help desk services. DPF maintains independent access to all data on franchisee systems without contractual limitations.

Additional Order Systems may become mandatory at any time. Franchisees currently must participate in an Additional Order System from a designated third-party vendor. SmartRecruiters is the mandated hiring platform.

### Advertising Fund
DNAF spent $593 million in FY2025: 84% on media placement, 7.3% on administrative expenses, 5.4% on other items, and 3.3% on production. An independent CPA audits the fund annually. The Domino's Marketing Advisory Council (8 franchisees + 2+ corporate reps) is advisory only. The "Roll-Up" mechanism, in effect since January 2010, allows DPF to redirect cooperative contributions to the national fund.

Vendor/supplier contributions in 2025: DNAF received ~$9.4M and DPL received ~$31.3M from programs including Coca-Cola, Dairy Management Inc., Uber Eats, DoorDash, and others.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Territory Protection
Domino's does not grant exclusive territories under any agreement type. Traditional Stores receive an "area of primary responsibility" — generally a 1-mile radius (½-mile in dense areas) — within which DPF will not operate or grant another franchise with an overlapping area. This is overlap protection, not exclusivity.

Critically, DPF retains the right to: (1) adjust the delivery/service area at its sole discretion to account for market conditions; (2) operate or franchise under different brands within the area; (3) distribute Domino's products through other channels. Enclosed malls, airports, stadiums, and similar venues are excluded from the area.

Non-Traditional Stores receive only premises-level protection. Development Agreement holders get area protection during the DA term but lose it upon termination (which can be triggered by missing development requirements).

### Encroachment Risk
The combination of non-exclusive territory, adjustable delivery areas, and excluded venue types creates meaningful encroachment risk, particularly as DPF pursues aggressive growth (188 projected new franchised openings in the next fiscal year). The third-party delivery partnerships with Uber Eats and DoorDash also expand the competitive landscape within any given area.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
The franchise term is 10 years for both Traditional and Non-Traditional agreements. Renewal is available for an additional 10 years, subject to: no material default, substantial compliance, signing the then-current franchise agreement (which may contain "other terms materially different" from the original), refurbishment or potential relocation per current standards, and possibly completing an Application for Franchise (Renewal). The "materially different terms" language is a significant risk factor at renewal.

### Termination
DPF may not terminate without cause. Franchisees may terminate if DPF breaches and fails to cure within 30 days. DPF's for-cause termination rights are extensive, with both incurable defaults (including bankruptcy, abandonment, felony, underreporting, repeated violations) and curable defaults with short cure periods (48 hours for insurance, 7 days for marks/safety/food quality, 10 days for payment, 30 days for other).

### Transfer
Transfer requires DPF approval. The transferee must meet current franchisee standards, not operate any other pizza business, sign the current form of franchise agreement, refurbish the store, complete training, and pay a $1,500 transfer fee. DPF has a right of first refusal to match any offer. On termination or non-renewal, DPF has an option to purchase the store based on a percentage of the prior 12 months' Royalty Sales less deductions.

### Death/Disability
Within 120 days, the personal representative must submit a transfer proposal to a DPF-approved party.

### Noncompete
During the franchise term: no involvement in any similar business anywhere — this is an unusually broad geographic scope. Post-termination: 1 year, within 10 miles of the store premises or at the store premises.

### Dispute Resolution
No arbitration or mediation is required. No choice of forum is specified. Choice of law is the state where the Store is located. This is relatively franchisee-friendly — many franchise systems mandate arbitration in the franchisor's home state.

### Litigation
The pending securities class action (State of Rhode Island v. DPI) alleges false/misleading statements about global net store growth guidance during December 2023–July 2024. Five derivative lawsuits allege breach of fiduciary duty and insider trading by directors/officers. Multiple joint employer lawsuits challenge DPL's relationship with franchisee employees. While these do not directly affect individual franchise operations, they indicate corporate governance scrutiny and could affect brand reputation.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

Domino's provides one of the more robust FPRs in franchising. The disclosure covers 5 years of Average Weekly Unit Sales (AWUS), median weekly sales, and pro-forma EBITDA analysis across 5 AWUS tiers.

**Sales Performance:**
The 2024 combined AWUS was $26,269/week ($1,366,000 annualized), up from $25,810 in 2023 and $25,334 in 2022 — a steady recovery from the post-pandemic normalization. Franchised AWUS ($26,467) was slightly above company-owned ($26,120) in 2024, reversing a 2020 gap. The median combined was $25,210, indicating modest positive skew.

In 2024, 51% of franchised stores and 50% of company-owned stores achieved or exceeded the AWUS. This near-50/50 split is expected given the metric is an average.

**EBITDA Analysis:**
The EBITDA tier analysis is particularly valuable. Based on 6,262 franchised stores (93.5% of the 6,699 operating at year-end):

- Bottom tier (<$15K AWUS, ~$780K annual): 1.7% EBITDA → ~$13K annual earnings. 490 stores (7.8% of population). More than half fall below even this level. These stores are marginal at best.
- Second tier ($15K–$20K, ~$910K annual): 6.7% EBITDA → ~$61K annual earnings. 1,135 stores.
- Middle tier ($20K–$25K, ~$1.17M annual): 10.1% EBITDA → ~$118K. 1,426 stores.
- Upper-middle tier ($25K–$30K, ~$1.43M annual): 12.1% EBITDA → ~$173K. 1,350 stores.
- Top tier ($30K+, ~$1.56M+ annual): 14.7% EBITDA → ~$229K+. 1,861 stores (29.7% of population).

Variable costs decline as a percentage from 80.9% to 76.4% as volume increases, demonstrating operating leverage. Fixed costs decline more sharply (20.2% to 11.4%), as rent, utilities, and manager salary are spread across more volume.

**Cohort Comparability:** The FPR uses actual franchised store results for 2024, not a pro-forma with different fee rates. The royalty rate in the disclosure is 5.5% and this is the rate applied to franchised stores. No cohort comparability gap identified.

**Substantiation:** Written substantiation is available upon reasonable request. Supplemental siting model data may also be provided for specific locations.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### Multi-Year Trajectory
Domino's shows consistent, moderate growth in its Traditional franchise system:
- 2023: +178 openings, -16 closures = net +162 franchised
- 2024: +160 openings, -9 closures = net +151 franchised
- 2025: +214 openings, -17 closures = net +197 franchised

The acceleration in 2025 openings (214 vs. 160 in 2024) and the increase in active Development Agreements (356 vs. 226 at end of 2023) signal an aggressive growth push. Projected next-year openings: 188 franchised + 7 company-owned.

### Transfer Activity
Transfers averaged ~520 per year over 2023–2025 (523, 550, 490). The 2025 figure of 490 includes significant activity in Texas (130 transfers, likely a multi-store portfolio sale), Maryland (transfers related to the company-store refranchising), and Washington (31). High transfer volume is normal for a system of this size and indicates an active resale market.

### Closures and Attrition
Terminations were minimal: 8 (2023), 2 (2024), 9 (2025). Non-renewals: 1, 1, 2 respectively. Ceased operations (mutual): 7, 6, 4. Total closures were 16, 9, and 17 across the three years. For a 7,000-unit system, this represents annual attrition well below 0.3% — exceptionally low.

The 2025 increase in terminations to 9 is worth noting but remains minimal. Ohio accounted for 7 of the 9 terminations in 2025.

### Company-Owned Operations
DPF operated company stores in 7 states at end of 2024 (FL, MD, MI, NV, TX, VA). In 2025, all 36 Maryland company stores were sold to a franchisee, reducing company-owned count from 292 to 262. NV and TX continue to grow company-owned presence. The sale of Maryland stores is consistent with DPL's historical practice of occasionally refranchising clusters.

### Projected Openings
29 franchise agreements are signed but not yet opened. 188 new franchised outlets and 7 new company-owned outlets are projected for the next fiscal year. Top projected states: TX (16), IN (11), FL (13), CA (10), TN (10), PA (8), GA (8).

### Gag Clause
Domino's explicitly states: "We have not entered into agreements with confidentiality clauses that would prevent franchisees from discussing their experience with Domino's." This is favorable for prospective franchisees seeking validation from current operators.

### Franchisee Organizations
Two independent organizations: Domino's Franchisee Association (San Antonio, TX) and Domino's Franchisee Forum (Elgin, IL). DPF sponsors three advisory groups: DMAC, Operations Advisory Council, and Supply Chain Advisory Committee.

## H. Item 21 — Financial Statements

### DPF Financial Statements (Exhibit C)

**Auditor and Opinion:**
PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP (Detroit, Michigan) issued an unqualified opinion on March 23, 2026. No going-concern language. No emphasis paragraphs. Clean opinion.

**Income Statement:**
DPF's sole revenue source is franchise royalties and fees: $512.1M in FY2025, up from $487.1M in 2024 and $463.3M in 2023. Revenue growth of 5.1% (2025 vs. 2024) reflects both same-store sales improvement and new store openings. Operating expenses are minimal: $20.1M total, primarily the management fee to DPL ($19.1M) plus depreciation ($381K) and other expenses ($637K). The management fee decreased from $23.6M in 2024 due to the September 2025 refinancing that restructured the fee allocation methodology. Income from operations was $492.0M — a 96.1% operating margin reflecting the pass-through nature of the entity.

Net income was $491.6M after minimal interest income ($34K, down from $454K) and trivial tax provision ($453K — non-resident withholding only, as DPF is a disregarded entity for tax).

**Balance Sheet:**
Total assets of only $17.5M underscore DPF's asset-light structure. Current assets ($14.0M) consist mainly of restricted cash ($2.9M) and accounts receivable ($10.6M). Property is minimal — two buildings worth $16.5M gross, $3.2M net of depreciation. Total liabilities are just $255K in payables. Member's interest of $17.2M represents the entity's thin capitalization. In 2025, DPF received a $1.0M capital contribution from the Parent to qualify for large franchisor registration exemptions.

**Cash Flows:**
Operating cash flow of $490.3M tracks closely to net income. Investing activities were negligible ($137K). Financing outflows of $489.1M consisted almost entirely of distributions to the Parent ($490.1M), partially offset by the $1.0M capital contribution. DPF is effectively a cash conduit.

**Distributions:**
Distributions to the Parent were $490.1M (2025), $478.5M (2024), and $440.2M (2023) — essentially 100% of net income. These funds service the securitized debt through the Parent structure.

**Debt Guarantees:**
DPF, along with other DPI subsidiaries, has guaranteed approximately $5.25 billion in securitized notes across five series (2025, 2021, 2019, 2018, 2017). The 2025 variable funding note facility allows up to $320M in additional borrowing. Debt covenants require a minimum 1.75x debt service coverage ratio. The securitized assets include royalty income, supply chain income, and intellectual property from all U.S. and international operations.

**Note Highlights:**
- Revenue recognized at 5.5% royalty rate on retail sales at point of delivery/carryout
- Fiscal year is 52 weeks ending Sunday nearest December 31
- Cash is restricted under Parent debt agreements
- No material subsequent events identified through March 23, 2026
- American Territories (PR, Guam, USVI) generated $2.0M of royalties
- Management fee allocation changed post-September 2025 refinancing; DPF portion expected lower going forward

## I. State Addenda Summary

State addenda are embedded in the FDD body (pages 89–95), covering 6 individual states plus a multi-state provision. No separate state addenda exhibit exists.

**Multi-State No-Waiver (14 states):**
Applicable to CA, HI, IL, IN, MD, MI, MN, NY, ND, RI, SD, VA, WA, WI. Prohibits waiver of franchise law claims or disclaimer of reliance on franchisor statements. This is standard protective language.

**California:**
The Fast Food Act (AB 1228) disclosures are the most significant addition. The $20/hour minimum wage for fast food employees (effective April 1, 2024) with Council authority to raise by up to 3.5% annually through 2028 directly affects labor economics. DPF provides no training or assistance related to Fast Food Act compliance — franchisees bear full responsibility.

**Illinois:**
Forum selection and choice of law exceptions preserve Illinois law claims.

**Maryland:**
Fee deferral is the key override — all initial fees deferred until pre-opening obligations are complete; development fees deferred until first franchise opens. This indicates the Maryland Securities Commissioner identified financial assurance concerns. Releases exclude Maryland Franchise Law liability. Bankruptcy termination may not be enforceable under federal law. 3-year statute of limitations for Maryland claims.

**Minnesota:**
Comprehensive protections: litigation cannot be required outside Minnesota; no jury trial waiver; no liquidated damages, termination penalties, or judgment notes; 90-day termination notice with 60-day cure; 180-day non-renewal notice; transfer consent not unreasonably withheld; trademark protection/indemnification; $30 service charge cap; 3-year statute of limitations.

**Washington:**
The most extensive state addendum. Key overrides: Washington Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails in conflicts; noncompete provisions void if employee earnings below $100K/year (independent contractor below $250K); no-poach provisions void per RCW 49.62.060; release excludes WA Franchise Act rights unless negotiated with independent counsel; transfer fees limited to reasonable costs. DPL also entered an Assurance of Discontinuance with the WA Attorney General in October 2018 regarding no-solicitation/no-hire provisions.
