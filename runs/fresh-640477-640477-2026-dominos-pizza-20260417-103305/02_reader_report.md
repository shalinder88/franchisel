# 02 Reader Report — Domino's Pizza Franchising LLC (Filing 640477, April 2026)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: Domino's Pizza Franchising LLC, a Delaware LLC, wholly-owned subsidiary of Domino's Pizza Master Issuer LLC; indirect subsidiary of Domino's Pizza LLC (DPL) and Domino's Pizza, Inc. (DPI, NYSE-listed). Issuance date April 1, 2026.
2. **System Size**: As of December 28, 2025: 7,210 Traditional Stores (6,948 franchised, 262 company-owned) and 26 Non-Traditional Stores (all franchised). Total 7,236 domestic stores. International: 14,956 franchises through DP International Franchising. 356 active Development Agreements.
3. **Founded**: 1960 (Traditional), 1990 (Non-Traditional). Franchising since 1967. Securitization structure since April 2007.
4. **Investment Range**: Traditional Store: $231,450–$743,500. Non-Traditional Store: $107,450–$709,500.
5. **Fee Burden**: Royalty 5.5% of Royalty Sales, Advertising Fund 4%, cooperatives 1–4%, tech transaction fee $0.385/digital order, PULSE license $4,200 initial + $819.25/yr enhancement, help desk $44/call, plus numerous technology hardware/licensing fees.
6. **Item 19 FPR**: 5-year AWUS data (2020–2024). 2024 franchised AWUS $26,467/week (~$1.376M annualized). EBITDA ranges from 1.7% (<$15K AWUS) to 14.7% (>$30K AWUS). 6,262 stores included in EBITDA analysis.
7. **Item 20 Growth**: Net +167 Traditional in 2025 (214 opened, 9 terminations, 2 non-renewals, 2 reacquired, 4 ceased). Steady growth trajectory. Projected 188 new franchised + 7 company-owned openings in next fiscal year.
8. **Financial Health (DPF)**: Net income $491.6M (2025) on franchise royalties/fees of $512.1M. Total assets $17.5M (asset-light securitization vehicle). PwC unqualified opinion. No going concern. Distributions $490.1M to parent.
9. **Territory**: Area of primary responsibility ~1 mile radius (Traditional); premises only (Non-Traditional). Not exclusive. Delivery/service area adjustable by franchisor.
10. **Renewal**: 10-year initial term, 10-year renewal. Must sign then-current agreement which may be "materially different." Refurbish/relocate possible at renewal.
11. **Noncompete**: During term: no involvement in any similar business anywhere. Post-term: 1 year, within 10 miles of Store premises.
12. **Litigation**: Active securities class action (Rhode Island v. DPI, Weiner, Reddy — false/misleading statements re net store growth guidance). Five derivative lawsuits. Joint employer claims. No concluded actions required to be disclosed.
13. **Special Risks**: No state-mandated special risks listed.
14. **Gag Clause**: Explicitly stated no confidentiality clauses restricting franchisee speech.

## Pass B: Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — Entity Structure and History
DPF is the franchisor of record since April 2007 following a securitization transaction. DPF is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Domino's Pizza Master Issuer LLC (DPMI), itself an indirect subsidiary of Domino's Pizza LLC (DPL), which is an indirect wholly-owned subsidiary of Domino's Pizza, Inc. (DPI, Delaware corp, publicly traded).

Key affiliates:
- **Domino's IP Holder LLC**: Owns all U.S. trademarks; 99-year license to DPF/DPMI.
- **Domino's Pizza Distribution LLC (DPD)**: Sole supplier of food products to franchisees. Delaware LLC, DPMI subsidiary. Revenue in FY2025: $2.7B (55.4% of DPL's $4.9B total).
- **Domino's National Advertising Fund Inc. (DNAF)**: Not-for-profit, DPL subsidiary. Administers advertising fund.
- **Domino's GC Inc.**: Gift card program, Florida corp, DPL subsidiary.
- **DP International Franchising Inc.**: International franchisor, 14,956 franchises as of Dec 28, 2025.

DPL is the actual operator providing all services under a management agreement. DPL also acts as DPF's franchise sales agent. If DPL fails, it can be replaced as servicer.

Franchise offering: Traditional Stores (retail shopping center locations) and Non-Traditional Stores (malls, airports, stadiums, etc.).

Franchisee qualifications: Must have been Store general manager for 12+ months (single store) or Store supervisor 12+ months (multi-unit). 51%+ ownership required (Controlling Person). No outside business involvement without written approval. Minimum net worth/liquidity requirements.

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Supplier Restrictions
- Must use DPF-specified brands/types/models for equipment, fixtures, signage.
- May purchase from any approved source; DPD and approved vendors supply. Approval process: submit specs, 180-day evaluation period.
- **Exclusive sources**: DPL sole supplier of PULSE software; DPD sole supplier of thin crust and fresh dough (unless approved commissary). One approved Gluten Free crust supplier.
- **Commissary**: Franchisee may operate if DPF/designee approves; must buy pre-mix from approved suppliers (DPD + 3 others).
- **Lease requirements**: Lease must contain DPF-specified terms; Rider to Lease (Exhibit K) mandatory; prior approval required.
- **Brand Technology**: DPF may modify all aspects; additions capped at 1.5% of cumulative Royalty Sales. Independent data access.
- **Beverage**: Coca-Cola designated as company standard; franchisees can buy from any supplier/retailer.
- **Gift Card Program**: Mandatory participation; Ceridian Stored Value Solutions processor; CARDCO CXXV Inc. issuer; Domino's GC Inc. distributor.
- **Pie Perks Program**: Optional national vendor discount program; no additional cost; no revenue to DPF.
- **Third-party agreements**: Uber Eats (through Jan 2027), DoorDash (through June 2029).
- **Supplier revenue**: DPD revenue from franchisee purchases $2.7B in FY2025. DPL collected $155M in online ordering/tech/credit card/help desk fees (3% of DPL revenue).
- **Insurance**: Property (replacement cost), GL ($1.5M/occurrence), auto ($1.5M/occurrence), workers' comp. Carrier rated B+ or better. 30-day notice to DPF.
- **DPD profit sharing plan**: Optional 10-year commitment to buy food from DPD in exchange for share of DPD center profits.

- Development area defined by map; not exclusive but protected during DA term.

- Termination triggers: failure to meet development requirements, non-compliance, cross-default, Controlling Person change.
- Does not prohibit different brands or other distribution channels within area.

- Area of primary responsibility: generally 1-mile radius (½-mile in dense areas).
- No overlap during term.
- Delivery/service area defined by DPF, adjustable at DPF's sole discretion.
- Must offer delivery during approved hours within service area; may limit for safety (annually reviewed).
- Cannot change boundaries without written consent.
- Exclusions same as DA.
- Relocation may be required at renewal.
- Does not prohibit different brands or other distribution channels.

**Non-Traditional Store:**
- Area of primary responsibility: the store premises only.
- Not exclusive; no territory protection beyond premises.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Franchise Agreement (Standard and Non-Traditional)

**Term**: 10 years initial.

**Renewal**: 10-year renewal if: written notice, no material default, substantial compliance, sign then-current FA (may contain materially different terms), maintain possession or secure substitute, refurbish/relocate per current standards, may require Application for Franchise (Renewal).

**Termination by franchisee**: Section 18.1 — If DPF breaches and doesn't cure after 30-day written notice.

**Termination by franchisor without cause**: None.

**Termination by franchisor with cause**: Section 18.2 — Multiple triggers.

**Defaults not curable**: Material misrepresentation, failure to open within 6 months, bankruptcy, assignment for benefit of creditors, abandonment, child labor violations, loss of possession, felony/dishonesty, intentional underreporting, unauthorized assignment, restrictive covenant violation, improper disposal of deceased/disabled person's interest, repeated violations, failure to pay after audit understatement within 10 days, asset seizure, public health/safety threat, failure to reopen/relocate within 6 months, anti-terrorism violations.

**Defaults with cure periods**: Insurance: 48 hours. Marks/safety/food quality: 7 days. Payment: 10 days. All other: 30 days. Health/sanitation cease operations: 7 days.

**Post-termination obligations**: Return Operating Manual, stop using customer lists/confidential info/Marks, assign phone numbers, pay outstanding amounts, de-identify store, make store accessible for DPF operation.

**Transfer**: Section 21.2/21.4 — DPF must approve; transferee must meet new franchisee standards, not operate other pizza business, sign current FA, refurbish, complete training, pay $1,500 transfer fee. Rider to Purchase Agreement required. Mutual termination agreement and customary release (subject to state law prohibitions).

**Right of first refusal**: Section 21.8 — DPF can match offer.

**Purchase option**: Section 19 — On termination/non-renewal, DPF may purchase at amount based on percentage of Royalty Sales during prior 12 months less deductions. If open <12 months, documented cost. May purchase real property at appraised value; commissary at net book value.

**Death/disability**: Section 21.5 — Personal representative must submit transfer proposal within 120 days; transferee must be DPF-approved.

**Noncompete during term**: Section 20.1 — No involvement in any similar business anywhere.

**Noncompete post-term**: Section 20.2 — 1 year, at store premises or within 10 miles.

**Modification**: Section 22.5 — No modifications except Operating Manual changes.

**Integration/Merger**: Sections 22.4/22.5 — Only FA is binding; does not disclaim FDD representations; subject to state law.

**Dispute resolution**: No arbitration or mediation required.

**Choice of forum**: None specified.

**Choice of law**: Section 22.6 — Laws of state where Store is located.
