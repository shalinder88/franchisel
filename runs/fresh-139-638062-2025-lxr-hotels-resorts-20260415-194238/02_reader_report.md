# Reader Report: LXR Hotels & Resorts FDD (638062-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand**: LXR Hotels & Resorts — Hilton's luxury collection brand. "Award-winning luxury hotel properties" positioned in "marquee destinations," each with its own distinct history and locale.
2. **Franchisor**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC (Delaware LLC, formed September 2007). Parent: Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. Ultimate parent: Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT).
3. **System size**: Extremely small — only 4 franchised hotels in the US as of December 31, 2024 (AZ 1, CA 1, NV 1, WA 1). Zero company-owned. Zero projected openings. Net +1 in 2024 (Arizona Biltmore).
4. **Investment range**: $5,364,048 to $845,773,369 for a typical 150-room LXR hotel, excluding real property. Up to $752,345 payable to franchisor/affiliates.
5. **Fee structure**: 5% monthly royalty on Gross Rooms Revenue + 4% monthly program fee (can increase by up to 1% over term) + 4.3% Hilton Honors fee on eligible guest folio + numerous technology, distribution, and compliance fees.
6. **Item 19**: No financial performance representations made. Standard no-FPR disclaimer.
7. **Franchise term**: New construction 23 years; conversion 10–20 years. NO renewal right — re-licensing only at franchisor's sole discretion, potentially under materially different terms.
8. **Territory**: Non-exclusive. No standard protected territory. May negotiate a "Restricted Area" but typically for a shorter period than the franchise term.
9. **Financial health**: Franchisor had $1.502 billion total revenue in 2024, $1.488 billion net income. All assets pledged as collateral for Hilton parent debt. Distributes ~$1.5 billion annually to parent.
10. **Litigation**: Multiple pending antitrust class actions (IDeaS, Amadeus, CoStar pricing algorithm cases). Concluded Texas AG settlement ($2.1M) and Nebraska settlement ($300K) over mandatory guest fee disclosure.
11. **Gag clause**: Present — "some current and former franchisees have signed provisions restricting their ability to speak about their experience."
12. **Special risk**: Out-of-state dispute resolution — Virginia (primary), New York (backup). Forum selection and governing law (New York) may be overridden by state addenda.
13. **Spa requirement**: Must install a luxury spa, estimated $0–$7.15M.
14. **Forbes Travel Guide**: Mandatory participation ($22,400/year), including on-site training and quality assessments.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction**: 23 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion**: 10–20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership**: Remaining term or as agreed
- **NO right to renew or extend**. Re-licensing only at franchisor's sole discretion.
- Re-licensing may require materially different terms, new PIP, and new franchise agreement
- HITS Agreement runs concurrently with Franchise Agreement

### Termination
**By franchisee**: Not authorized. Unilateral termination is a material breach → Termination Fee.

**By franchisor without cause:**
- Condemnation: terminates, no Termination Fee
- Casualty: 18 months to repair/rebuild; if elected not to, terminates (no Termination Fee if no competing hotel at site within 3 years)

**By franchisor with cause (curable):**
- Payment default: at least 10 days to cure
- Construction/opening deadline failure: at least 10 days to cure
- Insurance default: at least 10 days to cure
- Other Standard/Manual breach: at least 30 days to cure
- May require third-party guaranty as condition of curing payment default

**By franchisor with cause (non-curable, immediate termination):**
- Pattern of same non-compliance within 24 months (even if corrected after each notice)
- 3 default notices in any 12 months
- Insolvency/bankruptcy
- Loss of possession of hotel
- Failure to operate for 5+ consecutive days
- Contest of franchisor's System ownership
- Conduct adversely affecting brand reputation
- False books/concealed revenue/fraud
- Unauthorized transfer
- Become Sanctioned Person
- Gaming license issues
- Guarantor breach
- Public health/safety threat
- Become a Competitor
- Termination of any other Hotel Agreement

**Termination fees:**
- Pre-opening (no work begun, new hotel at site within 1 year): $25,000 × rooms
- Post-opening, before 2nd anniversary: Greater of (avg monthly royalty × 60) or ($25,000 × rooms)
- After 2nd anniversary, before last 60 months: Avg monthly royalty × 60
- Last 60 months: Avg monthly royalty × remaining months

**Post-termination obligations:**
- Immediate cessation of System use, Marks removal, sign removal
- Return Manual and Proprietary Information
- Transfer domain names to franchisor
- Pay all amounts due including termination fee

### Transfer
**Permitted Transfers** (no change of control):
- Public equity interest transfers: no notice needed
- Private <50% transfers with no control change: no notice needed
- Institutional investment funds with same manager: no notice needed
- Transfers to affiliates, family, on death: 60 days notice + consent + $5,500 fee

**Change of Ownership Transfers:**
- 60 days notice required
- Must not be in default; all amounts current
- Transferee must meet current franchise requirements
- Transferee signs current-form franchise agreement
- $150,000 Change of Ownership Application Fee
- PIP may be required
- Must not transfer to Competitor or Sanctioned Person

**No right of first refusal** (but must notify before marketing equity interests)
**No option to purchase**

**Death/disability:** Transfer to immediate family within 1 year, must meet then-current requirements.

### Non-Competition
- **During term**: Cannot be competitor brand owner/licensor. May be franchisee of competitor, manage competitor property (if not exclusive manager), or hold minority interest.
- **Post-term**: NO non-competition covenant.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Governing law**: New York (without choice-of-law principles)
- **Forum**: US District Court for Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria), or Fairfax County, VA state court, or New York, NY. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Arbitration**: None (except in Puerto Rico)
- **No waiver of jury trial** mentioned in standard agreement (though some state addenda address this)

### Modification
- Must be in writing, signed by franchisor officer
- May condition amendment on general release
