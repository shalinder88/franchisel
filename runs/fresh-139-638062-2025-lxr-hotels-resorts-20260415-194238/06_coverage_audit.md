## Contradictions Identified

1. **Arizona Biltmore in Table 3**: Shows as "+1 Ceased Operations - Other Reasons" in 2024, yet the system grew by 1 hotel. This appears to be a brand conversion (likely from Waldorf Astoria or another Hilton brand to LXR) rather than a cessation of operations. The coding is unusual but consistent with FDD table conventions for brand switches.

2. **Subsequent events date typo**: Note 9 of financial statements states "through March 18, 2024" — this is almost certainly a typographical error for "March 18, 2025" (matching the auditor's report date of March 18, 2025).

## Unresolveds

1. **Investment range utility**: The $5.4M–$845.8M range provides limited practical guidance. Franchisor acknowledges "we do not yet have actual initial investment information for an LXR hotel."
2. **HPMS migration**: Timeline and cost impact of transition from OnQ to HPMS is unknown; "not yet required for the Brand" but "anticipated."
3. **All assets pledged**: All franchisor assets pledged for Hilton parent debt maturing 2025-2033. Systemic risk factor.
4. **System maturity**: Only 4 hotels with zero projected openings — extremely limited US operational history for the brand.
5. **Forbes Travel Guide cost impact**: $22,400/year mandatory with no disclosed performance benchmarks.
6. **Spa ongoing management costs**: Build-out costs disclosed ($0–$7.15M) but ongoing spa management fees only described as "established by mutual agreement."
7. **Mandatory Guest Fee structure**: Included in GRR definition but no guidance on typical amounts or how they interact with rate-setting authority.
