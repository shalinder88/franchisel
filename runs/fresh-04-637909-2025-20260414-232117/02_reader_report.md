# Reader Report — Delah Coffee Franchising, LLC (637909-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand**: Delah Coffee — traditional Yemeni coffee, teas, cakes, and other menu items; on-premises dining, carryout, catering, delivery
2. **Franchisor**: Delah Coffee Franchising, LLC, a California LLC formed October 21, 2024; principal office at 7075 Commerce Circle, Pleasanton, CA 94588
3. **Ownership**: Majed Jahamee (CEO) and Omar Jahamee (District Manager/Trainer) are principals; family-operated
4. **System size**: 4 company-owned outlets in California (San Francisco x2, Oakland, Berkeley); 0 franchised outlets open; 2 license agreements signed (CA, IL) but not yet open; projects 9 new franchised outlets in next fiscal year
5. **Investment range**: $282,300–$424,000 (single unit FA); $342,300–$544,000 (multi-unit with MUDA)
6. **Initial franchise fee**: $35,000; Development Area Fee $30,000 per additional shop
7. **Ongoing fees**: Royalty 4.5% of Gross Sales weekly; Brand Development Fund up to 2% (currently not assessed); Local Marketing 1% monthly; Technology Fee up to $500/month (currently not assessed)
8. **Item 19**: FPR provided — 3 company-owned outlet financials (SF#1 2023/2024, Oakland 2024, Berkeley 2024); Gross Sales range $557K–$1.14M; Direct Gross Profit after fees 20.6%–22.2%
9. **Financial health**: Balance sheet only — $500 total assets (all cash), $0 liabilities, $500 members' equity as of Jan 2, 2025; auditor flagged going-concern evaluation language
10. **Special risks flagged**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (CA), spousal liability, short operating history, financial support concerns, mandatory minimum payments, unregistered trademark
11. **Territory**: Not exclusive; Designated Territory generally 5-mile radius or 30,000 population; extensive reserved rights for franchisor
12. **Contract**: 10-year term, one 10-year renewal; post-term noncompete 24 months / 25-mile radius + 10-mile from any other shop
13. **Litigation**: None disclosed
14. **Trademark risk**: Principal trademarks NOT federally registered; opposition proceeding filed Oct 2023 (Dellah Coffee & Brunch House, Serial No. 97553214)

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Not exclusive** — franchisor explicitly states this
- Designated Territory: smaller of 5-mile radius (by road) or 30,000 population area
- Non-traditional locations: territory may be limited to physical boundaries of facility
- No options or rights of first refusal for additional franchises
- **Reserved Rights** (extensive):
  - (a) Operate/grant franchises outside territory regardless of proximity
  - (b) Acquire/merge with competing businesses, operate under different marks even inside territory
  - (c) Grant shops at captive market locations inside territory (malls, airports, stadiums, etc.)
  - (d) Use marks to distribute products through wholesale, supermarkets, e-commerce inside territory
  - (e) All other activities not expressly prohibited
- No restrictions on franchisor soliciting/accepting orders from territory
- Franchisee limited to sales from shop location only; delivery/catering within territory only
- MUDA Development Area varies; no relocation of development area

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Franchise Agreement Relationship Table
- **Term**: 10 years
- **Renewal**: One additional 10-year term; conditions include: compliance, 180-day notice, sign then-current FA (may be materially different), general release, $5,000 fee, remodel/upgrade, maintain lease
- **Termination by franchisee**: Only if franchisor in material breach and fails to cure within 30 days
- **Termination by franchisor**: With cause only
  - **Curable (10 days)**: Failure to pay fees/obligations (if not intentional)
  - **Curable (30 days)**: Failure to lease/develop/open/operate per standards; insurance; operational compliance
  - **Non-curable**: 3+ curable defaults (even if cured); intentional/knowing refusal to comply; health/safety violations; abandonment; material misrepresentation; unauthorized transfer; disclosure of confidential info; dishonest conduct; insolvency/bankruptcy; and many more
- **Post-termination obligations**: Cease operations, de-identify, return materials, transfer data/listings, abide by noncompete
- **Transfer**: Requires prior written consent at franchisor discretion; $10,000 fee; transferee must meet all requirements; general release; training; ROFR
- **Death/disability**: 30 days to appoint approved Operating Manager; 12 months to transfer
- **Noncompete during term**: No competitive business
- **Noncompete post-term**: 24 months; within territory + 25-mile radius + 10-mile radius of any other shop
- **Dispute resolution**: Non-binding mediation then binding arbitration, both in Alameda County, CA
- **Choice of forum**: Alameda County, CA (state or federal)
- **Choice of law**: California law (subject to state addenda)
- **Modification**: Written agreement, except unilateral Ops Manual changes
- **Integration clause**: FA + schedules + exhibits binding; does not disclaim FDD representations

### Multi-Unit Development Agreement
- Term: varies (expires at earlier of final shop opening or deadline for last shop)
- No renewal
- No franchisee termination option
- Franchisor termination with cause: abandonment, 4-month inactivity, insolvency, missed development schedule, termination of any FA
- No transfer rights
- Same dispute resolution as FA (mediation/arbitration in Alameda County, CA)
