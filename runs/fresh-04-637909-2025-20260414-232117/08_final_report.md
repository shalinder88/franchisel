## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: 4 company-owned outlets in California (San Francisco x2, Oakland, Berkeley); 0 franchised outlets open; 2 license agreements signed (Elk Grove CA, Naperville IL) but neither has opened. The franchisor projects 9 new franchised outlets across 6 states in the next fiscal year — an aggressive target for a system with zero franchisee-operated locations.

2. **Entity structure**: Delah Coffee Franchising, LLC is a California LLC formed October 21, 2024. It is a family-operated organization led by Majed Jahamee (CEO) and Omar Jahamee (District Manager/Trainer). The franchisor entity itself has no operating history — company-owned shops are operated through separate affiliates (Delah Coffee Inc. and Delah Imports Inc.). Five affiliates share the same Pleasanton, CA address.

3. **Ownership history**: The first Delah Coffee shop opened in San Francisco in June 2022 under affiliate Delah Coffee Inc. The franchisor was not formed until October 2024, and franchising commenced on the issuance date of this FDD (March 11, 2025). The system is in its earliest possible stage of franchise development.

4. **Investment range**: $282,300–$424,000 for a single-unit franchise; $342,300–$544,000 for a multi-unit development agreement (3–5 shops). The initial franchise fee is $35,000.

5. **Fee burden**: Royalty of 4.5% of Gross Sales weekly; local marketing expenditure of 1% monthly; Brand Development Fund of up to 2% (currently not assessed); Technology Fee of up to $500/month (currently not assessed). Total assessed ongoing fee load is currently 5.5% of Gross Sales. If BDF and Tech Fee are activated, ongoing fees could reach 8%+ of Gross Sales.

6. **Item 19 highlights**: FPR based on 3 company-owned outlets. Gross Sales range $557K–$1.14M. After food, paper, labor, disclosed expenses, and simulated franchise fees, Direct Gross Profit ranges from $114,868 (20.6%) to $252,712 (22.2%). This is NOT net profit — it excludes insurance, G&A, owner compensation, interest, taxes, depreciation, and many other costs.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: Berkeley outlet (smallest, near UC Berkeley) generated $557K in Gross Sales vs. the system leader at $1.14M — a 2:1 spread across locations in the same metro area. Rent burden varies dramatically (6.3% to 11.4% of sales). A location with Berkeley-level sales and Oakland-level rent would produce very thin margins.

8. **Financial health**: The franchisor entity has $500 in total assets, $0 liabilities, and no revenue. This was flagged as a special risk: "The franchisor's financial condition as reflected in its financial statements calls into question the franchisor's financial ability to provide services and support to you." This is among the weakest franchisor balance sheets possible.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Company-owned outlets grew from 0 to 4 over 2022–2024. Projections for 9 new franchised outlets in one year are ambitious given zero franchise operational history and minimal corporate infrastructure.

10. **Territory**: Not exclusive. The Designated Territory is the smaller of a 5-mile radius or 30,000-population area, but franchisor retains extensive reserved rights including captive market locations, alternative distribution channels, and acquisition of competing businesses.

11. **Renewal risk**: One 10-year renewal available. Renewal requires signing "then current" Franchise Agreement which "may contain terms materially different from your current Franchise Agreement." This is a significant risk — the terms of the second decade could be substantially worse.

12. **Disputes**: Mediation and arbitration in Alameda County, California. California governing law. Jury trial waived. Punitive damages waived. No class actions.

13. **Trademark risk**: The principal "Delah Coffee" trademarks are NOT federally registered. An opposition proceeding was filed in October 2023 by "Dellah Coffee & Brunch House." If the opposition succeeds, franchisees may be forced to rebrand — a costly and brand-destructive outcome.

14. **Special risks**: Six risks formally disclosed: out-of-state dispute resolution, spousal liability, short operating history, financial support concerns, mandatory minimum payments, and unregistered trademark.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
The territory is **not exclusive**. The franchisor explicitly states: "You will not receive an exclusive territory."

The Designated Territory is the smaller of:
- 5-mile radius from shop location (by road), or
- Area encompassing 30,000 population

For non-traditional/captive locations, territory may be limited to the facility's physical boundaries.

During the FA term, while franchisee is not in default, franchisor will not establish or grant another Delah Coffee Shop within the Designated Territory — subject to extensive Reserved Rights.

### Reserved Rights (Encroachment Risk)
The franchisor reserves broad rights to:
1. Operate or franchise shops outside territory regardless of proximity
2. Acquire/merge with competing businesses and operate them under different marks even inside territory
3. Grant shops at captive market locations (malls, airports, stadiums, universities, etc.) inside territory
4. Distribute products through wholesale, supermarkets, e-commerce inside territory
5. All other activities not expressly prohibited

### Channel Restrictions
Franchisee is restricted to sales from shop location only:
- On-premises dining
- Personal carryout from shop
- Delivery within Designated Territory only
- Catering within Designated Territory only

Franchisor has no restrictions on soliciting/accepting orders from within franchisee's territory.

- 30 days to appoint approved Operating Manager

### Termination
**Curable defaults (10 days):** Failure to pay fees (if not intentional)
**Curable defaults (30 days):** Failure to develop/operate per standards, insurance lapses, operational compliance

### Noncompete

- **Post-term**: 24 months; within Designated Territory + 25-mile radius + 10-mile radius of any other Delah Coffee Shop

- **Spousal liability**: Owners and spouses must comply

- California governing law

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Overview
The FPR is provided and based solely on company-owned outlets. There are no franchise-operated outlets to report. Four company-owned outlets exist; data is disclosed for three that qualified as "Operational" (in operation before the start of the respective calendar year).

**Excluded**: San Francisco #2 (Sansome) — opened July 2024, classified as "New" for 2024.

### Outlet Performance Summary

| Outlet | Year | Gross Sales | Food % | Labor % | DGP % | DGP After Fees % |
|--------|------|-------------|--------|---------|-------|-------------------|
| SF#1 Fourth St | 2023 | $1,122,970 | 27.33% | 35.21% | 36.29% | 20.57% |
| SF#1 Fourth St | 2024 | $1,140,063 | 26.96% | 33.96% | 37.94% | 22.17% |
| Oakland W. Grand | 2024 | $834,648 | 27.00% | 26.53% | 45.32% | 22.74% |
| Berkeley Euclid | 2024 | $557,339 | 27.00% | 31.22% | 40.67% | 20.61% |

### Key Observations

**Gross Sales spread**: SF#1 ($1.14M) generates more than double Berkeley's revenue ($557K). This 2:1 spread in the same metro area suggests location and brand maturity matter significantly.

**Food cost consistency**: Remarkably stable at 27% across all outlets and years.

**Labor cost variation**: Ranges from 26.5% (Oakland) to 35.2% (SF#1 2023). SF#1 has the highest absolute labor cost but declining as a percentage as sales grow. Oakland's low labor cost (26.5%) may reflect different staffing models or market rates.

**Rent burden variation**: SF#1 pays $71,400/year (6.3% of sales); Oakland pays $95,040/year (11.4% of sales — nearly double the percentage). This is the single largest variable affecting franchisee economics.

**DGP after franchise fee adjustments**: Ranges from 20.6% to 22.7%. This is Direct Gross Profit — NOT net profit. It excludes: insurance, general & administrative expenses, owner compensation, depreciation, amortization, interest, taxes, and many other operating expenses. Actual net profit will be substantially lower.

### Critical Caveats
1. **Company-owned outlets only** — no franchise data exists
2. **Not GAAP, not audited**
3. **Management advantage**: Company outlets benefit from "extensively experienced management team" and local brand recognition
4. **No franchise fees paid**: Royalty, BDF, and tech fees are simulated adjustments, not actual expenses
5. **Population size**: 3 outlets in one metro area (SF Bay Area) — very small sample
6. **Exclusion of SF#2**: The newest outlet (and potentially most representative of a franchise location — bottom floor of office building) is excluded

### Rhode Island

### Washington
