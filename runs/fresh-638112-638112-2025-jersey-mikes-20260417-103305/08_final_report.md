## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and trajectory:** Jersey Mike's ended 2024 with 2,989 total outlets (2,955 franchised, 34 company-owned), making it one of the largest submarine sandwich franchise systems in the United States. The system has added over 300 net franchised outlets per year for three consecutive years (292 in 2022, 286 in 2023, 308 in 2024), with 574 franchise agreements signed but not yet opened and 270 projected new franchised openings in 2025. This represents consistent, accelerating growth.

2. **Entity structure:** The franchisor is A Sub Above, LLC, a Delaware LLC formed October 31, 2019 as part of a securitization transaction. The entity structure is layered: JMFS (the historical franchisor) → JM SPV Guarantor → Jersey Mike's Funding → A Sub Above. JMFS continues to provide all management, marketing, development, and operational services under a Management Agreement. The securitization structure means that franchise cash flows are pledged to service debt obligations.

3. **Ownership history:** In January 2025, Blackstone Inc. acquired a majority ownership position in JMFS through multiple intermediary entities. This represents a significant change in control, bringing institutional private equity management to what had been a founder-led system since 1987. Peter Cancro, who purchased the original Mike's Submarines shop in 1975 at age 17, is no longer listed as an officer in Item 2, though his continued involvement is not explicitly addressed.

4. **Investment range:** Total initial investment ranges from $185,903 to $1,417,592, with $46,500 to $48,500 payable directly to the franchisor. The wide range reflects variation in leasehold improvement costs ($75K–$722K), which depends heavily on the condition of the space, landlord contributions, and whether a freestanding building is involved.

5. **Fee burden:** The ongoing fee burden is substantial at 11.5% of Gross Receipts (6.5% royalty + 1% Corporate Ad Fund + 4% National Media Fund), plus approximately $5,600/year in mandatory technology fees, plus per-transaction online ordering fees (3.74% + $0.29/transaction). This is among the higher fee loads in the QSR sandwich segment.

6. **Item 19 highlights:** Average unit volume of $1,338,874 across 2,255 qualifying restaurants (median $1,285,259). The range spans from $472,525 to $4,131,736. Only 45% of restaurants met or exceeded the average, indicating a right-skewed distribution with a long tail of high performers pulling the average up. No cost build-up or profitability data is disclosed.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** A restaurant producing at the low end ($472,525) would pay approximately $54,440 in royalty and ad fund fees (11.5%), plus ~$5,600 in tech fees, for a total fee load of ~$60,000 before any operating costs. Given industry COGS of 28–32% and labor of 25–30%, a sub-$500K unit would face severe margin pressure.

8. **Financial health:** A Sub Above, LLC reported $279.9M in total revenue and $238.6M in net income for 2024 — an extraordinary 85% net margin, reflecting the asset-light securitization structure. However, the parent entity JMFS and Affiliates reported $1.7B in long-term securitization debt against a stockholder's deficit of ($857.9M). Net income at the consolidated level was only $5.0M on $652.8M in revenue. The system's cash flows are heavily leveraged.

9. **Growth trajectory:** Net growth is strong and accelerating. Zero terminations and zero non-renewals across three years is an exceptionally clean record. Ceased operations declined from 9 (2022) to 5 (2024). Transfers declined from 105 to 79 over the same period, possibly reflecting higher franchisee satisfaction or reduced turnover.

10. **Territory:** The Designated Area is modest (0.5–1 mile radius) and does not provide exclusive rights. The Company retains broad carve-outs for non-traditional venues and internet/catalog/direct marketing channels. The Tropical Smoothie Cafe affiliate (1,514 units) sells sandwiches, creating potential competitive overlap.

11. **Renewal risk:** Only one 10-year renewal term. Renewal requires signing the then-current franchise agreement, which may contain materially different terms, and executing a general release. This is a significant risk factor for long-term planning.

12. **Disputes:** Binding arbitration in Monmouth County, New Jersey — a significant burden for franchisees located far from the Northeast. Eight states have addenda that modify dispute resolution and other provisions.

13. **Special risks:** The FDD's cover page highlights three state-required risks: out-of-state dispute resolution in NJ, spousal guarantee liability, and supplier control. All three are material.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

A Sub Above, LLC is a special-purpose entity created for securitization. It owns the franchise agreements, trademarks, and IP. JMFS manages the day-to-day operations through the Management Agreement. The securitization structure means that franchise royalty revenues flow through Jersey Mike's Funding, LLC (the "Master Issuer") to service the securitized debt before reaching equity holders.

The Blackstone acquisition in January 2025 brought institutional ownership but also potential for changes in system economics, as private equity sponsors typically optimize cash flow extraction. Five of the 16 listed officers joined in 2025, and the CEO (Charlie Morrison) was formerly CEO of Wingstop, suggesting a growth-and-efficiency orientation.

### Fee Stack

The ongoing fee burden is one of the highest in the QSR sandwich segment:

**Percentage-based fees (on Gross Receipts):**
- Continuing Royalty: 6.5% (weekly via ACH)
- Corporate Advertising and Development Fund: 1.0% (may increase to 6%)
- National Media Fund: 4.0% (may increase to 6%)
- Cooperative Advertising Fund: 0% currently (may increase to 6%)
- Total cap: 6% across all three advertising funds
- **Current effective total: 11.5% of Gross Receipts**

The advertising fund structure is unusual in that the Company may reallocate amounts between the three funds at its sole discretion, and the aggregate cap of 6% means the Company could shift the entire advertising burden to a single fund without franchisee consent. In 2024, the Corporate Ad Fund spent 66.3% on production and 27.6% on administration — a relatively high administrative share. The National Media Fund spent 96.3% on media placement, which is strong.

**Monthly technology fees (fixed):**
- POS Software License & Support: $395/month (can increase to $795)
- Secure Network Fee: $31.85/month (can increase to $39.85)
- Back-Up Cellular Service: $30/month
- Gift Card Program: $9.50/month
- Total minimum: ~$466/month (~$5,600/year)
- Teamworx (optional labor management): $53.50/month

**Per-transaction fees:**
- Online Ordering: $0.2921/transaction + 3.74% of sale
- Third-Party Delivery: $6–$9 per delivery
- Text Messages: $0.025/text (franchisee-initiated)

**Event-driven fees:**
- Transfer: $15,000 + $5,000 grand opening ad
- Replacement Training: up to $2,500/trainee
- Relocation: $5,000 + costs
- General Contractor/Architect Approval: $5,000 each
- Liquidated Damages: avg monthly (royalty + ad) × 12 months × lesser of 36 or remaining months
- Late Fees: 1.5%/month interest + 5% or $25 penalty

At the median unit volume of $1,285,259, the annual fee burden breaks down approximately as:
- Royalty (6.5%): ~$83,542
- Ad funds (5.0%): ~$64,263
- Tech fees: ~$5,600
- Online ordering (estimated at 30% of sales, conservative): ~$14,400
- **Total estimated annual fees: ~$167,800 (13.1% of gross receipts)**

### Initial Investment

The total initial investment ranges from $185,903 to $1,417,592. The largest variable component is leasehold improvements ($75,172–$722,422), reflecting the wide range between turnkey locations with landlord TI allowances and ground-up buildouts. A typical Jersey Mike's operates from 1,000–2,000 sq ft in a retail shopping center.

Key investment components at high end: Leasehold improvements ($722K), equipment/furniture ($208K), training ($68K), uniforms/office/security ($66K), rent ($72K). The low end assumes free rent periods, no security deposits, and minimal buildout.

Typical time from franchise agreement signing to opening: ~9 months. From area development agreement signing: ~12 months. These timelines are subject to real estate availability, permitting, contractor schedules, and training completion.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting

### Supplier Restrictions

Jersey Mike's operates one of the most restrictive supplier control environments in franchising. Approximately 85% of ongoing purchases must be made from designated or approved suppliers, and approximately 35% of establishment costs similarly flow through controlled channels.

Mandatory purchases include: all proprietary meats and cheeses, bread, paper products with JM branding, uniforms, Pepsi beverages, and Frito Lay snacks. Distribution is primarily through Sysco Corporation. Franchisees must use Company-approved real estate brokers, general contractors, architects, and credit card processors.

The affiliate revenue from franchisee purchases is substantial:
- FoodCo (JM '75, LLC): $138.3M from suppliers based on restaurant purchases in 2024
- Company: $16.9M from credit card authorization vendor
- Company: $14.8M from POS/software licensing fees (5.3% of Company revenue)
- Jersey Shore Construction LLC: $2.3M from franchisee graphics purchases
- JMFS: $465K from furniture/equipment vendor (8%–49% of purchase price markups)
- Company: $462K from HR software vendor
- JMFS: $50K from bookkeeping services

The scale of affiliate-captured supply chain revenue ($138M+ through FoodCo alone) is material and should be understood by prospective franchisees. FoodCo receives rebates and commissions ranging from $0.50–$6.00/case, $0.07–$0.30/pound, $5.00–$13.90/gallon, and 10%–26% of purchase prices.

### Training Program

The training program is comprehensive, totaling approximately 400 hours across three phases plus a mandatory grand opening participation:

- **Phase 1** (180 hours, 8 weeks): On-the-job training at a Certified Training Store. Covers all core operations — food handling, bread baking, slicing, wrapping, cooking, cashier operations, inventory, and scheduling.
- **Phase 2** (5 days in NJ or 4 days in-market): Classroom training covering culture, coaching, leadership, P&L management, food cost, hiring, scheduling, and safety. First-time franchisees must attend in Point Pleasant, NJ.
- **Phase 3** (180 hours, 8 weeks): Advanced on-the-job training focusing on speed operations, profitability, and acting as store manager.
- **Grand Opening Participation** (39–54 hours, 5 days): Hands-on at another restaurant's grand opening.

Training is overseen by John Hughes (40+ years experience), Josh Funderburk (26+ years), Chad Tirpack (15+ years), and Brian Loughran (19+ years). Company does not charge for initial training but franchisees bear all travel, lodging, and employee salary costs, which can reach $68,000.

Replacement training costs $2,500 per trainee and must be paid in advance. Must maintain at least three trained employees at all times (one with all three phases, one with Phases 1 and 3, one with Phase 1).

### Technology Stack

The mandatory technology stack includes a PC-based POS system from ReSource Point of Sale, LLC (~$7,500/register), proprietary software licensing, and connection to Company's private redundant network. Company has full independent access to franchisee sales data including gross totals, employee hours, inventory, and product-level sales detail.

POS upgrades are capped at $50,000 during the franchise term. Monthly tech fees total approximately $466/month minimum. Company may increase POS software fees from $395 to $795/month within 12 months — a potential doubling.

### Marketing Fund Spending

In 2024, the Corporate Advertising and Development Fund (1% of Gross Receipts) spent: production (66.3%), administration (27.6%), meetings (<1%), PR (2.6%), media placement (2.8%). The National Media Fund (4% of Gross Receipts) spent: media placement (96.3%), production (3.7%), PR (<0.1%), administration (<0.1%).

The high administrative share (27.6%) in the Corporate Ad Fund and low media placement share (2.8%) warrant franchisee scrutiny. However, the National Media Fund allocates 96.3% to media placement, which is strong.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

The territory protection in Jersey Mike's is limited. The Designated Area under the Franchise Agreement is typically a 0.5–1 mile radius from the restaurant's front door, with smaller areas in urban, resort, or defined facility locations. The FDD states twice: "You will not receive an exclusive territory."

The Company retains broad carve-outs. Non-Traditional Venues (airports, stadiums, hospitals, military bases, casinos, amusement parks, convention centers, etc.) within the Designated Area may be enfranchised to others or operated by Company. The Company may use all other channels of distribution (internet, catalog, telemarketing, direct marketing) within the franchisee's territory using Company's marks, with no obligation to compensate the franchisee.

The affiliate overlap with Tropical Smoothie Cafe (1,514 units) creates potential cannibalization. TSC sells sandwiches (though it is primarily a smoothie brand), and the FDD acknowledges "some overlap with the products and services offered." The Company has no obligation to resolve conflicts between the JM and TSC systems regarding territory, customers, or support.

Continuation of territorial rights is not dependent on sales volume or market penetration. Franchisees may not solicit outside their Designated Area but may accept unsolicited orders from outside it. No right to acquire additional locations without a separate Area Development Agreement.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The initial franchise term is 10 years from the restaurant's opening date. Franchisees may renew for one additional 10-year term, subject to significant conditions: compliance with all FA provisions, bringing the restaurant to then-current System Standards, satisfying all monetary obligations, completing current training requirements, signing the then-current franchise agreement (which may contain materially different terms), and executing a general release (which also may contain materially different terms). The "materially different terms" risk at renewal is explicitly stated and should be weighted heavily in any investment analysis.

Franchisees must give 6–12 months advance notice of intent to renew. Company must give only 1 month notice of intent not to renew, with specific reasons.

### Transfer Provisions

Transfer requires the Company's prior written approval, exercised in its "sole and absolute discretion." The transfer fee is $15,000 per restaurant plus a $5,000 grand opening advertising fee. Conditions include: full compliance with all existing agreements, transferee signing the then-current FA and meeting qualifying criteria, upgrade to current System Standards, transferee training completion, general releases from both parties, 90 days written notice, and the sale price not being "excessive" in the Company's sole determination.

Company has a 15 business-day right of first refusal to purchase on the same terms as any bona fide third-party offer (or for cash). Upon termination or expiration, Company may purchase equipment, supplies, inventory, and branded items at the lesser of cost or fair market value within 30 days.

### Termination and Death/Disability

The franchise agreement provides 16 non-curable default triggers, including: abandonment for 2 days in 12 months, understatement of royalty fees by more than 3% for weekly periods more than twice during the term, failure to submit reports on 2 occasions in 12 months, 3+ separate non-compliance instances in 12 months, and cross-default with any other agreement between the franchisee and Company/affiliates. The cross-default provision is particularly broad — a default under the ADA or Coach Rod Smith financing could trigger FA termination.

Post-termination, the franchisee must pay liquidated damages calculated as: (average monthly royalty + ad fees for the preceding 12 months) × (the lesser of 36 months or the number of months remaining in the term). This is in addition to any other damages, creating potentially severe financial exposure.

Death or disability of a 50%+ owner triggers a provision where heirs/representatives may apply to continue operations or transfer. If they fail to meet conditions, the FA terminates and Company has a purchase option.

### Noncompete Restrictions

During the term: no ownership, engagement, consulting, or interest in any Competitive Business anywhere. "Competitive Business" means any restaurant or food service facility deriving 25% or more of revenue from sandwiches (broadly defined to include submarine, hoagie, hero, deli-style, panini, steamed, wrapped or rolled sandwiches on any bread type), or any business granting franchises for such restaurants. Excludes hot dogs, hamburgers, and fried chicken sandwiches.

Post-term: 2 years within the MSA in which the restaurant is located, or within a 10-mile radius of the restaurant or any other System business. The MSA-based scope can be geographically vast.

### Dispute Resolution

All disputes must first be mediated, then submitted to binding arbitration, both in Monmouth County, New Jersey. New Jersey law governs (except trademark disputes under the Lanham Act). State addenda modify these provisions for 8 states.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### Multi-Year Trajectory

The system has grown from 2,091 total outlets in January 2022 to 2,989 at year-end 2024 — an increase of 898 outlets (43%) in three years. This is exceptionally strong and consistent growth.

| Year | Franchised Start | Opened | Terminated | Non-Renewed | Ceased Ops | Franchised End | Net |
|------|-----------------|--------|------------|-------------|------------|----------------|-----|
| 2022 | 2,069 | 301 | 0 | 0 | 9 | 2,361 | +292 |
| 2023 | 2,361 | 296 | 0 | 0 | 10 | 2,647 | +286 |
| 2024 | 2,647 | 318 | 0 | 0 | 5 | 2,955 | +308 |

Zero terminations and zero non-renewals across all three years is an extremely rare and positive signal. It suggests either very high franchisee satisfaction, very selective franchisee screening, or both. The "ceased operations" category (9, 10, 5) is minimal relative to system size (<0.2% annually).

In 2024, 5 units were reacquired by the franchisor (up from 0 in prior years), primarily in California (2), Connecticut (1), Indiana (1), Texas (1), and Washington (1). This is a new development worth monitoring but is not alarming at this scale.

### Transfer Trends

Transfers declined from 105 (2022) to 96 (2023) to 79 (2024). The largest transfer markets in 2024 were California (20), Texas (12), Utah (10), and Wisconsin (5). The declining transfer volume may reflect either increasing franchisee retention or reduced market liquidity.

### Projected Openings

574 franchise agreements are signed but not yet opened. 270 new franchised outlets and 7 company-owned outlets are projected to open in 2025. The largest projected markets: California (25), Florida (22), Texas (20), New York (15), North Carolina (15), Pennsylvania (11), Utah (11), Ohio (11).

### Confidentiality Clauses

The FDD discloses that while the Company itself has not signed confidentiality clauses restricting franchisee speech during the last three fiscal years, JMFS (the parent/manager) has done so. This is a gag clause flag. The distinction between Company and JMFS is formal — JMFS manages all franchise operations — so the practical effect may be that some franchisees are restricted from speaking openly about their experience.

### Franchisee Advisory

The informal National Franchisee Advisory Council (NFAC) has 5 elected and 2 appointed members. It serves in an advisory capacity only with no veto power. Company manages the election process.
