## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** 2,989 total outlets at year-end 2024 (2,955 franchised + 34 company-owned). Net +314 system-wide in 2024. 574 franchise agreements signed but not yet opened; 270 projected new franchised openings in 2025.
2. **Entity structure:** A Sub Above, LLC is a Delaware LLC formed October 31, 2019, indirect wholly-owned subsidiary of Jersey Mike's Franchise Systems, LLC (JMFS). Securitization structure: JMFS → JM SPV Guarantor → Jersey Mike's Funding → A Sub Above.
3. **Ownership change:** Blackstone Inc. acquired majority ownership of JMFS on January 16, 2025, through multiple intermediary entities. 7 company-owned outlets reclassified as franchised post-acquisition.
4. **Affiliate brands:** Servpro, Tropical Smoothie Cafe, 7 Brew Coffee — all Blackstone portfolio companies, now affiliates.
5. **Investment range:** $185,903 to $1,417,592 total initial investment. Development fee $10,000 + initial franchise fee $20,000 + grand opening advertising $10,000 = $40,000–$48,500 to franchisor.
6. **Fee burden:** 6.5% royalty + 1% Corporate Ad Fund + 4% National Media Fund = 11.5% of Gross Receipts ongoing, plus technology fees ($395–$795/month POS + $31.85/month network + $30/month cellular + $9.50/month gift card + online ordering per-transaction fees).
7. **Item 19:** Average unit volume $1,338,874 for 2,255 Traditional Franchised Restaurants (median $1,285,259; high $4,131,736; low $472,525). 45% met or exceeded average.
8. **Bottom-quartile risk:** Low end of $472,525 on an 11.5% fee burden means ~$54k in royalty/ad fees alone, before tech fees, COGS, labor, and rent.
9. **Financial health (A Sub Above):** Total revenue $279.9M (2024), net income $238.6M. Total assets $36.0M, member equity $22.5M. Distributions of $241.6M in 2024 — essentially all net income flows out.
10. **Financial health (JMFS consolidated):** Total revenue $652.8M, net income $5.0M. Total assets $1.05B, stockholder's deficit ($857.9M). Long-term debt $1.7B (securitization). Heavy leverage.
11. **Growth trajectory:** Consistent +280–310 net franchised openings per year (2022–2024). Zero terminations across three years. Strong positive signal.
12. **Territory:** Designated Area typically 0.5–1 mile radius. Non-exclusive for non-traditional venues. No exclusive territory under FA.
13. **Renewal:** One 10-year renewal term. Must sign then-current form (may be materially different). General release required.
14. **Disputes:** Mediation then binding arbitration in Monmouth County, NJ. New Jersey law governs (state addenda override for 8 states).
15. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution in NJ; spousal guarantee liability; supplier control (nearly all purchases from designated/approved suppliers at set prices).

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Supplier Control
Highly restrictive. Must purchase all private/proprietary items (meats, cheeses, bread, paper products, uniforms) from designated vendors, primarily distributed through Sysco. Must use Pepsi beverages and Frito Lay snacks. Must use Company-approved real estate brokers and general contractors.

Revenue derived by affiliates from franchisee purchases:
- Company: $16.9M from credit card authorization vendor (2024)
- Company: $14.8M from POS/software licensing fees (5.3% of Company revenue)
- JMFS: $464,827 from furniture/equipment vendor (8%–49% of purchase price)
- FoodCo: $138.3M from suppliers based on restaurant purchases
- Jersey Shore Construction: $2.3M from franchisee graphics purchases
- Company: $462,320 from HR software vendor ($40/month/restaurant)
- JMFS: $50,400 from bookkeeping services

Purchases from approved suppliers represent ~35% of establishment costs and ~85% of ongoing purchases. No purchasing cooperatives.

### Item 9 — Franchisee's Obligations
Standard obligation table cross-referencing FA and ADA sections. Key obligations cover site selection, pre-opening purchases, training, fees, compliance with standards, trademark usage, product restrictions, insurance, advertising, indemnification, owner participation, records/reports, inspections, transfer, renewal, post-termination, non-competition, dispute resolution, licenses, and guaranty.

### Item 10 — Financing
Company generally does not offer direct financing. Listed on SBA Franchise Registry for expedited processing.

**Coach Rod Smith Program:** JMFS finances qualifying existing managers for development costs:
- Up to 100% of development costs (may require 20% contribution)
- May include initial franchise fee
- 5-year repayment term; amortized over 5 or 7 years (balloon at 5 years if 7-year amortization)
- Fixed interest rate: 6%–10.5%
- Personal guaranty required (including spouse)
- Security interest in restaurant assets
- JMFS may sell/assign the note to a third party
- Tenant improvement allowances and rent abatement must be applied to principal
- JMFS may require up to 50% of EBITDA as additional payments
- Default: entire balance accelerated; cross-default with FA

### Item 11 — Training and Assistance
**Pre-opening:**
- Phase 1: 180 hours on-the-job training at Certified Training Store (8 weeks minimum). Covers board of health, food handling, bread baking, slicing, wrapping, cooking, cashier, inventory, scheduling.
- Phase 2 (New Jersey): 5 days, 29.5 classroom + 8.5 OJT hours at Point Pleasant, NJ training center. Covers culture, coaching, leadership, employee engagement, P&L, food cost, hiring, scheduling, safety.
- Phase 2 (In-Market): 4 days, 24 classroom + 6.25 OJT hours. Available for existing franchisees opening additional restaurants.
- Phase 3: 180 hours on-the-job training at Certified Training Store. Covers speed operations, profitability, store management.
- Grand Opening Participation: 39–54 hours over 5 days at another restaurant.
- Total: ~360 hours OJT + ~30–38 hours classroom.

Training overseen by John Hughes (40+ years experience), Josh Funderburk (26+ years), Chad Tirpack (15+ years), Brian Loughran (19+ years).

First-time franchisees: 3 trainees complete Phase 1, 1 completes Phase 2 NJ, 2 complete Phase 3. Additional restaurants: 3 complete Phase 1, 1 completes Phase 2, 2 complete Phase 3.

**Post-opening:**
- 3 days (24 hours) on-site assistance during first month at Company's cost
- Additional assistance: $75/hour + expenses
- Continuing education: max 2x/year, 4 days total, at franchisee's expense
- Replacement training: $2,500 per trainee

**Advertising funds:**
- Corporate Ad Fund (2024): 66.3% production, 27.6% administration, <1% meetings, 2.6% PR, 2.8% media placement
- National Media Fund (2024): 96.3% media placement, 3.7% production, <0.1% PR, <0.1% administration

- POS license: $2,000/register

### Item 12 — Territory
**ADA:** Designated Territory defined by zip codes, county boundaries, highways, etc. Exclusive during ADA term IF franchisee is in compliance, EXCEPT for Non-Traditional Venues (airports, stadiums, hospitals, military bases, casinos, etc.).

**FA:** Designated Area typically 0.5–1 mile radius from restaurant front door (smaller for urban/resort areas). Protection from new JM restaurants within DA, except Non-Traditional Venues. Not exclusive — competition from other franchisees, company outlets, and other channels possible.

No right to additional locations without separate ADA. No solicitation outside DA (may accept unsolicited orders). No internet/catalog/direct marketing rights (reserved to Company). Company not required to compensate for solicitations in franchisee's DA. Continuation not dependent on sales volume.

Tropical Smoothie Cafe operates as affiliate and may compete in territory with limited sandwich overlap.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
Company owns all marks including JERSEY MIKE'S (registered 1996), MIKE'S WAY, A SUB ABOVE, SHORE POINTS, and 17+ registered marks. All renewals filed. No material trademark disputes.

### Item 14 — Patents and Proprietary Information
No patents. Common law copyright claimed for Manual, training materials, agreements. Strict confidentiality obligations — must sign NDAs for all employees with access.

### Item 15 — Owner Participation
Must be under direct on-premises supervision of Controlling Principal (10%+ equity owner) or approved full-time manager/shift supervisor who completed training. Full-time devotion required.

### Item 16 — Product Restrictions
Must sell only authorized sandwiches and menu items. Prohibited from selling unauthorized products. Company may add products, require remodeling (capped at $150,000 during FA term). Company may set minimum and maximum prices. Prohibited from non-authorized equipment, vending machines, newspaper racks, etc.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

Territory is limited. The Designated Area (0.5–1 mile radius) provides modest protection, but Company retains broad rights: non-traditional venues, internet/catalog sales, other channels of distribution, and the right to acquire competing franchise systems. No exclusive territory statement appears twice in both ADA and FA sections.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- Initial term: 10 years from opening date
- Renewal: 1 additional 10-year term
- Renewal conditions: compliance with all FA provisions; bring restaurant to current System Standards; 6–12 months advance notice; satisfy all monetary obligations; sign then-current FA (may contain materially different terms); complete current training; sign general release (may contain materially different terms)
- Company gives 1 month notice of intent not to renew

### Termination
**By franchisee:** If Company materially breaches and fails to cure within 30 days of written notice.

**By Company with cause (curable defaults):**
- Failure to pay within 10 days
- Material misuse of Marks without cure in 7 days
- Failure to comply with mandatory specifications without cure in 30 days

**By Company (non-curable defaults):**
- Failure to complete training or decorate premises
- Material misrepresentation in application
- Felony conviction or plea of no contest
- Unauthorized disclosure of Manual information
- Abandonment for 2 days in 12 months
- Surrender of control
- Understatement of royalty fees by 3%+ for weekly periods more than twice during term
- Failure to submit reports on 2+ occasions in 12 months
- Insolvency or bankruptcy
- Acts materially impairing Mark goodwill
- Continued health/safety/sanitation violations
- Failure to pay taxes
- Failure to open by deadline
- 3+ separate non-compliance instances in 12 months
- 2+ instances of same non-compliance in 12 months
- Cross-default: termination of another agreement with Company/affiliate

### Post-termination obligations
- Stop operations; assign lease to Company at request
- De-identify premises; pay all sums owed
- Pay liquidated damages (formula: avg monthly royalty+ad fees × lesser of 36 or remaining months)
- Company option to buy inventory/equipment at lesser of cost or FMV (appraiser if disagreement; costs shared equally)
- Comply with non-compete; cease all Online Presence
- Return all manuals and confidential information

### Transfer
- Requires Company's prior written approval (sole and absolute discretion)
- Transfer fee: $15,000 per restaurant + $5,000 grand opening advertising
- Conditions: compliance with all agreements; transferee signs then-current FA; meets qualifying criteria; upgrades to current standards; completes training; general release by both parties
- Company has 15 business days right of first refusal
- Sale price cannot be excessive (Company's sole determination)
- 90 days written notice required

### Death/Disability
Heirs/representatives may apply to continue or transfer within conditions. If not met, FA terminates and Company has purchase option.

### Non-compete
**During term:** No Competitive Business (25%+ revenue from sandwiches) anywhere.
**Post-term:** 2 years within MSA or 10-mile radius of restaurant or any System business.

### Dispute Resolution
- Mediation then binding arbitration in Monmouth County, NJ
- NJ law governs (state addenda override for specific states)
- FA has waiver of punitive damages; limitation of claims provisions (state overrides apply)
