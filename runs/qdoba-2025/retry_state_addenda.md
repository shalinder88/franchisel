# Retry: State-Specific Addenda Recovery — Qdoba (Exhibit H)

**Source pages:** 335–400+
**States with addenda:** California, Hawaii, Illinois, Michigan, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Virginia, Washington

Each state has a Disclosure Addendum plus separate Agreement Amendments for the Franchise Agreement, License Agreement, and Development Agreement.

---

## California (pages 335–339)

**Material overrides:**
- **Interest rate cap:** Maximum interest on late payments is 10% annually (vs 18% in base FDD).
- **Noncompete may be unenforceable:** The post-term covenant not to compete "may not be enforceable under California law."
- **Bankruptcy termination may be unenforceable:** Franchise/Development Agreement provision for termination upon bankruptcy "may not be enforceable under federal bankruptcy law."
- **General release limitation:** Cal. Corp. Code § 31512 voids waiver of rights under the Franchise Investment Law. Bus. & Prof. Code § 20010 voids waiver of rights under the Franchise Relations Act. General releases must exclude claims under these statutes.
- **Anti-fraud protection:** No disclaimer or statement signed by franchisee shall be construed as waiving any claim of fraud in the inducement, whether common law or statutory. This supersedes any inconsistent term.
- **Item 19 warning added:** Performance representations do not reflect costs of sales, operating expenses, or other costs that must be deducted to obtain net income or profit.
- **California franchise law controls** if inconsistent with the Franchise Agreement.

## Hawaii (pages 340–347)

**Material overrides:**
- **Fair market value compensation on termination:** Company must compensate franchisee for fair market value of inventory, supplies, equipment, and furnishings purchased from Company or designated suppliers. If termination is for purpose of converting to company-owned, Company must also compensate for loss of goodwill. Company may offset amounts owed.
- **General release limitation:** Release requirement excludes claims under the Hawaii Franchise Investment Law.
- **Anti-fraud protection:** Same as California — no waiver of fraud-in-the-inducement claims.
- **Registration states listed:** California, Hawaii, Illinois, Indiana, Kentucky, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, South Dakota, Texas, Virginia, Washington, and Wisconsin. No states have refused, revoked, or suspended the right to offer.

## Illinois (pages 348–362)

**Material overrides:**
- **Forum selection voided:** Any provision designating jurisdiction or venue outside Illinois is void under Section 4 of the Illinois Franchise Disclosure Act. However, the agreement may provide for arbitration outside Illinois. This directly overrides the San Diego, California forum clause for Illinois franchisees.
- **Illinois law applies** if jurisdiction requirements of the Illinois Franchise Disclosure Act are met (overrides California choice of law).
- **Termination provisions:** If inconsistent with Section 19 of the Illinois Act, Illinois law controls.
- **Anti-waiver:** Section 41 of the Illinois Act voids any condition purporting to waive compliance with any provision of the Act.
- Same amendments apply to Franchise Agreement, License Agreement, and Development Agreement.

## Michigan (pages 364–366)

**Material overrides:**
- **Noncompete limitation:** The post-term noncompete is "enforceable only during a reasonable time and within a reasonable area." If the noncompete is found unreasonable, a court may revise it to make it reasonable.
- **Filing does not constitute approval** by the state.

## Minnesota (pages 367–382)

**Material overrides (most extensive):**
- **Noncompete limited:** Minnesota Statutes, Section 80C.21 limits the duration and scope of noncompete covenants. The post-term noncompete is limited to 2 years after termination/expiration within the area of the franchise.
- **Termination good cause required:** Minn. Stat. § 80C.14 requires good cause for termination and provides 90 days' notice with 60 days to cure for most defaults.
- **General release limitation:** Minnesota law prohibits franchisor from requiring franchisee to waive rights under Minn. Stat. Ch. 80C. Any general release will not apply to claims under Minnesota franchise law.
- **Forum selection:** The franchise agreement's requirement to litigate in San Diego, California "shall not in any way abrogate or reduce any rights of the franchisee as provided for in Minnesota Statutes, Chapter 80C."
- **Governing law:** Minnesota law applies to claims arising under the Minnesota Franchise Act.
- **Injunctive relief:** Minn. Stat. § 80C.21 permits franchisee to submit matters to court jurisdiction in Minnesota.
- **Civil remedy statute of limitations:** 3 years from act or omission, or 1 year after reasonable discovery (Minn. Stat. § 80C.17, subd. 5).
- Same amendments apply to all three agreements.

## New York (pages 375–382)

**Material overrides:**
- **Termination provisions subject to NY GBL Article 33:** Franchisor may not terminate or refuse to renew without providing written notice stating all reasons at least 90 days prior to effective date.
- **Cure right:** Franchisee has 60 days to cure upon notice of default.
- **Noncompete limitation:** The post-term noncompete is enforceable only if the noncompete is reasonable in scope.
- **Anti-waiver:** Any provisions in the Franchise Agreement inconsistent with NY GBL Article 33 are superseded by the Article.
- **Forum selection:** Pursuant to NY GBL § 687.4 and § 687.5, any lawsuit must be permitted in New York.

## North Dakota (pages 383–389)

**Material overrides:**
- **Forum selection voided:** Section 51-19-09 voids any requirement to litigate outside North Dakota.
- **Governing law:** North Dakota law applies notwithstanding California choice of law.
- **Noncompete limitation:** Post-term noncompete covenants are generally unenforceable in North Dakota.
- **Arbitration:** Arbitration must take place at a site mutually agreed to.
- **General release limitation:** Franchisor may not require a general release as condition of renewal or transfer if it would release claims under North Dakota franchise law.
- **Waiver prohibition:** Any waiver of compliance with North Dakota franchise law is void.

## Rhode Island (pages 390–393)

**Material overrides:**
- **Forum selection:** Section 19-28.1-14 prohibits restriction of jurisdiction to a forum outside Rhode Island.
- **Governing law:** Rhode Island law applies to claims under the Rhode Island Franchise Investment Act.
- **General release limitation:** Release does not apply to claims under Rhode Island franchise law.

## Virginia (page 394)

**Material overrides:**
- **Registration statement:** Filed pursuant to Virginia's Retail Franchising Act.
- **Anti-fraud:** The franchise, as offered, meets the requirements of the Virginia Retail Franchising Act (Section 13.1-557 et seq.).

## Washington (pages 395–400+)

**Material overrides:**
- **Forum selection limited:** Washington franchisees may bring suit in Washington state courts for violations of Washington franchise law.
- **General release limitation:** Release of claims not permitted if it would release claims under Washington's Franchise Investment Protection Act (RCW 19.100).
- **Termination subject to Washington law** (RCW 19.100.180).
- **Registration statement** filed with Washington Department of Financial Institutions.
- **Material change:** If there is a material change after registration but before closing, a revised FDD must be provided.

## Summary of Material State-Level Overrides

| Override | States Affected |
|---|---|
| Forum selection voided/limited | IL, MN, ND, RI, WA, NY |
| Choice of law overridden | IL, MN, ND, RI |
| Post-term noncompete limited/unenforceable | CA, MI, MN, NY, ND |
| General release excludes state franchise law claims | CA, HI, MN, NY, ND, RI, WA |
| Anti-fraud/anti-waiver protections | CA, HI, IL, MN, NY, ND |
| Fair market value compensation on termination | HI |
| Termination good cause / 90-day notice / 60-day cure | MN, NY |
| Interest rate cap | CA (10% vs base 18%) |

**Key takeaway:** For franchisees in Illinois, Minnesota, North Dakota, and New York, the California forum/law provisions in the base franchise agreement are materially overridden. The post-term noncompete is weakened or unenforceable in California, Michigan, Minnesota, North Dakota, and potentially New York. Minnesota and New York impose the strongest additional protections (good cause termination, extended cure periods, mandatory notice). Hawaii uniquely requires fair market value compensation including goodwill on termination-for-conversion.
