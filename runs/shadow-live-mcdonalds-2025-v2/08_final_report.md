## A. Executive snapshot

1. McDonald's USA, LLC is the U.S. franchisor, a Delaware LLC wholly owned by McDonald's Corporation (the parent and predecessor). Headquartered at 110 N. Carpenter Street, Chicago, IL. The system comprises 13,559 U.S. outlets at year-end 2024 — 12,887 franchised (95.0%) and 672 McOpCo (company-owned).
2. Net 2024 U.S. growth was +102 outlets, reversing three years of near-flat net change (+19 in 2022, +2 in 2023). Franchised outlets grew +115 while McOpCo shrank by 13, continuing the refranchising drift.
3. The initial investment range for a traditional restaurant is $1,471,000 to $2,728,000, including a $45,000 initial franchise fee. STO/STR formats are $1.01M–$1.79M at a $22,500 initial fee. Satellites (including Walmart MIW) are $525K–$1.19M with $0–$500 initial fee. Nine of 31 McOpCo sales in 2024 exceeded the traditional high-end range, one by more than $2 million.
4. Royalty bifurcation is the most consequential 2024 change: 5% of Gross Sales for new restaurants, McOpCo acquisitions, and franchisor ROFR resales; 4% of Gross Sales for legacy family transfers, new-term-on-existing, inter-franchisee resales, and rebuilds.
5. Advertising obligation is ≥4% of Gross Sales, not paid to franchisor. OPNAD national co-op pledges 2.25% on top of local co-op contributions. Franchisor maintains no ad fund and has no obligation to spend anything on advertising.
6. Rent is the real economic lever: Monthly Base Rent (finance-factor against franchisor investment in land/building) + Pass Thru Rent (lease escalations) + Percentage Rent (generally 6%–23% of Gross Sales, with a floor of 11.50% from year 8). The Item 19 pro forma disclaims rent entirely — so headline "operating income before occupancy costs" of $734K–$879K is gross of this burden, not net of it.
7. Term is 20 years for traditional, 10 years for STO/STR, 3 years for BFL (with a 20-year conditional extension via option), varies for Satellite. **There is no contractual right to renew or extend.** Any successor franchise is governed by the "New Term Policy" (Exhibit K), which is explicitly not a contract right and is subject to franchisor discretion. This is the single largest structural kill switch in the McDonald's system.
8. Territory: no exclusive, protected, or reserved territory. Franchisor reserves rights to place new outlets (franchised or company-owned), sell McCafe retail, run e-commerce, and operate alternate brands. Encroachment is contractually permitted.
9. Supplier control: required purchases (from franchisor or approved suppliers) represent approximately 90%–95% of initial purchases and 55%–65% of ongoing purchases. Sesame POS, Cashless 3.0, and the P2W-administered Gift Card System are effectively mandatory. Franchisor holds uncapped independent access to transaction-level POS data.
10. In 2024, the franchisor earned $39,008,767 in loan guarantee fees, cashless incentives, and beverage supplier rebates — an entire second revenue channel on top of royalties and rent. Separately, real estate services and revenue from franchisees represented 68% of McDonald's USA, LLC total revenue ($7.21B of $10.63B).
11. Item 19 reports system-wide 2024 average annual sales of $4,002,000 across 12,572 traditional restaurants open at least one year; median $3,838,000. Franchised-only subset: avg $3,966,000, median $3,797,000, high $19,680,000, low $914,000. McOpCo restaurants outperform at $4.79M average and $4.61M median. 78% of franchised restaurants exceeded $3M; 64% exceeded $3.4M.
12. The Item 19 pro forma uses 11,332 independent franchisee traditional restaurants (excluding McOpCo, Satellites, and 2024 change-of-owner restaurants). At $3.0M sales, pro forma OIBO is $734K (76% of restaurants above); at $3.2M it is $812K (69% above); at $3.4M it is $879K (62% above). These figures explicitly exclude rent, royalty, D&A, interest, and income taxes. The royalty rate in the covered period was 4%. The note discloses that 2024 effective rent rates for franchised restaurants ranged from 0.00% to 33.33% of sales — meaning the real-world net burden is highly dispersed.
13. Audit: Ernst & Young LLP, Chicago, issued an unmodified (clean) opinion dated March 14, 2025. No going-concern modification. FY2024 total revenue $10,630.8M; net income $3,461.6M (up from $3,394.4M in 2023 and $3,095.7M in 2022).
14. The balance sheet is dominated by real estate: net property and equipment $13,080.4M + lease right-of-use $5,841.4M = $18.9B of the $22.2B asset base. Cash is a thin $34.6M. Due to parent (current) is $3,310.6M — a significant related-party exposure, plus $1,069.4M in royalty-to-parent expense for FY2024. Members' equity has been held constant at $10,222.0M, indicating full upstream of net income to the parent each year.
15. Litigation burden: at least 7 material pending cases. The two no-poach antitrust actions (Deslandes, Turner) were reversed by the Seventh Circuit in 2023 and remanded after SCOTUS cert denial. The Crawford/McPherson complex involves 77+ former Black franchisees alleging race discrimination. The Michell (CT) franchisee discrimination action had a partial dismissal in September 2025. The Williams E. coli Quarter Pounder class action (Oct 2024) and the Le McRib deceptive marketing class action (Dec 2025) are fresh consumer-side exposures.

## B. Fee stack, entry structure, initial investment

**Entity.** McDonald's USA, LLC, Delaware LLC, formed in 2004. Parent and predecessor is McDonald's Corporation, a Delaware corporation. All U.S. franchising runs through McDonald's USA, LLC; international franchising is run by affiliates (Exhibit Q). The franchisor also does not sell fixtures, equipment, food, or supplies directly to franchisees (Item 8).

**Initial franchise fees (Item 5).** $45,000 lump sum for traditional, payable on opening. $22,500 for STO/STR. $500 for Satellite; $0 for Walmart Satellite. $0 for McOpCo (no fee). Prorated if the term is ≤10 years. Rebuild/relocation: credit against prior fee, triggered at the earlier of first of month after year 7 post-reopening or end of previous term. The entire fee is refundable only if construction fails to complete within 1 year of signing; otherwise non-refundable.

**Ongoing fee stack (Item 6):**
- **Royalty**: 5% or 4% of Gross Sales (see §A.4). Payable monthly on the 10th.
- **Rent**: Monthly Base Rent + Pass Thru Rent + Percentage Rent. Percentage Rent generally 6%–23%, minimum 11.50% from year 8+. Total percentage-rent change through the term is capped at 3.5 percentage points absolute unless needed to reach the 11.50% floor. Co-investment policy permits optional reductions in 0.25% "Quarters" at a cost of ≥$30,000 per Quarter, with different criteria and floors for new traditional, relocated traditional, and STO/STR restaurants.
- **Advertising**: ≥4% of Gross Sales, spent on OPNAD (2.25% pledge) + local co-ops. Not paid to franchisor. OPNAD fund is independently audited annually.
- **Technology/SaaS stack**: Sesame $1,133 annual + $2,600 one-time; Global Mobile App/Digital $664; McDelivery POS $620; Self-Ordering Kiosk $1,500 one-time + $558 annual; eProduction $47; Back Office Integration $545; Payments and Fraud Management $740; Employee Engagement $385; Deployment/OTP/Execution/Support $2,529; Restaurant Network Management $1,134; Restaurant Hardware/Data Movement $951; Restaurant File Maintenance $690; Microsoft License $707; Edge $435 + $120 one-time; GRNT $185 + $491 one-time; DMB 2.0 $264 one-time; Store Mail $56.40. Optional: HHOT $50 + $500 one-time; PDW $167; McD Connect $250; Restaurant Employee Experience Survey $62; Pricing Engine $264. All tech fees are subject to periodic increase.
- **Event-driven**: Satellite Annual Fee $500–$2,500; Relocation Contribution $50,000; Audit/Inspection Fee at cost if audit finds ≥2% Gross Sales understatement.

**Initial investment (Item 7 traditional column):**
- Initial franchise fee: $45,000
- Real estate 3 months' base rent: $0–$313,000
- Signs, seating, equipment, decor: $1,100,000–$1,785,000 (Store Systems $150K–$250K inside this line)
- Opening inventory: $20,000–$39,000
- Misc opening expenses: $54,000–$66,000
- Travel & living: $2,000–$41,000
- Additional funds (3 months): $250,000–$439,000
- **TOTAL: $1,471,000–$2,728,000**, excluding percentage rent and royalty

Note 11 flags that 9 of 31 McOpCo transactions in 2024 exceeded the high end of the traditional range, by $65,593, $168,053, $388,069, $532,141, $582,364, $1,373,068, $1,459,004, $1,559,604, and $2,035,816 respectively. The published range significantly understates real-world acquisition cost in the more expensive half of the McOpCo refranchising pipeline.

## C. Supplier, operations, technology, training

**Supplier control (Item 8).** McDonald's does not itself sell goods to franchisees (except for incidental pilots) but imposes specification and approved-supplier regime. Required purchases represent ~90–95% of initial purchases and ~55–65% of ongoing purchases. Specific mandates:
- POS platform: Sesame, proprietary to McDonald's Corporation. Hardware from approved suppliers. Annual fee + one-time license.
- Cashless 3.0 system: hardware and software from designated suppliers; annual maintenance fee.
- Gift Card System: administered by P2W, Inc. NFP (board includes McDonald's employees and franchisees).
- Insurance: A+/A rated carriers, size category ≥IX per A.M. Best.
- 2024 franchisor revenue from loan guarantees, cashless incentives, beverage rebates: $39,008,767.
- 2024 total franchisor revenue ~$10.63B; real estate and services to franchisees ~$7.21B (68%).
- Franchisor holds uncapped independent access to transaction-level data from POS, Cashless, and Gift Card systems.

**Training (Item 11).** Hamburger University (Chicago) is the primary training center. Candidate Development Program runs via Campus and Fred platforms; typically 6 months to 2 years to complete all learning plans from Shift Leader through Restaurant Leader. Core on-job blocks: Crew Development 83 hrs, Area Management 60 hrs, Shift Leader 59 hrs, Department Leader 94 hrs, Restaurant Leader 31.75 hrs. Core classroom blocks: ServSafe 8 hrs, Developing the Leader in Me 24 hrs, Leading Great Restaurants Class 32 hrs (in-person at HU Chicago, 4 days). Business Learning Path adds ~150 additional classroom hours across 15 modules (Leading People, Operations/PACE, National Franchising Standards, Finance, Marketing, Tech, Back Office, etc.). Must complete before signing Franchise Agreement; no compensation during training; no employment relationship. Preliminary Agreement (Exhibit J) is the controlling document.

**Technology.** Store Systems = POS + Cashless + DMB + kiosks + table locators + hardware + software. Capital cost $150K–$250K (inside Item 7 equipment line). All new restaurants must deploy current Store Systems. Franchisee must submit monthly financial statements via the Franchisee Financial System (FFS): consolidated balance sheet, G&A expense statement, debt summary, and restaurant-level P&L.

**Site selection.** Franchisor exclusively selects the site; franchisee does not select or approve site locations. Franchisee cannot sign the Franchise Agreement until the site has been selected by the franchisor.

**Advertising fund governance.** OPNAD is independently audited annually; local co-ops variable. McDonald's is not required to spend any money on advertising. Franchisor does not maintain an ad fund.

## D. Territory, competition, channels, encroachment

- **No exclusive or protected territory.** Franchise Agreement grants only the right to operate at a specific address.
- **Reserved channels**: new franchised restaurants, new company-owned (McOpCo) restaurants, McCafe packaged coffee in supermarkets and retail, e-commerce, and alternate-mark concepts.
- **No rights** to prevent encroachment, no right of first refusal on nearby sites, no territorial development rights.
- Franchisee faces competition from: other McDonald's restaurants (franchised and McOpCo), other QSR chains, convenience stores, and — in STO locations — the co-located fuel/convenience store operator, which may sell fountain drinks and hot beverages within the same building.

## E. Contract burden and legal mechanics (Item 17)

**Term.** Traditional 20 years. STO/STR 10 years. BFL 3 years (with conditional 20-year extension via option). Satellite varies.

**Renewal.** There is **no right to renew or extend** the franchise. The New Term Policy (Exhibit K) is explicitly NOT a contract right and is subject to change in franchisor's sole discretion. Under current policy, the Percentage Rent for a new term franchise may be raised to an 8.50% floor if it is currently below, may increase based on franchisor additional institutional investment, expiring temporary reductions, or prior separate agreements; Pass Thru Rent may also increase in the new term. Materially different terms are a real possibility.

**Termination by franchisor with cause (Sections 18, 19).** Triggers include: failure to maintain restaurant standards; bankruptcy; any amount owing unpaid >30 days; unsatisfied judgment >$5,000 for >30 days; loss of right of possession; violation of franchise restrictions; knowing sale of unauthorized food; unauthorized transfer; denial of franchisor access; failure to pay undisputed invoices; misrepresentation in franchise application; reputation damage; felony conviction; intentional under-reporting of Gross Sales; repeated other breaches. Multiple enumerated categories are non-curable.

**Termination by franchisee**: Not Applicable. There is no contractual exit right.

**Transfer (Section 15).** Transfer means any direct/indirect/contingent transfer of any interest. Requires franchisor approval. Transferee must qualify under then-current standards; royalty steps up to the current 5% for the transferee (except for listed legacy categories); transferee assumes full and unconditional liability; original franchisee remains personally liable for the remainder of the term; no existing breach. Franchisor holds a **right of first refusal (Section 15(c))** to match any offer. On franchisee death or disability, franchisor holds an option to purchase the business if franchisor has been managing the restaurant for 1 year post-event. Upon termination, franchisor may purchase the furniture/fixtures/signs/equipment at fair market value — no payment for intangibles or goodwill. Spouse or nearest blood relative may train up to 18 months to attempt to qualify, but this is not a contractual obligation of the franchisor.

**Non-compete.**
- **In-term**: no involvement in any competing or similar business.
- **Post-term**: 18 months, 10-mile radius, including after assignment or sale.
- Exception: North Dakota explicitly flags these as generally unenforceable under state law, but McDonald's will seek to enforce to the extent enforceable.

**Dispute resolution.** Formal mediation/arbitration provisions are marked "Not Applicable" in Item 17. The "Special Risks" page (PDF page 4) declares that mediation, arbitration, and/or litigation will occur **only in Illinois**. This is flagged as a Special Risk for out-of-state franchisees because it may force less favorable settlements and higher dispute costs. Choice of law: Illinois (§27), with state-specific exceptions.

**Integration/merger clause.** Sections 28(c), (e), (f), (h), (i). Only the terms of the Franchise Agreement are binding (subject to state law). Nothing disclaims representations in the disclosure document itself.

**Litigation history (Item 3).**
- Pending (material):
  - Deslandes / Turner — no-poach antitrust (Sherman Act + IL state). 7th Circuit reversed district-court dismissal; SCOTUS denied cert. On remand in N.D. Ill.
  - Crawford et al. (77 former Black franchisees) + related McPherson complaint. Discrimination, bad faith, civil rights. Motions to dismiss pending since 2023.
  - Kytch, Inc. — ice cream machine diagnostic device. Transferred to N.D. Cal. Surviving claims include false advertising, trade libel, tortious interference with NDAs.
  - Michell et al. — CT franchisee discrimination, fraudulent inducement, tortious interference. Partial dismissal of amended complaint Sep 22, 2025.
  - Williams — Quarter Pounder E. coli nationwide class action. Motion to dismiss filed Sep 26, 2025.
  - Le et al. — McRib deceptive marketing class (CA + NY + IL + DC consumer laws). Filed Dec 23, 2025.
  - Sri Lanka collection action against Rusi Pestonjee (2024).
- Concluded (select): Lentini age-discrimination buyout $22M; Byrd Bros. $6.5M race-discrimination buyout; Washington $33.5M race-discrimination buyout; Ochoa CA joint-employer $3.75M; Puerto Rico Law 75 settlements $15.78M and $6.32M; Tavarua estate ROFR ruling + $15.6M sale; SEC Easterbrook administrative settlement (no monetary penalty); PFAS class settlements $145K.

- **Note 2**: "Product Sales" line is limited to product sales only; non-product sales and associated costs are in Other Operating Expenses.

## K. Contradictions

1. **Item 19 note on pro forma sample vs. headline.** The pro forma is described as drawn from "a total of 11,332 independent franchisee traditional restaurants," but the follow-on paragraph about OIBO percentiles says "Of the 11,322 independent franchisee traditional restaurants." The 11,332 vs. 11,322 difference is almost certainly a typo in the source document. Both numbers appear in the narrative. Flagged but not a material conflict.
