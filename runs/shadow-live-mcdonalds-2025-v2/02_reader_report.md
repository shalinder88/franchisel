# Reader Report — McDonald's USA, LLC (WI 638437-2025)

## Pass A — Executive snapshot

1. McDonald's USA, LLC is the U.S. franchisor; parent/predecessor is McDonald's Corporation (Delaware). HQ 110 N. Carpenter Street, Chicago, IL 60607.
2. ~95% of U.S. restaurants are franchised to independent operators; ~5% are operated by McOpCo (wholly-owned) subsidiaries. Total U.S. outlets 13,559 at year-end 2024 (12,887 franchised + 672 company-owned), net +102 vs. 2023.
3. Total initial investment for a traditional restaurant: $1,471,000 – $2,728,000, including $45,000 initial franchise fee. STO/STR: $1,014,500–$1,793,500 ($22,500 fee). Satellite: $525,000–$1,193,500 ($0–$500 fee).
4. Royalty: 5% of Gross Sales on post-Jan-1-2024 new restaurants / McOpCo sales / ROFR resales; 4% of Gross Sales on legacy arrangements (family transfers, new-term on existing, inter-franchisee resales, rebuilds).
5. Advertising: mandatory ≥4% of Gross Sales (not paid to franchisor). OPNAD national fund pledge currently 2.25% of sales; not contractually required.
6. Term: traditional 20 years; STO/STR 10 years; BFL 3 years; Satellite varies. **No right to renew or extend** — any "new term" franchise is discretionary per the "New Term Policy" (Exhibit K), which is explicitly not a contract right.
7. Territory: **no exclusive, protected, or reserved territory.** Franchisor reserves the right to compete via new franchisees, McOpCo outlets, alternate channels (McCafe retail, e-commerce), or different marks.
8. Real estate: franchisor owns or head-leases the premises; franchisee must sign an Operator's Lease in addition to the Franchise Agreement. Rent structure mixes monthly base rent + pass-thru rent + percentage rent (generally 6%–23% of Gross Sales, min 11.50% from year 8).
9. Item 19: avg annual sales $4,002,000 across 12,572 traditional restaurants; franchised-only average $3,966,000; median $3,797,000. Pro forma operating income before occupancy costs: $734K (at $3.0M sales), $812K ($3.2M), $879K ($3.4M) — excluding rent, royalty, D&A, interest, taxes.
10. Item 21: audited by Ernst & Young LLP, Chicago. Clean unmodified opinion dated March 14, 2025. FY2024 total revenue $10,630.8M; net income $3,461.6M; total assets $22,195.3M; members' equity $10,222.0M; **Due to parent, net $3,310.6M** (material related-party exposure).
11. Litigation burden is heavy: at least 7 actively pending cases — Deslandes/Turner antitrust (no-poach), Crawford/Byrd race-discrimination franchisee actions, Kytch trade libel, Michell (CT-based franchisee discrimination), Williams (E. coli class action, filed Oct 2024), Le (McRib class action, filed Dec 2025).
12. Special risk flag: franchisee must resolve disputes (mediation/arbitration/litigation) **only in Illinois**; Illinois governing law.
13. Post-term non-compete: 18 months within 10 miles. In-term no involvement in any competing or similar business.
14. Supplier control: no required purchases from franchisor directly, but required purchases (from franchisor or approved suppliers) represent ~90–95% of initial purchases and ~55–65% of ongoing purchases. Sesame POS + Cashless 3.0 + Gift Card System are mandatory. 2024 franchisor revenue from loan guarantee fees, cashless incentives, beverage rebates: $39,008,767.
15. Exhibit S lists 113 franchisees who left the system or stopped communicating in the most recent fiscal year.

## Pass B — Items 1–7 (front matter, fees, investment)

**Item 1.** Delaware LLC, wholly-owned subsidiary of McDonald's Corporation. Formed 2004; assets transferred in 2005 global alignment. Formats: Traditional (20-yr term), Satellite (term varies by location; Walmart satellites a sub-form), STO (fuel/convenience; 10-yr), STR (small-town retail; 10-yr), BFL (business facility lease; 3-yr with conditional option to extend up to 20 yrs). Franchisor does not itself offer franchises in any other line of business; international affiliates operate outside U.S. (Exhibit Q).

## Pass C — Items 8–11 (supply chain, operations, training, tech)

**Item 8.** Franchisor does not itself sell goods, but imposes specifications + approved-supplier regime. Required purchases represent ~90–95% of initial purchases and ~55–65% of ongoing purchases. Sesame POS, Cashless 3.0, and Gift Card System are effectively mandatory. P2W, Inc. (third-party non-profit) manages gift cards; board includes McDonald's employees and franchisees. Technology funds may be accumulated from supplier/processor payments, used "internally" for technology development but not held in a legally segregated trust. 2024 franchisor revenue from loan guarantee fees, cashless incentives, and beverage supplier rebates: **$39,008,767**. 2024 total revenue ~$10.63B, of which ~$7.21B (68%) came from real estate and services to franchisees. Insurance must be with carriers rated A+/A by A.M. Best with size ≥IX.

**Item 9.** Obligation matrix references Franchise Agreement §§1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 15, 17, 24, 28 and Operator's Lease §§2.01, 2.04, 2.06, 2.08, 3.01A/B, 3.02, 3.03, 4.02, 4.03, 4.06, 6.05, 7.01, 7.02. Explicitly marks territorial development/sales quotas, warranty/customer service, renewal, and dispute resolution as **"Not Applicable"** — reinforcing the no-renewal, no-territory, Illinois-only posture.

**Item 10.** No franchisor financing. BOA guarantee program: McDonald's Corp (predecessor) may guarantee term loans from Bank of America N.A. for remodels, acquisitions, BFL exercise. Guarantee fee 1.50% of average outstanding balance. Interest = Term SOFR + 0.10% + spread (initially 3.00%). As of March 17, 2025: Term SOFR 4.42%, APR 7.42%. Terms 3–5 yr, 7-yr amortization. Secured by equipment/seating/signage/décor/inventory. Personal guarantee + spouse. Default on loan = default on Franchise Agreement; franchisee must then sign Operator Assistance Program Agreement (Exhibit N), which requires waiver of claims against McDonald's. Jury trial waived; no class consolidation.

**Item 11.** Franchisor obligations (pre-opening): construct/remodel premises, provide specs and approved-supplier list, deliver O&T Manual, deliver training program. Operational: operations consultants, newsletters, O&T Manual, national advertising & marketing via in-house departments. Franchisor is explicitly **not required to spend any advertising dollars**; no franchisor-maintained ad fund. Site selection is exclusively franchisor's. Training: Hamburger University + field training. Candidate Development Program; can take 6 months to 2 years; must master all crew and management functions; no compensation during training. Must complete before signing Franchise Agreement. Training program table (Item 11, pp. 35–36) specifies hours:

- Food Safety/ServSafe 8 hrs classroom (varies by state)
- Department Leader 94 hrs

- Restaurant Leader 31.75 hrs
- Leading Great Restaurants Class 32 hrs classroom (HU Chicago, 4 days)
- Plus ~15 additional Business Learning Path modules (Leading People 22, Tech 14, Finance/Pricing 12, Ops/PACE/Food Safety 28.5, Mid-management 9.25, People Brand Standards 3.5, Marketing 6, Working w/ CPA 2, Reinvestment 6.5, National Franchising Standards 26.5, Dept Head Orientation 21.5, Back Office 13, Benchmarking 8, Preparing to Buy 3, Business Planning 2.75).

## Pass D — Items 12–16 (territory, trademarks, operations restrictions)

**Item 12.** Franchise Agreement contains **NO** exclusive/protected territory and no territorial development rights. Franchisor explicitly reserves rights to: establish new restaurants anywhere (franchisee or McOpCo), sell McCafe/similar via supermarkets, retailers, e-commerce, and operate under different trademarks. Sales and customer trading patterns subject to change. Encroachment is contractually permitted.

**Item 13.** Principal marks are Golden Arches Logo (Reg. 893,440, 1970), McDONALD'S name (Reg. 743,572, 1963), McDONALD'S + Golden Arches combined sign design (Reg. 1,287,408, 1984). Marks are actually owned by an affiliate, **Restaurant Brands, LLC**, and licensed to McDonald's USA, LLC for 20 years (auto-renewing). No material infringement litigation. Franchisor is not obligated to defend or indemnify franchisee for trademark-related claims.

**Item 14.** No patents. Copyrights (O&T Manual, menus, ad materials) are unregistered. Licensed from predecessor McDonald's Corp. Trade secrets clause: franchisee may not disclose financial performance information where it could be consolidated and used to influence McDonald's common stock. Strong confidentiality and post-term protection clauses.

**Item 15.** Full time and best efforts + personal on-premises supervision required (FA §§1(e), 13). No absentee ownership.

**Item 16.** May only sell products authorized by McDonald's and only operate the premises as a McDonald's restaurant. Franchisor may add/delete/change authorized products with no limits.

## Pass E — Item 17 (contract burden, legal mechanics)

- **Length:** Traditional ~20 years; STO/STR ~10; BFL ~3 (with 20-yr conditional extension). Satellite varies.
- **Renewal:** NO RIGHT TO RENEW OR EXTEND. Any successor term is governed by the "New Term Policy" (Exhibit K), a discretionary franchisor policy — explicitly NOT a contract right, subject to change in the franchisor's sole discretion. This is the #1 kill switch in the system. In a new term franchise, the Percentage Rent may be raised to 8.50% floor if current rate is below, may increase based on institutional investments, expiring temporary reductions, or prior separate agreements; Pass Thru Rent may increase.
- **Termination by franchisor with cause:** FA §§18, 19. Material breach triggers include: failure to maintain restaurant to standards; bankruptcy; any amount unpaid >30 days; unsatisfied judgment >$5,000; loss of right of possession; selling unauthorized food; unauthorized transfer; denial of access; misrepresentation; reputation damage; felony conviction; intentional under-reporting of Gross Sales; repeated breaches. No explicit cure-right schedule for enumerated material breaches.
- **Termination by franchisee:** Not Applicable.
- **Transfer:** FA §15. Definition includes direct, indirect, or contingent transfer of any interest. Requires franchisor approval. Transferee must qualify; royalty steps up to current (5%); transferee assumes full liability; original franchisee remains personally liable for the remainder of the term; no current breach. **Right of first refusal (§15(c))** — McDonald's can match any offer. **Option to purchase** on franchisee death/disability (§15(a)) if McDonald's has been managing the restaurant for 1 year post-event; and upon termination under §20 for fair market value of FF&E (no payment for intangible/goodwill).
- **Death/disability:** Franchise may be assigned to an approved heir/spouse. Spouse may train up to 18 months; no contractual obligation to extend.
- **Non-compete (in-term):** No involvement in any competing or similar business (FA §11).
- **Non-compete (post-term):** 18 months, 10-mile radius, including after assignment or sale.
- **Dispute resolution:** NOT APPLICABLE as a formal arbitration/mediation provision; but the Special Risks page (page 4) flags that "mediation, arbitration and/or litigation only in Illinois." Choice of law: Illinois (§27).
- **Integration/merger:** §§28(c), (e), (f), (h), (i). Only Franchise Agreement terms binding, but nothing disclaims representations in the FDD.
- **New franchises / Growth Policy (Exhibit L):** Franchisee has no right to acquire additional franchises.
