# Final Diligence Report — Dunkin' Donuts Franchising LLC
## FDD Filing 637953-2025 | Issued March 27, 2025 (Amended October 24, 2025)

---

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Dunkin' Donuts Franchising LLC** is a Delaware LLC formed March 15, 2006, headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia. It is a wholly-owned indirect subsidiary of DB Master Finance Parent LLC, which in turn sits within the Inspire Brands portfolio (ultimately owned by Roark Capital private equity). The franchisor has never operated a Dunkin' restaurant — it is a pure franchisor.

2. **System size**: 9,768 US Dunkin' restaurants as of December 29, 2024 (9,734 franchised, 34 company-owned in Ohio). This includes 8,480 single-branded Dunkin', 19 Multi-Brand Locations (co-located with Jimmy John's), and 1,269 Dunkin'/Baskin-Robbins combo restaurants. An additional 4,328 single-branded franchised Dunkin' restaurants operate internationally.

3. **Growth trajectory**: The system added a net +200 franchised standalone Dunkin' outlets in 2024, accelerating from +178 in 2023 and +77 in 2022. There are 278 projected new franchised openings and 115 signed-but-not-opened franchise agreements. Closures have declined sharply: 67 "ceased operations – other reasons" in 2024 vs. 139 in 2023 and 137 in 2022. Terminations virtually ceased: only 1 in 2024 vs. 4 in 2023 and 14 in 2022. Combo restaurants showed a net -14 in 2024 (31 opened, 40 ceased operations), likely reflecting Baskin-Robbins extraction from underperforming combo locations.

4. **Investment range**: $210,900–$1,832,500 depending on restaurant type (gas/convenience through freestanding). The midpoint for a traditional freestanding Dunkin' is approximately $1.18M.

5. **Fee burden**: CFF 5.9% + CAF 5.0% + Loyalty 1.4% = approximately 12.3% of Gross Sales in ongoing percentage-based fees, among the highest in QSR. Significant CFF/CAF reductions available through incentive programs for developing western and southern markets.

6. **Item 19 AUV**: $1,304,217 overall (7,190 restaurants). First quartile $2,073,245, fourth quartile $646,650. Traditional freestanding average $1,534,772. Drive-thru restaurants average $1,508,901 vs. $1,082,835 without. No cost or profitability data disclosed.

7. **Financial health**: DB Master Finance Parent reported $1.65B total revenue, $708M operating income, and $585M net income in 2024, all growing year-over-year. However, the entity carries $5.0B in long-term securitized debt against $8.3B in intangible assets. The securitization structure means franchise revenues serve as debt collateral, which could constrain franchisor flexibility in severe economic downturns.

8. **Territory risk**: No exclusive territory under the individual Franchise Agreement. The franchisor retains unrestricted rights to open competing locations, license alternative distribution (retail packaged products, K-Cups, bottled coffee), and grant competing franchises. Limited SDA protection is available but conditioned on full compliance.

9. **Noncompete**: 24 months post-term within 5 miles of Premises or any other Dunkin' restaurant. Competitive Business defined as >20% revenue from coffee and/or baked goods — a broad definition that could restrict subsequent career options.

10. **Renewal risk**: 20-year term with renewal available, but franchisee must sign the **then-current form of Franchise Agreement**, which may contain materially different terms. Combined with a general release requirement, this represents a significant power asymmetry at renewal.

11. **Dispute resolution**: No arbitration. Georgia law, Georgia courts. Subject to state addenda modifications in states like Minnesota (no out-of-state litigation required) and Maryland (franchise law claims may be brought in Maryland).

12. **Gag clause**: Present. Some current and former franchisees have signed confidentiality agreements restricting their ability to speak openly about their franchise experience.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Dunkin' Donuts Franchising LLC sits within a complex securitization structure created in 2015. DB Master Finance Parent LLC, the guarantor of franchisor obligations to franchisees, is a special-purpose entity formed specifically for the securitization. Dunkin' Brands, Inc. performs day-to-day management functions under a management agreement. This structure means that franchise fee revenues flow through securitized entities and serve as collateral for $5+ billion in debt. The unconditional guarantee from DB Master Finance Parent is the franchisee's primary protection.

The Inspire Brands portfolio is extensive: Arby's, Buffalo Wild Wings, Jimmy John's, Sonic, Baskin-Robbins, and (since April 2024) Subway, plus GoTo Foods brands, Driven Holdings automotive brands, ServiceMaster companies, and others. This creates both scale advantages (supply chain, technology) and potential conflict-of-interest risks (resource allocation across brands).

### Fee Stack

The ongoing fee burden for a standard new Dunkin' franchisee is approximately 12.3% of Gross Sales:

**Percentage-based ongoing fees:**
- Continuing Franchise Fee (CFF/Royalty): 5.9% of Gross Sales, paid weekly
- Continuing Advertising Fee (CAF): 5.0% of Gross Sales (2.5% for SDOs), paid weekly
- Loyalty Program Contribution: 1.4% of Loyalty Program Sales (declining to 1.2% in 2027+)

**Notable reduced CFF schedules for developing markets:**
The franchisor offers significant CFF/CAF incentives to encourage western and southern market development. Under the "Brewed for Growth" program (California, Texas, Oklahoma), the CFF starts at 1.9% for years 1-5 and gradually increases to the standard 5.9% by year 9. The "Select Developing Markets" program offers a 5-year ramp from 1.9% to 5.9%. The "Go West" program provides 2.4% for years 1-5. These programs can reduce the first-5-year fee burden by 40-70%, making entry economics substantially more attractive in expansion markets.

**Event-driven fees:**
- Transfer Fee (Dunkin', >3 years): $5,000-$20,000 tiered by trailing 12-month Gross Sales, on a scale from <$400K to >$1.4M
- Transfer Fee (Combo, >3 years): $12,500-$27,500 on same tiered scale
- Transfer Fee (<3 years): Additional $12,500 (Dunkin') or $20,000 (Combo) base
- Fixed Documentation Fee: $2,000/transferee (non-controlling transfers)
- SDA Transfer Fee: $10,000 (majority interest)
- Training: $4,000/person (2-4 included in IFF)
- Training cancellation: $1,000-$2,000/person depending on notice
- Supplier testing: $1,000-$10,000 for new supplier approval
- Relocation Fee: Pro-rated IFF for remaining term
- Enforcement Expenses: Actual costs if franchisor successfully enforces FA provisions

**Advertising fund spending (2024):** 44% media placement, 14% administrative, 5% promotions, 4% production, 2% POP materials, 31% other (research, sponsorships, readiness kits, PR, agency fees). The 14% administrative share is within the contractual 20% cap. Fund is independently audited annually.

**Loyalty Program economics:** The 1.4% LCP rate applies to in-store sales to loyalty members for eligible products. Franchisees receive NAPC (National Average Product Cost) reimbursement for reward redemptions. The franchisor provided a $3M initial loan to the Loyalty Fund. The fund is segregated, interest-bearing, and independently audited annually.

### Initial Investment

**Freestanding Restaurant: $526,900 – $1,832,500**
The largest components are building costs ($180K-$600K), equipment/fixtures/signs ($189K-$300K), restaurant technology ($65K-$118K), and site development ($13K-$350K). Building costs run $83-$566 per square foot for a 750-3,100 SF restaurant. Government impact fees can add $87,000+ in some markets. Land purchase, if applicable, adds $100K-$1.2M+.

**Shopping Center/Storefront: $437,500 – $1,333,500**
Lower building costs ($59-$378/SF for 600-2,800 SF) but otherwise similar component structure.

**Gas & Convenience: $210,900 – $1,065,500**
Lowest entry point with building costs at $56-$340/SF for 600-2,300 SF. IFF is pro-rated and may be as low as $10,000 for a short-term agreement. Self-serve configurations may cost less.

## C. Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Technology

### Supplier Restrictions

Dunkin' operates one of the more tightly controlled supply chains in QSR franchising. National DCP, LLC (NDCP), a franchisee-owned cooperative, is the designated purchasing and distribution supplier. Franchisees must become NDCP members ($2,500 one-time fee) and purchase all food and non-food products through NDCP. Approved/designated supplier purchases represent **more than 95%** of both establishment and operating purchases — one of the highest percentages in the industry.

The franchisor has exclusive supplier arrangements for purchasing/distribution services (NDCP), fountain and packaged beverages, and certain restaurant technology. In select markets, designated third-party bakery suppliers produce and deliver donut and bakery products, and franchisees must purchase from them for a specified period.

Supplier approval for alternatives is difficult: franchisees must submit a written request, the franchisor has 180 days to respond (deemed disapproved if no response), testing costs $1,000-$10,000, and the supplier must sign an NDA and performance guarantee. The franchisor may withdraw approval at any time.

### Training Program

The Dunkin' Training Program requires a minimum of 19 days: 56.5-71.5 hours of online training plus 210-415 hours of on-the-job training at a Certified Training Restaurant. The pass threshold is a cumulative 90% score on all written/verbal tests. The training team consists of 3 senior managers and 17 full-time training managers with an average tenure of 8 years.

Training requirements scale with network size: 2 people for 1-5 restaurants (included in IFF), 3 people for 6-19 restaurants, and 4 people for 20+ restaurants (at $4,000/person beyond IFF). English literacy and fluency are required.

The operations manual totals 817 pages across 7 subject areas: Operational Excellence (195 pages), CML/FP (117 pages), Food Safety (114 pages), Equipment (108 pages), Drive-Thru (96 pages), People (80 pages), and Digital/Mobile/Technology (70 pages).

### Technology Burden

The Restaurant Technology System is extensive and expensive. Initial technology costs range from $65,000-$118,000 for hardware alone, with significant ongoing monthly costs:

- POS System: $17K-$46K initial + $2.4K-$4.5K/year maintenance
- Store Network (managed internet): $225-$400/month
- Digital Menu Boards: Interior $5.8K-$8.6K + exterior drive-thru $13.5K-$14.3K
- Drive-Thru Audio: $4,500-$9,000
- Back-of-House Software: $51-$70/month
- Advance/Remote Ordering: 2.0-15.0% per transaction + $0-$75/month
- Various monitoring, music, patching, and support services

Critically, there are **no contractual limits** on the frequency or cost of technology upgrades. The franchisor has unlimited, independent access to POS data. PCI compliance is the franchisee's sole responsibility.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels

### No Exclusive Territory

Under the individual Franchise Agreement, the franchisee receives **no exclusive territory** and **no nonexclusive territory**. The franchisor retains unrestricted rights to: operate or license additional Dunkin' restaurants anywhere (including adjacent to the franchisee's location), grant competing franchises, use alternative distribution channels (internet, mail, retail products), and license the Dunkin' brand for packaged goods (K-Cups, bottled iced coffee, retail products) nationally and internationally.

The Store Development Agreement provides limited territorial protection within the SDA area during the SDA term, but this protection is conditioned on full SDA compliance and subject to exceptions for existing restaurants and SDO opportunities.

### Encroachment Risk

The no-territory structure, combined with Dunkin's aggressive growth targets (278 projected new openings), creates meaningful encroachment risk for existing franchisees, particularly in mature markets like the Northeast where restaurant density is already high. The franchisor considers "competition and potential for encroachment on other Restaurants of the same brand" when evaluating new sites, but has no obligation to protect existing franchisees from impact.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The franchise term is 20 years. Renewal is available but requires the franchisee to sign the **then-current form of Franchise Agreement**, which may be materially different from the original. Other renewal conditions include: written notice, maintained standards, no more than 3 default notices in the prior 10 years, no uncured defaults, all amounts paid, acceptable lease or substitute premises, general release, and a renewal fee. The "materially different terms" risk at renewal is among the most significant contract risks for long-term franchisees.

### Termination

The franchisee has **no right** to terminate the agreement. The franchisor may terminate only for cause, with cure periods ranging from 24 hours (health/safety/operations) to 30 days (general defaults). Non-curable defaults include bankruptcy, fraud, felony conviction, intentional Gross Sales underreporting, unauthorized transfer, and cross-default from other agreements. The cross-default provision means that a problem with one restaurant in a multi-unit network can trigger termination of all franchise agreements.

### Transfer

Transfer is broadly defined to include virtually any change in ownership, control, or management. The franchisor has right of first refusal (60 days) and an option to purchase operating assets at fair market value post-termination. Transfer conditions include transferee qualification, compliance, reasonable sale price, general release, and potentially refurbishing the restaurant to current standards — all at the franchisee's expense.

### Death and Disability

The legal representative must propose a transfer within 12 months of death and complete the transfer within 18 months, subject to franchisor consent.

### Noncompete

During the term, the franchisee and all owners, officers, directors, and guarantors are prohibited from having any interest in a "Competitive Business" — defined as deriving more than 20% of revenue from coffee and/or baked goods. This is a broad definition that would prohibit involvement in most coffee shops, bakeries, and breakfast-focused restaurants.

Post-term: 24 months within 5 miles of the Premises or any other Dunkin' restaurant. Given the density of Dunkin' restaurants in the Northeast (1,000+ in Massachusetts alone), this effectively creates a near-total prohibition on coffee/bakery competition in many markets.

### Dispute Resolution

No arbitration or mediation is required. Disputes are resolved in federal, state, or local courts in Atlanta, Georgia, under Georgia law. State addenda in Minnesota, Maryland, and potentially other states modify these provisions to protect franchisee forum rights.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

Dunkin' provides a comprehensive set of four financial performance representations, all based on Average Unit Volume (AUV = Gross Sales) for 7,190 "2024 Franchised Restaurants" — standalone Dunkin' locations that operated for the full 2024 fiscal year.

### Overall AUV

The systemwide average is $1,304,217 with a median of $1,240,107. The spread is significant: first quartile average is $2,073,245 while fourth quartile average is $646,650 — a 3.2x ratio. The highest-performing restaurant generated $5,720,770 while the lowest reported only $4,877 (likely a location that barely operated). 45% of restaurants exceeded the overall average.

### By Region

New England leads with an average AUV of $1,448,073 (1,785 restaurants), reflecting the brand's historical strength in its home region. The weakest regions are West South Central ($953,320, 156 restaurants) and Midwest West North Central ($996,410, 134 restaurants), both representing newer expansion markets with less brand awareness and market density.

### By Site Type

Traditional freestanding locations average $1,534,772 (3,105 restaurants), substantially above the overall average. Airport SDO locations also perform well at $1,527,194 (119 restaurants). Gas/convenience store locations average $1,071,111 (963 restaurants). The weakest category is SDO-Other at $723,001 (495 restaurants), encompassing schools, hospitals, military bases, and similar institutional settings.

### Drive-Thru Impact

Drive-thru restaurants average $1,508,901 (4,051 locations) compared to $1,082,835 for non-drive-thru (1,541 locations) — a 39% premium that underscores the critical importance of drive-thru capability for Dunkin' economics.

### Critical Caveats

Item 19 provides **revenue only** — no cost of goods, labor, occupancy, or EBITDA data. Franchisees must independently investigate operating costs and profit margins. The 7,190 restaurants in the sample have operated for an average of 16.4 years, meaning they include many mature, optimized locations; new restaurants may take years to reach these volumes. Exclusions removed 1,275+ restaurants from the sample, including all restaurants that opened during 2024, all that closed for extended periods, and all 34 company-owned locations.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### Growth Trajectory

Dunkin' standalone franchised restaurants showed accelerating net growth over three years: +77 (2022), +178 (2023), +200 (2024). This growth was driven by increasing openings (271 → 344 → 312) and sharply declining closures. "Ceased operations – other reasons" dropped dramatically from 137-139 in 2022-2023 to just 67 in 2024. Terminations fell to near zero: 14 (2022) → 4 (2023) → 1 (2024).

Company-owned locations are minimal: 34 total, all in Ohio, up from zero in early 2022. This is consistent with Dunkin's pure franchisor model.

Combo restaurants (Dunkin' + Baskin-Robbins) showed a net decline of -14 in 2024 after gains of +18 and +31 in prior years. The 40 combo "ceased operations" in 2024 likely reflects strategic extraction of underperforming BR components rather than complete restaurant closures.

### Transfer Activity

Transfer volume declined significantly: 392 (2022) → 224 (2023) → 137 (2024). This could reflect reduced turnover (healthy system), higher sale prices making deals harder to close, or franchisor policy changes affecting transfer approval.

### Projected Openings

278 new franchised Dunkin' outlets projected, with 115 franchise agreements already signed but not yet opened. Top projected states: New York (26), Ohio (23), Florida (22), Virginia (15), Massachusetts (14), Pennsylvania (14), Texas (13), Indiana (11), California (10), Michigan (10).

### Gag Clause

Present. The FDD states: "In some instances, current and former franchisees sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with us." These confidentiality agreements arise from dispute settlements. Prospective franchisees should be aware that franchisee references may not be fully candid.

### Independent Franchisee Association

## H. Item 21 — Financial Statements

### Auditor and Opinion

KPMG LLP (Atlanta, GA) issued an unqualified (clean) audit opinion on the consolidated financial statements of DB Master Finance Parent LLC and Subsidiaries for the three fiscal years ended December 29, 2024. No going-concern language was included. KPMG is a Big Four auditor, lending high credibility to the financial statements.

### Balance Sheet Highlights (Dec 29, 2024)

- **Total assets**: $9.73 billion, dominated by $8.30 billion in intangible assets (net) — primarily trademarks, franchise rights, and goodwill from the securitization structure and Inspire Brands acquisition history
- **Total current assets**: $922 million, up significantly from $220 million in 2023, driven by a $665 million note receivable from affiliate (new in 2024)
- **Property and improvements**: $138 million (net)
- **Operating lease assets (ROU)**: $338 million
- **Long-term debt**: $5.01 billion (net) — securitized notes
- **Current portion of long-term debt**: $43 million
- **Total member's equity**: $4.10 billion ($3.88B contributed capital + $219M retained earnings)
- **Deferred revenue**: $183 million total ($34M current + $149M long-term) — representing upfront franchise fees being recognized over the franchise term

### Income Statement Highlights (FY2024)

- **Total revenues**: $1.646 billion
  - Franchise fees and royalties: $800 million (49% of revenue)
  - Advertising contributions: $631 million (38% — pass-through)
  - Rental income: $134 million (8%)
  - Licensing fees: $69 million (4%)
  - Wholesale + other: $11 million (1%)
- **Total operating expenses**: $938 million
  - Advertising expenses: $631 million (pass-through match)
  - Management fees: $114 million (paid to Dunkin' Brands, Inc.)
  - Depreciation/amortization: $100 million
  - Occupancy expenses: $84 million
  - G&A: $6 million (remarkably low for a $1.6B revenue entity — reflects management fee structure)
- **Operating income**: $708 million (43% operating margin, excluding advertising pass-through)
- **Interest expense**: $163 million (on $5B+ securitized debt)
- **Interest income from affiliate**: $41 million (new in 2024, from $665M affiliate note)
- **Net income**: $585 million

### Cash Flow and Capital

- Operating cash flow: $681 million
- Distributions to parent: $366 million (down from $1.098 billion in 2023)
- New debt proceeds: $665 million
- Debt repayments: $308 million
- Net increase in debt: ~$357 million

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Asset composition**: 85% intangible assets — the entity is essentially a financial vehicle holding franchise IP and debt instruments. Physical assets (property $138M) are minimal relative to system size.

2. **Revenue growth**: Total revenue grew from $1.50B (2022) to $1.59B (2023) to $1.65B (2024), reflecting system growth and same-store sales improvement.

3. **Debt burden**: $5.0B in long-term securitized debt is manageable given $681M annual operating cash flow (7.4x debt/operating cash flow, ~7.0x debt service coverage counting interest). However, the securitization structure creates subordination risks and limits operational flexibility.

4. **Distribution pattern**: 2023 saw an outsized $1.098B distribution to parent (likely related to Inspire Brands/Roark capital structure). 2024's $366M distribution is more sustainable relative to $585M net income.

5. **Deferred revenue growth**: $183M total deferred revenue (up from $151M in 2023) reflects the growing pool of franchise fees being recognized over 20-year terms under ASC 606.

6. **Note receivable from affiliate**: A new $665M note receivable from an affiliate appeared in 2024, funded by new debt issuance. This intercompany transaction may represent capital reallocation within the Inspire Brands structure.

- General releases required for renewal/transfer **do not apply** to claims under Maryland Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law
