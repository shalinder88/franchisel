## A. Executive Snapshot

It is a wholly-owned indirect subsidiary of Restaurant Brands International Limited Partnership (RBILP, Ontario), whose general partner is Restaurant Brands International Inc. (RBI, Canadian public company). 3G Restaurant Brands Holdings LP holds approximately 26% of the combined voting power of RBI. BKC's predecessor, Burger King Corporation (founded 1954), was dissolved in December 2022 after the August 2022 Internal Reorganization that transferred all franchise agreements and operations to BKC.

2. **System size at 12/31/2024:** 19,732 BURGER KING Restaurants worldwide; 6,701 in the United States, of which 5,524 are franchised and 1,177 are owned by BKC or affiliates. The company-owned count jumped from 138 to 1,177 in 2024 because RBI acquired Carrols Restaurant Group on May 16, 2024, converting 1,023 franchised restaurants to company-owned overnight. US franchised outlets dropped from 6,640 to 5,524 in 2024 (-1,116 net), of which 1,061 were reacquired and the residual ~93 represented organic decline. ## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front matter and entity structure

BKC was formed as a Florida LLC on 2/4/2022. Predecessor BK Corporation was originally founded in 1954 as Burger King of Miami, Inc., became South Florida Restaurants in 1956, and "Burger King Corporation" in 1963; dissolved in December 2022. The August 2022 Internal Reorganization transferred all assets, liabilities, and existing franchise/development/target reservation agreements from BK Corporation to BKC. Two ownership types are recognized: Individual/Owner-Operator (D1 Franchise Agreement form) and Entity (D2 form). The Entity form has two further variants: Legacy Entity Franchise Agreement Addendum (J2) for franchisees previously approved under pre-6/18/2024 Entity Guidelines, and Corporate Addendum (J1) for publicly-traded entities or contract feeders. Crown Your Career has its own addendum (Z1/Z2) overlay.

International franchise affiliates (12/31/2024 counts): BK Europe (6,065), BK APac (1,851; +1,188 acquired in February 2025), BKA IP (2,345), BKL IP (2,232), BK Canada (381). Carrols LLC is a US affiliate that operates BK restaurants and may sell them under the Carrols Refranchise Program. RBI also owns Tim Hortons, Popeyes, and Firehouse Subs franchise systems.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

**Item 12 — Protected territory definition:** The Franchise Agreement grants the right to operate at one specific location. **No exclusive territory, no protected customer base, no protected radius.** The TRA and MTRA define one or more "Target Areas" only as authorized search areas — no exclusivity. There are no restrictions on BKC in the Target Areas. If BKC proposes to develop a Restaurant in a Target Area for itself or another franchisee, it gives the existing franchisee the option to develop, but if the option is not exercised BKC or the third party can develop and BKC can terminate the TRA/MTRA refunding remaining deposit. Development Agreements grant a "Territory" but explicitly **without exclusivity**.

**Carve-outs:**
- **AAFES military:** BKC has granted the Army & Air Force Exchange Service the right to develop and license restaurants on US military establishments and adjacent housing/support areas.
- **Alternative channels reserved:** BKC and affiliates retain the right to sell through internet, grocery, convenience stores, direct marketing, and other channels.
- **Sister-brand overlap:** Tim Hortons, Popeyes, and Firehouse Subs restaurants may share markets with BK locations.
- **Mattoon, IL (Item 13):** BKC cannot use the BURGER KING mark within a 20-mile radius of Mattoon, Illinois under a final court decree with a prior user.

**Relocation:** No right to relocate without prior written approval.

**Channel restrictions:** Franchisee may not use BURGER KING marks on websites, social media, or as part of domain/email addresses without approval. Franchisee may not solicit customers outside any granted territory. Delivery Restaurant operators waive all rights to dispute development of another restaurant.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and renewal
- 20 years for freestanding Restaurants; less for non-traditional or where property control is shorter
- **No right of renewal.** Successor option only — up to 20 years if compliant, property control obtained, then-current requirements met
- **Successor terms may be materially different.** Successor requires application, general release, current FA, successor franchise fee, Successor Addendum (H1-H3), building upgrades, inspections
- **No successor option for Big-Box Program or Delivery Restaurant**

### Transfer
- BKC consent required
- **Crown Your Career:** BKC may withhold consent for any reason during first 5 years
- **Carrols Refranchise:** BKC may withhold consent for any reason until remodels complete
- Buyer must complete training, pay transfer fees, sign current FA (which may differ materially), seller must sign general release
- **BKC has Right of First Refusal** to purchase the restaurant at the same terms as a third-party offer

### Termination by BKC
- 30-day notice; cause-based only
- Cure periods: 5 days (operational/quality/health/sanitation/abandonment/insurance), 10 days (royalty/advertising), 30 days (reports/equipment/maintenance/Operating Partner compliance), 60 days (other), 180 days (financial criteria)
- Non-curable defaults include: bankruptcy (caveat that may not be enforceable), conviction of indictable offense or fraud/dishonesty/moral turpitude crime, gross sales understatement, failure to close immediately for serious health/safety, unauthorized transfer, repeated breaches after notice, material misrepresentation
- **Cross-defaults across all BK agreements**: a default under any FA, lease, Reclaim the Flame Master Program Agreement, Crown Your Career, or Carrols Refranchise Addendum constitutes a default under the FA

### Death/disability
- Heir must be approved by BKC or interest sold within **12 months**
- BKC has option to operate or manage in interim

### Non-competition
- **During term:** No interest in any other hamburger business; no use of BK System/marks/trade secrets except for BK
- **Crown Your Career:** Operating Partner/Managing Owner cannot engage in any other business activity until 5th anniversary or full Note repayment
- **Post-term:** 1 year, 2-mile radius, no interest in any hamburger business (Sec. 19 of FA)
- Corporate Addendum holders qualifying as Contract Feeders may operate competing fast-food businesses subject to conditions

### Dispute resolution
- **No mandatory arbitration** for general claims
- **Mandatory non-binding mediation** for development disputes (Special Risk #2)
- Voluntary arbitration option for development disputes (Delivery Restaurant operators waive these rights)
- **Choice of forum: U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Florida or 11th Judicial Court Miami-Dade County, Florida** (Special Risk #1)
- **Choice of law: Florida law generally applies**
- State addenda override the venue/governing law in CA, IL, MD, ND, RI, VA, WA

### Key financial observations
- **Carrols acquisition impact on income statement:** Company restaurant sales jumped from $271M to $1,592M (+488%) and company restaurant expenses jumped from $242M to $1,328M as a direct result of Carrols becoming a consolidated operating segment. RBI now operates a substantial restaurant portfolio (1,177 BK restaurants in the US alone).
- **Goodwill and intangibles dominate the balance sheet:** $5,986M goodwill + $10,922M intangibles = $16,908M = **~69% of total assets**. RBI's value is largely brand-related; the Firehouse Subs $816M brand intangible recoverability is one of the two critical audit matters.
