## Pass A — Executive Snapshot

1. **System size & contraction.** 6,701 US BK Restaurants as of 12/31/2024 (5,524 franchised + 1,177 company-owned). Three-year US net change is roughly -403 outlets. The 2024 franchisee headcount dropped by 1,116 outlets — driven primarily by RBI's 5/16/2024 acquisition of Carrols Restaurant Group (1,023 units flipped franchised → company-owned), with residual ~93 organic decline. Globally 19,732 restaurants.
2. **Entity & ownership.** Burger King Company LLC, a Florida LLC formed 2/4/2022. Wholly-owned indirect subsidiary of RBILP (Ontario LP) → RBI (Canadian public company). 3G Restaurant Brands Holdings LP holds ~26% combined voting power of RBI. Predecessor BK Corporation (1954) was dissolved in December 2022 after the August 2022 Internal Reorganization that transferred all franchise agreements to BKC.
3. **Investment range.** $348,400 (Indoor MRS) to $4,705,600 (Traditional Freestanding with optional 2-Story Playground), excluding real estate. Amount payable to franchisor or affiliates: $57,750–$67,500.
4. **Fee burden.** 4.5% royalty + up to 4.5% advertising contribution + up to 2.0% Investment Spending (DMA-determined) = up to ~11% of Gross Sales in recurring fees, plus a $110/month Digital Services fee with 1% digital sales overlay (capped at $4,500/year), plus $700/year BK University, plus $1,000/year Burger King Foundation contribution.
5. **Item 19 — Sales only.** Traditional Restaurant consolidated average gross sales: $1,671,613; median $1,579,183; 43% of units met or exceeded the average. Non-Traditional franchisee-owned avg: $1,321,324; median $1,155,563; **15.9% below $0.7M**, **29.8% below $0.9M** — material bottom-quartile risk in Non-Traditional. No cost-of-sales, no EBITDA, no profit. Section B remodel uplift: avg sales uplift +12.3% (Full +11.4%, Scrape & Rebuild +26.1%) on a sample of 893 Modern Image remodels with CAPEX ≥$650K.
6. **Bottom-quartile financial risk.** In Non-Traditional, the lowest sales bracket (<$0.7M) holds 15.9% of franchisee-owned units; lowest Traditional franchisee-owned sales were $271,485 (well below the breakeven point implied by initial investment + ongoing fees).
7. **Financial health (parent).** Auditor: KPMG LLP, Miami, since 1989, unqualified opinion 2/21/2025. Auditing the **RBI consolidated** statements (and RBILP), not BKC standalone — California addendum confirms BKC's standalone balance sheet is **unaudited**. RBI 2024: $24.6B total assets, $8.4B revenue, $1.4B net income, $1.3B cash. Two critical audit matters: (a) Carrols acquisition fair-value of leases/franchise rights, (b) Firehouse Subs $816M brand intangible recoverability.
8. **Growth trajectory.** Projected new outlets next fiscal year: 48 franchised + 2 company-owned. Zero franchise agreements signed but not yet opened. Growth pipeline is thin and concentrated (TX 6, NY 5, GA 4, NJ 4, PA 4).
9. **Territory.** **No exclusive territory.** Franchisee gets a single-location grant with no protection. TRA/MTRA only define search areas, not exclusivity. BK reserves alternative distribution channels (internet, grocery), military AAFES carve-out, and overlap with Tim Hortons / Popeyes / Firehouse Subs sister brands.
10. **Renewal.** No right of renewal; option for a successor FA on then-current terms which **may be materially different**, plus successor fee, building upgrades, general release. **No successor option for Big-Box or Delivery Restaurants.** Reclaim the Flame 2 successor royalty options: 4.5%/5.0%/5.5%/6.0%; failed remodel: +3.0% royalty.
11. **Disputes / venue.** Mandatory non-binding mediation for development disputes. No mandatory arbitration for general claims. Litigation venue: U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Florida or 11th Judicial Court Miami-Dade County. Florida law. State addenda override these in CA, IL, MD, ND, RI, VA, WA (and others).
12. **Litigation.** Six pending material actions including: Arrington antitrust no-poach class action (mediation ordered before April 3, 2026 after Apr 2025 motion to dismiss denied); Plymouth County Retirement v RBI fiduciary duty class re Carrols acquisition (trial Feb 1, 2027); Pemberton/Hugi CIPA cookie tracking class actions against bk.com and firehousesubs.com; First International Fund (Canada, $500M+ damages claim, 2013 vintage). No bankruptcies (Item 4).
13. **Special risks.** Out-of-state dispute resolution (FL-only litigation); mediation for development disputes (delays suit).
14. **Structural complexity.** 26-letter exhibit set (A through Z), 50+ sub-exhibits, multiple overlapping incentive programs (Reclaim the Flame, Reclaim the Flame 2, Fuel the Flame, Crown Your Career, DIP 2017/2024, BKoT, Midterm Remodel Forbearance) and 4 franchise agreement variants (Individual, Entity, Legacy Entity, Corporate Addendum).

## Pass C — Supplier control, operations, training, tech (Items 8-11)

**Item 8 — Supplier control.** BKC establishes standards/specs for most goods, services, equipment, signs, software, and digital menu boards. Approved suppliers required for most items. BKC is **sole supplier** of: technology platforms (mobile app, ordering, loyalty), network services, digital menu boards, gift card services, cashless payments processing, quality assurance inspection services, food, beverages, cleaning supplies, packaging, certain equipment. Approved-supplier costs: 65%-90% of build-out costs and 30%-60% of non-occupancy operating expenses. FY2024 BKC required-purchase revenues: ~$227M / ~$1.427B total revenues = ~16%. Supplier rebates contributed 8.4% of consolidated ad fund revenue. Insurance minimums: $2M GL per occurrence; aggregate scales $2M (1-10 units), $5M (11-50), $10M (>50). RSI (Restaurant Services Inc.) is the not-for-profit independent purchasing co-op with 21-member board (18 franchisee-elected from 9 districts, 1 minority franchisee, 1 BKC, 1 independent). BKC officers personally own publicly traded shares of General Mills, J&J, Kraft Heinz, PepsiCo (suppliers).

**Item 9.** Standard franchisee obligations table; cross-references to Items 5-8 and 11-17.

**Item 10 — Financing.** BKC does not directly finance except: (a) Real estate leases on BKL/BKG sites, (b) Crown Your Career Program — financing for BK employees buying company-owned restaurants. Crown Your Career interest rate: WSJ Prime + ~1%-4%; loan term ≤10 years; secured by all restaurant assets; personal guaranty required; 2% above Note rate on overdue amounts; mandatory venue Miami-Dade County; jury waiver. Default cross-defaults the Franchise Agreement.

**Item 11 — Pre-opening assistance, ongoing support, training, marketing fund, computer systems.**
- Pre-opening: Site Approval, plans/specifications access, MOD Manual loaner, training. BKC does not negotiate the lease and generally does not own premises.

- Training: $7,500/trainee. Up to 20 hours classroom/online + up to 320 hours in-restaurant for new franchisees, before opening. ServSafe required. Training director: Greg Moscetti (since June 2025). IL requires classroom (no online cert). Continuing operations training: $7,500/person.
- POS: Required; ranges $20,000-$25,000 per Restaurant; ongoing software/maintenance $2,000-$4,000/year; data reporting system $200-$1,200/year; network standardization $220-$450/month.
- Loyalty: ROYAL PERKS Loyalty Program mandatory for all restaurants; currency = "Crowns".
- Advertising fund: Maintained by BKC; FY2024 expenditures: 79.22% working media, 6.60% agency fees, 9.42% other (research, PR, brand delivery, competitive positioning, promotion development), 3.72% production, 1.04% POP materials. NFA (National Franchisee Association) is the qualified Franchisee Association; Franchise Advisory Council (FAC) is consultative only with audit rights but no decision authority.

## Pass D — Territory, channels, encroachment (Items 12-16)

**Item 12 — Territory.** Franchisee receives **NO exclusive territory**. TRA/MTRA define Target Areas to search for sites only — no exclusivity. The Franchise Agreement grants the right to operate at one specific location only. BKC reserves all alternative distribution channels (internet, grocery, convenience stores, direct marketing). Tim Hortons, Popeyes, and Firehouse Subs sister-brand restaurants may share markets with BK locations. AAFES military carve-out: BKC granted Army & Air Force Exchange Service rights to develop and license restaurants on US military establishments. No relocation right without prior written approval.

**Item 13 — Trademarks.** BURGER KING (Reg. 869775, 1969), BURGER KING & Crescent Design, BK marks (3 separate registrations), BURGER KING & Hamburger Refresh Design (2021), HAVE IT YOUR WAY (1973), HOME OF THE WHOPPER (1965), KING JR (2018), WHOPPER (2010), BURGER KING YOUR WAY & Hamburger Refresh Design (2022). All registered/active. Limit: under a final decree, "BURGER KING" cannot be used by BKC within a 20-mile radius of Mattoon, Illinois (prior user dispute resolution).

**Item 14 — Patents/Copyrights/Proprietary.** No material patents or copyrights. Common-law copyright in MOD Manual and other manuals. Strict trade-secret restrictions including a notable AI restriction: franchisee cannot use trade-secret information for machine learning, training AI models, or generative AI inputs without approval and must opt out of AI providers using BKC trade secrets for training.

**Item 15 — Personal participation.** Operating Partner (Individual FA) must devote full time, no other operational/management commitments except other BK restaurants, no interest in any hamburger business except other BK restaurants, must own ≥50% equity. Managing Owner (Entity FA) must own ≥25% equity, devote full time, live in vicinity. Crown Your Career adds: until 5th anniversary or full Note repayment, Managing Owner cannot engage in other business activity or be employed elsewhere; must own 100% of franchisee entity.

**Item 16 — Restrictions on what franchisee may sell.** Restaurant solely for BK use. Specified menu only (BKC-approved). Required participation in ROYAL PERKS Loyalty Program and BK app/website ordering and delivery aggregator (e.g., Uber Eats). Maximum prices on Value Menu items. No phones, vending, juke boxes, lottery terminals, ATMs, internet terminals, video games without authorization. Drive-Thru-Only and Delivery-Only restaurants have customer restrictions (drive-thru and delivery customers respectively).

## Pass E — Contract burden, legal mechanics (Item 17)

**Term.** 20 years for freestanding; less for non-traditional or where property control is shorter.

**Renewal.** No right of renewal. Option to obtain a successor Franchise Agreement up to 20 years if compliant, property control obtained, and meets then-current requirements. **Successor terms may be materially different.** **No successor option for Big-Box Program or Delivery Restaurant.** Successor requires application + general release + then-current FA + successor franchise fee + Successor Addendum (H1-H3) + building upgrades.

**Termination by us.** Cause-based with 30-day notice. Cure periods: 5 days (operational/quality/health/sanitation/abandonment/insurance), 10 days (royalty/advertising non-payment), 30 days (reports/equipment/maintenance/Operating Partner compliance), 60 days (other defaults), 180 days (failure to meet financial criteria). Non-curable defaults: bankruptcy (caveat), conviction of indictable offense, fraud, material misrepresentation, gross sales understatement, serious health/safety failure to close, unauthorized transfer, repeated breaches.

**Transfer.** Consent required. Transfer fees: $2,000 first restaurant + $500 additional + $175 weekend/holiday. Buyer must complete all training, full FA, general release. Crown Your Career: BKC may withhold consent for any reason during first 5 years. Carrols Refranchise: BKC may withhold consent for any reason until remodels complete. Right of First Refusal at our discretion based on third-party offer terms.

**Death/disability.** Heir must be approved or interest sold within 12 months. BKC may operate or manage in interim.

**Non-competition during term.** No interest in any other hamburger business. Crown Your Career adds: no other business activity until 5th anniversary or full Note repayment.

**Non-competition post-term.** 1 year, 2-mile radius, no interest in any hamburger business (Sec. 19 of FA).

**Dispute resolution.** No mandatory arbitration for general claims. Mandatory non-binding mediation for development disputes. Voluntary arbitration option for development disputes (Delivery Restaurant operators waive these rights). Franchisor-initiated termination enforcement actions filed in S.D. Florida.

**Choice of forum / law.** Subject to state law: U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Florida or the 11th Judicial Court Miami-Dade County, Florida. Florida law generally applies. State addenda override these in CA, IL, MD, ND, RI, VA, WA at minimum (per Item 21 guarantor analysis).

**TRA/MTRA / Lease (BKL) / Development Agreement / APA.** Each has its own Item 17 table covering term (TRA 1-1.5 yr; MTRA 2-3 yr; Dev Agmt 3-15 yr), termination, transfer, dispute resolution. All require Florida venue. Development Agreement non-curable defaults include: failure to meet third-party debt/EBITDA ratio thresholds, F grade in any operational metric, breach of restrictive covenants, opening a Restaurant without franchise approval. BKL termination triggers cross-default with the Franchise Agreement.
