# Reader Report — McDonald's USA, LLC (638437-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: ~13,559 total U.S. outlets at end of 2024 (12,887 franchised + 672 company-owned). ~95% franchised to independent franchisees, ~5% McOpCo.
2. **Entity structure**: McDonald's USA, LLC (Delaware LLC), wholly-owned subsidiary of McDonald's Corporation (Delaware corp). Franchisor since 2005 when McDonald's Corp transferred US assets. Operating since 1955 under predecessor.
3. **Ownership history**: McDonald's Corporation began franchising 1955. Formed McDonald's USA, LLC 2004; transferred majority of US assets 2005. Puerto Rico/Virgin Islands sold to LatAm, LLC 2007.
4. **Investment range**: $1,471,000–$2,728,000 for traditional; $1,014,500–$1,793,500 for STO/STR; $525,000–$1,193,500 for Satellites. Initial franchise fee $45,000 traditional, $22,500 STO/STR, $500 Satellite.
5. **Fee burden**: Royalty 4-5% of Gross Sales (5% for post-Jan 2024 new restaurants). Rent: percentage rent 6-23% + base rent + pass-thru rent. Advertising min 4% (OPNAD currently 2.25%). Technology fees ~$10,000+/year aggregate. Total occupancy + royalty can exceed 20% of gross sales.
6. **Item 19 highlights**: Average annual sales $4,002,000 (all traditional); $3,966,000 (franchised only); $4,793,000 (McOpCo). Median: $3,838,000 (all), $3,797,000 (franchised). Pro forma operating income before occupancy costs: $734K at $3M sales (24.5%), $812K at $3.2M (25.4%), $879K at $3.4M (25.9%).
7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: Effective rent 0.00%-33.33%. Lowest annual sales volume $914,000. At $3M sales, 24% of restaurants had operating income below $734K before occupancy costs.
8. **Financial health (Item 21)**: Total revenue $10.63B (2024). Net income $3.46B. Total assets $22.2B. Members' equity $10.2B. Ernst & Young clean opinion. $2.4B intercompany debt converted to equity Feb 2025.
9. **Growth trajectory**: Net +102 outlets in 2024 (vs +2 in 2023, +19 in 2022). Projected 195 new openings (181 franchised + 14 company-owned).
10. **Territory**: No exclusive territory. No protected territory. Franchisor retains right to open competing locations and use marks in other channels (e.g., McCafe in supermarkets).
11. **Renewal**: No contractual right to renew or extend. New Term Policy is discretionary, not a contract right.
12. **Disputes/litigation**: Multiple pending cases including race discrimination (Crawford — 77 former franchisees), antitrust (Deslandes, Turner — no-poach clauses), IP (Kytch), E. coli class action (Williams), McRib marketing (Le). Significant concluded settlements totaling tens of millions.
13. **Special risks**: Illinois venue, no renewal right, no territory, gag clause, royalty escalation to 5%.

## Pass B: Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1-7)

### Item 1 — The Franchisor
McDonald's USA, LLC is a Delaware LLC with principal office at 110 N. Carpenter Street, Chicago, IL 60607. It is a wholly-owned subsidiary of McDonald's Corporation (Delaware corp, same address). The predecessor (McDonald's Corporation) began granting franchises in 1955. The LLC was formed in 2004, received US assets in 2005. Neither entity has offered franchises in any other line of business. International affiliates offer McDonald's franchises outside the US (Exhibit Q). The system operates quick service restaurants offering a limited menu of value-priced foods. About 95% of US restaurants are franchised to independent franchisees; ~5% to McOpCo companies. Franchise types include traditional (20-year term), Satellite (variable term, scaled-down menu), STO (small town oil, 10-year), STR (small town retail, 10-year), and BFL (business facilities lease, 3-year with purchase option).

### Item 3 — Litigation
**Pending cases (6 named + general labor/employment):**
1. Deslandes v. McDonald's — antitrust, no-poach clause in franchise agreement. Supreme Court denied certiorari Nov 2023. Remanded to district court.
2. Turner v. McDonald's — same no-poach issue, parallel case.
3. Crawford v. McDonald's — 77 former Black franchisees allege race discrimination, bad faith breach, fraudulent inducement. Multiple amended complaints. Related McPherson case consolidated.
4. Kytch v. McDonald's Corp — ice cream machine diagnostic device. False advertising, trade libel. Transferred to N.D. California.
5. Michell v. McDonald's — Hispanic franchisee alleges forced exit, discrimination. Counterclaim by McDonald's for breach.
6. Williams v. McDonald's — E. coli class action, Quarter Pounder, purported nationwide class.
7. Le v. McDonald's Corp — McRib marketing, purported nationwide class (filed Dec 2025).
8. General labor/employment lawsuits — joint employer allegations under Title VII, FLSA, etc.

**Concluded cases (12 named):** Settlements ranging from $2,500 to $33,500,000. Notable: Washington settlement ($33.5M for 13 franchises); Lentini ($22M for 6 franchises — age discrimination); Quijano ($6.3M for 10 Puerto Rico franchises); Ochoa ($3.75M labor settlement); Bramante (CAD $1.44M Happy Meal toys).

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8-11)

### Item 8 — Restrictions on Sources of Products and Services
McDonald's does not generally sell equipment/food/supplies to franchisees (except ongoing restaurant sales). Franchisees must deal only with McDonald's-approved suppliers and agencies. Supplier approval requires: ability to meet specs consistently, protect confidential info, production/delivery capability, integrity of ownership, financial soundness, legal compliance. McDonald's may elect not to approve if sufficient suppliers exist.

Required purchases from McDonald's: Operator's Lease (rent), Sesame POS software license + maintenance, technology platform fees.

Revenue from franchisee purchases: In 2024, McDonald's received $39,008,767 in loan guarantee fees, cashless incentives, and beverage supplier rebates. Revenue from sale/lease of real estate and services to franchisees was ~$7.21B (68% of $10.63B total revenue).

Approximate required purchases: 90-95% of establishment costs; 55-65% of ongoing operating purchases.

### Item 9 — Franchisee's Obligations
Standard FDD table format covering 24 obligation categories (a through x). Key items: site selection by McDonald's (not franchisee); pre-opening purchases per specs; training per program; fees per Items 5/6/7; compliance with O&T Manual; trademark usage; product restrictions; insurance; advertising; indemnification; owner participation; records/reports; inspections/audits; transfer restrictions; no renewal right; post-termination obligations; non-competition; dispute resolution. STO operators may maintain common areas and obtain utilities/insurance for fuel facility operator.

### Item 10 — Financing
No direct financing from McDonald's. Operator's Lease is a standard commercial lease, not financing. BFL provides conditional option to purchase restaurant assets.

McDonald's predecessor may guarantee Bank of America loans for: remodeling, acquisitions, BFL option exercise, other approved purposes. Terms: SOFR + 0.10% + 3.00% (APR 7.42% as of March 2025). Terms: 3-5 years, 7-year amortization. Secured by restaurant equipment/seating/signage/decor/inventory. Personal guarantee required (franchisee + spouse). Default = default under Franchise Agreement. Must sign Operator Assistance Program Agreement (waive claims against McDonald's).

### Item 11 — Franchisor's Assistance, Advertising, Computer Systems, and Training

**Pre-opening obligations:** McDonald's constructs/prepares premises; prescribes specifications; provides training program; allows viewing of O&T Manual before purchase.

**Operational obligations:** Periodic advice/consultation; new developments/techniques communication via visits, reports, seminars, newsletters, emails, online resources; O&T Manual access.

**Advertising:** No McDonald's advertising fund. Franchisee must spend min 4% of Gross Sales on advertising/promotion. OPNAD (national, currently 2.25% pledge) and local cooperatives are independent entities. McOpCo participates on same basis. OPNAD audited annually.

**Computer Systems (Store Systems):** Cost $150,000-$250,000. Includes POS (Sesame), Cashless 3.0, digital menu boards, kiosks, table locators, hardware, software. Transaction data transmitted to McDonald's with independent access. No contractual limits on upgrade frequency or cost.

**Site Selection:** McDonald's selects all sites. Franchisee does not select or approve sites.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
No exclusive territory. No protected territory. No right to exclude, control, or impose conditions on future locations. McDonald's may establish outlets that compete with franchisee's location. McDonald's reserves the right to use marks in any channel (e.g., McCafe packaged coffee in supermarkets, e-commerce). Internal development policies are not part of the Franchise Agreement and do not create contract rights.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
McDonald's does not own the marks. Licensed from affiliate Restaurant Brands, LLC. Principal marks: Golden Arches Logo (Reg. 893,440, 1970), McDONALD'S name (Reg. 743,572, 1963), combined mark (Reg. 1,287,408, 1984). License is 20-year with auto-renewals. No current infringement proceedings or challenges. Franchisee cannot use marks as part of operating company name.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, and Proprietary Information
No patents required to be disclosed. Copyrights claimed in O&T Manual, menus, advertising materials — not registered. Licensed from predecessor. Trade secrets include methods, specifications, marketing plans, real estate plans, recipes, formulae, customer programs. Strict confidentiality required. Financial Information disclosure restricted (anti-leaking provision for pre-public-reporting periods).

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
Full-time best efforts and personal on-premises supervision required (Franchise Agreement paragraphs 1(e) and 13).

### Item 16 — Restrictions on What Franchisee May Sell
Only McDonald's-authorized products. Only prescribed packaging, ingredients, methods. McDonald's may add, delete, or change required products without limit. Must operate in conformity with entire McDonald's System at all times. No limitations on customers, but only at specific franchised location.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- Traditional: 20 years. STO/STR: 10 years. Satellite: varies. BFL: 3 years.
- **No right to renew or extend.** New Term Policy (Exhibit K) is discretionary, not a contract right, subject to change at McDonald's sole discretion.
- Under current New Term Policy: percentage rent for new term will not be lower than previous term. If current rate below 8.50%, may be raised to 8.50%. May increase if McDonald's made additional investments or temporary reductions don't extend to new term.
- BFL: conditional option to purchase certain assets after first year, extending franchise up to 20 years.

### Transfer
- Requires McDonald's approval. Includes direct, indirect, or contingent transfer of any interest.
- Conditions: new franchisee qualifies; royalty increases to current fee (except for specified situations); new franchisee assumes full liability; original franchisee remains personally liable for remainder of term; no current breach.
- McDonald's has right of first refusal to match any offer for business (Section 15(c)).
- McDonald's can purchase business only if managing restaurant 1 year after death/disability; can purchase certain assets upon termination.

### Termination
- McDonald's can terminate only with cause (Sections 18, 19).
- Non-curable defaults: failure to maintain restaurant standards; bankruptcy; non-payment >30 days; judgment >$5,000 outstanding >30 days; loss of possession; franchise restriction violation; sale of unapproved/non-conforming foods; unauthorized transfer; denied access; unpaid undisputed invoices; misrepresentation; conduct damaging reputation; felony conviction; intentional under-reporting of sales; repeated breaches.
- Upon termination/non-renewal: 30 days to sell McDonald's furniture/fixtures/signs/equipment at fair market value (no payment for intangibles); return manuals and confidential materials; cease using system and trademarks.

### Non-Competition
- During term: no involvement in competing or similar business (Section 11(a)).
- Post-term: no competing business for 18 months within 10 miles, including after assignment or sale (Section 11(b)).

### Death/Disability
- Franchise may be assigned to approved purchaser or qualified spouse/heirs/nearest blood relative. McDonald's will work with spouse up to 18 months for training (not obligated).

### Dispute Resolution
- No formal arbitration or mediation required in Franchise Agreement.
- Governing law: Illinois (with specific exceptions).
- No choice of forum specified in agreement, but Special Risks page notes disputes resolved in Illinois.
- McDonald's has "open door policy" and Ombudsman process; mediation used as common practice (not required).

### Modification
- No modifications generally, but O&T Manual subject to change.
- Integration/merger clause: only Franchise Agreement terms binding (subject to state law).
