# Final Diligence Report — McDonald's USA, LLC
## FDD Filing 638437-2025 | Issuance Date: May 1, 2025 (amended February 1, 2026)

---

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and structure**: McDonald's operates approximately 13,559 U.S. restaurants as of December 31, 2024, of which 12,887 (95%) are franchised to independent franchisees and 672 (5%) are company-owned (McOpCo). The system added a net 102 outlets in 2024, reversing near-stagnation in 2022 (+19) and 2023 (+2). McDonald's projects 195 gross new openings in 2025 (181 franchised, 14 company-owned).

2. **Entity and ownership**: The franchisor is McDonald's USA, LLC, a Delaware limited liability company formed in 2004 and wholly owned by McDonald's Corporation. The predecessor (McDonald's Corporation) began franchising in 1955 and transferred the majority of U.S. assets to the LLC in 2005. Neither entity has offered franchises in any other line of business.

3. **Investment range**: Total initial investment for a traditional McDonald's franchise ranges from $1,471,000 to $2,728,000. This includes a $45,000 initial franchise fee and $1,100,000 to $1,785,000 for signs, seating, equipment, and decor. Store Systems (POS, kiosks, digital menu boards) account for $150,000 to $250,000 of the equipment cost. Nine of thirty-one McOpCo transactions in 2024 exceeded the published high-end range, some by more than $2 million.

4. **Fee burden**: Ongoing fees include a royalty of 4% or 5% of Gross Sales (5% for restaurants opened or acquired after January 1, 2024), percentage rent of 6% to 23% (escalating to a minimum of 11.50% from year 8), base rent, pass-through rent, mandatory advertising expenditure of at least 4% of Gross Sales, and technology fees aggregating approximately $10,000+ per restaurant per year. The total occupancy + royalty burden can exceed 20% of gross sales, creating significant cash flow pressure for lower-volume locations.

5. **Item 19 financial performance**: Average annual sales for all domestic traditional restaurants open at least one year were $4,002,000 in 2024 (median $3,838,000). Franchised-only average was $3,966,000 (median $3,797,000). McOpCo average was substantially higher at $4,793,000. Pro forma operating income before occupancy costs (excluding rent, royalty, D&A, interest, and taxes) was $734,000 at $3M sales (24.5%), $812,000 at $3.2M (25.4%), and $879,000 at $3.4M (25.9%). The lowest annual sales volume was $914,000.

6. **Financial health**: McDonald's USA, LLC generated total revenue of $10.63 billion and net income of $3.46 billion in 2024. Total assets were $22.2 billion. Ernst & Young issued a clean (unqualified) audit opinion. However, the entity distributes 100% of net income as dividends to its parent each year and carried $2.4 billion in intercompany debt (subsequently converted to equity in February 2025). Cash on hand was only $34.6 million.

7. **Growth trajectory**: After years of minimal net growth, McDonald's accelerated expansion in 2024 with 167 new franchised outlets opened and only 52 closures/terminations/non-renewals/reacquisitions. This represents the strongest organic growth in the reported three-year period.

8. **Territory and renewal risk**: The franchise grants no exclusive territory, no protected territory, and no contractual right to renew. The New Term Policy is discretionary, subject to change, and not a contract right. Under current policy, rent may increase at renewal.

9. **Litigation**: Multiple significant pending cases including race discrimination by 77 former Black franchisees, antitrust claims related to no-poach provisions, and an E. coli class action. Concluded settlements total tens of millions of dollars, including purchases of franchisee operations at substantial premiums.

10. **Gag clause**: The FDD discloses that current and former franchisees may have signed provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with McDonald's.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

McDonald's USA, LLC is a Delaware limited liability company with its principal office at 110 N. Carpenter Street, Chicago, Illinois 60607. It is the sole U.S. franchisor and has been since 2005 when McDonald's Corporation transferred the majority of U.S. operations to this subsidiary. The company does not own the McDonald's trademarks; they are licensed from an affiliate, Restaurant Brands, LLC, under a renewable 20-year license.

The franchise system encompasses five restaurant types: traditional (20-year term, full menu), Satellite (variable term, scaled-down menu, strip centers/airports/universities/hospitals), STO/small town oil (10-year term, full menu in fuel station/convenience store), STR/small town retail (10-year term, anchoring small retail centers), and BFL/business facilities lease (3-year term with purchase option). Approximately 95% of U.S. restaurants are franchised to independent third parties; the remaining 5% are operated by wholly-owned McOpCo subsidiaries.

### Fee Stack

The fee structure is among the most complex in franchising, with multiple layered components:

**Royalty**: 5% of Gross Sales for restaurants opened, acquired via McOpCo sale, or acquired via right of first refusal after January 1, 2024. Legacy restaurants retain 4% for family transactions, new terms on existing restaurants, franchisee-to-franchisee sales, and rebuilds. This dual-rate system means the effective systemwide royalty rate is transitioning upward over time.

**Rent (Traditional)**: A three-component structure: (a) Monthly Base Rent, calculated by applying a finance factor to McDonald's total land and building investment; (b) Pass-Through Rent for landlord escalations on leased sites; and (c) Percentage Rent of 6% to 23% of Gross Sales. The Percentage Rent increases during the first seven years, reaching a minimum of 11.50% from year 8. The total change generally does not exceed 3.50 percentage points over the term. A co-investment program allows franchisees to reduce Percentage Rent in 0.25% increments at a cost of approximately $30,000+ per quarter percent.

**Advertising**: Minimum 4% of Gross Sales, including contributions to OPNAD (currently 2.25% pledge) and local advertising cooperatives. Not payable to McDonald's. OPNAD and cooperatives are independent entities with elected/designated representatives.

**Technology fees**: Over 20 line items totaling approximately $10,000+ per restaurant annually. Key components include Sesame POS ($1,133/yr + $2,600 one-time), Deployment/OTP/Execution ($2,529/yr), Network Management ($1,134/yr), and numerous other platform fees. All fees are subject to periodic review and may increase. Some are optional (HHOT, PDW Reporting, McD Connect, Pricing Engine).

**Other fees**: Satellite annual fee ($500-$2,500), relocation contribution ($50,000), audit/inspection fee (cost of audit if understatement ≥2%).

### Initial Investment

The total initial investment for a traditional McDonald's restaurant ranges from $1,471,000 to $2,728,000. The largest component is signs, seating, equipment, and decor at $1,100,000 to $1,785,000, which includes Store Systems at $150,000 to $250,000. Additional funds for the first three months range from $250,000 to $439,000. Real estate and building costs (three months' rent) can add $0 to $313,000 in base rent plus percentage rent of 0% to 29%.

The FDD notably discloses that 9 of 31 McOpCo transactions in 2024 exceeded the published high-end investment range, with overages ranging from $65,593 to $2,035,816. This suggests the published range may understate actual acquisition costs for existing restaurants.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

McDonald's does not generally sell equipment, food, or supplies to franchisees directly (except when selling ongoing restaurant businesses). However, franchisees must deal exclusively with McDonald's-approved suppliers and agencies. The approval process evaluates manufacturing capability, confidentiality protection, production/delivery capacity, ownership integrity, financial soundness, and legal compliance. McDonald's may decline to approve new suppliers if sufficient suppliers already exist.

Required purchases represent approximately 90-95% of establishment costs and 55-65% of ongoing operating purchases. In 2024, McDonald's received $39 million in loan guarantee fees, cashless incentives, and beverage supplier rebates. Revenue from sale/lease of real estate and services to franchisees was approximately $7.21 billion, representing 68% of the company's $10.63 billion total revenue.

### Training Program

The Candidate Development Program is extensive, potentially taking 6 months to two years to complete. It includes a Restaurant Learning Path (crew development, area management, shift leader, department leader, restaurant leader) and a Business Learning Path (leading people, marketing, finance, operations, business planning). The culminating course is the 4-day Leading Great Restaurants Class at Hamburger University in Chicago. McDonald's bears training center costs; the franchisee bears travel, living, and compensation expenses. Training must be completed before signing the Franchise Agreement or paying any money.

### Technology Stack

Store Systems cost $150,000 to $250,000 and include the Sesame POS platform, Cashless 3.0 system, digital menu boards, self-ordering kiosks, table locators, and related hardware/software. Transaction-level data is transmitted to McDonald's, which has independent access with no contractual limits. There are no contractual limitations on the frequency or cost of required upgrades. Franchisees must submit monthly financial statements electronically via the Franchisee Financial System (FFS).

### Marketing Fund

McDonald's does not maintain an advertising fund. Franchisees must spend at least 4% of Gross Sales on advertising annually. OPNAD (national fund, currently 2.25% pledge) and local cooperatives are independent entities controlled by franchisee-elected representatives. The OPNAD fund is independently audited annually. McDonald's provides certain marketing, legal, and accounting personnel to OPNAD without charge.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory

There is no exclusive territory, no protected territory, and no right to exclude, control, or impose conditions on future McDonald's development. The Franchise Agreement contains only a limited grant of authority to operate at one specific location. Customer trading patterns may change due to ongoing development of the marketing network, and McDonald's expressly reserves the right to establish competing outlets.

### Channel Restrictions

McDonald's reserves the right to use its marks in any channel of distribution. The FDD specifically notes that McCafe packaged coffee is sold in supermarkets and other retail outlets and via e-commerce. McDonald's also reserves the right to sell similar goods and services under different trademarks. Internal development policies are not part of the Franchise Agreement and do not create contract rights.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The traditional franchise term is 20 years. STO/STR terms are 10 years. Satellite terms vary. BFL terms are 3 years with a conditional purchase option. The franchisee has no contractual right to renew or extend the franchise. The New Term Policy (Exhibit K) is a discretionary internal policy, not a contract right, and is subject to change at McDonald's sole discretion. Under current policy, Percentage Rent for a new term will not be lower than the previous term and may increase to a minimum of 8.50% or higher in specified circumstances. This creates the risk of a "materially different" arrangement at renewal.

### Transfer Provisions

All transfers (direct, indirect, or contingent) require McDonald's approval. Conditions include: new franchisee must qualify; royalty increases to the current rate (except in specified circumstances); new franchisee assumes full unconditional liability; original franchisee remains personally liable for the remainder of the term; no current breach exists. McDonald's maintains a right of first refusal to match any offer for the business. Upon death or disability, the franchise may be assigned to an approved purchaser or qualified family member, with McDonald's working with the spouse for up to 18 months.

### Termination and Death/Disability

McDonald's can terminate only with cause, which includes failure to maintain standards, bankruptcy, non-payment exceeding 30 days, outstanding judgment exceeding $5,000 for more than 30 days, loss of possession, sale of unapproved products, unauthorized transfer, denial of access, misrepresentation, reputation-damaging conduct, felony conviction, intentional under-reporting of sales, or repeated breaches. Upon termination or non-renewal, the franchisee has 30 days to sell furniture, fixtures, signs, and equipment to McDonald's at fair market value (with no payment for intangible assets).

### Non-Competition

During the term: no involvement in any competing or similar business. Post-term: no competing business for 18 months within 10 miles, including after assignment or sale. The North Dakota addendum notes that non-competes are generally unenforceable in that state but McDonald's will seek to enforce them to the extent permitted.

### Dispute Resolution

The Franchise Agreement does not require formal arbitration or mediation. As a matter of common practice, McDonald's often agrees to mediation after internal processes (open door policy, Ombudsman) are exhausted. Governing law is Illinois. The Special Risks page notes that out-of-state dispute resolution may force less favorable settlements and higher costs. State addenda for Minnesota and Maryland provide additional procedural protections.

### Litigation History

The FDD discloses 8 pending cases and 12 concluded cases. The most significant pending matter is Crawford v. McDonald's, in which 77 former Black franchisees allege systemic race discrimination, bad faith breach, and fraudulent inducement. Two antitrust cases (Deslandes, Turner) challenge no-poach provisions in franchise agreements that restricted employee mobility between McDonald's locations. The Kytch case involves allegations that McDonald's made false statements about a third-party ice cream machine diagnostic device. The Williams case is an E. coli class action arising from Quarter Pounder contamination. Concluded settlements include purchases of franchisee operations for $6.5 million to $33.5 million.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Sales Volume Distribution

McDonald's discloses three population tiers for annual sales volumes of domestic traditional restaurants open at least one year as of December 31, 2024:

**All traditional (~12,572 restaurants)**: Average $4,002,000; median $3,838,000; range $914,000 to $19,680,000. Approximately 79% exceeded $3 million; 72% exceeded $3.2 million; 65% exceeded $3.4 million.

**Franchised only (~12,023 restaurants)**: Average $3,966,000; median $3,797,000; same high/low range. Approximately 78% exceeded $3 million; 71% exceeded $3.2 million; 64% exceeded $3.4 million.

**McOpCo only (~549 restaurants)**: Average $4,793,000; median $4,612,000; range $1,820,000 to $10,030,000. Approximately 96% exceeded $3 million. The significantly higher McOpCo averages suggest company-owned locations are selected for stronger performance characteristics.

### Pro Forma Operating Statements

Based on 11,332 independent franchisee traditional restaurants (excluding McOpCo, Satellites, and restaurants that changed owners in 2024):

At $3,000,000 in product sales: Cost of Sales 28.3%, Other Operating Expenses 47.2%, Operating Income Before Occupancy Costs $734,000 (24.5%). At $3,200,000: OIBOC $812,000 (25.4%). At $3,400,000: OIBOC $879,000 (25.9%). The percentages improve with scale, suggesting operating leverage.

Attainment rates: 76% exceeded $734,000 OIBOC; 69% exceeded $812,000; 62% exceeded $879,000.

### Critical Caveats

The pro forma statements exclude rent, royalty, depreciation and amortization, interest, and income taxes. The royalties payable during the covered period were 4% (new restaurants now pay 5%). Effective rent percentages in 2024 ranged from 0.00% to 33.33%. After deducting a combined royalty and rent burden that could approach 20-30% of gross sales for some locations, the remaining cash flow for debt service, depreciation, and owner return may be significantly compressed, particularly for below-average-volume locations.

## H. Item 21 — Financial Statements

### Auditor and Opinion

Ernst & Young LLP issued an unqualified (clean) audit opinion dated March 14, 2025, on the consolidated financial statements of McDonald's USA, LLC for the three years ended December 31, 2024.

### Key Financial Highlights

**Revenue**: Total revenue grew from $9.59 billion (2022) to $10.57 billion (2023) to $10.63 billion (2024). Franchised restaurant revenues ($7.21 billion in 2024) represent 68% of total revenue, reflecting the real-estate-heavy franchise model.

**Profitability**: Operating income before royalty to parent was $5.73 billion (2024), representing a 54% operating margin. After a 2% royalty to McDonald's Corporation ($1.07 billion), operating income was $4.66 billion. Net income was $3.46 billion after interest ($159 million) and taxes ($1.04 billion). The effective tax rate was 23.2%.

**Balance Sheet**: Total assets of $22.2 billion are dominated by net property and equipment ($13.1 billion), lease right-of-use assets ($5.8 billion), and goodwill ($1.5 billion). Cash was minimal at $34.6 million. Members' equity was $10.2 billion, unchanged over the three-year period because all net income is distributed as dividends.

**Cash Flows**: Cash from operations was $4.55 billion (2024). Capital expenditures were $1.06 billion. Dividends to parent were $3.46 billion (equal to net income). The entity functions as a cash-flow pass-through to McDonald's Corporation.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **100% dividend payout**: Retained earnings remain flat at $4,633.9 million across all three years. All net income is immediately upstreamed to McDonald's Corporation.

2. **Intercompany debt**: The company had $2.4 billion in payable arrangements to McDonald's Corporation, bearing interest at the 12-month swap rate plus 1%. These matured February 17, 2025, and were converted to equity, eliminating the obligation.

3. **Massive real estate portfolio**: Property and equipment at cost totaled $22.9 billion. Net franchise property was $11.5 billion (including $3.9 billion in land). Future minimum rent receivable from franchisees totaled $14.2 billion.

4. **Lease portfolio**: Lease right-of-use assets of $5.8 billion (operating $4.8 billion, finance $1.0 billion). Total lease liabilities of $6.0 billion. Weighted average remaining lease term: 17 years (operating), 28 years (finance). Total future lease payments: $9.0 billion.

5. **Restaurant economics**: Franchised restaurant revenues of $7.21 billion break down as rents ($5.17 billion), royalties ($2.01 billion), and initial fees ($25.4 million). Company-owned restaurant costs include food/paper ($925.7 million), payroll ($1.11 billion), and occupancy ($746.5 million).

6. **Restructuring**: "Accelerating the Organization" initiative generated $63.6 million in cumulative restructuring charges. Remaining accrual of $4.4 million expected to be paid within 12 months.

## I. State Addenda Summary

Five states have specific addenda in Exhibit T:

**California**: Requires disclosure of the Fast Food Act (AB 1228), which may set minimum wages, working hours, and other employment standards. McDonald's does not provide training or assistance related to the Act; compliance is solely the franchisee's responsibility.

**Maryland**: Releases required for renewal/transfer do not apply to Maryland Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law liabilities. Termination upon insolvency may not be enforceable under federal bankruptcy law. Franchisee may bring Maryland Franchise Law claims in Maryland. Claims must be brought within 3 years of franchise grant.

**Minnesota**: 90 days' notice of termination (with 60 days to cure) and 180 days' notice of non-renewal required. Transfer consent will not be unreasonably withheld. Minnesota law governs franchisee rights under Minn. Stat. Ch. 80C.

**North Dakota**: Non-competition covenants are generally unenforceable. North Dakota law governs claims arising under North Dakota Franchise Investment Law.

**Washington**: McDonald's entered an Assurance of Discontinuance with the Washington Attorney General, agreeing to remove no-poach provisions from franchise agreements and not enforce existing ones. No fines or penalties assessed.

Fourteen states require registration: CA, HI, IL, IN, MD, MI, MN, NY, ND, RI, SD, VA, WA, WI. All show effective dates of May 1, 2025 (or close) as amended February 1, 2026.

## J. Unresolveds

1. **McOpCo performance gap** — McOpCo restaurants average $4,793,000 vs. franchised $3,966,000, a gap of $827,000 (21%). The FDD does not explain whether this reflects site quality differences, operational differences, or selection bias. Severity: medium. Source: Item 19, pages 43.

2. **Percentage rent opacity** — The specific Percentage Rent for a new restaurant is provided before signing but not disclosed in the FDD. The 6%-23% range is very wide. Actual rent burden is unknowable from the FDD alone. Severity: medium. Source: Item 6, page 21.

3. **Co-investment refund mechanism** — Under the second co-investment option (floor 12.50%), a partial refund is available if the restaurant does not achieve projected sales by year 3. The refund mechanism's specifics are not disclosed in the FDD. Severity: low. Source: Item 6, page 23.

4. **Operator Assistance Program waiver** — Franchisees who use McDonald's-guaranteed Bank of America loans must sign an Operator Assistance Program Agreement that includes a waiver of all claims against McDonald's. The scope of this waiver is not fully disclosed in the FDD body (documents in Exhibit N). Severity: medium. Source: Item 10, page 30.

5. **Administrative costs not charged** — McDonald's Corporation provides certain administrative functions to McDonald's USA, LLC without charge, and these costs are not reflected in the financial statements. The magnitude is undisclosed. Severity: low. Source: Exhibit A notes, page 71.
