## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: Zero franchise outlets as of December 31, 2024. Two company-owned outlets (Urbandale, Iowa since Oct 2022; Albuquerque, New Mexico since May 2024). Projecting 3 new franchised and 2 new company-owned outlets in next fiscal year.

2. **Entity structure**: Franchisor is Home Halo Franchising, LLC, a Delaware LLC formed March 14, 2024. IP held by affiliate Homecare Hub IP, LLC. Four operating affiliates in Iowa, New Mexico, Colorado, and Wisconsin (operating or pending). Sole principal: Daniel Deak, CEO/Founder.

3. **Ownership history**: Entity is less than one year old at time of financial statement (formed March 2024, FY ending Dec 2024). No predecessors. The affiliated businesses operated under the "Homecare Hub" brand before rebranding to "Home Halo" around December 31, 2024.

4. **Investment range**: $92,900–$157,300 for a single territory (250,000 population). $134,550–$311,650 for multiple territories (2–5 territories). Initial franchise fee of $49,000 (single), with multi-territory discounts.

5. **Fee burden**: Combined ongoing fees of approximately 9%+ of Gross Sales before contact center implementation — 5% royalty + 2% Brand Development Fund + 2% local marketing minimum. Technology fee adds $250/month (up to $750). Care management system adds $10–$16/client/month. If contact center is implemented, an additional 3% of Gross Sales. Minimum weekly royalties escalate to $1,010/week per territory at month 73+, creating a floor regardless of sales. Total effective fee burden on a mature single-territory outlet is estimated at 11–17% of Gross Sales depending on client volume.

6. **Item 19 highlights**: One company-owned outlet (Urbandale, Iowa) generated $970,528 in 2024 gross sales (up from $456,373 in 2023). Direct gross profit after all disclosed and franchise-related expenses: $278,016 (28.65% margin). Outlet operates in a 2-territory equivalent market and primarily serves VA-referred clients. No franchise outlet data exists.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: This is a brand-new franchise system with zero operating franchisees. The financial statements show total assets of $29,513 — the cover page itself warns that financial condition "calls into question the franchisor's financial ability to provide services and support." The primary trademarks are not federally registered. This is an inherently high-risk investment.

8. **Financial health**: Franchisor generated $55,013 in revenue (royalties from Iowa affiliate), net income of $24,668. Total assets of $29,513 (all cash). No debt but also no reserves, no receivables, no fixed assets. Shareholders' contributions of only $4,845. Extremely thin capitalization.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Two company-owned outlets, zero franchised. Projecting 3 new franchised + 2 company-owned in next fiscal year. The Iowa outlet doubled gross sales year-over-year (from ~$456K to ~$971K). The business model appears to scale through client/caregiver volume rather than physical expansion.

10. **Territory**: Not exclusive but protected from same-brand encroachment during term. Significant reserved rights including alternative distribution channels, acquisitions under different marks, and national accounts within your territory. 10% royalty on out-of-territory customers.

11. **Renewal**: One 10-year renewal on "then current" terms that may be "materially different." Requires general release, renewal fee (≥$7,500), 180-day notice.

12. **Disputes**: Non-binding mediation then binding arbitration in Arapahoe County, Colorado. Colorado law governs. Multiple state addenda override forum/choice of law for their residents.

13. **Special risks**: Six cover-page risk factors — out-of-state disputes, spousal liability, mandatory minimums, financial support concern, short operating history, unregistered trademarks. All are material and explicitly flagged by the franchisor.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Home Halo Franchising, LLC is a newly formed Delaware LLC (March 14, 2024) headquartered in Greenwood Village, Colorado. The franchisor has been offering franchises since April 16, 2024. The sole officer disclosed is Daniel Deak, who serves as CEO and Founder and also serves as President of all four operating affiliates.

The intellectual property (Licensed Marks) is owned by a separate affiliate, Homecare Hub IP, LLC, which licenses the marks to the franchisor under a 20-year license with automatic renewal. The operating affiliates operate businesses similar to the franchised business:
- Homecare Hub of Iowa, LLC — operating since October 2022 (the FPR data source)
- Homecare Hub of New Mexico, LLC — operating since May 2024
- Homecare Hub of Colorado, LLC — obtaining licensure
- Homecare Hub of Wisconsin, LLC — anticipates opening 2025

The franchised business is a non-medical at-home care operation providing seniors and veterans with daily living assistance, companionship, and related services. The business can potentially be operated from a home-based office with franchisor approval.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Source-restricted purchases represent approximately 5% of both establishment and ongoing costs. Required restricted-source items include System Supplies (uniforms, branded items), marketing materials, Business Management System, computer equipment, credit card processing, and insurance. Currently, neither the franchisor nor its affiliates serve as approved suppliers.

New supplier approval takes up to 60 days. The franchisor may charge a supplier review fee. The franchisor may designate itself or any third party as exclusive supplier at any time, "irrespective of the existence of competing suppliers."

During FY2024, the franchisor received no revenue from supplier rebates but reserves the right to implement rebate programs in the future.

### Training Program Detail
108 total hours (84 classroom, 24 OJT) over 10–16 weeks:
- **Pre-training (online, 44 hours)**: 1:1 consultation calls, operations manual workshop, business infrastructure, territory planning
- **New Franchisee Training (Greenwood Village, CO, 40 hours)**: Non-medical home care overview, care management system, business development, client acquisition (pipeline, intake, consultation, closing), client management (care plans, scheduling), caregiver acquisition (recruiting, interviews, onboarding, retention), operations (matching, scheduling, billing, payroll), finance best practices
- **Post-training (franchisee's territory, 24 OJT hours)**: Caregiver acquisition workshop, client acquisition workshop, operations workshop, community networking workshop

Lead instructor: Daniel Deak, CEO. Training offered quarterly. No charge for franchisee + 1 manager; $500 per additional attendee.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition and Carve-Outs
The territory is a designated geographic area of approximately 250,000 population per territory. The territory is NOT exclusive, but during the term and provided the franchisee is not in default, the franchisor will not open another Home Halo Agency or grant another franchise within the Operating Territory.

However, the franchisor retains extremely broad reserved rights:
1. Operate or franchise outside your territory regardless of proximity
2. Acquire or develop any business (even identical services) under different marks within your territory
3. Use Licensed Marks for alternative distribution (internet, catalog, telemarketing) within your territory
4. Use Licensed Marks for local/regional/national accounts within your territory
5. Engage in any activity not expressly prohibited

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights
- Franchisee must operate exclusively within Operating Territory
- Cannot directly solicit outside Operating Territory
- May serve customers in "Open Areas" (unassigned areas within 10-mile radius) with approval, but at 10% royalty rate
- Must surrender Open Area customers when area becomes assigned to another franchisee
- Corporate Accounts: Franchisor negotiates rates and terms; franchisee may participate or decline, but if declining, franchisor can service accounts in your territory through others
- No right to additional locations, no options, no rights of first refusal

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 10 years
- **Renewal**: One additional 10-year term
- **Renewal conditions**: Not in default, 180 days prior written notice, sign then-current Franchise Agreement (which may contain terms **materially different** from current agreement), general release, pay renewal fee (greater of $7,500 or 15% of then-current IFF), personal guarantee of renewal agreement by all owners and spouses
- **"Materially different" risk**: The renewal agreement is explicitly described as potentially containing materially different terms, giving the franchisor significant leverage at renewal

### Transfer Provisions
- All transfers require prior written consent, "which may be granted or withheld in our discretion"
- 30 days prior written notice required
- Conditions: not in default, transferee bound by agreement, transferee owners/spouses guarantee, general release from franchisee and all owners/spouses, assets transferred, transferee completes training, franchisor waives ROFR, transfer fee paid
- Transfer fee: $10,000 (to existing franchisee), $20,000 (to new party), up to then-current IFF (if franchisor procures the transferee)
- Franchisor has right of first refusal to match any purchase offer

### Termination and Death/Disability
- **Termination by franchisee**: Only if franchisor is in material breach and fails to cure within 30 days
- **Termination by franchisor**: With cause only (no termination without cause)
  - 10-day cure for payment defaults
  - 30-day cure for operational defaults (site, standards, insurance, compliance)
  - Non-curable: 3+ curable defaults (even if each was cured), intentional refusal to comply, health/safety violation, abandonment, material misstatement, false records, unauthorized transfer, confidentiality breach, dishonest conduct, breach of Owner/Spouse Agreement, failure to complete training, misuse of marks, insolvency/bankruptcy, unsatisfied judgment (30 days), dissolution, foreclosure
- **Death/disability**: 30 days to appoint Operating Manager, 60 days for training, 12 months to complete transfer to approved transferee

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term**: No involvement in any competitive business. Full confidentiality, non-disclosure, and non-solicitation.
- **Post-term**: 24 months, no involvement in any competing business within: (a) Operating Territory, (b) 25-mile radius of Operating Territory, (c) the Operating Territory of any other Home Halo Agency. Period tolled during non-compliance. Required of all owners and spouses.
- **State overrides**: California — post-term noncompete may not be enforceable. North Dakota — noncompetes generally unenforceable. Washington — noncompete void unless employee earns >$100K/year.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue**: Non-binding mediation, then binding arbitration, in Arapahoe County, Colorado
- **Governing law**: Colorado
- **Exceptions**: Claims for injunctive relief
- **Limitations of claims**: Except for payment claims, enforcement of restrictive covenants, and IP claims, all claims must be brought within 1 year of discovery or 2 years of the act/omission, whichever is earlier
- **State overrides**:
  - Michigan: Cannot require litigation outside state
  - Minnesota: Cannot require litigation outside state
  - North Dakota: Arbitration site must be agreeable to both parties, not remote from franchisee
  - Rhode Island: Forum selection outside state void for RI Franchise Act claims
  - Washington: Arbitration must be in WA or mutually agreed

## I. State Addenda Summary

### States with Addenda
California, Connecticut, Hawaii, Illinois, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Virginia, Washington, Wisconsin (13 states total).

### Material Overrides by State

**California**: Post-term noncompete may not be enforceable under CA law. Arbitration in Colorado — franchisees encouraged to consult counsel on enforceability. Colorado choice of law may not be enforceable. General release at renewal/transfer cannot waive CA Franchise Investment Law rights. Bankruptcy termination provision may not be enforceable under federal bankruptcy law.

**Connecticut**: Business opportunity disclosure required.

**Hawaii**: Transfer and renewal releases must preserve rights under Hawaii Franchise Investment Law. If franchise agreement conflicts with Hawaii law, Hawaii law controls for non-renewal/termination/transfer.

**Illinois**: Any waiver of claims under Illinois Franchise Disclosure Act is void. Illinois law governs the franchise relationship. Jurisdiction in Illinois courts for IL law claims.

**Maryland**: General release does not apply to Maryland Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law claims. Franchisee may bring lawsuit in Maryland. 3-year statute of limitations from grant of franchise. Bankruptcy termination may not be enforceable.

**Michigan**: Standard MCL 445.1527 protections — termination only for good cause (with 30-day cure), fair market value compensation on non-renewal (if term < 5 years and franchisee prohibited from operating similar business), no mandatory out-of-state litigation, transfer refusal only for good cause, escrow of initial investment if franchisor net worth < $100K.

**Minnesota**: 90-day notice of termination (with 60-day cure) and 180-day notice of non-renewal required. No prospective general release of claims subject to MN Franchise Act. Cannot require litigation outside Minnesota. NSF fee capped at $30. Franchisor must reimburse franchisee's trademark defense costs.

**New York**: Transfer and renewal releases must preserve rights under NY General Business Law §§ 680–695. Choice of law and forum provisions do not waive NY GBL rights. NY prospectus required for offers/sales to NY domiciled buyers.

**North Dakota**: Noncompetes generally considered unenforceable. Arbitration/mediation site must be agreeable to all parties, not remote from franchisee's business. Forum selection outside ND void. General release on renewal determined unfair/inequitable. Choice of law (non-ND) determined unfair. Jury trial waiver determined unfair. No consent to termination or liquidated damages required.

**Rhode Island**: Forum selection restricting venue to outside RI void for RI Franchise Investment Act claims. General release void for Act claims.

**Virginia**: Termination without reasonable cause unlawful under Virginia Retail Franchising Act.

**Washington**: WA Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails in any conflict. Arbitration/mediation must be in Washington or mutually agreed. Release cannot include WA Act rights. Transfer fees limited to franchisor's reasonable estimated or actual costs. Noncompete void for employees earning < $100K/year (adjusted for inflation). No-hire restrictions between franchisees void.

**Wisconsin**: Wisconsin Fair Dealership Law may affect termination provisions.
