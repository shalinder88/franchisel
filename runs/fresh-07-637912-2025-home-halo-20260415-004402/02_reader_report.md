## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Early-stage franchisor**: Home Halo Franchising, LLC was formed March 14, 2024 (Delaware LLC); began offering franchises April 16, 2024. As of December 31, 2024, zero franchise outlets have opened. This is a pre-revenue franchise system.

2. **Entity structure**: Franchisor is Home Halo Franchising, LLC. IP held by affiliate Homecare Hub IP, LLC. Four operating affiliates: Homecare Hub of Iowa (operating since Oct 2022), Homecare Hub of New Mexico (since May 2024), Homecare Hub of Colorado (obtaining licensure), and Homecare Hub of Wisconsin (anticipates opening 2025).

3. **Sole principal**: Daniel Deak, CEO and Founder. Previously Director of Training/Business Development at FirstLight Home Care Franchising (Sept 2019–Oct 2022). No other officers disclosed in Item 2.

4. **Business model**: Non-medical at-home care for seniors and veterans — daily living assistance, companionship, homemaking. Home-based office permitted with franchisor approval. Territory of ~250,000 population.

5. **Investment range**: $92,900–$157,300 (single territory); $134,550–$311,650 (multiple territories up to 5). Initial franchise fee: $49,000 (single), with multi-territory discounts. 10% veteran discount on first territory.

6. **Fee burden**: 5% royalty (or minimum weekly royalty escalating $144–$1,010/week), 2% Brand Development Fund, 2% local marketing (min $1,000/month/territory), up to $750/month technology fee (currently $250), $10–$16/client/month care management, potential 3% contact center fee (not yet implemented), 10% royalty on out-of-territory customers.

7. **Item 19 — FPR provided**: Based solely on one company-owned outlet in Urbandale, Iowa (population equivalent of 2 territories). 2024: $970,528 gross sales, 48.45% direct gross profit, 28.65% after disclosed expenses and franchise-related adjustments. 2023: $456,373 gross sales. Data is unaudited, not GAAP.

8. **Item 20 — Zero franchisees**: No franchise outlets opened, terminated, or transferred in any year (2022–2024). Two company-owned outlets at end of 2024 (Iowa, New Mexico). Projected openings: 3 franchised (FL, IA, NM) + 2 company-owned (CO, WI).

9. **Financial health — material concern**: Total assets $29,513 (all cash). Total revenue $55,013 (royalties from Iowa affiliate). Net income $24,668. No fixed assets, no liabilities. Shareholders' equity $29,513. Cover page risk factor #4 explicitly states financial condition "calls into question the franchisor's financial ability to provide services and support."

10. **Unregistered trademark**: Primary "Home Halo" marks are NOT federally registered — only pending USPTO applications (Serial Nos. 98273019, 98631620, 99021438). Cover page risk factor #6 flags this.

11. **Noncompete**: 24-month post-term, within operating territory + 25-mile radius + any other Home Halo territory. Spousal guarantee required.

12. **Dispute resolution**: Non-binding mediation then binding arbitration in Arapahoe County, Colorado. Colorado law governs. Subject to state-specific overrides.

13. **State addenda**: 13 states with material addenda — CA, CT, HI, IL, MD, MI, MN, NY, ND, RI, VA, WA, WI.

14. **Litigation/Bankruptcy**: None disclosed.

15. **Key risk flags**: (a) Brand-new system with zero franchisees, (b) financial weakness flagged on cover page, (c) unregistered trademarks, (d) mandatory minimum royalties regardless of sales, (e) spousal liability, (f) single principal with limited franchisor operating history, (g) heavy ongoing fee stack (~14%+ of gross sales before contact center).

## E. Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 10 years
- **Renewal**: One additional 10-year term
- **Renewal conditions**: Not in default, 180 days prior written notice, sign then-current form of Franchise Agreement (which may contain **materially different terms**), general release, renewal fee (greater of $7,500 or 15% of then-current IFF), owners must be in compliance, personal guarantee of renewal agreement

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: If in compliance and franchisor is in material breach and fails to cure within 30 days (or reasonable time if cannot cure in 30 days)
- **By franchisor with cause**:
  - 10-day cure: failure to pay fees/obligations
  - 30-day cure: failure to lease approved site, develop/open, comply with standards, maintain insurance, comply with system
  - **Non-curable**: 3+ curable defaults (even if cured), intentional refusal to comply, negligent operation creating health/safety threat, abandonment, material misstatement, false records, unauthorized transfer, disclosure of confidential info, dishonest conduct, breach of Owner/Spouse Agreement, failure to complete training, misuse of marks, insolvency/bankruptcy, judgment not satisfied in 30 days, dissolution, foreclosure

### Transfer
- Requires prior written consent (granted or withheld in franchisor's discretion)
- 30 days prior notice, not in default, transferee bound by agreement, transferee owners/spouses guarantee, general release, training, franchisor waives right of first refusal, transfer fee ($10K/$20K/up to current IFF)
- Franchisor has right of first refusal to match any offer

### Death/Disability
- Individual: 30 days to appoint Operating Manager, 60 days for training, 12 months to transfer
- Corporate Entity: 30 days to appoint replacement Operating Manager if other owners exist

### Post-Term Obligations
- Cease all operations, de-identify, return manuals, transfer data/telephone/digital media/web to franchisor

### Noncompete
- During term: no involvement in competitive business
- Post-term: 24 months, no involvement in competing business within Operating Territory, 25-mile radius, or any other Home Halo territory. Period tolled during non-compliance.

### Dispute Resolution
- Mediation (non-binding) → Arbitration (binding), Arapahoe County, Colorado
- Injunctive relief excepted
- Colorado law governs
- Subject to state-specific overrides

### Integration
- Only Franchise Agreement and signed exhibits binding; subject to state law. Does not disclaim FDD representations.
