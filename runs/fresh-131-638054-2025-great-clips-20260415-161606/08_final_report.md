## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System Size**: 4,439 franchised salons across the United States and Canada as of December 31, 2024 — the largest hair salon franchise system in North America. Zero company-owned outlets. The system added a net 12 salons in 2024 (115 opened, 103 closed), following flat growth in 2023 and a net loss of 20 in 2022.

2. **Entity Structure**: Great Clips, Inc. is a Minnesota corporation incorporated July 12, 1982. It has no parents, predecessors, or affiliates. The company began franchising in 1983, one year after opening its first salon. The franchise business is the sole line of business.

3. **Ownership & Leadership**: Privately held with 6,000 voting common shares and approximately 1.74 million nonvoting common shares. Robert D. Goggins is CEO and President (effective January 2026; with the company since 2007). Raymond L. Barton remains Chairman of the Board (since 2011; joined 1983). The leadership team is deep and long-tenured, with most executives having 10-25+ years with the company.

4. **Investment Range**: $187,800 to $419,900 total initial investment for a single franchise. This includes $20,000–$35,000 in initial franchise fees, $70,000–$200,000 in leasehold improvements, $40,000–$55,000 in fixtures/signage/technology, and $20,000–$60,000 in working capital. A typical salon is 900–1,200 sq ft in a shopping center.

5. **Fee Burden**: The ongoing royalty burden is heavy at 11% of gross sales (6% continuing franchise fee + 5% Ad Fund). Additional technology fees (POS $250/month, managed services $18.99/month, etc.) add approximately $4,000–$5,000/year per salon. Training ($200/year) and recruiting technology ($750/year) are modest. The total ongoing fee burden before occupancy and labor is approximately 13-14% of gross sales.

6. **Item 19 Highlights**: Average total sales of $399,179 across 4,147 eligible salons (median $382,316). For the 2,175 reporting salons that provided expense data, average operating cash flow was $82,637 (19.99% margin; median $75,851). However, 47.6% of salons did not report — and non-reporting salons skewed lower, meaning actual system-wide performance is somewhat worse than reported.

7. **Bottom-Quartile Risk**: 196 salons (9.01%) had annual sales below $250,000 and operated at an average loss of -1.74% ($3,635 negative cash flow). The breakeven point is approximately $250,000 in annual sales. Below that, occupancy costs consume 19.5% of revenue and labor takes 58%, leaving no margin.

8. **Financial Health**: Strong and improving. Net earnings of $22.3M in 2024. Total assets $135.2M. Shareholders' equity grew from negative ($12.3M deficit in 2021) to $43.8M by 2024. Operating profit $28.1M. Debt declining ($14.8M total, down from $26.6M in 2022). Cash position strong at $39.8M. No going concern issues. Unqualified audit opinion.

9. **Growth Trajectory**: System is mature and largely stable. Net growth has been minimal over the past three years (-20, 0, +12). However, the pipeline is substantial: 965 franchise agreements signed but not yet opened, with 90 projected openings in the next fiscal year. The system appears to be at a plateau with selective growth in certain markets (Texas, Georgia, Ohio).

10. **Territory**: Non-exclusive. Protected area is only a 3/4-mile radius (1/10-mile in dense areas). Non-Traditional Locations (airports, military bases, universities, big box stores, etc.) are carved out from the Protected Area. Great Clips retains broad rights to compete via other brands, distribute products through any channel (including Amazon/internet), and franchise Non-Traditional Locations within your Protected Area.

11. **Renewal**: No right to renew. Renewal is at Great Clips' discretion. If renewed, you must sign the then-current franchise agreement, which may have materially different terms, pay $1,750, potentially retrain, and execute a general release. This is a significant one-sided risk.

12. **Disputes**: Mandatory arbitration in Minneapolis, Minnesota. All litigation must be venued in Hennepin County, Minnesota. This is explicitly flagged as a Special Risk. State addenda modify this for California, Hawaii, Illinois, Maryland, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Virginia, and Wisconsin.

13. **Special Risks**: (1) Out-of-state dispute resolution forces Minnesota venue; (2) Supplier control — must purchase all or nearly all supplies from designated sources at prices they set.

14. **No Litigation, No Bankruptcy**: A clean history. No pending or past litigation or bankruptcy disclosures.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
Great Clips is a single-entity franchisor with no corporate parents, subsidiaries, or affiliates. The franchise grants a license to operate a single Great Clips salon at a designated Authorized Location. Three program options exist: (1) single franchise agreement ($20,000 fee), (2) Three Star Program (3 salons for $35,000 + $5,000 MDAF), and (3) Master Development Agreement ($4,000/salon development fee + $6,000/salon franchise fee for exclusive DMA development rights).

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Initial Franchise Fee: $20,000 (single), $35,000 (Three Star for 3), $6,000 (MDA per salon)
- Initial Advertising Contribution (MDAF): $5,000 (one-time, non-refundable)
- Architecture Fees: $100–$3,800
- Lease Liability Fee: $1,500 (if Great Clips guarantees lease)
- Lease Review Fee: $2,200–$2,500
- VetFran Rebate: $5,000 for qualifying veterans (first salon only)
- Expediter Rebate: Up to $20,000 for real estate acquisition costs

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Continuing Franchise Fee: 6% of Gross Sales (biweekly ACH)
- Ad Fund Contribution: 5% of Gross Sales (biweekly ACH)
- Training Fee: $200/year/salon
- Recruiting Technology Fee: $750/year/salon
- Local Co-op Dues: ~$100/month average
- POS Software License: $250/month/salon (to ICS)
- Managed Services: $18.99/month/salon (to Foundation Technologies)
- Mobile Device Management: $54/year/iPad
- Router Maintenance: $312/year/router
- Insurance: $1,500–$3,000/year (property + liability only)

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Renewal Fee: $1,750/salon (every 10 years, CPI-adjusted)
- Assignment Fee: $1,500/salon (CPI-adjusted)
- Salon Upgrades: $20,000–$80,000 (every 7–10 years)
- Ongoing Maintenance: $3,000–$9,000/year
- Late Charges: 14% per annum
- Audit/Inspection Cost: If 5%+ discrepancy in reported data
- Sister Store Review Appeal: $500–$1,000 (refundable if decided in franchisee's favor)
- Technology Transfer: $90/router + $30/iPad (on ownership change)

### Initial Investment
The total initial investment ranges from $187,800 to $419,900. The largest variable is leasehold improvements ($70,000–$200,000), driven by the condition of the space at handover. Fixtures, signage, and technology are relatively fixed at $40,000–$55,000 (purchased from Great Clips or designated suppliers). Grand opening advertising is $20,000–$25,000. Additional working capital needs are estimated at $20,000–$60,000 for 3–6 months of operations, excluding owner's salary.

Great Clips does not offer financing. Third-party financing availability depends on creditworthiness and local lending conditions. Great Clips is listed on the Franchise Registry for streamlined SBA loan processing.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Great Clips exercises tight supply chain control. Approximately 90–95% of establishment purchases and 80–85% of ongoing purchases must come from Great Clips or its designated suppliers. Great Clips is the sole supplier for salon fixtures, signage, flooring, workstations, chairs, and graphics. Salon Innovations, Inc. is the sole supplier for Great Clips exclusive brand haircare products and designated brand products. There are no affiliate suppliers.

Great Clips derived $16.27M (14.1% of total revenues) from required purchases in 2024, plus $598,165 in product royalties (0.3% of revenues). Suppliers also make donations and contributions toward Great Clips events.

Franchisees may request approval of alternative suppliers (written request, 90-day response, evaluation cost may be charged to franchisee). Supplier approval criteria include quality standards, production capacity, and willingness to allow periodic Great Clips inspections.

### Training Program Detail
- **QuickConnect** (franchisees): 49.56 hours classroom + 13 hours on-the-job. Blend of online (Great Clips University), live virtual, and in-person (2-day Building Your Legacy in Minneapolis). Must complete within 180 days of signing. No fee, but franchisee pays travel/expenses ($1,500–$2,500 estimated).
- **Great Clips Academy** (stylists/key personnel): 4.87 hours online + 3.5 hours live virtual. Must complete within 30 days of hire.
- **LEADS** (management): 5.52 hours online + 18 hours live virtual (6 sessions x 3 hours). Must complete within 90 days of hire/promotion.
- **In-Salon Training**: Required; completed in market at existing franchisee's salon or with own manager.
- **Annual Training Fee**: $200/year/salon covers all programs. Franchisee pays all wages, benefits, and travel for trainees.
- **Overseen by**: Michelle Sack, VP of Learning and Development (31 years with company).

### Technology Stack and Costs
- **POS Software**: Styleware iPad (from Innovative Computer Software, "ICS") — $250/month/salon. Mandatory. Includes iPad-based salon management, Net Check-In (online customer check-in), Styleware Vantage (real-time data), and Salondata.com (reporting).
- **Hardware**: Standardized iPad salon package from Foundation Technologies. Purchase or rent options.
- **Credit Card Processing**: Heartland Payments Solution (mandatory). $15 setup + $0.016/transaction + $5/month. Payment terminal: Ingenico Lane/5000, transitioning to Verifone P630 by June 2026.
- **Network/Managed Services**: Foundation Technologies — $18.99/month. Includes PCI compliance configuration.
- **Mobile Device Management**: $54/year/iPad. Management iPad: $24.96/month.
- **Data Access**: Great Clips has complete, independent, unimpeded access to all salon business data. All salon data is proprietary property of Great Clips, licensed to franchisee during term.

### Marketing Fund Spending
The North American Advertising Fund received franchisee contributions of $87.47M in 2024. Spending breakdown: 2.7% production, 80.0% media, 18.7% other (including 5.0% admin fee to Great Clips of $4.35M, agency fees, research, testing, meeting expenses). The Ad Fund expenses totaled 101.8% of revenue in 2024, meaning it ran a slight deficit. The fund is audited annually. Great Clips is not obligated to spend in any specific DMA. No Ad Fund dollars are used to solicit new franchisees. Surplus funds, if any, carry over to next year.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
The franchise grants a Protected Area defined as a 3/4-mile radius from the primary customer entrance. In densely populated areas, the Protected Area shrinks to 1/10-mile. This is not an exclusive territory — you may face competition from other franchisees, company-owned outlets (none currently), or other brands/channels controlled by Great Clips.

### Non-Traditional Location Carve-Outs
The following locations are explicitly excluded from your Protected Area and can be developed by Great Clips or others: military bases, airports/transit, college/university campuses, grocery/club/big box stores, office buildings, amusement parks, casinos, community events, racing events, and sporting events.

### Franchisor Rights
Great Clips retains the right to: (1) franchise or operate under a different trademark in any business including haircare, at any location including your Protected Area; (2) distribute products (including Great Clips branded) through any channel including Amazon, internet, catalog — without obligation to franchisees; (3) establish company-owned or franchised Great Clips salons at any location outside your Protected Area.

### Channel Restrictions
Franchisees may not use the internet, catalog, telemarketing, robocalling, texting, or other marketing methods to solicit business without Great Clips' prior written consent. No compensation is owed for territorial overlap or competitive activity.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
The initial franchise term is 10 years from salon opening date. There is **no right to renew** — renewal is at Great Clips' sole discretion. If renewed, the term is another 10 years. Renewal conditions include: compliance with the franchise agreement, notice, upgrade salon to then-current standards, pay $1,750 renewal fee (CPI-adjusted), potentially retrain, sign the then-current franchise agreement, and execute a general release. **Critical risk**: the then-current franchise agreement may have materially different terms and conditions.

### Transfer Provisions
Any transfer of any interest (however minor) requires Great Clips' prior written consent. The assignee must meet then-current qualification guidelines, sign a new franchise agreement (for the balance of the original term), pay the $1,500 assignment fee, sign a general release and personal guaranty, and assume all ongoing obligations. Cannot transfer if salon is not in operation (with limited exception for full system exit). Public offerings are prohibited.

### Termination
Great Clips cannot terminate without cause. Termination for cause includes:
- **Curable (7-day cure for payment; 30-day for others)**: Non-payment, non-compliance with system standards, misrepresenting financials, unauthorized products/services
- **Non-curable (immediate)**: Impairment of Marks/System, insolvency, abandonment, criminal conviction, unauthorized assignment, material misrepresentation in application, unauthorized use of confidential information, material misuse of customer data, failure to cure lease default
- **30-day notice**: Repeated breaches, material breach incapable of cure

### Death/Disability
Heirs or executor may apply for Great Clips' consent to assignment and must pay applicable assignment fee.

### Noncompete
**During term**: You and your spouse cannot be involved in any way in any haircare/grooming business, beauty/barber/cosmetology school, or business selling hair products, at any location or on the internet. The definition of "Retail Haircare Business" is broad — any service on the Great Clips price board, any related products, blow drying, styling, braiding, treatments, perms.

**Post-term**: 1 year after termination/expiration/assignment, within 5 miles of any Great Clips salon (not just yours). Subject to state law overrides.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: Mandatory in Minneapolis, Minnesota, subject to certain exceptions.
- **Litigation**: Hennepin County, Minnesota (federal or state court).
- **Governing Law**: Laws of the state where the salon is located.
- **Subject to state addenda** for California, Hawaii, Illinois, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Virginia, and Wisconsin.

### Right of First Refusal
Great Clips has 60 days to match any bona fide offer. If not exercised, transaction must close within 120 days. Any changed offer must be re-presented.

### Litigation History
No litigation required to be disclosed in Item 3. No bankruptcy in Item 4. The company states it is "subject to various legal proceedings in the normal course of business" in the financial statement notes but believes none will have a material adverse effect.
