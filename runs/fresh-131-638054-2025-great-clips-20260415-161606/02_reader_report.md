# Reader Report: Great Clips, Inc. FDD (638054-2025)

## Pass A — Executive Snapshot

1. **System Size**: 4,439 franchised salons across the US and Canada as of Dec 31, 2024. Zero company-owned units. Net growth of +12 in 2024 (115 opened, 103 closed).
2. **Entity Structure**: Great Clips, Inc. is a Minnesota corporation, incorporated July 12, 1982. No parents, no predecessors, no affiliates. Began franchising 1983.
3. **Ownership**: Privately held. 6,000 voting common shares and 1,742,153 nonvoting common shares outstanding. Stock repurchases via employee redemption agreements at fair value (EBITDA multiple). $2.59M in stock redeemed in 2024.
4. **Investment Range**: $187,800 – $419,900 for a single franchise agreement. MDA adds $14,000 – $46,000 development fee layer.
5. **Fee Burden**: 6% continuing franchise fee + 5% Ad Fund = 11% of gross sales. Plus $200/year training fee, $750/year recruiting technology fee, $250/month POS software, $18.99/month managed services, various hardware/warranty fees.
6. **Item 19 — FPR**: Average total sales $399,179 (4,147 salons). Median $382,316. Average operating cash flow $82,637 (19.99% margin, 2,175 reporting salons). Bottom quartile (<$250K) averages negative cash flow (-1.74%).
7. **Financial Health**: Strong. Net earnings $22.3M (2024), $19.9M (2023), $22.8M (2022). Total assets $135.2M. Shareholders' equity $43.8M (positive, growing from -$12.3M deficit in 2021). Operating profit $28.1M. Unqualified audit opinion. No going concern.
8. **Growth Trajectory**: Flat to slightly growing. Net +12 in 2024, flat in 2023, -20 in 2022. 965 franchise agreements signed but not opened; 90 projected openings in next fiscal year.
9. **Territory**: Non-exclusive. Protected area = 3/4-mile radius (1/10-mile in dense areas). Non-Traditional Locations carved out. MDA provides exclusive DMA.
10. **Renewal**: No right to renew — discretionary with franchisor. Must sign then-current agreement (potentially materially different terms). $1,750 renewal fee. General release required.
11. **Disputes**: Mandatory arbitration in Minneapolis, MN. Litigation venue: Hennepin County, MN. Governing law: state where salon is located.
12. **Noncompete**: During term: blanket prohibition on any haircare, grooming, beauty school business. Post-term: 1 year, 5-mile radius from any Great Clips salon.
13. **Special Risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (MN). Supplier control (~90-95% of setup purchases, ~80-85% ongoing purchases from designated sources).
14. **Litigation & Bankruptcy**: None disclosed.

## Pass C — Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Restrictions on Sources
- **Supply Control**: ~90-95% of establishment purchases and ~80-85% of ongoing purchases must be from Great Clips or designated suppliers.
- **Great Clips as Supplier**: Sells fixtures, signage, flooring, graphics, stations, chairs. Currently the only supplier for these items.
- **Haircare Products**: Must carry Great Clips exclusive brand + designated brands (Matrix, American Crew, Bosley MD, Sexy Hair, TIGI Bedhead, John Paul Mitchell). Sole supplier: Salon Innovations, Inc.
- **Revenue from Required Purchases**: $16.27M in 2024 (14.1% of total revenues of $115.2M).
- **Product Royalties**: Great Clips derives ~6% royalty on exclusive brand products sold through Salon Innovations. Revenue: $598,165 in 2024 (0.3% of total).
- **Supplier Approval Process**: Written request; 90-day response time. Cost of evaluation may be charged to franchisee.
- **No affiliates** are currently approved suppliers.
- **Insurance Requirements**: General liability $1M/$2M aggregate; "All Risk" property coverage; workers comp as required. Must name Great Clips as additional insured.

## Pass D — Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Protected Area**: 3/4-mile radius from primary customer entrance. 1/10-mile in densely populated areas. Determined by Great Clips at opening.
- **Non-Exclusive**: Franchisee does not receive an exclusive territory.
- **Non-Traditional Locations Carved Out**: Military bases, airports, transit, college campuses, big box stores, amusement parks, casinos, events — all excluded from Protected Area.
- **No Minimum Sales Quota** or market penetration requirement.
- **Population changes** do not affect Protected Area.
- **Alternative Channels**: Cannot use internet, catalog, telemarketing without written consent.
- **Franchisor Rights**: Can establish company-owned or franchised units outside Protected Area. Can operate under different trademark in any business including haircare. Can distribute products (including Great Clips branded) through any channel including internet/Amazon.
- **MDA**: Provides exclusive DMA protection during term if in compliance with development schedule.

## Pass E — Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial Term**: 10 years from salon opening date.
- **Renewal**: No right to renew. Discretionary with Great Clips. If renewed, another 10 years.
- **Renewal Conditions**: Notice, compliance, upgrade to then-current standards, $1,750 fee, may require retraining, must sign then-current franchise agreement (may have materially different terms), must sign general release.
- **MDA Term**: 10 years, renewal at Great Clips' discretion for up to 10 years.

### Termination
- **By Franchisee**: Only for good cause or grounds available by law.
- **By Great Clips Without Cause**: Not permitted.
- **Curable Defaults** (7 days for non-payment, 30 days for others): Failure to comply with system standards, failure to make payments, misrepresenting financials, unauthorized products/services.
- **Non-Curable Defaults** (immediate termination): Impair Marks/System, insolvency, assignment for benefit of creditors, inability to pay debts, abandonment, criminal conviction, unauthorized assignment, failure to renew lease, material misrepresentation in application, material misuse of customer data, failure to cure lease default, unauthorized use of confidential information.
- **30-Day Notice**: Repeated breaches or material breach that cannot be cured.

### Transfer
- Any transfer (however minor) requires Great Clips' prior written consent.
- Assignee must: meet qualifications, agree to operate as Great Clips salon, sign new franchise agreement (for balance of term), pay assignment fee ($1,500), sign general release and guaranty.
- No public offerings permitted.
- Cannot assign if salon not in operation (exceptions for full system exit).

### Right of First Refusal
- Great Clips has 60 days to match any bona fide offer.
- If not exercised, transaction must close within 120 days.
- Any changed offer must be re-presented to Great Clips.

### Post-Termination
- Cease all use of Marks. Assign phone number. Pay all sums. Return manuals. De-identify location. Execute general release. Comply with noncompete.
- Great Clips has option (not obligation) to purchase equipment/furnishings at appraised value.

### Noncompete
- **During Term**: You and spouse cannot be involved in any haircare, beauty school, barber school, or hair product business at any location or on the internet.
- **Post-Term**: 1 year, within 5 miles of any Great Clips salon.
- **After Assignment**: 1 year, within 5 miles of any Great Clips salon.
- "Retail Haircare Business" definition is broad: any business offering services on Great Clips price board, or related products.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: Mandatory, Minneapolis, Minnesota.
- **Litigation Venue**: Hennepin County, Minnesota (federal or state court).
- **Governing Law**: Laws of the state where salon is located.
- **Subject to state addenda overrides**.
