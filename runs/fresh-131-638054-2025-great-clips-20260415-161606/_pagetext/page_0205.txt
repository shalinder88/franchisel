9.8 
Compliance with Laws.  Franchisee must maintain the Salon in the highest degree 
of sanitation, safety, repair and condition as Great Clips may require and in compliance with all 
applicable laws pertaining to the business including federal and state antitrust laws, data privacy, 
protection and breach notification laws, all health, environmental, sanitation, cosmetology, and 
Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) laws. Franchisee is exclusively 
responsible for ensuring that all stylists are licensed by the applicable state Cosmetology Board or 
equivalent official licensing entity. Franchisee must operate and maintain the Authorized Location 
in strict compliance with all fire, safety and building codes, the Americans with Disabilities Act 
(ADA), and other requirements that may be prescribed by Great Clips or by public authority.   
Franchisee is exclusively responsible for the full knowledge and application of all employment 
laws, such as Title VII of the Civil Rights Act, the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), Family 
and Medical Leave Act (FMLA), Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act (COBRA), 
Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), National Labor Relations Act (NLRA), Occupational Safety 
and Health Administration (OSHA), all federal, state and local employment laws, wage and hour 
laws, pay transparency laws, paystub requirements, workers’ compensation, Internal Revenue 
Code, and immigration laws.  Franchisee understands that, as the sole employer of its employees, 
Franchisee is exclusively responsible for all employment decisions and actions related to 
Franchisee’s employees. 
Franchisee is also responsible for the full knowledge and application of all federal and state laws 
that are applicable to salon technology hardware, including all applicable computer security 
requirements, computer disposal and data wiping specifications, payment processing, or other 
technology used in the operation of the Salon.  Franchisee must comply with all state and federal 
data and consumer privacy laws and regulations, including, but not limited to, the Telephone 
Consumer Protection Act (TCPA), the National Automated Clearinghouse Association operating 
rules and all related and associated regulations, as well as any other applicable federal, state, and 
local laws and regulations related to direct marketing and consumer protection, along with the 
rules, requirements and regulations of any applicable jurisdiction, such as, without limitation, the 
California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018 (CCPA), the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) 
Colorado Privacy Act (CPA), Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act (VDCPA), The Connecticut 
Data Privacy Act (CTDPA), Utah Consumer Privacy Act (UCPA), Montana Consumer Data 
Privacy Act (MTCDPA), Iowa Consumer Data Protection Act (ICDPA), New Jersey Data Privacy 
Law (NJDPA), New Hampshire Privacy Act (NHPA), Oregon Consumer Privacy Act (OCPA), 
Delaware Personal Data Privacy Act (DPDPA), Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), 
Nebraska Data Privacy Act (NDPA), Tennessee Information Protection Act (TIPA), Minnesota 
Consumer Data Privacy Act (MCDPA), Maryland Online Data Privacy Act (MODPA), Indiana 
Consumer Data Protection Act (ICPDA), Kentucky Consumer Data Protection Act (KCDPA), 
Rhode Island Data Transparency and Privacy Protection Act (RIDTPPA), state data breach 
notification laws, information security requirements such as 201 Mass. Code Reg. 1.700, and all 
similar federal,  state, and local laws and all applicable industry standards concerning privacy, 
confidentiality and data security (collectively “Privacy  Laws”).
