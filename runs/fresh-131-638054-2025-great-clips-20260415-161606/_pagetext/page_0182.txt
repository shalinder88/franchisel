Note 5. 
Deferred Compensation 
The Company provides a deferred compensation plan for certain employees. Participants can elect to 
defer up to $100,000 of their base salary and bonus annually. Interest on the plan is calculated using the 
Company’s weighted-average cost of capital. Participants are eligible for distributions from the plan upon 
termination, death, an unforeseen emergency or a change in control. At December 31, 2024, 2023 and 
2022, approximately $2,064,000, $2,232,000 and $2,386,000, respectively, was recorded as a deferred 
compensation liability under this plan. The Company recorded approximately $77,000, $556,000 and 
$523,000 in current liabilities at December 31, 2024, 2023 and 2022, respectively. Deferred 
compensation expense for this plan for the years ended December 31, 2024, 2023 and 2022, was 
approximately $143,000, $147,000 and $84,000, respectively. 
 
On April 10, 2023, the Company adopted a new long-term incentive plan for certain employees. Under 
this plan, participants can earn a cash incentive award based upon the achievement of certain 
performance goals over a continuous three-year period, including growth in new salons and customer 
counts and Company cash flows as approved by the Board of Directors. However, if the targeted cash 
flow requirement is not met at the conclusion of the three-year period, no cash bonus amounts will be 
paid. Payment on the achievement of the performance goals will take place three years after the 
performance goal amount is earned and determined. Deferred compensation expense for this plan for the 
years ended December 31, 2024 and 2023, was approximately $467,000 and $401,000, respectively, and 
the corresponding long-term liabilities are included in deferred compensation of approximately $908,000 
and $401,000 recorded at December 31, 2024 and 2023, respectively. 
 
The Company also has a previous additional deferred incentive compensation plan for certain employees. 
On February 16, 2023, the Company terminated this plan using a valuation date of December 31, 2022, 
for the final measurement period. All participants became fully vested as of the valuation date. Previously, 
benefits accrued under an earnings-based formula whereby each unit vested over five years. Benefits 
earned will be paid in 2024 and 2025. The Company paid approximately $3,106,000 in 2024 with the 
remaining amount due of $1,537,000 to be paid in 2025. At December 31, 2024, 2023 and 2022, 
approximately $1,537,000, $4,190,000 and $3,432,000, respectively, was recorded as deferred 
compensation liabilities for this plan. The Company recorded approximately $1,537,000, $3,106,000 and 
$1,383,000 in current liabilities at December 31, 2024, 2023 and 2022, respectively. Deferred 
compensation expense for this plan for the years ended December 31, 2024, 2023 and 2022, was 
approximately $205,000, $1,940,000 and $2,230,000, respectively. 
 
As a form of payment over certain amounts, the Company issues notes payable to certain employees that 
are payable over five years. Total notes payable to employees included within the deferred compensation 
liability were approximately $0, $496,000 and $1,085,000, at December 31, 2024, 2023 and 2022, 
respectively. These notes payable carried interest of 8.50% and 3.25% at December 31, 2023, and 2022, 
respectively. The outstanding amounts of these notes payable were paid in full during 2024. 
 
Note 6. 
Financing Arrangements 
Revolving line of credit: The Company has a revolving line of credit (RLOC) agreement with its primary 
bank. The RLOC agreement provides a line of credit with a borrowing capacity of $35,000,000. There 
was no amount outstanding on the line of credit at December 31, 2024, 2023 and 2022, respectively. The 
borrowings bear interest based on the prime rate or secured overnight financing rate (SOFR) as 
administered by the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, plus an applicable margin. All outstanding 
borrowings on the line of credit are due in full upon the maturity date in August 2027.
