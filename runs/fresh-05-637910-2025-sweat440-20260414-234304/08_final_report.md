## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: 22 total studios at year-end 2024 — 17 franchised and 5 company-owned/officer-owned across 6 states (FL, NJ, NY, TX, NC, AL). The system is early-stage, having begun franchising in July 2019.

2. **Entity structure**: Sweat440 Franchise Systems, LLC (FL LLC, formed Nov 2018) is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Brickhouse Athletics, LLC (parent). The parent owns the SWEAT440 marks and patents, operates 2 studios in Florida, and acquired majority interest in LeadTeam, LLC in January 2024. LeadTeam is a mandatory affiliate marketing vendor that generated $391,235 from franchisee purchases in the last fiscal year.

3. **Ownership/leadership**: Two co-founders — Cody Patrick (CEO, since 2018) and Matthew Miller (CBO, since 2022). Both have extensive fitness industry backgrounds. The management bench is thin with only two disclosed principals and one named training instructor (Christina WooChing).

4. **Investment range**: $284,900 to $667,900 for a single unit; $553,400 to $1,320,800 for a 2-pack multi-unit development. Studios range from 2,500 to 3,500 square feet. Leasehold improvements ($50K-$300K) are the largest variable cost component.

5. **Fee burden**: Royalty of 7% of weekly Gross Sales (or $350/week minimum). Ongoing mandatory monthly fees total at least ~$6,832 before variable royalty: brand fund ($420), local advertising ($1,200 minimum), marketing management to LeadTeam ($1,200), software license ($695), and call center ($1,800). This minimum fee burden was flagged as a state-required special risk.

6. **Item 19 highlights**: 17 studios reported 2024 gross revenue ranging from $137,328 (Madison, AL) to $1,452,077 (Miami-Brickell). Mean revenue $718K; median $671K. Franchisee-only studios (13) averaged $608K with median $541K. Two parent-owned studios in Miami showed adjusted EBITDA of $526K (South Beach) and $581K (Brickell), representing ~40% margins. However, parent studio salary/payroll costs appear low ($183K and $231K), which may not be representative of franchisee operating reality.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: Four studios (Madison, Coral Springs, Austin-Highland, Austin-Zilker) generated less than $336K in annual revenue — roughly $28K/month — which is likely below breakeven given the ~$6,832 minimum monthly fee burden plus rent, labor, and other operating costs.

8. **Financial health**: The franchisor reported a member's deficit of ($608,338) at December 31, 2024, with cash of only $51,869. Net loss narrowed to ($59,056) for the 14-month period but the member took $415,000 in distributions despite losses. Total revenue of $1.36M was substantially driven by $985,736 in management agreement expenses flowing to the parent — meaning the parent company absorbs most of the franchisor's revenue as reimbursed expenses. The state-required special risk explicitly notes that the franchisor's financial condition "calls into question the franchisor's financial ability to provide services and support."

9. **Growth trajectory**: Net growth of +4 studios in 2024 (down from +10 in 2023). A pipeline of 32 signed-not-open franchise agreements exists, but 28 belong to a single New Jersey operator (Nicholas Marco), representing extreme concentration risk. One franchise was terminated in Alabama in 2024.

10. **Territory**: Protected territory equals the lesser of a 3-mile radius or 50,000 people — relatively modest. The franchisor retains broad rights including operating through dissimilar channels, internet sales, and captive market locations (malls, airports, universities, hospitals) even within a franchisee's MUD Designated Area.

11. **Renewal**: 10-year initial term with 2 additional 5-year renewals. Renewal requires signing the then-current franchise agreement, which "may contain materially different terms." Also requires remodeling, general release, and $10,000 successor fee.

12. **Disputes**: All disputes must be litigated in Florida (where corporate HQ is located). No arbitration or mediation. Both jury trial and punitive damages waivers apply. Seven state addenda (CA, IL, MD, MN, NY, VA, WA) provide various overrides.

13. **Special risks (state-required)**: (1) Out-of-state dispute resolution in Florida, (2) Short operating history — "likely to be a riskier investment," (3) Financial condition concerns, (4) Mandatory minimum payments regardless of sales, (5) Significant number of unopened franchises.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Sweat440 Franchise Systems, LLC is a Florida limited liability company formed in November 2018 as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Brickhouse Athletics, LLC. The parent company was formed in 2017 by co-founders Cody Patrick and Matthew Miller and developed the SWEAT440 concept — a 40-minute group fitness workout using four rotating stations, with new groups entering every 10 minutes.

The parent owns and operates two studios in South Beach and Miami-Brickell through affiliated entities. In January 2024, the parent acquired a majority interest in LeadTeam, LLC, which became a mandatory marketing services vendor for all franchisees. LeadTeam shares the same principal address as the franchisor and parent.

There is no litigation and no bankruptcy history to disclose. The franchise was first offered in July 2019.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees**:
- **Initial Franchise Fee**: $60,000 (lump sum, non-refundable). Veterans receive $5,000 discount.
- **Multi-Unit Development Fee**: $55,000/studio (2-pack), $45,000 (3-5), $40,000 (6-9), $35,000 (10). No separate IFF for MUD studios.
- **Initial Training Fee**: $5,000 (first studio only).
- **Market Introduction Plan**: $20,000–$30,000 (determined by LeadTeam after market assessment).
- **Opening Assistance**: $500–$1,000/day for 3-5 days (franchisee pays franchisor's travel and lodging).

**Ongoing Fees**:
- **Royalty**: Greater of 7% of weekly Gross Sales or $350/week. Begins at earlier of studio opening or opening deadline (meaning you pay even if you haven't opened on time).
- **Brand Fund**: $420/month. May increase up to 10% per year, and compounding is explicitly allowed (if they don't raise it for 2 years, they can impose 3 years of 10% increases at once).
- **Local Advertising**: Minimum $1,200/month (up to $2,300). Must use LeadTeam. Additional $1,000/month required during first 6 months and during any period when monthly sales decline by more than 5% over a 3-month window.
- **Marketing Management Fee**: $1,200/month to LeadTeam. May increase 10% per year.
- **Software License**: $695/month (iSweat platform + Mariana Tek). Subject to change on written notice.
- **Call Center**: $1,800/month (Red Valley Staffing — mandatory).
- **Music Streaming**: ~$147/month (FIT Radio).
- **Communication Services**: ~$300/month.

**Event-Driven Fees**:
- **Successor Fee**: $10,000 (capped at 50% of then-current IFF).
- **Transfer Fee (single)**: 25% of then-current standard IFF plus document review costs.
- **Transfer Fee (MUD)**: $5,000 per unmet development obligation.
- **Relocation Fee**: $5,000+ plus build-out costs.
- **Securities Offering Fee**: $10,000 or actual costs.
- **Convention**: $1,000/participant (up to $1,500). Attendance may be mandatory.
- **Remedial Training**: $500/day (up to 5 days/year, up to $1,000/person).
- **Management Fee**: Greater of $2,000/week or 20% of Gross Sales (only if Studio Manager not replaced within 30 days of death/disability/departure).
- **Liquidated Damages**: Complex formula — approximately 36 months of annualized royalty, technology, and brand fund fees, potentially triggered on early termination.
- **Audit penalties**: Understatement of Gross Sales: ≤2% = pay fees + interest; 2-5% = also pay audit costs; ≥5% = termination right (no cure).
- **Interest**: 1.5% per month on late payments (or max legal rate).

**Minimum monthly ongoing fee burden** (before variable royalty above $350/wk): Approximately **$6,832** — Royalty min $1,517 + Brand Fund $420 + Local Ad $1,200 + Marketing Mgmt $1,200 + Software $695 + Call Center $1,800. Adding music ($147), communication ($300), and insurance, the realistic monthly fixed cost floor likely exceeds **$7,500** before rent, labor, and other operating expenses.

### Initial Investment

**Single Unit (Table A)**: $284,900 to $667,900. The largest variable is leasehold improvements ($50,000–$300,000), driven by condition of premises, landlord contributions, location, and market. Rent is estimated at $25–$80 per square foot including CAM and taxes. Equipment ranges from $32,000 to $50,000 (lower for 2-person stations, higher for 3-person). Additional Funds for first 3 months: $20,000–$50,000 (excludes owner compensation and loan interest).

**2-Pack MUD (Table B)**: $553,400 to $1,320,800. Includes $110,000 MUD fee (2 x $55,000) plus two studios at reduced investment (IFF already covered by MUD fee).

No financing is offered by the franchisor, directly or indirectly.

The franchisor "will not grant approval for you to use an alternative supplier to LeadTeam." LeadTeam received $391,235 from franchisee purchases in FY 2024.
- **Red Valley Staffing**: Mandatory call center vendor ($1,800/month).
- **Designated architect**: Required for blueprint drawings (must submit to franchisor for approval before municipality submission).
- **Designated project manager**: Required for construction (currently Cresa or Build'M).
- **Designated site specialist**: Required for site selection (currently Sabor Life).
- **Equipment suppliers**: Certain fitness equipment, flooring, functional accessories, branded items, and computer hardware must be purchased from approved suppliers (estimated $50,000–$75,000).
- **Vendor rebates**: Franchisor received $26,556 in vendor rebates in FY 2024 (less than 2% of revenue). Rebates can range from less than 1% to 10%+ of manufacturer costs.
- **Estimated purchase concentration**: 35-50% of development costs and 15-20% of operating costs from designated/approved sources.

The supplier approval process requires franchisees to submit specifications and samples at their own expense, pay testing and evaluation fees, and wait for written approval. Franchisor may revoke approval with 30 days' notice.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory

After signing a lease for the Authorized Location, the franchisee receives a Protected Territory typically comprising the lesser of a **3-mile radius or 50,000 people** around the studio. In densely populated downtown areas, the territory may be "much smaller." The territory is exclusive — the franchisor will not place another SWEAT440 studio within it during the franchise term, provided the franchisee remains in compliance.

If no location is identified at signing, the franchisor designates a non-exclusive "Designated Search Area" where the franchisee must find and secure an approved location within **180 days** or face termination. The search area carries no territorial protection.

### Reserved Rights

The franchisor retains seven broad categories of rights within and outside the Protected Territory:
1. Operate or franchise studios **outside** the Protected Territory without restriction
2. Promote, sell, and distribute any products, services, memberships, and merchandise
3. Modify, add, or remove authorized products and services
4. Sell ancillary products through **dissimilar channels** (direct mail, wholesale, electronic) including within your territory
5. Promote the System and studios on the **Internet** and social media
6. Develop **other business systems** using the Marks or other names
7. Add or substitute **different trademarks** at no cost to the franchisor

### Franchisee Internet Restriction
The franchisee may **not** conduct business or advertise on the Internet (or any other electronic communication) without written authorization — including email marketing and digital marketing. This is a significant restriction given the mandatory LeadTeam relationship.

### Multi-Unit Development Agreement
MUD holders receive protected development rights within a larger Designated Area. However, the franchisor explicitly reserves the right to operate **Captive Market Locations** (malls, airports, transportation terminals, sports facilities, hospitals, universities, corporate campuses, hotels, grocery stores) within the Designated Area. Once the MUD expires (upon final studio lease or Development Schedule end), Designated Area protection ceases; only individual Protected Territories survive.

### Encroachment Assessment
The combination of a small Protected Territory (3 miles or 50K people), broad reserved rights, Captive Market Location carve-outs, and franchisor internet rights creates meaningful encroachment risk, particularly in dense urban markets where Sweat440 has concentrated its growth (Miami, NYC).

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 10 years
- **Renewal**: 2 additional 5-year terms (20 years total potential)
- **Renewal requires**: Signing the **then-current franchise agreement**, which "may contain materially different terms than your original Franchise Agreement." This creates substantial renewal risk — the deal you renew into may differ significantly from the deal you originally signed.
- **Other renewal conditions**: 6-9 months advance notice; in compliance; meet new franchisee requirements; complete refresher training; remodel studio; maintain premises; current licenses; pay $10,000 successor fee; sign general release.

### Transfer Provisions
- Franchisor approval required (not to be unreasonably withheld)
- **Conditions**: Transferee qualifies, completes training, pays initial training fee; all amounts owed are current; you are in good standing; transferee assumes existing agreement or signs then-current form; transfer fee paid (25% of then-current IFF for single, $5,000/unmet obligation for MUD); lease assigned; you sign non-compete and general release; you retain no security interest
- **Exceptions**: No transfer fee for controlled entities, 3-year franchisees, family members, or death/disability
- **Right of first refusal**: Franchisor can match any offer within 30 days; 60 days to close; may substitute cash for non-cash consideration

### Termination and Death/Disability
- **By franchisee**: No contractual right to terminate
- **By franchisor without cause**: Not applicable
- **By franchisor with cause**: Extensive cure periods (30 days general, 5 days fees, 72 hours health/safety) and non-curable defaults (3+ violations in 12 months, material misrepresentation, felony, insolvency, abandonment, audit understatement >5%, lease termination, sales minimum failures, and more)
- **Death/disability**: Estate must assign to approved buyer within 6 months. If Studio Manager dies/disabled and not replaced within 30 days, franchisor appoints manager at $2,000/week or 20% of Gross Sales.
- **Purchase option**: On termination or expiration, franchisor may purchase assets at book value.
- **Liquidated damages**: On early termination, formula equals approximately 36 months of annualized royalty, technology, and brand fund fees — a significant financial exposure.

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term**: Broad prohibition on any involvement with competitive fitness businesses (classes, instruction, apparel, accessories — wholesale or retail)
- **Post-term**: 2 years after expiration or termination; within 15 miles of your studio OR within 15 miles of **any** then-existing SWEAT440 studio; includes e-commerce businesses serving customers within those 15-mile zones
- **Scope**: The post-term non-compete is expansive — it covers not just your location but the entire growing system footprint
- **State overrides**: California addendum notes post-term non-compete may not be enforceable under California law

### Dispute Resolution
- **No arbitration or mediation**
- **Venue**: State or federal court in Florida (where corporate HQ is located). Franchisor may also sue you in the state where your studio is located.
- **Governing law**: Florida, without regard to conflict of laws
- **Waivers**: Jury trial waiver; punitive damages waiver
- **Attorneys' fees**: Non-prevailing party pays all costs and reasonable attorneys' fees

### State Addenda Overrides
- **California**: Non-compete may not be enforceable; franchise fee deferral; release/waiver limits
- **Illinois**: IL law governs; venue outside IL void; anti-waiver
- **Maryland**: May sue in MD; 3-year statute of limitations; release limits
- **Minnesota**: Fee deferral until studio opens; 90-day termination notice; 180-day non-renewal notice; liquidated damages may not be enforceable; jury trial waiver may not be enforceable; trademark indemnification required
- **New York**: Release does not waive GBL claims; NY franchise law governs claims under GBL
- **Virginia**: Fee deferral until pre-opening obligations complete; reasonable cause for termination required
- **Washington**: Franchisee Bill of Rights; fair pricing; treble damages available; no forced buy-back without good cause; anti-waiver; no punitive damages waiver

### Litigation History
No litigation required to be disclosed in Item 3. No pending material litigation referenced in Item 21 financial statement notes (Note 8).
