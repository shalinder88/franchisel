# Reader Report — Canopy by Hilton (638050-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 2007, wholly owned subsidiary of Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., itself a subsidiary of Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). Became Canopy franchisor October 15, 2014.
2. **Brand positioning:** Upper upscale lifestyle hotel brand competing with national chains and independents for business travelers, families, vacationers, and groups. Part of Hilton's portfolio of 20+ brands.
3. **System size:** 27 franchised Canopy hotels open as of December 31, 2024 (up from 26 in 2023, 23 in 2022). Zero company-owned. 10 signed but not yet opened. 1 projected new opening in next fiscal year. Small but growing brand.
4. **Investment range:** $64,100,689 to $141,712,721 for a 200-room newly constructed hotel, excluding real property, insurance, market studies, and interest. This is among the highest initial investments in franchise disclosure.
5. **Fee burden:** Royalty 5% of Gross Rooms Revenue + Program fee 4% GRR (can increase by up to 1% over term) = 9% GRR minimum ongoing. Hilton Honors 4.3% of eligible folio. Hilton Advance 1.35% of digital direct revenue. Numerous additional distribution, QA, and compliance fees.
6. **Item 19:** No financial performance representation made. Prospective franchisees receive no financial projections from the franchisor.
7. **Financial health:** HFH LLC total revenue $1.502 billion (2024), net income $1.488 billion. Total assets $1.27 billion. Clean audit opinion from Cherry Bekaert. Entity is essentially a pure royalty collection vehicle — operating expenses of only $11.4 million. Distributed $1.5 billion to parent in 2024.
8. **Growth trajectory:** Net +1 hotel in 2024, +0 in 2023, +3 in 2022. Pipeline of 10 signed-not-opened suggests moderate growth ahead. Zero terminations, non-renewals, or closures in any of the last 3 years — remarkable stability but also very small sample.
9. **Territory:** Non-exclusive by default. Optional Restricted Area Provision may be negotiated, but shorter than franchise term and with extensive carve-outs. Franchisor retains broad rights to compete.
10. **Renewal:** No renewal right. Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms. This is a critical risk factor.
11. **Contract term:** 23 years for new construction, 10–20 years for conversion. Non-renewable.
12. **Dispute resolution:** Venue in Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria) or Fairfax County, VA; New York law governs. Arbitration only in Puerto Rico. Jury trial waiver. Out-of-state dispute resolution highlighted as special risk.
13. **Special risks:** Principal mark "Canopy" lacked federal registration (now registered Oct 2024); multiple pending antitrust class actions involving Hilton Worldwide; debt guarantee with all assets pledged.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Non-exclusive license** at a specified location. No protected territory by default.
- Standard FA permits franchisor to own, operate, manage, franchise any business including competitive brands
- **Optional Restricted Area Provision:** May be negotiated for new development or conversion; not typically for COO or re-licensing
- **Restrictive Period:** Shorter than franchise term, usually tied to years from application approval date; may reduce in geographic scope over time
- **Carve-outs from Restricted Area:** (a) existing or approved hotels; (b) other brands; (c) hotels opening after Restrictive Period; (d) gaming hotels; (e) timeshare/vacation ownership; (f) acquisitions of 4+ hotel chains
- **No mechanism** for resolving conflicts between franchisee and other Network Hotels
- **Strategic Partnerships** (SLH, Autocamp, etc.) may locate within Restricted Area
- No relocation permitted
- No ROFR or options for additional franchises
- Franchisee may not register or use domain names containing the Marks

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction:** 23 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10–20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership:** remaining term or such other term as approved
- **NO renewal right.** Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion; may require materially different terms, new PIP, new fees
- **Restaurant Brand:** expires on earlier of Restaurant Brand Amendment termination or FA expiration/termination

### Termination
**By franchisee:** Not authorized before expiration. Unilateral termination = material breach + liquidated damages.

**By franchisor without cause:**
- Condemnation: may terminate on notice, no liquidated damages
- Casualty: if hotel closes, 18 months to repair/reopen; if franchisee elects not to rebuild, franchisor may terminate (no liquidated damages if no competitor hotel at site within 3 years)

**By franchisor with cause (curable defaults):**
1. Failure to pay sums due (10-day cure)
2. Failure to start/complete hotel work or open on time (10-day cure)
3. Failure to maintain insurance (10-day cure)
4. Failure to comply with any FA provision that can be cured (30-day cure)

**Non-curable defaults (immediate termination):**
1. Repeated same non-compliance within 24 months after cure
2. Three notices of material default in 12 months
3. Insolvency/inability to pay debts
4. Bankruptcy filing
5. Loss of possession of hotel/site
6. Failure to operate for 5 consecutive days (except force majeure)
7. Contesting ownership of System/Marks
8. Conduct adversely reflecting on reputation
9. Concealing revenues/false books
10. Unauthorized transfer
11. Becoming a Sanctioned Person
12. Adverse gaming license impact
13. Guarantor breach
14. Public health/safety threat
15. Becoming a Competitor
16. Termination of other Hotel Agreements

**Interim remedies:** Suspension from Reservation Service, directory removal, software disabling, fee accommodation revocation.

### Transfer
**Permitted Transfers (no change of control):**
- Publicly traded equity: no notice required
- Privately held equity (<50%, no control change): no notice required
- To affiliates, family, trust, on death: 60 days notice + consent + $5,500 fee

**Change of Ownership Transfer:**
- 60 days notice + full application process + $150,000 fee
- Transferee must meet current requirements, sign current FA, agree to PIP
- No transfer to Competitor or Sanctioned Person

**No ROFR for franchisor** (but must notify of marketing any equity interests)
**No option to purchase**

**Death:** Transfer to immediate family within 1 year if they meet requirements

### Noncompete
- **During term:** May not own or be licensor/franchisor of competing brand. May own minority interest in competitor, be franchisee of competitor, or manage competitor property.
- **Post-term:** NO post-term noncompete (explicitly "Not applicable")

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue:** U.S. District Court, Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria); or state court in Fairfax County, Virginia or New York, New York. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Governing law:** New York (without choice-of-law principles), subject to Lanham Act and state law
- **Arbitration:** Only in Puerto Rico (AAA or JAMS, Federal Rules of Civil Procedure)
- **Jury trial waiver:** Not explicitly stated in summary but implied by venue selection

### Liquidated Damages Formula
- Pre-opening (before hotel work + competitor hotel within 1 year): System's Average Monthly Royalty × 60
- Post-opening (before 2nd anniversary): Greater of Hotel's or System's Average Monthly Royalty × 60
- Post-opening (after 2nd anniversary, before final 60 months): Hotel's Average Monthly Royalty × 60
- Final 60 months: Hotel's Average Monthly Royalty × remaining months
