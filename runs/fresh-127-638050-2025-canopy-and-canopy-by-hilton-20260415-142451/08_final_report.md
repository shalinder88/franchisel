## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and structure:** Canopy by Hilton is a small but growing upper upscale lifestyle hotel brand with 27 franchised hotels and zero company-owned hotels as of December 31, 2024. The brand operates across 19 US states plus the District of Columbia, with 10 additional franchise agreements signed but not yet opened. One new franchised hotel is projected to open in the next fiscal year.

2. **Entity structure:** The franchisor is Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed in September 2007, wholly owned by Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., which is in turn owned by Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). HFH LLC became the Canopy franchisor on October 15, 2014. Four international affiliates offer Canopy franchises outside the US.

3. **Ownership history:** Hilton became the parent on January 4, 2017, as successor to Park Hotels & Resorts, Inc. (spun off). The Hilton brand family traces back to 1946. The Canopy brand was launched in 2014.

4. **Investment range:** $64,100,689 to $141,712,721 for a 200-room newly constructed hotel, excluding real property, market studies, insurance, and interest. This is one of the highest initial investment ranges among franchise disclosure documents, reflecting the upper upscale positioning and extensive consultant and technology requirements.

5. **Fee burden:** Ongoing royalty of 5% of Gross Rooms Revenue plus a program fee of 4% GRR (may increase by up to 1% over term) = 9% GRR minimum. Additional mandatory fees include Hilton Honors at 4.3% of eligible folio, Hilton Advance at 1.35% of digital direct revenue, and extensive technology, QA compliance, and distribution program fees. The total all-in fee burden is among the highest in the hotel franchise industry.

6. **Item 19:** No financial performance representation is made. Prospective franchisees receive no revenue, cost, or profitability data from the franchisor.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** The absence of FPR combined with the $64M–$142M investment requirement and 9%+ GRR fee load creates significant downside risk for franchisees who cannot independently validate market economics before committing.

8. **Financial health:** HFH LLC is a pure royalty collection vehicle generating $1.502 billion in total revenue (2024) with operating expenses of only $11.4 million. Net income of $1.488 billion, nearly all distributed to parent ($1.5 billion). Total assets $1.27 billion. Clean audit opinion from Cherry Bekaert. However, all assets and franchise contracts are pledged as collateral for Hilton parent debt maturing 2025–2033.

9. **Growth trajectory:** Net growth of +1 hotel in 2024, +0 in 2023, +3 in 2022. Pipeline of 10 signed-not-opened hotels suggests accelerating growth. Zero terminations, non-renewals, or closures in any of the last three years — reflecting both brand stability and small sample size.

10. **Territory:** Non-exclusive license by default. Optional Restricted Area Provision may be negotiated but is shorter than the franchise term and subject to extensive carve-outs. The franchisor retains broad rights to compete in all channels and under all brands.

11. **Renewal:** No renewal right. This is one of the most critical risk factors. Re-licensing is at the franchisor's sole discretion and may require signing a new agreement with materially different terms, a new PIP, and new fees.

12. **Dispute resolution:** All litigation must be brought in Virginia (Eastern District or Fairfax County) or New York, governed by New York law. Arbitration applies only in Puerto Rico. Out-of-state dispute resolution is highlighted as a special risk on the FDD cover.

13. **Special risks:** Pending antitrust class actions involving Hilton Worldwide and hotel pricing algorithms (IDeaS, Amadeus, CoStar/STR). Gag clause restricts some franchisees from discussing their experience. Debt guarantee pledges all company assets.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Hilton Franchise Holding LLC is a single-purpose franchise holding entity within the Hilton Worldwide corporate family. It licenses intellectual property from affiliate Hilton International Holding LLC on a royalty-free basis and sublicenses it to third-party hotel owners. Key service affiliates include Hilton Reservations Worldwide (reservations), Hilton Supply Management (procurement), Hilton Honors Worldwide (loyalty), and Hilton Systems Solutions (IT).

The brand competes in the upper upscale lifestyle segment against national chains and independent hotels. Canopy hotels must include food and beverage, recreational, and other facilities. The brand also offers optional Restaurant Brands (StiR Creative Collective) for hotel restaurants and bars.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Franchise Application Fee: $85,000 for new development/conversion (+ $400/room over 250), $150,000 for change of ownership, $85,000 for re-licensing
- PIP Fee: $10,000 (for conversions, COO, re-licensing)
- OnQ Computer Systems: $113,400–$276,000
- Opening Process Services Fee: $20,000
- Training: $5,000–$20,000
- Construction/Renovation Extension Fee: $10,000 per extension
- Restaurant Brand Initial Fee: $47,500 (optional, per brand)

In 2024, actual franchise application fees paid ranged from $37,500 to $100,000, indicating negotiation is possible.

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Monthly Royalty: 5% GRR (fixed for term)
- Monthly Program Fee: 4% GRR (may increase by up to +1% over term)
- Hilton Honors: 4.3% of eligible guest folio (waived for on-property enrollments)
- Hilton Advance: 1.35% of digital direct revenue (max $30/stay)
- Technology: OnQ connectivity $400–$600/month; maintenance $1,995–$5,689/month; Connected Room $805–$1,170/month; Delphi $858/user/year; Guest Internet $2,778–$6,500/month (circuit + service)
- QA compliance: Brand Non-Compliance $65–$145/room (max $50K/6 months); Stay Score Failure up to $300/room; Plans/Designs Late $8K/90 days; PIP/Renovation Failure $16K/6 months; Service Quality Improvement $20K–$50K/6 months
- Brand Conference: $2,500/attendee (mandatory for GM and DOS)
- Distribution programs: Centralized payments, travel clubs, government programs, consortia — collectively significant per-transaction and per-stay fees

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Change of Ownership Application: $150,000
- Permitted Transfer Processing: $5,500
- Lender Comfort Letter: $3,500
- Liquidated Damages: Complex formula based on System or Hotel Average Monthly Royalty × up to 60 months
- Overdue payment service charges: 1.5%/month
- Various non-compliance fees: $500–$5,500 per instance

The dominant cost components are:
- Construction: $44.6M–$105M (69%–74% of total)

- Additional Funds (3 months): $1.1M–$1.5M

- Technology systems: $262K–$518K

The franchisor does not estimate conversion or re-licensing costs due to extreme variability.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Franchisees must comply with Standards for all purchases. Required purchases represent an estimated 15%–20% of establishment costs and 2%–5% of operating expenses.

**Must purchase from franchisor/designated sources:**
- OnQ proprietary software (from HSS)
- Items bearing logo/trademark (from approved suppliers)
- Exterior signage (from licensed vendor)

**Must use franchisor-approved consultants (5 categories):**
- Designer (interior design)
- Interior Stylist (ongoing accessorizing, minimum 2x/year, up to $50K/year ongoing)
- Food & Beverage Consultant
- Lighting Consultant
- Audio Consultant

Each consultant must be approved, with no pre-approved lists (except designers). Approval typically takes 30–60 days.

**Optional but incentivized:**
- HSM (Hilton Supply Management) for FF&E, supplies — offers negotiated pricing; charges 4%–10% procurement fee
- StiR Creative Collective for F&B consulting ($65K–$175K for up to 2 outlets)

HSM received $67.1 million in rebates, administrative fees, and sales revenues from all brand franchisees in 2024. HSM administrative fees represent 0.5%–7% of purchases.

### Training Program Detail
- **Pre-opening required training:** $5,000–$20,000; GM and key personnel must complete before opening
- **GM/Commercial/Sales Leader Training:** Virtual, within 90 days of start, up to $1,200 (required for new GMs and those away from brand 24+ months)
- **Hilton Core Sales Skills:** Virtual, within 90 days, up to $600 (mandatory for DOS, sales managers, and involved directors/managers)
- **Brand Conference:** Annual, $2,500/attendee, mandatory for GM and DOS
- **Other programs:** Up to $5,000/program/attendee, some required, some optional

Franchisee also bears wages, travel, lodging, and food costs for all attendees.

### Marketing Fund and Advertising
The Monthly Program Fee (4% GRR) funds reservations, directories, marketing, QA, technology, and administration. There is no separate advertising fund or cooperative requirement. The franchisor is not required to maintain any particular advertising program. Franchisees must advertise locally per Standards and submit all materials for approval. Advisory councils may be convened but serve only in advisory capacity. No franchisee has veto power over marketing spend.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
There is no standard protected territory. The franchise agreement provides a non-exclusive license at a specified location. The franchisor may operate, franchise, or license any business (including competitive brands) anywhere, at any time, including within any area where a franchisee operates.

### Optional Restricted Area Provision
If negotiated (typically for new development or conversion, not COO/re-licensing):
- Boundaries vary by hotel, delineated by cities, streets, or radius
- Restrictive Period is shorter than franchise term, usually tied to years from application approval
- Does NOT restrict: existing/approved hotels, other Hilton brands, gaming, timeshare/vacation ownership, 4+ hotel chain acquisitions, hotels opening after Restrictive Period
- Historically extended for full term but "we do not intend to do so in the future"

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights
- Franchisee may not register domain names containing the Marks
- Must obtain approval for third-party website listings
- Franchisor may enter Strategic Partnerships (SLH, Autocamp, Evermore Resort, Field Station) that locate within Restricted Area
- No mechanism for resolving conflicts between franchisee and other Network Hotels
- Franchisor and affiliates may furnish services, products, and support to competitive businesses anywhere

### Encroachment Risk Assessment
HIGH. The combination of non-exclusive territory by default, extensive carve-outs to any Restricted Area, the right to operate any other brand anywhere, and Strategic Partnerships that may compete directly creates significant encroachment exposure. The only protection is the optional Restricted Area Provision, which is typically time-limited and excludes many competitive scenarios.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction:** 23 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10–20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership:** Remaining term or as approved
- **Renewal:** NONE. The franchise agreement is non-renewable. Re-licensing is at the franchisor's sole discretion and may require materially different terms, a new PIP, new fees, and the franchisor's then-current form of franchise agreement. This is a critical risk: a franchisee investing $64M–$142M has no contractual right to continue operating after the initial term.

### Transfer Provisions
- **Permitted Transfers (no control change):** Publicly traded equity — no notice needed. Privately held equity (<50%, no control change) — no notice needed. To affiliates, family, trust, on death — 60 days notice + consent + $5,500 fee.
- **Change of Ownership Transfer:** 60 days notice + full application + $150,000 fee + transferee must meet current requirements, sign current FA, agree to PIP. No transfer to Competitor or Sanctioned Person.
- **No franchisor ROFR** (but must notify of marketing equity interests)
- **No franchisor purchase option**

### Termination and Death/Disability
- **Franchisee termination:** Not authorized. Unilateral termination = material breach + liquidated damages.
- **Franchisor for cause (curable):** Payment default (10 days), construction delays (10 days), insurance failure (10 days), other FA breach (30 days)
- **Franchisor for cause (non-curable, immediate termination):** 16 categories including repeated non-compliance, 3 defaults in 12 months, insolvency, bankruptcy, loss of possession, 5-day closure, challenging Marks, reputational harm, false books, unauthorized transfer, sanctioned person, gaming license impact, guarantor breach, public safety threat, becoming Competitor, termination of other Hotel Agreements
- **Casualty:** 18 months to repair/reopen; if electing not to rebuild, no liquidated damages if no competitor hotel at site within 3 years
- **Death:** Transfer to immediate family within 1 year if meeting requirements

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term:** May not own or be licensor/franchisor of competing brand. May own minority interest, be franchisee of competitor, or manage competitor property.
- **Post-term:** NONE. No post-term noncompete. This is franchisee-favorable.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue:** U.S. District Court, Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria); or state court in Fairfax County, Virginia or New York, New York. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Governing law:** New York (without choice-of-law principles), subject to Lanham Act and state law
- **Arbitration:** Only in Puerto Rico
- **State addenda may override:** California, Illinois, Maryland, and other states have addenda that limit enforceability of venue selection and choice-of-law provisions

### Litigation History
Five pending actions, all involving Hilton Worldwide or HFH LLC. The most significant are four antitrust class actions alleging hotel pricing algorithm collusion through IDeaS, Amadeus, and CoStar/STR data exchange platforms. While these target Hilton Worldwide (not HFH LLC specifically), adverse outcomes could affect the entire system. Six concluded actions include state attorney general resort fee settlements (Texas $2.1M, Nebraska $300K), terminated franchise disputes (all settled with mutual releases), and a Hawaii resort fee class action ($178K).

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

Canopy by Hilton does NOT provide any financial performance representation. The FDD explicitly states:

> "We do not make any representations about a franchisee's future financial performance or the past financial performance of company-owned or franchised outlets. We also do not authorize our employees or representatives to make any such representations either orally or in writing."

The absence of FPR is notable given the $64M–$142M investment requirement. Prospective franchisees must independently evaluate market economics, typically through the market study the franchisor recommends and may require (Item 7, Note 3). The franchisor's acceptance of a market study "is not a financial performance representation on our part or a ratification of the projections performed by the consultant."

If an existing outlet is being purchased, actual records of that outlet may be provided. Any other financial information or projections should be reported to William Fortier (SVP Development Americas), the FTC, and state regulatory agencies.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Pure royalty vehicle:** Operating expenses are less than 1% of revenue ($11.4M vs $1.5B). The entity exists solely to collect franchise fees and distribute them to the parent.

2. **Heavy related-party dependency:** $703.9M in "other receivables due from Hilton affiliates" represents 55% of total assets. These receivables are settled periodically through distributions. The entity's cash position is minimal ($317K).

3. **Deferred revenue growth:** $558.9M in deferred revenues (up from $524.2M) reflects the backlog of upfront franchise fees being recognized over contract terms. This is a sign of continued system growth and new contract signings.

4. **Graduate brand acquisition:** In May 2024, HFH LLC acquired Graduate franchise contracts as part of Hilton's broader Graduate brand acquisition, recording $85.3M in franchise contract intangible assets (15-year useful life). This was funded by a short-term note from an affiliate, settled in February 2024 (sic — likely 2025).

5. **Debt guarantee risk:** HFH LLC unconditionally and irrevocably guarantees certain Hilton debt obligations maturing 2025–2033. All assets and franchise contracts are pledged as collateral. While no guarantee liability is recorded (due to parent/subsidiary relationship), this represents systemic risk to the franchise entity.

6. **No US income tax provision:** HFH LLC is treated as a disregarded entity for US tax purposes; all income flows through to Hilton Worldwide's consolidated return.

7. **Revenue growth:** 9.1% growth in total revenues (2023 to 2024) driven primarily by franchise royalty fee growth ($1.33B to $1.43B), reflecting both room rate increases and new hotel openings across all Hilton brands (not just Canopy).

- New York choice-of-law and Virginia venue provisions may not be enforceable
- Material modifications require prior delivery of updated FDD (Corp. Code §31125)

**Hawaii:**
- General release shall not relieve franchisor from Hawaii franchise law liability

**Illinois:**
- Governing law, venue, and choice-of-law provisions will not waive Illinois Franchise Disclosure Act rights
- Act governs Illinois licensees; waiver provisions are void (Section 41)

**Maryland:**
- Maryland law may supersede FA regarding termination and renewal
