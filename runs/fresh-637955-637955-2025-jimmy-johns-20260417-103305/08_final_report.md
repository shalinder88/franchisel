## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
Jimmy John's Franchisor SPV, LLC is a Delaware LLC formed June 12, 2017 as part of a securitization transaction. It is the franchisor of record but delegates all operational support to JJF under a management agreement ($31.3M management fee in 2024). The entity sits within a multi-layered corporate structure: JJF SPV → JJ Funding LLC → JJ SPV Guarantor LLC → JJBC LLC → JJ LLC → JJ Holding Company LLC → IRB Holding Corp → Inspire Brands, Inc.

The predecessor franchisor, Jimmy John's Franchise, LLC, operated the system from 1993–2017. Inspire Brands acquired the JJ system in October 2019 and is also parent to Arby's (3,365 units), Buffalo Wild Wings (1,323), Dunkin' (9,768), Baskin-Robbins (2,245), and Sonic (3,461). Additional affiliated franchise programs via Roark Capital include Subway (19,502), GoTo Foods brands (Auntie Anne's, Cinnabon, Jamba, McAlister's, Moe's, Schlotzsky's, Carvel), CKE (Carl's Jr./Hardee's), Driven Holdings (Meineke, CARSTAR, Take 5, etc.), and others.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees**:
- Standard initial franchise fee: $35,000 (non-refundable)
- Reopening: $5,000 (good standing) or $12,500
- Non-Traditional: $17,500 (pro-rated)
- Development fee: $10,000 per additional restaurant under DRA
- Design: $1,200 PSL / $750–$1,200 PKL

**Ongoing Fees**:
- Royalty: 6% of weekly Gross Sales (auto-debited via EDTA)
- Advertising & Development Fund: 4.5% of weekly Gross Sales (2.25% for Non-Traditional)
- Local marketing: 0.5% of weekly Gross Sales (required)
- Cooperative advertising: up to 2% of Gross Sales
- **Total ongoing burden: up to 13% of Gross Sales**

Multiple incentive programs (NRO, Deeper NRO, SDM, Deeper SDM, Drive-Thru Remodel/Relocation, VetFran, Pioneer, Non-Traditional College/University) can reduce royalties to as low as 1% in Year 1, ramping to standard 6% over 3–5 years. Incentive programs expire March 31, 2026.

**Event-Driven Fees**: Transfer ($2,500–$12,500), successor franchise ($10,000), relocation ($2,500 + costs), training ($1,500–$5,000/person), convention ($500), management fee ($600/person/day + expenses), non-compliance ($250–$1,000), late fee (10%), interest (1.5%/month), audit costs, liquidated damages (up to 36 months × avg monthly royalty).

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
The franchisor exercises tight supply chain control:
- **Trade Secret Food Products** (bread dough, meats, proprietary formulations): Exclusive sourcing from franchisor, designated affiliates, or specified sources only
- **Branded Products**: Same exclusive requirement
- **Equipment and POS**: Must purchase from designated suppliers
- **Insurance**: Minimum A.M. Best A-VII rating; specified coverages (GL, auto, workers' comp, EPLI, business interruption, property); franchisor and affiliates named as additional insureds
- **Third-Party Delivery Platforms**: May be required at franchisor's discretion

Supplier approval takes up to 30 days. Franchisor may charge evaluation fees. Franchisor and affiliates have the right to receive payments from suppliers and use amounts without restriction. Officers currently own no interest in suppliers except via Inspire Brands.

### Training
- **3-Week Initial Training**: 6 hrs classroom + 117–127 hrs OJT (Champaign, IL or Atlanta, GA)
- **4-Week Apprenticeship**: Required for Operations Partner
- **1-Week Training**: Available for experienced managers (6 hrs classroom + 34–37 hrs OJT)
- **District Manager Training**: Virtual + in-person workshop; required starting with 3rd restaurant; 1 DM per 3–5 restaurants

**Shift Coverage**: 10 certified-manager shifts/week (14/week during first 12 months of first restaurant). Operations Partner must work 5 half-shifts/week at first restaurant.

### Marketing Fund
The Advertising & Development Fund collects 4.5% of weekly Gross Sales. Spending breakdown and audit status are not detailed in the FDD beyond the Item 11 disclosure that the fund exists and is managed by the franchisor.

### Technology
Computer/POS system must be from designated suppliers. Video security may be required. Franchisor may charge for future computer system support and software upgrades.

### Reporting
All fees auto-debited via EDTA. If Gross Sales not reported, franchisor may debit 120% of prior week amounts. Weekly reporting required.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
**Under Franchise Agreement**: No exclusive territory of any kind. Franchisor retains the right to:
- Establish other JJ restaurants anywhere except at franchisee's specific premises
- Establish other brands and distribution channels (including internet)
- Allow any competing activity within franchisee's delivery area
- Currently sells Jimmy Chips® at national retail stores and online

**Under Development Rights Agreement**: Limited territorial exclusivity within a defined Territory (typically city/cities), with a major carve-out: Non-Traditional Locations (military bases, universities, airports, stadiums, food courts, hotels, hospitals, etc.) are excluded from exclusivity.

### Delivery Area
Assigned but non-exclusive. May be reduced at any time for any or no reason. If reduced, franchisee must immediately comply; no liability for sales reduction. Third-party delivery platforms may be required.

### Channel Restrictions
Franchisee may not use any distribution channels other than the restaurant premises and authorized delivery. No solicitation restrictions except delivery area boundaries.

### Encroachment Risk
**High**. No territory protection under the FA means the franchisor or another franchisee could open a competing JJ restaurant next door (other than at the same specific premises). Under the DRA, Non-Traditional Location carve-outs significantly limit the territorial exclusivity even within the defined Territory.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 10 years from opening date
- **Renewal**: One 10-year successor franchise; requires: full compliance, timely notice, remodel to current standards (regardless of cost), sign **then-current franchise agreement** (which "may differ materially"), pay $10,000 fee, sign release (where state law permits)
- **"Materially different" risk**: Explicitly disclosed that successor franchise terms may be materially different from original agreement

### Transfer
- Franchisor consent required (will not unreasonably withhold if conditions met)
- Transfer fee: $2,500 (family), $7,500 (existing franchisee ≥1 year), $12,500 (new franchisee)
- No transfer until restaurant opens
- 60-day lookback: no defaults allowed in period before request or between request and closing
- Transferee must sign current FA, qualify, train, upgrade/remodel
- Franchisor ROFR to match any offer
- DRA development rights: not assignable at all

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: Only if franchisor breaches and fails to cure
- **By franchisor without cause**: First 30 days only (evaluation period)
- **Curable defaults**: 24 hrs (delivery stoppage); 3 days (health/safety); 10 days (monetary to franchisor, insurance); 30 days (operational, monetary to third parties)
- **Non-curable defaults**: 13 categories including failure to secure site (12 months), failure to open (16 months), abandonment, unapproved transfers, material misrepresentation, felony conviction, repeated defaults (even if cured), cross-termination with other JJ agreements

### Death/Disability
9 months to assign to approved party. Franchisor may manage restaurant at $600/person/day + expenses.

### Noncompete
- **During term**: No ownership or services in competitive business **anywhere** (competitive = >50% non-beverage revenue from submarine/hero/deli/wrapped sandwiches)
- **Post-term**: 2 years at: (1) restaurant premises, (2) within 3 miles of premises, (3) within 3 miles of any JJ restaurant existing at signing, (4) within 1 mile of any JJ restaurant existing at termination/expiration
- Same restrictions apply after transfer

### Dispute Resolution
- **Litigation only** (no arbitration, no mediation)
- **Forum**: Courts closest to franchisor's then-current principal business address (currently Atlanta, GA)
- **Governing law**: Georgia
- **State overrides**: MD, MN, ND have riders allowing forum/law in home state; MN prohibits liquidated damages/termination penalties; ND noncompete generally unenforceable

### Litigation History
11 cases disclosed (10 resolved, 1 pending fee recovery):
- **CWL Investments (2017)**: Arbitration — panel found JJF breached transfer provisions; awarded $4.87M total
- **In Re Overtime (2014)**: Consolidated FLSA class action — court found JJF NOT joint employer; settled $1.835M
- **Martin/Erwin (2020)**: "All-Natural" cookie class action — settled $690K (supplier-funded)
- **C&C Resources (2021)**: IFDA §705/20 violation (untimely nonrenewal notice) — $0 damages awarded
- **Maryland consent order (2025)**: FDD omitted former franchisee contact info — $30K penalty
- 3 affiliate disclosures (Arby's/Dunkin' no-poach; Dunkin' cyber-attack)
