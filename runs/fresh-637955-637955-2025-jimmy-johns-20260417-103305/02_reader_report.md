# Reader Report — Jimmy John's Franchisor SPV, LLC (637955-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand**: JIMMY JOHN'S® — gourmet deli sandwiches, fresh-baked breads, carry-out/delivery/dine-in
2. **Franchisor entity**: Jimmy John's Franchisor SPV, LLC (Delaware LLC, formed June 12, 2017), securitization vehicle
3. **Parent**: Inspire Brands, Inc. (acquired October 18, 2019); affiliated via Roark Capital Management
4. **System size** (as of Dec 29, 2024): 2,689 total (2,647 franchised, 42 company-owned); 2,668 single-branded, 20 multi-brand
5. **Investment range**: $366,200–$728,200 (including initial real estate lease costs)
6. **Initial franchise fee**: $35,000 standard; $5,000–$12,500 for reopenings; $17,500 for Non-Traditional (pro-rated)
7. **Royalty**: 6% of weekly Gross Sales (reduced under incentive programs: NRO/SDM ramp 1%→6% over 3–5 years)
8. **Advertising fund**: Up to 4.5% of weekly Gross Sales (currently full 4.5%); 2.25% for Non-Traditional
9. **Item 19 AUV**: $986,095 overall (2,529 restaurants); 1st quartile $1,455,501; median $935,022; 4th quartile $596,390
10. **Drive-thru vs. no drive-thru**: Drive-thru AUV $1,082,680 (936 restaurants); No drive-thru AUV $926,973 (1,571 restaurants)
11. **Franchise term**: 10 years; one 10-year successor franchise available on then-current terms (may be materially different)
12. **Territory**: No exclusive territory under Franchise Agreement; Development Rights Agreement provides limited exclusivity subject to Non-Traditional Location carve-outs
13. **Net growth**: +45 units in 2024 (88 opened, 1 terminated, 13 non-renewed, 29 ceased operations-other)
14. **Financial health**: Net income $97.6M (2024) on revenue $153.7M; total assets $777.6M; member's equity $755.2M; securitization notes $1.049B outstanding (held by parent entity JJ Issuer)
15. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Georgia); noncompete 2 years post-term within 3 miles

- **People of IL v. JJE/JJF (2016)**: Employee non-compete enforcement — consent decree: $100K, ceased enforcing. Expired Dec 2019.

- **Conrad/Butler v. JJF (2018)**: Anti-poach provisions class action — settled: $10K. Dismissed Nov 2021.
- **Martin/Erwin v. JJF (2020)**: "All-Natural" cookie labeling — settled collectively for $690K (supplier-funded). Dismissed Oct 2021.
- **Patel v. JJF (2020)**: Oral franchise/IFDA claim — settled: $10K. Dismissed Oct 2021.
- **C&C Resources v. JJF (2021)**: Arbitration re transfer denials and IFDA claims — panel found IFDA §705/20 violation (untimely nonrenewal notice) but awarded $0 damages. JJF pursuing fee recovery.
- **Polizzi v. JJ LLC (2023)**: BIPA class action — dismissed without prejudice June 2025.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Franchise Agreement**: NO exclusive territory. No non-exclusive territory.
- Franchisor retains right to establish any competing outlets anywhere (except at franchisee's specific premises)
- Franchisor may establish other channels (including internet sales)
- Currently sells Jimmy Chips® at national retail stores and online
- Delivery area assigned but non-exclusive; may be reduced at any time for any reason
- Third-Party Delivery Platforms may be required

- **Development Rights Agreement**: Provides limited territorial exclusivity within defined Territory (typically city/cities)
- Franchisor may not place other traditional JJ restaurants in Territory while DRA is in effect
- **Major carve-out**: Non-Traditional Locations (military, universities, airports, stadiums, food courts, hotels, hospitals, etc.) are excluded from exclusivity
- No options, rights of first refusal, or similar rights for additional franchises
- No minimum sales volume requirement to maintain franchise

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term & Renewal
- **Initial term**: 10 years from opening date
- **Successor franchise**: One 10-year renewal available
- **Renewal conditions**: Full compliance, timely notice, remodel to current standards (regardless of cost), sign THEN-CURRENT franchise agreement (may be materially different), pay $10,000 successor fee, sign release (where state law allows)
- **DRA**: Not renewable

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: Only if franchisor breaches and fails to cure after notice
- **By franchisor without cause**: Only during first 30 days (evaluation period)
- **Curable defaults**: 24 hrs (delivery stoppage); 3 days (health/safety); 10 days (monetary defaults to franchisor, insurance); 30 days (operational defaults, monetary defaults to third parties)
- **Non-curable defaults**: Failure to secure site (12 months), failure to open (16 months), opening before approval, training failure, abandonment, loss of premises, unapproved transfers, material misrepresentation, felony conviction, dishonest conduct, unauthorized disclosure, repeated defaults (even if cured)

### Transfer
- No transfer until restaurant opens
- Franchisor consent required (will not unreasonably withhold if conditions met)
- **Transfer fee**: $2,500 (family), $7,500 (existing franchisee ≥1 year), $12,500 (new franchisee)
- **Conditions**: Transferee meets qualifications, not competitive, all amounts paid, no defaults in 60-day lookback, training completed, lease acquired, sign current FA, remodel/upgrade, sign release
- **ROFR**: Franchisor may match any offer
- **Post-term option**: Franchisor may purchase restaurant at FMV and/or sublease premises

### Death/Disability
- 9 months to assign to approved party
- Franchisor may manage restaurant ($600/day + expenses) if no qualified manager

### Noncompete
- **During term**: No ownership or services in competitive business anywhere (competitive = >50% non-beverage revenue from submarine/hero/deli/wrapped sandwiches)
- **Post-term**: 2 years; at Restaurant premises, within 3 miles of premises, within 3 miles of any JJ restaurant existing at signing, within 1 mile of any JJ restaurant existing at termination/expiration

### Dispute Resolution
- **Forum**: Courts closest to franchisor's then-current principal business address (currently Atlanta, GA)
- **Governing law**: Georgia (subject to state franchise law)
- **No arbitration** (litigation only)
- **No mediation requirement** noted

### State Overrides (from state addenda)
- **California**: Fast Food Act compliance; anti-waiver provision
- **Hawaii**: Filing notice; anti-waiver
- **Maryland**: Release limited re: MD Franchise Law; forum in MD for MD law claims; 3-year statute of limitations
- **Michigan**: See front-matter notice (pp.5–6) — prohibits certain unfair provisions
- **Minnesota**: 90-day termination notice (60 to cure); 180-day nonrenewal notice; no out-of-state litigation requirement; no liquidated damages/termination penalties/judgment notes; MN law applies
- **North Dakota**: Liquidated damages may not be enforceable; release limited; noncompete generally unenforceable; ND law applies; forum in ND for ND law claims
- **Washington**: Assurance of Discontinuance — removed no-poach provision
