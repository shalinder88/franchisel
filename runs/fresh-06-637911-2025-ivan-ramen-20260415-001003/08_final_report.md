## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** Ivan Ramen is an extremely early-stage franchise system with 1 company-owned restaurant in New York City (operated by affiliate Ivan Ramen USA LLC since May 2016) and 0 operating franchised units as of December 31, 2024. One signed franchise agreement exists with Venetian Las Vegas Gaming LLC for a food hall unit not yet open. A UK master franchise was sold in September 2023.

2. **Entity structure:** Ivan Ramen Franchising LLC is a New York LLC formed in July 2021. Its parent is Ivan Ramen Holdings Co, LLC (which acquired 100% ownership effective January 1, 2024). IP is held by Ivan Ramen IP Holdings LLC (Delaware) and cross-licensed to the franchisor under a 20-year renewable agreement with no termination provision.

3. **Ownership and history:** Founded by Ivan Orkin (CEO, executive chef) and Chad Combs (COO). The original Ivan Ramen restaurant opened in NYC in 2016. A "Slurp Shop" operated 2013–2020. Ghost kitchen licensing to DoorDash ceased in 2024. The brand has a tangential concept called "Ivan Mazemen" being developed only in the UK.

4. **Investment range:** $1,024,500–$1,982,100 for a single restaurant unit. Construction/leasehold improvements dominate at $500K–$1.1M. The ADA investment is $1,106,500–$2,302,100 for 3–10 units.

5. **Fee burden:** 5% continuing royalty on Gross Revenues; up to 1.5% system brand contribution (not yet established); up to 1.0% system technology fee (currently 0%); $7,333/month mandatory proprietary product purchases; $35,000 successor term fee. Total ongoing fee load could reach 7.5% of Gross Revenues plus monthly proprietary costs.

6. **Item 19:** No financial performance representation is made. This is a significant absence for a franchise requiring over $1 million in investment with no operating franchised units providing performance data.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk indicators:** State-mandated "financial condition" special risk flag; franchisor had only $5,992 in cash at 12/31/2024 (down from $139,242 a year earlier); members' equity of $1,040; net losses in all three audited years; $120K emergency capital contribution in February 2025. Three states (IL, MD, ND) have imposed fee deferral requirements due to franchisor financial concerns.

8. **Financial health:** The franchisor earned only $5,000 in franchise fee revenue in 2024 (from one Las Vegas unit with a reduced $25,000 fee). General and administrative expenses run approximately $80K/year. The entity is cash-flow negative and entirely dependent on capital contributions from ownership to maintain operations.

9. **Growth trajectory:** The system contracted in 2024 — the one franchised ghost kitchen (DoorDash) ceased operations, dropping from 2 total outlets to 1. One new unit (Las Vegas food hall) is signed but not open. One new franchise projected for New York. Growth is aspirational rather than demonstrated.

10. **Territory:** Non-exclusive territory with extensive franchisor reservations for alternative channels (internet, ghost kitchens, food trucks, etc.), nontraditional locations (hotels, airports, military bases, etc.), and national/institutional accounts. Franchisee has virtually no channel exclusivity.

11. **Renewal:** One 10-year successor term available; $35,000 fee; must sign then-current form of franchise agreement which may have materially different terms. General release and restaurant refurbishment required.

12. **Disputes:** Litigation only in New York (no arbitration/mediation); New York law governs. Multiple state overrides: IL, IN, and ND override to their own governing law; IL and ND void out-of-state venue clauses.

13. **Special risks:** Four state-mandated risk factors: out-of-state dispute resolution, short operating history, minimum sales quota ($1.25M/year with loss of territory protection), and financial condition concerns. Additionally, three states (IL, MD, ND) have imposed fee deferral requirements.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Ivan Ramen Franchising LLC was formed in New York in July 2021 as the franchising arm of the Ivan Ramen brand, which originated with Ivan Orkin's ramen restaurants in Tokyo and later New York City. The franchisor is wholly owned by Ivan Ramen Holdings Co, LLC (as of January 1, 2024). The intellectual property — trademarks, system, and know-how — is held by Ivan Ramen IP Holdings LLC, a Delaware entity, which has cross-licensed it to the franchisor under a non-exclusive license with a 20-year initial term and automatic 20-year renewals. This license requires the franchisor to pay a licensing fee of 10% of its gross revenues to the IP holder, reduced as necessary to avoid bringing the franchisor's net income below $100,000 per fiscal year.

The franchise concept is a casual dining restaurant of 2,500–3,500 square feet in high-traffic urban areas, specializing in ramen dishes, Japanese appetizers, rice bowls, and alcoholic beverages, for on- and off-premises consumption.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Initial Franchise Fee: $35,000 (non-refundable; one reduced fee of $25,000 was charged in FY 2024 for an atypical food hall location)
- Area Development Fee: $35,000 per committed unit (3–10 units = $105,000–$350,000; non-refundable; credited against each unit's IFF)

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Continuing Royalty: 5% of Gross Revenues (monthly, 15th of following month; fixed during term)
- System Brand Contribution: Up to 1.5% of Gross Revenues (fund not yet established)
- System Technology Fee: Up to 1.0% of Gross Revenues (currently 0%; reserved right)
- Proprietary Products: $7,333/month estimated (noodles $2,750, dashi/specialty $1,250, soup concentrates $3,333)

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Successor Term Fee: $35,000
- Transfer Fee: $10,000 (both FA and ADA)
- Testing Fee: $1,000–$1,500 (for alternative supplier testing)
- Additional Training: $2,500/person (beyond initial 6)
- On-Site Training: $2,500/day (optional, not required)

- Late Charge: 3% of overdue amount; $50/month for late financial statements
- Audit Expenses: If Gross Revenue understatement ≥2%, franchisee pays audit + amounts due + interest at highest legal rate; if ≥8%, franchisor can terminate

**Note on Alcohol:** If state/local law restricts royalty collection on alcohol sales, non-alcohol revenue percentages are increased to equalize the dollar amount.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
The franchisor requires purchase of proprietary products — noodles, dashi/specialty ingredients, and soup concentrates — from designated suppliers (currently Sun Noodles and Mutual Trading Company, both unaffiliated). The estimated monthly cost of proprietary products is $7,333. The franchisor reserves the right to add proprietary products and to receive revenue from suppliers (promotional allowances, rebates, commissions, etc.) — all retained as franchisor's exclusive property.

The POS system must be Toast (designated, unaffiliated). OpenTable is required for online reservations. No officer of the franchisor owns interest in any approved supplier. Alternative supplier approval requires written request; franchisor has 30 days to respond; testing costs $1,000–$1,500 at franchisee expense. No written supplier approval criteria exist.

Required purchases represent approximately 1–5% of establishment costs and 5–10% of ongoing operating expenses.

### Insurance Requirements
Comprehensive GL ($1M aggregate/$500K per occurrence), fire/extended coverage, business interruption (120 days minimum), auto liability ($1M/$2M/$3M), workers' comp ($1M employer's liability), employment practices liability ($250K), builders'/contractors' insurance during construction. Annual premiums estimated at $55,000–$65,000.

### Training Program
The Initial Training Program is 106 hours (19 classroom + 87 on-the-job) over approximately 5 days, conducted at the franchisor's affiliate restaurant and facilities in New York City. The program covers 14 subjects: ramen introduction, prep/line training, ingredients, inventory, equipment (negi machine), front-of-house (general, host, server), manager duties, POS, business administration, HR, and marketing. Training for up to 6 people is included in the IFF. Additional trainees: $2,500/person. Franchisee pays all travel, lodging, and living expenses.

The franchisor conducts the ITP approximately twice per year. Annual conferences/conventions may be required. Minimum instructor experience: 1 year in the field and 1 year in Ivan Ramen operations.

### Technology Stack
Toast POS system (4 terminals typical): ~$3,600 startup + $600/month. High-speed internet and email required. Franchisor has unrestricted access to all POS data. No contractual limit on franchisor's ability to require upgrades or additions. Franchisor has no contractual obligation for maintenance, repairs, or updates.

The franchisor does not currently have proprietary software but reserves the right to develop it. A System Technology Fee of up to 1.0% of Gross Revenues is reserved but currently not charged.

### Marketing Fund
No System Brand Fund or Regional Brand Fund has been established. When formed, franchisees will contribute up to 1.5% of Gross Revenues. The fund will not be a trust and the franchisor will not be a fiduciary. No obligation to spend in franchisee's area or in proportion to contributions. Annual statement of income/expenses available on request (not audited). Fund will not be used solely for franchise sales (but website may include franchise solicitations). Company-owned units will contribute the same percentage.

Franchisee may not maintain own website, URL, domain, or social media pages for the business without prior written approval (revocable). The franchisor owns all website and social media content copyrights.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
Non-exclusive territory consisting of all or a specified part of a city or municipality around the restaurant outlet, as determined by the franchisor. No minimum territory size is specified. The territory will be described in Exhibit A to the Franchise Agreement.

### Territorial Protections and Carve-Outs
Within the territory during the FA term, the franchisor will not operate a company-owned Ivan Ramen Restaurant or grant a franchise for the same type of business. However, the franchisor reserves extensive rights:

1. **Alternative Channels:** Internet, e-commerce, food trucks, ghost kitchens, catalogs, telemarketing, mail order, television sales, toll-free numbers — all reserved by franchisor within and outside territory.
2. **Nontraditional Locations:** Resorts, food retailers, schools, universities, hospitals, hotels, government facilities, condominiums, department stores, military bases — franchisor may place Ivan Ramen units at these locations within the territory.
3. **National/Regional/Institutional Accounts:** Reserved exclusively by franchisor; may give franchisee opportunity to fulfill orders but has no obligation.
4. **Acquisitions:** Franchisor may acquire/operate competitive businesses under different marks, even within the territory.

### Minimum Sales Quota
Franchisee must achieve at least $1,250,000 in Gross Revenues per calendar year. Failure does not allow termination but permits the franchisor to remove all territorial protections on notice. This effectively makes the territory contingent on performance.

### Channel Restrictions
Franchisee may only sell at retail from the restaurant. No wholesale distribution. Delivery only through franchisor-approved third-party aggregators. No alternative channel rights. No internet sales. No catalog or direct marketing.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
Initial term: 10 years from the date the franchisor countersigns. One successor term of 10 years is available. Renewal conditions: 6–9 months advance notice; compliance throughout initial term; current on all monetary obligations to franchisor, affiliates, landlord, and suppliers; evidence of lease renewal capability; sign general release (Exhibit G); retraining if required; pay $35,000 successor fee; refurbish restaurant to then-current standards; sign franchisor's then-current form of franchise agreement.

**Materially different risk:** The successor franchise agreement may have materially different terms and conditions, but will not require a new initial franchise fee, will incorporate successor franchise rights, will maintain the same territory boundaries, and will not impose a Continuing Royalty greater than that imposed on similarly-situated new franchisees.

### Transfer Provisions
No transfer without franchisor consent. Transfer fee: $10,000. Transferee must apply, interview, demonstrate qualifications, complete ITP, acquire permits, sign new FA (for balance of original term), upgrade restaurant to current standards. Franchisor has right of first refusal to match any offer. Franchisee and owners must sign general release. Total Sale Price must not be "so excessive" as to jeopardize business viability.

### Termination and Death/Disability
Termination by franchisee: by mutual written agreement or any grounds permitted by law. Non-payment of royalties after cure notice is deemed voluntary termination.

Curable defaults (30-day cure, 5-day cure for payment defaults): non-payment, false reports, unauthorized sales, supplier delinquency, unauthorized mark use, advertising violations, missing permits, lease defaults, staffing failures, and many more (19 enumerated categories).

Non-curable defaults (immediate on notice): failure to open within 15 months, ceasing operations 3+ days, misrepresentation, loss of location, felony conviction, unauthorized transfer, non-compete violation, training failure, revenue concealment, 8%+ revenue understatement, audit refusal, repeated defaults within 36 months, health/safety jeopardy, and many more (34 enumerated categories). Automatic termination without notice on bankruptcy, insolvency, receivership, dissolution.

Death/disability: Rights pass to estate; must provide acceptable Operating Principal within 1 month; estate has 6 months to sell. Franchisor may operate business during transition — management fee: greater of 2× salary or 10% of weekly Gross Revenue, in addition to royalties. $1,000 admin fee.

### Noncompete Restrictions
**During term:** No involvement in any competing business anywhere in the United States.
**Post-term:** 2 years within the territory, within 10 miles of the perimeter of the territory, or within 10 miles of any Ivan Ramen territory.

State overrides: California — post-term noncompete may not be enforceable; no-poach provision will not be enforced. North Dakota — noncompetes generally considered unenforceable.

### Dispute Resolution
No arbitration or mediation. Litigation only in state or federal court of competent jurisdiction in the state/county/judicial district of the franchisor's principal place of business (currently Westchester County, New York). New York law governs.

Franchisee waives right to punitive/exemplary damages (may not be enforceable in CA, IN, ND).

State overrides: Illinois — forum outside IL is void; IL law governs. Indiana — IN law governs; punitive damages waiver deleted. North Dakota — venue outside ND deleted; ND law governs; jury trial and punitive damages waivers deleted.

### Litigation History
No litigation required to be disclosed (Item 3). No bankruptcy required (Item 4).

### Key Financial Observations

**Leverage and Capitalization:** Members' equity collapsed from $162,003 to $1,040 over three years, entirely due to cumulative operating losses of ~$210,000. The entity had no debt but was nearly capital-depleted by year-end 2024.

**Cash Position:** Cash declined from $209,531 to $5,992 — a 97% drawdown. Operating cash burn was $63,904 in 2024, and $69,346 was additionally lent to related parties. Without the February 2025 capital contribution of $120,000, the entity would have been unable to fund ongoing operations.

**Related Party Transactions:** The franchisor lent $25,848 to Ivan Ramen USA LLC and $1,200 to Ivan Ramen Holdings Co. Inc., all with no payment terms and no interest. The 100% ownership of the company was transferred to Ivan Ramen Holdings Co. LLC effective January 1, 2024, making it a single-member LLC. The IP license requires 10% of gross revenues be paid to the IP holder (immaterial given near-zero revenues).

**Revenue:** The $5,000 in FY 2024 franchise fee revenue, combined with $20,000 in unearned revenue on the balance sheet, appears to relate to the Las Vegas franchise agreement (total fee $25,000, of which $5,000 recognized and $20,000 deferred pending pre-opening performance obligations).

**Asset Composition:** 82% of total assets ($27,048 of $33,040) are unsecured loans to related parties with no terms — essentially uncollectible for purposes of liquidity assessment.

## K. Contradictions

1. **Ghost kitchen classification** — The 2023 franchised outlet is counted in Item 20 tables as a "franchised" outlet but has a footnote stating it was "not an Ivan Ramen Restaurant but rather, an Ivan Ramen 'ghost kitchen' operated by DoorDash." The cover page states the franchise offers "Ivan Ramen Restaurant Businesses." This is a borderline classification issue rather than a true contradiction. **Items: 1, 20. Resolution: Disclosed via footnote.**

2. **State of formation** — Item 1 states the franchisor is "a New York limited liability company organized in July, 2021." Note 1 to the FY 2024 financial statements states it "was formed on July 12, 2021, as a Delaware limited liability company." One of these is incorrect. The franchisor's principal address is in New York and all operations appear to be NY-based. **Items: 1, Exhibit C Note 1. Resolution: Unresolved — possible conversion from DE to NY or error in the financial statements.**

3. **Revenue recognition** — $5,000 in franchise fee revenue was recognized in FY 2024, and $20,000 appears as unearned revenue. The Las Vegas franchise fee was $25,000. This accounts for the full $25,000 fee. However, the financial statement notes state revenue recognition for initial franchise fees should be "on a straight-line basis over the term of the respective franchise agreement" (10 years). If so, only ~$2,500 would be recognized in 2024 for the Las Vegas unit (assuming mid-year signing). The $5,000 recognized may include other revenue or reflect a different allocation. **Items: 5, 21, Exhibit C Note 2. Resolution: Unclear but immaterial given the small amounts.**
