**Material findings by state:**

### Hawaii
- Standard registration boilerplate
- Release language does not relieve franchisor from Hawaii franchise law liability
- 7-day disclosure requirement before agreement or payment

### Illinois
- **Illinois law governs** (supersedes NY law)
- Forum outside Illinois is void (but arbitration outside IL is allowed)
- Termination/non-renewal rights per IL Franchise Disclosure Act §§19-20
- **Fee deferral:** IL AG required deferral of all initial franchise/development fees until franchisor satisfies pre-opening obligations and franchisee commences operations — imposed due to franchisor's financial condition

### Indiana
- **Indiana law governs** (supersedes NY law)
- Unilateral termination without good cause prohibited (IC 23-2-2.7-1(7))
- Release language does not relieve from IN franchise law liability
- Noncompete enforcement provisions (§12.04) do not apply in IN
- "Withholding of Consent — Exclusive Remedy" provision does not apply in IN
- Jury trial and punitive damages waivers deleted
- Indemnification limited (no indemnification for franchisor's negligence or proper use of franchisor's materials)

### Maryland
- **Fee deferral:** MD Securities Commissioner required deferral of all initial fees until pre-opening obligations completed; ADA fees deferred until first franchise opens — due to franchisor's financial condition
- General release does not apply to liability under MD Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law
- MD law may supersede FA on termination/renewal
- Franchisee may sue in MD for MD franchise law claims (3-year statute of limitations)
- Bankruptcy termination may not be enforceable under federal law
- Franchise questionnaires/acknowledgments subject to NASAA policy

### New York
- Franchisor cannot use negotiation to get terms less favorable than FDD
- Franchisor represents no knowing material omissions or untrue statements
- General release for renewal/transfer preserves GBL Article 33 rights (§687(4) and (5))
- Injunction consent modified to consent to seeking (not entry)
- Choice of law does not waive GBL Article 33 rights
- Disclosure document must be provided at earlier of first personal meeting or 10 business days before agreement

### North Dakota
- **ND law governs** (supersedes NY law)
- Venue outside ND deleted
- Release language does not relieve from ND franchise law liability
- General release on renewal deleted
- Consent to termination/liquidated damages deleted
- **Noncompetes generally considered unenforceable** (NDCC §9-08-06)
- Jury trial waiver deleted
- Punitive damages waiver deleted
- **Fee deferral:** ND requires deferral of initial fees until all franchisor obligations fulfilled and franchisee commences business — due to financial capability concerns

### Wisconsin
- WI Fair Dealership Act (Ch. 135) governs
- 90-day notice for termination/cancellation/non-renewal/substantial change
- 60-day cure period for deficiencies (supersedes shorter FA cure periods)
