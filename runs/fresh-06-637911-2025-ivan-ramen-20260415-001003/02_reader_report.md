# Reader Report — Ivan Ramen Franchising LLC (637911-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor:** Ivan Ramen Franchising LLC, a New York LLC formed July 2021; parent Ivan Ramen Holdings Co, LLC; founder/CEO Ivan Orkin; COO/Secretary Chad Combs; Culinary Director Dale Watkins.
2. **System size:** Extremely small — 1 company-owned restaurant (New York City, since May 2016 via affiliate Ivan Ramen USA LLC); 0 operating franchised units in the US as of 12/31/2024. One signed franchise (Venetian Las Vegas food hall, not yet open). One UK master franchise sold Sept 2023.
3. **Investment range:** $1,024,500–$1,982,100 for a single unit; $1,106,500–$2,302,100 under an Area Development Agreement (3–10 units).
4. **Fee burden:** $35,000 initial franchise fee; 5% continuing royalty; up to 1.5% system brand fund (not yet established); up to 1.0% system technology fee (currently 0%); $7,333/month mandatory proprietary products (noodles, dashi, soup concentrates).
5. **Item 19:** No financial performance representation made. Material absence for a $1M+ investment.
6. **Financial condition flag:** Franchisor's total assets only $33,040 as of 12/31/2024; members' equity $1,040. Net losses all three audited years ($51,767 in 2022; $81,337 in 2023; $76,856 in 2024). Revenue only $5,000 in 2024 (one franchise fee). State-mandated "financial condition" special risk flag.
7. **Growth trajectory:** System contracted in 2024 — the one franchised ghost kitchen (DoorDash) ceased operations. One Las Vegas food hall unit signed but not open. Essentially a pre-scale franchise system.
8. **Territory:** Non-exclusive territory; franchisor retains broad rights for alternative channels, ghost kitchens, nontraditional locations, national accounts, e-commerce, even within franchisee's territory.
9. **Renewal:** One successor term of 10 years; $35,000 successor fee; must sign then-current form (materially different terms possible); must refurbish to then-current standards; general release required.
10. **Dispute resolution:** Litigation only in New York (no arbitration/mediation); New York law governs. State addenda may override for CA, IL, IN, MD, NY, ND, WI.
11. **Noncompete:** During term — no competing business anywhere in US. Post-term — 2 years within territory and 10-mile perimeter of any Ivan Ramen territory.
12. **Minimum sales quota:** $1,250,000 gross revenues per calendar year; failure does not terminate franchise but removes territorial protections.
13. **Special risks:** 4 state-mandated risk factors: out-of-state dispute resolution, short operating history, sales performance requirement, and financial condition concerns.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory

**Franchise Agreement:**
- Non-exclusive territory — minimum is all or part of a city/municipality around outlet; no minimum specified size
- Single Restaurant Location per FA; no relocation without approval
- Franchisor will not operate company-owned or grant franchise for same type business *within* territory during FA term, EXCEPT:
  - Alternative channels (internet, e-commerce, food trucks, ghost kitchens, catalogs, telemarketing, etc.) — all reserved by franchisor
  - Nontraditional locations (resorts, supermarkets, schools, hospitals, hotels, military bases, etc.) — all reserved
  - National/Regional/Institutional accounts — all reserved; franchisor may give franchisee opportunity to fulfill orders but has no obligation
  - Acquisition of other brands/chains — franchisor may acquire/operate competitive businesses under different marks within territory
- **Minimum sales quota:** $1,250,000 gross revenues per calendar year; failure → franchisor may remove territorial protections (but cannot terminate)
- No options/rights of first refusal for additional territories
- Sales outside territory prohibited; no alternative channel rights for franchisee

**Area Development Agreement:**
- Non-exclusive Development Territory
- Must open committed number of units per Development Schedule
- Same extensive franchisor reservation of alternative channels, nontraditional locations, national accounts
- ADA not renewable
- Failure to meet Development Schedule → ADA terminable (but existing FAs survive if in compliance)

Extensive list including: non-payment (5-day shortened cure), false reports, unauthorized sales, supplier account delinquency, unauthorized mark use, advertising violations, missing government permits, lease defaults, staffing failures, failure to implement system changes, unsatisfied judgments, non-compliance with any FA provision.

- Pay all money owed; stop using marks/confidential info; cancel "Ivan Ramen" assumed names within 15 days
- Damages on default termination may include lost profits, lost opportunities, reputation damage
- Franchisor may require lease assignment; may take possession if termination for cause
- Return all Brand Standards, software, customer lists, advertising materials
- 15 days to arrange inventory; franchisor has 30-day option to purchase assets at FMV
- Deidentify restaurant if franchisor doesn't take over
- Cease all customer/supplier/employee contacts

### Transfer
- No transfer without consent
- Transfer fee: $10,000
- Transferee must: apply, interview, demonstrate qualifications, complete ITP, acquire permits
- Transferee signs new FA (balance of term); no additional IFF
- Franchisee must cure all defaults, sign general release
- Total Sale Price must not be "so excessive" as to jeopardize viability
- Franchisor has right of first refusal (can match any offer)
- Post-ROFR noncompete: 2 years per Section 12.02

### Death or Disability
- Rights pass to Estate; must provide acceptable Operating Principal within 1 month; $1,000 admin fee
- Estate has 6 months to sell per transfer conditions
- Franchisor may (but need not) operate business during transition — management fee: greater of 2× salary or 10% of weekly Gross Revenue

### Noncompete
- **During term:** No involvement in competing business anywhere in the US
- **Post-term:** 2 years within territory, within 10 miles of territory perimeter, or within 10 miles of any Ivan Ramen territory
- ADA also includes no-poach/non-solicitation provision (subject to state law; CA will not enforce)

### Dispute Resolution
- **No arbitration or mediation** — litigation only
- **Forum:** State or federal court in state/county/judicial district of franchisor's principal place of business (currently Westchester County, NY)
- **Governing law:** New York
- Franchisor may bring injunction actions in any court with jurisdiction
- Franchisee waives right to punitive/exemplary damages (may not be enforceable in some states)
