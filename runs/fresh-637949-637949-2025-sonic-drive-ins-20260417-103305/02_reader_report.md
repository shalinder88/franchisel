# Reader Report — Sonic Drive-Ins 2025 FDD (Filing 637949)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: Sonic Franchising LLC, a Delaware LLC formed 3/23/2011; HQ Atlanta, GA 30328. Franchising since 1974 (via predecessors).
2. **Parent structure**: Sonic Franchising LLC → Sonic Capital LLC → Sonic LLC → Sonic Holding Company → IRB Holding Corp. → Inspire Brands, Inc. (ultimate parent). Inspire is controlled by Roark Capital Management, LLC.
3. **System size**: 3,461 Sonic Drive-Ins at end of 2024 (3,144 franchised, 317 company-owned). Average restaurant age 27 years. Largest concentration: Texas (888 franchised, 42 company-owned).
4. **System trajectory**: Net shrinkage — system lost 60 units in 2024, 25 in 2023, 6 in 2022. Franchised lost 50 in 2024 (30 opened, 12 terminated, 68 ceased operations/other). Company-owned lost 10 (1 opened, 11 closed).
5. **Investment range**: Traditional free-standing $1,676,000–$3,140,900; C-Store $669,200–$1,360,900; Drive-Thru Only $1,609,200–$2,965,900. Initial franchise fee $15,000.
6. **Fee burden**: Royalty 5% of Gross Sales (with NRO incentive reducing to 1%–2.5% in years 1–4); SBF 0.90%; SMF 1.625%–5% (market-dependent); BTF 0.25%. Total ongoing fee load: ~6.4%–11.15% of Gross Sales.
7. **Item 19 AUV**: $1,587,024 across 3,086 qualifying franchised restaurants. Median $1,500,713. 1st quartile average $935,705; 4th quartile average $2,398,950. Highest unit $4,838,846.
8. **Bottom-quartile risk**: 1st quartile AUV of $935,705. At 5% royalty + ~5% marketing/tech fees, that is ~$93K in franchise fees alone, before any operating costs. With a $1.67M+ initial investment, bottom-quartile ROI is deeply challenged.
9. **Securitization**: ~$1,417.3M outstanding under Sonic Securitization Notes. Sonic Franchising LLC is guarantor and has pledged substantially all assets. Legal final maturities extend to 2050/2051.
10. **Territory**: Protected Area radius 0.75–3 miles from front door based on population density. Numerous carve-outs (pre-existing restaurants, non-traditional locations, non-drive-in locations). Not exclusive.
11. **Renewal risk**: 10-year term, 2 additional 10-year renewals. Must sign then-current franchise agreement which "may contain materially different terms." $15,000 renewal fee. Must remodel and sign general release.
12. **Dispute resolution**: Litigation only (no arbitration/mediation) in Atlanta, GA (franchisor HQ county). Georgia law governs.
13. **Gag clause**: Present — "some franchisees signed provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with the Sonic Restaurant network."
14. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Atlanta, GA) — flagged on Special Risks page.

## Pass B: Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — The Franchisor
Sonic Franchising LLC was formed in 2011 as part of a securitization restructuring. The actual day-to-day franchise operations are performed by Sonic Industries Services LLC ("SIS"), an Oklahoma LLC, under a management agreement. If SIS fails, Sonic may replace it but remains accountable. The Sonic brand began in the early 1950s, franchising since 1974.

The franchisor is deeply embedded within the Inspire Brands conglomerate — a multi-brand restaurant company that includes Arby's (3,365 units), Buffalo Wild Wings (1,183 units), Dunkin' (9,768 units), Baskin-Robbins (2,245 units), Jimmy John's (2,689 units), and as of April 2024, Subway (19,502 U.S. units). Through Roark Capital, additional affiliated brands include GoTo Foods (Auntie Anne's, Carvel, Cinnabon, Jamba, McAlister's, Moe's, Schlotzsky's), Primrose Schools, Massage Envy, CKE (Carl's Jr., Hardee's), Driven Holdings (Meineke, Maaco, CARSTAR, Take 5, etc.), ServiceMaster (Merry Maids, Two Men and a Truck), Nothing Bundt Cakes, Mathnasium, i9 Sports, School of Rock, and more.

Three franchise formats:
- **Traditional Drive-In**: Free-standing 1,250–1,500 sq ft building with canopy stalls (6–12 cars), drive-thru, patio. Open 6am–11pm+.
- **Drive-Thru Only**: Free-standing, no stalls/patio.
- **Non-Drive-In Location** (C-Store): Within convenience stores, travel plazas; 1,200–2,500 sq ft.
- **Non-Traditional**: Military bases, airports, stadiums, etc. (Non-Trad Rider modifies FA).

Multi-Brand Location option with Arby's, BWW, Dunkin', Baskin-Robbins, Jimmy John's — subject to both franchise agreements.

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Technology (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Restrictions on Sources
Franchisees must purchase food, beverages, signs, paper products, uniforms, POS, POPS, equipment, technology from designated or approved suppliers. Standards may regulate equipment, supplies, and services. Franchisor provides specifications to approved suppliers (not to franchisees directly).

Supplier approval: No formal written criteria or fee. Typical 30-day response time. Franchisor retains exclusive right to approve all suppliers/distributors. No officer owns interests in approved suppliers except through Inspire Brands equity.

**Purchase concentration**: 16%–25% of establishment purchases, 34%–50% of operating purchases must come from designated/approved suppliers.

**Supplier contributions**: Over $76,750,000 contributed to Sonic in FY2024:

### Item 11 — Franchisor's Assistance, Advertising, Computer Systems, Training

**Pre-opening assistance**: Site evaluation, Sonic Plans & Specifications, Operations Manual access, initial training, written authorization to open, development site acceptance (DA).

**Ongoing assistance**: Operations Manual updates, SMF/SBF/BTF administration, consulting, FAC consultation on significant changes.

**Opening timeline**: 4 months construction typical after permits; 6–12 months from FA signing to opening; 1–2 years from DA signing to first restaurant.

**Advertising**:
- **SBF** (0.90% Gross Sales): Brand programs, advertising production (34%), product cost management (27%), marketing research (12%), customer relations (6%), convention (4%), Sonic Games (5%), mystery shop (1%). 15% admin fee to franchisor (capped at actual costs). Audited by independent CPA.
- **SMF** (allocated from coop contributions): National advertising — TV (27%), digital/internet (53%), agency fees (11%), radio (2%), customer research (5%). Audited.
- **BTF** (0.25% Gross Sales): Technology — software/services (55%), satellite/internet (16%), support (15%), cybersecurity (13%). Audited. Subject to 67% franchisee vote to continue post-3/2026.
- **Advertising Cooperatives**: DMA-based, 1 vote per restaurant (company/franchise equal). Must contribute ≥3.25% of Gross Sales (up to 5% in developing/new markets).
- **FAC**: 40 members selected by franchisor, 8 executive committee, 13 regional, 3 task groups (53 members). Advisory only.

**Computer Systems**: Windows-based PC required, high-speed internet (50/20 Mbps), POS from pre-approved providers, POPS digital marketing platform. Franchisor has unlimited access to POS data. Annual POS maintenance $4K–$8K. System costs $72K–$182K. No limits on upgrade frequency or cost.

**Training**:
- General Manager Training: 72 hours on-the-job at certified Sonic restaurant
- Shift Manager Training: 96 hours on-the-job (56 + 40 split)
- Assistant Manager Training: 72 hours on-the-job
- Team Member Positional Training: 96 hours on-the-job
- **Total**: 336 hours on-the-job, 0 classroom
- General Manager Leadership Class: 24 hours classroom (Oklahoma City or virtual)
- Certification Training (first 3 restaurants): ~5 weeks additional at certified restaurant
- ServSafe: 8 hours classroom (external)
- Training team: Kevin Knutson (20 years industry, 14 years with Sonic programs), Operations Services dept with combined 90+ years restaurant experience

### Item 12 — Territory
**Franchise Agreement**: Protected Area radius from front door:
- >125,000 people/sq mi: 0.75 miles
- MSA ≥75,000 and ≤125,000 density: 1.5 miles
- MSA 25,000–75,000: 2 miles
- MSA ≤25,000 or outside MSA: 3 miles

**Critical carve-outs**: Previously Protected Radii, Previously Protected Development Areas, all Non-Drive-In and Non-Traditional locations are EXCLUDED from Protected Area. "You will not receive an exclusive territory."

**Five reserved franchisor rights**: (1) Other brands/channels inside PA; (2) Same/similar products through dissimilar channels (including internet) inside PA; (3) Dissimilar products under Sonic marks inside PA; (4) Sonic restaurants outside PA regardless of proximity; (5) Right to be acquired by competitor.

No right to use internet/catalog/telemarketing for sales. Franchisor and affiliates may use these channels within PA without compensation.

**Development Agreement**: Territorial protection by Target Area, expiring when last FA in Target Area is signed or DA expires/terminates. Same carve-outs apply. No exclusive territory.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Term**: 10 years (Traditional/Non-Drive-In); lesser of 10 years or facility contract term (Non-Traditional)
- **Renewal**: 2 additional 10-year terms
- **Renewal conditions**: Written notice 6–12 months before expiration, pay all amounts due, $15,000 renewal fee, sign THEN-CURRENT franchise agreement (**may contain materially different terms, fees, Protected Area**), remodel restaurant, sign general release, complete re-training

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: No right to terminate
- **By franchisor without cause**: No right
- **By franchisor with cause — non-curable**: Insolvency, failure to secure Site, unauthorized transfer, terrorist activities, failure to operate, felony conviction, misuse of marks/trade secrets, public health endangerment, false books, 3+ defaults in 12 months (even if cured), termination of any other Sonic agreement
- **By franchisor with cause — curable**: 10 or 30 days to cure

### Transfer
- Franchisor must approve all transfers
- Conditions: Obligations satisfied, good standing, transferee qualifies, training, transfer fee paid ($1,000 non-control/$3,000 other), restaurant upgrade, general release, new FA signed
- **Right of first refusal**: Franchisor has ROFR on same terms as third-party offer
- **Option to purchase**: Within 30 days of expiration/termination, franchisor may purchase restaurant assets

### Death/Disability
Estate must assign to approved heir/buyer within greater of 120 days or completion of probate.

### Non-Competition
- **During term**: No involvement in any competing business
- **Post-term**: 18 months, no competing business within 3 miles of former restaurant or any other Sonic restaurant. Also cannot be landlord of competing business within those zones.

### Dispute Resolution
- **No arbitration/mediation** — litigation only
- **Venue**: County of franchisor HQ (currently Atlanta, GA; subject to state law overrides)
- **Governing law**: Georgia law (subject to state law overrides)
