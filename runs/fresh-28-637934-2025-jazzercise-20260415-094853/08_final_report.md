# Full Diligence Report — Jazzercise, Inc. (637934-2025)

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** 5,251 franchised outlets at year-end 2024, 0 company-owned. One of the largest franchise systems in dance fitness, with presence in 49 states plus DC. System has been declining (5,844 → 5,533 → 5,253 → 5,251) but net loss nearly flattened in 2024 (−2 net change vs. −280 in 2023).
2. **Entity structure:** Jazzercise, Inc. is a California corporation incorporated in 1979, 100% owned by founder Judi Sheppard Missett. CEO is daughter Shanna Missett Nelson. No parents, no affiliates that franchise. This is a family-controlled entity with concentrated governance.
3. **Ownership history:** Founded by Judi Sheppard Missett as a sole proprietorship in 1969. Incorporated 1979. Franchising since July 1982. The original principal mark was registered in 1977. Recent leadership changes include Bobbi Quick as President (Oct 2023) from EōS Fitness, and Clarissa Zulick as CFO (2024) from Arbonne.
4. **Investment range:** Extraordinarily low entry cost. Associate: $2,170–$2,780. Class Owner/Business Owner: $3,935–$40,745. Initial franchise fee: $1,250 for all. The franchise model is closer to a personal service license than a traditional brick-and-mortar franchise.
5. **Fee burden:** 20% royalty on gross member enrollment (among the highest in fitness franchising). Minimums: $250/mo class owner (may rise to $500), $500/mo business owner (may rise to $1,000). Additional: music royalties (~$200–$384/yr), insurance ($245–$345/yr), associate fee ($130/yr). Advertising fund and technology fee reserved but not yet established — potential future fee escalation.
6. **Item 19:** No financial performance representation. Jazzercise explicitly declines to provide any FPR. No revenue, profit, or unit economics data available from the FDD.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** The 20% royalty rate, non-exclusive territory, and lack of FPR data create challenges for prospective franchisees to model breakeven. The declining system size (though stabilizing) and franchisor's operating loss in 2024 are additional risk indicators.
8. **Financial health:** Revenue $20.53M (2024), net income $419K. Operating loss of $(87K) in 2024 — the first operating loss in the three-year reporting period. Net income was rescued by $691K of investment income. Cash $10.6M; total equity $14.1M; no debt. Balance sheet is strong but the operating trend is deteriorating.
9. **Growth trajectory:** New outlets (385 in 2024) exceeded exits (387) for near-equilibrium. 274 projected new franchised outlets for next year. The system is stabilizing after years of contraction.
10. **Territory:** Non-exclusive. No protected territory. Franchisor may license additional franchisees at any time. Franchisor also operates Jazzercise On Demand (digital), Simply Plated (nutrition), and Revel Dance Fitness (separate trademark) — all potential competition.
11. **Renewal:** 5-year term with one 5-year renewal. Renewal requires signing then-current agreement which may contain materially different terms. Business owners have no renewal right.
12. **Disputes:** Mediation then arbitration in Carlsbad, CA. California law governs. State addenda provide material overrides for 11 states.
13. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution, declining system, family control, operating loss, no noncompete.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
Jazzercise operates a dance fitness franchise system with three tiers: Class Owner instructors who teach and own their classes, Associate instructors who teach for other franchisees, and Business Owners who offer classes through hired instructors. The system is unique in that franchisees are primarily individual instructors operating personal service businesses, not traditional brick-and-mortar operators. The predecessor entity was Judi Sheppard Missett's sole proprietorship beginning in 1969; the company incorporated in 1979 and began franchising in 1982.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Standard initial franchise fee: $1,250
- Reduced for employees (90+ days): $625
- Recertification (returning >12 months): $625
- Returning franchisee (within 12 months): $250
- Gift program: existing franchisees receive one gift every 5 years (recipient pays $0)

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Royalty: 20% of gross member enrollment (10% for children's/special events/personal touch programs)
- Minimum continuing fee (class owner): $250/month, may increase to $500/month
- Minimum continuing fee (business owner): $500/month, may increase to $1,000/month
- Associate fee: $130/year lump sum or $13/month ($156/year); may increase to $300/year

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Jazzercise estimates that required purchases constitute over 85% of franchisee establishment and operating costs. Key required sources:
- **Routines:** Available only from Jazzercise (currently free; may charge in future)
- **Glofox platform:** Required third-party studio management (franchisor may replace at any time)
- **Payment processing:** Must use designated vendor (currently Stripe)
- **Jazzercise Apparel:** Only approved supplier for branded merchandise
- **Audio recordings:** May purchase from any source (iTunes, etc.)
- **Insurance:** May obtain through Jazzercise or any company acceptable to Jazzercise

**Franchisor revenue from required purchases (2024):**
- Insurance program: $605,956 gross (~3% of total revenue; net after remitting premiums)
- Merchandise/materials: $603,993 (~2.9% of total revenue; COGS ~50.5%)

### Training Program
All training currently conducted online. No in-person requirement as of issuance date.
- Before audition: anatomy/physiology (2–4 hrs), exam (0.5–1 hr), routine choreography (6–10 hrs), video calls (3 hrs)
- After audition: social media, onboarding, set structure (~1 hr)
- Additional for Class/Business Owners: 8 modules (~8 hrs) covering facilities, pricing, marketing, accounting, team building
- Trainers: minimum 2 years as Jazzercise franchisees + 2 years instruction experience
- No charge for training; must complete within 6 months of signing
- Must comply with all requests to attend ongoing training sessions

### Technology Stack
Required systems: Jazzercise Business Center (member transactions/billing), Glofox (class check-in/registration), The Studio (operations/training/marketing portal via WiseTail), Routines Database (video streaming).
- Computer cost: $500–$1,500
- OS: Windows 11 or MacOS 13+; Internet 5 Mbps+; virus protection required
- Transaction services via Stripe (processing fees apply; credit check may be required)
- **Data access:** Jazzercise may access and download all information on franchisee's computer system with no contractual limitations.

### Marketing
No advertising fund established. No advertising co-ops or councils. Jazzercise handles all advertising in-house through its marketing department. OneTouchPoint (OTP) marketing platform available at $15/month base (optional; may be required in future). All advertising materials must be Jazzercise-approved. No individual franchisee websites permitted.

Advertising spend by Jazzercise: $1,744,387 (2024), $1,432,864 (2023), $1,345,785 (2022) — increasing year over year but entirely corporate expenditure.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Territory
**Non-exclusive.** Territories are defined as a state or portion of a state. The franchisee receives no protected territory. Jazzercise may license additional franchisees in the same territory at any time when it determines there is sufficient demand. There are no territorial development quotas, sales quotas, or market penetration requirements — but there is a minimum continuing fee ($250/mo or $500/mo) regardless of revenue.

### Channel Restrictions
Franchisees may not have individual websites. May not sell Jazzercise merchandise online. May not use Jazzercise trademarks in any unauthorized manner. Must comply with social media policy (violation may result in termination).

### Franchisor Competition
- Company-owned facilities in Carlsbad and Oceanside, CA (instructors at these are franchisees)
- Jazzercise On Demand — online streaming fitness program competing directly with in-person classes
- Simply Plated — nutrition program (revenue-sharing with franchisees)
- **Revel Dance Fitness** — a separate dance fitness program under a different trademark. Currently digital only but may be franchised in the future at any location. This represents a direct competitive risk from the franchisor.
- Jazzercise reserves the right to use Internet, catalog, telemarketing, or other direct marketing channels under any trademark.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term:** 5 years
- **Renewal:** One 5-year renewal if conditions met. Franchisee must sign the then-current Franchise Agreement, which **may contain materially different terms**. Must be in compliance, meet current requirements, complete training if required, sign a general release. No 3+ default notices in any 24-month period. Jazzercise must not have decided to withdraw from market. Must have right to continue at current location. Renewal does not apply if franchisee has moved to a different state.
- **Business owners:** No right to renew.

### Transfer Provisions
Transfer only to another Jazzercise franchisee with franchisor consent. Conditions: good standing, lessor consent, transferee completes training and signs current FA, franchisee signs release. Jazzercise has right of first refusal to match any offer. May transfer a class to an existing franchisee in territory by notifying franchise business advisor.

### Termination
- **By franchisee:** 60 days' notice, good standing required
- **By franchisor without cause:** Not applicable
- **Curable defaults:** 5 days for nonpayment; up to 30 days for other noncompliance
- **Non-curable defaults (23 grounds):** Include bankruptcy, abandonment, material misrepresentation, unfavorable conduct, failure to obtain location (3 months), failure to teach first class (30 days), failure to open (3 months), failure to complete training (6 months), repeated noncompliance, felony conviction, unauthorized transfer, social media violations, SDN/blocked person, fraud, and others.

### Death/Disability
- **Death:** Executor may sell within 90 days to qualified buyer; associate conducts classes meanwhile. Payments to Jazzercise must continue.
- **Disability:** Must secure associate instructor. Disability may not extend beyond 24 weeks.

### Noncompete
**None.** No during-term noncompete. No post-term noncompete. This is unusual for franchise systems and removes one typical barrier to franchisee exit, but also means franchisees cannot rely on competitive protection from former franchisees.

### Post-Termination Obligations
Pay all amounts due (may be assigned to debt collection), discontinue trademarks, de-identify, return/destroy/delete all proprietary information including member lists, stop using phone numbers and email addresses, assist in transition, refrain from soliciting members or personnel, refrain from disparaging remarks, obtain tail insurance coverage.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Method:** Mediation first, then arbitration (except certain claims)
- **Venue:** City of Jazzercise HQ (currently Carlsbad, California)
- **Governing law:** California
- **Prevailing party:** Entitled to reasonable attorney's fees
- **State overrides:** Multiple states override venue, governing law, and/or release requirements

### Litigation History
Two historical state actions, both resolved:
1. New York (1987): Consent decree for registration violations
2. Wisconsin (1985): Stipulation for registration violations + antitrust (facility committees disbanded)

No current material litigation disclosed.

### Key Financial Observations

**No debt.** The company has zero long-term or short-term debt. All liabilities are operational (AP, deferred revenue, lease liabilities).

**Strong liquidity.** Current ratio: 2.59 (2024). Cash plus investments: $14.6M against total liabilities of $7.1M.

**Operating margin deterioration.** Operating margin fell from 6.4% (2022) to 4.0% (2023) to −0.4% (2024). SG&A expenses grew 6.6% in 2024 while revenue grew only 5.9%. COGS grew 31.6% due to merchandise cost increases.

**Investment-dependent profitability.** Net income in 2024 was entirely dependent on $691K of non-operating investment income. Without this, the company would have reported a pre-tax loss. This is the first year in the reported period where operations alone could not sustain profitability.

**No distributions.** No dividends or distributions to the sole stockholder were recorded. All net income flows to retained earnings.

**Related-party lease.** HQ leased from Impala Building, LLC (100% common ownership). Rent: $309,084 (2024). New 10-year lease effective Jan 1, 2025, at $26,400/month with 2.5% annual increases. The VIE accounting alternative was elected (not consolidated).

**Related-party loan.** $543,891 receivable from sole stockholder's Irrevocable Trust for life insurance premiums. Interest-only payments; principal due within 9 years of each disbursement.

**Stock reclassification.** On December 2, 2024, the company split its 2,000 shares of common stock into 200 Class A voting shares and 1,800 Class B non-voting shares. Both owned by sole stockholder. Purpose not disclosed in FDD.
