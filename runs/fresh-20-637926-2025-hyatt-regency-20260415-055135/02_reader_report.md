## Pass A — Executive Snapshot

1. **System**: 103 Hyatt Regency hotels in North America (41 franchised, 62 company-owned/managed) as of 12/31/2024.
2. **Franchisor entity**: Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. (Delaware LLC); parent is Hyatt Hotels Corporation (NYSE-listed).
3. **Concept**: Upscale, full-service hotels with 250–1,000 guest rooms, full F&B, banquet/meeting/convention facilities.
4. **Investment range**: $73.5M – $492.6M total (excludes land/real estate). This is among the highest in all franchise systems.
5. **Application fee**: Greater of $100,000 or $400/room. Actual initial fees paid by 2024 signers: $83,500 – $215,870.
6. **Royalty**: 6% of Gross Rooms Revenue + 3% of Gross F&B Revenue, monthly.
7. **Item 19 FPR**: All 103 Covered Hotels; franchised avg occupancy 65.3%, ADR $175.40, RevPAR $114.58. No cost/profit data disclosed.
8. **Growth trajectory**: Slow, stable. Net -3 outlets in 2024 (franchised +1, company-owned -4). 3 franchise agreements signed but not yet opened.
9. **Territory**: Limited AOP (Area of Protection), typically 1–5 years post-opening, then no territorial protection.
10. **Term**: 20-year initial term. Renewal for 10 years on then-current terms (may be materially different).
11. **Noncompete**: No post-term covenant not to compete. During term: neither franchisee nor management company may be a "Competing Brand Owner."
12. **Dispute resolution**: Mandatory arbitration within 10 miles of Hyatt's principal office (Chicago, IL). Illinois law governs (subject to state addenda).
13. **Litigation**: One action against franchisee in 2024 (unpaid royalty fees — Imperial Hotels). No other material litigation.
14. **Financial health**: Parent Hyatt Hotels Corp reported 2024 net income of $1.296B on total revenue of $6.648B. Strong balance sheet with $3.826B total equity.
15. **Special risk**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Illinois).

## Pass D — Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Area of Protection (AOP)**: Defined by geographic boundaries; size varies from a few blocks (urban) to ~3-mile radius (suburban).
- **AOP Term**: Begins at FA effective date; typically ends 1–5 years after opening.
- **After AOP Term expires**: NO territorial protection. Franchisor and affiliates may open competing Brand Hotels within AOP.
- **Exception during AOP**: If Hyatt acquires a group of 4+ hotels, one or more in AOP, those can be converted to Brand Hotels.
- **Operating Brand Hotels**: May receive NO AOP at all.
- Non-exclusive rights in all other respects. Hyatt and affiliates may engage in any activity using or not using Proprietary Marks.
- Other Hyatt brands (Hyatt Place, Hyatt House, Hyatt Centric, etc.) may operate in AOP at any time.
- No options or rights of first refusal for additional franchises.
- No sales territory restrictions.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
- Marks owned by Hyatt Corporation, licensed to franchisor via perpetual Trademark License Agreement (Nov 1, 1993, as amended).
- Key marks: "Hyatt" (Reg 945,384), "Hyatt Regency" (Reg 982,652), "Regency Club" (Reg 1,204,963), "World of Hyatt" (Reg 5,201,881), and logos.
- All renewals current. No pending material proceedings.
- Hyatt Corp may terminate trademark license if: loss of majority ownership, uncured payment/quality failures.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
- No material patents.
- Copyright protection claimed for Copyrighted Materials (not registered with Copyright Office).
- Extensive Confidential Information protections (11 categories).
- Guest Information is franchisor's property during term. Post-term, franchisee retains use of hotel-generated Guest Information under Hyatt's privacy policies.
- Franchisee must assign all Innovations to Hyatt.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
- Must use Hyatt-approved management company or self-manage with Hyatt approval.
- Core Management must devote full time to Hotel; may not work at other lodging facilities concurrently.
- GM must complete GMU. If GM fails QA requirements, Hyatt may require replacement.
- Guaranty required from Controlling Ownership Interest holders — personal, unlimited guaranty of all FA obligations.
- Guarantor Monetary Threshold: minimum net worth and liquidity, CPI-adjusted annually.
- Revenue manager must be certified within 90 days; may manage no more than 20 Brand Hotels.
- Management company may not be a "Competing Brand Owner" (5+ hotels under competing trade name).

### Item 16 — Restrictions on Sales
- Must offer all authorized products/services per System Standards.
- May not sell unauthorized products/services.
- F&B Operator relationship requires Hyatt approval.
- Mixed-Use Site restrictions: no pawnshops, adult businesses, marijuana dispensaries, gambling (without consent); bars deriving >50% revenue from alcohol within 300 feet need consent.
- Room rates set by franchisee subject to System Standards and best-rate guarantee compliance.

## Pass E — Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 20 years from first day of month Hotel opens.
- **Successor franchise**: 10-year term on then-current terms (**may be materially different**, including fees).
- **Renewal conditions**: Substantial compliance during term, current full compliance, meet then-current new franchisee standards, passing QA scores for prior 3 years, rights to possession for 10+ years, renovate Hotel, sign general release (where permitted), pay PIP fee.

### Termination
- **Franchisee**: May not terminate FA except as law allows. May terminate Central Services Agreement on material breach (10 days' notice); Hosting Services Agreement on material breach (30 days) or without cause (6 months' notice).
- **Franchisor without cause**: May not terminate FA. May terminate Central/Hosting Services Agreements with notice.
- **Franchisor with cause — curable**: 24 hrs (health/safety); 10 days (failure to pay, insurance, real estate docs); 30 days (taxes, development deadlines, Providers, other defaults); 60 days (Guarantor Monetary Threshold).
- **Franchisor with cause — non-curable**: Bankruptcy events, undischarged judgments >$100K, cease operations, IP contest, unauthorized transfer, felony conviction, adverse acts, false books, unauthorized disclosure, law violations, repeated defaults.

### Transfer
- No transfer of FA, Hotel, assets, or Controlling Ownership without Hyatt approval.
- Non-Controlling Ownership transfers permitted if transferee is not Competing Brand Owner/sanctioned, no accumulated control shift, notice within 30 days.
- Transfer conditions: transferee qualifies, pay Application Fee, training complete, sign then-current agreements (may be materially different), general release, renovation commitment.
- No public offering by franchisee or Controlling Owner; private offerings subject to review.
- Death/disability: 6 months to transfer to approved transferee.

### Noncompete
- **During term**: No traditional noncompete, but neither franchisee nor management company may be a "Competing Brand Owner."
- **Post-term**: No noncompete covenant.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: Mandatory for all disputes; within 10 miles of Hyatt's principal office (currently Chicago, IL).
- **Forum**: Illinois (subject to state law).
- **Governing law**: Illinois (except Federal Arbitration Act; subject to state addenda).
- **Waiver of punitive damages and jury trial**: Yes (subject to state overrides).

### Liquidated Damages
- Formula: min(60, remaining months) x (avg monthly GRR x 6% + avg monthly F&B x 3% + avg monthly GRR x 3% + rooms x $36.25/CPI).
- **150% multiplier** for Consequential Termination (transfer to Competitor or 3+ agreement terminations).
- Condemnation: no damages if sign Termination Agreement + general release, but 24-month non-compete at $6,500/room if build competing hotel in AOP.
- Casualty: if cost > Damage Threshold, may terminate with payment = min(liquidated damages, Net Recovery).

### Right of First Offer
- Only if Exhibit C to FA is executed (strategic markets only).
