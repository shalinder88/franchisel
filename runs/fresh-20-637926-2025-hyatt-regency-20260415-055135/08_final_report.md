## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: 100 Hyatt Regency hotels in North America at year-end 2024 (41 franchised, 59 company-owned/managed). The system has been essentially flat for three years, growing from 100 (2022) to 103 (2023) and declining back to 100 (2024).

2. **Entity structure**: Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C., a Delaware LLC, is the franchisor. Its parent is Hyatt Hotels Corporation (NYSE: H), a publicly-traded global hospitality company. Hyatt Corporation, a key affiliate, provides most services to franchisees. Rosemont Project Management provides optional purchasing services.

3. **Ownership history**: The franchisor was incorporated in Delaware as Hyatt Franchise Corporation and converted to an LLC. It has offered Hyatt Regency franchises since December 1994 (with a gap from April 1997 to September 2006). Multiple affiliate franchisors operate internationally (Canada, EAME, Latin America, Asia Pacific, Brazil).

4. **Investment range**: $73.5 million to $492.6 million total initial investment (excluding real estate), making this one of the highest-investment franchise systems in existence. The range reflects hotels with 250 to 1,000 guest rooms. Construction/improvements alone range from $56.7M to $409.5M.

5. **Fee burden**: Royalty is 6% of Gross Rooms Revenue plus 3% of Gross F&B Revenue. The Commercial Services Fee adds $448/room/year plus 3.0% of GRR. World of Hyatt assessment is 4.5% of eligible revenue. Total ongoing fee load on rooms revenue approaches 14.85% before considering GDS fees, mandatory contracts, and other corporate services.

6. **Item 19 highlights**: All 103 hotels reported. Franchised hotels averaged 65.3% occupancy, $175.40 ADR, and $114.58 RevPAR in 2024. Owned/managed hotels significantly outperformed at $154.18 RevPAR. No cost or profitability data is disclosed. Smith Travel data shows franchised hotels roughly at fair share for occupancy (index 102.4) but below fair share on rate (index 97.4).

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: The lowest franchised RevPAR was $39.40, with median at $103.95. Only 41.5% of franchised hotels exceeded the average RevPAR. The spread from $39.40 to $193.15 is wide, indicating significant performance variability.

8. **Financial health**: Hyatt Hotels Corporation reported $6.648B total revenue and $1.296B net income in 2024. Total assets of $13.324B, total equity of $3.826B. The parent provides an absolute and unconditional Guarantee of Performance. However, a $110M goodwill impairment was taken in the management and franchising segment. Long-term debt of $3.326B.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Essentially flat. Net -3 outlets in 2024. Only 3 franchise agreements signed but not opened (Alaska, California, New York). 1 projected new franchised outlet in next fiscal year. The company-owned segment is shrinking (-4 in 2024), consistent with Hyatt's stated asset-light strategy.

10. **Territory**: Area of Protection expires 1–5 years after opening. After expiration, no territorial protection of any kind. Other Hyatt brands can operate in the AOP at any time. This is minimal protection for a $73M+ investment.

11. **Renewal**: 10-year successor franchise on then-current terms, which may be "materially different." This is a significant uncertainty for a 20-year relationship.

12. **Disputes**: Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, Illinois. Illinois law governs. One franchise litigation in 2024 (unpaid royalties). State addenda modify dispute resolution for MD, MN, and ND.

13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Illinois). Liquidated damages formula with 150% multiplier for Consequential Termination. No post-term noncompete but during-term Competing Brand Owner restriction.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. is the direct franchisor, but Hyatt Corporation (its affiliate) provides the majority of operational services through the Mandatory and Non-Mandatory Services framework. The distinction between franchisor and service-providing affiliate is important: fees flow to different entities.

The franchisor expects only entities (not individuals) to acquire franchises. A Guaranty from Controlling Ownership Interest holders is required, making the guarantors personally bound by all franchise agreement obligations, including monetary and non-monetary provisions. The Guarantor Monetary Threshold (minimum net worth and liquidity) is CPI-adjusted annually.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Application Fee: Greater of $100,000 or $400/room.
- Design Services Review: $75,000–$160,000.
- IT Project Management: $69,900+ (up to 250 rooms) + $10,815–$17,510 expenses.
- Training: $19,450–$46,650 in fees + $4,241–$9,900 in expense reimbursement.
- Pre-opening advisory (if needed): $0–$375,000.
- PIP fee (conversions): $10,000.
- Comfort letters: $2,500 each.
- Rosemont purchasing (optional): $85,000–$275,000.
- Revenue management (pre-opening): $0–$7,000.
- Field marketing (pre-opening): $0–$16,482.
- Actual total initial fees paid by 2024 signers: $83,500–$215,870.

**Ongoing Fees (Franchise Agreement):**
- Royalty: 6% GRR + 3% Gross F&B Revenue.
- World of Hyatt: 4.5% eligible revenue (2.25% for on-property enrollments).
- QA compliance (FIP): $5,000–$20,000 per 6-month stage; BPP: $45,000/year.
- On-site QA inspections: $1,500–$2,000/year.
- Training tools: $9.01/room/year.
- Owner convention: $2,000–$5,000/attendee (annual/biennial).
- GM University: $2,700 per new GM.
- Late fee: $225 + 1.5%/month on overdue amounts.
- De-identification failure: $5,000/day.

**Mandatory Services (System Services Charges):**
- Commercial Services Fee: $448/room/year + 3.0% of GRR. This is a significant charge covering marketing, GPGS reservations, revenue management technology, sales, PMS, digital services, and other technology.
- Digital Acquisition Fee: 1.35% of GRR through Digital Channels.
- GDS/reservations: ~$8.50/reservation.
- Mandatory contracts: $1,500–$25,000/month (credit card processing, music licensing, telecom).
- Other corporate services: $500–$15,000/month.

**Non-Mandatory Services (optional but may be required in some circumstances):**
- Field Marketing: $996–$2,747/month (3 tiers).
- Revenue Management: $1,000–$3,500/month (3 tiers; may be required based on experience).
- Central Sales: $3,850–$6,650/month (group), $1,000–$5,000 (lead), $1,600–$3,200 (transient), $3,000–$3,500 (admin).
- GPGS Add-On: $3.50–$16/service interaction.
- Digital Ancillary: $1,250/month + 5% commission.
- HSF booking fees: 6% Group Contract Revenue (cap $30K), 3% GRR transient, 1% GRR referrals.

**Fee load illustration on rooms revenue**: For a hotel achieving the franchised average RevPAR of $114.58 with 400 rooms:
- Annual GRR estimate: ~$16.7M
- Royalty (6%): ~$1.0M
- Commercial Services Fee (3% + $448/room): ~$680K
- World of Hyatt (4.5% on ~45% of revenue): ~$338K
- Digital Acquisition (1.35% on ~72% of revenue): ~$162K
- GDS (~$8.50/reservation, estimated): ~$100K
- Mandatory contracts and other: ~$120K–$480K/year
- **Total estimated fee burden on rooms: ~$2.4M–$2.8M (~14–17% of GRR)**

This excludes F&B royalty (3%), QA fees, training, and convention costs.

### Initial Investment Detail

Total: $73,485,976 – $492,565,733 (excludes real estate).

The single largest component is construction ($56.65M–$409.5M), reflecting that this is a ground-up, full-service hotel development franchise. FF&E runs $9.1M–$46.2M. Architecture/design adds $1.4M–$13.9M. The range is wide because it covers 250-room to 1,000-room hotels across varying market conditions.

The franchisee must maintain a capital reserve of 5% of total operating revenue monthly for future upgrades and renovations.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

Approximately 95% of purchases to establish and 95% to operate the Hotel must come from designated/approved suppliers or per System Standards. This is an unusually high supplier control percentage.

Key mandatory suppliers/services:
- Opera PMS from designated vendor ($3.90/room/month).
- Hosting Services Agreement (required).
- Designated cyber endpoint protection and web filtering.
- Designated travel agent commission provider.
- Designated QA inspection company.
- Avendra (volume purchasing) — optional but practical for Hyatt-specific items.
- CRS, GDS, ADS systems — Hyatt-designated only.

In 2024, Hyatt Corporation received $336,271 in supplier rebates (used to offset System Services Costs). Franchisees received $7,393,115 more in loyalty reimbursements than they paid. Hyatt affiliates collected about $2,577,599 from franchisee services (excluding Commercial Services Fee).

### Training Program

The initial training program has 7 parts totaling approximately 277–316 classroom hours and 55–112 OJT hours:
1. Owner Briefing (8 hrs, Chicago or virtual).
2. Owner/Operator Orientation (16 hrs).
3. General Manager University (53–58 hrs — eLearning, virtual, 3-day in-person Chicago).
4. Operations Training (47–79 hrs classroom + 40–76 hrs OJT).
5. Sales/Events/Revenue Management (136.5 hrs).
6. Other Personnel Training (17–34 hrs + 15–36 hrs OJT).
7. Pre-Opening Brand Training (2–3 trainers on-site for min 7 nights).

Ongoing: GM replacements must complete GMU within 6 months. Annual retraining for Human Trafficking and Global Privacy programs. Leadership summits approximately every 4 years.

### Technology Stack

Required systems: Property Management (Opera), Point of Sale, Revenue Management, Events (Envision), Key Lock, PBX/Phones, In-Room Entertainment, HSIA, Guest Computing, Music, Digital Surveillance, Scent. Cost: $1,508,950–$3,296,000 to acquire and install.

No contractual limit on frequency or cost of required technology changes/upgrades. Hyatt has unlimited independent access to all data generated by the Technology System.

### Marketing Fund

There is no separate advertising fund. Marketing is funded through the Commercial Services Fee ($448/room/year + 3.0% GRR). Hyatt will not prepare periodic accounting of how these funds are spent. Hyatt need not spend any amount in the area where the Hotel is located. No franchisee advertising councils exist.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory

The Area of Protection is defined by geographic boundaries (streets, rivers, or radius) and varies from a few blocks (urban) to approximately 3 miles (suburban). The AOP Term is negotiated per deal and typically runs 1–5 years after the Hotel's opening date. After the AOP Term expires, there is NO territorial protection whatsoever.

### Encroachment Risk

This is a high-encroachment-risk franchise for several reasons:

1. **AOP expiration**: After 1–5 years, Hyatt can authorize competing Brand Hotels directly adjacent to the franchisee's hotel.
2. **Acquisition exception**: Even during the AOP Term, if Hyatt acquires a group of 4+ hotels and one or more is in the AOP, those can be converted to Brand Hotels.
3. **Other Hyatt brands**: Hyatt Place, Hyatt House, Hyatt Centric, Thompson, Caption by Hyatt, JdV by Hyatt, Hyatt Studios, Unbound Collection, and other brands can operate in the AOP at any time.
4. **Non-exclusive rights**: Rights under the FA are nonexclusive in all respects beyond the limited AOP. Hyatt may use common systems and provide services to competing hotels.

### Channel Restrictions

Franchisee may only use CRS, authorized GDS, and authorized ADS for reservations. Must honor all confirmed CRS/GDS/ADS reservations with first priority. May not develop any website with "Hyatt" in the domain. Best-rate guarantee compliance required.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

- Initial term: 20 years from first day of month Hotel opens.
- Successor franchise: 10 years on then-current terms, which **may be materially different** (including fee structure). This is explicitly disclosed twice in Item 17 and represents a significant risk — a franchisee investing $73M+ may face substantially different terms at renewal.

### Transfer Provisions

- All transfers of the FA, Hotel, assets, or Controlling Ownership require Hyatt approval.
- Transfer fee is the then-current Application Fee.
- Transferee must sign then-current agreements (may be materially different), complete training, commit to renovation.
- General release required from selling franchisee and guarantors (where state law allows).
- No public offerings permitted; private offerings subject to review ($5,000 fee).
- Hyatt has right of first offer only if Exhibit C is executed (strategic markets).

### Termination and Death/Disability

- Franchisee cannot terminate the FA except as law allows.
- Cure periods range from 24 hours (health/safety) to 60 days (Guarantor Monetary Threshold).
- Non-curable defaults include bankruptcy events, felony conviction, undischarged judgments >$100K, IP challenges, unauthorized transfers, repeated defaults.
- Death/disability of Controlling Ownership holder: 6 months to transfer to approved transferee.

### Noncompete Restrictions

- During term: No traditional noncompete, but neither franchisee nor management company may be a "Competing Brand Owner" (entity owning/franchising/managing a full-service hotel brand with 5+ hotels and ADR at least 60% of Brand Hotels' ADR).
- Post-term: No noncompete.

### Dispute Resolution

- Mandatory arbitration within 10 miles of Hyatt's Chicago headquarters.
- Illinois law governs (subject to state addenda).
- Waiver of punitive damages and jury trial (subject to state overrides).
- State addenda modify: Maryland (may bring MD Franchise Law claims in MD), Minnesota (90 days' termination notice, 180 days' non-renewal notice, no waiver of jury trial, MN courts available), North Dakota (liquidated damages deemed unfair by Commissioner, mutual agreement on arbitration site, ND law may apply, ND courts available).

### Litigation History

One franchise action in 2024: Hyatt Franchising v. Imperial Hotels LLC et al. (AAA 2024) for unpaid royalty fees. No other required disclosures. No bankruptcy. This is a very clean litigation profile.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Overview

Hyatt provides three FPRs based on all 103 Brand Hotels operating in North America during the full year 2024. This is a complete population — no hotels were excluded from the Covered Hotels.

### FPR 1: Occupancy, ADR, RevPAR

| Metric | Franchised (41) | Owned/Managed (62) | All (103) |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Avg Occupancy | 65.3% | 67.1% | 66.6% |
| Avg ADR | $175.40 | $229.69 | $214.75 |
| Avg RevPAR | $114.58 | $154.18 | $143.07 |

**Franchised statistics:**
- Occupancy range: 34.9%–87.8% (median 64.3%, 48.8% exceeded average).
- ADR range: $112.73–$265.93 (median $166.70, only 39% exceeded average).
- RevPAR range: $39.40–$193.15 (median $103.95, only 41.5% exceeded average).

**Key observation**: Franchised hotels significantly underperform owned/managed hotels on ADR ($175.40 vs $229.69, a $54.29 or 23.6% gap) and RevPAR ($114.58 vs $154.18, a $39.60 or 25.7% gap). The FDD does not explain this gap. Possible factors include location mix, property age, and management quality differences. The average rooms at Covered Hotels is 541, and the franchised hotel mix may skew toward smaller or lower-tier markets.

### FPR 2: Smith Travel Indices

| Metric | Franchised | All |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Occ Index | 102.4 | 103.0 |
| Rate Index | 97.4 | 99.3 |
| RevPAR Index | 99.8 | 102.4 |

Franchised hotels capture slightly more than their fair share of occupancy (102.4) but fall below fair share on rate (97.4). The RevPAR Index of 99.8 means franchised Hyatt Regency hotels just barely capture their competitive set share. The median franchised RevPAR Index is 96.1, meaning more than half of franchised hotels underperform their competitive sets on RevPAR.

### FPR 3: World of Hyatt Contribution

- 42.5% of franchised revenue comes from World of Hyatt members.
- 44.3% of franchised room nights are WOH-occupied.
- Average 40,381 WOH room nights per franchised hotel.

This is a significant loyalty program contribution, but lower than owned/managed hotels (45.0% revenue, 55.1% room nights, 87,640 avg room nights). The disparity again likely reflects location and market mix differences.

### Channel Mix

Franchised: 71.5% direct, 28.5% indirect. Owned/managed: 77.5% direct. Higher indirect channel reliance at franchised hotels means higher third-party commission costs.

### Critical Caveats

1. No cost, expense, or profitability data is disclosed. Prospective franchisees cannot assess margin from these FPRs.
2. Data is not audited by a CPA.
3. The Covered Hotels had operated under "Hyatt Regency" for an average of 30.2 years. A new franchisee would lack this brand equity.
4. Average rooms at Covered Hotels is 541 (range 182–2,032), which may not match a prospective franchisee's planned hotel.
5. Written substantiation available on reasonable request.
