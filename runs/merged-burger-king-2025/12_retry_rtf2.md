# RT-06: Exhibit X1 — Reclaim the Flame 2 Master Program Agreement

**Source:** PDF pages 969-990 (Exhibit X1, 22 pages)
**Status:** Fully surfaced

---

## Overview

The RTF2 MPA is a binding agreement between BKC and individual franchisees under which the franchisee commits to remodel specific restaurants to the "Sizzle Image" standard by BKC-set deadlines, in exchange for cash contributions and reduced successor franchise fees. It is the current mandatory remodel program.

---

## Key Mechanics

### Remodel Deadlines
- Specific Remodel Restaurants listed in Section 1 with individual deadlines.
- Remodel commitments scheduled across 2026-2030 (total commitment and per-year breakdown specified).
- Deadlines are non-negotiable after signing. BKC controls the schedule.

### Completion Definition
"Complete" means the RTF2 Remodel has been completed in accordance with the Remodel Requirements, **as determined by BKC in its sole and absolute discretion**, including the method and scope of all inspections by BKC or its designated third-party inspector (Steritech Group or other).

### Scope of Work
- All interior and exterior to "Sizzle Image" standard.
- Minimum requirements (Exhibit A): parking lot, landscaping, interior/exterior building remodel per Sizzle Image Guidelines at designwithbk.com.
- Includes all deferred repair and maintenance work identified by BKC.
- Double Drive Thru optimization, LED lighting, ADA compliance, kiosks, digital menu boards, POS updates, managed network, order-ready screens.
- Franchisee solely responsible for all costs and expenses.

### Pre-Inspections
BKC may (at sole option) conduct pre-inspection and issue a Facility Inspection Report (FIR). FIR becomes binding on franchisee unless disputed within 10 days. Franchisee may substitute one Alternate Restaurant from Schedule 1 if FIR reveals excessive work, but only if Alternates are available.

## Fees and Incentives

### Successor Franchise Fee
$2,500 per additional year purchased. Franchisee must purchase enough years so total term = 20 years from Remodel Deadline (or lesser if lease-limited, with pro-rata contribution reduction).

### Royalty Rate Election
Franchisee selects from 4.5%, 5.0%, 5.5%, or 6.0% for the successor Franchise Agreement. Higher royalty rate = larger BKC cash contribution. Election must be made 30 days before Remodel Deadline; default is 4.5%.

## Default and Penalty Mechanics

### Missed Remodel Deadline
1. **Immediate +3.0% royalty surcharge** on all Gross Sales under existing FA, commencing on Remodel Deadline, continuing until BKC verifies Completion or FA expires/terminates.
2. **1-year Remodel Cure Period** granted from the Remodel Deadline.
3. If completed during Cure Period, Franchisor Contribution reduced by:
   - $50,000 (Scrape/Rebuild)
   - $30,000 (Full-Legacy Image)
   - $10,000 (Full-20/20 Image)

### Failure to Complete within Cure Period
- Event of default under this Program Agreement AND under the existing Franchise Agreement for that restaurant.
- For Carrols refranchise restaurants: also default under every FA for every Purchased Restaurant in the transaction.
- No notice or opportunity to cure (except as required by state law).

### Program-Level Events of Default (Section 7(b))
Immediate termination of entire Program Agreement, forfeiture of all incentives for uncompleted remodels:
1. Failure to complete any remodel within Cure Period.
2. Any non-curable default or uncured curable default under any FA, lease, or agreement between Franchisee/affiliates and BKC.
3. Knowingly submitting false/misleading documentation.
4. Failure to meet minimum financial criteria per BKC Financial Policy (measured quarterly).
5. Breach of any other provision of the Program Agreement.

### Cross-Default (Section 8)
Any event of default under the RTF2 MPA = event of default under all area development agreements, TRAs, and MTRAs between Franchisee/affiliates and BKC. **Survives expiration/termination of the Program Agreement.**

### Reversion on Termination (Section 10)
If the RTF2 MPA expires or is terminated before a remodel is completed, the remodel deadline reverts to the earliest deadline under the original Franchise Agreement (including Midterm Remodel), even if that deadline has already passed. If the Midterm Remodel deadline has passed, BKC may terminate the FA without providing any opportunity to cure.

## General Release (Section 9)
Franchisee unconditionally releases BKC and all Related Parties from all claims, known or unknown, from the beginning of the world through the Effective Date. **Includes California Civil Code Section 1542 waiver.** Release does not cover future obligations under existing agreements.

## Other Terms
- **Assignment:** Prohibited without BKC's written consent (sole discretion).
- **Governing law:** Florida. Forum: Southern District of Florida or Miami-Dade County.
- **Jury trial waiver:** Yes (knowing and voluntary).
- **Joint and several liability** if Franchisee consists of multiple persons.
- **FSS Grade scope:** BKC may consider performance of all restaurants operated by Franchisee, affiliates, and entities sharing common ownership or management.

## Diligence Assessment

The RTF2 MPA is the single most consequential program document in the current BK system. Key features:

1. **BKC has sole discretion** over completion determination — franchisee has no appeal mechanism.
2. **The royalty/contribution tradeoff is material:** electing 6.0% royalty (vs. 4.5%) yields $375,000–$575,000 more in Franchisor Contribution for a Legacy Full remodel, but costs ~$25,000/year more per $1.66M AUV restaurant.
3. **The penalty for missing a deadline is punitive and self-reinforcing:** +3.0% surcharge reduces cash flow available for the remodel itself.
4. **Cross-default exposure is extreme:** a default on one remodel can cascade to terminate development rights, TRAs, and (for Carrols restaurants) every FA in the refranchise transaction.
5. **The general release at signing is broad** — covers all claims "from the beginning of the world."
6. **D/F operators are locked out** of all cash contributions but still face the remodel obligation and penalty structure.
