# Burger King FDD (637918-2025) -- Exhibit P State Addenda

Source: `/fdd-vault/wi-dfi/archive/637918-2025-Burger-King.pdf`, pages 706-801 (Exhibit P, dated 03/2025; WA and ND as amended 01/2026).

Each state entry below summarizes material overrides to the base Franchise Agreement (FA), Development Agreement (DA), Multiple Target Reservation Agreement (MTRA), and/or FDD disclosures that are directly surfaced in the addenda text.

---

## California

**Addendum titles:** "Amendment to Burger King Restaurant Franchise Agreement (Entity) Required by the State of California" (p 707) | "Addendum to BKC FDD -- Information Required by the State of California" (p 782-783).

| Override | Affected contract | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| California Franchise Relations Act (Bus. & Prof. Code 20000-20043) supersedes FA termination/non-renewal provisions where inconsistent | FA | Franchisee gets statutory term/nonrenewal protections that override BKC's contractual termination rights |
| Florida governing-law clause may not be enforceable under CA law | FA, DA, MTRA (Sec 21.C, Art X 11.5, Sec 15.4) | Disputes involving CA franchises may be governed by CA law instead of FL law |
| General release required at renewal/transfer void to extent it waives rights under CA Franchise Investment Law (Corp. Code 31512) or CA Franchise Relations Act (Bus. & Prof. Code 20010) | FA, DA | Franchisee cannot be forced to release statutory claims as condition of renewal or transfer |
| Post-term noncompete (FA Sec 19, DA Sec 7.5) may not be enforceable under CA law | FA, DA | CA's strong anti-noncompete policy likely voids covenant |
| Jury-trial waiver may not be enforceable under CA law | FA | Franchisee retains right to jury trial |
| Venue restriction to forum outside CA is void for claims arising under/relating to a CA franchise (Bus. & Prof. Code 20040.5) | FA, FDD Item 17(V)(W) | Litigation must be available in CA |
| Liquidated damages clause (DA Sec 5.7) may not be enforceable under CA law | DA | Development shortfall liquidated damages at risk |
| Pre-sale questionnaires/acknowledgments cannot waive franchise-law claims or disclaim reliance | FA | Protects fraud-in-the-inducement claims |

## Illinois

**Addendum titles:** "Amendment to Burger King Restaurant Franchise Agreement (Entity) Required by the State of Illinois" (p 711) | "Amendment to MTRA Required by the State of Illinois" (p 756).

| Override | Affected contract | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Illinois law governs the FA and MTRA (overrides FL choice of law) | FA, MTRA | Full IL law applies, not FL |

| Waiver of IL Act compliance is void (Sec 41) | FA, MTRA | Cannot contract around IL franchise law |
| Termination-on-bankruptcy clause may not be enforceable under federal bankruptcy law | FA | Standard bankruptcy-law carve-out |

**Addendum titles:** "Amendment to Burger King Restaurant Franchise Agreement (Entity) Required by the State of Maryland" (p 713) | "Addendum to BKC FDD -- Information Required by the State of Maryland" (p 788).

## Minnesota

**Addendum titles:** "Amendment to Burger King Restaurant Franchise Agreement (Entity) Required by the State of Minnesota" (p 715-716) | "Addendum to BKC FDD -- Information Required by the State of Minnesota" (p 789-790).

| Override | Affected contract | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 90 days' notice of termination with 60 days to cure; 180 days' notice of non-renewal (Minn. Stat. 80C.14 subds. 3, 4, 5) | FA, DA | Among the longest statutory notice/cure periods; far exceeds base FA |
| Transfer consent may not be unreasonably withheld (80C.14 subd. 5) | FA | Limits franchisor discretion on transfers |

| BKC prohibited from requiring: litigation outside MN, jury-trial waiver, consent to liquidated damages, termination penalties, or judgment notes (80C.21, Rule 2860.4400(J)) | FA, DA | Comprehensive prohibition on anti-franchisee dispute provisions |
| Venue/jurisdiction outside MN unenforceable for causes of action enforceable in MN courts (except exclusive arbitration clause permitted) | FA, DA | Forum override to MN |
| BKC must protect franchisee's right to use BK marks or indemnify for losses from mark-use claims (80C.12 subd 1(g)) | FA (Sec 11.A.(2)) | Trademark indemnification obligation |

## North Dakota

| Limitation of claims is deleted; ND statute of limitations applies | FA | Longer ND SOL may apply |

## Washington

**Addendum titles:** "Washington Addendum to Burger King Restaurant Franchise Agreement (Entity)" (p 720-722; as amended 01/2026) | "Washington Addendum to BKC FDD" (p 799-801; as amended 01/2026).

| Override | Affected contract | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| WA Franchise Investment Protection Act (ch. 19.100 RCW) prevails in conflict of laws | FA, DA, MTRA, FDD | WA law trumps FL law |
| Arbitration/mediation site in WA or mutually agreed location; litigation may be brought in WA | FA, FDD | Forum override to WA |
| General release void unless executed pursuant to negotiated settlement after agreement in effect, with independent counsel (RCW 19.100.220(2)); release at renewal/transfer likewise void | FA (Secs 15, 17), FDD | Strongest release limitation -- requires independent counsel + post-closing negotiation |
| Statute of limitations restrictions and jury-trial waivers may not be enforceable | FA, FDD | Preserves full SOL and jury right |
| Franchisor cannot unilaterally modify FA during term without franchisee consent (RCW 19.100.180(2)(j)), unless terminated for good cause | FA | Protects against mid-term standard/fee changes |
| Purchasing requirements must be at fair and reasonable price (RCW 19.100.180(2)(d)) | FA | Supply-chain pricing constraint |
| Waiver of exemplary/punitive damages void; treble damages available (RCW 19.100.190) | FA, FDD | Significant damages exposure for franchisor |
| Indemnification obligation modified: franchisee not required to indemnify franchisor for losses caused by franchisor's own negligence, willful misconduct, strict liability, or fraud | FA | Mutual-fault limitation on indemnity |
| Attorney's fees provision applies only if franchisor is prevailing party | FA | Fee-shifting becomes bilateral in practice |
| Noncompete void/unenforceable against employees earning < $100K/yr annualized and independent contractors < $250K/yr (RCW 49.62.020, .030) | FA | Limits enforcement of post-term noncompete |
| No-poach / no-solicitation of other franchisees' employees prohibited (RCW 49.62.060) | FA | Confirms 2018 Assurance of Discontinuance; anti-poach provisions void |
| Franchisor discretion limited to good-faith standard (RCW 19.100.180(1)) | FA | Overrides "reasonable business judgment" standard |
| Prohibition on restricting franchisee from communicating with regulators (RCW 19.100.180(2)(h)) | FA | Whistleblower-type protection |

## Cross-state summary of material themes

| Theme | States with override |
|---|---|
| **Forum / venue must be in-state** | CA, IL, MD, MN, ND, RI, SD, WA |
| **Governing law overridden from FL** | IL, MN, ND, SD, WA |
| **Release at renewal/transfer void or limited** | CA, HI, MD, MN, ND, NY, WA |
| **Noncompete void or limited** | CA, ND, SD, WA |
| **Termination notice / cure enhanced** | IL (10-30 day cure), MN (90 days notice / 60 cure), SD (30 day cure), VA (reasonable cause) |
| **Liquidated damages / termination penalties void** | CA (DA only), MN, ND |
| **Jury trial waiver void** | CA, MN, ND, WA |
| **Punitive/exemplary damages waiver void** | ND, WA |
| **Fair-value repurchase on termination** | HI (FMV of inventory/equipment; goodwill if converting to company-owned) |
| **Statute of limitations preserved (no contractual shortening)** | MD (3 yr from grant), ND (state SOL), WA |
