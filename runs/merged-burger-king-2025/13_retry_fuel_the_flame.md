# RT-07: Exhibit Y1 — Fuel the Flame Co-Investment Agreement

**Source:** PDF pages 999-1008 (Exhibit Y1, 10 pages)
**Status:** Fully surfaced

---

## Overview

The Fuel the Flame (FTF) Co-Investment Agreement governs an advertising rate increase program under which BKC invested $120M in the Ad Fund (Oct 2022–Dec 2024) and participating franchisees pay an additional 0.5% of Gross Sales ("Incremental Rate") above their Legacy Rate, for a total "FTF Rate." New franchisees are not eligible to participate.

---

## Key Definitions

- **Legacy Rate:** The advertising contribution rate in the franchisee's existing Franchise Agreement.
- **Incremental Rate:** 0.5% of monthly Gross Sales (the additional FTF contribution).
- **FTF Rate:** Legacy Rate + Incremental Rate (total advertising contribution during the FTF Rate Period).
- **Participating Restaurants:** Existing restaurants + any new/acquired restaurants during the term.

---

## Restaurant 4-Wall EBITDA Definition (Section 10)

**"Restaurant 4-Wall EBITDA" means earnings from the relevant BURGER KING restaurant before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization, excluding any general and administrative expenses (including above-restaurant personnel) allocated to such restaurant.**

Key methodology details:
- Calculated by BKC using monthly P&Ls collected by BKC and RSI from all US BK Restaurants (including Company Restaurants).
- P&Ls prepared consistent with the **RSI "Profit and Loss Definitions and Guidelines" dated May 1, 2019** (attached as Exhibit A — not transcribed, referenced as attached).
- **Rental and leasing costs held consistent with a Base Year of 2021**, allowing actual rent/leasing cost increases not to exceed **7.4% over the Base Year** for the EBITDA calculation.
- If any Owner fails to submit P&Ls by January 31, 2027 (with 30-day cure period), BKC deems all of that Owner's restaurants to be at the threshold EBITDA amount.
- BKC may audit submitted P&Ls and exclude materially discrepant ones.

## Timeline and Rate Mechanics

### Base EBITDA Condition (Already Met)
The agreement acknowledges the Base EBITDA Condition has been met: average trailing 12-month Restaurant 4-Wall EBITDA for US BK Restaurants as of 12/31/2024 was not less than **$175,000 per restaurant.** The FTF Rate Period has commenced.

### FTF Rate Period: January 1, 2025 – December 31, 2026

### Renewal Period Decision: By March 31, 2027
The FTF Rate Period extends through 12/31/2028 (the "Renewal Period") if EITHER:

**(a) Renewal EBITDA Condition met:** Average trailing 12-month Restaurant 4-Wall EBITDA for US BK Restaurants as of 12/31/2026 equals or exceeds **$230,000 per restaurant.** OR

**(b) Renewal Survey approved:** If the EBITDA condition is not met, BKC conducts a vote (Renewal Survey) by March 31, 2027:
- **Who votes:** Participating US BK Restaurants + Company Restaurants. One vote per restaurant.
- **Participating Owners vote uniformly** (all votes in favor or all against).
- **Non-voters automatically counted** with the then-majority of votes actually cast.
- **Minimum favorable vote required** depends on FTF participation rate:

| FTF Participation Rate | Min Favorable Vote % |
|---|---|
| 90% | 74% |
| 95% | 70% |
| 100% | 67% |
| Below 90% | 0.667 / participation rate |

**(c) Binding on dissenters:** If the Renewal Survey is approved, all participants must pay the FTF Rate during the Renewal Period, even if they voted against.

### Term Expiration
Agreement expires on the earlier of 12/31/2026 (if not renewed) or 12/31/2028.

### Retroactive Catch-Up
BKC does not invoice the Incremental Rate until the month after the EBITDA determination, but then invoices retroactively to January 1 of the rate period. Catch-Up Contributions due within 90 days.

---

## Transfer and Successor Conditions

- **Transfer:** Transferee must enter into a Co-Investment Agreement as a condition of BKC consent to the transfer.
- **Successor franchise:** Continuing participation in FTF is a condition of BKC offering a successor Franchise Agreement. BKC may refuse to successor if franchisee declines.
- **New/acquired restaurants:** Must sign a Co-Investment Agreement concurrently with the Franchise Agreement.

---

## Default and Remedies

- **10-day cure period** for any breach.
- **Uncured breach = event of default** under the Franchise Agreements for each Participating Restaurant, with no further opportunity to cure.
- BKC may terminate applicable Franchise Agreements upon written notice.

---

## Use of FTF Funds (Section 8)

- FTF Funds (BKC Investment + Incremental Contributions) must be used **solely for television media advertising expenses.**
- **Cannot be used for:** G&A expenses, digital development costs, or agency fees (except for buying/placing TV advertising).
- Legacy Rate advertising contributions are not subject to these restrictions.
- BKC established a MAC sub-committee to review Ad Fund spending twice per calendar year. Abridged summaries posted to BK Gateway.

---

## RSI Third-Party Audit Right (Section 10(e))

If within 10 days of BKC's EBITDA determination the RSI Board votes to audit, RSI retains a third-party auditor to review P&Ls and BKC's methodology. Audit must be completed within 30 days. If the audit concludes the Renewal EBITDA Condition was not met, BKC has an additional 30 days to conduct the Renewal Survey.

---

## Diligence Assessment

1. **The $230,000 EBITDA threshold is calculated with rent capped at 7.4% above a 2021 base year.** If actual rents have risen more than 7.4% since 2021, the EBITDA figure used for the threshold determination will be higher than a franchisee's actual 4-Wall EBITDA. This is a favorable adjustment for meeting the threshold.
2. **Non-voters are counted with the majority.** This means apathy favors the majority, and a small active minority cannot block renewal by abstaining.
3. **Default under FTF = FA default with no further cure.** The 10-day cure applies to the FTF agreement, but once uncured, it cascades immediately to the FA with no second bite.
4. **Transfer and successor are conditioned on FTF participation.** This makes the program effectively mandatory for the participating base — it cannot be shed at transfer or renewal.
5. **TV media only restriction** gives franchisees some transparency into how the incremental 0.5% is spent, but BKC's Legacy Rate spending is unrestricted.
