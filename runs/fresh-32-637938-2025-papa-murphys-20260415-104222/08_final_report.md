# Final Diligence Report — Papa Murphy's International LLC
## FDD #637938-2025 | Issuance: March 28, 2025 (amended October 10, 2025)

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** 1,079 total US stores at end of FY2024 (December 2, 2024): 1,001 franchised, 43 company-owned, plus 35 international (Canada, UAE). System has contracted by 195 units over 3 years (1,239 → 1,044).
2. **Entity structure:** Papa Murphy's International LLC (DE LLC), wholly-owned subsidiary of Papa Murphy's Company Stores, Inc. (WA) → Papa Murphy's Holdings, Inc. (DE) → MTY Franchising USA, Inc. (TN, guarantor) → MTY Food Group Inc. (TSX-listed Canadian public company). Acquired May 2019.
3. **Ownership history:** Founded 1995, predecessors since 1982. Merged into current entity. Acquired by MTY Food Group in 2019. MTY operates 50+ restaurant brands globally.
4. **Investment range:** $367,428–$733,124. Franchise fee $25,000 (first), $15,000 (subsequent).
5. **Fee burden:** Royalty 5% + Brand Marketing 2% + Local Marketing min 5% + tech fees ~$200/mo + loyalty $53/mo + online ordering $0.35/txn. Effective ongoing load 12%+ of Net Sales before rent, labor, COGS.
6. **Item 19:** Average Net Sales $688,133 (2024), median $616,110. Only 40.2% exceeded average. Dramatic regional disparity: West $819k avg vs. East $489k avg. No cost or EBITDA data disclosed.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** Bottom 10% average Net Sales $311,462. At 5% royalty + 2% BMF + 5% local marketing = $37,375/year in fees alone on $311k revenue, before rent, COGS, labor, and tech fees. Marginal viability at bottom quartile.
8. **Financial health:** Guarantor (MTY Franchising USA) had FY2022 revenue of $264M, net income $19.6M, total assets $1.18B (mostly intangible: goodwill $238M + intangibles $522M). Significant debt load (interest expense $18M/yr). Current-year financials (FY2024/2023) are image-only and not yet fully extracted.
9. **Growth trajectory:** Declining. Net loss of 118 franchised units in 2024 (96 terminations, 28 reacquisitions, only 8 openings). 9 projected new franchised openings in next FY.
10. **Territory:** No exclusive territory. Franchisor reserves unlimited right to compete. Internet/ECS reserved exclusively to franchisor.
11. **Renewal:** 5-year successive term on then-current FA, which may have different terms including higher fees. General release required.
12. **Disputes:** Mediation then litigation in WA. Washington law governs. State addenda modify for 10 states.
13. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution; guarantor liabilities exceed tangible assets; Maryland special risk re financial condition; Brand Marketing Fund $7.5M deficit; gag clause on former franchisees.
14. **Gag clause:** Former franchisees have signed provisions restricting ability to speak openly — limits due diligence.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
Papa Murphy's International LLC is a Delaware limited liability company headquartered in Vancouver, Washington. It is a wholly-owned subsidiary within the MTY Food Group corporate family. The financial statements provided are for the guarantor, MTY Franchising USA, Inc. — not the franchisor entity itself. This is an important distinction: the franchisee's contractual counterparty is Papa Murphy's International LLC, but the financial disclosure covers the much larger MTY USA consolidated entity.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Initial Franchise Fee: $25,000 (first store), $15,000 (subsequent)
- Multiple Store Fee: Same structure, nonrefundable
- Extension Fee: $2,500 (6-month extension to open)
- Development Billing Agreement reimbursement: $144,764–$283,450

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Royalty: 5% of Net Sales (weekly ACH). Can increase to 15% during breach (min 14-day charge).
- Brand Marketing Fee: 2% of Net Sales (weekly ACH)
- Local Marketing: Minimum of greater of 5% of Net Sales or $2,000/month
- Online Ordering: $0.35 per transaction
- Loyalty Program: $53/month
- CRM: $10/month
- Food Service Incident Management: $3/month
- Technology stack: ~$206/month (Store Solutions $51.25 + Core Software $114 + Firewall $30+ + Payment Gateway $11)
- Internet: $50–$200/month
- Gift Card Redemption: 6.06%–13.63% of redemption amount

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Transfer Fee: $12,500–$25,000 (sliding scale)
- Successive Franchise Fee: Half of then-current franchise fee
- Non-Participation Fee: $100/day
- Relocation Fee: $5,000
- Document Administration Fee: $500
- Early Termination Damages: Formula-based (see below)
- Step-In Costs: Personnel + admin + 15%
- Interest: 18% annual on unpaid amounts
- Convention: $1,000–$3,500 (plus travel)

**Early Termination Damages Formula:**
Average monthly Royalty + BMF paid for any consecutive 12-month period within the preceding 48 months, multiplied by months remaining in term, divided by 2. If franchisee does not give 6 months written notice, the result is NOT divided by 2 — effectively doubling the damages.

### Initial Investment
Total: $367,428–$733,124. The dominant costs are leasehold improvements ($139,000–$325,000) and the opening package ($150,114–$230,000). Average leasehold cost in 2024 was $173/sq ft. The "Kitchen Delite" format is the current store design. Average store size: 1,490 sq ft (range 1,238–1,754 sq ft). Figures based on ~3 stores built in 2024.

No financing offered by franchisor.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
All food products must come from franchisor, affiliates, or approved sources. Franchisor is the only approved supplier for Store Solutions Team Support. New supplier approval takes 60–90 days and is at franchisor's sole discretion. 80–98% of total operating purchases must come from approved suppliers. 45–60% of initial investment goes to franchisor or approved suppliers.

### Revenue from Supplier Relationships
In FY2024: $709,363 from equipment/services sold to franchisees (0.13% of $69.6M revenue). $980,037 from supplier payments for conventions/meetings (1.41%). Murphy's Marketing Services (affiliate) received $2,268,033 in vendor payments to Brand Marketing Fund.

### Training
Comprehensive program: 250 hours in-store training + 50–75 hours enterprise solution training + 15 hours Foundations Class (virtual). Free for 2 signers, $750 each additional. Must complete to franchisor satisfaction or franchise may be terminated. Certified Manager required at each location.

### Technology Stack
NCR POS system (PMI Enterprise Solution). Initial cost $21,000–$24,000 (included in Opening Package). Ongoing: help desk $615/yr, software maintenance $430/yr, hosting/antivirus $630/yr, managed firewall $360–$1,080/yr, payment gateway $132/yr. Franchisor has unrestricted access to POS data. Franchisor is sole owner of guest data.

### Marketing Fund
Brand Marketing Fund had revenues of $26.3M and expenses of $26.6M in FY2024, with a carried-forward deficit of $(7.2M) that deepened to $(7.5M). Expense allocation: media 30%, production/agency 20%, field marketing 33%, admin 17%. Franchisor has no fiduciary duty regarding the fund. Unaudited summary available on request.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
None. The franchise agreement grants a specific location address only — no protected territory of any kind. Franchisor reserves unlimited rights to: (i) open company-owned or franchised stores at any location; (ii) sell products through any channel including internet, wholesale, mail order, government/military; (iii) acquire or merge with competitors; (iv) offer franchises on different terms.

### Channel Restrictions
ECS (Electronic Communication Systems) — which includes internet, social media, websites, and all digital channels — is reserved exclusively to the franchisor. Franchisee may NOT independently market on the internet or social media. Franchisee may not use franchisor marks on the internet. All advertising must receive written approval; deemed disapproved if no response within 10 days.

### Competing Brands
Within MTY family: Johnnie's New York Pizzeria, Sauce Pizza Wine (US pizza concepts through Kahala Franchising). Additionally, Giorgio, Pizza Delight, Tosto, Toujours Mikes, Vanellis (Canadian pizza brands through MTY Canada). Conflicts resolved through litigation.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
Initial term: 10 years. One 5-year successive term available, subject to extensive conditions. **Critical:** Renewal requires signing the then-current franchise agreement, which "may have different terms and conditions (e.g., higher royalty and/or marketing fees)." This is a "materially different terms" risk — the franchisee has no guarantee of economic continuity at renewal. General release (Exhibit N) required.

### Transfer
All transfers require franchisor approval. Purchase price must not exceed fair market value by more than 20%. Transferee must sign new FA on then-current terms, complete training, pay Transfer Fee ($12,500–$25,000). Seller must sign release. Right of first refusal: franchisor has 21 days to match any offer.

### Termination and Death/Disability
Termination by franchisor with cause only. Curable defaults: 30 days (operational), 10 days (payment), 24 hours (health/safety). Incurable defaults include abandonment, repeated default, fraud, criminal conviction, insolvency, lease termination, unauthorized transfer, **cross-default** (termination of any other FA with franchisor). On death/disability: 180 days for heirs to continue or transfer.

### Noncompete
During term: No interest in any pizza or competing business. Post-term: Same restriction, 25-mile radius, 2 years. All owners must agree. State addenda may limit enforceability (CA, MN, ND, WA).

### Dispute Resolution
Two-step: (1) Non-binding mediation via US Arbitration & Mediation Service, at office nearest franchisor's Support Center. (2) Litigation in federal/state court in franchisor's principal place of business (WA). Washington law governs. Multiple state addenda override forum selection and governing law (IL, MD, MN, NY, ND, RI, VA, WA).

### Litigation History
Two significant Papa Murphy's-specific cases — both franchisee class actions from 2014 alleging Item 19 misrepresentations and local marketing issues. Settled with payments ranging from $0 to $4M per group and store buybacks. Additional MTY affiliate matters (mostly state registration violations). Current: no pending material litigation.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

Papa Murphy's provides a substantial FPR covering all domestic franchise-owned stores open for the full trailing 52 weeks, for three fiscal years (2024, 2023, 2022), with three regional breakdowns (West, Central, East).

### All Franchise Stores
| Metric | 2024 (1,015) | 2023 (1,085) | 2022 (1,102) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Average Net Sales | $688,133 | $680,607 | $666,663 |
| Median Net Sales | $616,110 | $612,750 | $593,609 |
| % Exceeding Average | 40.2% | 40.4% | 40.6% |
| Top 10% Average | $1,384,981 | $1,389,069 | $1,384,276 |
| Bottom 10% Average | $311,462 | $288,383 | $273,615 |

Average Net Sales grew modestly ($666k → $688k, +3.2% over 2 years), but this is partly survivorship bias — lower-performing stores closed while stronger ones remained.

### Regional Performance
The West region (AK, CA, HI, ID, NV, OR, WA) dramatically outperforms: $819k average vs. $640k Central vs. $489k East. The West accounts for 43% of stores but represents the brand's heartland with the highest brand awareness. East region stores are substantially underperforming and shrinking rapidly (253 → 196 stores, -22.5% in 2 years).

### Critical Caveats
1. **Sales-only data:** No cost, profit, or EBITDA information is provided. A prospective franchisee cannot model unit-level profitability from the FDD alone.
2. **Unaudited, self-reported:** Data from franchisee weekly sales reports and period P&L statements. Not verified or audited.
3. **Survivorship bias:** The declining store count means the weakest-performing stores have been removed from the population, artificially inflating averages.
4. **Less than 41% exceed average** in all years — the distribution is right-skewed with a small number of high-performing stores pulling the average up.

### Item 19 Cohort Comparability
The FPR uses the same 5% royalty rate that current new franchisees pay. No discrepancy identified between the cohort economics and the current new-franchisee fee structure. The Brand Marketing Fee is also uniformly 2%. **No comparability warning warranted.**

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### Multi-Year Trajectory
The system is in sustained contraction:
- 2022: Net loss of 71 total outlets
- 2023: Net loss of 41 total outlets
- 2024: Net loss of 83 total outlets

Franchised stores declined from 1,212 (start 2022) to 1,001 (end 2024) — a loss of 211 units (17.4%) in three years.

### 2024 Activity Detail
- **Openings:** Only 8 new franchised stores opened
- **Terminations:** 96 (dramatically up from 45 in 2023)
- **Reacquisitions by franchisor:** 28 (new category — 0 in prior years)
- **Key termination states:** Alabama 11, Florida 10, Indiana 10, Texas 9, Colorado 7, Michigan 7

The reacquisition pattern is notable: the franchisor reacquired 28 stores from franchisees, primarily in Tennessee (15), North Carolina (7), Alabama (3), Georgia (3). Combined with 8 new company-owned openings (Indiana 3, Michigan 5), the company-owned count surged from 8 to 43/44. This suggests a deliberate strategy to take over distressed markets.

### Projected Openings
Only 9 new franchised outlets and 1 new company-owned outlet projected. 26 FAs signed but not yet opened. The pipeline is thin relative to the closure rate.

### Confidentiality/Gag Clause
"Current and former franchisees sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with the Papa Murphy's franchise system." This is a material disclosure that limits a prospective franchisee's ability to conduct independent due diligence by speaking with former franchisees.

## I. State Addenda Summary

### States with Addenda
California, Hawaii, Illinois, Maryland, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Virginia, Washington.

### Material Overrides by State

**California:** Post-term noncompete may not be enforceable. Washington governing law may not be enforceable. No waiver of franchise law claims.

**Illinois:** Forum selection outside IL void. Governing law provision void. No waiver of Franchise Disclosure Act.

**Maryland:** **CRITICAL** — Special risk: "franchisor's financial condition calls into question the franchisor's financial ability to provide services and support." All initial fees deferred until franchisor completes pre-opening obligations. General release on renewal/transfer may not cover Maryland Franchise Law claims. Forum selection modified to allow MD filing.

**Minnesota:** 90 days notice of termination (60 days to cure), 180 days notice of non-renewal. Forum outside MN may be prohibited. Governing law may not abrogate MN franchise rights. No jury trial waiver. No general release as condition of renewal. Item 19 caveat paragraph deleted.

**New York:** Release may not apply to NY General Business Law claims. Forum outside NY void for NY law claims.

**North Dakota:** Noncompete may be unenforceable. Forum outside ND void. Liquidated damages/termination penalties void. Governing law outside ND void. Jury trial waiver void. Punitive damages waiver void. General release on renewal void. Limitation of claims void. Prevailing party gets attorney fees.

**Rhode Island:** Forum outside RI void for RI Franchise Investment Act claims.

**Virginia:** No cancellation without reasonable cause. No waiver of franchise law claims.

**Washington:** WA Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails in conflicts. Arbitration/mediation must be in WA or mutually agreed location. Release cannot include WA franchise law rights. Transfer fees limited to reasonable costs. Post-term noncompete void for employees earning <$100k/yr and contractors earning <$250k/yr. No-poach provisions void.
