## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** 6,701 U.S. restaurants as of Dec 31, 2024 (5,524 franchised, 1,177 company-owned); 19,732 worldwide. Significant shift in 2024 due to Carrols acquisition.
2. **Entity structure:** Burger King Company LLC, a Florida LLC formed Feb 4, 2022, wholly-owned indirect subsidiary of Restaurant Brands International LP (Ontario). Predecessor BK Corporation (founded 1954, dissolved Dec 2022). 3G Restaurant Brands Holdings LP holds ~26% voting power of RBI.
3. **Ownership history:** Founded 1954 as Burger King of Miami. Became BK Corporation 1963. Internal reorganization Aug 2022 transferred assets to current LLC. RBI acquired Carrols Restaurant Group May 16, 2024 (1,023 locations becoming company-owned).
4. **Investment range:** $348,400 (Indoor MRS) to $4,705,600 (Traditional Freestanding), excluding real estate.
5. **Fee burden:** Royalty 4.5% of Gross Sales; Advertising up to 4.5%; Digital Services Fee $110/mo + 1% digital sales (capped $4,500/yr); Investment Spending up to 2%; rent if BKL. Total ongoing fee load can exceed 11% before rent.
6. **Item 19 highlights:** Traditional avg sales $1,671,613 (median $1,579,183); 43% meet or exceed average. Non-traditional avg $1,321,324. No cost data or EBITDA disclosed — gross sales only.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** 4.8% of traditional restaurants below $700K; 29.8% of non-traditional below $900K. Kiosk fuel co-branded avg only $973,531.
8. **Financial health:** RBI consolidated — net income $1.445B (2024), $1.718B (2023). BK segment income $410M (2024). Significant long-term debt. RBILP/RBI provide performance guarantees.
9. **Growth trajectory:** Net shrinkage — system lost 77 total US restaurants in 2024 (63 franchised opened, 1,116 net franchised lost due to Carrols reacquisition + closures). 48 projected new franchised + 2 company-owned for 2025.
10. **Territory:** No exclusive territory. No protected territory under Franchise Agreement. Development Agreement provides geographic area but not exclusive. Franchisor and affiliates (Tim Hortons, Popeyes, Firehouse Subs) can compete freely.
11. **Renewal:** No right of renewal — only option for successor franchise agreement (which may have materially different terms). Requires general release, building upgrades, current franchise fee.
12. **Disputes:** Litigation must be in S.D. Florida or Miami-Dade County. Florida law governs. Mandatory non-binding mediation for development disputes. No mandatory arbitration.
13. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution (Florida); mediation required before suing on development disputes.

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8-11)

### Item 8: Restrictions on Sources
- Standards and specs for most goods/services; must obtain from approved suppliers/distributors.
- Purchases from approved sources: ~65-90% of establishment costs; ~30-60% of non-occupancy operating costs.
- BKC is sole supplier of digital/loyalty technology platforms.
- Double Drive Thru required for all except food courts, in-lines, indoor MRS, Big-Box, ghost kitchens.
- Delivery required via approved third-party aggregators (e.g., Uber Eats).
- RSI (not-for-profit purchasing cooperative) acts as purchasing agent. 21-member board (2 per 9 districts + minority rep + BKC + independent).
- Supplier rebates contributed to ad fund: ~8.4% of consolidated fund revenue in 2024.
- BKC revenues from required Franchisee purchases/leases: ~$227M (16% of $1.427B total revenue) in 2024.
- Insurance requirements: CGL $2M per occurrence; auto $1M; all-risk property; workers comp.

### Item 9: Franchisee's Obligations
Standard cross-reference table. Notable: obligations span multiple agreements (FA, TRA/MTRA, DA, Lease, Investment Spending, Digital Services, Carrols APA).

### Item 10: Financing
- No general financing offered.
- **BKL leases:** BKC may own/lease and sublease to franchisee.
- **Crown Your Career financing:** BKC may finance purchase price (up to amount of private financing). Prime + 1-4%; typically 10-year term. Secured by all restaurant assets. Personal guaranty required. Cross-default with all other agreements. Venue: Miami-Dade.

### Item 11: Franchisor's Assistance, Advertising, Computer Systems, Training
**Pre-opening:** Site designation, site approval, building plans, pre-opening training, on-site assistance, MOD Manual.
**Continuing:** Merchandising/marketing data, new developments, ongoing support.
**Site selection:** Franchisee finds site within Target Area; BKC approves. Typical time TRA/MTRA to opening: several months to 2+ years.

**Training:**
- New Franchisee Training: up to 20 hours classroom/online + up to 320 hours in-restaurant.
- Fee: $7,500 per trainee.
- ServSafe required. Illinois requires classroom (no online).
- All training staff average 3+ years QSR/hospitality experience.

**Technology:**
- POS from approved vendors. Standard config: 2 front counter + 2 drive-thru devices, digital menu boards, KDS, kiosks, etc.
- POS cost: ~$20K-$25K per restaurant.
- Network standardization: $220-$450/month.
- Data reporting: $0-$300 setup + $200-$1,200/year.
- Digital Services Fee: $110/mo + 1% digital sales (cap $4,500/yr). Royal Perks loyalty program mandatory.
- Free guest Wi-Fi required.

**Advertising Fund:**
- Administered by BKC; annual audit available.
- 2024 expenditure breakdown: Working media 79.22%, Production 3.72%, Agency fees 6.60%, POP materials 1.04%, Other 9.42%.
- Minimum 65% on working media (contractual).
- National Franchisee Association (NFA) / FAC serves advisory role; no decision-making power.
- Investment Spending (IS) program: DMA-level local marketing up to 2%. Majority clause may require participation if 66.7%+ in DMA opt in.

**MOD Manual:** ~639 pages as of issuance date. Components: OPS Manual, Restaurant Equipment Manual, RSI Red Book, Approved Brands List, Approved Equipment List, BK University platform.

### Item 12: Territory
- **No exclusive territory** under Franchise Agreement.
- TRA/MTRA: Target Areas for site search — not exclusive. BKC retains full rights.
- Development Agreement: geographic Territory but still not exclusive. Competition from own system, company-owned, and alternative distribution channels all permitted.
- BKC and affiliates reserve right to sell through grocery, convenience, internet, other channels.
- Tim Hortons, Popeyes, Firehouse Subs may operate in same markets. Overlapping menus acknowledged.
- Army & Air Force Exchange Service has rights to develop on military establishments.
- BKC may acquire competing restaurant chains.

### Item 13: Trademarks
- Principal marks: BURGER KING, BK, WHOPPER, HAVE IT YOUR WAY, HOME OF THE WHOPPER, KING JR, various design marks — all registered on Principal Register.
- Final decree restricts BK mark within 20-mile radius of Mattoon, Illinois (prior user).
- No pending material trademark litigation.

### Item 14: Patents, Copyrights
- No material patents.
- Common law copyright and proprietary rights in MOD Manual.
- **AI restriction:** May not use trade secrets for ML, AI training, or input into generative AI platforms without prior approval.

### Item 15: Obligation to Participate
- **Individual/Owner-Operator:** Operating Partner must personally participate in on-premises operation, live near restaurant, devote full time, retain min 50% equity.
- **Entity:** Managing Owner must own ≥25%, have authority to bind entity, devote full time to supervision. If also MO of other BK restaurants, must designate on-premises manager.
- **Legacy Entity:** May designate Managing Director (no equity required) for day-to-day.
- **Corporate Addendum:** Parent entity can be MO; must designate Managing Director.
- No interest in any other hamburger business (except certain Contract Feeders under Corporate Addendum).
- Crown Your Career: MO cannot engage in any other business until later of 5th anniversary or loan repayment.

### Item 16: Restrictions on Sales
- Must operate solely as BK restaurant.
- Must offer all and only approved products/services.
- Delivery required where delivery aggregator services the area.
- Royal Perks loyalty program mandatory.
- Value Menu items subject to maximum price set by BKC.
- No vending machines, ATMs, video games without BKC authorization.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Term:** 20 years for freestanding; may be less for non-traditional or where property control is shorter.
- **Renewal:** No right of renewal. Option for successor FA (up to 20 years) if in compliance, property control obtained, meet then-current successor requirements.
- **"Materially different" risk:** Successor FA "may have materially different terms" — explicitly stated.
- **Successor fee:** Required. Plus general release, building upgrades, sign current FA.
- **No successor option** for Big-Box or Delivery restaurants.

### Transfer
- BKC consent required. Buyer must be approved, complete training, all monies current, transfer fee paid.
- Transfer fee: $2,000 first restaurant + $500 each additional.
- BKC has right of first refusal on same terms as third-party offer.
- Crown Your Career: transfer may be withheld for any reason during first 5 years.
- Carrols Refranchise: transfer may be withheld until remodels completed.

### Termination
**Curable defaults (cure periods):**
- 5 days: operational/quality/health/sanitation; unapproved products; abandonment; insurance.
- 10 days: non-payment of royalty/advertising.
- 30 days: late reports; unapproved equipment; failure to maintain/remodel; bad behavior; unsatisfied judgment; building damage restoration; non-compliance with laws; MO/OP/MD requirements.
- 60 days: failure to pay bills; other FA/lease/agreement defaults.
- 180 days: failure to meet minimum financial criteria.

**Non-curable defaults:** Bankruptcy; criminal conviction (>1 year imprisonment, fraud, moral turpitude); unfair competition; false statements; gross sales understatement; failure to close for serious health/safety; unauthorized transfer; repeated breaches after notice.

### Death/Disability
- Heir must be approved or sell within 12 months. BKC has option to operate/manage restaurant pending resolution.

### Noncompete
- **During term:** No interest in any hamburger business.
- **Post-term:** 1 year, within 2 miles of restaurant, no hamburger business.
- **AI restriction in Item 14** is a practical noncompete extension on trade secrets.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue:** S.D. Florida or Miami-Dade County state court.
- **Governing law:** Florida.
- **Mediation:** Mandatory non-binding mediation for development disputes only.
- **Arbitration:** None mandatory. Voluntary for development disputes at franchisee election.
- **Jury waiver:** In some agreements (Crown Your Career).

### Lease/Sublease (BKL)
- Term matches FA (typically 20 years). No right of renewal.
- Termination: failure to cure defaults (5-30 days depending on type).
- Cross-default with FA and all other agreements.
- Condemnation disputes: arbitration in Miami-Dade.

### Development Agreement
- Term: typically 3-15 years. No right of renewal.
- 180-day cure period for failure to meet cumulative opening targets.
- Non-curable: transfer; duplication of system; confidentiality breach; restrictive covenant breach; financial ratio violations (debt/EBITDA, debt+rent/EBITDAR); FSS "F" grade.
- Transfer requires BKC consent, which may be withheld in sole discretion.
