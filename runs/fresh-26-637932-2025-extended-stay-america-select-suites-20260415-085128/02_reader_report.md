# Reader Report — Extended Stay America Select Suites FDD (637932-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor:** ESH Strategies Franchise LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 16, 2010, HQ in Charlotte, NC. Began operating in hotel business in early 2016; began offering ESA Select Suites franchises in September 2022.
2. **Ownership:** Jointly owned by Starwood Capital Group (SCG) affiliates and Blackstone (BX) affiliates through Eagle Strategies Holdings LLC. Complex PE-backed ownership structure.
3. **System Size:** As of December 31, 2024: 5 franchised + 206 company-owned = 211 total ESA Select Suites hotels. Rapid growth: 0 → 87 → 131 → 211 over 2022–2024. The franchised unit count is extremely small (5 hotels).
4. **Investment Range:** New construction (123-room): $8.6M–$13.3M (excl. real property). Conversion (100-room): $195K–$3.8M.
5. **Fee Burden:** Royalty 5.5% + System Services Fee 4.5% = 10% of Gross Room Revenue ongoing. Additional booking commissions, digital marketing commission (5% of paid digital GRR), Agoda marketing fee (0.6%).
6. **Item 19:** Average occupancy 75.7%, ADR $55.89, RevPAR $42.30 across 127 hotels. Extended stay occupancy (EsOcc) 67.5%. 70.5% of consumed room nights are 30+ day stays.
7. **Financial Health:** FY2024 total revenue $25.8M, net income $1.5M (turned profitable after FY2023 net loss of $2.2M). Total assets $19.2M, member's capital $8.2M. Cash $9.2M.
8. **Special Risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution (NC), short operating history, spousal liability guarantee.
9. **Renewal:** NO right to renew or extend the franchise term (20-year term for new-build).
10. **Noncompete:** NONE — no during-term or post-term noncompete covenants.
11. **Territory:** Not exclusive by default; territory may or may not be granted at franchisor's discretion. Significant reserved rights even if territory is granted.
12. **Litigation:** Pending antitrust class action (IDeaS revenue management software). Prior guest complaint class action settled for $100K.
13. **Growth Trajectory:** Aggressive — 80 company-owned hotels added in 2024 (primarily rebrandings from ESA Suites). 22 hotels being sold to potential franchisees as of March 2025. Only 2 franchise agreements signed but not yet open.
14. **Projected Openings:** 2 new franchised hotels projected (FL, NE).

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
**Critical: Non-exclusive by default.** Territory may or may not be granted at franchisor's sole discretion based on financial/operational capability.

If territory is granted:
- During the Term, franchisor/affiliates will not open a Brand Hotel physically in the Territory (while franchisee is not in default)
- BUT protection applies ONLY to the EXTENDED STAY AMERICA SELECT SUITES brand — does NOT prevent ESA Suites, ESA Premier Suites, or any other brand
- Franchisor reserves right to: own/operate/franchise Brand Hotels outside territory; operate Network Hotels of any brand inside territory; market Brand Hotels inside territory; use all distribution channels inside territory; acquire/merge with competing chains
- Pre-existing Brand Hotels in territory will continue and may be renewed, expanded, or replaced
- Franchisor may operate a Brand Hotel in territory starting 6 months before franchise expiration
- No right to relocate the site
- No ROFR or option for additional outlets under the Franchise Agreement

**No restrictions on franchisee from soliciting guests outside territory**, but cannot use internet/social media without consent. Can use catalog sales, telemarketing, direct marketing.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Term:** 20 years for new-build from Opening Date. Conversion term at franchisor's discretion.
- **Renewal:** **NONE.** No right to renew or extend. This is a significant risk factor — at end of term, the franchise simply expires with no guaranteed path to continuation.
- HTA: Initial 3-year term with automatic 1-year renewals, coterminous with Franchise Agreement.

### Termination
**By Franchisee:** Only if Hotel damaged by fire/casualty exceeding coverage limits, or under applicable state law.

**By Franchisor without cause:** If Hotel taken/condemned or damaged beyond coverage limits. HTA can be terminated at any time with 10 days' notice.

**By Franchisor with cause (curable):** Most defaults are curable with 30-day cure period (10 days for monetary defaults).

**By Franchisor with cause (non-curable, immediate termination):** Bankruptcy, cessation of operations, attachment of assets, contesting marks, felony conviction, false records, 3 defaults in 12 months, 2 similar defaults in 6 months, safety/security breaches, data privacy violations, insurance/indemnification breaches.

### Transfer
- All transfers require franchisor approval (except certain non-control-change transfers)
- Conditions: satisfy all monetary obligations, pay PIP fee, transferee must update/remodel to current Standards, general release required, transferee pays then-current initial franchise fee as transfer fee, transferee must enter then-current franchise agreement (which **may contain materially different terms**)
- Death/disability: must transfer to third party (may include heirs) within required timeframe; franchisor may terminate if transfer not completed

### Right of First Offer
Only if Exhibit F is signed (at franchisor's discretion for strategic markets). Not a ROFR — it's a right of first offer.

### Noncompete
**NONE** — no during-term or post-term noncompete covenants.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration:** Required for most disputes (except indemnification, equitable relief, enforcement of arbitral awards)
- **Venue:** Arbitration in Charlotte, NC (franchisor's headquarters)
- **Governing Law:** North Carolina
- **Jury Trial Waiver:** Yes, waived
- **Non-exclusive jurisdiction:** North Carolina courts for non-arbitrable disputes

### Post-Termination Obligations
Cease all Brand use, de-identify Hotel, return Standards/Confidential Information, change phone listings/internet registrations, pay Termination Fee (liquidated damages) within 10 business days, permit franchisor inspection until de-identified.

### Modification
No modification except in writing signed by both parties. BUT franchisor may unilaterally change Standards.

### Integration
Standard merger clause — only written agreements binding. However, nothing disclaims representations in FDD.
