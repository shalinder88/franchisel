**Brand:** Extended Stay America Select Suites
**Franchisor:** ESH Strategies Franchise LLC
**FDD Issuance Date:** March 27, 2025
**Filing ID:** 637932-2025 (Wisconsin DFI)
**Extraction Date:** April 15, 2026

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System Size & Growth:** As of December 31, 2024, the ESA Select Suites system comprises 211 hotels (5 franchised, 206 company-owned). The brand launched franchising in September 2022 and has grown explosively — from zero to 211 hotels in under three years — primarily through rebranding of company-owned properties from the ESA Suites brand (43 in 2023, 80 in 2024).

2. **Entity Structure:** ESH Strategies Franchise LLC is a Delaware LLC formed in 2010, jointly owned 50/50 by Starwood Capital Group (SCG) and Blackstone (BX) affiliates through Eagle Strategies Holdings LLC. The franchisor does not own or operate hotels directly; its affiliate ESH Hospitality, Inc. owns 572 hotels across all ESA brands, operated by ESA Management LLC.

3. **Ownership History:** Originally part of Extended Stay America, Inc., which sold its ownership interest to Eagle Strategies Holdings LLC in June 2021 following the Blackstone/Starwood Capital acquisition of ESA.

4. **Investment Range:** New construction (123-room prototype): $8.6M–$13.3M excluding real property. Conversion (100-room): $195K–$3.8M. No franchisor financing available.

5. **Fee Burden:** Core ongoing fees total 10% of Gross Room Revenue (5.5% royalty + 4.5% System Services Fee). Additional fees include a 5% Digital Marketing Commission on paid digital channel GRR (going into effect 2025), 0.6% Agoda marketing fee, booking commissions, and $3.87/room/month in PMS technology fees.

6. **Item 19 Highlights:** Average RevPAR of $42.30 across 127 hotels ($55.89 ADR, 75.7% occupancy). Strong extended-stay profile with 70.5% of room nights from 30+ day stays. However, 126 of 127 hotels in the population are company-owned — franchisee performance data is essentially non-existent. No cost data, EBITDA, or expense ratios disclosed.

7. **Bottom-Quartile Risk:** The ADR of $55.89 is very low relative to the hospitality industry, indicating value-tier positioning. Combined with a 10%+ fee burden on GRR, margins at bottom-quartile properties could be extremely thin. Without disclosed cost data, prospective franchisees cannot model unit economics from the FDD alone.

8. **Financial Health:** The franchisor entity turned profitable in FY2024 ($1.5M net income on $25.8M revenue) after a $2.2M loss in FY2023. Cash position is healthy at $9.2M. However, FY2023 required a $3M cash infusion from the parent entity, and the auditor included an Emphasis of Matter regarding significant related party transactions.

9. **Growth Trajectory:** Rapid company-owned expansion (primarily rebrandings) with a nascent franchise program. The system is dominated by company-owned units (97.6%). Twenty-two company-owned hotels are being sold to franchisees as of March 2025, which will significantly reshape the franchise system.

10. **Territory:** Non-exclusive by default. Territory may or may not be granted at franchisor's sole discretion. Even if granted, protections apply only to the ESA Select Suites brand — other ESA brands (Suites, Premier Suites) can compete in your territory. Franchisor reserves broad rights.

11. **Renewal:** NO right to renew or extend the 20-year franchise term. This is a significant and unusual risk — at expiration, the franchise simply ends with no guaranteed path to continuation.

12. **Disputes:** Pending antitrust class action involving IDeaS revenue management algorithm. All disputes resolved by arbitration in Charlotte, NC under North Carolina law (with state-specific overrides available in 12 states).

13. **Special Risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution (NC venue), short operating history (early-stage development), spousal liability guarantee required.

14. **No Noncompete:** Unusually, there are no during-term or post-term noncompete covenants. A franchisee could theoretically open a competing hotel immediately upon termination.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

ESH Strategies Franchise LLC is a single-purpose franchise entity within a complex PE-backed ownership structure. The entity was formed in 2010 but began hotel operations in 2016 and ESA Select Suites franchising in September 2022. It is structured as a Delaware LLC, meaning it is a pass-through entity for tax purposes (not subject to federal income taxes).

The ownership chain runs: ESH Strategies Franchise LLC → ESH Hospitality Strategies LLC → Eagle Strategies Holdings LLC → 50% Starwood Capital Group / 50% Blackstone. The trademark is owned by a sibling entity (ESH Strategies Branding LLC) and licensed to the franchisor through a Trademark and System License Agreement expiring December 31, 2030.

Key affiliated entities:
- **ESH Hospitality, Inc.** — Owns 572 hotels across ESA brands
- **ESA Management LLC** — Manages owned properties and some franchised hotels
- **ESH Strategies Branding LLC** — Trademark owner

Related competing brands: ESA Suites (107 franchised), ESA Premier Suites (17 franchised), Yotel (3 franchised), Shimao brands (China), and potentially Field & Stream.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
The initial franchise fee is $50,000 (negotiable; range $40,000–$50,000 in prior year). Additional initial fees to the franchisor include: Project Management Fee ($5,000), GM Certification ($1,900/person), New Owner Orientation ($1,900/person), Pre-Opening On-site Training ($2,500, waivable), Construction Site Visits ($6,000), IT Site Visits ($2,000–$4,000). Total initial fees to franchisor: $66,350–$87,850 (new construction) or $60,350–$83,850 (conversion).

**Ongoing Fees:**
| Category | Rate/Amount |
|----------|-------------|
| Royalty | 5.5% of Gross Room Revenue |
| System Services Fee | 4.5% of Gross Room Revenue |
| **Subtotal Core** | **10.0% of GRR** |
| Digital Marketing Commission | 5% of GRR from paid digital channels |
| Agoda Marketing Fee | 0.6% of GRR on Agoda bookings |
| Booking & Commissions | Varies (pass-through) |
| PMS License/Hosting/Support | $3.87/room/month |
| AHLA Dues | $3.00/room/year |
| Annual Conference | $1,200/attendee (mandatory for 1) |

The effective total fee burden is significantly above 10% when booking commissions, the Digital Marketing Commission, and technology fees are included. For a 123-room hotel at the average RevPAR ($42.30), the annual GRR would be approximately $1.9M, generating ~$190K in royalty + SSF alone. The PMS fees add ~$5,700/year.

**Gross Room Revenue Definition:** Broadly inclusive — encompasses barter/credit transactions, loyalty point redemptions, mandatory guest fees, group booking rebates, business interruption insurance proceeds, and no-show/cancellation fees. Excludes only taxes collected for transmittal, vending, and laundry.

**System Services Fee Spending (FY2024):** $5,326,228 (2.4% of total franchisee GRR). Allocation: 72% digital marketing/internet advertising, 17% administrative, 11% other marketing including SEO. Notably, SSF contributions are pooled across all ESA Network brands, with no obligation to benefit any specific hotel, market, or brand proportionately.

**Liquidated Damages:** Complex formula for early termination:
- Before Opening Date: rooms × $3,000 (e.g., 123 rooms = $369,000)
- After opening, first year: average monthly royalty × 60
- After first year, >5 years remaining: 5 × prior 12-month royalty
- <5 years remaining: remaining days × average daily royalty
Full contractual rates used for calculation even if temporary reductions were granted.

### Initial Investment

**New Construction (123-room prototype):** $8,612,237 – $13,285,766 excluding real property
The largest component is improvements ($7.35M–$11.25M), followed by FF&E ($500K–$750K), technology ($181K–$237K), and OS&E ($115K–$200K). The prototype is a 4-story building requiring at least 2.0 acres in a suburban location. Urban locations, unusual site conditions, or organized labor markets will increase costs. Three months of additional funds ($175K–$250K) are recommended.

**Conversion (100-room):** $195,146 – $3,759,926 excluding real property
The wide range reflects that existing extended-stay hotels with kitchens require minimal renovation (low end), while significant upgrades to meet brand standards push costs higher. The low end assumes the property is operating during conversion, existing insurance satisfies Standards, and no environmental study is needed.

No financing is available from the franchisor or its affiliates.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

The franchisor maintains tight control over supplies and suppliers. An estimated 80-90% of establishment purchases and 65-75% of ongoing operating purchases must comply with franchisor standards or come from approved suppliers. The franchisor currently designates sole suppliers for:
- Property Management System (PMS)
- Revenue Management Software (RMS)
- Centralized Reservation System (CRS)
- Credit card terminals and payment processing
- Digital credit card authorizations
- Inbound/outbound call routing

For non-approved IT vendors, franchisees must pay an IT Certification Fee of $2,000–$5,000. Alternative supplier requests require a 60-day review period. The franchisor has no obligation to approve additional suppliers for items where a sole supplier exists.

The franchisor derives discounts, rebates, commissions, and promotional allowances from supplier relationships. In FY2024, the affiliate ESA Management received $2.5M in management services revenue from a limited number of franchisee-owned ESA Suites hotels.

### Training Program

Four required training components:
1. **New Owner Orientation** (12 hours, Charlotte NC) — Required within 6 months (new build) or 3 months (conversion) of signing FA. Covers brand introduction, standards, systems, construction process, revenue management, billing, reporting. $1,900/person.
2. **GM Certification** (12-14 hours, online or Charlotte NC) — Required before opening. Covers brand requirements, technology, quality programs, guest relations. $1,900/person. New GMs must complete within 60 days.
3. **Pre-Opening On-site Training** (12 hours OTJ, your hotel) — ESA trainer visits for ~2 days before opening. $2,500 (waivable for experienced operators).
4. **Service Culture Training** (1 hour, online) — All associates within 90 days of hire. Compliance tracked via reports.

Additionally, unlimited access to ESU Online learning management system (currently free). Remedial training ($4,000–$7,500) if QA inspection fails. Annual human trafficking awareness training (self-provided).

### Marketing Fund Spending

The System Services Fee (4.5% of GRR) functions as the marketing fund, but it also covers reservation systems, technology support, quality assurance, national sales, training, and other programs. In FY2024, $5.3M was spent on marketing (72% digital/internet advertising, 17% admin, 11% SEO/other). Franchisees may NOT engage in any digital or email marketing — the franchisor controls all digital marketing exclusively.

No periodic accounting of SSF spending is provided. No advertising council exists (franchisor reserves right to create one). No local/regional advertising cooperatives (franchisor reserves right to require). The franchisor has no obligation to spend any SSF funds in any particular market or for any particular hotel.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition and Carve-outs

The franchise agreement grants a **non-exclusive** right by default. Whether a territory is granted, and its size, depends entirely on the franchisor's assessment of the franchisee's financial and operational capability to meet extended-stay demand in the market. This is discretionary — there is no formula or guaranteed territory.

Even if a territory is granted, the protection is narrow:
- Applies **ONLY** to the "Extended Stay America Select Suites" brand
- Does **NOT** prevent ESA Suites, ESA Premier Suites, or any other ESA brand from operating in the territory
- Does **NOT** prevent the franchisor from marketing or accepting reservations from guests in the territory

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights

The franchisor reserves extremely broad rights even within a granted territory:
- May own, operate, franchise, or license Brand Hotels outside the territory
- May operate Network Hotels of **any** brand at **any** location inside or outside the territory
- May market and advertise for Brand Hotels inside the territory
- May use any distribution channel (internet, telemarketing, catalog, direct marketing) inside the territory
- May acquire or be acquired by competing hotel chains
- May replace removed Brand Hotels with new ones (potentially larger)
- May operate a Brand Hotel in the territory beginning 6 months before franchise expiration
- Pre-existing Brand Hotels in territory continue and may be renewed, expanded, or replaced

The franchisee has no right to relocate the hotel or obtain additional franchise locations under the existing agreement. Internet and social media marketing requires franchisor consent.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The franchise term is 20 years for new-build hotels (from Opening Date). For conversions, the term is determined by the franchisor based on the hotel's current age, condition, and location — which could result in a shorter term.

**There is no right to renew or extend the franchise term.** This is a significant and unusual provision in the franchise industry. At the end of the term, the franchise simply expires. The franchisee would need to de-identify the hotel, cease using the brand, and either negotiate a new franchise agreement (with no guarantee of success or favorable terms) or exit the system entirely.

### Transfer Provisions

All transfers require franchisor approval. Transfer conditions include:
- Satisfy all monetary obligations to franchisor and affiliates
- Pay then-current PIP fee
- Transferee must agree to update/remodel hotel to current Standards
- Franchisee and owners must sign a general release (limited in CA, MD, MN, WA)
- Transferee must submit new franchise application and pay then-current initial franchise fee as transfer fee
- Transferee must meet current ownership criteria
- **Transferee must enter into the then-current form of franchise agreement, which may contain materially different terms and conditions**

This last point is critical — a transfer is effectively a new franchise deal at the franchisor's current terms, not a simple assignment.

Right of First Offer: Only applies if Exhibit F is signed (at franchisor's discretion for strategic markets). This is a right of first offer, not refusal.

### Termination and Death/Disability

**By franchisee:** Only if the hotel is damaged by casualty exceeding coverage limits, or under applicable state law.

**Death/disability:** Must transfer to a third party (may include heirs) meeting all transfer conditions within the required timeframe. Franchisor may terminate if transfer not completed.

### Noncompete Restrictions

**None.** There are no during-term or post-term noncompete covenants. This is unusual in franchising and means a terminated franchisee could immediately open a competing hotel under a different brand.

### Dispute Resolution

Disputes are resolved by arbitration in Charlotte, NC under North Carolina law. Jury trial is waived. Exceptions: claims for indemnification, equitable relief, and enforcement of arbitral awards.

**State overrides:** 12 states have addenda modifying dispute resolution:
- Governing law overridden to state law: CA, IL, IN, MD, MN, ND, WA, WI
- Venue/arbitration must include home state: IN, MD, MN, ND, WA
- Jury trial waiver challenged: ND
- Extended cure periods: MN, WI (60-day cure, 90-day termination notice)

### Litigation History

**Pending:** A consolidated antitrust class action (In re Extended Stay Hotel Antitrust Litigation, Case No. 24-cv-6324) in the N.D. of California alleges that Extended Stay America and other hotel companies violated the Sherman Act by using IDeaS revenue management algorithm software to coordinate room pricing, artificially raising rates and suppressing occupancy. The company denies the allegations. A motion to dismiss was pending at the time of FDD issuance.

**Prior:** Brittian v. ESA Management LLC — a class action alleging unfair practices regarding denying guest accommodations and failing to provide refunds. Settled in July 2024 for $100,000. Individual claims dismissed with prejudice; class claims dismissed without prejudice.

No bankruptcies.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Population and Methodology

The Item 19 FPR covers 127 hotels (126 company-owned/affiliate-operated + 1 franchised) that were open for the full 12-month period ended December 31, 2024. The single franchised hotel's data was combined with the owned hotels because (a) its identity would be discernible, and (b) its results are "not materially different" from the owned hotels.

This means the FPR data is overwhelmingly driven by company-owned operations, which may have structural advantages (corporate management, economies of scale, centralized purchasing) or disadvantages (corporate overhead allocation) relative to franchised operations.

### Operating Metrics (Table 19-1)

| Metric | Average | Median | % at/above Avg | Range Context |
|--------|---------|--------|----------------|---------------|
| Occupancy | 75.7% | 76.5% | 56% (71/127) | Healthy for extended stay |
| ADR | $55.89 | $55.44 | 49% (62/127) | Very low — value tier |
| RevPAR | $42.30 | $42.52 | 51% (65/127) | Low absolute number |
| EsOcc | 67.5% | 68.3% | 52% (66/127) | Strong extended stay mix |

The median slightly exceeds the average for occupancy, RevPAR, and EsOcc, suggesting a roughly symmetric distribution without extreme low outliers dragging down the average significantly.

### Reservation Channel Mix (Table 19-2)

Franchisor-controlled channels (Call Center 31.2% + esa.com 30.3%) account for 61.5% of bookings, demonstrating significant system-level value. Property Direct accounts for 28.6%, with OTAs at only 8.7%. This low OTA dependency is favorable for margins.

### Length of Stay (Table 19-3)

The stay profile is overwhelmingly extended-stay: 70.5% of room nights are 30+ day stays, and 89.2% are 7+ day stays. Only 10.8% of room nights are transient (0-6 nights). This is the core business model — long-term guests at lower nightly rates but with lower operating costs (reduced turnover, cleaning, check-in labor).

### B2B Revenue (Table 19-4)

95.3% of B2B revenue comes from 7+ night bookings, confirming that the national sales team's corporate and project-based booking efforts align with the extended-stay model.

### Critical Caveats

1. **No cost data disclosed.** Prospective franchisees have no FDD basis for estimating operating expenses, gross operating profit, or EBITDA.
2. **No quartile distribution.** Cannot assess bottom-quartile risk from the FPR alone.
3. **126/127 hotels are company-owned.** The sample is not representative of franchisee operations.
4. **The ADR of $55.89 is extremely low.** At 10%+ fee burden, even small expense increases or occupancy dips could erode margins significantly.
5. **RevPAR varies significantly among all properties** — the franchisor's own caveat.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Profitability turnaround:** Moved from a $2.2M loss in 2023 to $1.5M profit in 2024. Revenue grew 40% ($18.4M → $25.8M) as the franchise system expanded from 114 to 129 total franchised hotels across all ESA brands.

2. **System service expense deficit:** System service expenses ($12.2M) exceed system service fee revenue ($10.9M) by $1.3M, indicating the franchisor is spending more on system services than it collects from franchisees. This is positive for franchisees in the short term but could signal future fee pressure.

3. **Other revenue spike:** FY2024 other revenues of $2.5M (vs. $324K in 2023) include $2.1M in contract termination fees — an unusual one-time item.

4. **Parent support diminishing:** FY2023 required $3M in cash member contributions. FY2024 required zero cash contributions (only $87K in non-cash contributions), suggesting the entity is becoming self-sustaining.

5. **Leverage and related party exposure:** Due to affiliates decreased from $4.8M to $2.8M, a positive trend. The entity has no external debt.

6. **Intangible assets:** $5.4M net (franchise agreements at 10-year weighted remaining life + software licenses at 3-year remaining life). The franchise agreements are valued at $4.9M gross.

7. **Brand launch expenses:** $2.5M incurred in 2022 for payments to existing franchisees related to territory exemptions to facilitate the ESA Select Suites launch. $1.6M remains in accrued liabilities as of 2024 — being satisfied through fee reductions.

8. **Trademark license:** The franchisor pays 5% of franchise royalties ($429K in 2024) to ESH Strategies Branding LLC for the trademark license.

9. **Total franchised system (all ESA brands):** 129 hotels, 13,351 rooms as of December 31, 2024 (up from 114 hotels, 11,836 rooms at end of 2023).
