# Reader Report — Spark by Hilton (638065-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, Delaware LLC formed Sept 2007; wholly owned subsidiary of Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., which is owned by Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT).
2. **Brand launch**: Spark by Hilton launched Nov 11, 2022 as an economy hotel brand. Conversions only — no new construction.
3. **System size**: 0→8 hotels (2023), 8→91 hotels (2024). Explosive early growth. 189 franchise agreements signed but not opened; 140 projected openings in next fiscal year. Zero company-owned.
4. **Total investment**: $3,102,531–$5,514,193 for a 100-room conversion (excludes real property, insurance, market studies).
5. **Fee burden**: Royalty 5.5% GRR + Program fee 3.0–3.5% GRR = 8.5–9.0% GRR ongoing. Franchise application fee $75,000. Hilton Honors 2.0% of total eligible guest folio.
6. **Item 19**: No financial performance representations made. Explicit no-FPR statement.
7. **Territory**: Non-exclusive license. No standard protected territory. Restricted Area possible but shorter than term, not standard.
8. **Renewal**: No right to renew or extend. Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms.
9. **Term**: 10–15 years for conversions; measured from Opening Date.
10. **Noncompete**: During term only — cannot own/be franchisor of competing brand. No post-term noncompete.
11. **Dispute resolution**: Litigation in Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria) or Fairfax County, VA, or New York. NY law governs. No arbitration except Puerto Rico.
12. **Litigation**: Multiple antitrust class actions pending (IDeaS/SAS, Amadeus, CoStar/STR price-fixing allegations). Settled Texas and Nebraska deceptive trade practices cases re: mandatory guest fees.
13. **Financial health**: Franchisor entity generated $1.502B total revenue in 2024 ($1.434B royalty fees). Net income $1.488B. Distributed $1.5B to parent. Total assets $1.27B, member's equity $577M. Clean audit opinion, no going-concern.
14. **State addenda**: 11 states with disclosure document addenda (CA, HI, IL, MD, MI, MN, NY, ND, RI, VA, WA). Separate state addenda to franchise agreement also present.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- Non-exclusive license. No standard protected territory.
- Franchisor and affiliates may compete anywhere under any brand.
- Restricted Area possible but optional and negotiated. Restricted Area Provision typically restricts only same-brand hotels, for a period shorter than the term.
- Carve-outs from Restricted Area: (a) existing/approved hotels; (b) other brands; (c) post-Restrictive Period; (d) gaming-oriented; (e) shared ownership; (f) chain of 4+ hotels acquired in single transaction.
- Strategic Partnerships are "Other Businesses" — no protection from Strategic Partners.
- No relocation permitted. No options or rights of first refusal for additional franchises.
- Internet: franchisee may not own domain names with Marks; only approved Sites.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
- Principal Mark: "Spark" — registered on USPTO Principal Register (Reg. 7243625, Dec 12, 2023; design Reg. 7483241, Aug 20, 2024). "HILTON" word mark (Reg. 2478190, Aug 14, 2001).
- Marks owned by affiliate HIH (Hilton International Holding LLC), licensed to franchisor.
- Perpetual agreements with Lipscomb University and Accelya (formerly Farelogix) re: SPARK trademark coexistence. Conditions: no dedicated meeting space/planners, no clothing-optional hotels, no airline reservation platform.
- No known infringing uses. All renewals filed.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
- No patents or registered copyrights material to franchise business.
- Unregistered copyrights claimed on Manual and OnQ software.
- All information obtained from franchisee's hotel/guests is franchisor's property.
- Must comply with all privacy laws.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
- Hotel must be operated by franchisee or approved Management Company.
- Franchisee must not be a Competitor (defined as owner/franchisor/licensor of competing brand).
- If Management Company becomes a Competitor, 90 days to replace.
- Guaranty required — may include spouse of owner.
- No post-term noncompete required.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on What the Franchisee May Sell
- Must operate 24/7.
- No dedicated meeting rooms.
- No gaming/casino without express permission.
- No timeshare/vacation ownership sales without permission.
- Must comply with Hilton Honors and all discount/promotional programs.
- Must use Reservation Service exclusively for outgoing referrals.
- No Mandatory Guest Fees without consent.
- No limit on franchisor's right to change the System.

## Pass E: Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- Term: 10–15 years from Opening Date (Conversion). No standard renewal right. Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion — may require materially different terms and PIP compliance.

### Termination by Franchisee
- Not authorized. If franchisee terminates unilaterally, it constitutes material breach and triggers liquidated damages.

### Termination by Franchisor
**Without cause:** Condemnation (no damages owed) and Casualty (18-month rebuild window; no damages if no competitor hotel at site for 3 years).

**With cause (curable):** Payment defaults (10 days cure); failure to commence/complete Hotel Work (10 days); insurance default (10 days); other FA/Manual/Standards default (30 days).

**Without cure:** Repeat non-compliance in 24 months; 3 default notices in 12 months; insolvency/bankruptcy; loss of possession; ceasing operations 5+ days; contesting Marks ownership; conduct harmful to reputation; false records/fraud; unauthorized transfer; becoming Sanctioned Person; guarantor breach; public health threat; becoming Competitor; Hotel Agreement termination.

### Transfer
- All transfers prohibited except: (a) Permitted Transfers (no change of control, some without notice); (b) Change of Ownership Transfers ($75,000 application fee, new FA on then-current terms, PIP).
- Death: transfer to family member within 1 year if they meet requirements.
- No right of first refusal, but must notify franchisor of intent to market equity.

### Noncompete
- During term: cannot own/be franchisor/licensor of competing brand. May be franchisee of or manage competitor hotels.
- Post-term: No noncompete covenant.

### Dispute Resolution
- Governing law: New York (without NY choice-of-law principles).
- Forum: US District Court ED Virginia (Alexandria), or Fairfax County, VA state court, or New York, NY. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- No arbitration (except Puerto Rico).
- No jury trial waiver in base agreement (but state addenda may override).

### Liquidated Damages
- Pre-opening termination: $10,000 × number of approved guest rooms.
- Post-opening, before 2nd anniversary: greater of (Average Monthly Royalty × 60) or ($10,000 × rooms).
- After 2nd anniversary but before last 60 months: Average Monthly Royalty × 60.
- Last 60 months: Average Monthly Royalty × remaining months.

### Post-Termination Obligations
- Cease using System, Marks, trade name immediately.
- Remove signs, custom decorations, promotional materials.
- Return Manual, proprietary information.
- Assign domain names to franchisor.
- Pay all sums due including liquidated damages.
