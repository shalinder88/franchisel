# Full Diligence Report — Spark by Hilton (638065-2025)

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and trajectory**: Spark by Hilton is Hilton's newest brand, launched November 2022 as an economy-segment conversion-only hotel concept. The system exploded from 0 to 8 units in 2023 and from 8 to 91 units in 2024. As of December 31, 2024, there are 189 franchise agreements signed but not yet opened, with 140 projected new openings in the next fiscal year. Zero company-owned units. Zero terminations or non-renewals to date.

2. **Entity structure and ownership**: The franchisor is Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 2007. It is wholly owned by Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. (formed July 2016), which is owned by Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT, formed March 2010). The franchisor entity licenses all Hilton brands in the US. Hilton's history traces to 1946 (Hilton Hotels Corporation). Park Hotels & Resorts was spun off in January 2017, divesting nearly all company-owned hotels.

3. **Investment range**: $3,102,531 to $5,514,193 for a standard 100-room conversion, excluding real property, market studies, and insurance. The largest components are construction/renovation ($1.2M–$2.7M), FF&E ($850K–$1M), and additional working capital ($300K–$500K for 3 months).

4. **Fee burden**: Ongoing fees total 8.5–9.0% of Gross Rooms Revenue (5.5% royalty + 3.0–3.5% program fee), plus Hilton Honors fees (2.0% of eligible guest folio), plus technology fees ($2,000–$4,500/month for OnQ, connected room, and internet), plus various distribution program fees. The franchise application fee is $75,000.

5. **Item 19**: No financial performance representations made. Explicit no-FPR statement.

6. **Financial health**: The franchisor entity generated $1.502B in total revenue in FY2024 ($1.434B from franchise royalty fees across all Hilton brands). Net income was $1.488B on only $11.4M in expenses — the entity is essentially a pass-through IP licensing vehicle. It distributed $1.5B to its parent in 2024. Total assets of $1.27B with $577M in member's equity. Clean audit opinion from CBH, no going-concern issues.

7. **Growth trajectory**: This is one of the fastest-growing hotel brands in the US. From zero to 91 units in 2 years, with 189 more in pipeline. The conversion-only model allows rapid deployment by re-flagging existing economy hotels.

8. **Territory risk**: Non-exclusive license with no standard protected territory. The franchisor explicitly reserves the right to place other Hilton brands and Strategic Partnership properties anywhere, including adjacent to your hotel.

9. **Renewal**: No right to renew. Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion, potentially on materially different terms.

10. **Disputes**: New York law governs. Litigation must be brought in Virginia (Eastern District or Fairfax County) or New York. No arbitration except Puerto Rico. Multiple antitrust class actions pending against Hilton Worldwide (IDeaS, Amadeus, CoStar/STR price-fixing allegations).

11. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Virginia or New York). Spark hotels may not have dedicated meeting rooms (per Lipscomb coexistence agreement).

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
The Brand Package system is the key supplier control mechanism. HSM is the sole Brand Package procurement services provider. You must purchase all Brand Package items from approved suppliers only, and you must use HSM for procurement coordination. You cannot obtain Brand Package items or procurement services from any other source.

For non-Brand Package items, you may purchase from any supplier meeting Hilton's Standards and specifications. However, Hilton may in the future require approved suppliers for these items too.

HSM receives multiple revenue streams from the supply chain:
- Procurement services fees: up to 10% of cost of goods
- Rebates and allowances from manufacturers: $19.6M across all brands (FY2024)
- Administrative fees from manufacturers: $38.7M across all brands (FY2024)
- Direct goods/services revenue: $8.8M across all brands (FY2024)

Hilton affiliates received $1.101B in revenues from sales to franchisees across all brands in FY2024 (excluding royalty and program fees).

### Training Program
14 required/optional training programs. Key programs:
- GM/Commercial Leader Training: up to 18 hours virtual, $1,200, within 90 days of start
- Core Sales Skills: 24 hours virtual, $600, within 90 days of start
- HPMS Training: 1–5 hours online, no fee
- Owner Orientation: 16 hours virtual, no fee (required for first-time franchisees)
- New to Hospitality Owner Education: 20 hours virtual, up to $2,500 (vendor-provided)
- Brand Conference: annual, up to $2,500/attendee, mandatory for GM and DoS
- Annual Compliance Training: 3–4 hours, topics include harassment prevention, human trafficking, cybersecurity

### Technology Stack
The technology burden is substantial and grows over time:
- **OnQ**: Proprietary PMS, reservations, revenue management. Refresh every 3 years. $33.5K–$87.8K initial + $1,442–$2,935/month ongoing.
- **Guest Internet Access (StayConnected)**: $51K–$73K initial + $1,359–$2,465/month ongoing. Refresh every 4 years.
- **Connected Room**: Streaming apps/Hilton Honors App control. $24.6K–$29.4K initial + $455–$720/month ongoing.
- **GRO (IDeaS)**: Revenue optimization. $3,400 initial. No ongoing fee currently.
- **Delphi**: Cloud sales/events (Amadeus Hospitality). No fee (covered by Program Fee). Optional $858/user/year.
- **Digital Key**: Included in OnQ. No separate fee.
- **Digital Payment (Adyen)**: $750–$1,500 initial. Up to $2,100/year ongoing.
- **Digital Floor Plan**: $1,500 one-time.

Franchisor has independent access to all data in all systems with no contractual limitations.

### Marketing Fund
There is no separate advertising fund. The Monthly Program Fee (3.0–3.5% GRR) funds all marketing, technology programs, quality assurance, reservation systems, and administrative overhead. The franchisor has sole discretion over how these funds are spent and is not obligated to spend proportionately for any individual property. The Program Fee "does not constitute a trust or 'advertising fund'" and the franchisor "is not a fiduciary."

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
There is no standard protected territory. The franchise grants a non-exclusive license to operate at a specified location. The franchisor and all affiliates may compete freely anywhere under any brand.

A Restricted Area Provision may be negotiated, but it is optional, typically shorter than the term, and excludes: (a) existing/approved Brand hotels; (b) other Hilton brands; (c) gaming-oriented facilities; (d) shared ownership properties; (e) chains of 4+ hotels acquired in a single transaction; and (f) Strategic Partnership properties.

### Channel Restrictions
- Must use Reservation Service exclusively for outgoing referrals
- Cannot register or own domain names with Hilton Marks
- Cannot cross-market with non-System/Network hotels without consent
- Must participate in Hilton Honors and honor all discount/promotional programs
- Must honor room rates quoted through any reservation channel

### Encroachment Risk
The encroachment risk is high. Hilton operates approximately 20 brands and explicitly reserves the right to place any of them — including other economy brands — adjacent to your hotel. There is no mechanism for resolving conflicts between your hotel and other Hilton hotels. "Any resolution of conflicts regarding location, customers, support or services will be entirely within the business judgment of Hilton Worldwide and us."

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
The initial term is 10–15 years from the Opening Date for conversions. There is no right to renew or extend. If Hilton agrees to re-license (at its sole discretion), you may be required to sign a new franchise agreement with materially different terms and comply with any PIP requirements.

### Transfer
All transfers are prohibited except Permitted Transfers (no change of control) and Change of Ownership Transfers ($75,000 application fee, new FA on then-current terms, PIP compliance). The proposed transferee must meet Hilton's then-current requirements and cannot be a Competitor or Sanctioned Person. Death transfers are permitted to immediate family within 1 year if they meet all requirements.

### Termination
Hilton may terminate immediately without cure opportunity for 16 different grounds including: repeat non-compliance in 24 months, 3 default notices in 12 months, bankruptcy, ceasing operations for 5+ days, fraud, becoming a Competitor, and public health threats.

Curable defaults include: payment (10 days), Hotel Work delays (10 days), insurance (10 days), and other FA/Manual defaults (30 days).

### Liquidated Damages
The liquidated damages formula is aggressive:
- Before 2nd anniversary: greater of (Average Monthly Royalty × 60) or ($10,000 × rooms)
- After 2nd anniversary: Average Monthly Royalty × 60
- Last 60 months: Average Monthly Royalty × remaining months
For a 100-room hotel with even modest revenues, this could easily exceed $1M.

### Noncompete
During term: you cannot own or be the franchisor/licensor of a competing brand. You may be a franchisee of, or manage, competitor properties.
Post-term: No noncompete covenant.

### Dispute Resolution
- Governing law: New York (without NY choice-of-law principles)
- Venue: US District Court ED Virginia (Alexandria), or Fairfax County VA state court, or New York NY
- Franchisor may sue where hotel is located
- No arbitration (except Puerto Rico — AAA or JAMS)
- No standard jury trial waiver in base agreement

### Litigation History
Five pending class actions relating to antitrust/price-fixing allegations through revenue management software (IDeaS, Amadeus) and industry data sharing (CoStar/STR). These cases are against Hilton Worldwide, not the franchisor entity specifically, but the outcomes could affect the entire system. Notable settlements include Texas ($2.1M) and Nebraska ($300K) for mandatory guest fee disclosure practices.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Key Financial Observations
1. **Pass-through entity**: The franchisor entity has minimal expenses ($11.4M on $1.5B revenue). It is essentially an IP licensing pass-through vehicle.
2. **Massive distributions**: Distributes virtually all earnings to Hilton parent ($1.5B in 2024). Retained earnings are declining despite positive net income.
3. **Pledged collateral**: All assets and franchise contracts are pledged as collateral for Hilton's corporate debt (maturing 2025–2033). This is a structural risk — in a Hilton bankruptcy scenario, franchisee contracts could be implicated.
4. **Disregarded entity**: Not a separate taxpayer. All items included in Hilton's consolidated tax returns.
5. **Graduate acquisition**: Acquired Graduate brand franchise contracts in May 2024 for ~$85M (recorded as franchise contract intangible assets, 15-year amortization).
6. **Intercompany relationships**: Large intercompany balances with Hilton affiliates ($703.9M in other receivables, $262M equity contra). Hilton's centralized cash management system controls these flows.
7. **No operator compensation**: Despite an operator agreement with a Hilton affiliate, no compensation has been paid in any of the 3 years presented.
8. **Revenue growth**: 7.7% YoY in 2024 (vs 12.0% in 2023), driven by royalty fee growth across all Hilton brands (not just Spark).
