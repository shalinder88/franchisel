## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Novus Franchising 2 LLC** is a Delaware LLC formed April 13, 2017, headquartered at 650 Pelham Boulevard, Suite 100, St. Paul, Minnesota 55114. The entity acquired the Novus Glass franchise system from predecessor Novus Franchising, Inc. (NFI) on May 31, 2017.

2. **Ownership chain:** Novus is wholly owned by Mondofix USA LLC, which is wholly owned by Mondofix Inc., a Canadian corporation based in Blainville, Quebec. Mondofix is a multi-brand automotive aftermarket platform operating Fix Auto, ProColor Collision, Speedy Auto Glass, and Novus Glass globally.

3. **System size:** 128 franchised outlets and 0 company-owned outlets as of December 31, 2024. The system experienced a severe contraction in 2023, dropping from 148 to 125 units (-23), driven by 18 non-renewals (14 in Minnesota alone) and 9 terminations against only 5 openings. The system showed early recovery in 2024 with 7 openings, 2 terminations, and 0 non-renewals, netting +3 units.

4. **Investment range:** $90,000–$284,590 for a leased retail location franchise; $69,500–$169,390 for a mobile franchise. Of these amounts, $50,700–$77,910 (retail) or $50,700–$65,910 (mobile) are payable to the franchisor or affiliates.

5. **Fee burden:** The ongoing fee load is substantial at 12%+ of Gross Revenues: 6% royalty (with mandatory minimum monthly payments of $350/$500), plus 2% marketing contribution, plus 4% minimum local advertising. Additional recurring costs include vehicle lease ($1,300–$1,600/month), POS software ($300–$350/month), NAGS license ($700–$950/year), and email fees.

6. **Item 19 highlights:** Gross sales only (no cost data). Mobile single-unit franchisees (n=33) reported average gross sales of $427,168 (median $276,856). Retail single-unit franchisees (n=21) reported average gross sales of $791,251 (median $702,787). Only 67 of 128 outlets were included in the representations.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** 18.2% of mobile franchisees (6 of 33) reported gross sales of $100,000 or less in 2024. One mobile franchisee reported $0 in gross sales. The lowest retail franchisee reported just $68,157.

8. **Financial health:** FY2024 revenue of $6,442,275, net income of $799,994, total assets of $3,239,114, and net membership interest of $1,219,398. Audited by Ernst & Young with an unqualified opinion. However, operating cash flow dropped sharply from $1,394,229 (2023) to $140,049 (2024), and distributions to the parent company totaled $500,000 in 2024 and $1,400,000 in 2023.

9. **Growth trajectory:** System is recovering from a significant 2023 decline. Franchisor projects up to 8 new outlets in 2025. The trajectory of 151→148→125→128 over 2022–2024 suggests stabilization but not meaningful growth.

10. **Territory:** Protected against other Novus glass businesses using the Marks within the designated territory, but subject to substantial carve-outs including existing franchisees/licensees, Speedy Auto Glass affiliate stores, TAG Network referrals, and broad reservation of rights for acquisitions and other channels.

11. **Renewal risk:** Franchisees must sign the then-current franchise agreement at renewal, which may contain "materially different" terms. The 210-day notice requirement and $4,000 re-franchise fee add complexity.

12. **Dispute resolution:** Mediation required at a location at least 200 miles from either party's offices, with litigation in Ramsey County, Minnesota. This is flagged as a special risk on the cover page. State addenda override these provisions for IL, IN, ND, RI, WA, and MI franchisees.

13. **Confidentiality clause:** Current and former franchisees have signed confidentiality clauses restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience.

14. **Special risks highlighted:** (1) Out-of-state dispute resolution, (2) Mandatory minimum royalty and advertising payments regardless of sales levels.

15. **Vehicle leasing risk:** Mandatory vehicle lease program creates subordination — if Mondofix USA's master lease with Doering Leasing is terminated, all franchisee subleases terminate automatically.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Novus Franchising 2 LLC operates as the franchisor for the Novus Glass system in the United States. It was formed as a Delaware LLC on April 13, 2017, specifically to acquire the Novus Glass franchise system from NFI/TCG International. The entity is a single-member LLC owned by Mondofix USA LLC, which itself is wholly owned by Mondofix Inc., a Canadian parent based in Blainville, Quebec.

The Mondofix family operates multiple franchise brands: Fix Auto (auto body repair, 654 locations worldwide), ProColor Collision (30 US locations, 157 Canadian), Collision Repair Experts (17 in Canada), Novus Glass (128 US, plus international), and Speedy Auto Service. Mondofix owns all Novus trademarks and patents and licenses them to Novus Franchising 2 LLC under a perpetual license.

Key affiliates that interact with franchisees:
- **Novus 2 LLC** — sells resins, leases windshield repair equipment to franchisees; distributes products under SRP brand
- **Speedy Novus Glass LLC** — operates company-owned glass businesses under "Speedy Auto Glass" and runs the TAG Network claims management service
- **Mondofix** — holds all IP, operates international franchising

The franchise offers two formats: (1) a retail location providing full glass repair and replacement services, and (2) a mobile-only franchise operating from equipped vehicles. Both formats serve automotive, commercial, and residential glass markets. Customers include individual vehicle owners, dealerships, rental agencies, fleet operators, body shops, commercial/residential building owners, and insurance companies.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
The initial franchise fee is $10,500, payable in full upon signing. Discounts apply for military personnel ($1,000 off), ProColor co-locations ($2,500 fee), NAPA 5-Year co-locations ($7,000 fee), and existing franchisees acquiring additional territories ($5,250 or waived for same-territory). Re-franchise fee at renewal is $4,000. Area Development fee is $10,500 per unit committed, credited against individual franchise fees.

The initial training fee is $14,000 (covers 2 persons), reduced to $10,000 with ProColor Amendment. Additional person training costs $2,000–$5,000 per program.

**Ongoing Fees (Every Fee from Item 6):**
- **Royalty:** 6% of Gross Revenues or Minimum Monthly Royalty (whichever greater). Minimum starts at $350 in month 4, increases to $500 in month 7. Conversion businesses and transfers start at $500 immediately.

- **Local Advertising:** Minimum 4% of Gross Revenues spent locally (paid to suppliers, not franchisor — unless franchisee underspends, then paid to franchisor).
- **Vehicle Lease:** $1,300–$1,600/month per vehicle (includes $100 admin fee + taxes). 60-month term.
- **Vehicle Shipping:** $800–$1,700 one-time (each direction).
- **Vehicle Maintenance:** Actual costs at franchisor's discretion.
- **Vehicle Default Fees:** Termination value (PV of all payments at 3.5% discount + estimated residual).
- **Vehicle Fines/Liens:** Actual cost + 15% administrative fee.
- **POS Software License:** $300–$350/month (5 user licenses). Additional users $25–$30/month each.
- **Shop Onboarding Fee:** $300–$350 per location for software updates.
- **NAGS License:** $700–$950/user/year.
- **Email:** $6.30–$9.18/month per account (minimum 1 required).
- **Meetings:** $1,000–$1,500 registration per person, 2 meetings/year (plus travel/food/lodging).
- **Additional Training:** $0–$5,000 (replacement employee training).
- **NOVUS Success Team:** Up to $1,000/week plus travel (business coaching).
- **EDI Transaction:** Up to $2/transaction for insurance electronic notifications.
- **Branding Non-Compliance:** Up to $500/month until brand standards met.
- **TAG Network:** $300 initial fee + $15 per repair referral, $25 per replacement referral.

- **Transfer Fee:** $7,500 ($5,000 for existing franchisee; $5,000 for NAPA 5-yr).

**MSO Royalty Credit Program:** Multi-store operators (2+ retail locations) may qualify for royalty fee credits based on quarterly sales goals. Franchisor reserves the right to modify or discontinue at any time.

### Initial Investment

**Retail Location Franchise: $90,000 – $284,590**

The wide range is driven primarily by: vehicle ($3,800 lease vs. $50,000 purchase), leasehold improvements ($2,500–$40,000), furniture/fixtures ($0–$25,000), and calibration package ($0–$11,990). All fees and payments are nonrefundable. The franchisor does not finance any of the initial investment except through the vehicle and equipment leasing programs.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

The franchisor maintains significant supplier control. Approximately 90% of initial purchases and 50% of ongoing annual expenses must comply with franchisor specifications. Five categories of products/services are sole-sourced:

1. **Resins** — from Novus only
2. **Windshield repair bridges and equipment** — from Novus 2 LLC only
3. **Urethane adhesives** — from SRP (Novus 2 affiliate) only
4. **Vehicle insurance** — from designated provider only
5. **POS software** — from designated vendor only

Franchisees may request approval for alternative suppliers on non-sole-source items. The minimum approval fee is $250, and the franchisor will typically respond within 30 days. There are no written criteria for supplier approval. The franchisor may revoke approval at any time.

The franchisor earned $1,263,967 from franchisee purchases in 2024 (19.6% of its total revenue). Affiliate Novus 2 earned an additional $212,714. A buying program provides rebates of 0%–11% to the franchisor, with partial credit passed back to franchisees quarterly. The franchisor may change or eliminate this program at any time. Note that prices charged to company-owned businesses may be lower than prices charged to franchisees based on volume and other factors.

### Training Program Detail

Initial training totals approximately 62–72 hours of classroom instruction and 62–68 hours of on-the-job training across 7 subjects. Training is provided monthly or as needed. Glass repair training is conducted at the St. Paul, MN headquarters. Glass replacement training occurs at Regional Training Centers in Concord, CA; Atmore, AL; Newton, IA; or Longmont, CO (or St. Paul). Online components cover business operations, sales skills, business management, and some marketing content.

Post-opening, a "fast start" field representative provides up to 2 weeks of in-field training for retail (1 week for mobile) during the first 2 months. Additional telephone support is available for 60 days after training completion.

Replacement employee training costs $5,000. If an employee fails to complete glass replacement training on time, an additional $500 fee applies.

### Technology Stack and Costs

The core technology requirement is the franchisor's POS software and accounting system, licensed through a designated vendor. First-year cost is $3,000 (setup + license). From year 2 onwards, the base license is $300–$350/month for 5 users, with add-ons available:
- Additional users: $25–$30/month each
- Technician Route Direct: $50–$60/month/user
- Mobile application: $20–$35/month/user
- Microsoft Office Standard: $20–$25/month/user
- QuickBooks integration: $250–$350 one-time setup per location
- NAGS license: $700–$950/year

Hardware requirements include a laptop and printer (provided in the $1,500 Vehicle Office Package) and a smartphone (brand not designated). The franchisor has independent access to information on the franchisee's computer system. An optional calibration package costs up to $11,990.

### Marketing Fund Spending Breakdown and Audit Status

The 2% Marketing Fund contributions were spent in 2024 as follows: Advertising Placements (44%), Trade Shows and Events (29%), Online Marketing Program Maintenance/Customer Service/Tracking (18%), General Administration (4%), Reputation Management (3%), Social Media Campaigns (2%).

**The Marketing Fund is NOT audited.** A report is available to franchisees on request within 120 days of year-end. The fund is administered by the franchisor, which controls all spending decisions. An advisory council of 5 franchisees (3 elected, 2 appointed by franchisor) serves in an advisory capacity only. The franchisor may change or dissolve the council at any time. The fund is not used for franchise sales advertising.

Franchisees are also required to spend a minimum of $500/month with the designated digital marketing supplier (currently Eulerity) after the first 6 months. This counts toward the 4% local advertising requirement. The franchisor pays for the first 6 months of this media spend.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition and Carve-Outs

Each franchise receives a designated territory consisting of one or more zip codes (or partial zip codes), typically covering approximately 120,000+ registered vehicles. The territory is determined using mapping software and vehicle data from a third-party source, with consideration for demographics, geography, competing businesses, and site availability.

The territory is protected against the franchisor operating or granting franchises for Novus glass repair/replacement businesses using the Marks. However, this protection is subject to substantial carve-outs:

1. **Prior rights:** Existing franchisees, licensees, and bolt-on license holders retain their rights, including renewal and transfer.
2. **Speedy Auto Glass:** Affiliate-operated Speedy stores may open and operate within the territory (but currently will not use Novus products if that would contravene exclusive territory).
3. **TAG Network:** The affiliate-owned claims referral network refers glass business to non-Novus providers, including within the franchisee's territory.
4. **Novus 2 products:** The affiliate may sell resins and equipment under non-Novus brand names to competitors within the territory.
5. **National/regional accounts:** If the franchisee fails to service these accounts, the franchisor may redirect them to others without compensation.
6. **Acquisitions:** The franchisor can purchase, merge with, or acquire any competing business and operate it within the territory.
7. **Other channels:** Internet, catalog, telemarketing, and other direct marketing are unrestricted.

The franchise agreement includes an explicit non-exclusive territory statement: "You will not receive an exclusive territory. You may face competition from other franchisees, from outlets that we own, or from other channels of distribution or competitive brands that we control."

The franchisor cannot unilaterally change the territory, but the franchisee may request changes (subject to approval and a $2,500 fee).

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights

Franchisees may sell anywhere within their territory but cannot conduct or solicit business outside without franchisor consent. The franchisor generally provides consent if the franchisee is in compliance and there are no significant complaints, provided the out-of-territory work does not infringe on another franchisee's area. Franchisees must refer customers for services they don't offer to the nearest Novus business before referring elsewhere.

The franchisor also reserves the right to establish multi-area marketing programs (internet, shows, directories, affinity programs) that may require franchisee participation, commission payments, and adherence to maximum pricing structures.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The initial franchise term is 10 years. Franchisees have an option to "re-franchise" (renew) under the then-current franchise agreement, which **may contain materially different terms and conditions**. Renewal requirements include: 210 days' advance notice, satisfaction of all material requirements under the current agreement, payment of all amounts due, modernization of the business, signing the new agreement, and paying the $4,000 re-franchise fee.

The ProColor Amendment and 5-Year NAPA Co-Location Addendum each reduce the term to 5 years. Area Development Agreements have a 10-year term and cannot be renewed.

In Minnesota, the franchisor must provide 180 days' notice of non-renewal. In Michigan, the franchisor cannot refuse to renew without fairly compensating the franchisee for inventory, supplies, equipment, and fixtures (if the term is less than 5 years and the franchisee is restricted from continuing in a similar business).

### Transfer Provisions

Transfer fee: $7,500 ($5,000 if transferee is existing Novus franchisee). The franchisor has a right of first refusal — the franchisee must offer the sale to the franchisor before any third-party sale. Conditions for approval include: payment of all debts to franchisor, transferee meets standards, transferee signs current franchise agreement, transferee completes training, transferee is not a competitor, and franchisee signs a general release.

Vehicle leases cannot be transferred at all. Equipment leases and software sublicenses require franchisor consent. The general release requirement is voided in Minnesota, North Dakota, and Washington (except through negotiated post-agreement settlement with independent counsel).

### Termination and Death/Disability

The franchisor cannot terminate without cause. Curable defaults include: failure to complete training (30 days), failure to open within 6 months (30 days), material breach (30 days), failure to pay (15 days), failure to meet Quarterly Minimum Gross Revenues (90 days). Non-curable defaults include: felony conviction, bankruptcy/insolvency, abandonment, material trademark impairment (24-hour notice), failure to meet Annual Minimum GR (Repair Only), withholding financial system access, and revoking EFT authorization.

Upon abandonment termination, liquidated damages of 60 months × average monthly Gross Revenues apply. This is a very aggressive provision — Minnesota and North Dakota explicitly prohibit liquidated damages and termination penalties.

On death or disability, heirs may assume franchise rights but must meet all transfer requirements.

### Noncompete Restrictions

**During term:** Franchisee may not participate in any competing business. Repair Only franchisees who can demonstrate they operated a glass replacement business before signing may continue that business (but no glass repair).

**Post-term:** For 2 years after expiration/termination, the franchisee may not participate in any business competitive with or similar to Novus within: (a) the franchisee's territory, (b) the territory of another franchisee, or (c) 10 miles of any Novus business. North Dakota considers these covenants generally unenforceable. Washington limits enforcement based on earnings thresholds ($100K for employees, $250K for independent contractors).

### Dispute Resolution

All disputes (except certain claims) must first be submitted to non-binding mediation. The mediator selects the location, which must be at least 200 miles from either party's offices. If mediation fails, litigation must be in Ramsey County, Minnesota, governed by Minnesota law.

State addenda override these provisions: Illinois requires Illinois law and Illinois venue. Indiana requires litigation in Indiana. North Dakota requires an agreeable location and ND law. Rhode Island voids venue restrictions outside RI. Washington allows arbitration/mediation/litigation in Washington. Michigan prohibits arbitration outside Michigan (except by post-agreement consent).

### Litigation History

No pending or concluded litigation involving Novus Franchising 2 LLC. Two concluded predecessor (NFI) matters from 2012–2013: both involved non-compete enforcement against former franchisees, both settled. No lawsuits were initiated against franchisees in 2024.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Leverage:** Total liabilities ($2.02M) exceed net membership interest ($1.22M) by $800K, a leverage ratio of 1.66:1. However, the largest liability component is deferred revenue ($1.37M combined), which is a pre-collected obligation to perform services rather than financial debt.

2. **Distributions:** The parent company extracted $500K in 2024 and $1.4M in 2023 — totaling $1.9M over two years against cumulative net income of approximately $2.0M. Nearly all profits are being distributed upstream.

3. **Related party transactions:** Advances to related parties total $802K (to common control entity and parent). New in 2024: $283K advanced to parent company and $100K AR from parent. The direction of intercompany cash flow reversed — in 2023, the parent advanced $546K to Novus; in 2024, Novus advanced funds to the parent.

4. **Asset composition:** $300K in "Rights" represents the exclusive area developer rights for the US Novus Glass network, carried at cost and not amortized (indefinite life). New in 2024: $226K in right-of-use assets from adopting lease accounting for office space.

5. **Operating cash flow decline:** Cash from operations dropped 90% from $1.39M to $140K. Primary drivers: $441K decrease in deferred revenue (recognizing previously collected initial fees), $179K decrease in AP, and $157K increase in AR. This suggests 2024 had fewer new franchise signings generating upfront fees compared to 2023's re-franchising wave.

6. **Revenue composition:** Royalties ($3.13M, 48.7%) remain the primary revenue stream.
