## Unresolveds

1. **HIGH — Zero operating Trend hotels**: MPHOA has never operated or franchised a Trend hotel. All estimates are theoretical. There is no real-world performance data, no operating franchisees to contact, and no FPR.

2. **HIGH — Brand viability unknown**: Franchise offered since June 2020 (nearly 5 years). Zero outlets opened, zero projected openings. This raises questions about demand and brand viability.

3. **MEDIUM — Marketing fund shared with My Place**: Fund is not audited. Allocation between Trend and My Place at franchisor's sole discretion. Current shortfall of $430,306 loaned by MPHOA.

4. **MEDIUM — My Place encroachment in Protected Area**: Franchisor may franchise its competing brand within Trend's PA with no conflict resolution mechanism.

5. **MEDIUM — Related party concentration**: ~26% of revenue from related parties. Significant expenses to related parties. All leases are with related entities on short-term cancelable terms.

6. **MEDIUM — NY addendum investment/equity gap**: Investment figure ($5.68M–$6.95M) exceeds stockholders' equity ($805K). Note: NY figures appear to reference the My Place new-build model, not Trend conversion.

7. **LOW — Item 3 cross-reference only**: "See state specific addenda" without body text in Item 3. Only the WA AOD is substantively disclosed.

## Contradictions

1. **Investment figures in NY addendum vs. Item 7**: NY addendum states initial investment of $5,677,950–$6,951,450, while Item 7 states $272,500–$2,947,000. The NY figures likely apply to My Place (new-build), not Trend (conversion). This is a potential disclosure inconsistency if the Trend FDD addendum uses My Place numbers.

2. **Franchise Agreement says "non-renewable" but Item 17(c) says "option to renew"**: Section 3(A) of the FA states "this Agreement is non-renewable and grants you no rights of license renewal." But Item 17(c) says "We will provide you an option to renew the franchise agreement for 5 years." The FA then provides an "option to extend" (Section 3(C)). The terminology is inconsistent — it's an extension, not a renewal, but Item 17 labels it as renewal.
