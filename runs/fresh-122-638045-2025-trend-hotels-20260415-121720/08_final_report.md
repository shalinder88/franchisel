## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

MPHOA is a South Dakota LLC with two classes of membership interest: Class A (voting, 9,600 units) and Class B (non-voting, 400 units). The operating agreement provides for up to $10,000,000 in Class B units. Ryan Rivett is CEO and the principal developer of both the My Place and Trend concepts. The company has six affiliates, all at the same Aberdeen, SD address, providing various services including technology (MP Technology LLC), construction (Legacy Builders), management (Legacy Management, TLC Management), and design (Legacy Design Group).

Trend Hotels is a conversion-only brand, meaning franchisees must already own or acquire an existing hotel property and convert it to the Trend brand and system standards. No new-build Trend hotels are contemplated in this FDD.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Initial Franchise Fee: $45,000 (lump sum, nonrefundable)
- Application Fee: $2,500 (nonrefundable, credited toward franchise fee)
- Training Fee: $10,000 (Resource Evaluation & Guidance Program)
- Computer Hardware/Software: $6,000–$22,000 (PMS, CRS, CRM setup)

**Ongoing Percentage Fees (all on Gross Room Revenue):**
- Royalty: Ramps from 0% (first 30 days) → 2.5% (days 31–90) → 4% (days 91–180) → 5% (thereafter). Once a rate tier is achieved, it never decreases. Acquisitions of existing Trend hotels pay 5% immediately.
- Marketing & Reservation Fund: 2.5%
- Revenue Intelligence Service: 1% (rate changeable on 120 days' notice)
- Guest Loyalty Program (Stay Rewarded): 1% (rate changeable on 120 days' notice)
- **Total steady-state: 9.5% of GRR**

**Ongoing Fixed Fees:**
- Technology Subscription: $1,204/month (~$14,447/year), variable by rooms/computers
- Website Fee: $1,000/year

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Transfer: 50% of then-current Initial Franchise Fee (no fee for 51%+ ownership transfers)
- Renewal/Extension: Then-current Application Fee + Initial Franchise Fee
- Retraining: $4,125 per occurrence
- Reinspection: $2,500 after failed QA
- Audit: Cost of audit + underpaid amounts + interest (triggers at 2%+ underpayment)
- Approved Supplier Review: Up to $1,000
- Change of Opening Date: $5,000 + actual costs
- Convention: Registration fee + travel/lodging/meals (mandatory attendance)
- Securities Offering: 50% of then-current franchise fee
- Late Fee: $200 per occurrence
- Dishonored Check: $60 (SD maximum)
- Interest: Highest rate allowed by law on delinquent amounts
- Liquidated Damages: $1,000/day for pre-approval operation; 36-month fee projection for premature termination

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory

MPHOA grants an exclusive Protected Area around the franchisee's hotel location. Size depends on population, market factors, highway proximity, and existing PA boundaries — larger in rural areas, smaller in urban areas. The franchisor's determination is final.

No sales quotas or performance requirements are needed to maintain territorial exclusivity.

### Key Carve-Outs and Encroachment Risk

**Critical**: MPHOA reserves the right to franchise **My Place Hotels** (its competing brand) within the Trend Protected Area. There is no mechanism for resolving conflicts between Trend and My Place outlets. Resolution is "entirely within franchisor's business judgment."

Additionally, MPHOA may use the Internet, catalog sales, telemarketing, and other direct marketing to sell within the PA using its trademarks, and will not pay compensation for these sales.

Franchisees may not relocate (except in natural disasters with written approval) and do not have automatic rights to additional franchises.

### Channel Restrictions

Franchisees may solicit and accept reservations from consumers outside the PA. Both parties may use Internet and direct marketing channels within and outside the PA.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The initial franchise term is 10 years from the date the business begins operating. The franchise agreement explicitly states it is "non-renewable." However, franchisees in Good Standing may exercise an **option to extend** for 5 additional years by providing 180 days' notice before the 10th anniversary. Extension conditions: compliance with then-current System Standards and PA adjustment at franchisor's sole discretion.

Item 17(c) states: "When renewing you will not be asked to sign a contract with materially different terms and conditions than your original contract." This is a **favorable provision** that mitigates the "materially different terms" risk common in franchise renewals.

Renewal/extension fee: Then-current Application Fee + then-current Initial Franchise Fee.

Franchisees also have an **early termination option** at year 5 (180 days' notice, no penalty if in Good Standing). The Washington addendum adds a franchisee termination right (Section 8(C)) for material franchisor defaults with 90-day cure period.

### Transfer Provisions

Franchisor approval required but will not be unreasonably withheld. Transfer fee: 50% of then-current Initial Franchise Fee (no fee if transferring to entity where franchisee retains 51%+ ownership). Conditions include: applications completed, new franchisee qualifies, debts paid, training arranged, release signed, refurbishment satisfied, new agreement signed.

The franchisor has an option to purchase: for cash sales, it can match the offer; for non-cash sales, it can buy at appraised value. 30 days to decide. No right of first refusal.

### Termination and Death/Disability

Franchisor cannot terminate without cause after hotel opens. Curable defaults get 30 days to cure. Non-curable defaults include: 3 material defaults in 24 months, loss of possession, unapproved transfers, criminal conviction, abandonment, trademark misuse, 5 consecutive days not operating, and 13 other triggers.

Post-termination: complete de-identification, payment of all amounts due. Liquidated damages: 36-month fee projection for premature termination. $1,000/day for use of marks after termination.

Death/disability: franchise may be assigned to estate/legal guardian upon conditions satisfactory to franchisor.

### Noncompete Restrictions

**During term**: No competing business within Protected Area without written authorization.
**Post-term**: 2-year noncompete within Protected Area (including after assignment).

Multiple state addenda limit enforceability:
- California: post-term noncompete may not be enforceable
- North Dakota: noncompetes contrary to NDCC 9-08-06 are generally unenforceable
- Washington: noncompete void unless employee earns $100K+/year (employee) or $250K+/year (contractor)
- Minnesota: franchisee cannot be required to consent to liquidated damages or injunctions

### Dispute Resolution

Mediation is required first (mutual obligation). If unsuccessful, franchisor may require arbitration in Aberdeen, SD. Non-arbitration litigation: South Dakota at franchisor's election. Governing law: South Dakota (unless state law requires otherwise).

**State overrides to dispute resolution:**
- Illinois: venue outside IL is void; arbitration may be outside IL
- Maryland: franchisee may sue in MD for MD franchise law claims
- Minnesota: litigation may not be required outside MN
- North Dakota: arbitration at mutually agreeable site; litigation in ND
- Rhode Island: venue outside RI is void for RI Franchise Investment Act claims
- Washington: arbitration/mediation in WA or mutually agreed location

### Litigation History

Item 3 references "see state specific addenda." The Washington addendum discloses: MPHOA entered an Assurance of Discontinuance with the WA Attorney General regarding a no-hire provision in franchise agreements. The AOD was approved by King County Superior Court on October 7, 2019. No fines or monetary penalties assessed. MPHOA agreed to remove the provision and notify franchisees.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Minimal leverage**: Total debt is only $13,858 (a 0% vehicle note). The $500K line of credit with U.S. Bank was not renewed when it matured in December 2023.

2. **Deferred revenue dominance**: Deferred revenue ($3.5M) is the largest liability, representing pre-collected franchise fees, development fees, marketing fees, and loyalty program fees being recognized over franchise terms. This is an asset-light franchisor model.

3. **Member distributions vs. equity**: MPHOA distributed $654,541 (2024), $706,937 (2023), and $506,120 (2022) to members — exceeding net income in some years. Class A members have **negative equity** (-$1,755,172 in 2024), suggesting cumulative distributions exceeded cumulative income allocations.

4. **Related party concentration**: Approximately $2,512,049 (26%) of 2024 revenue came from related party franchisees. $1,750,422 in expenses paid to related parties. All leases are with related entities on short-term cancelable terms (30 days' notice).

5. **Revenue trend**: Revenue grew 15.4% in 2024 (vs. -2.3% in 2023). Net income recovered to $998K from a trough of $190K in 2023. The 2023 dip was driven by higher salaries and the absence of conference fees (biennial event).

6. **Pass-through entity**: As an LLC, MPHOA pays no income taxes — taxable income is allocated to members.

## I. State Addenda Summary

State addenda exist for **9 states** (California, Hawaii, Illinois, Maryland, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Washington), plus a **Michigan Notice** on pages 6–7.

### Material Overrides by State

**California**: Post-term noncompete may not be enforceable. Liquidated damages clauses may be unenforceable (Civil Code §1671). Arbitration venue and governing law (SD) may not be enforceable. General release excludes Franchise Investment Law and Franchise Relations Act claims.

**Hawaii**: Standard filing disclosure language. 7-day pre-execution delivery requirement.

**Illinois**: Illinois law governs. Venue outside IL is void (but arbitration may be outside IL). Anti-waiver protection.

**Maryland**: Franchisee may sue in MD for MD franchise law claims (3-year statute). General release excludes MD Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law. Arbitration forum selection may not be enforceable.

**Michigan** (Notice, not addendum): Termination only for good cause (30-day cure). Non-renewal requires fair market value compensation. Venue outside MI void for arbitration. Transfer refusal only for good cause.

**Minnesota**: Franchise trademark indemnification required. Liquidated damages cannot be imposed by consent. Injunctions cannot be required by consent. Jury trial waiver prohibited. Litigation cannot be required outside MN. 90-day termination notice (60-day cure) and 180-day non-renewal notice. 3-year statute of limitations.

**New York**: Special risk: initial investment exceeds stockholders' equity (NY figures: $5,677,950–$6,951,450 vs. equity of $805,182 — these appear to be My Place new-build numbers). Franchisee may terminate on any grounds available by law. No assignment without willing and able assignee. General Business Law Article 33 rights preserved.

**North Dakota**: Noncompetes contrary to NDCC 9-08-06 generally unenforceable. Arbitration at mutually agreeable site. Litigation in ND. ND law governs.

**Rhode Island**: Venue outside RI void for RI Franchise Investment Act claims. Anti-waiver provision.

**Virginia**: Termination only for reasonable cause per Article 13.1-564.

**Washington**: Most comprehensive addendum (18 provisions). Conflict of laws: WA Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails. Arbitration/mediation in WA or mutually agreed location. General release void except in negotiated settlements with independent counsel. Transfer fees limited to reasonable costs. Franchisee may terminate for any grounds under state law. Indemnification limited (no obligation for franchisor's negligence/misconduct). Noncompete void unless employee earns $100K+/year. Non-solicitation restrictions void. Treble damages available. Franchisee termination right added (Section 8(C): 90-day cure for franchisor material defaults). AOD with WA AG re no-hire provisions.

## J. Unresolveds

1. **HIGH — Zero operating Trend hotels**: MPHOA has never operated or franchised a Trend hotel. All cost and operational estimates are theoretical, derived from industry averages and My Place experience. A prospective buyer has no real-world data to evaluate.

2. **HIGH — Brand viability unknown**: Trend has been offered since June 2020 — nearly 5 years with zero outlets opened, zero agreements signed, and zero projected openings. This raises fundamental questions about market demand for the Trend concept.

3. **MEDIUM — Marketing fund shared with My Place and not audited**: The fund serves both brands with allocation at franchisor's sole discretion. A cumulative shortfall of $430,306 has been loaned by MPHOA. Franchisees receive no proportional benefit guarantee. The fund is not independently audited.

4. **MEDIUM — Encroachment from My Place within Protected Area**: MPHOA may franchise My Place Hotels within the Trend PA with no conflict resolution mechanism. This creates a direct competitive threat from the franchisor's own brand.

5. **MEDIUM — Related party concentration**: ~26% of MPHOA's 2024 revenue ($2,512,049) came from related party franchisees. Substantial expenses flow to related entities. All leases are short-term cancelable with related parties. This creates governance and independence concerns.

6. **MEDIUM — NY addendum investment figures**: The NY addendum states an investment range of $5,677,950–$6,951,450 that significantly exceeds the Item 7 range of $272,500–$2,947,000 and the entity's total equity of $805,182. These appear to be My Place new-build numbers erroneously included in the Trend FDD.

7. **LOW — Item 3 cross-reference only**: Item 3 states "see state specific addenda" without body text. Only the Washington AOD is substantively disclosed. The scope of additional litigation history, if any, is unclear.

## K. Contradictions

1. **NY addendum investment vs. Item 7**: The NY addendum states initial investment of $5,677,950–$6,951,450. Item 7 states $272,500–$2,947,000 for a Trend conversion hotel. The NY figures are most likely the My Place Hotels new-build investment range that was not updated for the Trend FDD. **Unresolved** — flagged as potential disclosure error.

2. **FA "non-renewable" vs. Item 17 "option to renew"**: Section 3(A) of the Franchise Agreement states "this Agreement is non-renewable and this Agreement grants you no rights of license renewal." However, Item 17(c) describes an "option to renew the franchise agreement for 5 years." Section 3(C) of the FA provides an "option to extend" for 5 years. **Resolved** — terminologically inconsistent but functionally equivalent. The extension provides substantially similar terms.
