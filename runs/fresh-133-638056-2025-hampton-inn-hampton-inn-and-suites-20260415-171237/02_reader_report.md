# Reader Report — 638056-2025 Hampton Inn / Hampton Inn & Suites

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** 2,369 US hotels (1,345 Hampton Inn + 1,024 Hampton Inn & Suites) as of Dec 31, 2024, all franchised, zero company-owned. Pipeline of 305 signed-not-opened plus 52 projected new openings.
2. **Entity structure:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC (Delaware LLC, formed Sep 2007) → Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. → Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). Franchisor is a pure franchise-fee-collection entity.
3. **Ownership history:** Hampton Inn brand franchised since 1983; Hampton Inn & Suites since 1993. Predecessors include Promus Hotel Systems Inc. (1995–2007) and Hampton Inns Franchise LLC (2007–2015). Current franchisor since March 30, 2015.
4. **Investment range:** Hampton Inn (89 rooms) $15.2M–$22.2M; Hampton Inn & Suites (113 rooms) $17.7M–$26.0M — both excluding real property.
5. **Fee burden:** 6% royalty + 4% program fee (may increase to 5%) = 10%+ of Gross Rooms Revenue, plus Hilton Honors 4.9% of eligible folio, plus technology fees, distribution fees, quality assurance fees, brand conference fees, and numerous event-driven fees.
6. **Item 19 highlights:** Average room rate $145.82 (HI) / $152.19 (H&S); average occupancy 71.7%; RevPAR Index avg 121.0 (above fair share). No cost build-up or EBITDA disclosed.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** Room rates range as low as $77.60 (HI) / $86.35 (H&S); occupancy as low as 30.5%. Only ~33% of franchisee-managed hotels exceed average room rate. Wide performance dispersion.
8. **Financial health:** Franchisor entity had $1.50B revenues, $1.49B net income (99%+ margin) in 2024. Distributed $1.5B to Hilton affiliates. Clean audit opinion (CBH). Asset-light entity with $703M intercompany receivables.
9. **Growth trajectory:** Hampton Inn essentially flat (-1 net 2024); Hampton Inn & Suites growing (+9 net 2024). Combined pipeline of 305 properties suggests continued moderate growth. Mature system.
10. **Territory:** Standard franchise agreement provides NO exclusive territory. Franchisor retains unrestricted right to compete under other brands. Restricted Area may be negotiated but is discretionary and time-limited.
11. **Renewal:** Franchise is NON-RENEWABLE. Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms and mandatory PIP.
12. **Disputes:** Venue is E.D. Virginia (Alexandria) or Fairfax County, VA; fallback New York. Governing law: New York. Puerto Rico franchisees: arbitration. Multiple pending antitrust class actions re: pricing algorithms (IDeaS, Amadeus, STR).
13. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution (VA/NY); no post-term non-compete; broad franchisor termination rights; liquidated damages = 60× average monthly royalty.

## Pass B: Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — Entity and Structure
Hilton Franchise Holding LLC is a Delaware LLC formed September 2007. It became the US franchisor for Hampton brands on March 30, 2015. The parent chain runs through Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. (formed July 2016) up to Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT, formed March 2010). CEO is Christopher J. Nassetta; CFO is Kevin J. Jacobs.

Key affiliates providing services to franchisees:
- Hilton Reservations Worldwide LLC (reservation services, Addison TX)
- Hilton Supply Management LLC ("HSM") — procurement
- Hilton Honors Worldwide LLC — loyalty program
- Hilton Systems Solutions LLC ("HSS") — IT systems

Hampton Inn brand first franchised 1983; Hampton Inn & Suites first franchised 1993. Predecessors: Promus Hotel Systems Inc. (1995–2007), Hampton Inns Franchise LLC (2007–2015). The system is upper midscale, limited food & beverage, targeting business travelers, families, and vacationers.

### Item 10 — Financing
Generally no direct financing. Development Incentive program available at franchisor's discretion:

Franchise Application Fee may occasionally be paid in installments.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
**Critical: NO exclusive territory in standard franchise agreement.** Franchisee receives a non-exclusive license to operate at a specified location.

Restricted Area Provision may be negotiated but:
- At franchisor's sole discretion
- Usually shorter than the term ("Restrictive Period")
- Only applies to Hampton Brand, NOT to other Hilton brands
- Does not prevent franchisor/affiliates from operating other brands (Hilton, Hilton Garden Inn, Home2, Tru, etc.) within the Restricted Area
- Franchisor may acquire/merge with competitors who operate in the area

No relocation permitted. No options, rights of first refusal, or similar rights for additional franchises.

**Strategic Partnerships** (distribution agreements that may impact channels): Small Luxury Hotels, Autocamp, Evermore Resort, Field Station.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
All marks owned by Hilton International Holding LLC (HIH), licensed royalty-free to franchisor. Key marks: Hampton Inn (reg. 1984), Hampton Inn & Suites (reg. 1995), Hampton (reg. 2001). "Hampton by Hilton" application pending (Sep 2023). No known material infringements or pending challenges. All required filings current.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
Building plans copyrighted (HI: 1984, expire 2059; H&S: 1994, expire 2069). No patents. Manual and all Proprietary Information must be kept confidential; non-disclosure/non-competition clauses required for employees with access.

### Item 15 — Management Participation
- Must provide qualified management (self-managed with approval or approved Management Company)
- Management Company must attend required training
- If Management Company becomes unsuitable, must replace within 90 days
- NO post-term non-compete for franchisee, manager, or Management Company
- Cannot be a Competitor (brand licensor/franchisor of competing brand) during term
- Guaranty requirements based on financial review

### Item 16 — Restrictions on Sales
- No restrictions on customers
- Must operate 24/7 unless special circumstances
- Must comply with Standards for products/services/amenities
- Prior written approval for room count changes or significant structural changes
- Franchisor may require specific products, services, amenities (restaurants, pools, meeting space, pet-friendly programs, EV charging, etc.)

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction:** 22 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10–20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership:** remaining term or as approved
- **HITS Agreement:** concurrent with Franchise Agreement

**CRITICAL: NO RENEWAL RIGHT.** Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion. Franchisee may be required to sign a new agreement with materially different terms and conditions and comply with any PIP requirements.

### Termination by Franchisee
Not authorized before term expiration. Unilateral termination = material breach + liquidated damages (or actual damages in certain circumstances).

### Termination by Franchisor
**Curable defaults (with notice/cure period):**
- Failure to pay (10+ days cure)
- Failure to commence/complete Hotel Work (10+ days)
- Failure to maintain insurance (10+ days)
- Failure to comply with FA, Manual, or Standards (30+ days)

**Non-curable (immediate termination):**
- Pattern of non-compliance (same breach twice in 24 months)
- 3 notices of material default in 12 months
- Bankruptcy/insolvency
- Loss of possession of hotel
- Failure to operate for 5+ consecutive days
- Contesting marks ownership
- Conduct likely to adversely affect reputation
- Fraud/concealed revenues
- Unauthorized transfer
- Becoming a Sanctioned Person or Competitor
- Public health/safety threat

**Interim Remedies:** Suspension from Reservation Service, removal from directories, disabling software, revoking financial accommodations.

### Transfer
**Permitted Transfers** (no change of control): Some without notice/consent (public equity, <50% private equity). Others with 60-day notice + consent + $5,500 fee (affiliates, family, death, >50% cumulative private equity change).

**Change of Ownership Transfers:** 60-day notice, not in default, transferee must meet all current requirements, sign current-form FA, pay $200,000 COO fee, agree to PIP.

### Death/Disability
Transfer to immediate family within 1 year, subject to qualification requirements.

### Non-Competition
- During term: cannot be licensor/franchisor of competing brand
- Post-term: NONE

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue:** U.S. District Court E.D. Virginia (Alexandria) or Fairfax County, VA state court. If no jurisdiction: New York. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Puerto Rico:** Arbitration (AAA/JAMS, Federal Rules of Civil Procedure)
- **Governing law:** New York (without choice-of-law principles)
