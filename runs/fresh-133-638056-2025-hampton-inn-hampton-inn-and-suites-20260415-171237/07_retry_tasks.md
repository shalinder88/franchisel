### States with Disclosure Document Addenda (Exhibit J-1):
1. **California** — Liquidated damages may be unenforceable (Civil Code §1671). Termination/non-renewal governed by Bus. & Prof. Code §§20000-20043. NY governing law may not be enforceable. VA venue may not be enforceable. General release cannot waive FIL rights.
2. **Hawaii** — General release cannot relieve franchisor from liability under Hawaii franchise law. 7-day disclosure delivery required.
3. **Illinois** — Termination and non-renewal rights under 815 ILCS 705/19-20. Governing law/venue/jury waiver provisions cannot waive Illinois Franchise Disclosure Act rights.
4. **Maryland** — State law may supersede FA on termination/renewal. Franchisee may sue in Maryland for claims under MD law. 3-year statute of limitations. General release cannot waive MD franchise law rights.
5. **Michigan** — Detailed prohibitions on unfair provisions (same as front matter pp.5-6): no restrictions on franchisee association, no waiver of rights, termination only for good cause (30-day cure), compensation on non-renewal (if <5yr term), renewal on generally available terms, no out-of-state litigation, transfer only denied for good cause.
6. **Minnesota** — Trademark indemnification required. 90-day termination notice (60-day cure), 180-day non-renewal notice. No liquidated damages. No out-of-state litigation. No jury trial waiver. General release cannot relieve of MN FIL liability.
7. **New York** — NY Franchises Law governs NY-related claims. GBL Article 33 §§680-695 may supersede FA. General release on renewal/transfer may not be enforceable. Injunctive relief requires proper proofs.
8. **North Dakota** — ALL liquidated damages deleted. ND law supersedes NY law. Out-of-state venue deleted. Jury trial waiver deleted. General release cannot relieve ND Franchise Disclosure Act liability.
9. **Rhode Island** — Bankruptcy termination may not be enforceable. Release on transfer/renewal does not waive RI FIA claims. Out-of-state venue/foreign law void for RI FIA claims.
10. **Virginia** — Cancellation without reasonable cause is unlawful under VA Retail Franchising Act.
11. **Washington** — WA FIPA supersedes FA on termination/renewal. Release/waiver limited. WA law prevails in conflicts. Transfer fees limited to reasonable costs. Arbitration/mediation in WA or mutually agreed location. Non-compete restrictions limited by income thresholds ($100K employee/$250K independent contractor). No-poach provisions void.
