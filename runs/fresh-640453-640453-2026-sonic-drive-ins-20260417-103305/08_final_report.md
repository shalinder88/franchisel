## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and trajectory:** The Sonic Drive-In system comprised 3,412 restaurants at the end of fiscal year 2025 — 3,120 franchised and 292 company-owned. The system has been contracting: a net loss of 134 restaurants over three fiscal years (3,546 at end of 2022 to 3,412 at end of 2025). Franchised outlets declined from 3,220 to 3,120; company-owned outlets declined from 326 to 292.

2. **Entity structure:** Sonic Franchising LLC is a special-purpose Delaware LLC formed in 2011 as part of a securitization restructuring. It serves as the legal franchisor but all day-to-day franchise operations are performed by Sonic Industries Services LLC (SIS) under a management agreement. America's Drive-In Brand Properties LLC (ADIBP) owns all Sonic trademarks and intellectual property, licensed to Sonic Franchising LLC for 99 years.

3. **Ownership chain:** Sonic Franchising LLC → Sonic Capital LLC → Sonic LLC → Sonic Holding Company → IRB Holding Corp → Inspire Brands, Inc. → Roark Capital Management, LLC (private equity). Inspire Brands is a global multi-brand restaurant company also owning Arby's, Buffalo Wild Wings, Dunkin', Baskin-Robbins, Jimmy John's, and affiliated with Subway, Dave's Hot Chicken, GoTo Foods (7 brands), CKE (Carl's Jr./Hardee's), Driven Brands (9+ automotive brands), ServiceMaster, Nothing Bundt Cakes, Mathnasium, Primrose Schools, Massage Envy, and others.

4. **Investment range:** Traditional Drive-In: $1,485,200–$2,522,900 (leased); C-Store: $670,200–$1,412,900; Urban Inline: $679,200–$1,250,900. Building costs ($600K–$800K for Traditional) and site work ($450K–$700K) constitute the largest components.

5. **Fee burden:** Standard royalty of 5% of Gross Sales, plus 0.90% SBF contribution, plus 3.25%–5% cooperative/SMF, totaling 9.15%–10.90% of Gross Sales. Additional Inspire Payment Services fee of $365–$565/month. Multiple incentive programs can reduce royalty to 1% in early years.

6. **Item 19 performance:** Average AUV of $1,552,145 across 3,057 franchised restaurants (median $1,465,090). Only 44% exceeded the average. Bottom quartile averaged $919,629 with the lowest at $225,308. No cost, expense, or profit data is disclosed.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** The Q4 range ($225,308–$1,123,408) is extremely wide. A restaurant operating at the Q4 median ($958,893) with a total fee burden of approximately 10% would pay approximately $96K in fees alone, before any operating costs. Without disclosed cost data, profitability assessment at this volume is impossible from the FDD.

8. **Financial health:** Sonic Franchising LLC reported net income of $2.8M (2025), declining from $8.1M (2023) — a 65% decline in two years. Revenue fell from $58.7M to $51.9M. Distributions to the parent have exceeded net income in all three years, reducing member's equity from $31.8M to $25.2M. SIS consolidated reports net income of $168.9M on $279.8M revenue, with $1.40B in securitization debt.

9. **Growth trajectory:** System is contracting at approximately 1.4% per year. Projected openings (16 franchise, 0 company) are insufficient to offset closures. Company is accelerating company-store dispositions (21 closed, 4 sold in 2025 vs. 3 closed in 2023).

10. **Territory:** Non-exclusive. Protected Area varies by population density (0.75–3 mile radius) with significant carve-outs for prior protected areas, Non-Drive-In, and Non-Traditional locations. Franchisor reserves broad competitive rights including operating other brands and internet sales in the Protected Area.

11. **Renewal risk:** Renewal requires signing the then-current Franchise Agreement, which "may contain materially different terms, including different fees, different Protected Area, and/or franchisee's rights in a protected area." This is a significant kill switch — a franchisee who has invested $1.5M–$2.5M and operated for 10 years faces potential fee increases, territory shrinkage, or other unfavorable terms at renewal.

12. **Disputes:** Litigation in Atlanta, Georgia courts. Georgia law governs. No arbitration. Post-term noncompete: 18 months within 3 miles of any Sonic restaurant.

13. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution (Atlanta, GA). Gag clause confirmed — some franchisees signed confidentiality agreements restricting ability to speak openly.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Sonic Franchising LLC is a Delaware limited liability company formed March 23, 2011, with its principal business address at Three Glenlake Parkway NE, Atlanta, Georgia 30328. The franchisor is a special-purpose entity created as part of a 2011 securitization transaction. Under this structure, Sonic Capital LLC (the parent) and certain subsidiaries (the "Sonic Co-Issuers") issued $1.4 billion in securitization notes secured by franchise fees, royalty payments, and lease payments. Sonic Franchising LLC guarantees these obligations and has pledged substantially all its assets.

Day-to-day franchise operations — including support, training, and enforcement — are performed by SIS under a management agreement. If SIS fails to perform, Sonic Franchising LLC may replace it, but Sonic Franchising LLC remains accountable for all franchise obligations regardless of who performs the services.

The brand began in the early 1950s with franchising since 1974. The Sonic system offers three formats: Traditional Drive-In (free-standing building with canopies for 6–12 drive-in stalls, drive-thru, and patio), Non-Drive-In Location (C-Stores, gas stations, travel plazas), and Non-Traditional Location (airports, military bases, stadiums, etc.). Multi-Brand Locations combining Sonic with other Inspire brands (Arby's, BWW, Dunkin', Baskin-Robbins, Jimmy John's) are also offered.

### Fee Stack

**Ongoing Fees:**
The standard royalty is 5% of Gross Sales, payable monthly. The Sonic Brand Fund (SBF) contribution is 0.90% of Gross Sales. The advertising cooperative and System Marketing Fund (SMF) contribution ranges from 3.25% to 5% of Gross Sales depending on market classification (Core: 2.75%–4.5% SMF + 0.5% cooperative; Developing: 4.5% SMF + 0.5% cooperative; New: 5% SMF, no cooperative). The total ongoing fee burden at standard rates is 9.15%–10.90% of Gross Sales.

In addition, franchisees pay Inspire Payment Services fees of $365–$565 per month for cybersecurity and payment processing services. Late charges accrue at 1.75% per month on overdue amounts. If an audit reveals an understatement of Gross Sales by 3% or more, the franchisee must reimburse audit costs plus interest and a 10% administrative fee on unpaid amounts.

**Incentive Programs:**
The Standard Incentive program (available for Traditional Restaurant FAs signed by March 31, 2027) reduces the royalty to 1% in Year 1, escalating to 5% by Year 5, with a 50% reduction in SMF contributions through Year 5. If the restaurant's AUV is below $1.4 million after Year 3, an additional extension reduces the royalty to 2.5% through Year 8. The Relocation Incentive offers 1%→2%→3%→5% over the first four years. The Early Opening Incentive provides 0% royalty from actual opening until the required opening date (up to 6 months). The VetFran Program credits $10,000 per drive-in (up to $100,000), and the Pioneer Incentive provides $50,000 per drive-in for the first 2 locations in DC, Maine, New Hampshire, or Vermont.

**Event-Driven Fees:**
The renewal fee is $15,000. Transfer fees are $1,000 for non-control/family/wholly-owned entity transfers and $3,000 for other transfers. Additional training fees are $250 per person for the GM Leadership Class and $1,000 per failed inspection occurrence. The management fee (if the franchisor manages the restaurant post-termination) is 3% of Gross Sales.

### Initial Investment

The estimated initial investment for a Traditional free-standing Drive-In (leased) ranges from $1,485,200 to $2,522,900. The largest components are building costs ($600,000–$800,000), site work ($450,000–$700,000), restaurant equipment ($175,000–$310,000), and POS/POPS/technology ($105,000–$160,000). The Traditional Drive-In occupies a 34,000–43,560 square-foot lot with a 1,250–1,500 square-foot building, 6–12 drive-in stalls, a drive-thru lane, and patio seating.

The C-Store format (leased) ranges from $670,200 to $1,412,900, reflecting lower building and site costs. The Urban Inline format (leased) ranges from $679,200 to $1,250,900. Neither we nor our affiliates offer financing for any part of the initial investment.

The Development Agreement requires a $10,000 fee per restaurant (credited toward franchise fees), with no separate initial investment beyond the per-restaurant fees.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Franchisees must purchase food, beverages, signs, paper products, uniforms, POS systems, POPS digital marketing platforms, back-office systems, mobile ordering technology, payment technologies, audio systems, equipment, and other products only from approved or designated suppliers meeting Sonic's specifications. The franchisor retains the exclusive right to approve all suppliers and distributors. There is no formal procedure for alternative supplier approval, though typically the franchisor responds within 30 days.

Collectively, required purchases and leases represent approximately 16%–25% of establishment costs and 34%–50% of ongoing operating costs. During fiscal 2025, suppliers contributed over $76.5 million to the chain based on franchisee and affiliate purchases: $41.6M to the Brand Technology Fund (now dissolved), $17.6M to the SBF, $14.1M to the SMF, and $3.2M for the annual convention.

### Training Program
The Sonic Training Program is a 5-week intensive program requiring the Principal and two other team members. Week 1 consists of virtual fundamentals (10–15 hours of eLearning prerequisites plus 24 hours of classroom training). Weeks 2–4 are in-restaurant fundamentals and practice at a Certified Training Restaurant (40–45 hours OJT each week plus additional eLearning). Week 5 covers in-restaurant manager training. Total training: 44–59 hours classroom plus 120–135 hours on-the-job, approximately 164–194 hours total. The first 3 trainees' program fee is included in the franchise fee; additional trainees cost $5,000 each.

The General Manager Leadership Class is a 24-hour classroom course required 60 days before opening (or within 6 months for GMs hired after opening), at $250 per attendee. All Management Personnel must also complete 8-hour ServSafe certification through a third party.

### Technology Stack and Costs
The restaurant must have a Windows-based PC with 50 Mbps internet, a POS system, the POPS digital marketing platform, a back-office system, mobile ordering and payment technology, and various approved software systems. Technology costs range from approximately $72,000 for a Non-Drive-In Location without stalls to approximately $182,000 for a Traditional Drive-In with 30 stalls. Annual POS maintenance is $4,000–$8,000; field repairs and optional services add $1,000–$5,000. The franchisor has unlimited, independent access to all POS data.

### Marketing Fund Spending
The SBF (0.90% contribution) spent its 2025 funds as follows: 65.91% advertising/marketing, 9.01% product cost equalization/volatility management, 8.66% Sonic Games, 8.09% administrative expenses, 3.90% customer relations, 3.61% point-of-purchase support, 0.82% other. The SBF pays SIS up to 15% of receipts for administrative costs.

The SMF spent its 2025 funds: 83.32% advertising/marketing, 11.14% agency retainer/travel, 4.44% customer research, 1.10% other. Both funds are audited annually by independent CPAs.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
The Protected Area is defined at the Franchise Agreement's effective date based on population density: 0.75-mile radius in areas with >125,000 people per square mile; 1.5-mile radius in MSAs with 75,000+ population; 2-mile radius in MSAs with 25,000–75,000; and 3-mile radius in MSAs with 25,000 or fewer (or outside MSAs). The Protected Area may be reduced based on census data updates.

### Critical Carve-Outs
The territory is NOT exclusive. The Protected Area excludes: (1) previously protected radius of existing Sonic Restaurants, (2) previously protected Development Areas, (3) Non-Drive-In Locations (C-Stores, gas stations), and (4) Non-Traditional Locations (airports, stadiums, etc.) — even those physically inside the Protected Area.

### Franchisor Reserved Rights
The franchisor specifically reserves: the right to operate any brand in the Protected Area under different marks; the right to sell similar products through internet/catalog/telemarketing inside the Protected Area; the right to sell dissimilar products under Sonic marks inside the Protected Area; the right to place Sonic Restaurants anywhere outside the Protected Area regardless of proximity; and the right to be acquired by a competitor. The franchisee cannot use internet, catalog, telemarketing, or other direct marketing channels.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
The initial term is 10 years for Traditional and Non-Drive-In locations (lesser of 10 years or facility contract for Non-Traditional). Two additional 10-year renewal terms are available. Renewal requires 6–12 months advance written notice, payment of a $15,000 renewal fee, signing the then-current form of Franchise Agreement (which may contain materially different terms, including different fees, different Protected Area, and different franchisee rights), restaurant remodeling, execution of a general release, and participation in retraining programs.

The "materially different terms" renewal risk is significant. A franchisee who has invested over $1.5 million and operated successfully for 10 years has no guarantee of maintaining the same royalty rate, territory protection, or contractual rights at renewal.

### Transfer Provisions
All transfers require the franchisor's approval. Conditions include: good standing, all monetary obligations satisfied, transferee qualifies under current standards, transferee completes training, transfer fee paid ($1,000 for non-control/family/wholly-owned entity transfers; $3,000 for other transfers), restaurant upgraded to current standards, and general release signed by the transferor. The franchisor has a right of first refusal on the same terms offered by a third party. Upon termination or expiration, the franchisor has an option to purchase the restaurant's assets within 30 days.

### Termination
The franchisee has no right to terminate. The franchisor may terminate immediately for non-curable defaults (insolvency, unauthorized transfer, terrorism, felony conviction, trademark misuse, health/safety endangerment, false books, 3+ defaults in 12 months, termination of any other Sonic agreement) or with 10–30 days' notice for curable defaults.

### Death and Disability
The franchise must be assigned to an approved buyer or heir within the greater of 120 days or completion of probate proceedings.

### Noncompete
During the franchise term: no involvement in any competing business. Post-term: 18 months, within 3 miles of the former Sonic restaurant or any other Sonic restaurant. Also cannot serve as landlord for a competing business within the restricted area.

### Dispute Resolution
No arbitration — disputes resolved through litigation in courts in the county where the franchisor maintains its headquarters (currently Atlanta, Georgia). Georgia law governs all claims, subject to state-specific addenda overrides.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Overview
The franchisor provides Annual Unit Volume (AUV) data for 3,057 2025 Franchised Traditional Restaurants. AUV equals Gross Sales as defined in the franchise agreement. The population excludes 29 restaurants opened during 2025 that did not operate the full year, 23 closed for extended periods (remodeling, casualty, relocation), 1 Multi-Brand Location, 10 Non-Traditional Locations, 3 restaurants transferred from company ownership in December 2025, 56 restaurants that closed during 2025, and all company-owned restaurants.

### Quartile Distribution
The overall average AUV was $1,552,145 with a median of $1,465,090. The distribution shows significant spread:

- **1st Quartile (764 restaurants):** Average $2,332,093 (median $2,228,716), ranging from $1,886,236 to $4,800,647. Only 38% exceeded the quartile average.
- **2nd Quartile (764 restaurants):** Average $1,659,107 (median $1,653,634), ranging from $1,465,475 to $1,886,035. 47% exceeded the quartile average.
- **3rd Quartile (764 restaurants):** Average $1,298,578 (median $1,301,671), ranging from $1,123,556 to $1,465,090. 51% exceeded the quartile average.
- **4th Quartile (765 restaurants):** Average $919,629 (median $958,893), ranging from $225,308 to $1,123,408. 59% exceeded the quartile average.

The bottom of the range ($225,308) represents a restaurant generating less than $1,000 per day in gross sales — likely a deeply distressed or partially operating location.

### Venue Type Breakdown
Free-standing and urban storefront locations (2,988 restaurants) averaged $1,562,839 (median $1,472,566). Gas/C-Store locations (69 restaurants) averaged $1,089,017 (median $1,049,767) — approximately 30% lower than free-standing.

### Critical Caveats
The FPR includes only AUV/Gross Sales data. No cost of goods, labor costs, rent, or other operating expenses are disclosed. No EBITDA or net profit data is provided. The data is sourced from franchisee-submitted reports and POS systems and has not been independently audited. The average restaurant age is 28 years — the established base may perform differently from a new restaurant. 68% of the included restaurants have drive-thru windows.

## H. Item 21 — Financial Statements

### Sonic Franchising LLC (Franchisor Entity)

Sonic Franchising LLC is a special-purpose entity whose primary function is to serve as the legal franchisor and guarantor of the Sonic securitization notes. Its financial statements are minimal in scope.

**Balance Sheet (December 28, 2025):** Total assets of $34.1 million, consisting primarily of intangible assets (franchise agreements) at $28.1 million net and accounts receivable of $6.0 million. Total liabilities of $8.9 million (entirely deferred franchise fee revenue). Member's equity of $25.2 million.

**Income Statement:** Revenue of $51.9 million in 2025 (franchise fees and royalties), down from $59.0 million in 2024 and $58.7 million in 2023. The decline reflects lower royalty collections due to system contraction and potentially lower per-restaurant Gross Sales. Royalty fees paid to affiliates (ADIBP trademark license) consumed $45.5 million — 87.6% of revenue. After $787K in G&A and $2.8M in amortization, operating income was $2.8 million. Net income was $2.8 million, down from $6.0 million (2024) and $8.1 million (2023) — a 65% two-year decline.

**Equity Erosion:** Distributions to the parent totaled $4.0 million in 2025, $8.1 million in 2024, and $11.3 million in 2023, exceeding net income in each year. Member's equity declined from $31.8 million (January 2023) to $25.2 million (December 2025), a 20.7% reduction. This pattern of distributions exceeding income is a concern for the entity's long-term capitalization, though the entity's guarantor role means its equity position is ultimately backstopped by the Sonic system's cash flows.

**Securitization Guarantee:** Sonic Franchising LLC guarantees $1.4 billion in Sonic Securitization Notes through maturity dates in 2050–2051. This is the single largest obligation — if franchise fee and royalty cash flows decline sufficiently, the guarantee could be triggered.

**Key Notes:**
- Intangible assets: franchise agreements with $48.1M gross carrying value, 17-year useful life, $2.8M annual amortization.
- Deferred franchise fees: $8.9M total ($417K current, $8.5M noncurrent), declining from $10.5M (2023) as new franchise activity has not kept pace with recognition.
- No income tax — single-member LLC.

SIS is the operating entity that provides all franchise services, owns real estate, and manages the Sonic system.

**Balance Sheet (December 28, 2025):** Total assets of $2.32 billion, dominated by goodwill ($822.6M), intangible assets ($1.25B including $1.1B trademark/trade name), and property ($139.3M). ## I. State Addenda Summary

State-specific addenda exist for 6 states, with state-specific riders to the Franchise Agreement for 7 states:

**California:** Adds requirement to comply with the Fast Food Act (AB 1228) regarding minimum wages, working hours, and working conditions. **Maryland:** (1) General release at renewal/transfer does not apply to claims under Maryland Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law. (4) 3-year statute of limitations for Maryland franchise claims.

**Minnesota:** Standard registration notice per Minnesota franchise laws.

**North Dakota:** Addendum with state-specific modifications (detailed provisions not fully walked in A1).

**Washington:** Reference to Assurance of Discontinuance (AOD) settled with Washington Attorney General regarding no-poaching provisions.

**State-Specific FA Riders:** Riders for Illinois, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, North Dakota, Washington, and Wisconsin modify Franchise Agreement terms to comply with state-specific franchise laws. These riders override FA provisions that conflict with state law and typically address venue, choice of law, release limitations, termination cure periods, and noncompete enforceability.
