# Reader Report — Hilton Garden Inn FDD (638057-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed Sep 2007. Wholly owned subsidiary of Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., itself a subsidiary of Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). HQ: McLean, VA.
2. **Brand positioning:** Upscale hotel segment; "approachable full-service" experience targeting business transient travelers. Typical 132-room, 2–6 story hotel with restaurant, exercise room, self-service laundry, parking.
3. **System size (Dec 31, 2024):** 750 franchised hotels, 0 company-owned. Net growth: +3 in 2024, +4 in 2023, +11 in 2022. Slow but positive growth; asset-light model (100% franchised).
4. **Investment range:** $21,933,588–$32,384,982 excluding real property (132-room prototype).
5. **Fee burden:** Royalty 5.5% + Program Fee 4% = 9.5% of Gross Rooms Revenue as base ongoing fees. Hilton Honors 3.6% of eligible folio. Technology fees $2,000–$4,000/month. Numerous additional quality, distribution, and compliance fees.
6. **Franchise term:** 22 years (new construction); 10–20 years (conversion). Non-renewable; re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms.
7. **Item 19 highlights:** FPR provided. Average room rate $162.75 (2024); average occupancy 72.1% (2024). RevPAR Index average 114.9 (2024). Only 31.9% of franchisee-managed hotels exceeded the average room rate.
8. **Financial health (Item 21):** Revenue $1.502B (2024); net income $1.488B (2024); near-total profit pass-through to parent. Total assets $1.27B. Clean audit opinion (Cherry Bekaert). No going concern. Assets pledged as collateral for Hilton parent debt.
9. **Territory:** No exclusive territory. Franchisor may compete within any area under any brand.
10. **Dispute resolution:** Virginia venue (ED VA federal or Fairfax County state); fallback New York. New York governing law. No arbitration except Puerto Rico.
11. **No post-term noncompete.** No in-term noncompete for being a franchisee of competing brands; only prohibition is on being a franchisor/licensor of a competing brand.
12. **Gag clause:** Some current and former franchisees have signed provisions restricting their ability to speak about their experience.
13. **Litigation:** Multiple pending antitrust class actions (IDeaS/STR/Amadeus room pricing); one breach-of-contract suit by franchisee. Several concluded matters (Texas/Nebraska resort fee cases settled).
14. **Growth trajectory:** 90 franchise agreements signed but not opened; 16 projected new franchised hotels in next fiscal year. Pipeline modestly healthy.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **No exclusive territory.** Non-exclusive license at a specified location.
- Standard FA has NO protected area provisions.
- Franchisor and affiliates may own, operate, franchise, license competitive facilities anywhere, including within any Restricted Area (if one exists), under other brands.
- May license hotels under any Hilton brand next to your hotel.
- "There is no mechanism for resolving any conflicts."
- Some FAs may include a "Restricted Area" protecting against same-brand (HGI only) competition, but this is negotiated, not standard.
- No relocation permitted.
- No options, ROFR, or rights to additional franchises.

- Must operate Hotel directly (with approval and training) or through approved Management Company.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Term:** 22 years (new construction), 10–20 years (conversion), remaining term or negotiated (COO).
- **Renewal/extension:** NONE. "You do not have the right to renew or extend the Franchise Agreement."
- **Re-licensing:** At franchisor's sole discretion. Must sign new agreement with potentially materially different terms. Must comply with PIP.
- **"Materially different" risk:** PRESENT. Re-licensing requires signing "then-current" agreement which "may be" materially different.

### Termination
- **By franchisee:** Not authorized. If you terminate, it's a material breach; must pay liquidated damages.
- **By franchisor without cause:** Condemnation or casualty (with release from liquidated damages under certain conditions).
- **Curable defaults (at least 10–30 days cure):**
  - Failure to pay (10 days)
  - Failure to commence/complete hotel work (10 days)
  - Failure to maintain insurance (10 days)
  - Failure to comply with any FA provision, Manual, Standard (30 days)
- **Non-curable defaults (immediate termination):**
  - Repeated same non-compliance within 24 months after cure
  - 3 default notices in 12 months
  - Bankruptcy/insolvency
  - Loss of possession of hotel
  - Failure to operate 5+ consecutive days (non-force majeure)
  - Contesting System/Marks ownership
  - Conduct adversely reflecting on reputation
  - Concealing revenues/false records
  - Unauthorized transfer
  - Becoming a Sanctioned Person
  - Gaming license issues
  - Guarantor breach
  - Public health/safety threat
  - Becoming a Competitor
  - Any Hotel Agreement terminated for franchisee breach

### Transfer
- **Permitted Transfers** (no change of control): publicly traded equity interests; <50% private equity transfer; institutional fund transfers; transfers to affiliates, family, trusts, on death (with notice + consent + $5,500 fee).
- **Change of Ownership Transfers:** 60 days notice; pay all amounts due; transferee must meet current standards, submit COO Application, pay $200,000 fee, sign then-current FA, comply with PIP.
- **ROFR:** None. But must notify franchisor of intent to market equity interests.
- **Death/disability:** Transfer to family member allowed within 1 year if family member meets requirements.

### Noncompete
- **During term:** You/affiliates may NOT own or be licensor/franchisor of competing brand. But you MAY be franchisee of or manage competing brand hotels. You MAY own minority interest in Competitor under certain conditions.
- **Post-term:** NONE. No post-term noncompete restriction.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue:** US District Court, ED Virginia (Alexandria), or state court in Fairfax County, VA. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Governing law:** New York (without choice-of-law principles).
- **Arbitration:** None, except Puerto Rico (AAA/JAMS arbitration).
- **Jury trial waiver:** Not explicitly stated in standard FA (but may be in state addenda overrides).
