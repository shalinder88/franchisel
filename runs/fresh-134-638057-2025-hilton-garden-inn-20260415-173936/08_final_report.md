**Brand:** Hilton Garden Inn
**Franchisor:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC
**Filing ID:** 638057-2025 (Wisconsin DFI)
**Issuance Date:** March 30, 2025
**Extraction Date:** April 15, 2026

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** 750 franchised hotels, 0 company-owned, as of December 31, 2024. 100% asset-light franchise model. Net growth of +3 in 2024 (6 openings, 3 terminations, 2 non-renewals). Pipeline: 90 signed agreements not yet open; 16 projected openings next fiscal year.

2. **Entity structure:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC is a Delaware LLC wholly owned by Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., itself a subsidiary of Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). The franchisor is essentially a royalty pass-through vehicle with 99.2% operating margins — all economic value flows up to the publicly traded parent.

3. **Ownership history:** Brand launched in 1990 by Hilton Inns, Inc. Franchisor entity formed September 2007 as part of Hilton's restructuring. Current franchisor has offered HGI franchises since March 30, 2015. The Hilton family of brands spans 20+ brands from budget (Spark) to luxury (Waldorf Astoria). Blackstone retains influence through Jonathan Gray as Chairman.

4. **Investment range:** $21,933,588 to $32,384,982 for a typical 132-room new development, excluding real property. This is a capital-intensive investment requiring $15.5M–$22.5M in construction alone, plus $2.5M–$3.6M in FF&E.

5. **Fee burden:** Base ongoing fees total 9.5% of Gross Rooms Revenue (5.5% royalty + 4% program fee). Adding Hilton Honors (3.6% of eligible folio), technology fees ($2,100–$4,400/month), distribution fees, and quality assurance fees, the effective all-in fee-to-revenue ratio is estimated at 14–16% of gross rooms revenue. This is among the highest in the upscale segment.

6. **Item 19 highlights:** Average room rate $162.75 (2024); average occupancy 72.1%. RevPAR Index 114.9 (significantly above competitive set). However, only 31.9% of franchisee-managed hotels exceeded the average room rate — a right-skewed distribution. Bottom quartile risk is real: room rates ranged from $73.24 to $312.46 and occupancy from 38.6% to 97.5%.

7. **Financial health:** Franchisor revenue $1.502 billion (2024); net income $1.488 billion. Clean audit opinion from Cherry Bekaert. No going concern. However, all franchisor assets are pledged as collateral for Hilton parent debt (maturing 2025–2033), creating systemic risk if parent faces distress.

8. **Growth trajectory:** Slowing — net additions declined from +11 (2022) to +4 (2023) to +3 (2024). Transfer activity also declining (45 → 36 → 21), suggesting reduced secondary market liquidity. Pipeline of 90 signed agreements and 16 projected openings suggests moderate forward momentum.

9. **Territory:** No exclusive territory in the standard franchise agreement. Franchisor reserves the right to place competing brands (including other Hilton brands) directly adjacent to your hotel. "There is no mechanism for resolving any conflicts."

10. **Renewal:** Non-renewable franchise. Re-licensing possible at franchisor's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms. This is a significant risk — after investing $22–$32M and building a business for 22 years, there is no guaranteed right to continue operating.

11. **Dispute resolution:** Virginia venue, New York governing law. Out-of-state litigation is a highlighted special risk.

12. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution. Gag clause present (some franchisees restricted from speaking about their experience). Multiple pending antitrust class actions regarding revenue management software pricing.

13. **No post-term noncompete.** After termination or expiration, franchisee may operate a competing hotel at the same location (after de-identification).

14. **Litigation:** 5 pending actions including 4 antitrust class actions (IDeaS, Amadeus, STR/CoStar) alleging hotel industry price coordination through revenue management software. One breach-of-contract suit by a terminated HGI franchisee. Concluded matters include Texas ($2.1M) and Nebraska ($300K) resort fee settlements.

The entity was formed in September 2007 as part of Hilton's franchise restructuring. ## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
- Must comply with Brand Standards (Manual) for all products and services
- Must use proprietary OnQ system software from HSS (sole source)
- Must purchase exterior signage from licensed vendor
- Must participate in Reservation Service
- FF&E may generally be sourced freely if Standards met, but franchisor may restrict in future
- HSM is optional procurement agency (except for Spark and LivSmart brands)
- Alternative supplier approval: 60–90 days typical review

### HSM Revenues (2024, all Hilton brands):
- Rebates/allowances: $19.6M
- Direct sales: $8.8M
- Administrative fees: $38.7M
- Total: ~$67.1M

Hilton affiliate revenues from franchisee purchases (2024, all brands): $1.101 billion (excluding royalty/program fees).

Required purchases represent approximately 15–20% of establishment cost and 2–5% of operating expenses.

### Training Program
12 training programs required, ranging from online self-paced (no fee) to biennial brand conferences ($2,750/attendee). GM and Director of Sales must attend brand conference. All new GMs must complete training within 90 days. Sales staff must complete Core Sales Skills (24 hours virtual, $600). Owner Orientation required for first-time franchisees (16 hours virtual, no fee). New to Hospitality education for non-hotel industry franchisees (20 hours, $2,500).

### Technology Stack
The mandatory technology stack is extensive and represents a significant ongoing cost:
- OnQ (property management/reservations): $29K–$117K initial; $1.1K–$2.9K/month maintenance; $400–$600/month connectivity; refresh every 3 years
- Guest Internet Access: $63K–$93K initial; $1.7K–$5.9K/month ongoing
- Connected Room: $31K–$37K initial; $567–$864/month
- GRO (IDeaS revenue optimization): $3,400 initial; no ongoing fee
- Delphi (Amadeus sales/events): $900–$33K initial; $858/user/year
- Digital Floor Plan: $1,500
- Digital Key: included in OnQ

Total estimated technology cost: $130K–$285K initial; $3,900–$10,200/month ongoing.

### Marketing Fund
The 4% Monthly Program Fee funds: reservation systems, directories/internet sites, advertising/marketing, quality assurance, technology programs, and administrative overhead. The franchisor may allocate these monies to any regions or localities. The fee does not cover optional marketing programs or cost of operating to Standards.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
The standard Franchise Agreement provides NO exclusive territory. The franchise is a non-exclusive license at a specified location.

Some FAs may include a "Restricted Area" that protects against same-brand (HGI only) competition. However, this is negotiated individually and is not a standard provision. Even with a Restricted Area, the franchisor and affiliates retain the right to:
- Operate, franchise, or license any other Hilton brand within the Restricted Area
- Modify the System and use System elements in other businesses
- Acquire or merge with competing chains and operate them anywhere, including within the Restricted Area (provided HGI brand protection is observed)

The FDD explicitly states: "There is no mechanism for resolving any conflicts that may arise between your Hotel and other hotels described in this paragraph."

### Channel Restrictions
- May not register, own, or maintain any websites relating to the Hotel or Marks
- Must obtain advance written approval for any third-party website listing
- No relocation permitted
- No options, ROFR, or rights to additional franchises

### Encroachment Risk
This is a significant risk area. With 750 hotels and 90 in the pipeline, plus 20+ other Hilton brands that can be placed anywhere, the risk of same-area competition from the Hilton family is substantial. The FDD explicitly acknowledges this and disclaim any liability for it.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction:** 22 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10–20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership:** Remaining term or negotiated
- **Renewal:** NONE. The franchise agreement is non-renewable. Re-licensing is at the franchisor's sole discretion and requires signing the then-current form of agreement, which may contain materially different terms.

This is a critical risk: a franchisee investing $22–$32M has no guaranteed right to continue operating after the term expires. Re-licensing is entirely discretionary.

### Transfer Provisions
- **Permitted Transfers** (no change of control): Public equity, <50% private equity, institutional funds, affiliates, family, trusts, death — with varying notice/consent requirements and $5,500 fee.
- **Change of Ownership Transfers:** 60 days notice, $200,000 fee, transferee must meet current standards, sign then-current FA, comply with PIP. No ROFR.
- **Death/disability:** Transfer to family within 1 year if requirements met.

### Termination and Death/Disability
- Franchisee cannot terminate without paying liquidated damages (60 months of average royalty fees)
- Franchisor can terminate for condemnation/casualty (with LD release), and for curable and non-curable defaults
- 16 non-curable default triggers including repeated non-compliance, bankruptcy, 5-day operational shutdown, adverse conduct, false records, unauthorized transfer, becoming Competitor or Sanctioned Person
- Interim remedies include suspension from Reservation Service, directory removal, software disabling

### Noncompete
- **During term:** Cannot be franchisor/licensor of competing brand. CAN be franchisee of or manage competing brand hotels. CAN own minority interest in Competitor under conditions.
- **Post-term:** No noncompete restriction. This is favorable for franchisees.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue:** US District Court, ED Virginia (Alexandria) or state court in Fairfax County, VA. Franchisor may also sue where hotel is located.
- **Governing law:** New York (without choice-of-law principles)
- **Arbitration:** None (except Puerto Rico — AAA/JAMS)
- **Jury trial waiver:** Not explicitly required in standard FA; some state addenda address this

### Litigation History
- 5 pending matters, including 4 antitrust class actions
- The IDeaS/STR/Amadeus class actions allege that hotel companies (including Hilton) shared competitive data through revenue management software to coordinate room pricing — a systemic industry-wide allegation
- One franchisee breach-of-contract suit (AAAA Property Partners, HGI Orlando)
- Concluded matters include resort fee disclosure settlements ($2.1M Texas, $300K Nebraska)

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Asset-light pass-through:** This entity is essentially a royalty collection vehicle. With $1.5B in revenue and only $11M in expenses, virtually all cash flow is distributed upstream to Hilton parent. Operating expenses ($1.4M in 2024) represent less than 0.1% of revenue.

2. **Distributions exceed net income:** The entity distributed $1.5B to the parent in 2024, exceeding net income of $1.488B. Retained earnings declined from $541M to $529M. This pattern has persisted for 3 years ($1.4B in 2023, $1.55B in 2022).

3. **Leverage and guarantees:** The entity guarantees certain Hilton parent debt obligations maturing 2025–2033. All assets and franchise contracts are pledged as collateral. While the entity itself has no direct debt, the parent guarantee creates systemic risk.

4. **Credit losses increasing:** Allowance for credit losses on receivables increased from $7.3M (2023) to $10.4M (2024), a 42% increase. Provision for credit losses was $6.2M in 2024 vs $4.4M in 2023. This may signal increasing franchisee financial stress.

5. **Graduate brand acquisition:** In May 2024, the entity acquired franchise contracts for the Graduate brand as an asset acquisition, recording $85M in franchise contract intangible assets with a 15-year useful life.

6. **Related party dominance:** The balance sheet is dominated by intercompany receivables ($704M current + $262M equity component). Cash is negligible ($317K). The entity's liquidity is entirely dependent on the Hilton centralized cash management system.

7. **Deferred revenues growing:** Total deferred revenues ($559M) represent pre-paid franchise fees being recognized over contract terms. This is a positive indicator of franchise pipeline health.
