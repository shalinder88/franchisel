# Reader Report: DoubleTree by Hilton FDD (638052-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand:** DoubleTree by Hilton / DoubleTree Suites by Hilton. Full-service hotel brand within the Hilton family.
2. **Franchisor entity:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 2007. Wholly-owned subsidiary of Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., which is indirectly held by Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT).
3. **System size:** 359 franchised hotels (0 company-owned) as of December 31, 2024. Net change: -2 in 2024 (from 361). 304 classified as Comparable Hotels.
4. **Investment range:** $30.6M-$105.6M (DoubleTree 250-room) / $32.1M-$122.6M (DoubleTree Suites 250-room), excluding real property.
5. **Fee burden:** 5% royalty on Gross Rooms Revenue + 4% Monthly Program Fee (can increase by up to 1% over term) + Hilton Honors 4.3% of eligible folio + numerous technology, distribution, and compliance fees.
6. **Item 19 highlights:** Average room rate $159.30 (2024), median $149.23. Average occupancy 69.0%. RevPAR Index average 104.6. Only 32.4% of franchisee-managed hotels met or exceeded average room rate.
7. **Territory:** Non-exclusive. No protected territory. Franchisor reserves all rights to compete.
8. **Term:** 23 years (new construction), 10-20 years (conversion). No renewal right — re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms.
9. **Financial health (franchisor):** Revenue $1.502B (2024), net income $1.488B. Total assets $1.27B. Distributed $1.5B to parent. Clean audit opinion.
10. **Special risk:** Out-of-state dispute resolution (Virginia / NY fallback).
11. **Litigation:** Multiple pending antitrust class actions (IDeaS/STR data sharing). One breach-of-contract claim from former franchisee. Concluded mandatory guest fee settlements with Texas ($2.1M) and Nebraska ($300K).
12. **Projected openings:** 31 signed but not opened; 21 projected new franchised hotels in next fiscal year.
13. **No post-term noncompete.** During-term restriction: cannot own or be licensor/franchisor of competing brand.
14. **Liquidated damages:** significant — up to 60x average monthly royalty fees.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
**Non-exclusive license** at a specified location. No protected territory. Franchisor may own/operate/manage/franchise/license any other business anywhere, including competitive brands, at any time, even in the franchisee's area. No mechanism to resolve conflicts.

Franchisor may modify the System, use System elements in other businesses, and license the System to others. Franchisee has no claims for damages from any such activities.

**Restricted Area:** Some franchise agreements may include a Restricted Area (details in the specific Addendum), but the standard FA provides no territorial protection.

No relocation permitted. No options or rights of first refusal for additional franchises.

All internet presence controlled by franchisor. Franchisee cannot register domains with Marks.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
Principal Mark: "DoubleTree" registered on USPTO Principal Register. Marks owned by HIH (Hilton's IP holding affiliate), licensed to franchisor. Key registrations: DoubleTree, DoubleTree by Hilton, DoubleTree Suites by Hilton, plus logo marks. All affidavits and renewals filed. No material pending litigation on marks. No agreements limiting use of marks.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights
No patents or registered copyrights material to the franchise business. Proprietary Information includes the Manual and all operational methods/techniques. Franchisee must treat as confidential. All guest data belongs to franchisor.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
Hotel must be operated by franchisee or approved Management Company. Franchisee cannot be a "Competitor" (owner/licensor/franchisor of a competing brand). Management Company cannot be exclusive manager for a Competitor.

Guaranty requirements: determined after reviewing financial information. Each required guarantor (may include spouse) signs Guaranty.

No post-term noncompete for franchisee or Management Company.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on What Franchisee May Sell
No customer restrictions. Must comply with Standards for services, amenities, products. Must participate in Hilton Honors and all required frequency programs. Must honor discount/promotional programs. No gaming/casino without written permission. No timeshare/condo sales without permission. No shared facilities with other lodging without permission.

Cannot charge Mandatory Guest Fee without consent per Brand Standards.

## Pass E: Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction:** 23 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10-20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership:** remaining term or other approved term
- **No renewal right.** Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion. May require materially different terms and conditions. Must comply with new PIP. This is a significant contractual risk.

### Termination
- **By franchisee:** Not authorized before expiration. Unilateral termination is material breach → liquidated damages.
- **By franchisor without cause:** Condemnation or casualty (if franchisee elects not to rebuild). Liquidated damages waived if franchisee doesn't operate competitor hotel at site within 3 years.
- **By franchisor with cause (curable):** Payment default (10-day cure), failure to commence/complete work (10-day cure), insurance default (10-day cure), other FA breach (30-day cure).
- **By franchisor with cause (non-curable):** 16 grounds including: repeated non-compliance within 24 months, 3 notices in 12 months, bankruptcy/insolvency, loss of possession, 5+ consecutive days non-operation, contesting marks, reputational harm, false records, unauthorized transfer, becoming Competitor, etc.

**Interim Remedies:** Upon default, franchisor may suspend from Reservation Service, remove from directories, divert reservations, disable software, charge intervention fees, revoke financial accommodations.

### Transfer
- **Permitted Transfers** (no change of control): publicly traded equity, minority private equity, institutional fund transfers — some without notice, some with 60-day notice + consent + $5,500 fee.
- **Change of Ownership:** 60-day notice, franchisee not in default, all amounts current, transferee must meet qualifications, submit COO Application, pay $175K fee, sign current FA form, comply with PIP, provide guarantors.
- **Right of first refusal:** None, but must notify franchisor of marketing equity interests.
- **Death/disability:** transfer to immediate family member or entity, within 1 year, must meet qualifications.

### Noncompete
- **During term:** Cannot own or be licensor/franchisor of competing brand. May own minority interest or be franchisee of competitor.
- **Post-term:** None.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Governing law:** New York
- **Forum:** U.S. District Court, E.D. Virginia (Alexandria), or state court in Fairfax County, VA, or New York, NY. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Puerto Rico:** Arbitration (AAA/JAMS), venue Fairfax County VA, seat New York.
- **No general arbitration clause** (except Puerto Rico).
- **Jury waiver:** Not explicitly stated in the summary tables (would need to check FA exhibit).
- **State addenda** may override venue and governing law provisions.

### Integration/Merger
Only signed agreements are enforceable. Subject to state law.
