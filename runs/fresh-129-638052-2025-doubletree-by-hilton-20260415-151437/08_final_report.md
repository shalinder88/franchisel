# Full Diligence Report: DoubleTree by Hilton
## Franchise Disclosure Document — Filing 638052-2025

**Issuance Date:** March 30, 2025
**Franchisor:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC
**State Filed:** Wisconsin DFI
**Extraction Date:** April 15, 2026

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand and system:** DoubleTree by Hilton and DoubleTree Suites by Hilton are full-service hotel brands within the Hilton family. As of December 31, 2024, the system consisted of 359 franchised hotels in the United States with zero company-owned outlets. The system experienced a net decline of 2 units in 2024, the first net contraction in the reported three-year period, following growth of +9 (2022) and +7 (2023).

2. **Entity structure and ownership:** The franchisor is Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 2007. It is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. (Delaware, formed July 2016), which is indirectly held by Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT, Delaware, formed March 2010). The franchisor entity is essentially a licensing pass-through with ~99% operating margin.

3. **Ownership history:** Predecessors include Doubletree Franchise LLC (Oct 2007 – Mar 2015) and Doubletree Hotel Systems Inc. (Feb 1989 – Oct 2007, originally an Arizona corporation). The DoubleTree brand traces to DHS's franchising operations beginning in 1989. HHC became the ultimate parent in December 1999. The current entity structure resulted from a 2017 spin-off of Park Hotels & Resorts Inc.

4. **Investment range:** $30,550,859 to $105,621,829 for a newly constructed 250-room DoubleTree hotel (excluding real property). For a 250-room DoubleTree Suites hotel, $32,050,859 to $122,625,079. These are among the highest franchise investment ranges in the industry, reflecting the full-service hotel segment.

5. **Fee burden:** The core ongoing fee load is substantial: 5% royalty on Gross Rooms Revenue, 4% Monthly Program Fee (can increase by up to 1% over the term), 4.3% Hilton Honors loyalty fee on eligible guest folio, plus extensive technology fees ($3,800-$9,000+/month), distribution program charges, quality assurance fees, and numerous event-driven fees. The total fee burden on room revenue alone (royalty + program + Honors) approaches 13-14% before technology, distribution, and compliance fees.

6. **Item 19 highlights:** Average room rate was $159.30 (2024), up modestly from $157.99 (2023). Average occupancy was 69.0%. RevPAR Index averaged 104.6 (above fair share). However, only 32.4% of franchisee-managed hotels met or exceeded the average room rate — a significant skew indicating a long tail of lower-performing properties. No cost build-up or EBITDA data is disclosed.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** The wide range in room rates ($96.32-$294.93) and occupancy (39.4%-99.1%) indicates significant performance dispersion. A prospective franchisee at the low end faces substantially different economics than the average suggests.

8. **Financial health:** The franchisor entity generated $1.502 billion in revenue and $1.488 billion in net income in 2024, with virtually all earnings distributed to the parent. The entity has minimal cash ($317K) and all assets are pledged as collateral for Hilton parent debt. While the franchisor is financially sound through its parent, it operates as a pass-through entity entirely dependent on Hilton.

9. **Growth trajectory:** The system added 21 units in 2024 but lost 23, yielding a net -2. With 31 signed but not opened and 21 projected new openings, the pipeline suggests stabilization. Transfer activity declined from 21 (2022) to 11 (2024).

10. **Territory and renewal:** No protected territory. No renewal right — re-licensing is at the franchisor's sole discretion and may involve materially different terms. These are among the most significant risk factors for prospective franchisees.

11. **Disputes and litigation:** Multiple pending antitrust class actions involving allegations that hotel chains used revenue management software (IDeaS) and industry data services (STR/Amadeus) to coordinate room pricing. Additionally, concluded settlements with Texas ($2.1M) and Nebraska ($300K) over mandatory guest fee disclosure practices. One pending breach-of-contract claim from a former franchisee.

12. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution (Virginia or New York). Jury trial waiver in FA Section 17.1, with state-specific overrides in Minnesota, North Dakota, Illinois, and Wisconsin.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Hilton Franchise Holding LLC operates as the franchisor for all Hilton family brands in the US. The entity licenses intellectual property from Hilton International Holdings LLC (HIH), an affiliate, on a royalty-free basis. The CEO of Hilton Worldwide, Christopher J. Nassetta, has served since December 2007. The newly appointed General Counsel, Caroline Krass, joined in March 2025 from the U.S. Department of Defense.

The franchisor also licenses eforea spa and Restaurant Brand concepts as optional addenda to the hotel franchise agreement. Restaurant Brands include Spencer's, Poppy's, Haley & Bear, The Comeback, Che Vita, and Magnolia Smokehouse & Kitchen. As of the filing date, zero Restaurant Brand licenses were in operation, and only 2 eforea spas were franchised.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Franchise Application Fee (New/Conversion): $85,000 + $400 per room over 250
- Franchise Application Fee (Change of Ownership): $175,000
- OnQ Computer System: $103,500-$273,500
- Opening Process Services: $20,000
- Training: $5,000-$20,000
- Various other startup fees totaling approximately $130K-$340K to the franchisor before opening

**Ongoing Fees (percentage-based):**
- Monthly Royalty: 5% of Gross Rooms Revenue
- Monthly Program Fee: 4% of GRR (can increase up to +1% over term)
- Hilton Honors: 4.3% of total eligible guest folio (mandatory)
- Hilton Advance Program: 1.35% of Digital Direct Revenue (cap $30/stay)

**Ongoing Fees (fixed/per-transaction):**
- OnQ Connectivity: $400-$600/month
- Hardware/Software Maintenance: $2,422-$6,815/month
- Connected Room Maintenance: $980-$1,395/month
- Delphi Sales System: $858/user/year
- Brand Conference: $2,500/attendee (mandatory)
- Guest Assistance handling fees: $200-$300 per transaction + cost of guest rebate
- Centralized Payment Programs: various per-stay/transaction charges
- Quality assurance fees: $65-$145/room for non-compliance, up to $50K/6 months cap

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Transfer/COO: $175,000 application fee
- Re-licensing: $85,000
- PIP Fee: $10,000
- Liquidated Damages: up to 60x average monthly royalty fees

In 2024, the franchisor agreed to reduce the Monthly Royalty Fee in 21 instances, indicating some negotiation flexibility for multi-unit or experienced operators.

### Initial Investment

**DoubleTree Hotel (250 Rooms):** $30,550,859 to $105,621,829
The largest components are construction/leasehold improvements ($17.6M-$72.2M), FF&E ($7M-$10.5M), and contingencies ($1.76M-$7.22M, recommended at 10% of construction). Technology systems total approximately $286K-$563K. Additional funds cover 3 months of operating expenses at $1M-$1.4M.

**DoubleTree Suites Hotel (250 Rooms):** $32,050,859 to $122,625,079
Higher construction costs ($17.6M-$84.6M) and FF&E ($8.5M-$12.75M) reflect the suites configuration.

Both tables exclude real property, market studies, insurance, and interest. The actual total cost to develop a DoubleTree hotel including land will typically be significantly higher than these ranges.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions and Approved Supplier Process

Franchisees must build, design, furnish, equip, and supply their hotels in accordance with the Standards compiled in the Manual. Standards are regularly updated. Franchisees must purchase OnQ computer system software from Hilton/HSS. Items bearing the Hilton logo/trademark must come from approved suppliers. No officer of the franchisor owns a material interest in any approved supplier.

Hilton Supply Management (HSM), a wholly-owned subsidiary of Hilton, negotiates with manufacturers/suppliers for distribution of hotel supplies. Use is optional but HSM charges 4-10% procurement/services fees. For fiscal year 2024, Hilton and affiliates had revenues of $1,101,161,898 from sales to franchisees across all brands (excluding royalty/program fees and pass-through commissions).

The Fixed Renovation Cycle Management (FRCM) program mandates periodic furniture/fixtures/finishes upgrades per a renovation schedule.

### Training Program Detail

13 training programs are specified. Key mandatory programs include:
- Brand Conference: varies by year, $2,500/attendee, required for GM and Director of Sales
- GM/Commercial/Sales Leader Training: up to 18 hours virtual, within 90 days of start
- Hilton Core Sales Skills: 24 hours virtual, for all sales personnel
- Owner Orientation: 16 hours virtual
- New to Hospitality Owner Education: 20 hours virtual
- Annual Compliance Training: 2-3 hours

### Technology Stack and Costs

The technology burden is substantial:
- **OnQ Property Management System:** Proprietary, mandatory. Initial: $103.5K-$273.5K. Refresh every 5 years.
- **GRO (Global Revenue Optimization):** IDeaS-based, mandatory. Provides pricing recommendations.
- **StayConnected Guest Internet Access:** $120K-$175K initial + $30K-$90K/year ongoing. Refresh every 4 years.
- **Connected Room System:** $55K-$66K initial + $980-$1,395/month.
- **Delphi Sales & Events:** $900-$41K initial + $858/user/year.
- **Digital Floor Plan, Hilton Opening Transition Tool:** $6,500 initial setup.

Monthly technology costs: approximately $3,800-$9,000+/month before internet circuit costs.

### Marketing Fund Spending and Audit Status

The 4% Monthly Program Fee funds various programs: reservation services, directories, internet sites, marketing, quality assurance, technology, and administrative overhead. The franchisor has sole discretion over spending. The fund is not a trust and the franchisor is not a fiduciary. There is no separate audit of Monthly Program Fee spending. Expenditures need not be proportionate to any individual franchisee's contributions.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition and Carve-Outs

There is **no protected territory**. The franchise is a non-exclusive license to operate at a specified location. Some franchise agreements may include a "Restricted Area" provision in the Addendum, but the standard FA provides no territorial protection.

The franchisor explicitly reserves the right to own, operate, manage, franchise, license, or affiliate with any other business of any nature, under any brand (including competitive brands), at any time and in any location — including in the franchisee's immediate market. There is no mechanism to resolve conflicts.

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights

The franchisor controls all internet presence. Franchisees cannot register domains containing the Marks, cannot operate independent hotel websites, and must obtain prior approval for any third-party site listing. All Sites must conform to identity and graphics standards.

No relocation permitted. No options, rights of first refusal, or similar rights to acquire additional franchises.

The franchisor has Strategic Partnerships with Small Luxury Hotels, Autocamp, Evermore Resort, and Field Station, enabling reservations through Hilton's distribution system.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

- **New Construction:** 23 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10-20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership:** remaining term or other approved term

**No renewal right.** Re-licensing is at the franchisor's sole discretion. The franchisee may be asked to sign a contract with materially different terms and conditions, and must comply with any PIP. This creates significant end-of-term risk, particularly given the large capital investment required.

### Transfer Provisions

Two tiers of transfers are permitted:
1. **Permitted Transfers** (no change of control): publicly traded equity, minority private interests (<50%), institutional fund transfers. Some require no notice; others require 60-day notice, consent, and $5,500 processing fee.
2. **Change of Ownership:** 60-day notice, all amounts current, transferee must meet qualifications, submit COO Application ($175,000), sign current FA form, comply with PIP, and provide guarantors.

No right of first refusal, but franchisor must be notified if equity interests are being marketed.

### Termination and Death/Disability

Franchisee cannot terminate before expiration — doing so is a material breach triggering liquidated damages. The franchisor can terminate without cause upon condemnation or casualty if the franchisee elects not to rebuild.

Curable defaults: payment (10-day cure), construction/work deadlines (10-day cure), insurance (10-day cure), other FA breach (30-day cure).

Non-curable defaults (16 grounds): include repeated non-compliance within 24 months, 3 notices in 12 months, bankruptcy, loss of possession, 5+ days non-operation, contesting marks, reputational harm, false records, unauthorized transfer, becoming a Competitor, sanctions violations, gaming license issues, breach by guarantor, public health threat, termination of related hotel agreements.

On death: transfer to immediate family member permitted within 1 year if qualifications are met.

### Noncompete Restrictions

During term: Franchisee cannot own or be licensor/franchisor of a competing brand. May own a minority interest in a Competitor under certain conditions. May be a franchisee of a Competitor. May manage a Competitor's property (if not exclusive manager).

Post-term: **No noncompete.** This is favorable to franchisees.

### Dispute Resolution

- **Governing law:** New York (without choice of law principles)
- **Venue:** U.S. District Court, E.D. Virginia (Alexandria), or state court in Fairfax County, VA. If no federal jurisdiction, Fairfax County, VA or New York, NY. Franchisor may elect to bring action where hotel is located.
- **Jury waiver:** Yes (FA Section 17.1). Overridden in Minnesota (deleted), North Dakota (deleted), Illinois (cannot waive FDD Act rights), Wisconsin (may not be enforceable).
- **Punitive damages waiver:** Yes (FA Section 17.2). Indemnification for third-party punitive damages preserved.
- **Arbitration:** None, except in Puerto Rico (AAA/JAMS, Fairfax County VA venue, NY seat).

### Litigation History

**Pending (5 actions):**
- AAAA Property Partners v. HFH (M.D. Fla.): Breach of contract from franchise termination for payment defaults. Not yet served.
- Three antitrust class actions (IDeaS/SAS/Amadeus) alleging hotel chains used revenue management and data analytics software to coordinate room pricing. These are significant industry-wide cases.
- One antitrust class action (CoStar/STR) alleging competitive data sharing led to higher room rates.

**Concluded (notable):**
- Texas mandatory guest fee: $2.1M settlement (January 2025). Hilton agreed to total-cost pricing display.
- Nebraska mandatory guest fee: $300K settlement (February 2024).
- Portland Hotel Ownership (Curio brand): $30M counterclaim, settled with mutual releases (July 2022).
- 1970 HHC antitrust (hotel supplier conspiracy): permanent injunction entered 1971, still in effect.

No collection suits against franchisees in 2024. No bankruptcies to disclose.

### Key Financial Observations

1. **Leverage and structure:** The entity is a licensing pass-through with ~99% operating margin and near-zero operating expenses. All assets ($1.27B) are pledged as collateral for Hilton parent debt (maturing 2025-2033). The entity has virtually no independent financial autonomy.

2. **Distributions:** Virtually all net income is distributed to the parent ($1.5B in 2024). In 2022, distributions ($1.55B) actually exceeded net income ($1.23B).

3. **Related party:** $704M in receivables from Hilton affiliates. The entity participates in Hilton's centralized cash management system over which it has no control. Cash balance of $317K is essentially zero.

4. **Asset composition:** The largest non-current asset is franchise contracts ($393M), which increased by $154M in 2024 due to the Graduate brand acquisition ($85M intangible) plus ongoing contract acquisition costs.

5. **Revenue growth:** 9.1% YoY growth ($1.38B to $1.50B), driven by royalty fee growth. Termination fee revenue spiked to $18.3M in 2024 from $2.4M in 2023.

6. **Tax treatment:** The entity is treated as a disregarded entity for US tax purposes — all taxable income is included in Hilton's consolidated return.

7. **Credit losses:** Allowance for credit losses on accounts receivable increased from $7.3M to $10.4M, and provision for credit losses increased from $4.4M to $6.2M.

## L. Final Coverage Note

### Fully Surfaced
All 23 items have been completely extracted. All material tables (19 tables across Items 5, 6, 7, 9, 11, 17, 19, 20, 21) have been captured with full detail. The financial statements (balance sheet, income statement, cash flows, and all 9 notes) are complete. ### Not Directly Surfaced
- **Cost build-up / EBITDA:** Not disclosed in Item 19. This is the most significant gap for prospective franchisee analysis, but it is the franchisor's deliberate disclosure choice.
- **Insurance cost estimates, real property costs, market study costs, environmental assessment costs:** Listed as "Varies" in Item 7 — not quantifiable from the FDD alone.
- **Marketing fund audit:** No separate audit of Monthly Program Fee spending is disclosed.
- **Individual state addenda provisions:** States identified but not individually parsed in full detail.

### Recovered via Retries
- **Jury waiver (R1):** Confirmed in FA Section 17.1. State overrides documented for Minnesota, North Dakota, Illinois, Wisconsin.

### Assessment
This is a high-quality, substantially complete extraction. The DoubleTree by Hilton FDD is a well-organized, comprehensive document reflecting a mature franchise system backed by a major publicly traded hotel company. The primary risk factors for prospective franchisees are: (1) the absence of any protected territory, (2) no renewal right (re-licensing at sole discretion with potentially materially different terms), (3) the heavy fee burden approaching 13-14%+ of room revenue, (4) significant liquidated damages exposure, and (5) the wide dispersion in performance metrics suggesting that many franchisees underperform the averages.
