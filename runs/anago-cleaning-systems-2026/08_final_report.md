## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System overview**: Anago Cleaning Systems operates a two-tier master franchise system for commercial janitorial services. The franchise offered is the right to act as a master franchisee (subfranchisor) who sells and services Anago unit franchises within a defined territory. As of December 31, 2025, the system includes 44 franchised subfranchisors and 1 company-owned outlet domestically, plus 3 international subfranchisors (all in Canada).

2. **Entity structure**: Anago Franchising, Inc. (AFI) is a Florida corporation formed in 1995, wholly owned by Anago Cleaning Systems, Inc. (ACS). ACS owns the Anago trademarks and guarantees AFI's performance under all Subfranchise Rights Agreements. The predecessor entity, Anago International, Inc. (AII), was formed in 1989 and ceased operations December 31, 2023.

3. **Ownership and management**: Led by CEO & President Adam D. Povlitz (with AFI since 2009). The leadership team of 11 officers includes a Chief Strategy Officer (joined 2022), COO (joined 2023), and a newly hired CMO (September 2025). Three officers hold CFE designations. Two affiliates (Estrellita, Inc. in South Florida and CTAD, Inc. in Boise) operate as company-affiliated subfranchisors.

4. **Investment range**: $219,000 to $339,000 total initial investment, anchored by a $98,000 fully earned, non-refundable initial fee. No financing is offered by the franchisor. A VetFran program provides a 15% discount on the initial fee plus 6 months royalty waiver for qualifying veterans.

5. **Fee burden**: The ongoing fee stack is significant: 5% royalty on Gross Revenues (with escalating minimums starting at $1,500/month in year 2, increasing $1,500/year thereafter), 2% administrative support fee, $550/month NBDS software fee, and a mandatory $50,000/year client marketing spend. An advertising fund of up to 2.2% of Gross Revenues is authorized but not currently assessed. The total ongoing fee load exceeds 7.5% of Gross Revenues before the marketing spend requirement.

6. **Item 19 highlights**: FPR is disclosed with four years of data. For 2025, average annual sales across 37 qualifying subfranchisors was $3,453,102 (median $3,531,399). The range is wide: $282,393 to $8,243,733. The bottom quartile averaged $1,266,186 while the top quartile averaged $6,162,068. These are gross sales figures (total billed), not profits or franchisee income.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: A bottom-quartile subfranchisor generating $1.27M in annual sales would face royalty obligations of at least $63,000 (5%) plus $25,000 in admin fees (2%) plus $6,600 in NBDS fees plus the $50,000 marketing spend — over $144,000 in franchisor-directed costs before operating expenses. The escalating minimum royalty (reaching $6,000+/month by year 5) creates additional pressure on lower-performing units.

8. **Financial health**: ACS consolidated revenue was $9.39M in 2025 (essentially flat from $9.42M in 2024). However, operating income dropped 34% from $1.32M to $869K, and EBITDA fell from $1.45M to $1.02M. The balance sheet is strong: $6.65M in equity, $2.55M cash, $3.78M in investments, no debt. Clean audit opinion from Miller CPA, PLLC. A new $880K non-qualified deferred compensation liability appeared in 2025.

9. **Growth trajectory**: The system is flat. Total domestic outlets: 45 at start of 2025, 45 at end (with a slight shift from franchised to company-owned). Franchised count actually declined from 45 to 44. Some shrinkage is attributed to conversions to a new Market Developer model (launched December 2024, separate FDD, 2 Market Developers). Projected openings for 2026: 5 new franchised.

10. **Territory**: Defined counties with minimum 500,000 population. Not technically exclusive, but franchisor won't grant another subfranchisor in the same area during the term. Significant reservations: franchisor retains national accounts (10+ location clients), right to operate under different marks, and Internet/catalog/direct mail sales into the area.

11. **Renewal**: One 10-year renewal. Critical risk: the successor agreement may contain materially different terms. The minimum monthly royalty at renewal is locked at least to the average of the last year's royalty — preventing any downward reset.

12. **Disputes and litigation**: No pending or prior litigation requiring disclosure. All disputes resolved via binding arbitration in Pompano Beach, FL under AAA Commercial Rules. Florida governing law, subject to state-specific overrides in 8 states (CA, IL, MD, MI, MN, NY, VA, WA).

13. **Special risks (state-flagged)**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (FL), mandatory minimum payments, sales performance requirements (territory can be reduced for underperformance).

14. **Gag clause**: Present. The FDD discloses that certain subfranchisors have signed confidentiality provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience.

15. **No franchisee termination right**: The agreement does not provide a contractual right for the franchisee to terminate.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

The franchisor, Anago Franchising, Inc. (AFI), is a purpose-built franchise-selling entity. Its parent, ACS, owns the trademarks, employs the technology team, and guarantees AFI's performance. The prospective franchisee must form an LLC or corporation with a 4-character entity name (e.g., "JKCA, Inc.") and operate as d/b/a Anago of [City]. All individual shareholders must provide personal guaranties.

This is a master franchise — the buyer does not clean buildings. The buyer sells unit franchises to individuals who perform the cleaning work. The subfranchisor's revenue comes from a share of the unit franchisees' client billings, plus unit franchise sale fees.

### Fee Stack

**Initial fees:**
- Initial Fee: $98,000 (fully earned, non-refundable upon signing)
- VetFran: 15% discount + 6-month royalty waiver for qualifying veterans

**Ongoing fees (recurring):**
- Royalty: 5% of Gross Revenues, with escalating monthly minimums ($0 yr1 → $1,500/mo yr2 → +$1,500/year thereafter)
- Administrative Support Fee: 2% of Gross Revenues
- NBDS Software: $550/month
- Client Marketing Spend: minimum $50,000/year (shortfall paid to franchisor)

**Event-driven fees:**
- Transfer Fee: $10,000
- Sale of Unit Franchise Fee: $400 per unit sold
- Training Fees: $500/day (only if refresher required)
- Client Bid Deficiency Fee: $150 per missed bid
- Banking Fees: 4.75% per CC transaction (optional)
- Website Maintenance: $1,500/month (only if franchisor takes over website)
- Billing & Collection: Greater of $1,000 or 1% of Gross Revenues + $25/unit statement (only on default)

**Authorized but not currently assessed:**
- Technology Fee: up to 1.5% of Gross Revenues
- Advertising Fund: up to 2.2% of Gross Revenues

- All NBDS data is franchisor's Confidential Information; franchisor has full access
- No contractual limitations on upgrade frequency or cost

### Marketing/Advertising
- $50,000/year mandatory client marketing spend (shortfall paid to franchisor)
- Advertising Fund authorized up to 2.2% of Gross Revenues (not currently collected)
- All advertising requires prior written approval
- No advertising committees or cooperatives currently exist
- If Fund established: not required to be audited; no fiduciary obligation; franchisor discretion on spending

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
- Defined counties with minimum 500,000 population
- Agreed upon prior to signing
- Not technically exclusive, but franchisor agrees not to grant another subfranchisor in the area during the term (subject to compliance)
- Territory may be reduced if minimum Client Bid schedule is not met

### Franchisor's Reserved Rights
Broad and explicitly enumerated:
- Operate other businesses under different marks (anywhere, including in Area)
- Sell similar services via Internet, catalogs, direct mail (in or outside Area)
- Grant retail businesses using Anago marks outside Area
- Bid on and enter National Account contracts (clients with 10+ locations in Area, multi-location clients, or RFP responses)
- Advertise the System anywhere

### Channel Restrictions
- Cannot solicit Unit Franchisees or clients outside Area (including via Internet, catalogs, telemarketing)
- Must refer all National Accounts to franchisor
- Cannot enter National Account contracts without written consent

### Market Developer Overlap
- New Market Developer program launched December 2024 (separate FDD)
- 2 Market Developers as of issuance date
- Franchisor does not anticipate overlap but has no specific conflict resolution method established

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 10 years
- **Renewal**: One additional 10-year term
- **Renewal conditions**: Written notice 9-12 months prior; not in default; sign then-current successor agreement + general release; meet current qualification/training requirements
- **CRITICAL**: Successor agreement "may contain materially different terms or conditions"
- **Royalty floor**: Successor minimum monthly royalty ≥ average of last year under current agreement

### Transfer
- Requires prior written approval
- Conditions: not in default, general release, franchisor-approved transferee, $10,000 transfer fee
- Right of First Refusal: 30 days to match bona fide offer; 60 days to close
- Death/disability: transfer within 6 months; replacement manager required immediately

### Termination
- By franchisee: No contractual right
- By franchisor without cause: Not applicable
- By franchisor with cause: 25 non-curable defaults (including insolvency, bankruptcy, felony, audit denial, repeated complaints, failure to achieve minimum performance) + curable defaults (30-day cure period)
- Note: 3+ notices for same default in 12 months = non-curable even if cured

### Non-Competition
- During term: No involvement with any Competitive Business; no interference with franchisor or franchisees
- Post-term: 24 months in Area, any other subfranchisor's area, or within 20 miles of perimeter

### Dispute Resolution
- Pre-arbitration: 60-day good-faith negotiation
- Binding arbitration per AAA Commercial Rules, 1 arbitrator, within 50 miles of Pompano Beach, FL
- Governed by Federal Arbitration Act
- Exception: franchisor may seek court injunctive relief for IP matters
- Choice of forum: state/federal court closest to Pompano Beach
- Choice of law: Florida

### Litigation History
- No pending actions
- No prior actions required to be disclosed

6. **Advertising spending declining**: $671K (2025) vs $919K (2024) vs $1.03M (2023) — a 35% reduction over two years.
