## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and geography**: 30 franchised locations, 0 company-owned as of December 31, 2024. All franchised. Concentrated in California (24 of 30 units), with Texas (4), Montana (1), and North Carolina (1). Aggressive growth: 6→10→19→30 over three years. Projecting 27 new franchised and 1 company-owned location in 2025 across 12 states.

2. **Entity structure**: ProColor Collision USA LLC, a Delaware LLC formed April 23, 2020. Owned by Mondofix USA LLC → Mondofix Inc. (Canada). Mondofix is a multi-brand automotive aftermarket conglomerate that also operates Fix Auto (654 worldwide locations), Novus Glass (128+ US franchised), Carrossier ProColor (157 in Canada), and other collision/glass brands.

3. **Ownership history**: Mondofix Inc. acquired Carrossier ProColor assets from Uni-Select Inc. in September 2019, transferred to Mondofix in February 2020. ProColor Collision USA LLC formed April 2020 to franchise the ProColor concept in the United States. Began offering franchises July 2020. Licensed IP from Mondofix under indefinite License Agreement dated June 30, 2020.

4. **Investment range**: Conversion of existing auto repair facility: $38,300–$199,500. New build: $316,800–$3,129,500. The wide range on new builds reflects the high cost of purchasing/developing a facility versus leasing.

5. **Fee burden**: Monthly franchise fee of 3% of Gross Sales (minimum $2,000/month) for new locations; conversion franchisees pay a split base/growth structure (1.5% on base volume + 3% on growth). Marketing fund at 0.75% of Gross Sales. Technology stack (CCC ONE) costs approximately $1,770+/month. Convention fees $1,000-$1,500/person, 2x/year. Alternative supplier fees up to 2.6% additional.

6. **Item 19**: No financial performance representations made. Zero revenue, cost, or profitability data disclosed by franchisor. Prospective franchisees must rely entirely on their own research and franchisee interviews.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: This is a young franchise system (began franchising July 2020) with a state-mandated "short operating history" special risk warning. Cumulative deficit of $4.17M. Parent has injected $4.83M in capital. Going concern language appeared in 2023 financials but was removed in 2024 as losses narrowed. System is highly concentrated in one state (California).

8. **Financial health**: Revenue quintupled from $507K (2022) to $2.57M (2024). Net loss nearly eliminated ($1,896 in 2024 vs. $661K in 2023 vs. $1.34M in 2022). Still operating cash-flow negative ($324K outflow in 2024). No new parent capital contributions in 2024, but $427K in advances flowed upstream to the parent — a cash direction concern.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Strong net growth (+4, +9, +11 units per year). However, 6 terminations across 3 years in a system that started with only 5 units represents meaningful churn. California is saturating (24 units, 7 more signed but not opened); geographic diversification is just beginning.

10. **Territory**: Zip-code based, targeting 25% of collision spend capacity. Limited exclusivity with significant franchisor carve-outs: other brands (Fix Auto, Novus) can operate inside your territory, M&A activity can bring new competitors into your territory, and failure to service insurance/fleet accounts can result in loss of exclusivity.

11. **Renewal**: 5-year initial term + 2 renewal terms of 5 years each (max 15 years). Renewal requires signing a new franchise agreement that may contain "materially different terms and conditions." This is an explicit risk factor — your economics could change at renewal.

12. **Disputes**: Mediation first (mediator-selected location), then arbitration in Minneapolis, Minnesota. Minnesota law governs (subject to state overrides). State addenda for CA, HI, IL, MN, NY, WI provide material protections.

13. **Special risks (state-mandated)**: (a) Out-of-state dispute resolution in Minnesota, (b) Short operating history — early stage, riskier investment, (c) Mandatory minimum royalty payments for conversions regardless of sales levels.

14. **No post-term noncompete**: The FDD explicitly states post-term noncompete is "Not applicable." This is franchisee-favorable — you can continue operating a collision repair business after the franchise ends.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

ProColor Collision USA LLC is a Delaware limited liability company formed April 23, 2020, with principal offices at 650 Pelham Boulevard, Suite 100, St. Paul, Minnesota 55114. The company is wholly owned by Mondofix USA LLC, which is in turn wholly owned by Mondofix Inc., a Canadian corporation headquartered in Blainville, Quebec.

The franchise concept centers on full-service auto collision repair — appraisal, insurance billing, repair, painting, and after-care warranty — under the ProColor Collision mark. The franchisor's preferred model is conversion of an existing auto repair facility, though new builds are also supported. An owner-operator model is preferred, with a full-time Operator required at all times.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions and Approved Supplier Process

All inventory, supplies (including paint and parts), equipment, furniture, fixtures, design elements, branded items, signage, computer hardware and software, technology, and advertising materials must meet franchisor specifications. Approved supplier lists are provided before opening.

**Paint supplier transition**: If a conversion franchisee has an existing paint vendor agreement that cannot be terminated without penalty, the franchisee may continue purchasing from that vendor for the remaining agreement term — but must pay the franchisor an administrative fee of 1.6% of monthly Gross Sales during that period.

**Parts sourcing mandate**: Franchisees must purchase OEM, aftermarket, and recycled parts from designated suppliers at a minimum 85% participation rate. If the threshold is not met, the franchisor will issue a compliance plan. Failure to comply within 90 days (or longer if specified in the plan) triggers an additional 1% of Gross Sales administrative fee.

**Supply chain fees**: The combined alternative supplier fees can reach 2.6% of Gross Sales — a meaningful addition to the 3% royalty and 0.75% marketing fund.

### Training Program

**Initial Training**: 3-4 days, approximately 18-20.5 total hours (9.5-10 classroom + 8.5-10.5 on-the-job). Conducted at Minnesota HQ, online, or at franchisee location. No training fee, but franchisee pays all travel and living expenses. Must complete within 60 days of signing FA, before opening. Failure to complete to franchisor satisfaction may result in termination with no refund.

Training covers: business operations (SOP, production scheduling, blueprinting, estimate review, QC), photo documentation, insurance processes (policy review, total loss, arms rental management), customer service (greeting, complaint handling, same-day contact events), advertising and marketing, and body shop employee training (industry overview, ProColor benefits, shop role).

Training organized by Kevin Beane (Implementation Manager since July 2024, previously Implementation Specialist) and Garrett Pollick (Franchise Operations Specialist since January 2024, previously at GEICO). On-site trainers have minimum 5 years of collision repair experience.

**Additional Training**: New Operators must complete the Initial Training Program. Mandatory training may be required at franchisor's discretion. Training fees of $200-$300/day/trainer plus reimbursement of travel expenses apply for additional on-site training.

**Convention**: Owner and Operator must attend all national, regional, and state meetings (generally 2 per year). Registration fee: $1,000-$1,500 per person, payable regardless of attendance. Travel, lodging, and food at franchisee expense.

### Technology Stack and Costs

The technology burden is significant:
- **CCC ONE Package** (mandatory auto shop management software): Setup $3,395-$4,395; monthly subscription $1,200 + $110/additional user; 36-month minimum contract. Includes estimating, appointment booking, shop management, electronic check-in, customer engagement, DRP tools, KPI dashboards, parts sourcing.
- **Central Review** (mandatory): $155/month
- **Engage** (mandatory): $255/month
- **Insurance Mailboxes** (mandatory, min 3): $160-$215/month
- **Hardware**: Minimum 5 PCs, 2 printers (1 multifunction), POS system, phone, business-grade internet
- **Software**: Microsoft Office (~$1,000 or preinstalled), antivirus, QuickBooks ($300-$500 or $25-$150/month online), Office 365 email ($6.30-$9.18/month/account)

**Estimated monthly technology burden**: $1,770-$1,830/month minimum (CCC ONE + mailboxes + Central Review + Engage + email), before hardware costs.

The franchisor can independently access all electronic information and data from franchisee systems without compensation. No contractual limitation on franchisor's right to receive or use this data.

### Marketing Fund

Marketing Fund contributions of 0.75% of Gross Sales are deposited in a separate account. In fiscal year 2024, spending was allocated: Advertising Placements 65.8%, Trade Show and Events 27.8%, General Administration 3.7%, Reputation Management 1.6%, Social Media Management 0.8%, Online Marketing/Support 0.3%.

Key terms: fund statements are prepared annually but NOT audited. No obligation to ensure pro-rata benefit to any franchisee. No advertising council (if formed, would be advisory only). Franchisor may loan amounts to the fund on self-determined terms.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition

The Territory is defined by one or more zip codes (or partial zip codes) determined by mapping software, based on the franchisee having capacity to process 25% of the available collision spend in those zip codes. There is no minimum territory size. The exact boundaries may also depend on demographics, geography, natural boundaries, competing businesses, site availability, and other factors.

### Exclusivity and Carve-Outs

While the franchisee is in compliance with the Franchise Agreement, the franchisor will not operate or grant a third party the right to operate a ProColor Collision Center within the Territory. However, this protection is subject to significant carve-outs:

1. **Insurance/fleet/national account failure**: If the franchisee fails to properly service an insurance, fleet, or national account, the franchisor can appoint other ProColor shops (owned by franchisor, affiliates, or other franchisees) to service those accounts in the Territory — permanently, with no compensation.

2. **Other brands**: The franchisor (through Mondofix) operates Fix Auto and Novus Glass brands. Fix Auto US franchises are sold by an unaffiliated third party (79411 USA Inc.). There is NO policy for resolving conflicts between ProColor and Fix Auto franchisees. Novus Glass may also operate in the same market. If there is an overlap in services, management will "attempt to resolve" but has no formal policy.

3. **M&A carve-out**: The franchisor can purchase, merge with, acquire, or affiliate with any business, operate those businesses using any mark (including ProColor Collision) inside the Territory.

4. **Capacity overflow**: If the franchisor determines that demand exceeds the franchisee's production capacity, the franchisee has 30 days to commit to expand or open a second location (requiring a new FA with potentially different terms). Failure to respond or complete within 6 months means the franchisor can open or grant rights to others in the Territory.

### Channel Restrictions

The franchisee may not solicit customers via the Internet, telemarketing, or other direct marketing without franchisor approval. No independent websites, social media profiles, accounts, or hashtags are permitted. All advertising must be franchisor-approved. Other franchisees may serve customers from within the Territory.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

- **Initial term**: 5 years
- **Renewals**: Up to 2 additional 5-year terms (maximum 15 years total)
- **Renewal notice**: Written notice 210-270 days before expiration
- **Renewal conditions**: (1) Not in default, (2) Sign new franchise agreement — which **may contain materially different terms and conditions** including different financial terms, (3) Pay $5,000 renewal fee, (4) Execute general release of claims (subject to state law), (5) Complete refresher training, (6) Remodel/update to current standards

The "materially different terms" language is a significant risk factor. Your royalty rate, territory, and other key terms could change at renewal.

### Transfer Provisions

All transfers require franchisor consent. Conditions include: transferee meets qualifications, signs new FA for remaining term (may have different fees/territory), no new initial franchise fee required of transferee, $10,000 transfer fee, release required (subject to state law), must appoint franchisor as attorney-in-fact for CCC ONE account transfer.

The franchisor has a right of first refusal to match any offer for the ProColor Collision Center or a controlling interest.

### Termination

- **By franchisee**: Not applicable — no voluntary termination right
- **By franchisor without cause**: Not applicable
- **By franchisor with cause**: Yes, but only on default
- **Curable defaults**: 30 days to cure (5 days for monetary defaults)
- **Non-curable defaults**: Bankruptcy, insolvency, abandonment, loss of premises, Gross Sales understatement (2+ times in 12 months), criminal acts, disqualification from insurance work, unsatisfied judgments, noncompete violations, default under other agreements
- **Death/disability**: Not specifically addressed in the FDD

**Holdover**: If franchisee continues operating after term expiration without renewal, the arrangement is at-will and may be terminated by the franchisor on 30 days' notice.

### Noncompete Restrictions

- **During term**: No involvement in the operation of an automotive collision repair center or a franchise system, automotive aftermarket marketing banner, or branded chain related to the car collision industry. Subject to state law.
- **Post-term**: **Not applicable.** No post-term noncompete. This is explicitly stated in Item 17.

### Dispute Resolution

- **Method**: Mediation first (at location selected by mediator), then arbitration (in Minneapolis, Minnesota)
- **Governing law**: Minnesota (subject to state law overrides)
- **Cost shifting**: Franchisee bears all costs including attorneys' fees if franchisor secures relief or successfully defends a claim

### Litigation History

No litigation involving ProColor Collision USA LLC directly. Two related-party matters, both involving Mondofix and both resolved:
1. 79411 USA Inc. v. Mondofix (2017 arbitration, resolved 2020) — Fix Auto master license renewal dispute
2. Automotive Aftermarket Franchises v. Mondofix (Ireland, 2020, settled October 2021) — termination dispute

No lawsuits initiated against ProColor franchisees in 2024.

## J. Unresolveds

1. **Termination rates in early years** — Severity: MEDIUM
   - 3 terminations in 2022 out of 10 end-of-year units is a 30% termination rate. The FDD does not disclose reasons for these terminations. Were they quality failures, financial defaults, or mutual exits? Source: Item 20, page 58.

2. **Fix Auto conflict resolution** — Severity: MEDIUM
   - Fix Auto US is operated by an unaffiliated third party. There is no policy for resolving conflicts between ProColor and Fix Auto franchisees, even though Mondofix owns both brands. ProColor franchisees could face direct competition from a sister brand with no recourse mechanism. Source: Item 12, pages 43-44.

3. **Insurance company volume rebate terms** — Severity: LOW
   - Volume rebates to insurance companies are described as "typically 1-5% of the payment received" but "can be greater in some instances." Specific terms are not disclosed in the FDD. These rebates reduce the effective payment the franchisee receives for insurance work. Source: Item 6, Note 7, page 21.

4. **80% modification rule** — Severity: MEDIUM
   - Any modification to the Franchise Agreement accepted by 80% of ProColor Collision Centers is binding on all franchisees. With 30 current locations, this means 24 franchisees can impose contract changes on the remaining 6. In a system this small, a few multi-unit operators could control the outcome. Source: Item 17, FA Section 29.8.

5. **Advances to parent company** — Severity: MEDIUM
   - $427K in non-interest-bearing advances to the parent company, plus $29K to common-control companies. These are not repayable before January 1, 2026. Cash is flowing upstream from the franchise entity to the parent while the franchise entity is still operating cash-flow negative. Source: Exhibit E, pages 94, 101.
