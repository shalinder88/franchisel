# Final Diligence Report — McDonald's USA, LLC FDD (638437-2025)

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** 13,559 U.S. restaurants (12,887 franchised, 672 company-owned) as of December 31, 2024. Approximately 95% franchised to independent operators.
2. **Entity structure:** McDonald's USA, LLC (Delaware LLC), wholly-owned subsidiary of McDonald's Corporation (Delaware). Formed 2004, U.S. assets transferred 2005. Parent retains legal title to certain property and IP.
3. **Ownership history:** McDonald's Corporation began franchising in 1955. 70 years of continuous franchise operations. Puerto Rico and Virgin Islands sold to LatAm, LLC in 2007.
4. **Investment range:** Traditional $1.47M-$2.73M. Store Systems alone cost $150K-$250K. Of 31 McOpCo sales in 2024, 9 exceeded the high end by up to $2M.
5. **Fee burden:** Royalty 5% (new) or 4% (legacy). Percentage Rent 6-23% (min 11.50% from year 8). Advertising min 4%. Technology fees ~$10,400+/yr. Total occupancy burden can exceed 20% of gross sales. Effective rent range in 2024: 0-33.33%.
6. **Item 19 highlights:** Avg gross sales $4.0M (all traditional). Pro forma OIBOC at $3M = $734K (24.5%). CRITICAL: Pro forma at 4% royalty; new franchisees pay 5%, reducing OIBOC by ~$30-40K/year.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** Lowest annual sales $914K. At bottom quartile, after rent + royalty + advertising, cash flow could be negative. 22% of restaurants had sales below $3M.
8. **Financial health:** E&Y clean opinion. $10.6B revenue, $3.46B net income. Total assets $22.2B. But: 100% of net income distributed as dividends to parent. $2.4B intercompany debt converted to equity in Feb 2025.
9. **Growth trajectory:** Net +102 in 2024 (vs +2 in 2023). 195 projected new openings for 2025. Strong acceleration after flat years.
10. **Territory:** No exclusive territory. No protected area. Encroachment explicitly allowed.
11. **Renewal:** No right to renew. Discretionary new term. Materially different terms possible.
12. **Disputes:** IL governing law. No mandatory arbitration. Significant pending litigation (antitrust no-poach class actions, racial discrimination by 77 former franchisees, E. coli class action).
13. **Special risks:** IL venue for dispute resolution. Gag clause restricting franchisee speech. Washington AOD on no-poach provisions.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
McDonald's USA, LLC is a Delaware limited liability company headquartered in Chicago, Illinois. It is a wholly-owned subsidiary of McDonald's Corporation. The franchisor develops, operates, franchises, and services restaurants under the McDonald's System. All U.S. restaurants are operated under franchise agreements by either independent franchisees (~95%) or McOpCo companies (~5%).

Restaurant types: Traditional (20-year term, $45K initial fee), STO/STR (10 years, $22.5K), Satellite (variable term, $500), BFL (3 years, $45K on option exercise).

### Fee Stack

**Ongoing fees to McDonald's:**
- **Royalty:** 5% of Gross Sales for new restaurants after January 1, 2024. 4% continues for legacy scenarios (family transfers, new terms on existing restaurants, franchisee-to-franchisee sales, rebuilds).
- **Rent:** Three components: (1) Monthly Base Rent based on McDonald's investment, (2) Pass Thru Rent for lease escalations, (3) Percentage Rent generally 6-23% of Gross Sales for new traditional restaurants. Minimum 11.50% starting year 8. Total increase over term generally capped at 3.50% from year-1 baseline.
- **Relocation Contribution:** $50,000 per relocated restaurant.

**Technology fees (annual per restaurant, collected by McDonald's):**
Sesame POS $1,133 | Global Mobile App $664 | McDelivery POS $620 | Self-Ordering Kiosk $558 | HHOT $50 (optional) | eProduction $47 | Back Office $545 | Payments/Fraud $740 | Employee Engagement $385 | PDW Reporting $167 (optional) | McD Connect $250 (optional) | REES $62 (optional) | Deployment/OTP/Support $2,529 | Network Management $1,134 | Hardware/Data $951 | RFM $690 | Microsoft License $707 | Pricing Engine $264 (optional) | GRNT $185 | Edge $435 | Store Mail $56.40. **Aggregate mandatory: ~$10,400+/year.**

**Advertising (not to McDonald's):**
Minimum 4% of Gross Sales annually. OPNAD contribution 2.25%. Local cooperative rates vary. McDonald's provides marketing/legal/accounting staff to OPNAD at no charge but maintains no advertising fund.

**Event-driven fees:**
Audit/inspection fee (cost of audit if understatement ≥2%). Satellite annual fee $500-$2,500.

### Initial Investment
Traditional: $1,471,000-$2,728,000. Largest component: Signs, Seating, Equipment, Decor ($1.1M-$1.79M). Additional Funds (3 months operating): $250K-$439K. Note that 9 of 31 McOpCo sales in 2024 exceeded the high end, up to $2.04M above the stated range.

**Co-investment option:** Franchisees may co-invest in building/site improvements to reduce Percentage Rent in 0.25% increments. Cost generally ≥$30,000 per Quarter. Two options for new restaurants: floor at 11.50% or 12.50% (with partial refund if sales targets not met).

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Franchisees must deal only with McDonald's-approved suppliers and agencies. McDonald's does not generally sell directly but derives $7.21B (68% of revenue) from leasing arrangements. Supplier approval requires meeting 6 criteria through Supply Chain Management. McDonald's may limit the number of approved suppliers. Required purchases represent 90-95% of establishment costs and 55-65% of operating costs.

### Training Program Detail
The Candidate Development Program takes 6 months to 2 years. Restaurant Learning Path includes Crew Development (83 hrs), Area Management (60 hrs), Shift Leader (59 hrs), Food Safety (8 hrs classroom), Department Leader (94 hrs), and Leading Great Restaurants (32 hrs/4 days at Hamburger University in Chicago). Business Learning Path adds ~178 additional hours across 15 modules. Must complete all training before signing the Franchise Agreement.

### Technology Stack and Costs
Store Systems cost $150,000-$250,000 (POS, Cashless 3.0, digital menu boards, kiosks, table locators, hardware, software). Annual technology fees approximately $10,400+. No contractual limits on frequency or cost of upgrades. McDonald's has independent, unlimited access to transaction-level data from POS and cashless systems.

### Marketing Fund
No formal advertising fund maintained by McDonald's. OPNAD (national advertising fund) is an independent entity managed by franchisee-elected representatives. Contribution rate currently 2.25% of sales. OPNAD fund is independently audited annually. McDonald's provides certain marketing/legal/accounting staff at no charge. McDonald's is not required to spend any amount advertising any individual restaurant.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
None. The franchise grants authority to use the McDonald's System at a specific address only. No exclusive grant, exclusive area, exclusive territorial rights, or any right to exclude, control, or impose conditions on future McDonald's development.

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights
McDonald's reserves the right to use the Marks in any channel of distribution. McCafe packaged coffee is currently sold in supermarkets and via e-commerce. McDonald's reserves the right to sell other goods and services under the same or different marks. The Franchise Agreement explicitly states: franchisees may face competition from other franchisees, company-owned outlets, or other channels/brands controlled by McDonald's.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
Traditional franchise term: 20 years. No right to renew or extend. McDonald's may, at its sole discretion, offer a new term franchise via the New Term Policy (effective January 1, 2025). This policy is not a contract right. Application in year 16, recommendation in year 17 based on National Franchising Standards performance. The New Term Committee (U.S. management) makes the final decision. If not offered new term, franchisee may sell to a qualified buyer.

New term franchise: same royalty rate, new initial franchise fee at then-current rates, rent per then-current policy (not lower than previous; may increase to 8.50% if below). Must sign then-current form of franchise agreement — a "materially different" contract.

### Transfer Provisions
Requires McDonald's prior written consent (not arbitrarily withheld). ROFR: 10 days to match any offer. Conditions: new franchisee qualifies, assumes full liability, original franchisee remains personally liable for the remainder of the term. Royalty automatically adjusts to the then-current percentage upon transfer.

### Termination and Death/Disability
17 enumerated material breach triggers (Section 18) — all non-curable. Include: failure to maintain standards, bankruptcy, non-payment (30 days), judgment >$5K, loss of possession, competing interest, unauthorized transfer, reputation damage, felony conviction, intentional gross sales understatement. Repeated other breaches (Section 19) also grounds for termination.

Death/disability: may assign to qualified spouse/heirs. If no qualified assignee, McDonald's may manage for up to 12 months, then option to purchase at FMV.

### Noncompete Restrictions
During term: no involvement in competing or similar restaurant business. Post-term: 18 months, 10-mile radius (including after assignment or sale). Exception for <2% ownership in publicly traded company. North Dakota addendum: generally unenforceable.

### Dispute Resolution
Governing law: Illinois. No mandatory arbitration or mediation clause. Open door policy and Ombudsman process as common practice (not contractual). No choice of forum clause in base agreement. State addenda modify for Maryland (may bring MD franchise law claims in MD) and Washington (WA Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails).

### Litigation History
7 named pending cases including antitrust no-poach class actions (Deslandes, Turner — Supreme Court denied cert), racial discrimination by 77 former Black franchisees (Crawford), ice cream machine dispute (Kytch), Hispanic franchisee discrimination (Michell), E. coli class action (Williams), McRib marketing class (Le). Plus numerous joint-employer lawsuits. 12+ concluded cases with settlements ranging from $2,500 to $33.5M (Washington racial discrimination).

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Sales Data
Of ~12,572 domestic traditional restaurants open 1+ year as of December 31, 2024:
- Average annual sales: $4,002,000
- Median annual sales: $3,838,000
- Range: $914,000 to $19,680,000
- 79% had sales >$3M, 72% >$3.2M, 65% >$3.4M

Franchised only (~12,023): avg $3,966,000, median $3,797,000.
McOpCo only (~549): avg $4,793,000, median $4,612,000 (21% higher than franchised average).

### Pro Forma Statements
Based on 11,332 independent franchisee traditional restaurants. OIBOC at three tiers:
- $3M sales: OIBOC $734,000 (24.5%) — 76% exceeded this
- $3.2M sales: OIBOC $812,000 (25.4%) — 69% exceeded this
- $3.4M sales: OIBOC $879,000 (25.9%) — 62% exceeded this

### Cohort Comparability Warning (HIGH SEVERITY)
The pro forma royalties were computed at 4% of Gross Sales. However, new franchisees after January 1, 2024 pay 5% royalty. This 100 basis point difference is NOT reflected in the pro forma OIBOC figures.

**Dollar impact at each tier (directly computed from disclosed data):**
- At $3M sales: -$30,000/year → adjusted OIBOC ~$704,000
- At $3.2M sales: -$32,000/year → adjusted OIBOC ~$780,000
- At $3.4M sales: -$34,000/year → adjusted OIBOC ~$845,000
- At median franchised sales ($3,797,000): -$37,970/year

This overstates OIBOC for new franchisees by approximately 4-5% of the stated OIBOC.

### Critical Caveats
- OIBOC excludes rent, royalty, D&A, interest, and income taxes
- Effective rent in 2024 ranged from 0.00% to 33.33%
- Organization overhead costs (non-restaurant personnel, automobile) not included
- McOpCo data excluded from pro forma (if included, OIBOC range $730K-$868K)
- Written substantiation available upon reasonable request

## I. State Addenda Summary

Six states have addenda:

1. **California:** Disclosures regarding California Fast Food Act (AB 1228). McDonald's does not provide training or assistance related to the Act; compliance is solely the franchisee's responsibility.

2. **Hawaii:** Filing notice. No statement or acknowledgement waives claims under state franchise law or disclaims reliance on franchisor statements.

3. **Maryland:** (a) Release required for renewal/transfer will not apply to MD Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law liability. (b) Termination upon insolvency may not be enforceable under federal bankruptcy law. (c) Forum selection: franchisee may bring MD franchise law claims in Maryland. (d) Choice of law: MD franchise law actions may be brought in MD. (e) 3-year deadline for MD franchise law claims.

4. **Minnesota:** Franchise Agreement will not abrogate MN Chapter 80C rights. 90 days' notice of termination (60 days to cure), 180 days' notice for non-renewal. Transfer consent not unreasonably withheld.

5. **North Dakota:** Noncompetes generally unenforceable. ND law governs ND franchise law claims.

6. **Washington:** WA Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails over inconsistent FDD/agreement provisions. Additionally: Assurance of Discontinuance (AOD) with WA Attorney General — McDonald's agreed to remove no-poach employee restrictions from franchise agreements and not enforce existing ones in Washington.
