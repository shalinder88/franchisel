# B3 Conflict Adjudication

## Conflict 1 (C1 / R5) — Post-term noncompete radius

**Context.** Item 17 table row (r) stacks two different post-term noncompete variants:
- Franchise Agreement (FA): 1 year / 5-mile radius of the designated area and of any System location.
- Franchise Deposit Agreement (FDA): 1 year / 50-mile radius of the designated area and of any System location.
Both marked "subject to State Law."

**Prior run evidence.** The prior run cites FA Sect. XVI.D at p.122 for the 5-mile variant, and FDA.5 for the 50-mile variant. Both section references are preserved but no reconciliation is provided.

**New run evidence.** Item 17 table (pp.45-46) is the authoritative source; the FA exhibit itself was not line-extracted in the new run.

**Adjudication.**
The FA and FDA are separate contracts executed at different times. A prospective franchisee first signs the FDA (with the 50-mile variant), then signs the FA at site selection (with the 5-mile variant). If the franchisee terminates under the FDA (180-day no-site window), the 50-mile variant controls. If the franchisee proceeds to the FA, the FA 5-mile variant controls for post-term obligations after restaurant operation.

This is a sequencing mechanism, not a true contradiction, but it is still a **buyer beware** item because:
1. A prospective franchisee who drops out during the 180-day FDA window faces an unusually broad 50-mile noncompete that covers all WI + southern MN — effectively locking them out of the regional pan-pizza/pasta market for a year even though they never operated a restaurant.
2. The enforceability under Wisconsin public policy is uncertain; WI Fair Dealership Act protections may not apply pre-operation.

**Resolution status.** Explained but **not fully resolved** as a legal matter. Prospective franchisee should obtain written clarification and state-law review before signing either document. Entry remains in `unresolveds` as U1 / C1.
