# Final Diligence Report — Rocky Rococo Pizza and Pasta (Filing 638785, 2025)

## A. Executive snapshot
1. Rococo Franchise Corporation (RFC) is a regional Wisconsin/Minnesota quick-service pan-style pizza and pasta franchisor, incorporated June 13, 1995, headquartered at 105 East Wisconsin Avenue, Oconomowoc, WI 53066. It offers franchises exclusively for Rocky Rococo Pizza and Pasta restaurants and conducts no other line of business.
2. System size is very small. At fiscal year-end 12/30/2024 there were 31 total outlets — 24 franchised, 7 owned by RFC affiliates (not by RFC itself), and 0 predecessor-franchised. The system has been flat at 31-34 units for the last three fiscal years.
3. Geographic concentration is extreme: 23 of 24 franchised units are in Wisconsin and one is in Minnesota. All 7 affiliate units are in Wisconsin.
4. The founding Hester family controls the system. Thomas R. Hester (Co-CEO since 2020, President/Treasurer 1995-2020) and Sharon R. Hester (Co-CEO) have run the company since inception. Thomas R. Hester III is President. Amy R. Bartley is Secretary. Long-tenured operating bench: Earl Rambo (Operations/Training since 2006/2013) and Kurt Kimball (Controller since 2000).
5. Total estimated initial investment is $211,000 – $705,500, including a $25,000 initial franchise fee ($17,500 for a second store). Initial investment does not reflect free-standing builds, which RFC says are "substantially higher" and not projected.
6. Fee burden is mid-range for QSR: 5% royalty (with a retroactive 3.5% step-down for stores below a $425,000 annual gross sales threshold), 0.75% Marketing Fund (currently collected), and up to 3.5% Local Ad Fund (not currently collected, reinstateable on 30 days' notice).
7. There are no Item 19 financial performance representations. Prospective franchisees must rely entirely on Item 20 franchisee outreach or the actual records of an existing store offered for sale.
8. Financial health of the franchisor is sound: clean Vrakas S.C. audit opinion dated March 6, 2025; FY2024 revenue $1.38M, net income $70,626, operating cash flow $148,606, cash $669,550, total equity $446,700, no long-term debt.
9. Growth trajectory is thin. Zero new openings in 2024 (all three new franchised entries came from predecessor-franchised transfer-ins). Four new openings in 2023, none in 2022. Projected FY2025: 3 new franchised units (2 WI + 1 MN).
10. Territory protection is narrow: 1-mile radius, with carveouts eliminating protection inside major regional shopping malls (500k+ sq ft, 2+ anchors) and in central business districts. The FDD states plainly: "You will not receive an exclusive territory."
11. Term / renewal: 15 + 15 years with no renewal fee, but renewal requires signing the then-current Franchise Agreement which may contain "materially different" terms, executing a general release, remodeling, and re-training.
12. Litigation / disputes: none required to be disclosed in Items 3 or 4. Disputes are arbitrated under AAA at RFC's nearest office under Wisconsin law, except Wisconsin Franchise Investment Law and Wisconsin Fair Dealership Act claims which are explicitly carved out of arbitration.
13. Special risk called out: out-of-state dispute resolution (AAA arbitration away from franchisee's home). Wisconsin law may not provide the same protections as local law.
14. Marketing Fund is explicitly unaudited. FY2024 spend mix (per Item 11): 66.83% marketing/advertising, 3.63% administration, 29.53% public relations — a high PR allocation relative to media.
15. Related-party flow is material relative to franchisor P&L: FY2024 management/consulting payments to related parties totaled $167,977 (≈12% of revenue, ≈170% of operating income). The Company's operating facility is leased from a related party at zero rent.

## B. Fee stack, entry structure, initial investment

### Front matter and entity structure
RFC is a Wisconsin C corporation whose sole business is franchising Rocky Rococo restaurants. Rocky Rococo Corporation — a predecessor and affiliate — owns the trademarks and licenses them to RFC under a perpetual Intellectual Property License Agreement dated June 26, 1995. Five additional affiliates operate (but do not franchise) seven Rocky Rococo restaurants: Pizza Rococo Inc. (1 unit, since 1992), TRH Restaurants Inc. (1, since 1994), Airport Pizza Roc Inc. (2, since 2003), TRH Restaurants II Inc. (2, since 2007), and TRH Restaurants III Inc. (1, since 2021). No holding-company parent sits above RFC. RFC has no affiliate that offers franchises in any other line of business.

### Fee stack — every fee in Item 6
- **Royalty: 5% of weekly gross sales**, paid on or before the Friday after the end of the preceding week. If a restaurant fails to reach $425,000 in gross sales in a 13-period fiscal year (or $433,173 in a 53-week year), the royalty is retroactively reduced to 3.5% for that year and the difference is refunded within 30 days of year-end — provided the franchisee is current on all fees. "Gross sales" includes all food/beverage/goods sold in or away from the restaurant, cash or credit, excluding sales/service taxes.
- **Marketing Fund: up to 0.75% of gross sales**, weekly. Currently collected. Fund may borrow, may be unreimbursed to the franchisee's local area, and is not audited. RFC may use Fund money to pay salaries of RFC employees "working for or on behalf of the Fund," administrative overhead, and to pay interest on Fund borrowings.
- **Local Ad Fund / Local Advertising: up to 3.5% of gross sales**, tiered by media type (1.5% no electronic media, 2.5% with radio, 3.5% with television). Not currently collected; can be reinstated on 30 days' notice. If no Local Ad Fund is active, the franchisee must spend the equivalent amount on local advertising and submit quarterly verification within 15 days of quarter-end.
- **Transfer fee: 50% of the then-current initial franchise fee** upon approval of assignment; covers credit checks, attorney fees, training of new franchisee.
- **Additional training / on-site assistance:** Cost of lodging, meals, transport; franchisee pays.
- **Amounts advanced by RFC:** Repayable immediately.
- **Interest:** Maximum rate allowed by law, or 1.5% per month if no maximum.
- **Indemnification:** Uncapped; covers eleven broad categories including acts/omissions of franchisee, its employees, contractors, partners, customers, visitors; third-party trademark claims; libel/slander; product liability; latent defects; and claims "whether or not such losses … were actually or allegedly caused in part through the active or passive negligence of RFC."
- **Audit:** If audit discloses underreporting, franchisee pays the deficiency plus interest, and if the deficiency exceeds 2% of reported gross sales, franchisee also pays all accountants' and attorneys' fees. RFC may terminate for false reports or underreporting.
- **Grand opening advertising (Item 11):** not less than $5,000 for the period 30 days before through 30 days after opening.
- **Computer system (Item 11):** approximately $10,000 initially; ongoing upgrades required.

### Initial investment (Item 7)
Total range **$211,000 – $705,500**. Components:

- Equipment & Fixtures: $80,000 – $300,000
- Leasehold Improvements: $80,000 – $325,000
- Opening Inventory: $4,000 – $12,000

- Miscellaneous (legal, permits): $1,000 – $4,500
- Additional Funds (3 months): $10,000 – $20,000
- Insurance: $5,000

Three format footprints are contemplated: enclosed mall (~900 sq ft), strip-center mall (~2,800 sq ft), and free-standing (~3,800 sq ft). RFC does not project free-standing costs and warns they are "substantially higher." The Additional Funds estimate assumes an owner-operated business with no salary or owner's draw, and covers only three months. RFC explicitly warns that franchisees "can expect to put additional cash into the business during at least the first 3 to 9 months, and sometimes longer." No financing is offered directly or indirectly, and no guarantee of notes, leases, or obligations is made.

## C. Supplier control, operating control, technology burden, reporting burden

The designated-supplier regime is unusually light for QSR. RFC designates a single soft-drink supplier and requires the franchisee to use that supplier; neither RFC nor its affiliates derive revenue from the designation. Otherwise, 100% of goods, services, equipment, inventory, computer hardware/software, and real estate may be sourced from a supplier of the franchisee's choosing, subject to conformance with RFC's specifications and the Operations Manual. No purchasing cooperative exists.

Products must conform to RFC recipes/specifications covering type, quality, taste, weight, dimensions, ingredients, uniformity, preparation, and packaging. RFC may conduct market research and test-market new products, and the franchisee must purchase "a reasonable quantity" and offer test-marketed products for sale. Standards also cover hours/days of operation, delivery limits, safety, sanitation, employee dress and demeanor, use of marks, forms, ownership identification, and advertising.

**Training.** Ten weeks of formal pre-opening training are conducted at RFC's training store in Mayfair Mall, Wauwatosa, Wisconsin — 5 business days per week, 8-10 hours per day. RFC pays for the training itself; the franchisee covers transportation, lodging, meals, wages, and employee benefits. Up to an additional 20 weeks of optional on-the-job training are offered at affiliate restaurants during the franchisee's construction phase. Five business days of opening assistance are provided (one RFC staff member for the first 2 days of operation) with RFC paying wages for the first 5 days and the franchisee paying for any extended period. Initial training is mandatory; failure to satisfactorily complete it is grounds for termination at RFC's option. Every training subject in the program matrix is OJT-only (0 classroom hours): VCM/Dough (12 hrs), Pizza Prep (24), Quality Control/Oven (36), Bussing/DMO (4), Customer Service (36), Delivery (12), Administration (36).

**Technology stack and costs.** Franchisees must use an RFC-specified POS / bookkeeping / inventory / training / marketing / employee-selection / operations / financial-information system, at an approximate initial cost of $10,000. Franchisees must maintain a dedicated line for RFC access, transmit information to RFC through the system, and process every transaction on the system in RFC's required manner. All data retrieved from the system is RFC property. RFC does not have an obligation to provide ongoing maintenance, repairs, upgrades, or updates, and cannot estimate their costs. The computer system may not be fully amortizable over the remaining term of the Franchise Agreement. RFC does NOT have access to franchisee's computer information — this statement contradicts the earlier statement that RFC has the right to retrieve information and data from the system, and appears to be a narrow disclosure that RFC does not have automatic/pervasive access.

**Marketing Fund.** Franchisees pay up to 0.75% of gross sales weekly into the Fund. The Fund is used for advertising, marketing, media placement, PR, research, and websites. RFC may use Fund money for employee salaries working "for or on behalf of the Fund," for Fund administration, and for interest on Fund borrowings. The Fund may borrow from RFC or outside lenders. RFC is not obligated to make expenditures proportional to any franchisee's contribution. The Fund is **not audited**; only an unaudited statement is prepared annually on request. During FY2024 the Fund spent 66.83% on marketing/advertising, 3.63% on administration, and 29.53% on public relations. Contributions FY2024 were $243,568 against spending of $269,344, drawing down the ending balance from $120,256 to $94,480.

**Grand opening and local advertising.** Franchisees must spend at least $5,000 on grand-opening advertising in the 30 days before and 30 days after opening. Absent a Local Ad Fund (not currently active), franchisees must spend up to 3.5% of weekly gross sales on local advertising, and must submit verification within 15 days of quarter-end.

## D. Territory, competition, channels, encroachment

The franchise is granted for a specific approved location, not for an area. During the 15-year term, RFC will not operate or franchise another Rocky Rococo within a 1-mile radius of the franchisee's location, subject to two carveouts:
- **Major regional shopping malls** (500,000+ sq ft with ≥2 department-store anchors): Rocky Rococos inside such malls are excluded from the 1-mile protection in both directions. In other words, another Rocky Rococo can be placed in a nearby mall inside the franchisee's otherwise-protected 1-mile zone.
- **Central business districts:** When RFC deems a franchised location to be in a CBD, the 1-mile protection does not apply at all.

Beyond the 1-mile zone RFC retains broad rights to operate or license Rocky Rococos and to sell products through "similar or dissimilar channels of distribution" under the Rocky Rococo marks or different marks. RFC explicitly states: "You will not receive an exclusive territory. You may face competition from other franchisees, from outlets that RFC owns, or from other channels of distribution or competitive brands that RFC controls." There is no restriction on other franchisees soliciting customers inside the franchisee's territory. The franchisee has no option, right of first refusal, or similar right to acquire additional franchises in the territory or contiguous territories. RFC may approve relocation subject to standards and territorial non-infringement.

## E. Contract burden and legal mechanics

**Term.** 15 years. A Franchise Deposit Agreement precedes the Franchise Agreement and provides a 180-day site-search window. Both sides may terminate the FDA if no acceptable site is found in 180 days.

**Renewal.** One 15-year renewal is available if the franchisee is in good standing. There is no renewal fee. But renewal requires giving notice, signing the **then-current** Franchise Agreement (explicitly allowed to contain "materially different terms and conditions than the original contract"), executing a General Release, remodeling the restaurant, and completing re-training.

**Transfer.** RFC must approve any transfer (defined to include contract/asset transfers and ownership changes), and will not unreasonably withhold approval. Conditions: new franchisee qualifies, accounts made current, transfer fee paid (50% of then-current initial franchise fee), training arranged, release signed, covenant not to compete signed, remodel, and a current-form FA signed by the transferee. RFC has a right of first refusal to match any offer. On death or disability, the estate has 6 months to assign the franchise to an approved buyer or heir.

**Termination.** The franchisee has no termination right (except under the FDA for failure to acquire a site within 180 days). The franchisor has no termination-without-cause right (same FDA exception). With cause, curable defaults carry a 60-day cure (reports, financial data, bankruptcy, levies, acts impairing goodwill, lease defaults, insurance lapses, failure to operate, failure to maintain trade accounts, tax delinquencies, and any other default not listed in Section XV) and non-payment of fees carries a 10-day cure. Non-curable defaults include bankruptcy adjudication, assignment for creditors, abandonment, criminal misconduct, material misrepresentation, loss of possession, unauthorized assignment, unauthorized disclosure, failure to comply with mandatory standards, intentional understatement of gross sales, and repeated non-compliance.

**Post-termination obligations.** Payment of amounts due, complete de-identification, and compliance with the non-compete covenant.

**Noncompete during term.** No involvement in any competing business anywhere (subject to state law).

**Noncompete after termination or expiration — internal contradiction.** The Item 17 table stacks two different variants:
- Per the Franchise Agreement: no competing business for 1 year within a 5-mile radius of the designated area or within 5 miles of any other System location.
- Per the Franchise Deposit Agreement: no competing business for 1 year within a 50-mile radius of the designated area or within 50 miles of any other System location.
Both are marked "subject to state law." This is a material conflict that should be clarified before signing.

**Dispute resolution.** Either party may request binding arbitration of any dispute under the United States Arbitration Act and the rules of the American Arbitration Association at its office nearest to the franchisee's home office. Wisconsin law governs the Franchise Agreement. Wisconsin Franchise Investment Law and Wisconsin Fair Dealership Act claims (as amended by AB 218, Ch. 171, 1977 Laws) are NOT subject to arbitration. There is no choice-of-forum restriction.

**Integration / modification.** No modifications to the Franchise Agreement are allowed, but the Operations Manual may be changed unilaterally by RFC. The integration clause preserves the Franchise Agreement, Exhibits, and signed ancillary agreements as the enforceable terms; express representations in this FDD are not disclaimed.

**Litigation history.** None required to be disclosed in Item 3. None in Item 4 either.
