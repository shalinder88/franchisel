## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System Size**: 32 Brand Hotels operating as of December 31, 2024 (21 franchised, 11 company-owned/managed). Steady +1/year growth trajectory over 3 years. Zero terminations, zero non-renewals, zero closures in 3-year reporting period.
2. **Entity Structure**: Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C., a Delaware LLC, is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Hyatt Hotels Corporation (NYSE: H). Hyatt Corporation administers World of Hyatt and provides Mandatory Services. Rosemont Project Management provides optional purchasing services. Multiple international franchise affiliates.
3. **Ownership History**: Incorporated as Hyatt Franchise Corporation, converted to LLC under Delaware law. Has offered Hyatt Centric franchises since February 2015. Parent Hyatt Hotels Corporation has operated hotels since 1957.
4. **Investment Range**: $42.7M to $143.1M (excluding real estate) — among the highest-investment franchise concepts. Construction alone represents $30.9M-$113.4M. Typical hotel: 150-300 rooms in primary urban markets.
5. **Fee Burden**: Royalty 5% of GRR + Commercial Services Fee ($448/room/year + 3.0% GRR) + Digital Acquisition Fee (1.35% digital GRR) + World of Hyatt assessment (4.5% eligible revenue) + GDS fees (~$8.50/reservation) + mandatory contracts ($1.5K-$25K/month) + other corporate ($500-$15K/month). Total ongoing rooms-revenue-linked fees easily exceed 12% of gross rooms revenue before adding per-room and per-reservation charges.
6. **Item 19 Highlights**: Franchised hotels averaged 73.3% occupancy, $221.52 ADR, $162.49 RevPAR. Owned/managed hotels significantly outperform on ADR (+23%) and RevPAR (+24%). Smith Travel RevPAR Index for franchised hotels is 98.2, slightly below competitive set fair share. World of Hyatt drives 56.7% of franchised revenue.
7. **Bottom-Quartile Risk**: Wide performance dispersion — franchised RevPAR ranges from $79.59 to $276.96. Bottom-quartile franchisee at ~$100-$120 RevPAR faces severe economic pressure given the high fixed cost base. Only 40% of franchised hotels exceeded average RevPAR.
8. **Financial Health**: Parent Hyatt Hotels Corporation reported $6.6B total revenue, $1.3B net income, $13.3B total assets, $3.8B equity in FY2024. Performance guarantee is absolute and unconditional. Strong credit profile.
9. **Growth Trajectory**: Modest — 4 signed agreements not yet opened (CA 2, MD 1, SC 1), zero projected openings in next fiscal year. This suggests measured expansion rather than aggressive unit growth.
10. **Territory**: Area of Protection with AOP Term typically 1-5 years post-opening. After AOP Term expires, no protection. Expect Hyatt Network Hotels under other brands in your market.
11. **Renewal**: 10-year successor franchise on then-current terms that may be MATERIALLY DIFFERENT. Requires renovation, PIP fee, general release.
12. **Disputes**: Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, IL. Illinois law. Waiver of punitive damages and jury trial.
13. **Special Risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Illinois). No post-term noncompete, but substantial liquidated damages formula.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Approximately 95% of both establishment and operating purchases must be from franchisor-designated or approved sources, or per System Standards. This is an exceptionally high supplier control percentage.

Must use Opera PMS from designated vendor. Must use designated CRS, GDS, ADS. Must use designated cyber endpoint protection. Must participate in Avendra purchasing (optional but impracticable to avoid for Hyatt-specific items).

Supplier approval for non-approved sources: submit written request, typically 30-day response time, $20,000 testing fee.

FF&E Reserve: Must deposit 5% of total operating revenue monthly into separate account for capital expenditures and renovations.

### Training Program Detail
Seven-part initial training program totaling approximately 300+ hours:
1. Owner Briefing: 8 hours, 2 days (Chicago or virtual).
2. Orientation: 16 hours, 2 days (quarterly).
3. GMU: 53-58 hours (self-paced + virtual + 3-day in-person). Must complete 6 months before opening.
4. Operations Training: 47-79 hours classroom + 40-76 hours OJT.
5. Sales/Events/Revenue: 136.5 hours classroom.
6. Other Personnel: 17-34 hours + 15-36 hours OJT.
7. Pre-Opening Brand Training: 2-3 trainers onsite for minimum 7 nights.

Required ongoing training: 12-29 hours + 1-6 hours OJT (including annual retraining for human trafficking and privacy).

Replacement training for new Core Management must occur within 90 days.

### Technology Stack and Costs
12 required technology systems: PMS, POS, Revenue Management, Events/Lead Gen, Key Lock, PBX, In-Room Entertainment, HSIA, Guest Computing, Music, Digital Surveillance, Scent.

Installation cost: $1,508,950-$3,296,000.
Ongoing: PMS/POS vendor $3.90/room/month. Music $18.95/month. Additional technology costs included in Commercial Services Fee.

No limit on frequency or cost of required technology changes, upgrades, or updates. Franchisor has independent, unlimited access to all Technology System data.

### Marketing Fund Spending
No separate advertising fund. Marketing is included in the Commercial Services Fee (3.0% of GRR + $448/room/year). Franchisor will not prepare periodic accounting of how funds are spent. No franchisee advertising councils (may form/dissolve at franchisor's discretion).

Pre-opening: minimum spend of $500/room on pre-opening marketing program.

No cooperative advertising requirement, but voluntary cooperatives may be formed.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
Area of Protection (AOP): Boundaries negotiated before signing. Defined by geographic references or as a circle around the Hotel. Size varies from a few blocks (densely-populated urban) to about 1-2 mile radius (less populated areas).

### AOP Term and Carve-Outs
AOP Term begins at FA effective date and typically expires 1-5 years after Hotel opening. This is notably short for a 20-year franchise term.

During AOP Term: Franchisor will not open another Brand Hotel within AOP, with one critical exception — if franchisor acquires a group of 4+ hotels, one or more of which are in the AOP, those may be converted to Brand Hotels.

After AOP Term expires: No territorial protection whatsoever. Franchisor and affiliates may open unlimited Brand Hotels within former AOP.

### Channel Restrictions and Franchisor Rights
- No restrictions on where any Brand Hotel operator solicits customers.
- Franchisor may use internet, catalog, telemarketing, direct marketing in AOP without compensation.
- Expect Hyatt Network Hotels under other brands (25+ lodging brands) in your area.
- You may not use internet or any method other than CRS to accept reservations.
- You need franchisor approval for any website or online presence mentioning the Hotel.

### Encroachment Assessment
The combination of (a) short AOP Term, (b) broad franchisor reservation of rights after AOP, and (c) 25+ competing Hyatt brands creates significant encroachment risk. A prospective franchisee should negotiate the longest possible AOP Term and carefully evaluate the competitive landscape including all Hyatt-branded properties.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- Initial term: 20 years from 1st day of month Hotel opens.
- Successor franchise: 10 years on then-current terms.
- **Materially different risk**: The FDD explicitly states renewal terms "may be materially different" including fees. This is a significant risk — after investing $40M-$143M, the franchisee's ability to continue operating may depend on accepting terms that are substantially worse than the original agreement.
- Renewal conditions: Substantial compliance, current compliance, meet then-current new franchisee standards, passing QA scores for 3 years, premises rights for 10+ years, renovate, PIP fee, general release.

### Transfer Provisions
- No transfers of FA, Hotel, assets, or Controlling Ownership Interests without approval.
- Transfer fee: No separate fee, but must pay then-current Application Fee.
- Conditions: Transferee qualifies, training, sign then-current FA (may be materially different) or assumption, renovate, release.
- Non-controlling interests may transfer without approval if not to Competing Brand Owner.
- Death/disability: 6 months to transfer.
- Right of first offer: Only in "strategic markets" (Exhibit C).

### Termination and Death/Disability
- Franchisee cannot terminate (except as law allows).
- Franchisor terminates only for cause.
- Cure periods: 24 hours (health/safety), 10 days (payments/documents), 30 days (most defaults), 60 days (Guarantor Monetary Threshold).
- Non-curable: Bankruptcy, judgments >$100K, ceasing operations, unauthorized IP use, unauthorized transfer, felony, adverse actions, false records, repeated defaults.

### Noncompete Restrictions
- During term: No formal noncompete, but neither franchisee nor management company may be a "Competing Brand Owner" (entity that owns/franchises/manages a brand of 5+ hotels competing with Brand Hotels).
- Post-term: No noncompete restrictions.

### Dispute Resolution
- Mandatory arbitration within 10 miles of franchisor's principal address (currently Chicago, IL).
- Illinois law governs (except FAA and other federal law).
- Both parties waive rights to jury trial, punitive damages, exemplary damages, treble damages.
- Prevailing party receives costs and attorneys' fees.
- State overrides may apply (see state addenda).

### Litigation History
One case in FY2024: Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. v. Imperial Hotels, LLC et al. (AAA 2024) — unpaid royalty fees. This is a collections action, not a systemic dispute. Minimal litigation profile is a positive indicator.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

### Overview
The FDD provides three FPRs plus reservation channel mix data for the 32 Covered Hotels that operated during all of 2024 (out of 33 total Brand Hotels in North America). One hotel excluded because it opened during 2024.

Population: 20 Franchised, 12 Owned/Managed. Average room count: 219 (range 72-487). Average years operating: 10.1 years.

### First FPR: Occupancy, ADR, RevPAR

| Metric | Franchised (20) | Owned/Managed (12) | All (32) |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Avg Occupancy | 73.3% | 73.9% | 73.6% |
| Avg ADR | $221.52 | $273.13 | $240.92 |
| Avg RevPAR | $162.49 | $201.94 | $177.24 |

**Franchised Statistics:**
- Occupancy: 46.1%-92.8%, median 72.8%. 10/20 (50%) exceeded average.
- ADR: $126.34-$312.00, median $219.55. 10/20 (50%) exceeded average.
- RevPAR: $79.59-$276.96, median $150.45. **Only 8/20 (40%) exceeded average RevPAR.**

The 40% exceeding average on RevPAR indicates meaningful right-skew — a few high-performing hotels pull up the average.

### Second FPR: Smith Travel Indices

| Index | Franchised | Owned/Managed | All |
|-------|-----------|---------------|-----|
| Occupancy Rate Index | 103.5 | 104.8 | 104.0 |
| Rate Index | 95.2 | 101.6 | 97.6 |
| RevPAR Index | 98.2 | 106.8 | 101.4 |

Franchised Hyatt Centric hotels fill rooms slightly better than their competitive set (103.5 occupancy index) but underperform on pricing (95.2 rate index). This suggests the brand has good demand generation through the Hyatt network but may not command the premium pricing its comp set achieves. The net RevPAR Index of 98.2 means franchised hotels capture slightly less than fair share of the competitive set's RevPAR.

Owned/Managed hotels significantly outperform on all indices (106.8 RevPAR Index), suggesting operational advantages from Hyatt's direct management.

### Third FPR: World of Hyatt Contribution

| Metric | Franchised | All |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| % Revenue from WOH | 56.7% | 56.6% |
| % WOH Room Nights | 47.6% | 48.7% |
| Avg # WOH Room Nights | 26,707 | 27,816 |

World of Hyatt drives a majority of revenue (56.7% for franchised hotels). This represents a critical dependency on the loyalty program. The 4.5% WOH assessment is the cost of this demand engine.

### Reservation Channel Mix
- Franchised: 56.9% direct, 43.1% indirect.
- Direct channels (e-rooming list, GPGS voice, Hyatt.com/app) generate lower acquisition costs than indirect (GDS, OTAs).
- Indirect still represents a large share, meaning significant GDS and OTA commission costs.

### Critical Caveats
- **Revenue-only FPR**: No cost of sales, operating expenses, or profit data disclosed. A prospective franchisee must independently investigate operating costs.
- **Unaudited**: Figures based on internal data (except Smith Travel).
- **Performance dispersion**: The gap between the lowest-performing franchisee ($79.59 RevPAR) and the average ($162.49) is enormous. At 200 rooms, the bottom franchisee would generate roughly $5.8M in annual rooms revenue — which must cover a 12%+ fee load, substantial debt service on a $40M+ investment, and full operating costs.
- **Owned/Managed outperformance**: The 24% RevPAR premium of owned/managed hotels over franchised hotels is a notable gap that prospective franchisees should investigate.
- **Substantiation available**: Franchisor will provide written substantiation on request.
