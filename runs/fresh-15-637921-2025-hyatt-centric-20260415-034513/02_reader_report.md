# Reader Report: Hyatt Centric FDD (637921-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C., a Delaware LLC, subsidiary of Hyatt Hotels Corporation (NYSE: H). Incorporated as Hyatt Franchise Corporation, converted to LLC. Principal office: 150 N. Riverside Plaza, Chicago, IL 60606. Has offered franchises for Brand Hotels since February 2015.
2. **Concept**: Upscale, full-service lifestyle hotel brand. Typically 150-300 guest rooms with full F&B, banquet, and meeting facilities. Targets transient business and leisure guests seeking cosmopolitan hotel experience in exciting destinations.
3. **System Size**: 32 Brand Hotels operating at end of 2024 (21 franchised, 11 company-owned/managed). Steady growth: 29→30→31→32 over 2022-2024. Zero terminations, zero non-renewals in 3 years. 4 agreements signed but not yet opened.
4. **Investment Range**: $42,720,726 to $143,100,233 (excluding real estate/land). This is an exceptionally high-investment franchise. Of this, $279,406 to $1,099,432 is paid to franchisor/affiliate.
5. **Fee Burden**: Royalty 5% GRR + Commercial Services Fee ($448/room/year + 3.0% GRR) + Digital Acquisition Fee (1.35% of GRR via digital channels) + World of Hyatt assessment (4.5% eligible revenue) + GDS fees (~$8.50/reservation). Total ongoing fee load on rooms revenue is very substantial.
6. **Item 19 FPR**: All 32 Covered Hotels. Franchised: avg occupancy 73.3%, ADR $221.52, RevPAR $162.49. Owned/Managed outperform: 73.9% occ, $273.13 ADR, $201.94 RevPAR. Smith Travel RevPAR Index: 98.2 (franchised), 106.8 (owned/managed). WOH drives 56.6% of revenue.
7. **Term**: 20 years. Successor franchise for 10 years on then-current terms (may be materially different). Renewal requires renovation, PIP fee, general release (if state law allows).
8. **Territory**: Area of Protection with limited AOP Term (typically 1-5 years after opening). After AOP Term expires, no territorial protection. Exception: acquired groups of 4+ hotels. Nonexclusive in all other respects.
9. **Dispute Resolution**: Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, IL. Illinois law governs. Waiver of punitive damages. Waiver of jury trial.
10. **No Post-Term Noncompete**: No covenant not to compete post-termination. During term: cannot be Competing Brand Owner.
11. **Liquidated Damages**: Substantial — lesser of 60 months or remaining term × (5% + 3% of Average Monthly Revenue + $36.25/room adjusted by CPI). 150% multiplier for Consequential Termination.
12. **Parent Financial Health**: Hyatt Hotels Corporation — total revenue $6.6B (2024), net income $1.3B, total assets $13.3B, total equity $3.8B. Strong balance sheet. Performance guarantee provided.
13. **Litigation**: Minimal — one case in 2024 (unpaid royalty fees against Imperial Hotels). No bankruptcy.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- Must operate from approved site only.
- Area of Protection (AOP): Negotiated before signing. Boundaries via geographic references or radius. Size varies: few blocks (urban) to 1-2 mile radius (suburban).
- AOP Term: Begins at FA effective date, typically expires 1-5 years after opening.
- During AOP Term: Franchisor/affiliates will not open another Brand Hotel within AOP, EXCEPT: if acquiring a group of 4+ hotels with one or more in AOP.
- After AOP Term: No territorial protection whatsoever.
- Existing Brand Hotels may receive no AOP at all.
- No options, rights of first refusal for additional franchises.
- No sales quotas or market penetration requirements.
- Franchisor may use internet, catalog, telemarketing, direct marketing in AOP without compensation.
- Expect Hyatt Network Hotels under other brands in AOP now and in future.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
- Principal marks: Hyatt (Reg. 945,384), Hyatt Centric (Reg. 4,937,906), Hyatt Centric logo (4,942,723), "C" logo (4,942,724), World of Hyatt (5,201,881).
- All registered on Principal Register. All renewals filed.
- Licensed from Hyatt Corporation via Trademark License Agreement (perpetual term, terminable for cause).
- No material litigation or known infringing uses.
- Cannot use Proprietary Marks with restaurant operations without written consent.
- Must notify franchisor of any infringement; franchisor controls all proceedings.
- Franchisor may modify/discontinue marks without reimbursement.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
- No material patents.
- Copyright protection claimed for Copyrighted Materials (not registered with Copyright Office).
- Extensive Confidential Information definition (11 categories).
- Guest Information is franchisor's property. Franchisor has unlimited right to access PMS to retrieve Guest Information.
- Post-term: franchisee may retain Hotel-generated Guest Information from PMS if complying with data policies.
- Innovations belong to franchisor (work-for-hire).

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
- Must use franchisor-approved management company, or self-manage with approval.
- Franchisor may refuse Competing Brand Owner as management company.
- Core Management must devote full time to Hotel.
- Guaranty required from Controlling Ownership Interest holders.
- Guarantor Monetary Threshold: minimum net worth and liquid assets, increasing annually by CPI.
- F&B Operations and Spa Operations must comply; operators subject to franchisor approval and revocation.
- Revenue managers must be certified within 90 days, serve no more than 20 Brand Hotels.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on Sales
- Must offer all authorized products/services; may not sell unauthorized items.
- Mixed-Use Site restrictions: no pawnshops, check-cashing, gentlemen's clubs, pornography retailers, package liquor stores, marijuana dispensaries, gambling businesses without consent.
- Bar/lounge within 300 feet of Hotel requires approval if >50% revenue from alcohol.
- Franchisee sets room rates subject to System Standards and best rate guarantee compliance.
- Cannot change number of guest rooms without consent.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- Initial term: 20 years from 1st day of month Hotel opens.
- Successor franchise: 10 years on then-current terms (may be MATERIALLY DIFFERENT including fees).
- Renewal conditions: substantial compliance during term, current compliance, meet then-current standards, passing QA scores for 3 years, rights to premises for 10+ years, renovate Hotel, pay PIP fee, sign general release (if state law allows).

### Termination
- Franchisee may NOT terminate FA (except as law allows).
- Franchisor may NOT terminate without cause.
- Curable defaults: 24 hours (health/safety shutdown), 10 days (insurance/document failures, payment failures), 30 days (taxes, development deadlines, other defaults), 60 days (Guarantor Monetary Threshold).
- Non-curable: bankruptcy, judgments >$100K, ceasing operations, unauthorized IP actions, unauthorized transfer, felony conviction, adverse actions, false books, unauthorized disclosure, law violations, repeated defaults.

### Transfer
- No transfers without approval (FA, Hotel, assets, Controlling Ownership Interests).
- Non-Controlling Ownership Interest transfers without approval allowed IF transferee is not Competing Brand Owner or sanctioned person.
- Transfer conditions: transferee qualifies, pay transfer fee (Application Fee), training completion, sign then-current FA or assumption documents, renovate, you sign release.
- Death/disability: 6 months to transfer to approved transferee.
- Right of first offer: Only if Exhibit C signed (strategic markets).

### Noncompete
- During term: No formal noncompete, but cannot be Competing Brand Owner.
- Post-term: NO noncompete covenants.

### Dispute Resolution
- Mandatory arbitration, within 10 miles of franchisor's principal address (Chicago, IL).
- Illinois law governs (subject to Federal Arbitration Act and state law).
- Waiver of punitive, exemplary, treble, and other multiple damages.
- Waiver of jury trial.
- Prevailing party gets costs and attorneys' fees.

### Post-Termination Obligations
- De-identify Hotel, return confidential materials, change phone listings, stop references to association, cancel assumed name registrations, allow inspections, pay amounts owed, pay liquidated damages.
- $5,000/day royalty for failure to de-identify.
