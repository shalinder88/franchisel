# Reader Report — McDonald's USA, LLC (638437-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: McDonald's USA, LLC, a Delaware LLC, wholly-owned subsidiary of McDonald's Corporation. Principal office at 110 N. Carpenter Street, Chicago, IL 60607.
2. **System Size**: 13,559 total outlets at end of 2024 (12,887 franchised, 672 company-owned). Net +102 units in 2024. Approximately 95% franchised.
3. **Franchise Types**: Traditional (20-year term), Satellite (variable term), STO/STR (10-year term), BFL (3-year term with option to purchase).
4. **Initial Franchise Fee**: $45,000 traditional; $22,500 STO/STR; $500 Satellite (Walmart: $0).
5. **Royalty**: 5% of Gross Sales for new restaurants post-Jan 1, 2024; 4% for legacy/existing franchisee transactions.
6. **Total Initial Investment**: $1,471,000 to $2,728,000 (traditional); $1,014,500 to $1,793,500 (STO/STR); $525,000 to $1,193,500 (Satellite).
7. **Item 19 FPR**: Average annual sales $4,002,000 (all traditional); median $3,838,000. Pro-forma OIBOC at $3M sales = $734,000 (24.5%). **Critical**: Pro-forma uses 4% royalty; new franchisees pay 5%.
8. **Rent Structure**: Percentage Rent generally 6%-23% of Gross Sales for new restaurants; minimum 11.50% starting year 8. Plus Monthly Base Rent and Pass Thru Rent.
9. **Financial Health**: Total revenue $10.63B (2024). Net income $3.46B. Total assets $22.2B. Audited by Ernst & Young LLP, unqualified opinion. No going concern.
10. **Growth Trajectory**: Net +102 outlets in 2024 (+115 franchised, -13 company-owned). 181 projected new franchised openings + 14 company-owned. Active growth.
11. **Territory**: No exclusive territory. No protected territory. McDonald's may open competing restaurants anywhere.
12. **Renewal**: No right to renew or extend. New term at McDonald's sole discretion under New Term Policy (not contractual).
13. **Noncompete**: No competing business during term; 18 months / 10 miles post-term.
14. **Gag Clause**: Present. "In some instances, current and former franchisees sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with McDonald's." (p. 55)
15. **Special Risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Illinois).

## Pass B: Front Matter, Fees, and Investment (Items 1-7)

### Item 1 — Entity Structure
McDonald's USA, LLC is a Delaware LLC formed in 2004 by McDonald's Corporation. In 2005, McDonald's Corp transferred a majority of its U.S. business assets including McOpCo companies and franchises. The company develops, operates, franchises, and services a system of quick-service restaurants under the McDonald's System. About 95% franchised, 5% McOpCo. International affiliates offer franchises outside the U.S. (Exhibit Q). Puerto Rico/Virgin Islands sold to LatAm, LLC in 2007 (not an affiliate).

Restaurant types: Traditional (freestanding/storefront, 20 yr), Satellite (food courts/airports/malls/hospitals, variable term), STO (fuel station/convenience store combo, 10 yr), STR (anchors small retail center in rural communities, 10 yr), BFL (business facilities lease, 3 yr with option to purchase).

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Technology (Items 8-11)

### Item 8 — Restrictions on Sources
McDonald's does not sell equipment/food/supplies to franchisees (except ongoing business sales). All items must meet McDonald's specifications and be from approved suppliers. Supplier approval through Supply Chain Management with 6 criteria: quality consistency, confidentiality, production/delivery capability, integrity of ownership, financial soundness, Code of Conduct compliance.

McDonald's may decline new suppliers if sufficient approved suppliers exist. Insurance must be from carriers rated A+/A by AM Best with financial size IX+.

McDonald's leases premises to franchisee via Operator's Lease (incorporated into Franchise Agreement). Revenue from this leasing arrangement is detailed in Item 6.

Required purchases represent ~90-95% of establishment costs and ~55-65% of ongoing operating purchases.

In 2024, McDonald's and predecessor received $39M in loan guarantee fees, cashless incentives, and beverage supplier rebates. Total revenue $10.63B; real estate/services to franchisees $7.21B (68% of total).

### Item 9 — Franchisee's Obligations
Standard 24-item obligation table. Key cross-references: Site selection by McDonald's only (§1(a)(b)); Compliance via §§1(c),1(d),4,12; Fees via §§8(a),9; No territorial development quotas; No renewal right (§28(a)); Noncompete §§11(a)(b).

STO locations may require franchisee to maintain common areas and obtain insurance for fuel facility operator.

### Item 10 — Financing
Typically no financing from McDonald's. Operator's Lease is standard commercial lease. BFL may provide conditional option to purchase restaurant assets.

McDonald's predecessor may guarantee loans from Bank of America, N.A. Terms: SOFR + 0.10% + 3.00% (APR 7.42% as of 3/17/2025). Loan terms 3-5 years, 7-year amortization. Secured by equipment/seating/signage/decor/inventory. Personal + spousal guarantee required. Default = default under Franchise Agreement. Borrower must sign Operator Assistance Program Agreement (waives all claims against McDonald's). Jury trial waiver required.

### Item 11 — Training, Advertising, Computer Systems, Site Selection

**Training**: Candidate Development Program through Campus and Fred platforms. 6 months to 2 years to complete. Includes Hamburger University (HU) in Chicago. Key components:
- Restaurant Learning Path: Crew (83hr), Shift Leader (59hr), Area Mgmt (60hr), Dept Leader (94hr), Restaurant Leader, Leading Great Restaurants Class (31.75hr at HU, 4 days)
- Business Learning Path: Leading People (22hr), Finance/Pricing (12hr), Technology (14hr), Marketing (6hr), etc.
- Must complete all curricula including HU class before operating

Training head: Bethany Tate Cornell, SVP & Chief Learning/Development Officer (since Nov 2020, 30yr career at IBM/GE/Boeing).

**Advertising**: No McDonald's advertising fund. Franchisees must spend ≥4% of Gross Sales on advertising. OPNAD (national) + local co-ops are independent entities. OPNAD contribution currently 2.25% of sales. OPNAD is independently audited annually. McOpCo companies participate as members without controlling voting power.

**Computer Systems**: Store Systems ($150K-$250K) include POS (Sesame), Cashless 3.0, indoor/outdoor digital menu boards, kiosks, table locators. Transaction data transmitted to McDonald's with independent access. No contractual limits on frequency/cost of hardware/software upgrades.

**Site Selection**: McDonald's selects all sites. Franchisee does not select or approve sites.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
**No exclusive territory. No protected territory. No exclusive area.** Franchise is a limited grant to operate at a specific address only. McDonald's may establish other outlets that compete with franchisee's location. McDonald's reserves right to use Marks in any channel of distribution (e.g., McCafe coffee in supermarkets, e-commerce). Internal development policies are not contractual.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
Principal marks: Golden Arches Logo (Reg. 893,440), McDONALD'S (Reg. 743,572), McDonald's+Logo Sign Design (Reg. 1,287,408). McDonald's does not own marks — licensed from affiliate Restaurant Brands, LLC (20-year renewable license). No pending infringement or cancellation proceedings.

### Item 14 — Patents/Copyrights
No patents. Copyrights in O&T Manual and marketing materials (unregistered). Licensed from predecessor. Trade secrets in O&T Manual, real estate plans, marketing plans, recipes, formulae.

Financial Information disclosure restrictions: Cannot disclose operating results where McDonald's hasn't publicly disclosed for that period and info could be consolidated with other restaurants and made public to influence stock investment decisions.

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
Full time and best efforts required. Personal on-premises supervision of day-to-day operations.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on Sales
Only authorized McDonald's products. Only authorized packaging/ingredients/methods. McDonald's has unlimited right to add, delete, or change products. No customer restrictions.

## Pass E: Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- Traditional: 20 years. Satellite: varies. STO/STR: 10 years. BFL: 3 years.
- **No right to renew or extend.** New Term Policy (Exhibit K) is NOT part of Franchise Agreement and can be changed at McDonald's sole discretion. Not a contract right.
- BFL with option: may extend up to 20 years if conditions met and option exercised.

### Termination
- Franchisee cannot terminate.
- Franchisor can terminate only with cause (Sections 18, 19).
- **Curable defaults**: Not explicitly separated — cure period is embedded in the non-curable defaults.
- **Non-curable/Material Breaches**: Failure to maintain clean/wholesome restaurant; bankruptcy; non-payment within 30 days; judgment >$5,000 outstanding >30 days; loss of possession; violation of franchise restrictions; sale of unapproved/non-conforming foods; unauthorized transfer; denying McDonald's access; non-payment of undisputed invoices; ownership misrepresentation; conduct damaging reputation; felony conviction; intentional under-reporting of Gross Sales; repeated breaches.

### Post-Termination
30 days after termination at McDonald's request: sell furniture/fixtures/signs/equipment at fair market value (no payment for intangibles); return manuals/confidential materials; cease using McDonald's System and trademarks.

### Transfer
- Requires McDonald's approval.
- Conditions: New franchisee qualifies; royalty increases to current rate (5%); new franchisee assumes full unconditional liability; seller remains personally liable for remainder of term; no current breach.
- ROFR: McDonald's can match any offer for franchisee's business.
- Death/disability: Franchise may be assigned to approved purchaser or spouse/heirs/blood relative who is qualified. McDonald's may purchase business if managing restaurant 1 year after death/disability.

### Noncompete
- During term: No involvement in competing or similar business.
- Post-term: No competing business for 18 months within 10 miles (including after assignment/sale).

### Dispute Resolution
- No arbitration or mediation required by contract (used as common practice).
- Choice of law: Illinois.
- Choice of forum: Not specified in Franchise Agreement; Operator's Lease: Illinois.

### Key Operator's Lease Provisions
- Same term as Franchise Agreement. No renewal right.
- Termination triggers: Non-payment of rent; failure to submit reports; Franchise Agreement non-compliance; abandonment; bankruptcy.
- Post-termination: Remove equipment/fixtures; continue paying rent.
- Choice of law: Illinois.
