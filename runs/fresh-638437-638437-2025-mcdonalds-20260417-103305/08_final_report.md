## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: McDonald's USA, LLC is a Delaware limited liability company and a wholly-owned subsidiary of McDonald's Corporation. Its principal offices are at 110 N. Carpenter Street, Chicago, Illinois 60607. The entity was formed in 2004 and received the majority of McDonald's Corporation's U.S. business assets in 2005.

2. **System Size and Structure**: As of December 31, 2024, the McDonald's system in the United States comprised 13,559 total restaurants — 12,887 franchised (95%) and 672 company-owned (5%). The system grew by a net 102 outlets in 2024, with 167 new franchised openings and 14 new company-owned openings, offset by 73 terminations, 15 non-renewals, 8 reacquisitions, 3 company closures, and 32 sales to franchisees. The system projects 181 new franchised and 14 new company-owned openings in the next fiscal year.

3. **Franchise Types**: McDonald's offers five franchise types: Traditional (20-year term, $45K fee), Satellite (variable term, $500 fee), Small Town Oil/STO (10-year, $22.5K fee), Small Town Retail/STR (10-year, $22.5K fee), and Business Facilities Lease/BFL (3-year, $45K fee payable at option exercise).

4. **Investment Range**: Traditional restaurants require $1,471,000 to $2,728,000 total initial investment. STO/STR: $1,014,500 to $1,793,500. Satellite: $525,000 to $1,193,500.

5. **Fee Burden**: The ongoing fee stack is substantial. Royalty is 5% of Gross Sales for new restaurants (post-January 1, 2024) or 4% for legacy transactions. Rent includes Monthly Base Rent (based on McDonald's investment), Pass Thru Rent (lease escalation passthrough), and Percentage Rent (6%-23% of Gross Sales for new restaurants, with a minimum 11.50% starting in year 8). Advertising minimum is 4% of Gross Sales (through OPNAD and local cooperatives). Technology fees exceed $11,000 per year per restaurant.

6. **Item 19 Highlights**: Average annual sales for all traditional restaurants open at least one year: $4,002,000 (median $3,838,000). Pro-forma Operating Income Before Occupancy Costs (OIBOC) at $3.0M sales is $734,000 (24.5%). Critical caveat: the pro-forma uses a 4% royalty rate, but new franchisees pay 5%, creating an approximately $40,000 per year overstatement of OIBOC at average sales levels.

7. **Financial Health**: Total revenue $10.63 billion in 2024 (up from $10.57B in 2023). Net income $3.46 billion. Total assets $22.2 billion. Audited by Ernst & Young LLP with an unqualified opinion. No going concern language. The company paid $3.46 billion in dividends to its parent and $1.07 billion in royalty to parent. Total property and equipment (net) of $13.1 billion.

8. **Growth Trajectory**: Net +102 outlets in 2024 (the strongest growth year in the three-year reporting period), following +2 in 2023 and +19 in 2022. Projected 195 gross new openings. Transfer activity: 843 transfers in 2024, up from 672 in 2023.

9. **Territory**: No exclusive territory. No protected territory. McDonald's reserves the right to open competing outlets anywhere and use the marks in any distribution channel.

10. **Renewal**: Franchisees have no contractual right to renew or extend. The New Term Policy (Exhibit K) governs new term decisions but is explicitly not a contract right and can be changed at McDonald's sole discretion.

11. **Noncompete**: No competing business during the franchise term. Post-term: 18 months within 10 miles, including after assignment or sale.

12. **Disputes and Litigation**: Franchise agreement governed by Illinois law. No contractual arbitration or mediation requirement (mediation used as common practice). Seven material pending cases including antitrust no-poach class actions, racial discrimination suits by former franchisees, and consumer class actions. Notable concluded settlements include franchise purchases totaling tens of millions of dollars.

13. **Special Risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution in Illinois. Gag clause present — some franchisees have signed provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience.

14. **Contradictions**: Item 19 pro-forma computed at 4% royalty while new franchisees pay 5%. This is a structural FDD design issue, not an error, but it materially affects the comparability of the pro-forma for prospective buyers.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

McDonald's USA, LLC is the franchisor entity, a Delaware LLC wholly owned by McDonald's Corporation. The company develops, operates, franchises, and services McDonald's restaurants in the United States. McDonald's Corporation is the parent and predecessor; it began granting franchises in 1955. The U.S. business was transferred to McDonald's USA, LLC in 2005 as part of a global alignment. Puerto Rico and the Virgin Islands were sold to LatAm, LLC (not an affiliate) in 2007.

The McDonald's System encompasses trademarks, manuals, operational expertise, real estate, and marketing information. All U.S. restaurants operate under franchise agreements. The franchisor selects all sites and leases premises to franchisees — franchisees do not select or approve restaurant locations.

### Fee Stack — Initial Fees

The initial franchise fee for a traditional restaurant is $45,000, payable at opening. STO and STR locations pay $22,500. Satellite locations (non-Walmart) pay $500; Walmart locations pay no initial fee. BFL franchisees pay $45,000 when they exercise their purchase option. Rebuild and relocation franchisees receive a credit for the previously paid fee. The fee is fully refundable if construction is not completed within one year of signing; no refunds otherwise.

### Fee Stack — Ongoing Fees

The royalty structure underwent a significant change effective January 1, 2024. New restaurants, McOpCo sales, and ROFR exercise transactions now pay 5% of Gross Sales. Legacy transactions — family restaurant transfers, new terms on existing restaurants, franchisee-to-franchisee sales, and rebuilds — continue at 4%. This two-tier structure is a critical consideration for prospective franchisees.

Rent is the most complex and consequential fee component. For new and relocated traditional restaurants opening on or after January 14, 2026, the rent structure includes: (1) Monthly Base Rent based on McDonald's total investment with a finance factor applied; (2) Pass Thru Rent for any lease escalations McDonald's faces; and (3) Percentage Rent, which is the primary occupancy charge, generally ranging from 6% to 23% of Gross Sales. The Percentage Rent is designed to achieve a target allocation of returns between the franchisee and McDonald's. It generally increases during the first seven years and reaches a minimum of 11.50% in the eighth year. The total increase over the term generally does not exceed 3.50% from the first-year rate.

For new term franchises, the Percentage Rent will not be lower than the previous term's rate and may increase to 8.50% if currently below that level. McDonald's has adopted a co-investment policy allowing franchisees to reduce Percentage Rent in 0.25% increments by making additional investments (minimum $30,000 per Quarter).

Advertising: Franchisees must spend at least 4% of Gross Sales annually on advertising and promotion. McDonald's does not maintain an advertising fund. OPNAD (the national advertising cooperative) currently collects 2.25% of sales. Local cooperatives set their own rates. OPNAD is independently audited.

Technology fees are extensive. The mandatory annual technology fee burden exceeds $11,000 per restaurant, covering Sesame POS ($1,133/yr), Global Mobile App ($664), McDelivery POS ($620), Self-Ordering Kiosk ($558), Deployment/OTP/Support ($2,529), Network Management ($1,134), Hardware/Data ($951), RFM ($690), Microsoft License ($707), and numerous other line items. One-time fees for new restaurant deployment total approximately $5,475. All technology fees are subject to periodic increase.

Additional fees: Relocation contribution of $50,000 if relocating; Audit/Inspection fee (cost of audit) if Gross Sales understated by 2% or more; Satellite annual fee of $500-$2,500.

The effective rent range for franchised restaurants in 2024 was 0.00% to 33.33% — an extraordinarily wide range reflecting the diverse real estate situations across the system.

### Initial Investment

For traditional restaurants, the total initial investment ranges from $1,471,000 to $2,728,000. The largest component is Signs, Seating, Equipment, and Decor at $1,100,000 to $1,785,000 (includes Store Systems at $150,000-$250,000). Real estate and building (3 months' rent) ranges from $0 to $313,000. Additional funds for the first three months range from $250,000 to $439,000.

A notable disclosure: of 31 McOpCo restaurant sales in 2024, nine exceeded the high end of the investment range, including one that exceeded it by $2,035,816. This suggests that acquiring an existing high-performing restaurant from McDonald's may require substantially more capital than the Item 7 range indicates.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden

### Supplier Restrictions (Item 8)

McDonald's does not directly sell equipment, food, or supplies to franchisees (except in ongoing business sales). However, all items must be purchased from McDonald's-approved suppliers, and all items must meet McDonald's specifications. The supplier approval process requires: quality consistency, confidentiality agreements, production/delivery capability, ownership integrity, financial soundness, and Code of Conduct compliance. McDonald's may decline new suppliers if sufficient approved suppliers already exist.

Required purchases represent approximately 90-95% of establishment costs and 55-65% of ongoing operating purchases — an extremely high dependency on the McDonald's supply chain.

In 2024, McDonald's and its predecessor received $39 million in loan guarantee service fees, cashless incentives, and beverage supplier rebates. McDonald's total revenue was $10.63 billion, of which $7.21 billion (68%) came from the sale or lease of real estate and services to franchisees. This revenue concentration highlights the real estate-centric nature of the McDonald's franchise model.

### Training (Item 11)

The Candidate Development Program is comprehensive and demanding. It takes 6 months to 2 years to complete and includes both a Restaurant Learning Path (crew through restaurant leader positions) and a Business Learning Path (finance, marketing, technology, operations). The culminating event is the Leading Great Restaurants Class at Hamburger University in Chicago (31.75 hours, 4 days in-person). Training head is Bethany Tate Cornell (SVP & Chief Learning/Development Officer since November 2020).

Franchisees receive no compensation during training and are not McDonald's employees. McDonald's bears the cost of HU and training centers but franchisees pay all travel, living, and personal expenses. Ongoing training may require additional fees.

### Computer Systems

Store Systems cost $150,000-$250,000 per restaurant and include POS (Sesame), Cashless 3.0, digital menu boards, kiosks, and table locators. Transaction-level data is transmitted to McDonald's which has independent access with no contractual limits. There are no contractual limits on the frequency or cost of hardware/software upgrades — a meaningful open-ended obligation.

### Advertising

McDonald's does not maintain an advertising fund and is not required to spend any amount to advertise franchisee restaurants. Franchisees must independently ensure they spend at least 4% of Gross Sales on advertising. OPNAD and local cooperatives are independent entities that franchisees may voluntarily join, though consistent involvement is measured as part of National Franchising Standards compliance.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

McDonald's provides no exclusive territory, no protected territory, and no exclusive area. The franchise is a limited grant to operate at a single specific address. McDonald's may establish other outlets — franchised or company-owned — that directly compete with a franchisee's location. McDonald's also reserves the right to use the marks in any distribution channel, including supermarket and e-commerce sales (e.g., McCafe packaged coffee). Internal development policies that McDonald's may apply to site decisions are explicitly not part of the franchise agreement and create no contractual right.

This is one of the most aggressive territory provisions in major franchise systems. The franchisee has no protection against encroachment and no mechanism to object to new restaurant development that may cannibalize sales at their existing location.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

Traditional franchise terms are 20 years. STO/STR terms are 10 years. Satellite terms vary. BFL terms are 3 years with an option to purchase and extend.

The renewal structure is perhaps the single most significant buyer-risk factor in this FDD. **Franchisees have no right to renew or extend.** The New Term Policy (Exhibit K) describes McDonald's current approach to granting new terms, but it is explicitly stated to not be part of the Franchise Agreement, to be subject to change at McDonald's sole discretion, and to not constitute a contract right. If McDonald's does not grant a new term, the franchisee has the opportunity to sell the franchise during the remaining term. A qualified purchaser may then enter into a new-term agreement.

Under a BFL, if the conditional purchase option is met and exercised, the franchise may extend up to 20 years.

### Termination

The franchisee cannot terminate. McDonald's can terminate only with cause. Material breaches include fifteen enumerated grounds: failure to maintain restaurant standards, bankruptcy, non-payment within 30 days of due date, outstanding judgment exceeding $5,000 for more than 30 days, loss of possession, franchise restriction violations, sale of unapproved or non-conforming foods, unauthorized transfer, denying McDonald's access, non-payment of undisputed invoices, ownership misrepresentation, conduct damaging McDonald's reputation, felony conviction, intentional under-reporting of Gross Sales, and repeated other breaches.

Post-termination, the franchisee must (within 30 days at McDonald's request) sell furniture, fixtures, signs, and equipment to McDonald's at fair market value (with no payment for intangible assets), return all business manuals and confidential materials, and cease using the McDonald's System and trademarks.

### Transfer

All transfers require McDonald's approval. Conditions include: the new franchisee meets qualification standards, royalty increases to the current rate (5% for post-2024 transactions), the new franchisee assumes full unconditional liability, the seller remains personally liable for the remainder of the term, and there is no current breach.

McDonald's has a right of first refusal — it can match any offer for the franchisee's business. On death or disability, the franchise may be assigned to an approved purchaser or qualified spouse/heir/blood relative. If McDonald's has been managing the restaurant for one year after death or disability, it may purchase the business.

### Noncompete

During the term: no involvement in any competing or similar business. Post-term: no competing business for 18 months within 10 miles of any McDonald's restaurant, including after assignment or sale. This is a moderately broad noncompete.

### Dispute Resolution

No contractual arbitration or mediation requirement. McDonald's uses mediation as common practice through an internal Ombudsman process and open-door policy. The franchise agreement is governed by Illinois law. The Operator's Lease specifies Illinois as the forum.

### Death/Disability

McDonald's will work with a surviving spouse for up to 18 months to become qualified — but this is a common practice, not a contractual obligation.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

McDonald's provides one of the more informative Item 19 disclosures in the QSR industry, with both distribution data and pro-forma operating statements.

### Sales Volume Distribution

For all 12,572 domestic traditional restaurants open at least one year as of December 31, 2024, the average annual sales volume was $4,002,000 and the median was $3,838,000. The range spans from $914,000 to $19,680,000 — a 21:1 ratio indicating extreme variance in unit economics.

For the 12,023 franchised traditional restaurants specifically, average sales were $3,966,000 (median $3,797,000). McOpCo restaurants averaged $4,793,000 (median $4,612,000) — approximately 21% higher than franchised units. This persistent McOpCo premium suggests that company-selected and company-operated locations may systematically outperform, though selection bias (McDonald's retains high-performing locations) is a likely factor.

Approximately 78% of franchised restaurants exceeded $3 million in annual sales, 71% exceeded $3.2 million, and 64% exceeded $3.4 million.

### Pro-Forma Operating Statement

Based on 11,332 independent franchisee traditional restaurants (excluding McOpCo, Satellites, and ownership-change restaurants), the pro-forma at three sales tiers shows:

At $3.0M sales: Cost of sales 28.3%, other operating expenses 47.2%, OIBOC $734,000 (24.5%).
At $3.2M sales: Cost of sales 28.2%, other operating expenses 46.5%, OIBOC $812,000 (25.4%).
At $3.4M sales: Cost of sales 28.1%, other operating expenses 46.1%, OIBOC $879,000 (25.9%).

Approximately 76% of the sample had OIBOC greater than $734,000; 69% exceeded $812,000; 62% exceeded $879,000.

### Critical Cohort Comparability Warning

**The pro-forma royalties were computed at 4% of Gross Sales.** However, new franchisees entering the system after January 1, 2024 pay 5% royalty. This 100 basis point difference is material:

- At $3.0M sales: additional royalty burden = $30,000/year
- At $3.2M sales: additional royalty burden = $32,000/year
- At $3.4M sales: additional royalty burden = $34,000/year
- At $4.0M average sales: additional royalty burden = $40,000/year

This means the OIBOC figures in the pro-forma are overstated for prospective new franchisees by these amounts. For a restaurant at the average sales level, the adjusted OIBOC would be approximately $40,000 lower than what the pro-forma suggests, before accounting for rent and other occupancy costs.

Additionally, rent is excluded from OIBOC. With effective rent percentages ranging from 0% to 33.33% in 2024, the actual cash flow available to a franchisee after all occupancy costs can vary dramatically.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### Multi-Year Trajectory

The McDonald's U.S. system has shown accelerating growth over the reporting period. After a modest net increase of 19 outlets in 2022 and near-flat performance (+2) in 2023, the system grew by a net 102 outlets in 2024. This acceleration was driven primarily by franchised outlet growth: 167 new franchised openings in 2024 versus 128 in 2023 and 84 in 2022.

Company-owned outlets decreased from 691 (end 2023) to 672 (end 2024), continuing a deliberate refranchising strategy. In 2024, 32 company-owned outlets were sold to franchisees while only 8 were reacquired from franchisees, and 14 new company-owned outlets opened.

### Transfer Activity

Transfer volume was 843 in 2024, up significantly from 672 in 2023 (which was itself down from 1,169 in 2022). The 2022 spike in transfers (1,169) may reflect post-pandemic portfolio repositioning. The FDD notes that transfers include "spin" transactions in which McDonald's acquires from one franchisee and immediately sells to another without operating.

Top transfer states in 2024: Texas (77), Illinois (68), California (64), Florida (55), Georgia (49), Arizona (43), Pennsylvania (40).

### Terminations and Non-Renewals

Terminations totaled 73 in 2024 (up from 33 in 2023). Non-renewals were 15 in 2024 (down from 29 in 2023). The FDD states that substantially all terminations and non-renewals result from closings by mutual agreement.

Only 8 franchised outlets were reacquired by the franchisor in 2024 (down from 13 in 2023 and 47 in 2022). The large reacquisition count in 2022 (47) likely included strategic portfolio consolidation.

### Projected Openings

McDonald's projects 181 new franchised outlets and 14 new company-owned outlets, for a total of 195 gross new openings. Texas leads with 50 projected franchised openings, followed by Florida (11), California (10), and Arizona (7). No franchise agreements were signed but not yet opened as of year-end.

### Franchisee Organizations

Six organizations are endorsed by McDonald's: NFLA (National Franchisee Leadership Alliance), AMOA (Asian), MHOA (Hispanic), NBMOA (Black), WON (Women), and OPN (Operator Pride). The independent National Owners Association (NOA) has asked to be included.

### Gag Clause

**McDonald's discloses a gag clause**: "In some instances, current and former franchisees sign provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experience with McDonald's." The scope of this restriction is unknown — neither the number of affected franchisees nor the specific terms of the restriction are disclosed. This is a material consideration for due diligence, as prospective franchisees may be unable to obtain candid feedback from certain current and former operators.

## H. Item 21 — Financial Statements

### Auditor and Opinion

The consolidated financial statements of McDonald's USA, LLC were audited by Ernst & Young LLP, Chicago, Illinois. The opinion, dated March 14, 2025, is unqualified. There is no going-concern language. The audit covers the years ended December 31, 2024, 2023, and 2022.

### Income Statement

McDonald's USA, LLC generated total revenues of $10,630.8 million in 2024, up from $10,568.4 million in 2023 and $9,588.4 million in 2022. The revenue mix is heavily weighted toward franchised restaurants: revenues from franchised restaurants were $7,210.6 million (68% of total), comprising $5,170.7 million in rents, $2,014.5 million in royalties, and $25.4 million in initial fees. Company-owned restaurant sales were $3,196.9 million. Other revenues (technology platform fees, brand licensing) were $223.3 million.

Total operating costs and expenses were $4,897.8 million, yielding operating income before royalty to parent of $5,733.0 million. After a $1,069.4 million royalty payment to McDonald's Corporation (2% of all restaurant sales), operating income was $4,663.6 million. Interest expense (net of capitalized interest) was $159.1 million. Income before taxes was $4,506.5 million. The provision for income taxes was $1,044.9 million (effective rate 23.2%). Net income was $3,461.6 million.

The company distributed its entire net income as dividends to McDonald's Corporation: $3,461.6 million in 2024, $3,394.4 million in 2023, and $3,095.7 million in 2022.

### Balance Sheet

Total assets were $22,195.3 million as of December 31, 2024. The asset base is dominated by property and equipment (net $13,080.4 million, of which $11.5 billion is under franchise arrangements). Lease right-of-use assets were $5,841.4 million. Goodwill was $1,498.7 million. Cash was only $34.6 million.

Total current liabilities were $4,054.4 million, including $3,310.6 million due to parent (net intercompany payable). Long-term lease liability was $5,849.0 million. Deferred income taxes were $1,416.7 million. Members' equity totaled $10,222.0 million ($5,588.1 million members' capital + $4,633.9 million retained earnings).

### Cash Flow

Cash provided by operations was $4,552.4 million in 2024. Property and equipment expenditures (capex) were $1,055.2 million. The company purchased restaurant businesses for $135.4 million and received $151.1 million from sales. Dividends to parent consumed $3,461.6 million. Net cash decrease was $7.9 million.

### Key Financial Observations

**Leverage and parent dependency**: McDonald's USA, LLC had $2,400.0 million in payable arrangements with McDonald's Corporation ($1,500M and $900M credit lines). These bore interest at the prevailing 12-month swap rate + 1% and matured February 17, 2025. Per the subsequent events note, these payables were converted to equity after year-end, eliminating the obligation.

**Related party structure**: The company pays its parent a 2% royalty on all restaurant sales ($1,069.4M in 2024). It also distributes 100% of net income as dividends. The parent provides certain administrative functions without charge (not reflected in results). The parent has retained legal title to certain property and equipment assets used by the company under intercompany agreements.

**Real estate dominance**: Net property and equipment under franchise arrangements totaled $11.5 billion (including $3.9 billion in land), after $8.4 billion in accumulated depreciation. Future minimum rent payments due to the company from franchise arrangements total $14.159 billion.

**Lease portfolio**: Total lease liabilities were $6,044.9 million (operating $5,262.1M + finance $782.8M). Weighted-average remaining lease term was 17 years (operating) and 28 years (finance). Total future lease payments (undiscounted) were $9.002 billion.

**Restructuring**: The Accelerating the Organization initiative (announced January 2023) resulted in $63.6 million in total restructuring charges since inception, primarily employee termination benefits. The accrued balance at December 31, 2024 was $4.4 million.

**Goodwill**: $1,498.7 million, primarily from purchases of restaurants from franchisees. In 2024, acquisitions recorded ~$10M net tangible assets, ~$110M identifiable intangibles, and ~$20M goodwill. No impairment identified.

**Income taxes**: Effective rate 23.2% (2024). Gross unrecognized tax benefits of $75.8 million. The company is included in McDonald's consolidated federal return.

## I. State Addenda Summary

Six states have specific addenda in Exhibit T:

**California**: The California Fast Food Act (AB 1228) may impose health, safety, and employment standards including minimum wages. McDonald's provides no training, assistance, or fee offset related to compliance. The Additional Funds estimate in Item 7 accounts for increased labor costs. Compliance is solely the franchisee's responsibility.

**Hawaii**: Standard anti-waiver provision — no signed questionnaire or acknowledgement may waive claims under applicable franchise law or disclaim reliance on franchisor statements.

**Maryland**: Five specific overrides: (1) Any release required for renewal/transfer does not apply to Maryland Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law liability; (2) Termination upon insolvency may not be enforceable under federal bankruptcy law; (3) Franchisee may bring Maryland Franchise Law claims in Maryland; (4) Maryland law applies for Maryland Franchise Law claims; (5) Claims must be brought within 3 years of franchise grant.

**Minnesota**: McDonald's must protect franchisees against trademark infringement claims. Requires 90 days notice of termination (60 days to cure) and 180 days notice of non-renewal. Transfer consent will not be unreasonably withheld. Nothing abrogates Minnesota Statutes Chapter 80C.

**North Dakota**: Post-term noncompete covenants are generally unenforceable. North Dakota law governs claims under the North Dakota Franchise Investment Law.

**Washington**: Washington Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails over inconsistent franchise agreement terms if applicable by its terms. Additionally, McDonald's entered an Assurance of Discontinuance with the Washington Attorney General removing the no-poach/no-solicitation provision from franchise agreements, agreeing not to enforce such provisions in existing agreements, and requesting franchisees amend agreements accordingly. No fines or penalties were assessed.

These state-level overrides are material. In Minnesota, the effective termination protection is significantly stronger than the base franchise agreement. In North Dakota, the 18-month/10-mile noncompete is likely unenforceable. In Maryland, the release provisions on renewal/transfer are limited. In Washington, the no-poach restriction has been removed.
