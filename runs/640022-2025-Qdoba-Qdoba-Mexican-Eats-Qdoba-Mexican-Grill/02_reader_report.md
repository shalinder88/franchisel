# 02 Reader Report — Qdoba Franchisor LLC (640022-2025)

## A. Executive Snapshot

Qdoba Franchisor LLC is a Delaware limited liability company formed on September 7, 2023, as part of a securitization transaction involving the Qdoba brand. The franchisor's principal address is 350 Camino De La Reina, Suite 400, San Diego, California 92108. The FDD is dated December 2025 (fiscal year ended September 28, 2025).

As of September 28, 2025, the system comprised 827 restaurants (175 company-operated, 652 franchised) across 45 U.S. states, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, Japan, South Korea, and Canada. The brand operates in the fast-casual Mexican food segment, competing with national chains such as Chipotle, Moe's Southwest Grill, and local independents. The system showed net growth of +50 restaurants in FY2025 (57 opened, 1 non-renewal, 8 reacquired by franchisor, 9 ceased operations among franchisees; +9 opened, 8 reacquired, -3 closed among company-owned).

The franchisor entity (Qdoba Franchisor LLC) was organized as a special-purpose vehicle for the securitization. It does not directly operate restaurants. Qdoba Restaurant Corporation (QRC), a predecessor and affiliate, provides management services under a management agreement. QRC is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Qdoba Corporation, which is owned by New MRC Holdings LLC, an entity connected to Butterfly Equity LP.

Key risk factors identified: out-of-state dispute resolution (California), spousal liability for guarantees, securitization-driven corporate structure separating franchise operations from restaurant management, CID-encoded financial statements for the Funding Holdco entity reducing transparency, and the franchise system's aggressive growth trajectory (447 to 652 franchised units in 3 years, largely via refranchising).

## B. Pass 1 — Front Matter, Formats, Entry Structure, Fee Stack, Initial Investment

**Formats offered:** Traditional Qdoba restaurant (1,500–3,000 sq ft, 15–80 seats) and Non-Traditional locations (military bases, universities, airports, etc. via License Agreement).

**Development structure:** Development Agreements grant rights to develop one or more restaurants within a Development Area. Minimum development commitment is 1 restaurant. Development fee is $10,000 per site (credited toward franchise fee).

**Fee stack — Initial:**
- Franchise Fee: $40,000 (traditional), $20,000 (non-traditional). Prorated if term < 10 years.
- Development Fee: $10,000 per site (credited to franchise fee).
- During last fiscal year, developers paid $0 to $10,000 per site.

**Fee stack — Recurring:**
- Royalty: 5% of Gross Sales (traditional FA); 3% (License Agreement).
- Marketing Fund: 4.5% of Gross Sales (traditional); 1.75% (License). Maximum 6% requires majority system vote. In FY2025, combined marketing/local advertising was 4.0%; increased to 4.5% effective September 29, 2025.
- Technology Fee: $250 per restaurant per period (13 periods/year) plus 0.21% of Gross Sales. Subject to modification with 90 days notice.
- Transfer Fee: $25,000 (traditional), $10,000 (non-traditional/license).
- Renewal Fee: $10,000.
- Audit Fee: Cost of audit if underreported by 3%+ of Gross Sales.
- Learning Management System: $24–$40 per restaurant per month.
- Additional Training: Up to $1,600 per trainee.
- New Restaurant Training Support: $3,500 per trainer (3rd+ restaurant).
- Inspection: Cost of follow-up inspection if failed.

**Estimated Initial Investment (traditional):**
- Total: $548,100 to $1,294,000 (includes $40,000 to franchisor/affiliates).
- Non-traditional: $234,500 to $898,000 (includes $20,000 to franchisor/affiliates).
- Under Development Agreement (1 restaurant): $549,100 to $1,299,000.

**Cash incentive program:** Up to $100,000 per restaurant for early openings or incremental units beyond committed, limited to first 150 qualifying restaurants. Expiration: September 27, 2026 for agreements signed before June 15, 2025; 24 months from signing for agreements after June 15, 2025.

## C. Pass 2 — Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

**Supplier control:** Franchisees must purchase food products, paper, packaging, supplies, fixtures, equipment, and computer systems meeting Qdoba's specifications from approved suppliers or meet approved specifications. Qdoba may itself be an approved supplier. The franchisor did not receive any revenue from products and services purchased or leased by franchisees in the most recent fiscal year (per Item 8 disclosure).

Alternative suppliers may be requested but franchisee bears cost of Qdoba's evaluation and approval process, including travel, lodging, and meals.

**Technology burden:** Comprehensive technology stack required:
- POS system (Windows-based, PA-DSS compliant) with integrated credit card processing, loyalty system, online ordering, catering system, kitchen display, inventory/labor management.
- Master Technology Agreement (Exhibit K): $250/restaurant/period + 0.21% of Gross Sales.
- Hardware firewall, structured cabling, internet connectivity (minimum specifications defined).
- Required to participate in third-party delivery platforms and online ordering as directed.
- Qdoba retains right to modify technology requirements with no stated limits on capital obligations.

**Reporting burden:** Franchisees must submit financial statements via Qdoba's online portal. Required to maintain standardized chart of accounts, statement of earnings, and balance sheet as prescribed. POS data transmitted to franchisor. Qdoba has independent access to franchisee's bank accounts via ACH/prearranged payment authorization (Exhibit L).

**Insurance requirements:** Commercial general liability ($2M/$4M aggregate, $5M for nontraditional), business auto ($1M), workers' comp as required, employer's liability ($1M), all-risk property, builders' all risk.

## D. Pass 3 — Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

**Territory structure:** No exclusive territory under either Development Agreement or Franchise Agreement. A "Protected Territory" may be granted under the Franchise Agreement, generally a 2-mile radius from the restaurant, but may be smaller or larger. Under the Development Agreement, a "Development Area" is granted with protection during the DA term.

**Carveouts and encroachment risks:**
- Franchisor retains right to allow pre-existing restaurants to operate, be transferred, or renewed within protected territories.
- Franchisor may sell products/services through dissimilar channels of distribution within territories.
- Non-traditional locations (military, university, airport, hospital, etc.) may be operated within protected territories.
- Franchisor retains unrestricted right to operate or franchise outside any protected territory.
- Loss of development fee credit if development schedule not met; material default allows immediate termination of Development Agreement.

**Competition:** Franchisor states it does not currently operate or franchise any business selling primarily Mexican food under a non-Qdoba trademark. However, Butterfly Equity LP affiliates may hold investments in competing restaurant enterprises.

## E. Pass 4 — Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

**Term:** Franchise Agreement: 10 years. Development Agreement: varies by commitment.

**Renewal:** One renewal for 10 years. Conditions: 6–18 months advance notice, not in default, meet current standards, sign then-current form of FA (which may have materially different terms), pay $10,000 renewal fee, remodel if required, sign general release.

**Transfer:** Permitted with franchisor consent (not unreasonably withheld). Conditions include: transferee meets qualifications, transferee signs then-current FA, $25,000 transfer fee, training completion, general release, right of first refusal for franchisor.

**Termination by franchisor:** Immediate for: bankruptcy/insolvency, abandonment, material misrepresentation, criminal conviction, imminent danger to public health/safety, repeated defaults (3+ in 12 months). With 30-day cure for other material defaults. With 60-day cure for failure to meet performance standards.

**Non-compete:** During term: no competing Mexican food restaurant. Post-term: 2 years within 10 miles of any Qdoba restaurant (including former restaurant). Post-transfer: 2 years within 10 miles.

**Dispute resolution:** Mediation first, then binding arbitration in San Diego, California (JAMS rules). Waiver of jury trial and punitive damages.

**Governing law:** California.

**Guaranty requirement:** If franchise is held by entity, owners and spouses must personally guarantee all obligations regardless of ownership interest. This is highlighted as a special risk in the front matter.
