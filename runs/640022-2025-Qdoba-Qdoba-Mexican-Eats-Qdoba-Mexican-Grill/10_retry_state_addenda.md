# RT-02: State-Specific Addenda Recovery — Exhibit H

## Overview
Exhibit H (pages 333-409) contains state-specific addenda for 11 states, modifying the FDD, Franchise Agreement, License Agreement, and Development Agreement to comply with state franchise laws.

## State-by-State Material Overrides

### California (pages 335-339)
- General release at renewal/transfer subject to Cal. Corp. Code §31512 (cannot waive franchise investment law rights)
- Franchise relations act (Bus. & Prof. Code §20000-20043) provides termination/transfer protections
- Post-term non-compete may not be enforceable under California law
- Termination upon bankruptcy may not be enforceable under federal bankruptcy law
- Maximum interest rate: 10% annually
- Registration does not constitute state endorsement

### Hawaii (pages 340-347)
- No waiver of claims under state franchise law (including fraud in inducement)
- Registration effective in multiple states; none refused/revoked/suspended
- FDD addendum and agreement amendments for FA, LA, DA

### Illinois (pages 348-355)
- Illinois law applies if jurisdiction requirements of Illinois Franchise Disclosure Act of 1987 are met (overrides California choice of law for IL franchisees)
- Applies to FA, LA, and DA choice-of-law provisions
- FDD addendum and agreement amendments for FA, LA, DA

### Maryland (pages 356-363)
- Termination upon bankruptcy may not be enforceable under federal bankruptcy law
- Maryland Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law claims exempt from choice of law/forum provisions
- No waiver of claims under state franchise law (including fraud in inducement)
- FDD addendum and agreement amendments for FA, LA, DA

### Michigan (pages 364-366)
- Comprehensive list of void/unenforceable provisions under Michigan Franchise Investment Law (MCL 445.1527):
  - Cannot require litigation/arbitration outside Michigan
  - Cannot require waiver of jury trial or consent to liquidated damages
  - Cannot refuse transfer except for good cause (specific criteria defined)
  - Cannot require resale of non-uniquely-identified items
  - Cannot directly/indirectly convey, assign, or transfer franchise without franchisee consent
- FA amendment only (3 pages)

### Minnesota (pages 367-374)
- Minn. Stat. §80C.21 prohibits: requiring litigation outside MN, waiver of jury trial, liquidated damages, termination penalties, judgment notes
- Cannot abrogate franchisee rights under MN Chapter 80C
- 3-year statute of limitations under §80C.17
- No waiver of claims including fraud in inducement
- FDD addendum and amendments for FA, LA, DA

### New York (pages 375-382)
- Article 33 of General Business Law rights preserved
- GBL §§687(4) and 687(5) non-waiver provisions apply
- Franchisee may terminate on any grounds available by law
- Choice of law/forum does not waive GBL Article 33 rights
- No waiver of claims including fraud in inducement
- FDD addendum and amendments for FA, LA, DA

### North Dakota (pages 383-389)
- ND Securities Commissioner deems following provisions unfair/unjust/inequitable:
  - Restrictive covenants contrary to §9-08-06 NDCC
  - Arbitration at remote location from franchisee's business
  - Jurisdiction outside North Dakota
  - Liquidated damages and termination penalties
  - Choice of law other than North Dakota for ND franchise law claims
  - Waiver of jury trial
  - Waiver of exemplary and punitive damages
- FDD addendum and amendments for FA, LA, DA

### Rhode Island (pages 390-393)
- RI Franchise Investment Act §19-28.1-14: venue/jurisdiction restriction outside RI and choice of law to another state void for claims under RI Act
- FDD addendum and amendments for FA, LA, DA

### Virginia (page 394)
- Brief addendum (1 page)
- Standard state registration provisions

### Washington (pages 395-409)
- Comprehensive Franchise Investment Protection Act (RCW 19.100) protections:
  - WA law prevails in conflict of laws
  - Franchisee Bill of Rights (RCW 19.100.180) may supersede agreement terms
  - Arbitration/mediation/litigation must be in WA or mutually agreed location
  - General release at renewal/transfer limited to exclude claims under WA Act
  - Transfer fees limited to reasonable estimated/actual costs
  - Franchisee may terminate under any state law grounds
  - Buy-back provisions without consent unlawful unless termination for good cause
  - Products/services must be at fair and reasonable price
  - Cannot waive exemplary/punitive damages (treble damages available per RCW 19.100.190)
  - Franchisor must exercise discretion in good faith (RCW 19.100.180(1))
  - Indemnification provisions modified
- Most extensive state addenda in this FDD (15 pages covering FDD, FA, LA, DA)

## Key Themes Across States
1. **Choice of law/forum override:** CA, IL, MD, MI, MN, NY, ND, RI, WA all limit or override California choice of law/forum to some degree
2. **Non-compete limitations:** CA, ND, WA raise enforceability concerns
3. **Jury trial waiver voided:** MI, MN, ND
4. **Punitive damages waiver voided:** ND, WA
5. **Transfer restrictions:** MI requires "good cause" standard
6. **General release limitations:** CA, WA limit scope
7. **No waiver of franchise law claims:** HI, MD, MN, NY
