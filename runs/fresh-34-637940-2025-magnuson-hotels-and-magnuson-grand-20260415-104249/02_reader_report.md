# Reader Report — Magnuson Hotels and Magnuson Grand (637940-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: Magnuson Franchising, LLC, a Wyoming LLC formed March 2021; parent is Magnuson Company, L.L.C. (WY LLC, est. 2002). Began franchising January 2022.
2. **Brands**: Magnuson Hotels® (standard hotels) and Magnuson Grand® (hotels with restaurants, spa, convention centers).
3. **Conversion model**: Franchises offered only to existing hotel operators converting their "Incumbent Hotel" to a Branded Hotel. No greenfield development.
4. **System size**: 11 franchised outlets at end of 2024 (up from 0 in 2021). Zero company-owned. Zero projected openings for 2025. One post-FY termination (TX). Two left the system in FY2024 (1 TX terminated, 1 IL terminated without opening).
5. **Key personnel**: Melissa Magnuson (Chair, co-founder since 2002); Adnan Malik (CEO since Dec 2023, with company since Aug 2018).
6. **Investment range**: $175,000–$400,000 (excludes real estate/occupancy). Initial franchise fee $10,000.
7. **Fee burden**: 8% continuing fee on Gross Room Revenue + GDS booking fees ($11.50/booking) + third-party distribution channel fees (10-20%) + Airbnb connection ($100-260/mo) + minimum invoice revenue ($250/mo).
8. **Item 19**: No financial performance representations made.
9. **Financial health**: VERY SMALL entity. Total assets $30,886 (2024). Revenue $259,560 (2024). Net income $55,077. Member distributions $50,799. Cash $19,493. Special risk flag: "financial condition calls into question franchisor's financial ability to provide services and support."
10. **Territory**: NO exclusive territory. Franchisor retains all rights to place competing hotels anywhere.
11. **Term**: 5 years initial, auto-renewal for one additional 5-year term. Renewal may require signing new agreement with materially different terms.
12. **Non-compete**: NONE — no non-competition covenants during or after the term.
13. **Dispute resolution**: Binding arbitration via AAA, venue within 50 miles of Casper, WY. Wyoming governing law. Waiver of punitive damages and jury trial. 1-year statute of limitations for claims. Class action waiver.
14. **Special risks flagged**: (1) WY dispute resolution, (2) early stage/limited operating history, (3) financial condition concerns, (4) minimum performance requirements, (5) spousal liability.
15. **Fee deferral required in multiple states**: CA, IL, MD, MN, ND, SD, WA all require fee deferral until pre-opening obligations completed.

## Pass B: Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1-7)

### Item 1 — Franchisor Structure
- Magnuson Franchising, LLC is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Magnuson Company, L.L.C.
- Magnuson Company owns the IP (Licensed Brand and Licensed System) and licenses it to Magnuson Franchising under a perpetual license agreement (dated Jan 4, 2022), terminable by Magnuson Company on 90 days' notice for breach or 6 months' notice without breach.
- Magnuson Company operates "Affiliate Hotels" — third-party hotels receiving marketing services on the Magnuson Cloud PMS. These compete with Branded Hotels.
- No predecessors. No affiliates offering franchises. Franchisor does not own or operate any Branded Hotels.
- Began offering franchises January 2022.

## Pass C: Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8-11)

### Item 8 — Restrictions on Sources
- **Signage and branded supplies**: Must comply with Brand Standards. Pre-launch approval required for all signage.
- **PMS requirement**: Must use Approved PMS or Magnuson Cloud PMS. If Approved PMS, must reimburse franchisor $300-$2,000/year.
- **Distribution channels**: Franchisor has sole and exclusive right to list hotel on Third-Party Distribution Channels. Franchisee must transfer existing OTA accounts to franchisor.
- **Insurance**: Minimum $1M GL per occurrence/aggregate, workers comp per law, franchisor as additional named insured.
- **Approved vendor purchases**: ~15-30% of conversion purchases, ~5-10% of ongoing operating purchases.
- **No purchase cooperatives or rebates**: Neither franchisor nor affiliates derive rebate revenue from vendors.
- **Optional marketing**: Franchisor may offer marketing/digital advertising materials for additional cost; purchase optional.

### Item 9 — Franchisee Obligations
- Standard FDD obligation cross-reference table. Notable: "Not Applicable" for territorial development/sales quotas and non-competition covenants.

### Item 10 — Financing
- No financing offered by franchisor, affiliates, or agents.

### Item 11 — Franchisor Assistance
**Pre-opening:**
- Brand Standards Manual provided
- Revenue manager made available (up to ~100 hours pre-launch)
- Conversion review and approval

**Ongoing:**
- Access to Magnuson Cloud PMS (if elected)
- Listing on Third-Party Distribution Channels
- Revenue management support (continued, amount depends on franchisee request)
- Brand Standards Manual updates

**Training**: No formal training program. No mandatory training. Revenue management support described above is the only structured assistance. Franchisee responsible for all personnel training.

**Advertising**: No mandatory advertising contributions. No advertising fund. No local/regional ad cooperatives. Franchisor has right to conduct advertising at its discretion with no spend obligations. Franchisor has no obligation to spend in any market. No advertising council.

**Computer Systems**: Must use Approved PMS or Magnuson Cloud PMS. No expected additional hardware costs (conversion model assumes existing equipment). Franchisor may modify Brand Standards for computer systems with no frequency/cost limitations.

**Online Presences**: Franchisor owns all Online Presences. Franchisee must transfer existing online presences to franchisor. Franchisee may not create online presences without approval.

**Brand Standards Manual**: PMS Operating Manual (40 pages), Brand Standards Manual (12 pages).

**Conversion timeline**: 30-90 days typical (90-day deadline from signing).

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12-16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **NO EXCLUSIVE TERRITORY**. Explicitly stated in both FDD and franchise agreement.
- Franchisor retains ALL rights to place Branded Hotels and conduct any business activities in any location, including internet, catalog, telemarketing.
- No rights of first refusal for additional franchises.
- May not relocate hotel.
- Franchisor has exclusive right to offer Guest Rooms through internet and Third-Party Distribution Channels.
- Affiliate Hotels (operated by Magnuson Company) compete with Branded Hotels; franchisor will resolve conflicts as it "deems appropriate."
- No obligation to compensate franchisee for encroachment.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
- Trademarks owned by Magnuson Company (not franchisor directly), licensed via Licensing Agreement (Jan 4, 2022).
- Key registrations: Magnuson Hotels (Reg. 3688282, 2009), Magnuson Grand (Reg. 6948027, 2023), Magnuson (Reg. 6948021, 2023), Magnuson Hotels Worldwide (Reg. 7019246, 2023).
- **License termination risk**: Magnuson Company can terminate license on 90 days' notice for breach, 6 months' notice without breach → franchisor would lose ability to license marks.
- Franchisor not required to protect franchisee's right to use trademarks or indemnify franchisee.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
- No material patents.
- Copyrights claimed (unregistered) on Brand Standards, Online Presences, Magnuson Cloud PMS.
- Extensive confidential information provisions. All ideas/concepts/materials created by franchisee become franchisor's property (work-for-hire).

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
- Managing Owner must supervise day-to-day operations full-time.
- No delegation without franchisor consent (no management companies without approval).
- Personal guaranty required from all Owners and spouses.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on What Franchisee May Sell
- 100% of Guest Rooms must be available for sale at all times.
- No removal of rooms from inventory >14 days without approval (else Extended Closure Fee).
- Must honor all confirmed reservations (else Re-booking Fee at 200%).
- Minimum Customer Rating: 3.4/5 (Magnuson Hotels), 3.6/5 (Magnuson Grand) — falling below is termination cause.
- Franchisor may designate minimum or maximum prices if permitted by law.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 5 years from Effective Date.
- **Renewal**: Auto-renewal for one 5-year term unless franchisee gives 90-180 days' notice of non-renewal, or franchisor elects not to renew for any reason.
- **Renewal conditions**: Must sign new agreement that may contain materially different terms. Must sign general release. Must bring hotel into compliance with current Brand Standards.

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: May terminate if franchisor breaches and fails to cure within 30 days.
- **By franchisor without cause**: Not applicable.
- **By franchisor with cause — curable**: 72 hours for health/safety/sanitation; 30 days for other defaults.
- **By franchisor with cause — non-curable**: Material misrepresentation, abandonment (3+ consecutive days or 10 days in 12 months), unauthorized transfer, felony conviction, loss of premises, confidentiality violation, health/safety concern, GRR understatement (3+ times or >3% once), insolvency, 3+ violations in 12 months (or 2+ of same violation), falling below Minimum Customer Rating.

### Transfer
- All transfers require franchisor approval.
- Conditions: application, release of claims, full compliance, transferee signs current-form agreement (may differ materially), evidence of transfer logistics.
- No right of first refusal by franchisor.
- Death/disability: 6 months to sell to approved person.

### Non-competition
- **None**. No non-competition covenants during or after the term.
- Non-disparagement and non-interference covenants apply during and after the term.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Binding arbitration** via AAA, within 50 miles of Casper, WY.
- **Venue**: State or federal court closest to Casper, WY (subject to arbitration).
- **Governing law**: Wyoming (subject to Federal Arbitration Act).
- **Punitive damages waived**. **Jury trial waived**.
- **1-year limitation of claims** (except non-payment).
- **Class action bar**.
- **Individual basis** — no class arbitration, no consolidation.

### Post-Termination Obligations
- Immediately close hotel, remove all Licensed Brand materials, return Confidential Information, cease use of marks, transfer Online Presences.
- Termination Damages if franchisor terminates for breach or franchisee terminates without cause (continuing fees through earlier of 12 months or term expiration).
- Inconvenience Fee ($2,000) if <90 days' notice.
- De-Identification Processing Fee ($9,995) if not completed within 30 days.
