# Final Diligence Report — Magnuson Hotels and Magnuson Grand
## FDD Filing ID 637940 | Issuance Date: March 27, 2025

---

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: 11 franchised hotels across 8 states as of December 31, 2024. Zero company-owned outlets. Zero projected new openings for 2025.
2. **Entity structure**: Magnuson Franchising, LLC (Wyoming LLC, formed March 2021) is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Magnuson Company, L.L.C. (WY LLC, est. 2002). The parent owns all intellectual property and licenses it to the franchisor under a perpetual (but terminable) license agreement.
3. **Ownership history**: Co-founded by Melissa Magnuson (Chair, 2002). Franchising entity created in 2021; began offering franchises January 2022. Parent had a Washington state consent order in 2020 for selling franchises without proper FDD/registration.
4. **Investment range**: $175,000–$400,000 total (conversion model — excludes real estate and existing operating costs). Initial franchise fee: $10,000.
5. **Fee burden**: 8% continuing fee on Gross Room Revenue + GDS booking fees ($11.50/booking) + third-party distribution fees (10-20% depending on channel) + Airbnb connection ($100-260/mo) + minimum invoice floor ($250/mo). Effective total franchisor take on room revenue is among the higher ranges in the hotel franchise industry, particularly for a brand with limited recognition.
6. **Item 19**: No financial performance representations made.
7. **Bottom-quartile financial risk**: Total assets of $30,886 as of year-end 2024. The FDD itself flags financial condition as a special risk. Seven states require fee deferral due to the franchisor's financial condition.
8. **Financial health**: Revenue growing rapidly ($18K → $173K → $260K, 2022-2024) but the absolute base is tiny. Member distributions nearly equal net income ($51K vs. $55K), leaving almost no capital retention. Cash balance of $19,493.
9. **Growth trajectory**: System grew from 0 to 11 over three years (4, then 5, then 2 new units per year). Growth is decelerating. Zero projected openings for 2025. One post-FY termination.
10. **Territory**: Absolutely no exclusive territory. Franchisor retains all rights to place competing hotels anywhere and controls all distribution channels.
11. **Renewal**: 5+5 year term with auto-renewal. Renewal may require signing agreement with materially different terms, plus general release and hotel upgrades.
12. **Disputes**: Binding arbitration in Casper, Wyoming via AAA. Wyoming governing law. 1-year statute of limitations. Jury trial and punitive damages waived. Class action barred.
13. **Special risks**: Five flagged: Wyoming dispute resolution, early stage/no operating history, financial condition concerns, minimum performance/termination, spousal liability.
14. **Trademark license risk**: Parent company (Magnuson Company) can terminate the trademark license on 6 months' notice without cause, which would strip the franchisor (and all franchisees) of the right to use the Magnuson brand.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Magnuson Franchising, LLC was formed on March 17, 2021, as a Washington LLC (later organized in Wyoming). It is a single-member LLC wholly owned by Magnuson Company, L.L.C. The parent provides administrative functions and owns all intellectual property. The franchisor operates no hotels itself and has no company-owned units.

The franchise model is exclusively a hotel conversion model: only existing hotel operators who wish to rebrand their property to Magnuson Hotels or Magnuson Grand are eligible. No greenfield development. Magnuson Grand is reserved for hotels with restaurants, spas, or convention centers; Magnuson Hotels is for standard properties without such facilities.

Two key individuals operate the entity: Melissa Magnuson (co-founder, Chair) and Adnan Malik (CEO since December 2023, with the organization since August 2018 in various technology and revenue roles).

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Initial franchise fee: $10,000, non-refundable, due at signing. One waiver granted in the last fiscal year. Subject to deferral in 7 states (CA, IL, MD, MN, ND, SD, WA) until pre-opening obligations completed.

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Continuing Fee: 8% of Gross Room Revenue, monthly. This is the primary ongoing fee.
- GDS Booking Fees: $11.50 per GDS booking, subject to change.
- Third-Party Distribution Channel Fees: Reimbursement of all fees — currently estimated at 10% for GDS reservations, 15-20% for other channels (e.g., Expedia, Booking.com). Subject to change.
- Airbnb Connection Fees: $100-$260/month, subject to change.
- Minimum Invoice Revenue: $250/month — if total franchisor fees fall below this floor, franchisee pays the deficiency. Subject to 10%/year compounding increase.

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Approved PMS Connection Fee: $300-$2,000/year (only if not using Magnuson Cloud PMS).
- Insufficient Funds Fee: $25 per occurrence.
- Late Payment Fee: 5% one-time + 1.5%/month (18% annual) on overdue balances.
- Conversion Violation: $1,000/month for failure to convert by deadline.
- Standards Violation: $2,000/month for Brand Standards non-compliance.
- Re-booking Fee: 200% of booking fee or alternate accommodation cost (whichever higher).
- Failed Inspection Fee: $2,500 + all follow-up inspection costs.
- Insurance Certificate Fee: $250/month for late certificates.
- Re-Activation Fee: $750 + $25/day (if suspended for default).
- Extended Closure Fee: $750 + $25/day (room out of inventory >14 days without approval).
- Inconvenience Fee: $2,000 (up to $5,000) for termination/closure with <90 days' notice.
- De-Identification Processing Fee: $9,995 if not completed within 30 days of termination.
- Termination Damages: Continuing fees from termination to earlier of (a) 12 months or (b) term expiration.
- Enforcement Costs: Prevailing party recovers costs and attorneys' fees.
- Audit Costs: Reimbursement if understatement of Gross Room Revenue discovered.

**Fee Notes:**
- Fees are generally non-refundable and may be negotiated (not always uniformly applied).
- If franchisee fails to report or misreports Gross Room Revenue, fees are calculated at 110% of the 3-month average with no obligation for franchisor to reimburse overpayments.
- No marketing fund, no ad cooperative, no transfer fee, no renewal fee.

### Initial Investment

The estimated initial investment of $175,000-$400,000 is structured for the conversion model and assumes the franchisee already owns and operates the hotel property. The estimate is based on a 60-Guest Room property and includes:

| Component | Low | High |
|-----------|-----|------|
| Initial Franchise Fee | $10,000 | $10,000 |
| Signage & Branded Supplies | $3,000 | $40,000 |
| Insurance | $10,000 | $40,000 |
| Professional Fees | $2,000 | $10,000 |
| Additional Funds (3 months) | $150,000 | $300,000 |
| **Total** | **$175,000** | **$400,000** |

The "Additional Funds" category ($150K-$300K) is the dominant component, covering wages, utilities, payroll taxes, supplies, maintenance, and working capital for the first three months of operation. The wide range reflects variability in hotel size, location, and local market conditions.

Excluded from the estimate: real estate purchase or lease costs, security deposits, occupancy costs, and any costs the operator already incurs for the Incumbent Hotel (equipment, furniture, fixtures, ongoing services). No financing is offered by the franchisor.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Supplier control is relatively light compared to typical hotel franchises. The franchisor requires:
- All signage and branded supplies must comply with Brand Standards. Franchisor must approve all signage before installation.
- Professional-quality photographs of the rebranded hotel must be provided to the franchisor at franchisee cost, both at launch and periodically during the term.
- Computer systems must meet Brand Standards for minimum hardware, software, integration, and connectivity.

Specified/approved vendor purchases represent approximately 15-30% of conversion purchases and 5-10% of ongoing operating purchases. Neither the franchisor nor its affiliates derive revenue from vendor rebates. No purchasing cooperatives have been established.

### Approved Supplier Process
The franchisor does not currently designate specific approved suppliers for most products and services beyond signage. The approved supplier framework is primarily limited to the PMS requirement and signage compliance.

### Supply Chain Fees
No formal supply chain fees or rebate arrangements. The franchisor and affiliates received no revenue from selling goods to branded hotel owners in FY2024 (other than Item 6 fees).

### Training Program
There is no formal training program. No mandatory training of any kind is required. The franchisor's only structured support is revenue management assistance — one revenue manager made available (up to ~100 hours pre-launch), with ongoing support at the franchisee's request. The franchisee is solely responsible for training all hotel personnel. This is a notably light support model, consistent with the conversion-only approach targeting experienced hotel operators.

### Technology Stack and Costs
- **Magnuson Cloud PMS** or an **Approved PMS** is required. If franchisee uses their own Approved PMS (instead of the franchisor's proprietary system), the franchisee must reimburse franchisor for integration/connectivity costs ($300-$2,000/year).
- Franchisee must ensure franchisor has unrestricted access to PMS data at all times, including guest information, inventory, reservations, and financial data.
- No expected additional hardware costs (conversion model assumes existing equipment).
- Franchisor may modify computer system Brand Standards with no limits on frequency or cost; no reimbursement required.
- The Magnuson Cloud PMS Operating Manual is 40 pages covering setup, rooms, channel management, reservations, rates/inventory, payments, reports, and support.

### Marketing Fund / Advertising
No marketing fund exists. No advertising contributions required. No local or regional advertising cooperatives. The franchisor has no obligation to spend any amount on advertising of any type in any market. The franchisor retains sole control over all marketing programs and materials. Optional marketing materials may be offered for additional cost.

### Reporting Burden
- All Gross Room Revenue must be reported through the PMS, including walk-in and cash transactions.
- Quarterly balance sheet, P&L, and cash flow statement due within 15 business days of quarter-end.
- Annual balance sheet, P&L, and cash flow statement due within 15 business days of year-end.
- Franchisor may conduct financial audits at any time without notice.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
There is absolutely no protected territory, no exclusive territory, and no territorial rights of any kind. This is explicitly and emphatically stated in both the FDD (Item 12) and the Franchise Agreement (Section 5, titled "No Territory"). The franchisor retains all rights to place Branded Hotels and conduct any business activities in any manner or location, regardless of proximity to existing franchisees.

### Carve-Outs and Franchisor Rights
The franchisor and its affiliates retain the right to:
- Open competing Branded Hotels at any location, including next door to an existing franchisee
- Acquire or be acquired by competitive businesses
- Conduct business through internet, catalog sales, telemarketing, or other distribution channels
- No obligation to compensate franchisee for exercising any of these rights

The parent company (Magnuson Company) operates "Affiliate Hotels" — third-party properties receiving marketing services on the Magnuson Cloud PMS — that offer services competitive with Branded Hotels. If conflicts arise between Affiliate Hotels and Branded Hotels, the franchisor will "analyze the conflict and take any action it deems appropriate." No formal conflict resolution protocol is specified.

### Channel Restrictions
The franchisor has the sole and exclusive right to list the hotel and Guest Rooms with all Third-Party Distribution Channels (OTAs, GDS, corporate partnerships, travel agencies, etc.). The franchisee may not self-list on any OTA or distribution channel without franchisor approval. If the franchisee has existing OTA accounts at the time of signing, they must transfer exclusive control to the franchisor. This represents significant control over the franchisee's online visibility and booking pipeline.

The franchisee may conduct general marketing (non-internet) to solicit customers in any location, but may not use any Online Presence or Third-Party Distribution Channel without approval.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
The initial term is 5 years from the Effective Date, with automatic renewal for one additional 5-year term. Renewal occurs automatically unless:
- Franchisee provides 90-180 days' notice of non-renewal, OR
- Franchisor notifies franchisee of non-renewal for any reason (subject to applicable state law)

**Renewal conditions (materially different risk)**: The franchisor may condition renewal on: (a) compliance history, (b) signing a new franchise agreement with terms that may differ materially, (c) signing a general release of all claims, and/or (d) upgrading the hotel to current Brand Standards for new hotels. This "materially different terms" provision is a significant risk — the franchisee could face substantially changed economics at renewal.

### Transfer Provisions
All transfers require franchisor approval. Transfer is broadly defined to include voluntary or involuntary assignments, sales, gifts, encumbrances, mergers, issuance of securities, death, disability, divorce, insolvency, foreclosure, or loss of control. Conditions include: written application, general release, full compliance, transferee signs current-form agreement (may differ materially), and evidence of proper transfer logistics. No transfer fee is specified. No right of first refusal held by the franchisor.

### Termination and Death/Disability
**By franchisee**: May terminate if franchisor breaches and fails to cure within 30 days. Any other termination by franchisee is deemed "without cause" and triggers Termination Damages.

**By franchisor with cause**: Cure periods of 72 hours (health/safety) or 30 days (other defaults). Non-curable events include material misrepresentation, abandonment (3+ consecutive days or 10 days in any 12 months), unauthorized transfer, felony conviction, loss of premises, confidentiality violations, health/safety concerns, GRR understatement (3+ times or >3% once), insolvency, repeated violations (3+ in 12 months or 2+ of same violation), and falling below the Minimum Customer Rating.

**Post-termination**: Immediate closure, de-identification, return of Confidential Information, transfer of Online Presences. Termination Damages payable if franchisor terminates for franchisee breach or franchisee terminates without cause. Inconvenience Fee ($2,000) if <90 days' notice. De-Identification Processing Fee ($9,995) if not completed within 30 days.

**Death/disability**: 6 months to sell to franchisor-approved person. During that period, hotel must remain compliant.

### Noncompete Restrictions
No non-competition covenants during or after the franchise term. This is unusual and favorable to franchisees — they may operate competing hotels during and after the relationship. However, non-disparagement and non-interference covenants apply indefinitely (during and after the term).

### Dispute Resolution
All disputes submitted to binding arbitration via AAA. Venue: within 50 miles of Casper, Wyoming (franchisor's principal place of business). One arbitrator. Wyoming governing law. Federal Arbitration Act governs arbitration matters.

**Waivers**: Punitive/exemplary damages waived. Jury trial waived. Class action barred. No class arbitration. No consolidation with other proceedings.

**Statute of limitations**: 1 year from date party knew or should have known of facts (except non-payment claims).

**State overrides**: Multiple states modify these provisions — MN prohibits litigation outside MN; WA requires arbitration in WA; MD, MN, ND extend limitations periods; MN and ND may void termination damages; ND may void non-competes.

### Litigation History
One matter: Washington DFI consent order (November 10, 2020) against the parent company (Magnuson Company, L.L.C.) for selling franchises without franchise disclosure documents or state registration. $2,500 investigative costs, cease and desist. This is the parent, not the franchisor entity, but it indicates historical non-compliance with franchise registration laws.

## H. Item 21 — Financial Statements

### Auditor and Opinion
The financial statements were audited by an accounting firm located in Melville, New York (firm name appears in letterhead graphic, not captured by text extraction). The report is dated March 25, 2025. The auditor issued an **unqualified (clean) opinion** — the financial statements present fairly in all material respects in accordance with US GAAP.

Notably, the auditor's report describes responsibility to evaluate going concern but does not include a going concern paragraph. However, the FDD's Special Risks section independently flags the franchisor's financial condition as calling into question its ability to provide services and support.

### Balance Sheet (December 31, 2024)

| Line Item | 2024 | 2023 |
|-----------|------|------|
| Cash | $19,493 | $13,794 |
| Accounts receivable | $10,673 | $14,221 |
| Prepaid expenses | $720 | $0 |
| **Total Assets** | **$30,886** | **$28,015** |
| AP & accrued expenses | $6,051 | $7,458 |
| Member's equity | $24,835 | $20,557 |
| **Total L&E** | **$30,886** | **$28,015** |

This is an extraordinarily small balance sheet — $31K in total assets. The entity has no long-term assets, no fixed assets, no intangible assets on its balance sheet (the trademark and IP are owned by the parent). It operates as essentially a fee-collection pass-through entity. Current ratio is 5.1x (assets/liabilities), so it is solvent on a current basis, but the absolute dollar amounts are minimal.

### Income Statement

| Line Item | 2024 | 2023 | 2022 |
|-----------|------|------|------|
| Continuing fees | $259,560 | $173,198 | $18,128 |
| Franchise fees | $0 | $0 | $10,000 |
| **Total revenue** | **$259,560** | **$173,198** | **$28,128** |
| Operating expenses | $204,483 | $137,230 | $84,176 |
| **Net income (loss)** | **$55,077** | **$35,968** | **($56,048)** |

Revenue has grown dramatically from the $28K startup year in 2022 to $260K in 2024, but the absolute level remains tiny. The franchisor generated $260K in total revenue from 11 hotels — implying average fees of approximately $23,600 per hotel per year, or roughly $1,967/month. At 8% of Gross Room Revenue, this implies average Gross Room Revenue of approximately $295,000 per hotel per year, or approximately $24,600/month — consistent with a small, lower-tier hotel operation.

Operating expenses totaled $204K in 2024. Of this, $127,435 (62%) was a management fee paid to the parent company (Magnuson Company) for administrative services. This is a related-party transaction that represents the single largest expense. The remaining ~$77K covers other operating costs (not disaggregated).

The entity was unprofitable in 2022 ($56K loss) due to startup costs with minimal revenue, turned profitable in 2023 ($36K), and earned $55K in 2024.

### Cash Flow

| Line Item | 2024 | 2023 | 2022 |
|-----------|------|------|------|
| Net cash from operations | $56,498 | $26,059 | ($61,907) |
| Member distributions | ($50,799) | ($50,802) | $0 |
| Net change in cash | $5,699 | ($24,743) | ($61,907) |
| Cash ending | $19,493 | $13,794 | $38,537 |

The entity generated $56K from operations in 2024 but distributed $51K to its parent — retaining only $5,699 in cash. This near-total distribution of earnings is a significant concern: the entity is not building capital reserves. Cash started at $100K (from member contributions in 2022) and has declined to $19K. If revenue growth stalls or a material obligation arises, the entity has minimal buffer.

### Key Financial Observations
1. **Leverage**: No debt. No long-term liabilities. The entity is entirely equity-financed (member contributions).
2. **Distributions**: Member distributions of ~$51K in both 2023 and 2024, nearly equal to net income, suggesting the parent company treats this as a cash cow rather than a growth entity.
3. **Related party**: The management fee to the parent ($127K) represents 49% of total revenue. This is the franchisor's primary expense and is effectively a transfer price within the corporate family.
4. **Asset composition**: 100% current assets. No fixed assets. No goodwill. No intellectual property on the balance sheet (owned by parent). The entity is a pure licensing vehicle.
5. **License agreement**: 99-year term with parent, but no license fee has been charged to date. This could change.
6. **Tax**: Disregarded entity (single-member LLC) — no provision for income taxes. All income/loss passes through to the parent's return.

## I. State Addenda Summary

### States with Addenda
CA, HI, IL, MD, MN, NY, ND, RI, SD, VA, WA (11 states, plus Michigan provisions in the front matter).

### Fee Deferral States (7)
California, Illinois, Maryland, Minnesota, North Dakota, South Dakota, and Washington all require deferral of the initial franchise fee and other initial payments until the franchisor has completed all pre-opening obligations and the hotel has begun operating. This is imposed due to the franchisor's financial condition — regulators have determined the franchisor has not demonstrated adequate capitalization.

### Material Overrides by State

**California**: Franchise Investment Law applies; liquidated damages clause may be unenforceable; California law may override Wyoming choice of law; release provisions limited by §31512; fee deferral required (franchisor not adequately capitalized).

**Illinois**: Illinois law governs; venue outside Illinois void per §4 of IL Franchise Disclosure Act; fee deferral per AG order based on financial condition.

**Maryland**: Releases do not apply to MD Franchise Registration and Disclosure Law claims; 3-year statute of limitations for MD law claims; fee deferral per Securities Commissioner based on financial condition.

**Minnesota**: 90 days' notice of termination with 60 days to cure; 180 days' notice for non-renewal; termination damages may be unenforceable per MN Rule 2860.4400J; 3-year statute of limitations for MN law claims; trademark protection/indemnification obligation per §80C.12; releases limited; fee deferral.

**New York**: NY General Business Law Article 33 rights preserved; assignee must be willing and financially able; disclosure document delivery timing rules.

**North Dakota**: Non-competes generally unenforceable; termination damages may be unenforceable; arbitration site must be mutually agreed; ND claims may be brought in ND; releases limited; fee deferral.

**Washington**: 19 specific provisions; WA Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails; arbitration in WA; general releases void except post-agreement negotiated settlements; transfer fees limited to actual costs; fair and reasonable pricing required; treble damages available (waiver may be void); non-compete covenants void (employee <$100K/yr, contractor <$250K/yr); non-solicitation restrictions void; fee deferral.
