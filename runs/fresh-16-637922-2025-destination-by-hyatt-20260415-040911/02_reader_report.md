# Reader Report — Destination by Hyatt (637922-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C., a Delaware LLC, subsidiary of Hyatt Hotels Corporation (NYSE: H). Principal address: 150 North Riverside Plaza, Chicago, IL 60606. Has offered Destination by Hyatt franchises since May 2019 (acquired Two Roads Hospitality brands in Dec 2018).
2. **System size**: 14 total Brand Hotels as of Dec 31, 2024 — 4 franchised (CA, CO, NV, WA) and 10 company-owned/managed. Net change 2022→2024: franchised grew from 1 to 4; company-owned shrank from 15 to 10. Very small system.
3. **Concept**: Lifestyle resort hotels operating under a separate tradename the owner controls, affiliated with "Destination by Hyatt" brand. Unique properties reflecting local market character. 150–500 guest rooms.
4. **Investment range**: $45,790,776 to $253,820,733 (excluding real estate). This is among the highest franchise investment ranges in the industry. Of this, $279,406 to $1,199,942 is paid to franchisor or affiliate.
5. **Fee burden**: Royalty 7% of Gross Rooms Revenue from Channel Bookings + Commercial Services 3.5% GRR + World of Hyatt 4.5% eligible revenue + Digital Acquisition 1.35% GRR via digital channels + GDS ~$8.50/reservation + mandatory contracts $1,500–$25,000/month + other corporate services $500–$15,000/month. Total ongoing fee load is substantial.
6. **Item 19**: NO financial performance representations. Franchisor does not disclose any revenue, cost, or profit data.
7. **Territory**: Area of Protection with limited AOP Term (1–5 years post-opening). After AOP Term expires, no territorial rights whatsoever. Franchisor and affiliates may open competing Brand Hotels within Area of Protection.
8. **Term**: 20 years from first day of month Hotel opens. Successor franchise for 10 years on then-current terms (may be materially different). Must renovate/remodel to current standards for renewal.
9. **Litigation**: Minimal — 1 case in 2024 (Hyatt v. Imperial Hotels for unpaid royalty fees).
10. **Bankruptcy**: None required to be disclosed.
11. **Financial health**: Parent Hyatt Hotels Corporation had $6.65B total revenue (2024), net income $1.30B, total assets $13.3B, total equity $3.83B. Guarantee of Performance from parent. Audited by Deloitte & Touche LLP (auditor since 2003). Very strong financial backing.
12. **Noncompete**: No post-term noncompete. During term, franchisee and management company cannot be a Competing Brand Owner.
13. **Dispute resolution**: Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, Illinois. Illinois law governs (subject to state addenda overrides).
14. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution in Illinois highlighted per state requirements.
15. **State addenda**: Exhibit M covers California, Hawaii, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Virginia, Washington, plus multi-state no-waiver provision.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Area of Protection (AOP)**: Defined before signing FA. Size varies: few blocks (urban) to ~3 miles radius (less populated). Uses geographic references or circle.
- **AOP Term**: 1–5 years after Hotel opening. During AOP Term, franchisor will not open/authorize another Brand Hotel within AOP.
- **Exception during AOP Term**: If franchisor acquires 4+ hotels, one or more within AOP, they may convert those to Brand Hotels regardless.
- **After AOP Term**: No territorial protection whatsoever. Franchisor may open competing Brand Hotels within AOP.
- **Existing Brand Hotels**: May receive no AOP at all when signing successor FA.
- **No exclusive territory**: Franchisee may face competition from other franchisees, company-owned outlets, or other Hyatt brands.
- **No options or rights of first refusal** for additional franchises.
- **Other Hyatt brands**: Expect Hyatt-branded facilities (Hyatt Regency, Hyatt Place, etc.) in AOP at all times.
- **Internet/channel sales**: No restrictions on where anyone may solicit customers. Franchisor may use any channel (Internet, telemarketing, etc.) in AOP without compensation.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
- 4 principal marks registered with PTO: "Hyatt" (1972), "World of Hyatt" (2017), "Destination by Hyatt" (2022), Destination by Hyatt logo (2022).
- Licensed from Hyatt Corporation via perpetual Trademark License Agreement (since Nov 1, 1993).
- Hotel operates under its own Tradename (owned by franchisee) + affiliation with Destination by Hyatt brand.
- No currently effective material determinations, pending proceedings, or known superior prior rights.

### Item 14 — Patents, Copyrights, Proprietary Information
- No material patents.
- Copyright protection claimed for Copyrighted Materials (not registered with US Copyright Office).
- Extensive Confidential Information definition (11 categories including training materials, guest information, operating results, technology).
- Guest Information is franchisor property during term. Unlimited franchisor access to PMS and guest data.
- Post-termination: Franchisee may use Hotel-generated Guest Information if complying with data policies.
- All Innovations are franchisor property (works made-for-hire).

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
- Must be managed by franchisor-approved management company or by franchisee if approved.
- Franchisor may revoke management company approval if it becomes a Competing Brand Owner or fails quality standards.
- F&B Operators and Spa Operators require approval; approval may be revoked.
- GM candidates subject to franchisor approval; may require replacement for quality failures.
- Core Management must devote full business time to Hotel.
- Revenue manager must be certified within 90 days; max 20 Brand Hotels per revenue manager.
- **Guaranty**: Controlling Ownership Interest owners must sign Guaranty (all obligations, monetary and non-monetary).
- **Guarantor Monetary Threshold**: Minimum net worth and minimum liquid assets, increasing annually by CPI. Annual financial statement verification required.

### Item 16 — Restrictions on What Franchisee May Sell
- Must offer all authorized products/services; may not sell unauthorized items.
- Mixed-Use Site restrictions: No pawnshops, adult businesses, package liquor stores, marijuana dispensaries, gambling (without consent). Bars within 300 feet of Hotel need consent if >50% revenue from alcohol.
- Room rates: Set by franchisee within System Standards; best rate guarantee compliance required.
- Cannot change room count without consent.

## Pass E: Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 20 years from first day of month Hotel opens.
- **Successor franchise**: 10 years on then-current terms (which may be **materially different**, including fees).
- **Renewal conditions**: Must have substantially complied during term, currently in full compliance, meet then-current new franchisee standards, received passing QA scores for previous 3 years, have possession rights for 10+ more years, renovate/remodel Hotel, sign then-current FA, pay PIP fee, sign general releases (if state law allows).

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: Cannot terminate FA or Confidentiality Agreement except as law allows. Can terminate Central Services Agreement and Hosting Services Agreement under specific conditions.
- **By franchisor without cause**: Cannot terminate FA without cause. Can terminate most ancillary agreements on 30–90 days' notice.
- **Curable defaults**: 24 hours for health/safety threats; 10 days for insurance/payment failures; 30 days for development deadlines, provider payments, other defaults; 60 days for Guarantor Monetary Threshold failure.
- **Non-curable defaults**: Bankruptcy events, failure to discharge $100K+ judgments, ceasing operations, contesting IP, unauthorized transfer, felony conviction, false books/reports, unauthorized CI disclosure, law violations, repeated defaults.

### Transfer
- No transfers of FA, Hotel, or Controlling Ownership Interests without approval.
- Conditions include: Transferee qualifies, pay amounts owed, training completed, sign then-current or assumed agreements (may be materially different), renovate, sign releases (if state law allows), remain liable after transfer.
- No public offerings. Private offerings require review.
- Right of first offer (Exhibit C): Only for strategic markets franchisor designates.
- Death/disability: 6 months to transfer to approved transferee.
- Non-Controlling Ownership Interest transfers: Permitted without approval if transferee is not a Competing Brand Owner or sanctioned person, and transfer doesn't create/transfer Controlling Ownership Interest.

### Noncompete
- **During term**: No formal noncompete, but franchisee and management company cannot be a Competing Brand Owner.
- **Post-term**: No post-term noncompete.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: Mandatory for all disputes, at a location within 10 miles of franchisor's principal business address (currently Chicago, IL).
- **Choice of forum**: Illinois (subject to arbitration and state law overrides).
- **Choice of law**: Illinois (except Federal Arbitration Act, other federal law, and state law overrides).
- **Waiver of jury trial**: Implied by mandatory arbitration.
