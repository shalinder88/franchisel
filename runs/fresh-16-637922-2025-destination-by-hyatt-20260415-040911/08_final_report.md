## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size**: 14 total Destination by Hyatt hotels as of December 31, 2024 — 4 franchised and 10 company-owned/managed. The franchise system is extremely small but growing, having expanded from 1 franchised outlet in 2022 to 4 by end of 2024. Company-owned outlets have been declining (15 → 10), reflecting Hyatt's broader asset-light strategy. 3 additional franchise agreements are signed but not yet opened (FL, IL, MO).

2. **Entity structure**: Franchisor is Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C., a Delaware LLC. Parent is Hyatt Hotels Corporation, a publicly traded company (NYSE: H) with 25+ lodging brands worldwide. The franchisor acquired the Destination by Hyatt brand through the December 2018 acquisition of Two Roads Hospitality. Hyatt Hotels Corporation provides an absolute and unconditional Guarantee of Performance of the franchisor's obligations.

3. **Ownership history**: Hyatt Franchise Corporation incorporated in Delaware, converted to LLC form as Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. Offered various full-service hotel franchises since 1994. Destination by Hyatt franchises offered since May 2019. Also franchises Hyatt, JdV by Hyatt, Hyatt Centric, Hyatt Regency, Unbound Collection by Hyatt, Caption by Hyatt, and Hyatt Studios brands in the US.

4. **Investment range**: $45,790,776 to $253,820,733 (excluding real estate). This is one of the highest franchise investment ranges in the US hospitality market. The concept targets new-construction or conversion luxury/lifestyle resort hotels with 150-500 guest rooms. Of the total, only $279,406 to $1,199,942 flows directly to the franchisor or its affiliates.

5. **Fee burden**: Ongoing fees are substantial and multi-layered. On Channel Bookings (non-OTA), the royalty is 7% of Gross Rooms Revenue. On all revenue: Commercial Services Fee 3.5% GRR, World of Hyatt 4.5% of eligible loyalty revenue, Digital Acquisition 1.35% GRR from digital channels, plus GDS fees (~$8.50/reservation), mandatory contracts ($1,500-$25,000/month), and other corporate services ($500-$15,000/month). Total ongoing fee load on channel revenue effectively exceeds 15% of GRR before adding transaction-level charges.

6. **Item 19**: No financial performance representations. The franchisor does not disclose any revenue, cost, profit, or loss data for franchised or company-owned outlets. A prospective franchisee must conduct independent financial due diligence with no guidance from the FDD.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: The absence of Item 19 data combined with the extremely high investment threshold ($46M minimum) and very small franchise system (4 outlets) creates significant risk. There is essentially no franchise track record to evaluate — the oldest franchised outlet dates to before 2022, and only 3 others have opened since then. No terminations or closures among franchised outlets, which is positive but reflects the system's youth rather than proven resilience.

8. **Financial health**: Hyatt Hotels Corporation reported $6.65B total revenue in FY2024, net income of $1.30B (inflated by $1.25B gains on real estate sales), total assets of $13.3B, total equity of $3.83B, and cash of $1.01B. The parent is financially robust with investment-grade credit. The franchisor entity itself has $159M total assets and $149M member's equity.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Total system flat at 14 for 3 years, but composition shifting from company-owned to franchised. 3 new franchise agreements signed, 1 projected opening in next fiscal year. The brand is in early franchise growth phase — transitioning from primarily managed/owned properties to a franchise model.

10. **Territory**: Area of Protection with AOP Term of 1-5 years post-opening only. After expiration, no territorial protection whatsoever. Franchisor can open competing Brand Hotels within AOP. Other Hyatt brands may always operate in AOP.

11. **Renewal**: 10-year successor franchise on then-current terms which may be materially different, including fees. Must renovate/remodel, sign general releases (if state law allows), meet current new-franchisee standards, and have passing QA scores for previous 3 years.

12. **Disputes**: Mandatory arbitration in Chicago, Illinois. Illinois law governs. State addenda modify dispute resolution for certain states. One litigation case in 2024 (unpaid royalty fees), no bankruptcy.

13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state arbitration/litigation in Illinois per state-required disclosure. Very high capital commitment with no FPR to support investment decision. Expiring territorial protection.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
Hyatt Franchising, L.L.C. is a single-member LLC wholly owned by Hyatt Hotels Corporation. The franchisor does not operate Brand Hotels itself — operations are handled by affiliates (particularly Hyatt Corporation) which provide System Services to the franchise network. Key affiliates include:
- **Hyatt Corporation**: Administers World of Hyatt loyalty program, provides Mandatory and Non-Mandatory Services, and has operated/managed hotels since 1957.
- **Rosemont Project Management, L.L.C.**: Optional purchasing and project management services.

Multiple international franchise affiliates exist for Canada, EAME, Latin America, Brazil, and Asia Pacific operations.

### Fee Stack

#### Initial Fees
The total initial fee paid to franchisor in 2024 ranged from $79,600 to $148,450. Key components:
- **Application Fee**: $100,000 or $400/room (whichever is greater). Partially refundable (less $7,500) if application withdrawn before approval; entirely non-refundable after approval. Additional $400/room for rooms added before opening.
- **IT Project Management Services**: $69,900 (up to 250 rooms) plus $10,500-$17,000 expense reimbursement.
- **Design Services Review Fee**: $75,000-$160,000.
- **Pre-Opening Training**: $19,450-$46,650 in fees plus $4,241-$9,900 in expense reimbursement.
- **PIP Fee**: $10,000 (conversions only).
- **Comfort Letter Fee**: $2,500 per letter.
- **Extension Fee**: $10,000 if opening deadline extension needed.
- **Revenue Management**: $0-$7,000 pre-opening (if participating).
- **Field Marketing**: $0-$16,482 pre-opening (if participating).
- **Pre-Opening Purchasing Services (optional, Rosemont)**: $85,000-$275,000.
- **Additional Pre-Opening Advisory**: $0-$375,000 (if requested or determined needed).

#### Ongoing Fees — Franchise Agreement
- **Royalty**: 7% of Gross Rooms Revenue from Channel Bookings. Channel Bookings = all bookings except third-party OTAs (Expedia, Booking.com, etc.). OTA bookings are royalty-free but still subject to other fees. May be reduced for first 1-3 years on new construction.
- **World of Hyatt Assessment**: 4.5% of eligible revenue from loyalty program members (2.25% for on-property enrollments). Significant offset: in 2024, Brand Hotel franchisees received $21.8M more in loyalty reimbursements than they paid in assessments.
- **Quality Assurance/Compliance**: FIP stages ($5,000-$20,000 per 6-month stage); BPP ($45,000/year if failing beyond Stage 3). Up to 100% reduction for improved performance.
- **On-site QA Inspections**: $1,500-$2,000/annual; $2,000 re-inspection.
- **Additional Training**: $950/trainer/day plus expenses.
- **Training Tools/Materials**: $9.01/room/year.
- **Owner Convention**: $2,000-$5,000/attendee (typically annual or biennial).
- **GM University**: $2,700/newly hired or promoted GM.
- **Late Fee/Interest**: $225 late fee plus 1.5%/month interest on overdue amounts.
- **Liquidated Damages**: Formula based — lesser of 60 or remaining months × (Average Monthly Revenue × 3.5% + Average Monthly Revenue from Channel Bookings × 7%). 150% multiplier for Consequential Termination (transfer to Competitor or 3+ cross-defaults).

#### Ongoing Fees — Mandatory System Services
- **Commercial Services Fee**: 3.5% of Gross Rooms Revenue. Covers brand marketing, reservations, revenue management technology, group sales support, PMS services, digital products (Hyatt.com/app), and other technology.
- **Digital Acquisition Fee**: 1.35% of GRR from Digital Channels (Hyatt.com, World of Hyatt app).
- **GDS/Reservation Services**: ~$8.50/reservation average (passed at cost, no markup).
- **Mandatory Contracts**: $1,500-$25,000/month (credit card, music license, telecom).
- **Other Corporate Services**: $500-$15,000/month (including 5% commission on Upsell Revenue).

#### Non-Mandatory System Services
- Revenue Management: $1,000-$3,500/month (3 tiers).
- Field Marketing: $996-$2,747/month (3 tiers).
- Central Sales: $3,850-$6,650/month (group) + other options.
- GPGS Add-On: $3.50-$16/interaction.
- Digital Ancillary: $1,250/month + 5% commission.
- HSF booking fees: 6%/3% of group revenue; 3%/1% of GRR per room night.
- May be reclassified as Mandatory under certain circumstances.

#### Event-Driven Fees
- Transfer: Then-current Application Fee (refundable less $7,500 if denied).
- PIP Fee (conversion/successor): $10,000.
- Application Fee for new rooms (post-opening): Greater of $10,000 or $400 × new rooms.
- Fee for review of offering materials: $5,000.
- Royalty fee upon termination: $5,000/day while failing to de-identify.
- Audit expenses: Cost of audit if 3%+ understatement or willful underpayment.
- Insurance fee: $200/room/year if franchisor procures insurance on franchisee's behalf.

### Initial Investment
Total estimated: **$45,790,776 to $253,820,733** (excluding real estate).

The dominant cost is construction/improvements ($34.0M-$204.8M), followed by FF&E ($5.6M-$23.1M), telecom/tech systems ($1.5M-$3.3M), architecture/design ($1.0M-$9.2M), and OS&E ($1.7M-$8.1M). The wide range reflects the 150-500 room hotel size spectrum and geographic variation in resort markets.

Real estate is excluded entirely. Brand Hotels are typically in resort locations on 2-10 acres. Land/rent/purchase costs vary too widely to estimate.

The estimate does not include finance charges, interest, or debt service, which for a project of this scale would be very substantial.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions
Approximately 75% of total purchases to establish and operate the Hotel must come from designated/approved sources or comply with System Standards. The franchisor does not operate as a significant direct supplier — Hyatt Corporation received only $99 in supplier rebates from Brand Hotel franchisee purchases in 2024 (entirely offset against System Services Costs), and affiliates collected about $491,320 in fees from providing products/services.

Key mandatory suppliers/systems:
- **Opera PMS**: Must use; must sign Hosting Services Agreement for remote hosting; designated vendor support at $3.90/room/month.
- **CRS/GDS/ADS**: Must participate in authorized reservation systems; honor all confirmed reservations.
- **Quality assurance inspections**: Designated third-party provider.
- **Travel agent commissions**: Designated third-party provider.
- **Cyber endpoint protection and web filtering**: Designated tools required.
- **Music/sound system**: Designated vendor at $18.95/month.
- **Credit card acceptance, telecommunications**: Mandatory contracts through Hyatt Group.

Franchisees who choose not to use Avendra (the Hyatt Group's purchasing company) may find it impracticable to obtain certain branded products (e.g., Hyatt-branded bathroom amenities). Rosemont purchasing is optional.

### Training Program Detail
The initial training program has 7 mandatory parts totaling approximately 290-420 classroom/eLearning hours plus 56-118 OJT hours:
1. Owner Briefing (8 hrs classroom, Chicago/virtual)
2. Orientation (16 hrs, Chicago/virtual)
3. GMU (53-58 hrs, eLearning + virtual + Chicago)
4. Operations Training (47-79 hrs classroom + 40-76 hrs OJT)
5. Sales/Events/Revenue (136.5 hrs, virtual/eLearning)
6. Other Personnel (17-34 hrs + 15-36 hrs OJT)
7. Pre-Opening Brand Training (7+ nights on-site with 2-3 trainers)

Ongoing training obligations include GM replacement training, Core Management replacement within 90 days, owner conventions, leadership summits, and various eLearning requirements.

### Technology Stack and Costs
Required systems: Property Management (Opera), Point of Sale (Simphony), Revenue Management, Events (Envision), Key Lock, PBX/Phones, In-Room Entertainment, HSIA, Guest Computing, Hotel Music, Digital Surveillance, Scent.

Estimated technology system cost: $1,508,950-$3,296,000 for acquisition and installation. Ongoing costs include $3.90/room/month for PMS/POS vendor support, $18.95/month for sound system updates, plus technology services included in Commercial Services Fee. No contractual limit on frequency or cost of required technology upgrades.

Franchisor has independent, unlimited access to all Technology System data.

### Marketing Fund
There is no separate advertising fund or local/regional cooperative. Marketing, advertising, and PR costs are covered through the Commercial Services Fee (3.5% of GRR) as part of the broader System Services. The Hyatt Group develops advertising programs and is not required to spend any amount in the franchisee's area. No periodic accounting of how Commercial Services Fee funds are spent. No franchisee advertising councils exist (though franchisor may form/dissolve them).

### Capital Reserve Requirement
Franchisee must deposit 5% of total Hotel operating revenue monthly into a separate capital reserve account for future renovations and upgrades.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory Definition
The "Area of Protection" (AOP) is individually negotiated before signing the Franchise Agreement. Boundaries may use geographic references (streets, rivers) or a defined radius from the Hotel entrance. Typical size ranges from a few blocks in dense urban areas to approximately 3 miles in less populated resort areas.

### AOP Term and Expiration
Protection is time-limited by the "AOP Term," which typically runs 1-5 years after the Hotel's opening date. **After the AOP Term expires, the franchisee has no territorial protection of any kind.** The franchisor and its affiliates may then open competing Brand Hotels within the former AOP, including under franchise applications submitted during the AOP Term.

### Carve-Outs During AOP Term
Even during the AOP Term, if the franchisor or any affiliate acquires a group of at least 4 hotels, one or more of which are within the AOP, the franchisor may convert those acquired hotels to Brand Hotels regardless of the AOP restriction.

### Channel Restrictions
- No restrictions on where anyone may solicit customers or accept orders.
- Franchisor may use any distribution channel (Internet, telemarketing, etc.) within the AOP without compensating the franchisee.
- Franchisee may use channels outside AOP with approval.
- CRS is the exclusive means for accepting reservations (except approved ADS/GDS).

### Other Hyatt Brand Competition
Franchisees should expect to find hotels under other Hyatt brands (Hyatt Regency, Hyatt Place, Hyatt House, etc.) within the AOP at all times. These share common reservation systems, loyalty programs, and services. No options or rights of first refusal for additional franchises.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term**: 20 years from the first day of the month the Hotel opens. If acquiring an existing Brand Hotel, term = remaining term on seller's agreement. Term extends during any closure period from casualty.
- **Successor franchise**: 10 years on then-current terms. The then-current form of franchise agreement **may be materially different** from the original, including fees. Conditions: substantial compliance during original term, current full compliance, meet then-current new franchisee standards, passing QA scores for prior 3 years, possession rights for 10+ years, renovate/remodel to current standards, pay PIP fee, sign then-current FA and ancillary documents, sign general releases (if state law allows).

### Transfer Provisions
All transfers of the FA, Hotel, substantially all assets, or Controlling Ownership Interests require franchisor approval. Conditions include transferee qualification, payment of all amounts owed, completion of training, signing then-current or assumed agreements (which may be materially different), renovation commitment, financial adequacy post-transfer, and signing of releases (if state law allows). Franchisee remains liable after transfer. No public offerings permitted. Private offerings require document review for $5,000 fee.

Non-Controlling Ownership Interest transfers are permitted without approval if transferee is not a Competing Brand Owner or sanctioned person, doesn't create a Controlling Ownership Interest, and franchisee notifies within 30 days.

Right of first offer applies only for certain strategic markets the franchisor designates, and only if Exhibit C is signed.

### Termination and Death/Disability
Franchisee cannot terminate the FA except as law allows. Death or disability of controlling owner: 6 months to transfer to approved transferee.

Franchisor may suspend CRS/GDS/ADS access, remove Hotel from advertising/website, suspend fee reductions, and/or refuse support upon any default (after applicable cure period).

### Noncompete Restrictions
**During term**: No formal covenant not to compete. However, neither the franchisee nor the approved management company may be a "Competing Brand Owner" (entity that franchises/owns a competing brand with 5+ hotels, manages exclusively for such a brand, or is an affiliate of such an entity).

**Post-term**: No post-term noncompete. However, liquidated damages provisions create an economic disincentive — the Consequential Termination 150% multiplier applies if the Hotel is transferred to a Competitor.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Arbitration**: Mandatory for all disputes. Location: within 10 miles of franchisor's principal business address (currently Chicago, IL). Administered by AAA (based on the Imperial Hotels case).
- **Choice of forum**: Illinois (subject to arbitration obligation and state law).
- **Choice of law**: Illinois law (except Federal Arbitration Act, other federal law, and state law overrides).
- **Limitation of actions**: Subject to FA terms and state law.

### Litigation History
One case in 2024: Hyatt Franchising v. Imperial Hotels, LLC et al. (AAA 2024) — unpaid royalty fees, brought by franchisor against franchisee. Minimal litigation history for a franchise system this size, though the system is very young.
