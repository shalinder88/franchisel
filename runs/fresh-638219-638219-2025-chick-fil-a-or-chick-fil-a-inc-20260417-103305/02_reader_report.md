2. **Entity Structure:** Chick-fil-A, Inc. (GA corp, formed 1964). Key affiliates: CFA Supply (distributor), Bay Center Foods (lemon juice), CFA Servco (gift cards), CFA Properties (IP owner), CFA Canada, CFA Puerto Rico, CFA Asia (Singapore), CFA UK, Truett's Group (Dwarf House/Truett's Grill legacy restaurants).

6. **Item 19 Highlights:** Non-mall Operator restaurants: median $9.23M, average $9.32M annual sales (2,179 units). Mall units: median $3.39M, average $4.52M (197 units). No cost/expense data disclosed.

10. **Territory:** NO exclusive or protected territory. CFA can open competing locations anywhere.
11. **Renewal:** Auto-extends 1 year unless 30 days' notice by either party. CFA can change terms with 45 days' notice.
12. **Disputes:** Litigation in Georgia only. Georgia law applies. No arbitration.
13. **Special Risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution (Georgia).
14. **Unique Model:** CFA owns/leases all premises and equipment. Operator is essentially a profit-sharing manager. 0 transfers in 3 years (franchise is not transferable to third parties).

## B. Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — Entity Structure
Chick-fil-A, Inc. is a Georgia corporation formed March 23, 1964. Principal address: 5200 Buffington Road, Atlanta, GA 30349. The company uses the trade name "Chick-fil-A" exclusively. There are no parents or predecessors.

Key affiliates:
- **CFA Supply, LLC** (GA LLC, formed 2018): Approved distributor of food products, ingredients, and supplies. May be sole primary distributor in its service area. Began operations July 2019.
- **Bay Center Foods, LLC** (GA LLC, formed 2018): Produces proprietary lemon juice for Chick-fil-A Lemonade. May be sole approved supplier in its service area. Began operations 2020.
- **CFA Servco, Inc.** (VA corp): Administers gift card program.
- **CFA Properties, Inc.**: Owns all trademarks, patents, copyrights, trade secrets; licenses them to Chick-fil-A.
- **Truett's Group, LLC** (GA LLC, formerly Dwarf House Group): Operates ~18 Dwarf House and Truett's Grill heritage restaurants. 100% acquired by CFA on October 31, 2024. Not counted in Item 19 or Item 20 statistics.
- **International affiliates:** CFA Canada (since 2017), CFA Puerto Rico (since 2020), CFA Asia/Singapore (2020, not yet offering franchises), CFA UK (2022/2025).

The franchise model is unique: CFA enters into franchise agreements only with individuals (not entities), though assignment to a wholly-owned entity is permitted. CFA selects and controls all restaurant locations, owns or leases all premises, and provides all equipment. The operator's out-of-pocket investment is just the $10,000 franchise fee.

Restaurant types: free-standing, mall, in-line, drive-thru only, captive venue, delivery kitchen, and food trucks. Each has different occupancy agreements.

### Item 5 — Initial Fees
- **Initial franchise fee:** $10,000 ($5,000 fully earned and non-refundable; $5,000 working capital deposit refundable at termination after final reconciliation within 2.5 months).
- **Additional business fee:** $5,000 per additional restaurant.
- **Pre-opening insurance:** $4,100–$200,000 annually (not due until opening).
- **Opening inventory from CFA Supply:** $15,000–$65,000 (not due until opening).

### Item 6 — Fee Stack (All Fees)
The fee structure is extraordinarily complex, built around a profit-sharing formula:

**Ongoing Fees:**
| Fee | Amount | Frequency |
|-----|--------|-----------|
| Advertising Fund | 0%–3.25% of GR (currently 0% since June 30, 1989) | Monthly |
| Advertising Support & Services | $100/hr blended rate + costs | As incurred |
| Additional Franchise Fee | $5,000 per additional business | At signing |
| Business Services Fee | $300/mo | Monthly |
| Rent (Traditional) | $2,725–$96,285/mo (includes percentage rent) | Monthly |
| Occupancy Charge (Captive Venue) | 4%–30% of GR | Monthly or per Concession Agreement |
| Use/Occupancy Charge (Delivery Kitchen) | 4%–30% of GR | Monthly or per DK Lease |
| Food Truck Usage Fee (Subleased) | $2,100–$3,738/mo + additional costs | Monthly |
| Food Truck Fee (Vendor-leased) | $7,000–$7,500 | At signing or monthly |
| Food Truck Insurance Fee | $305–$525/mo | Monthly |
| Insurance | $260–$10,240/mo | Monthly |
| Equipment Rental | $750–$5,000/mo | Monthly |
| Hardware/Software Support + Internet | $9,500–$20,000/yr | As incurred |
| Operating Service Fees (Base + Additional) | Formula-based | Monthly |
| Credit Card/Gift Card Fees | Varies | As incurred |
| Highway Signage | Varies | As incurred |
| Interest on Late Payments | Max legal rate or 1.25%/mo | As incurred |
| Cash Handling System | $85–$450/mo | Monthly |
| Fines (Minimum Standards) | Varies | As incurred |
| Indemnification | Varies | As incurred |
| Holdover Liquidated Damages | Double base + percentage rent | As incurred |

**Operating Service Fee Formula:**
- **Base Operating Service Fee** = 15% of Gross Receipts minus (equipment rental + business services fee) for each restaurant per month.
- **Operating Profit** = Gross Receipts minus ordinary and necessary expenses (excluding operator compensation, above-market family wages, and the service fees themselves).
- **Net Profit (Initial Business)** = Operating Profit minus Base Operating Service Fee minus Base Profit ($1,000/mo) minus offsets.
- **Additional Operating Service Fee** = 50% of Net Profit.
- **Operator retains:** Base Profit ($1,000/mo), Extra Profit (up to $1,500/mo advance on Additional Profit), and 50% of Net Profit (Additional Profit).

The net effect: CFA takes approximately 15% of gross receipts plus 50% of net profits after all expenses. The operator retains about 50% of net profits plus guaranteed draws of $1,000/mo (Base Profit) and $1,500/mo (Extra Profit).

**Rent Policy:** Internal CFA policy (since Jan 1, 2015) caps rent at 6% of annual GR. This is not contractual and can be revoked at any time.

Equipment rental by type:
- Free-standing and in-line: $5,000/mo
- Drive-through only: $4,000/mo
- Mall: $3,000/mo
- Captive venue: $750–$3,000/mo
- Delivery kitchen: $2,500–$5,000/mo

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Supplier Restrictions
CFA maintains extensive supplier control:
- **Proprietary seasoning:** CFA purchases from producers, shipped to authorized poultry suppliers who apply it. CFA receives ~$0.06/lb of seasoned chicken.
- **Proprietary seasoned coater:** Purchased by CFA from producer, sold to distributors who resell to Operators. CFA applies a markup.
- **CFA Supply (affiliate distributor):** May be sole primary distributor in its service area. Pricing includes distribution fee plus fuel/freight allowance.
- **Bay Center Foods (affiliate):** Sole supplier of proprietary lemon juice in its service area.
- **CFA Servco (affiliate):** Gift card program administrator.
- **All food, drink, non-food inventory, ingredients, packaging, and marketing materials** must come from CFA-approved suppliers. CFA can disapprove suppliers at any time.
- **Insurance:** Mandatory participation in The Chicken Coop Insurance Company (Operator-owned captive reinsurance company) for workers' comp, GL, auto, and EPLI.
- CFA and affiliates earned $1,778,691,073 (19.63% of $9.06B total consolidated revenue) from Operator/Licensee purchases and leases in 2024.
- CFA Supply received $1,996,943,030 from Operator/Licensee purchases.
- Bay Center received $186,616,788 from distributor purchases.
- Estimated 40%–63% of overall purchases from approved/designated suppliers.
- National advertising fund receives supplier contributions of up to 33% of Operator purchases from designated suppliers.
- Warehouse goods at CFA cost + ~2.5%.

No formal process for evaluating alternative suppliers. CFA reserves right to designate sole suppliers for any item.

## D. Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
**No exclusive or protected territory.** This is one of the most restrictive territory provisions in franchising:
- License limited to specific location(s) designated by CFA
- No exclusive grant, area, territorial rights, or right to exclude other locations
- CFA has unrestricted right to open company-owned, franchised, or licensed outlets anywhere
- CFA may use any distribution channel (Internet, catalog, telemarketing) within or outside Operator's trading area
- Any internal policies CFA develops regarding restaurant development are non-contractual
- Only CFA can decide to relocate a restaurant
- If Operator wants different location, must terminate existing FA and execute new FA with new fee
- Truett's Group (Dwarf House/Truett's Grill) restaurants may compete
- Licensed Units (schools, universities, airports, etc.) may compete
- Little Blue Menu (CFA affiliate delivery kitchen concept) being tested
- CFA may operate under different brand names in the future

### Item 13 — Trademarks
- All marks owned by CFA Properties, Inc. (affiliate)
- Licensed to Chick-fil-A, Inc. for sublicense to Operators
- 60+ registered marks on USPTO Principal Register

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term:** Terminates on December 31 of the year the FA is signed (effectively less than 1 year)
- **Renewal:** Automatic 1-year extensions unless 30 days' written notice by either party
- **Materially different terms risk:** CFA can change FA terms with 45 days' written notice before end of any term period
- Lease and Concession Sublicense terms match FA term

### Termination
**By Franchisee:** 30 days' written notice (subject to state law)
**By Franchisor Without Cause:** 
- 30 days' written notice; OR
- Immediately upon written notice + payment of greater of $1,000 or formula amount; OR
- Immediately without notice in first 90 days (no payment); OR
- Upon termination of the Lease; OR
- Upon death or permanent disability of Operator/Operator-Owner

**By Franchisor With Cause (non-curable defaults):**
- Public scandal
- Breach/threatened breach of any material duty (including Lease, manuals, prime lease)
- Failure to pay any required payment
- Business discontinued
- Fraud, bankruptcy, insolvency
- Failure to comply with policies, procedures, operating standards
- Failure to participate in or complete training
- Violation of in-term noncompete
- **Opening on Sunday or Christmas Day**
- Unauthorized assignment
- Force majeure (frustration by law, pandemic, etc.)

### Transfer
- **No transfer allowed** except to a single wholly-owned entity for convenience of ownership
- Zero transfers occurred in 2022, 2023, or 2024
- CFA has right to approve all transfers
- No ROFR or option to purchase
- CFA has no restriction on its right to assign

### Death/Disability
- FA terminates **automatically** upon death or permanent disability
- Working capital deposit refunded to estate

### Noncompete
- **During term:** 5 miles from any of Operator's locations OR any other existing Chick-fil-A Restaurant. Covers ownership, management, consulting, supervisory employment of any fast food/QSR.
- **Post-term:** 1 year, 5 miles from any of Operator's former locations. Same scope.
- Subject to state law overrides (Exhibit G)

### Dispute Resolution
- **No arbitration or mediation**
- Litigation in Atlanta, Georgia area
- Georgia law applies
- Jury trial waived
- Class claims waived
- Punitive damages waived
- Subject to state law overrides (14 states listed)

### Post-Termination Obligations
- Immediately vacate premises
- Return all equipment to CFA
- Pay all sums due
- Cease all trademark/patent/copyright use
- Return all manuals and proprietary information
- Terminate all banking, credit card, and delivery arrangements
- Comply with noncompete
