## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System Size and Reach:** Chick-fil-A operates one of the largest quick-service restaurant systems in the United States, with 2,629 franchised Operator restaurants, 55 company-operated restaurants, and 425 Licensed Units for a total of 3,109 domestic Chick-fil-A outlets as of December 31, 2024. System-wide sales approximated $22.75 billion in 2024, making Chick-fil-A one of the highest-grossing QSR brands per unit in the country.

2. **Entity Structure:** Chick-fil-A, Inc. is a Georgia corporation formed in 1964, privately held by the Cathy family. Key affiliates include CFA Supply (distribution), Bay Center Foods (proprietary lemon juice production), CFA Servco (gift cards), CFA Properties (IP ownership), and international affiliates in Canada, Puerto Rico, Singapore, and the United Kingdom. In October 2024, CFA acquired Truett's Group, LLC (formerly Dwarf House Group), which operates approximately 18 heritage-brand restaurants.

3. **Ownership History:** The Cathy family has maintained continuous ownership and control since S. Truett Cathy founded the original Dwarf Grill restaurant in 1946. Dan T. Cathy serves as Chair of the Board, Andrew T. Cathy as CEO (since November 2021), and Donald "Bubba" Cathy as Executive Vice President. There are no parents, predecessors, or outside investors required to be disclosed.

4. **Investment Range:** The total estimated initial investment ranges from $426,735 to $2,339,525. Critically, the only out-of-pocket capital required from the operator is a $10,000 franchise fee. Chick-fil-A provides the restaurant premises, all equipment, and furniture. All other costs are funded from restaurant operations after opening.

5. **Fee Burden:** The fee structure is a profit-sharing model, not a traditional royalty. Operators pay a Base Operating Service Fee of 15% of Gross Receipts (minus equipment rental and business services fee), plus an Additional Operating Service Fee of 50% of Net Profit. Rent is capped at 6% of annual GR by internal policy (non-contractual). Equipment rental ranges from $750 to $5,000 per month depending on restaurant type.

6. **Item 19 Highlights:** Non-mall franchised Operator restaurants (2,179 units) achieved median annual sales of $9.23 million and average annual sales of $9.32 million in 2024. Mall units (197) achieved median $3.39 million. However, no cost, expense, or profit data is disclosed — only revenue.

7. **Bottom-Quartile Risk:** The bottom third of non-mall units had annual sales below $8.325 million, and the lowest unit generated $1.879 million. For mall units, the bottom third was below $2.7 million with a floor of $1.22 million. Given the heavy fee burden (15% + 50%), bottom-quartile operators face significant economic pressure.

8. **Financial Health:** Chick-fil-A reported total consolidated revenue of $9.06 billion, net earnings of $1.044 billion, and total assets of $16.59 billion in 2024. The company is audited by PricewaterhouseCoopers with a clean (unqualified) opinion. Cash from operations was $2.39 billion, and the company invested $2.68 billion in property and equipment. Total debt was approximately $3.07 billion against equity of $5.84 billion.

9. **Growth Trajectory:** The system added 135 net franchised restaurants in 2024 (243 opened, 102 reacquired, 6 ceased other, 0 terminations). CFA projects 183 new franchised restaurant openings and 25 new Licensed Units in the next fiscal year. CFA Supply expanded from 3 distribution centers in 2022 to 9 in 2024.

10. **Territory:** No exclusive or protected territory. Chick-fil-A has unrestricted rights to open competing outlets anywhere and through any distribution channel. This is one of the most permissive encroachment provisions in franchising.

11. **Renewal and Term:** The franchise term expires on December 31 of the year it is signed, with automatic 1-year renewals unless either party gives 30 days' notice. CFA can change terms with 45 days' notice. This is an extremely short-term commitment with no guaranteed long-term occupancy.

12. **Dispute Resolution:** All disputes must be litigated in Atlanta, Georgia under Georgia law. Operators waive jury trial, class claims, and punitive damages. Subject to state law overrides in 13 states.

13. **Special Risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution in Georgia is the only state-required special risk disclosure.

14. **Unique Model Characteristics:** Chick-fil-A's franchise model is fundamentally different from most QSR brands. The operator is more akin to a profit-sharing general manager than a traditional franchise owner. Zero franchise transfers have occurred in three years because the franchise is not transferable to third parties — it is a personal license that terminates on death, disability, or 30 days' notice by either party.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

Chick-fil-A, Inc. is a Georgia corporation with no parents or predecessors. The company was formed on March 23, 1964, and its principal business is the development and franchising of quick-service chicken restaurants. The franchise is offered exclusively to individuals — not partnerships, corporations, or LLCs — although post-grant assignment to a wholly-owned entity is permitted under strict conditions.

The company has nine disclosed affiliates, each playing a specific role in the system: CFA Supply (distribution), Bay Center Foods (proprietary lemon juice), CFA Servco (gift cards), CFA Properties (IP), CFA Canada, CFA Puerto Rico, CFA Asia, CFA UK, and Truett's Group. The acquisition of Truett's Group in October 2024 for $67.6 million added approximately 18 heritage-brand restaurants to the CFA portfolio.

### Fee Stack

The Chick-fil-A fee structure is unlike any other major franchise system. Rather than a fixed royalty percentage, CFA operates a complex profit-sharing arrangement that effectively captures a large share of restaurant economics:

**Base Operating Service Fee:** This is calculated as 15% of Gross Receipts minus the monthly equipment rental ($750–$5,000) and business services fee ($300). For a restaurant with $9 million in annual gross receipts, this fee would be approximately $1.28–$1.29 million per year. The financial statement notes indicate the actual range is 12%–15%, with base operating service fees totaling $3.21 billion across the system in 2024.

**Additional Operating Service Fee:** 50% of Net Profit, where Net Profit is defined as Operating Profit minus the Base Operating Service Fee, minus Base Profit ($1,000/mo), minus various offset amounts. The system collected $1.29 billion in additional operating service fees in 2024.

**Rent:** Traditional locations range from $2,725 to $96,285 per month, including percentage rent where applicable. An internal rent policy (non-contractual, revocable at CFA's sole discretion) caps rent at 6% of annual Gross Receipts. Rental income from operators totaled $1.60 billion in 2024. For captive venue and delivery kitchen units, occupancy charges of 4%–30% of GR replace traditional rent.

**Equipment Rental:** $750–$5,000 per month depending on restaurant type (free-standing/in-line: $5,000; drive-thru only: $4,000; mall: $3,000; captive venue: $750–$3,000; delivery kitchen: $2,500–$5,000).

**Advertising:** Currently 0% of Gross Receipts (reduced from 3.25% in July 1989). CFA reserves the right to reinstate up to 3.25% at any time. Local/regional advertising funds may be established by majority vote of operators in a market area.

**Business Services Fee:** $300 per month, mandatory.

**Hardware/Software Support:** $9,500–$20,000 per year for POS system support, software upgrades, and internet access.

**Insurance:** $260–$10,240 per month. All operators must participate in The Chicken Coop Insurance Company, a captive reinsurer owned by the operators themselves.

**Cash Handling:** $85–$450 per month for cash management services.

**Other fees:** Credit card processing (system-negotiated rates), highway signage (variable), fines for non-compliance with standards, indemnification for claims, interest on late payments (max legal rate or 1.25%/mo compounded), and holdover liquidated damages (double rent) if operator fails to vacate after termination.

**Effective Operator Economics:** Under this structure, the operator's net take-home is approximately: 50% of Net Profit (after all expenses, including CFA's 15% base fee, rent, equipment rental, business services, insurance, and all other costs), plus a guaranteed Base Profit of $1,000/month and Extra Profit advance of $1,500/month. The actual dollar take-home depends entirely on restaurant profitability, which is not disclosed in the FDD.

### Initial Investment

The total estimated initial investment ranges from $426,735 to $2,339,525, but this figure is misleading because the operator's actual cash outlay is just $10,000. Chick-fil-A's model provides the premises, builds the restaurant, purchases and installs all equipment, and finances the opening. All costs beyond the $10,000 franchise fee are deducted from restaurant operations after opening.

The $10,000 franchise fee consists of $5,000 fully earned on payment and $5,000 maintained as a working capital deposit, refundable upon termination within 2.5 months after final reconciliation.

For additional restaurants offered by CFA, the fee is $5,000 per additional location.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting

### Supplier Restrictions

Chick-fil-A maintains among the most comprehensive supplier control systems in franchising. All food, beverages, non-food inventory, ingredients, packaging, marketing materials, and most equipment must come from CFA-approved suppliers. CFA has not adopted formal criteria for evaluating alternative suppliers and handles requests case-by-case, reserving the right to designate sole suppliers for any item.

Key proprietary supply chain components include:
- **Proprietary seasoning:** CFA purchases from producers, shipped to authorized poultry suppliers who apply it to chicken. CFA receives approximately $0.06 per pound of seasoned chicken.
- **Proprietary seasoned coater:** CFA purchases from producers and sells to distributors with a markup; distributors resell to operators.
- **Proprietary lemon juice:** Bay Center Foods (CFA affiliate) produces lemon juice for Chick-fil-A Lemonade. May be the sole approved supplier in its service area.
- **CFA Supply (affiliate):** The primary distributor of food products, ingredients, and supplies. May be the sole approved primary distributor in its service area. CFA Supply grew from 3 to 9 distribution centers between 2022 and 2024.

In 2024, CFA and its affiliates collected $1.78 billion (19.63% of total consolidated revenue) from operator/licensee purchases and leases. CFA Supply separately collected $1.997 billion from operator/licensee purchases. Bay Center received $186.6 million from distributor purchases.

Operators estimate that 40%–63% of their overall purchases come from approved or designated suppliers. The national advertising fund also receives supplier contributions of up to 33% of operator purchases from designated suppliers.

### Training Program

The initial training program lasts approximately 5 weeks and includes approximately 97 hours of instructor-led training in Atlanta, Georgia, 98 hours of on-the-job training in company-operated restaurants, and 8 hours at the WinShape Retreat in Mt. Berry, Georgia. Training is conducted approximately 7 times per year. CFA pays for all training costs; the operator is not compensated during training.

Training covers orientation, ServSafe certification, leadership, customer care, financial systems, inventory management, food safety, facilities, employment law, and launch support.

### Technology Stack

CFA provides the POS hardware/software system and management PC as part of the equipment rental. Operators pay $9,500–$20,000 per year for hardware/software support, upgrades, and internet access. CFA has independent access to all POS data. The operations and training manual is 619 pages.

### Advertising

The advertising fund contribution has been 0% of Gross Receipts since July 1, 1989, but CFA reserves the right to reinstate it up to 3.25% at any time. Local and regional advertising may be established by majority vote of operators in a geographic area. CFA administers these funds but they are not audited. CFA's own national advertising spending is funded from general revenue — the financial statements show $46.2 million in advertising revenues recognized from franchisee contributions for national media activities in 2024.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

Chick-fil-A provides **no exclusive or protected territory** of any kind. The franchise agreement contains no exclusive grant, exclusive area, territorial rights, or right to exclude, disapprove, control, or impose conditions on any current or future CFA restaurant.

CFA retains unlimited rights to:
- Open company-owned, franchised, or licensed outlets anywhere, including within the immediate trading area of an existing operator's restaurant
- Use any distribution channel including Internet, catalog, telemarketing, or other direct marketing
- Develop any internal policies regarding restaurant placement (these are non-contractual and revocable)
- License others to sell CFA products at captive audience locations (schools, universities, airports, hospitals)
- Operate under different brand names (currently testing "Little Blue Menu" delivery kitchen concept through affiliate New Mile Ventures, LLC)

The Truett's Group legacy restaurants (Dwarf House, Truett's Grill) may compete with franchised operators depending on proximity. Only CFA can decide to relocate a restaurant. If an operator wants a different location, they must terminate the existing franchise agreement and execute a new one with a new franchise fee.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The franchise term is remarkably short: it terminates on December 31 of the year the franchise agreement is signed, meaning the initial term is always less than one year. The agreement auto-extends for 1-year periods unless either party gives 30 days' written notice of non-renewal.

CFA can change the terms of the franchise agreement by providing 45 days' written notice before the end of any term period. This means the operator has no guaranteed long-term stability — the terms of the deal can change annually.

### Termination

**By Franchisee:** 30 days' written notice to CFA.

**By CFA Without Cause:** CFA can terminate with 30 days' notice, or immediately upon written notice plus payment of the greater of $1,000 or a formula-based amount, or immediately without notice or payment in the first 90 days, or upon termination of the site lease, or automatically upon the operator's death or permanent disability.

**By CFA With Cause:** Listed defaults include breach of any material duty, failure to pay, public scandal, fraud, bankruptcy, failure to comply with standards, failure to complete training, violation of noncompete, opening on Sunday or Christmas Day, unauthorized assignment, and force majeure (including pandemic/epidemic).

The Sunday and Christmas Day closure requirement is distinctive and reflects CFA's corporate values. It is a terminable default if violated.

### Transfer

No transfer is permitted except to a single wholly-owned entity for convenience of ownership. Third-party transfers are not allowed. Zero transfers occurred in 2022, 2023, or 2024. The franchise has no resale value because it cannot be sold.

### Death/Disability

The franchise agreement terminates **automatically** upon the death or permanent disability of the operator (or Operator-Owner if assigned to an entity). The working capital deposit is refunded to the estate.

### Noncompete

During the franchise term: the operator may not own, manage, consult for, or be employed by any fast food or QSR within 5 miles of any of their CFA locations **or** any other existing CFA restaurant anywhere. Given that CFA has over 2,600 domestic restaurants, this effectively prohibits any QSR involvement nationwide.

Post-term: 1 year within 5 miles of any of the operator's former CFA locations. Both provisions are subject to state law overrides.

### Dispute Resolution

No arbitration or mediation is provided. All disputes must be litigated in the Atlanta, Georgia area under Georgia law. The operator waives jury trial, class claims, punitive damages, and rights to redeem after re-entry. These provisions are subject to override in 13 states with franchise relationship laws.

## F. Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations

Chick-fil-A discloses revenue data for 2,380 qualifying domestic non-captive venue franchised Operator restaurants that were open for a full calendar year as of December 31, 2024.

**Non-Mall Restaurants (2,179 units — free-standing and drive-thru only):**
The median annual sales volume was $9,226,669 and the average was $9,317,007. The highest unit generated $19,318,796 and the lowest $1,878,888. Approximately 49% of units met or exceeded the average. The bottom third had sales below $8,325,000 and the top third exceeded $10,155,000.

**Mall Restaurants (197 units):**
The median annual sales was $3,386,837 and the average was $4,516,855. The range was $1,219,834 to $18,918,134. Only 34% of units met or exceeded the average, reflecting significant skew. The bottom third was below $2,700,000 and the top third above $4,500,000.

**Delivery Kitchen Restaurants (4 units):**
With only 4 qualifying units, this is a very small sample. The median was $3,048,803 and average $3,526,342 (range $2,786,286–$5,221,575).

**Critical Limitations:**
- Revenue only — no cost, expense, profit, or take-home data is disclosed
- The FDD explicitly states: "The Financial Performance Representations do not reflect the costs of sales or operating expenses that must be deducted from the gross revenue or gross sales figures to obtain your net income or profit."
- 25 captive venue units, 55 company-operated restaurants, 425 Licensed Units, and restaurants not open a full year are excluded
- 19 restaurants permanently closed during 2024 (3 mall, 16 non-mall)

**System-Wide Sales Context:** The financial statement notes disclose total system-wide sales of approximately $22.75 billion in 2024 across 2,730 restaurants (including international), implying system-wide average sales per unit of approximately $8.33 million.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### System-Wide Trajectory

Chick-fil-A has been in sustained, rapid growth mode. Total franchised restaurants grew from 2,230 at the start of 2022 to 2,629 at the end of 2024 — a net increase of 399 units (17.9%) over three years. Company-owned restaurants decreased from 81 to 55 over the same period as CFA continued its pattern of operating restaurants temporarily and then transitioning them to operators.

Licensed Units grew from 393 to 425 over the same three years, with CFA projecting 25 additional licensed openings in the next fiscal year.

### Transfer Activity

Zero transfers occurred in any of the three reporting years (2022, 2023, 2024). This is not a mere data point — it reflects the fundamental design of the Chick-fil-A model. The franchise is a personal, non-transferable license. An operator cannot sell their franchise to a third party. When an operator leaves the system for any reason, the restaurant reverts to CFA.

### Reacquisition and Turnover

In 2024, CFA reacquired 102 franchised restaurants from operators. The financial statement notes clarify that "reacquired" includes all instances where a restaurant transfers from an operator to CFA for any reason — voluntary termination (often to obtain a commitment for a different restaurant), CFA termination, mutual consent, or operator departure.

CFA then re-sold 86 of its company-operated restaurants to new operators in 2024, closed 25 company-operated units, and ended the year with 55 company-owned restaurants. The high reacquisition and re-sale volume reflects CFA's active management of its operator pool — moving operators between locations, replacing underperformers, and managing system transitions.

### Projected Growth

CFA projects 183 new franchised restaurant openings and 25 new Licensed Unit openings in the next fiscal year. There are 38 franchise agreements already signed but not yet opened, representing a strong development pipeline. The largest projected growth states are Texas (20), Florida (20), California (17), Georgia (11), and New York (10).

### Gag Clause

The FDD discloses: "During the last three fiscal years, some former Operators have signed provisions restricting their ability to speak openly about their experiences with us." This gag clause means that prospective operators should be aware that not all former operators can share their experiences freely, which may limit the quality of due diligence conversations.

## H. Item 21 — Financial Statements

### Auditor and Opinion

PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP (Atlanta, Georgia) issued an unqualified (clean) opinion on March 21, 2025, covering the consolidated financial statements for the three years ended December 31, 2024.

### Revenue and Earnings

Chick-fil-A's total consolidated revenue and income grew from $6.37 billion in 2022 to $7.89 billion in 2023 to $9.06 billion in 2024 — a 42% increase over two years. Revenue is composed of:

- **Franchise fees:** $4.50 billion (2024), consisting of $3.21 billion in base operating service fees (the 15% royalty) and $1.29 billion in additional operating service fees (the 50% profit share), less $52.8 million in franchisee incentives.
- **Rental income:** $1.60 billion from leasing premises and equipment to operators.
- **Product sales:** $2.35 billion, primarily from CFA Supply's distribution operations ($2.07 billion in distribution and lemon juice revenue) plus company-operated restaurant sales of $345 million.
- **Licensee fees:** $76.8 million from Licensed Units.
- **Other income:** $189 million including gift card program revenues ($16.9 million in breakage), advertising revenues ($46.2 million), and investment income.

Net earnings were $1.044 billion in 2024, $1.058 billion in 2023, and $989 million in 2022. The stable earnings despite rapidly growing revenue reflect increased SGA expenses ($4.20 billion in 2024 vs. $3.17 billion in 2022) and depreciation ($1.05 billion vs. $706 million).

### Balance Sheet

Total assets reached $16.59 billion in 2024, up from $14.49 billion in 2023. The asset base is dominated by property and equipment ($14.14 billion net), reflecting CFA's ownership/lease model — the company directly controls virtually all restaurant premises and equipment. Cash and equivalents were $1.41 billion.

Total liabilities were $10.74 billion, including $2.83 billion in long-term notes payable, $2.59 billion in long-term operating lease liabilities, and $1.75 billion in long-term finance lease liabilities. Total stockholders' equity was $5.84 billion, with retained earnings of $5.57 billion.

The debt maturity schedule shows $236 million due in 2025, $392 million in 2026, $724 million in 2027, $225 million in 2028, and $476 million in 2029, with $1.02 billion due thereafter.

### Cash Flow

Cash from operating activities was $2.39 billion in 2024, driven by $1.044 billion in net earnings and $1.054 billion in depreciation/amortization. The company invested $2.68 billion in property and equipment (new restaurant development and reinvestment) and raised $750 million from new notes payable. Treasury stock repurchases totaled $162 million and dividends paid were $37.6 million.

### Key Financial Observations

- **Leverage:** Debt-to-equity ratio of approximately 1.84x. Total liabilities to equity of 1.84x. Manageable given the strong, predictable cash flows from the franchise model.
- **Capital intensity:** $2.68 billion in annual capex reflects CFA's ownership model — the company builds and equips every restaurant. This is fundamentally different from franchise systems where franchisees fund development.
- **Distributions:** CFA repurchased $162 million of treasury stock (850,379 shares at ~$190/share implied) and paid $37.6 million in dividends. The company also issued 2,733,225 shares of Series A preferred stock in 2023 with a 7.0% cumulative dividend and $196/share liquidation preference.
- **Pension obligations:** The qualified defined benefit pension plan was underfunded by $202 million in 2024 (up from $117 million in 2023). A new non-qualified pension plan for senior management had an underfunded balance of $131 million. Combined pension underfunding of $333 million is material but manageable relative to the company's size.
- **Related party transactions:** CFA provides services to affiliates and received $6.6 million in administration fees. A principal shareholder sold $27.6 million in fixtures to CFA. The Truett's Group acquisition involved 13 land leases with related-party shareholders, modified at arm's length.

## I. State Addenda Summary

Exhibit G contains state-specific addenda and agreement amendments for 13 states: California, Hawaii, Illinois, Indiana, Maryland, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, South Dakota, Virginia, Washington, and Wisconsin.

Common override themes include:
- **Choice of law/venue:** State franchise laws may override the Georgia choice of law and Atlanta venue provisions
- **Noncompete restrictions:** May be limited or unenforceable in certain states (particularly California and North Dakota)
- **Termination protections:** State franchise relationship laws may impose good cause requirements for termination and non-renewal
- **Integration clause limitations:** State law may prevent disclaiming representations made outside the franchise agreement
- **California specifics:** CFA has a right of first refusal (60 days) to purchase the franchise interest — a provision not in the base franchise agreement
