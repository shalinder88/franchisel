In certain instances, Chick-fil-A, or a wholly-owned subsidiary of Chick-fil-A, may become the
owner of the premises on which a Chick-fil-A Restaurant is operated as a result of the exercise of
option rights under a ground lease or other type of acquisition. If this occurs during the term of
your Franchise Agreement and its attached Lease(s), your then-current sublease will terminate and
you will become either: (1) a lessee of the premises and must comply with the obligations imposed
by Chick-fil-A upon you as primary lessee of the premises, including making monthly lease and
other payments to Chick-fil-A; or (2) a sublessee of the premises and must comply with the
obligations imposed upon Chick-fil-A as primary lessee of the premises by Chick-fil-A’s affiliate
acting as the landlord, including making monthly sublease and other payments to Chick-fil-A or
Chick-fil-A’s affiliate, all as determined by Chick-fil-A in the exercise of its sole and exclusive
business judgment.

For a site that is owned by Chick-fil-A (or a wholly-owned subsidiary of Chick-fil-A), the
calculation of monthly rent for the site can generally be explained as follows – there will be no base
rent charge and the total amount of monthly rent you will be obligated to pay Chick-fil-A will be
based on a percentage rent calculation that Chick-fil-A will perform pursuant to the terms of the
Lease. To calculate the percentage and therefore monthly rent due to date each month of each year
under the Lease, the amount by which your then calendar year-to-date gross receipts for your Chick-
fil-A Restaurant business multiplied by the percentage rent multiplier set forth in the Lease exceeds
the percentage rent breakpoint will be determined by Chick-fil-A and such amount will be the total
amount of monthly rent due to date each month of each year under the Lease. The total amount of
monthly rent due to date less any amounts actually paid to date by the Operator for monthly rent
during the same calendar year will be determined by Chick-fil-A and such amount will be the
monthly rent due and owing for the calendar year. Because there is typically no base rent due under
this type of lease, the percentage rent breakpoint will be set typically to $0.00. As the Operator of
a site that is owned by Chick-fil-A (or a wholly-owned subsidiary of Chick-fil-A), you will not be
obligated generally to pay Chick-fil-A base rent under this type of lease beyond the percentage rent
due under the Lease as described above, subject to there being any additional lease/license costs
for the site (as discussed below), or any renewal and/or renegotiation with Chick-fil-A during the
term of your Franchise Agreement and the Lease.

If you are the Operator of a site that is owned by Chick-fil-A (or a wholly-owned subsidiary of
Chick-fil-A), there may be additional lease/license costs for the site. The amount of any additional
lease/license costs for the site (as that term is used above and in the Rent Calculation Report for the
site) could be different and set higher than $0.00 in certain situations. For example, if Chick-fil-A
has purchased the primary site and then leases from a third-party and then subleases to you
additional parking or other facilities either before or after the commencement of the Lease, then the
annual percentage rent breakpoint will be adjusted and reflected on your Rent Calculation Report
for the site to account for any additional lease/license costs you are obligated to pay under the
Lease. For example, if the additional parking sublease referenced above or any other similar
additional lease/license cost item was $12,000/year, or $1,000/month, the annual percentage rent
breakpoint would be set at $200,000 ($12,000/6%), not $0.00.

For a site that is leased by Chick-fil-A (or a wholly-owned subsidiary of Chick-fil-A) pursuant to
a ground lease, the calculation of monthly rent for the site can generally be explained as follows -
the total annual rent due under the ground lease, which sum will be divided by twelve. The monthly
base rent could increase when and if, among other things, the rent under the ground lease increases
pursuant to the terms of the ground lease. Additionally, as the Operator of a site that is leased by
Chick-fil-A (or a wholly-owned subsidiary of Chick-fil-A) pursuant to a ground lease, you will be
obligated to pay Chick-fil-A percentage rent under this type of lease to the extent the calendar year-
to-date gross receipts of a Chick-fil-A Restaurant business exceed a certain percentage rent
breakpoint.
