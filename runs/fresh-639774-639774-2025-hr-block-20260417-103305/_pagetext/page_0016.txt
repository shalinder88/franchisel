Vocational Consultants, LTD, et al. v. H&R Block Tax Services LLC, et al. (Case No. 13-L-11). Plaintiffs
filed suit in the Circuit Court of Crawford County, Illinois in September 2013, alleging that the company
violated the Illinois Franchise Disclosure Act and the Illinois Consumer Fraud and Deceptive Business
Practices Act by taking over two franchise offices operated by plaintiffs in Robinson and Marshall, Illinois
after plaintiffs failed to have a valid Electronic Filing Identification Number (EFIN) and were unable to
electronically file tax returns during the 2013 Tax Season. Plaintiffs claim that the company interfered with
their property and business rights by taking over the offices and terminating plaintiffs’ franchise agreement.
The company reached an agreement to settle the case for $840,000 in December 2018.

In the Matter of H&R Block, Inc., HRB Digital LLC, and HRB Tax Group, Inc. (FTC Docket No. 9427).
On February 23, 2024, the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) filed an administrative complaint before the
FTC alleging unfair or deceptive business acts or practices in connection with certain aspects of the
company’s DIY tax preparation services. A hearing before an administrative law judge (ALJ) of the FTC
was scheduled for October 23, 2024. Prior to the hearing, on October 21, 2024, the company reached an
agreement to resolve the allegations for a monetary payment of $7M, plus certain business practice changes
relating to its DIY products and advertising of Free DIY, which became final and effective on January 8,
2025.

 Colorado Tax Specialists Co. v. H&R Block Tax Services LLC (Case No. 4:25-CV-00047-RK). Plaintiff
filed suit in the United States District Court for the Western District of Missouri in January 2025 alleging
that the company wrongfully terminated its franchise agreements and violated the Missouri Franchise Act.
The company filed counterclaims alleging that plaintiff violated its franchise agreements by diverting
business to a third-party competitor and breached its post-termination contractual obligations. In August
2025, the court dismissed plaintiff’s Missouri Franchise Act claim without prejudice. The lawsuit is
ongoing.

H&R Block Tax Services LLC v. Calise Enterprises, LLC, Frank J. Calise and Edward H. Rodriguez (Case
No. 4:24-CV-00513-DGK). In August 2024, the company filed suit in the United States District Court for
the Western District of Missouri alleging that defendants breached their non-competition covenants and
other contractual post-termination obligations and failed to pay amounts owed under their franchise
agreements. Defendants filed counterclaims against the company alleging that the company wrongfully
terminated their franchise agreements and violated the Missouri Franchise Act. In May 2025, the parties
reached an agreement to settle the case, which included the parties’ joint filing of a motion for entry of
consent injunction, under which defendants are to honor their post-termination restrictive covenants through
April 30, 2026.
