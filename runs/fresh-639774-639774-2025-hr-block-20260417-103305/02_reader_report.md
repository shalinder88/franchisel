# 02 Reader Report — H&R Block Tax Services LLC (639774-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor**: H&R Block Tax Services LLC, a Missouri LLC and indirect wholly owned subsidiary of H&R Block, Inc. (NYSE: HRB). Organized July 1, 2008 via merger with predecessor H&R Block Tax Services, Inc.
2. **System Size**: 8,688 total outlets at FY2025 end (1,987 franchised, 6,701 company-owned). Franchised outlets have declined every year: 2,561 → 2,339 → 2,135 → 1,987 over FY2023–FY2025.
3. **Investment Range**: $34,080–$158,750 total initial investment for a new single retail office; initial franchise fee of $2,500.
4. **Fee Structure**: Tiered royalty — 60% on first $5,000 of revenue (50% if early-pay), 40% on revenue above $5,000 (30% if early-pay), 20% incentive rate on incremental revenue above 2-year average; 15% (10% early-pay) on business services; 20% on POM and Second Look.
5. **Item 19**: No financial performance representations made. Explicit refusal to provide FPR.
6. **Parent Guarantee**: H&R Block, Inc. unconditionally guarantees franchisor performance (Exhibit C).
7. **Financial Health (Parent)**: Revenue $3.76B (FY2025), net income $605.8M, total assets $3.26B, stockholders' equity $88.9M. Audited by Deloitte & Touche LLP, unqualified opinion. No going concern.
8. **Franchise Term**: Ends June 1 immediately following 10th full Tax Season after Effective Date.
9. **Renewal**: No automatic renewal; franchisor may offer successor franchise at sole discretion under then-current (potentially materially different) terms.
10. **Territory**: Non-exclusive. Franchisor and affiliates retain broad rights to compete via internet, other brands, company-owned offices outside territory, and acquired businesses.
11. **Noncompete**: During term — 45 miles from territory or any Network Office; post-term — 25 miles for 2 years + non-solicitation.
12. **Dispute Resolution**: Litigation in state or federal court, Jackson County, Missouri. Missouri law governs. Subject to state-specific amendments.
13. **Special Risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution (Missouri); seasonality of business (Jan–Apr peak).
14. **Gag Clause**: Confirmed — current and former franchisees have signed provisions restricting their ability to speak openly (p.56).
15. **Growth Trajectory**: Franchised system in sustained contraction. Reacquisitions by franchisor dominate outlet losses (118 reacquired in FY2025). Company-owned side is growing (+72 in FY2025).

### Item 6 — Fee Stack

**Royalty (tiered by revenue and payment timing)**:
- 60% on first $5,000 of calendar year revenue (50% if paid within 4 days of reporting period end)
- 40% on revenue above $5,000 (30% if paid within 4 days)
- 20% "Incentive Royalty Rate" on incremental revenue above 2-year average (if paid within 4 days; not eligible until 2 full Tax Seasons completed)
- 15% Product-Specific Royalty on business services revenue (10% if early-pay)
- 20% Product-Specific Royalty on POM and Second Look reviews

Note: The early-pay discount requires (a) payment within 4 days of reporting period end AND (b) no other amounts overdue.

**Reporting Periods**: Semi-monthly in Feb–Mar (1st–15th, 16th–end); monthly May–Jan; special periods in April around Tax Season end.

**POM Extended Service Plan**: Required. Per-premium charge (did not exceed $15 last Tax Season). $50 deductible per approved claim. Costs determined annually by actuarial evaluation.

**Transfer Fee**: $2,500 (may be waived at franchisor's discretion).

**Audit Fee**: Cost of inspection/audit — only if audit reveals ≥2% understatement of revenue.

**Additional Training**: $35 annual fee per participant for ongoing training beyond required training.

**Term Loan Default Interest**: 4% increase if franchisee defaults under either security agreement.

### Item 12 — Territory
Franchise Territory: described in Exhibit A to FLA. Rural = municipal boundaries; metro = specific address or mapped area. Minimum may be limited to a specific address.

**Non-exclusive**: Franchisee will NOT receive an exclusive territory. Franchisor, affiliates, and other franchisees may compete from locations outside the territory. Franchisor retains rights to:
- Sell internet/virtual tax services under any marks inside territory
- Sell non-Authorized Services under any marks inside territory
- Sell Authorized Services under non-Licensed Marks inside territory
- Sell Optional Services under Licensed Marks if franchisee declines to offer them
- Acquire or be acquired by competitors operating in territory
- No compensation to franchisee for exercising these rights

No options, ROFR, or rights to acquire additional franchises. Must get written permission to relocate or add locations. If franchisee closes/abandons a location, franchisor may operate it and amend territory to exclude it.

Continuation of territory rights does not depend on sales volume or market penetration.

## Pass D: Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

**Protected territory**: The territory is non-exclusive. The franchisor explicitly reserves the right to compete inside the territory through company-owned offices (outside territory boundaries), internet/virtual services, non-H&R Block brands, Optional Services if franchisee declines, and acquired businesses. No obligation to compensate franchisee.

**Channel restrictions**: Franchisee cannot operate internet website or sell online unless authorized. Must operate from Approved Locations only. National retail store or military base operations require FLA amendment.

**Encroachment risk**: High. Company-owned outlets are growing while franchise outlets contract. Reacquisitions dominate outlet losses. Franchisor retains "any products, any marks, any channel" reservation inside territory for internet/virtual services.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **Term**: Ends June 1 following 10th full Tax Season after Effective Date
- **Renewal**: No automatic renewal. Franchisor MAY offer successor franchise at sole discretion. Terms may be materially different.

### Termination
- **By franchisee**: 30 days' written notice before June 1 of any year, if not in default. Requires payment of 25% of 3-year gross sales average (or 100% of prior year if <3 years). Subject to state law.
- **By franchisor with cause (curable)**: 15+ days to cure breaches not listed as incurable.
- **By franchisor without cure**: Prohibited competition/disclosure, 3+ defaults in 12 months, criminal conviction likely to adversely affect business, fraud, e-file prohibition/suspension, assignment for benefit of creditors, receivership, bankruptcy, refusing inspection, false books, prohibited assignments, cross-default from termination of another franchise with related parties.
- **By franchisor without cause**: Not permitted.

### Post-Termination Obligations
Complete de-identification. Payment of all sums. Return of files. Assignment of phone numbers and leases (at franchisor's election). If franchisee owns real property, 2-year prohibition on similar-service use. Noncompete (see below).

### Transfer
Franchisor approval required for all transfers except: individual-to-wholly-owned entity, entity-to-substantially-same entity, and non-controlling equity changes. Conditions: payment of debts, no default, $2,500 transfer fee, transferee qualifications, potentially sign then-current FLA. Franchisor ROFR: 30 days to match offer terms.

### Death/Disability
Estate/legal representative must complete transfer within 6 months.

### Noncompete
- **During term**: Franchisee and Associates prohibited from owning/working at similar business within 45 miles of territory or any Network Office.
- **Post-term**: 2-year prohibition within 25 miles of territory + non-solicitation of clients. Exception if franchisee terminates under §15 and meets all requirements.
- Subject to state law modifications.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue**: State or federal court, Jackson County, Missouri
- **Governing law**: Missouri
- Subject to state-specific amendments (11 states have amendments)
- No arbitration/mediation provision

### Other Key Provisions
- No restriction on franchisor's right to transfer/assign
- Modification must be in writing signed by both parties
- Integration clause — only FLA terms binding (subject to state law)
- Limitation on litigation (§39 of FLA)
