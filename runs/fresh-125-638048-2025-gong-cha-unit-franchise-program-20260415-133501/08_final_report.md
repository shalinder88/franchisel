## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size:** 229 US Gong cha stores at year-end 2024 (including master franchisee system); only 6 are direct franchisor outlets (4 franchised, 2 company-owned). Globally, 2,162 stores in 27 markets.
2. **Entity structure:** Gong cha USA Franchising, LLC is a Delaware LLC formed January 10, 2023, wholly owned through a chain: GCA → GCG (UK, IP owner) → Midco → Holdco → Gong Cha Limited. The franchisor does not own the intellectual property; it licenses it from GCG.
3. **Ownership history:** Originally founded 2006 in Taiwan (Royal Tea Taiwan / GCI). Restructured in 2020 (IP to GCG) and 2023 (US Restructuring creating current franchisor). Active consolidation: buying out CA and 13-state master franchisees in 2025–2026.
4. **Investment range:** $184,750 to $627,060 for a 500–1,500 sq ft unit. Leasehold improvements ($51,500–$270,500) drive most of the variance.
5. **Fee burden:** 6% royalty + 1%–2% brand marketing + up to 2% regional marketing + local advertising = potentially 10%–12% of Net Sales in ongoing fees and required spending, plus technology SaaS ($295/month + $313/year).
6. **Item 19 highlights:** Median Net Sales of $401,211 across 203 stores. Average $439,522. Top quartile averages $711,899. No cost data — profitability unknown.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** 52 stores in the bottom quartile averaged $227,558; the lowest store had $72,037 in annual Net Sales. At these levels, covering royalties, marketing, COGS, rent, and labor would be extremely challenging.
8. **Financial health:** Franchisor is profitable but small: $4.1M revenue, $361K net income, $1.015M equity on $2.17M assets. Dependent on related-party service agreement revenue ($1.5M from GCG). $3.6M acquisition of GCCA is a material commitment relative to the balance sheet.
9. **Growth trajectory:** Systemwide growth decelerated sharply: +40 (2022), +37 (2023), +5 (2024). Driven partly by master franchisee transitions and 3 development right terminations, but signals a maturing US expansion.
10. **Territory:** Up to 2-mile radius, not exclusive, with broad franchisor reservations (Non-Traditional Sites, internet, alternative brands, acquisitions, delivery).
11. **Renewal:** One 10-year renewal. Franchisee must sign new FA with potentially materially different terms, remodel, execute general release, and pay 50% of then-current IFF.
12. **Disputes:** Mediation then arbitration in Massachusetts; Massachusetts law. State addenda for CA, HI, MN, WI override forum/choice-of-law.
13. **Special risks (state-flagged):** Out-of-state dispute resolution (MA), spousal liability via guaranty, and short operating history.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
- Typical: up to 2-mile radius around Store
- During FA term, if in compliance: no other Gong cha store within Territory

### Carve-Outs and Reservations
The territory protection has broad exceptions:
1. **Non-Traditional Sites excluded:** airports, stadiums, malls, casinos, hospitals, universities, military complexes, convention centers, etc. — Gong cha can be placed within your Territory at any of these.
2. **Outside Territory:** Franchisor can operate or license stores at any location outside Territory without limit.
3. **Alternative brands:** Franchisor can operate different-branded businesses within Territory.
4. **Retail/internet/mail order:** Franchisor can sell products (including under Gong cha marks) through grocery, convenience, internet, mail order within Territory.
5. **Delivery:** Other Gong cha stores can deliver into your Territory.
6. **Acquisitions:** Franchisor can acquire and operate competing businesses within Territory if part of an Acquired System. If not part of an Acquired System, franchisee gets option to purchase.

### Channel Restrictions on Franchisee
- All sales face-to-face at Store (eat-in, carry-out)
- Delivery: requires approval; may be required to use specific approved platforms
- No internet, wholesale, grocery, other distribution without consent
- No unapproved social media or website

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **Initial term:** 10 years
- **Renewal:** 1 additional 10-year term, subject to conditions:
  - Written notice
  - Good standing; no defaults
  - Sign new Franchise Agreement ("which may contain materially different terms and conditions")
  - Comply with then-current qualifications and training
  - Remodel Store to then-current standards
  - Evidence of property control
  - Execute general release
  - Pay Renewal Fee (50% of then-current standard IFF)
- **"Materially different" risk:** The renewal FA may have different royalty rates, different territory provisions, or other changed terms. This is one of the most significant contract risks.

### Transfer Provisions
- All transfers require franchisor approval.
- Transferor must: be current on all obligations, not in default, pay Transfer Fee, execute release.
- Transferee must: meet qualifications, sign new FA (may be materially different), upgrade Store, complete training.
- No new IFF required from transferee (but Transfer Fee = 50% of then-current IFF).
- ROFR: Franchisor can match any third-party offer.

### Termination
- No termination right for franchisee.
- Franchisor terminates only for cause.
- Curable defaults: 30 days to cure (7 days for monetary, inspection refusal, or operating hours).
- Non-curable defaults include: failure to open on time, fraud/conviction, public health threat, loss of premises, unauthorized transfer, false records, confidentiality breach, mark misuse, repeated defaults (3+ in 12 months), abandonment, insolvency.
- Cross-default: IP License Agreement termination between GCG and franchisor would trigger FA termination.

### Death and Disability
- Death: rights must be assigned to approved party within 12 months.
- Permanent disability: assigned within 6 months after notice of disability.

### Noncompete Restrictions
- **During term:** No involvement in any business that primarily offers or sells tea products, or is same/similar to Gong cha. No diverting customers.
- **Post-term:** 2 years, no involvement in any business primarily offering bubble tea, coffees/teas, smoothies, or juices within 5-mile radius of your Store or any other Gong cha store. State overrides may limit enforceability (CA: may be unenforceable).

### Dispute Resolution
- **Process:** Mediation first (mandatory), then binding arbitration.
- **Venue:** Massachusetts (subject to state overrides).
- **Governing law:** Massachusetts (subject to state overrides).
- **Jury trial waiver:** Implied through arbitration.
- **State overrides:** CA (noncompete may be void, choice of law may not be enforceable), MN (no out-of-state litigation/arbitration, no jury trial waiver), MI (no out-of-state arbitration), WI (90-day notice, 60-day cure required).

### Litigation History
Four matters, all resolved, all relating to predecessor/affiliate activity:
1. CA registration violation by GCI: $7,500 penalties (consent order Jan 2025)
2. GCCA renewal dispute: settled Jul 2021, no damages
3. MD registration violations (GCI, franchisor, GCDMV): $30,000 penalty (consent order Dec 2024)
4. VA registration violations (GCI, franchisor): $3,000 penalty (settlement order Mar 2025)

No pending litigation.
