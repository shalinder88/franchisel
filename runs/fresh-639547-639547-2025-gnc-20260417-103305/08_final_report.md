## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **GNC Holdings, LLC** is a Delaware limited liability company headquartered in Pittsburgh, PA, offering franchises for retail health, wellness and performance stores specializing in vitamins, minerals, supplements, sports nutrition, beauty/health products under the GNC, GNC Live Well, and General Nutrition Centers marks.

2. **Ownership**: GNC is ultimately owned by Harbin Pharmaceutical Group Holding Co., Ltd. (China), through intermediate holding companies ZT Biopharmaceutical LLC and Jacks Management (Suzhou) Limited. The current entity acquired the assets of bankrupt predecessor General Nutrition Corporation on October 7, 2020.

3. **System size**: As of December 31, 2024, the system comprised 703 franchised stores and 1,437 company-owned stores (2,140 total) across the United States. The system has been shrinking rapidly: franchised stores declined from 789 (2022) to 703 (2024), a loss of 86 units in two years. Company-owned stores declined from 1,555 to 1,437 in 2024 alone (118 net closures). Total system contracted by 165 outlets in 2024.

4. **Investment**: New franchise store total investment ranges from $187,719 to $506,542. Conversion stores range from $112,719 to $463,042. The initial franchise fee is $20,000 for new franchisees ($15,000 for existing/employees/veterans).

5. **Fee burden**: Ongoing fees total approximately 9%+ of gross sales (6% royalty + 3% advertising) plus $358+/month in technology fees plus optional $220–$345/month GNFS financial services. The combined fee load at median gross sales (~$444K) consumes approximately $40,000 in royalties and advertising alone, before technology, rent, payroll, COGS, and other operating costs.

6. **Item 19**: Average franchised store gross sales of $475,925 and median of $443,685 (675 stores, FY 2024). Only 41% of stores met or exceeded the average. No cost/expense/EBITDA data is disclosed, making unit-level profitability analysis impossible from this FDD alone.

7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: With the low end of gross sales at $139,454 and the extensive fee burden (9%+ of gross plus fixed tech/service fees plus rent), stores in the lower quartile face severe profitability pressure. The $60,000 minimum inventory requirement further constrains working capital for struggling locations.

8. **Financial health**: PricewaterhouseCoopers issued a clean unqualified audit opinion with no going concern language. However, the financial trajectory is deeply concerning: revenue declined from $1.47B (2022) to $1.17B (2024) — a 20% decline in two years. Net losses of $141.2M in 2024 and $32.3M in 2023 followed the only profitable year (2022, net income $10M). Total debt of $371.5M against member's equity of only $60.6M. Current liabilities ($454.5M) exceed current assets ($309.9M), indicating negative working capital. Long-lived asset impairments of $23.1M in 2024 signal management's own assessment of declining asset value.

9. **Growth trajectory**: Negative across all metrics. Only 3 new franchised and 2 new company-owned outlets projected for the next fiscal year, against a system losing 165 outlets per year. No area developers currently in the system.

10. **Territory**: Non-exclusive, minimal protective period (1 year only), with extensive franchisor reservation of rights for alternative channels (GNC.com, Amazon, Walmart.com, Tik Tok Shop, Uber Eats, wholesale to Rite Aid, CVS, Walmart, and other major retailers). Territory may be reduced upon renewal.

11. **Renewal**: 5-year terms with renewal available, but franchisee may be required to sign a form with "materially different terms and conditions." Renewal fee of $12,500 plus potential remodel ($25,000–$150,000). General release required.

12. **Disputes**: Arbitration in Pennsylvania; Pennsylvania law. Jury trial waiver. Confession of judgment. State addenda may override in 12 states.

13. **Special risks flagged**: Out-of-state dispute resolution, spousal liability for all financial obligations, supplier control (95%+ from franchisor), and short operating history of current entity (since 2020).

14. **Key leadership concern**: Both the COO (Rodney Lastinger) and CFO (Oded Shein) have connections to Conn's Home Plus, which filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in July 2024. Lastinger was President of Conn's when it filed; Shein was a Director and Audit Committee Chair.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure

GNC Holdings, LLC was formed after Harbin Pharmaceutical Group acquired the assets of bankrupt General Nutrition Corporation through a Chapter 11 sale in October 2020. The franchise is for a fixed-location retail GNC store selling health, wellness and performance products. The system also offers an Area Development Agreement (minimum 2 stores) and an optional Smoothie Bar addendum.

The competitive landscape includes independent and chain vitamin/health stores, mass merchandisers, drug stores, grocery stores, and online retailers. GNC itself competes with its franchisees through GNC.com, third-party marketplaces (Amazon, Walmart.com, Tik Tok Shop, Uber Eats), and wholesale relationships with Rite Aid, CVS, Walmart, Walgreens, Sam's Club, and other major retailers.

### Fee Stack

The ongoing fee burden is substantial and multi-layered:

**Percentage-based fees**: The royalty of 6% of total gross sales and national advertising fund contribution of 3% combine to a 9% ongoing fee on all revenue. The advertising fee can be changed on 30 days' notice, and company-owned stores are not required to contribute to the fund.

**Technology fees**: A mandatory monthly technology stack costs approximately $358 minimum: POS maintenance ($75 first register + $25 second), network connection ($233), credit card processing ($16), in-store Wi-Fi ($18), and cellular plan ($16 if iPad used). These fees are subject to annual re-evaluation and can increase without cap.

**Financial services (GNFS)**: Optional but potentially required financial services range from $220/month (15+ stores) to $345/month (single store). Services include accounting, financial statements, bank reconciliation, and annual physical inventory. The 3-month free opt-in incentive requires a 12-month commitment.

**Event-driven fees**: Transfer fees up to the then-current initial franchise fee (or $1,000 intra-family, $2,500 for minority interest). Relocation fee of $10,000+. Remodeling $25,000–$150,000 every 5 years. Renewal fee $12,500. Default cure fee up to $1,000. Annual inventory fee $540. Securities offering fee $5,000.

**Penalty fees**: Late payment at 1.5% per month. Insufficient funds $30 per occurrence. Insurance reimbursement $500. Non-compliance management fee of 25% of gross sales (if franchisor assumes operations). Operating management fee $2,000/week (death/disability).

**Liquidated damages**: On termination for default, franchisee must pay the greater of (a) average monthly royalty for the prior 2 years or (b) average monthly royalty for the prior 12 months, multiplied by the remaining months in the term. For a store with $475K gross sales and 3 years remaining, this could be approximately $85,000+.

### Initial Investment

The total initial investment for a new franchise store ranges from $187,719 to $506,542. The largest variable component is construction ($35,000–$250,000), which depends on location condition (as-is vs. vanilla shell). Opening inventory of $80,000–$85,000 is the largest fixed component, and franchisees must maintain at least $60,000 in inventory at all times. Training costs are included in the franchise fee, but travel/lodging to Pittsburgh is at franchisee's expense.

For conversion stores (existing company-owned locations), the range is $112,719 to $463,042, which includes asset purchase amounts that vary based on goodwill and store value.

Average annual lease costs from company store data: strip centers average $43,689 total occupancy, malls average $50,700, downtown locations average $131,289.

## C. Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

### Supplier Restrictions

GNC maintains extraordinary supplier control. Required purchases represent approximately 95% of establishment costs and approximately 100% of ongoing purchases. GNC is the sole approved supplier for POS equipment, most inventory (including all GNC Brand Supplements), and fixtures/signage/equipment for standard store design. All products must be purchased through NutriMarket, GNC's proprietary ordering platform.

Third-party products may be carried only with GNC's prior written approval, which may be withheld for any reason. GNC may disapprove previously approved products at any time. GNC retains all rebates, discounts, and payments from suppliers and is not required to account for or share these amounts with franchisees.

Revenue from required franchisee purchases in FY 2024 totaled $143.9M (12.2% of GNC's $1.17B total revenue), consisting of $140.7M in inventory, $1.4M in POS fees, $1.5M in rent, and $253K in equipment/fixtures.

### Training Program

Training is conducted in three phases totaling approximately 72–96+ hours:
- **Phase I** (40 hours on-the-job + up to 16 hours store visits): Orientation in an existing GNC store near the franchisee's residence.
- **Phase II** (up to 5 days, ~16 hours classroom): Held in Pittsburgh, PA or virtually. Covers GNC introduction, credit/collections, site selection, marketing, recruiting, compliance, POS, ordering, space planning, P&L overview.
- **Phase III** (up to 7 days at franchisee's store): Grand opening setup, receiving initial inventory, store preparation.

Failure to complete any phase to GNC's satisfaction can result in repeat training or franchise termination.

### Technology Stack

The mandatory POS system costs $3,500–$7,000 initially, with hardware refresh every 3–5 years. Monthly technology costs total $358+ minimum. GNC has unlimited, independent access to all POS data. The POS software is proprietary and must be returned at expiration/termination (with depreciated value credit).

A separate PC and printer (Windows 10+) is required for the Franchise Portal, a secure website for company communications, training, and marketing materials.

### Advertising Fund

All franchisees contribute 3% of gross sales. Company-owned stores are not required to contribute. In FY 2024, spending was: 50% media placement, 38% other marketing, 9% merchandising, 3% production, 0% new franchise solicitation. GNC is not required to spend proportionally in any franchisee's territory. Unaudited annual report available on request.

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory

The protected territory is density-based and notably small: urban/metro locations receive only premises to 3/10 mile radius; suburban/rural locations get 1–2 miles. The **protective period is only 1 year** from store opening. After that, franchisee has only a right of first refusal (30 days to respond) for new GNC store sites within the territory.

### Franchisor Reservation of Rights

GNC retains exceptionally broad rights:
- Sell products through GNC.com, Amazon, Walmart.com, SamsClub.com, Tik Tok Shop, Uber Eats, and any future platform
- Wholesale to Rite Aid (existing agreement), CVS, Walmart, Walgreens, Sam's Club, H-E-B, Pilot Flying J, and others — products may be identical and within the protected territory
- Operate kiosks or non-Store outlets using GNC marks within the territory at any time
- Acquire competing franchise networks and operate them as GNC stores within the territory
- Relocate existing GNC stores within the territory, even during the protective period
- Establish non-GNC branded outlets within the territory

The territory may be reduced, altered, or eliminated upon renewal or relocation.

### Continuation

Willingness to continue the protected territory does not depend on sales volume, but if the store is not profitable ("break even"), GNC may not agree to renew — effectively terminating the territory.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal

The franchise term is 5 years from the store opening date (may be shortened to match lease). Renewal is for additional consecutive 5-year terms, but the franchisee must sign the then-current form of Franchise Agreement, which **may have materially different terms and conditions**. This is a significant renewal risk — the franchisee has no guarantee of comparable economics or obligations at renewal.

Renewal requirements include: no defaults, satisfaction of all monetary obligations, demonstrated profitability, $12,500 fee, general release, compliance with then-current training requirements, remodeling if required, and evidence of lease rights for the renewal period.

### Transfer Provisions

All transfers require prior franchisor approval. The transferee must qualify, complete training, assume all obligations, and pay a transfer fee up to the then-current initial franchise fee ($20,000). A general release is required (where state law permits). The franchisor has a right of first refusal to match any offer for a controlling interest. An escrow company must be used. The transferor remains liable for all pre-transfer obligations.

Intra-family transfers ($1,000 fee) and minority interest transfers ($2,500 fee) have reduced requirements.

### Termination

The franchisor may terminate for non-curable defaults including: bankruptcy, insolvency, felony conviction, abandonment, unauthorized transfer, loss of premises, failure to open by end of pre-opening period, fraud, misrepresentation, selling banned products, 3+ defaults in 12 months, wholesaling, and SDN designation. Curable defaults have a 30-day cure period (5 days for payment).

**Cross-termination** is a significant risk: termination of any agreement with GNC can trigger immediate termination of all other agreements with the franchisee or any entity in which the franchisee or principals have any interest.

### Death/Disability

Upon death or disability, the franchise must be transferred to an approved buyer within 6 months. GNC may operate the store at $2,000/week during this period.

### Noncompete

During the term: no involvement in any competing business. Post-term: 1 year, within 5 miles of the Approved Location, any GNC store, or any other franchisor/affiliate outlet. Includes Internet and electronic commerce. Subject to state law — several states limit enforceability.

### Dispute Resolution

Arbitration in Pennsylvania. Pennsylvania law governs. Jury trial waiver in all agreements. Confession of judgment provisions in Franchise Agreement, Guarantee, Product Sales Agreement, and Sublease. Forum non conveniens waiver.

### Liquidated Damages

On termination for default: max(average monthly royalty for 2 years, average monthly royalty for 12 months) × remaining months in term. For a mid-performing store ($475K gross, 6% royalty = ~$2,380/month royalty) with 3 years remaining = approximately $85,680. This formula creates significant exposure for early-term defaults.

## G. Item 20 — Outlet Data

### System Trajectory

The GNC franchise system is in accelerating contraction:

| Year | Franchised Start | End | Net | Company Start | End | Net | Total Net |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2022 | 789 | 774 | -15 | 1,554 | 1,526 | -28 | -43 |
| 2023 | 774 | 750 | -24 | 1,526 | 1,555 | +29 | +5 |
| 2024 | 750 | 703 | **-47** | 1,555 | 1,437 | **-118** | **-165** |

The 2024 year saw the steepest decline: 47 net franchised closures and 118 net company-owned closures. Franchised attrition in 2024: 22 terminations, 36 non-renewals, 5 reacquired by franchisor, 6 ceased operations — only 22 new openings. The non-renewal count (36) is notably high, suggesting many franchisees are choosing not to continue at the end of their terms.

### State-Level Highlights

**Texas** has the largest franchised presence (154 stores at YE 2024) but also the most turbulence: 8 terminations, 9 non-renewals, 7 new openings, 9 transfers in 2024 alone. Florida declined from 80 to 71 (4 terminations, 6 non-renewals). Georgia continues a steady decline (34→32→29→27). New York added a net 1 in 2023 but lost 3 net in 2024.

Bright spots: North Carolina grew from 19 to 22 with 4 openings and no terminations in 2024. Minnesota and South Dakota also showed modest growth.

### Transfer Activity

Transfers increased from 11 (2023) to 23 (2024). Texas dominated with 9 transfers (39% of total). This may indicate distressed sales or portfolio reshuffling.

### Company-Owned Trends

Company-owned stores saw dramatic contraction in 2024: 117 closures against only 13 openings. Notable: 19 stores sold to franchisees (up from 4 in 2023), consistent with the "Conversion Store" program. Large states: Ohio lost 8 company stores, South Carolina lost 8, Pennsylvania lost 5, Arizona lost 9.

### Projected Openings

Only 4 franchise agreements signed but not opened. Only 3 projected new franchised openings and 2 projected company-owned openings — anemic growth for a 2,140-unit system.

### Gag Clause

**No gag clause.** "During the last three fiscal years, no current or former franchisees have signed confidentiality clauses that restrict them from discussing with you their experiences as a franchisee in our franchise system."

## I. State Addenda Summary

The FDD includes state addenda for 12 states: California, Hawaii, Illinois, Indiana, Maryland, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, South Dakota, Virginia, and Washington.

**Key override families identified** (detailed per-state extraction deferred to A2):

- **Forum selection**: Multiple states override the Pennsylvania forum requirement (CA, IL, IN, MN, NY, ND, WA)
- **Governing law**: PA law may not apply in certain states (CA)
- **Noncompete limitation**: Post-term noncompete may be unenforceable (CA, WA)
- **Termination good cause**: State law may require good cause for termination (MI notice, IN, MN)
- **General release**: Waiver of franchise law rights voided in certain states (CA, IL, IN, MD, MN, NY, ND, RI, SD, VA, WA)
- **Interest rate cap**: California caps at 10% annually (vs. 1.5%/month = 18% annual in FA)
- **No-poach waiver**: GNC agrees not to enforce non-solicitation of employees in CA and WA
- **Liquidated damages**: May be unenforceable under CA Civil Code §1671
- **Confession of judgment**: Not enforceable in several states

Detailed structured extraction of per-state overrides is a priority for A2.
