Initial Term Loan

          On September 29, 2020, the Company entered into a five-year agreement ("Facilities Agreement") with the Bank of
China ("the Lender"), whereby the Lender made available a term loan facility of $400 million ("1L Term Loan"). The Company
was required to repay amounts borrowed in semi-annual installments beginning 12 months following the date of agreement over
the five-year term of the loan. The interest was payable at a rate of 4.25% per annum plus LIBOR. In December 2022, the Federal
Reserve Board adopted a rule implementing the Adjustable Interest Rate Act, which identified benchmark rates based on the
secured overnight financing rate (SOFR) as a replacement for LIBOR in contracts ("2022 LIBOR Act"). In accordance with the
2022 LIBOR Act, ICE Benchmark Administration, the administrator of LIBOR, ceased the publication of all LIBOR settings no
later than June 30, 2023. As such, on June 9, 2023, the Company and Bank of China amended the Facilities Agreement such that
Term SOFR would be the principal benchmark replacing LIBOR in the calculation of 1L Term Loan interest, which commenced
in the third quarter of 2023.

         In August 2024, the Company and Bank of China agreed to amend and restate the 1L Term Loan, whereby the Company's
next repayment obligation on the 1L Term Loan of $20.0 million is due in May 2025 and the remaining balance of the loan is due
in October 2025 at the original maturity date of the loan. The Company satisfied its $20.0 million obligation in May 2025.
Pursuant to the terms of the amended and restated 1L Term Loan, interest will continue to be payable at a rate of 4.25% per annum
plus SOFR through the end of 2024, and will increase to 5.25% per annum plus SOFR beginning on January 1, 2025. The cost of
the amendment was 0.85% of the outstanding balance of the loan at the time of execution, which is capitalized within long-term
debt on the Consolidated Balance Sheet. Pursuant to the terms of the August 2024 amended and restated 1L Term Loan, the
Company is required to resume its semi-annual test of compliance with financial covenants as of December 31, 2024, as discussed
below.
