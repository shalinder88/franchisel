Long-lived Asset Impairment

          During the third quarter of 2024, the Company performed a detailed review of its domestic store portfolio and identified
certain underperforming stores. Underperforming stores are defined as those with historical and expected future losses or stores
that management intends on closing in the near term. In connection with the review of the store portfolio, the Company recorded
$12.7 million of impairment charges within the U.S. and Canada segment. The impairment charge consisted of $5.2 million
related to its property, plant and equipment for the underperforming stores and $7.5 million related to right-of-use assets, presented
as long-lived asset impairments on the Consolidated Statement of Operations.

         The impairment test was performed at the individual store level as this is the lowest level which identifiable cash flows
are largely independent of other groups of assets and liabilities. If the undiscounted estimated cash flows were less than the
carrying value of the asset group, an impairment charge was calculated by subtracting the estimated fair value of the store's assets
from its carrying value. Fair value was estimated using a discounted cash flow method (income approach) utilizing the
undiscounted cash flows computed in the first step of the test.

          Additionally, during the fourth quarter of 2024, the Company impaired its remaining 26 Canada stores following the its
strategic decision to wind down operations in Canada and close all locations by the end of 2025. The Company recorded $1.3
million of impairment charges within the U.S. and Canada segment, consisting of $0.1 million related to its property, plant and
equipment and $1.2 million related to its right-of-use assets, presented as long-lived asset impairments on the Consolidated
Statement of Operations.
