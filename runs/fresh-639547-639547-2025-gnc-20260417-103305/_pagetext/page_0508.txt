Excess Cash Flow
           The Company is required to make certain mandatory prepayments, including a requirement to prepay the 1L Term Loan
first and the 2L Term Loan second, in each case annually with amounts based on excess cash flow, as defined in the Company’s
1L Term Loan agreement as being greater than or equal to $50 million as of December 31 each year the agreement is in effect.
The payment will be 60% of excess cash flow, less any taxes paid or reasonably estimated, toward the 1L Term Loan. Any amount
in excess of $50 million thereafter is subject to prepayment of 15% toward the 2L Term Loan, provided that the net senior leverage
ratio is less than or equal to 2.00 to 1.00. Pursuant to the terms of the Facilities Agreement, any required excess cash flow payment
is due at the first 1L Term Loan repayment date following delivery of the Company’s annual financial statements to Bank of
China. Based on the Company's results for the year ended December 31, 2023, the Company is required to make an excess cash
flow payment of $0.6 million towards the 1L Term Loan, due at the time of the next required amortization payment in May 2025.
The $0.6 million excess cash flow payment is classified as current on the Company's Consolidated Balance Sheets as of March
31, 2025 and December 31, 2024. The Company remitted $0.6 million to Bank of China in May 2025 in conjunction with its
aforementioned amortization payment. Based on the Company's results for the period ended December 31, 2024, the Company
was not required to make an excess cash flow payment.
