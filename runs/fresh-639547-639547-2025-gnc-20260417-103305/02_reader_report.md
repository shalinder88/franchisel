# Reader Report — 639547-2025-GNC

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand**: GNC (General Nutrition Centers) — health, wellness and performance retail stores specializing in vitamins, minerals, supplements, sports nutrition, diet products, beauty/health products.
2. **Franchisor**: GNC Holdings, LLC, a Delaware LLC headquartered at 75 Hopper Place, Suite 501, Pittsburgh, PA 15222. Acquired assets from predecessor General Nutrition Corporation (which filed Chapter 11 in June 2020) via sale to Harbin Pharmaceutical Group Holding Co., Ltd. (ultimate parent, China) on October 7, 2020.
3. **System size at YE 2024**: 703 franchised stores, 1,437 company-owned stores (2,140 total). Franchising since 1987 (predecessor); current entity since February 2021.
4. **Investment range**: $187,719–$506,542 (new franchise store); $112,719–$463,042 (conversion store). Area Development first store: $195,219–$514,042.
5. **Fee burden**: 6% royalty on gross sales + 3% national advertising fund + ~$358/month tech fees (POS maintenance $75–$100, network $233, EMV $16, Wi-Fi $18, cellular $16) + GNFS $220–$345/month if enrolled. Total ongoing fee load is approximately 9%+ of gross sales plus tech/services fees.
6. **Item 19 (FPR)**: Average gross sales $475,924 (675 stores, FY 2024), median $443,685. 41% at or above average. High $1,591,918, low $139,454. No cost/expense/EBITDA data disclosed.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: At median gross sales of ~$444K, the 9%+ fee load alone consumes ~$40K. With typical rent ($37K–$123K), payroll, and COGS, stores in the lower quartile (below ~$350K gross) face severe profitability pressure. Minimum inventory requirement of $60K wholesale further constrains working capital.
8. **Financial health (Item 21)**: PwC audit, unqualified opinion, no going concern. Revenue declining ($1.47B in 2022 → $1.33B in 2023 → $1.17B in 2024). Net loss of $141.2M in 2024, $32.3M loss in 2023. Total assets $783M; total liabilities $723M; member's equity $61M (down from $202M in 2023). Accumulated deficit of ($116.6M). Current debt $225M. Significant leverage.
9. **Growth trajectory**: Negative. Franchised stores declining: 789 (2022) → 774 → 750 → 703 (2024). 47 net closures in 2024 alone. Company-owned also declining: 1,555 → 1,437 in 2024 (118 net closures). Projected openings: only 3 new franchised, 2 new company-owned in next fiscal year.
10. **Territory**: Protected territory ranges from premises/1/10 mile (urban) to 1–2 miles (suburban/rural). Only 1-year protective period, then right of first refusal. Extensive franchisor reservation of rights. Alternative channels (GNC.com, Walmart.com, Amazon, Tik Tok Shop, wholesale to Rite Aid, drug stores, etc.) may compete within protected territory.
11. **Renewal**: 5-year terms, renewable for additional 5-year terms. Must sign then-current form which may have materially different terms. Renewal fee $12,500. General release required. Must demonstrate profitability ("break even").
12. **Disputes**: Arbitration in Pennsylvania; PA law. Jury trial waiver. Confession of judgment provision. State addenda override in 12 states.
13. **Special risks**: Out-of-state dispute resolution, spousal liability, supplier control (95%+ from franchisor), short operating history (current entity since 2020).
14. **Ownership**: Chinese-owned (Harbin Pharmaceutical Group, ultimate parent). Key leadership: CEO Michael Costello (since July 2024), COO Rodney Lastinger (since Dec 2024), CFO Oded Shein (since April 2024).

## B. Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — Franchisor Entity and Structure
GNC Holdings, LLC is a Delaware LLC formed after Harbin Pharmaceutical Group acquired the assets of bankrupt General Nutrition Corporation in October 2020. The parent chain runs through ZT Biopharmaceutical LLC (immediate parent, sole member), Jacks Management (Suzhou) Limited (intermediate), and Harbin Pharmaceutical Group Holding Co., Ltd. (ultimate parent, China). No affiliates provide products/services to franchisees — GNC itself is the sole supplier for most inventory.

The franchise is for a GNC retail store selling vitamins, supplements, sports nutrition, beauty/health products. Two purchase paths: New Franchise Store or Conversion Store (existing company-owned converted). Optional Smoothie Bar addendum ($1,000 fee + $32K–$65K investment). Minimum 2 stores under Area Development Agreement.

GNC has been franchising since 1987 (predecessor). The current entity has offered franchises since February 9, 2021. GNC also operates company-owned stores, GNC.com, Amazon marketplace, Uber Eats, Walmart.com, SamsClub.com, and Tik Tok Shop. It wholesales to Rite Aid, Walgreens, Walmart, CVS, Sam's Club, and numerous other retailers.

## C. Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Supplier Restrictions
GNC is the sole approved supplier for POS equipment, most inventory (including all GNC Brand Supplements), and fixtures/signage/equipment for standard store construction. Required purchases represent approximately **95% of establishment costs** and **100% of ongoing purchases**. Franchisees must purchase through NutriMarket (GNC's ordering platform).

Third-party products may be carried with GNC approval; GNC may disapprove at any time for any reason. Perishable goods require prior written approval with specific guidelines. GNC negotiates vendor price reductions and passes them through but retains all rebates, discounts, and payments from suppliers without accounting or sharing.

Revenue from required franchisee purchases in FY 2024: $140.7M inventory, $253K equipment/fixtures, $1.4M POS fees, $1.5M rent = $143.9M total (12.2% of GNC's $1.17B revenue).

### Item 9 — Franchisee Obligations
Standard 26-item obligation table (a through z). Notable: all principals and spouses must personally guarantee (z); post-termination non-competition covenants (w); POS and financial services obligations (y).

### Item 10 — Financing
GNC does not offer direct financing. It extends a revolving credit line for inventory purchases to qualified franchisees: credit = (1.5 × avg monthly gross sales over 12 months) + avg monthly royalties/ad fees. Payment terms: net 50 days. No interest. GNC may place credit holds.

Security interest: GNC takes first-position UCC-1 lien on store fixtures, equipment, and inventory. May subordinate for SBA loans (sole discretion). All principals and **spouses** must sign personal guarantee. Confession of judgment provisions in multiple agreements.

### Item 11 — Training and Assistance
**Training Program (3 phases)**:
- **Phase I**: 40 hours on-the-job training in existing GNC store near residence + up to 16 hours store visits with Director of Franchise Operations.
- **Phase II**: Up to 5 days classroom training (in Pittsburgh or virtual). Covers: GNC intro, credit/collections, site selection, marketing, recruiting, compliance, POS, ordering, space planning, P&L.
- **Phase III**: Up to 7 days (new franchisee) or 5 days (existing) at franchisee's store — grand opening setup.
- Failure to complete any phase to satisfaction → may require repeat or franchise termination.

**Advertising Fund**: 3% of total gross sales. Spending breakdown (FY 2024): 50% media placement, 38% other marketing, 9% merchandising, 3% production, 0% soliciting new franchises. Company-owned stores not required to contribute. Unaudited annual report available on request.

**Technology**: POS system and peripherals mandatory ($3,500–$7,000). Hardware refresh every 3–5 years. Monthly tech costs: $75+$25 (POS maintenance), $233 (network), $16 (EMV), $18 (Wi-Fi), $16 (cellular) = $358+ per month. GNC has unlimited access to all POS data.

**Operations Manual**: 419 pages across 20+ sections. Loaned electronically.

**Store opening**: 180–240 days from signing to opening. 1,000–1,500 sq ft required.

## D. Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
Protected territory is density-based:
- Urban/Metro (density class 1–3): Premises or 1/10 to 3/10 mile radius
- Suburban/Rural (density class 4–7): Premises or 1–2 mile radius

**Protective period: 1 year only.** After 1 year, franchisee gets right of first refusal (30 days to respond). Territory is non-exclusive. Franchisor reserves extensive rights:
- Sell products via any channel (GNC.com, Amazon, Walmart.com, Tik Tok Shop, Uber Eats, mail order, wholesale)
- Wholesale to Rite Aid, CVS, Walmart, Walgreens, Sam's Club and other retailers within territory
- Acquire/operate competing franchise networks under GNC marks within territory (after protective period)
- Relocate existing GNC stores within territory at any time, even during protective period
- Grant kiosks or other outlets (non-Store format) within territory at any time

May reduce/alter territory upon renewal or relocation.

### Item 13 — Trademarks
Principal marks: GNC, GNC LIVE WELL, GNC GENERAL NUTRITION CENTERS — all on US Principal Register. No superior prior rights or pending infringement. Non-exclusive license — franchisor may substitute marks (at franchisor's cost unless coinciding with remodel).

### Item 14 — Patents/Copyrights/Proprietary Info
No material patents or copyrights. Strict confidentiality obligations during and after term. Confidentiality agreement required for all employees with access.

### Item 15 — Participation
Manager or owner must supervise full-time on premises. Manager need not be owner. All owners and spouses must sign personal guarantee. Manager may need to complete training program.

### Item 16 — Product Restrictions
May sell only GNC-approved products. May not use alternative distribution channels. Franchisor may set minimum and/or maximum prices to fullest extent of law. No customer restrictions beyond requiring in-store purchases only.
