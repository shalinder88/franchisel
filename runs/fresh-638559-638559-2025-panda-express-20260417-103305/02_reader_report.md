# Reader Report — 638559-2025-Panda-Express

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Franchisor:** Citadel Panda Express, Inc., a California corporation, wholly-owned subsidiary of Panda Express, Inc. (PEI), which is wholly-owned by Panda Restaurant Group, Inc. (PRG). Family-founded (Andrew & Peggy Cherng) since 1983.
2. **System Size (end FY2024):** 2,502 total Panda Express restaurants — 173 franchised + 2,329 company-owned (by affiliates). Franchised system grew from 146 (2022) to 173 (2024), net +27 over 3 years.
3. **Investment Range:** $514,500 to $3,275,500 for a single captive-venue restaurant. Drive-thru pilot adds up to $1,500,000.
4. **Fee Burden:** 8% royalty on Gross Volume (or $4,000 minimum per Royalty Period, whichever is greater); no ad fund contribution; 10% surcharge on Proprietary Products; $25,000 initial franchise fee.
5. **Item 19 Highlights:** 156 captive-venue restaurants reported for FY2024; system-wide gross sales $247.3M; average gross sales $1,585,064; median $1,186,865. Airport locations average $3.9M. Only 42% exceed the average.
6. **Territory:** NO exclusive territory. No protected area. Franchisor and affiliates can operate anywhere, including next to your restaurant.
7. **Contract Term:** Unlimited license term with 10-year Operating Terms per restaurant; single 5-year successor term available.
8. **Renewal Risk:** Successor term may require signing a "materially different" agreement and paying $10,000 successor fee. Must comply with then-current new-unit standards (potential remodel).
9. **Financial Health:** Strong — total assets $70M, retained earnings $96.7M, net income $24.8M (FY2024). Auditor: CliftonLarsonAllen LLP, unqualified opinion. S Corporation — no federal income tax at entity level.
10. **Growth Trajectory:** Company-owned grew +81 in 2024 (87 opened, 6 closed). Franchised grew +8 in 2024 (11 opened, 2 terminated, 1 reacquired). 123 projected company-owned openings in 2025 vs. 14 projected franchised openings.
11. **Special Risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution (California); mandatory minimum royalties; training completion cancellation risk; significant unopened franchise agreements.
12. **Litigation:** Active data breach class action settlement pending; multiple labor-related cases (wage/hour). NovaDine arbitration resulted in net PRG win ($5.3M award vs. $1.7M counterclaim).
13. **Gag Clause:** Present — FDD discloses that current/former franchisees may have signed provisions restricting ability to speak openly.

## Pass B: Front Matter, Formats, Fees, Investment (Items 1–7)

### Item 1 — The Franchisor
Citadel Panda Express, Inc. was incorporated in California on October 25, 1990. Principal place of business: 1683 Walnut Grove Avenue, Rosemead, CA 91770. Wholly-owned subsidiary of PEI, which is wholly-owned by PRG. PRG is the holding company for all Panda companies, and PRG owns all trademarks.

## Pass D: Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
**NO exclusive territory.** No protected area of any kind. Franchisor and affiliates can:
- Locate other restaurants anywhere (including next to yours)
- Use any distribution channel (Internet, catalog, telemarketing, direct marketing)
- Develop/associate with other concepts, including dual branding
- Acquire/merge with competitors
- Require brand conversions at your expense

No solicitation restrictions on you, the franchisor, or other franchisees.

Relocation requires written consent. If venue contract expires/terminates without your default, you can terminate that restaurant's Operating Term with 90 days' notice — but must pay minimum royalty for 90 days + 24 months' equivalent royalties.

Performance Standards: Franchisor can implement correction process if restaurant scores below system average or below 90% of affiliate restaurant scores. Must cooperate with inspections at any time.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **License Agreement term:** Unlimited, subject to termination rights; expires after 2 years if no restaurant authorized
- **Operating Term per restaurant:** 10 years from opening
- **Successor term:** Single 5-year period — no further renewal
- **Successor conditions:** (a) 6–12 months advance written notice; (b) Good Standing; (c) comply with then-current new-unit standards (possible complete remodel); (d) all monetary obligations satisfied; (e) $10,000 successor fee; (f) general release; (g) **may be required to sign materially different agreement**

### Termination
**By franchisee:** (1) mutual agreement; (2) 30 days' notice if no restaurant operating and no authorization issued; (3) material default by franchisor uncured; (4) venue lease/contract expires or discontinued without your default (90 days' notice + pay min royalty 90 days + 24 months' royalties)

**By franchisor without cause:** (1) mutual agreement; (2) 30 days' notice if no restaurant operating; (3) if IP licensing agreement with PRG terminates

**Curable defaults (15-day cure):** confidentiality/noncompete/trademark violations; inaccurate Gross Volume reporting; failure to pay; refusal of audit/inspection; health/safety violations; operations likely to harm brand; failure to respond to guest complaint within 10 days. Other curable defaults get 30 days (60 if cannot reasonably cure in 30).

**Non-curable defaults (immediate):** material misrepresentation in application; abandonment/non-operation; bankruptcy/insolvency; failure to get required licenses or criminal misconduct; felony; unauthorized transfer; >5% Gross Volume understatement; cross-default; failure to complete training; lease terminated for cause; failure to pay vendors; unauthorized catering/delivery; revoke direct deposit authorization; 3 defaults within 12 months.

### Post-Termination Obligations
- Pay all amounts within 10 days; cease operations immediately; de-identify restaurant; return all IP/manuals
- Sell equipment/fixtures to franchisor at net book value (5-year amortization)
- **If terminated for your default:** Pay sum equivalent to 24 months' Royalties + minimum Royalty for 90 days post-closure

### Transfer
- All transfers require franchisor consent
- Transfer fee: $10,000/restaurant (max $30,000/transaction) + out-of-pocket costs
- Franchisor has 30-day Right of First Refusal (can substitute cash for other payment forms)
- Extensive conditions: transferee qualifies, signs current agreement, completes training, restaurant meets current standards, all debts current, general release signed, no pending litigation, Client consent if required
- Transferor remains liable without express release

### Noncompete
- **During term:** Cannot sell/offer competing Asian-style food at same facility
- **Post-term:** Subject to applicable state law, noncompete covenants continue in full force after termination/expiration

### Dispute Resolution
- Mediation, then binding arbitration (AAA rules) in Los Angeles County (or wherever headquarters is)
- Jury waiver; no class actions; limitation on damages types and statutes of limitation
- California law governs (Federal Arbitration Act preempts where applicable)
- Noncompete enforced under law of state where breach occurs
- Subject to state-specific overrides

### Death/Disability
Not addressed in License Agreement (marked "Not applicable" in Item 17 table).
