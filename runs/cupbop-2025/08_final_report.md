# Cupbop Franchise, LLC — 2025 Diligence Report

## A. Executive snapshot
1. **Cupbop Franchise, LLC** — Utah LLC formed 2014-11-14; began offering franchises March 2017; wholly-owned subsidiary of **Cupbop Co.** (parent since Jan 2021). Two additional parents: Gold Light Holdings LLC (UT) and MAK Holdings LLC (NV). Issuance date: **2025-03-26**.
2. **Concept**: Korean-style barbeque rice cups, salads, wings, potstickers; carry-out, on-premises, catering. 1,200–2,000 sq ft footprint.
3. **System size EOY 2024**: 59 outlets (30 franchised, 29 company-owned). Net adds 2024: +5. Three-year trajectory 29→59 (more than doubled).
4. **Initial investment**: $296,400 – $664,400 single unit. $41,250–$42,000 of that flows to franchisor or affiliate.
5. **Initial fee**: $40,000 first / $30,000 subsequent. **Royalty 6%** of Gross Sales. Ad fund up to 4.5%, currently 2%. Local advertising 0.5% min. Total cumulative ad cap 6.5%.
6. **Item 19 highlights** (n=50 all-restaurants set): avg $728,821; median $710,250; high $1,585,417; low $347,132. Franchised-only avg $658,208 vs company-owned $794,003 — a $136K gap suggesting corporate operating advantage.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk**: franchised low band (<$550K) averages $400,962. With 6% royalty + 2% ad fund + the **50% sales-performance kill switch**, this band sits inside default territory in any soft year.
8. **Financial health (entity)**: Forvis Mazars LLP **unqualified** opinion. FY2024 revenue $2,027,743 (+8.9% YoY); net income $1,025,658 (+31.3%). **But** distributions of $1,896,607 (185% of net income) collapsed member's equity from $892,301 to $21,352 — a 97.6% drop.
9. **Growth trajectory**: franchised 9→19→27→30; company-owned 20→26→27→29; 0 transfers in 3 years. First franchised closures in 2024 (3 Las Vegas units, all Gold Bowl LLC).
10. **Territory**: no exclusive territory; protected territory variable; aggressive channel reservation including Non-Traditional Venues (arenas, stadiums, airports already operated by Cupbop Co.).
11. **Renewal risk**: 10-year term; renewal at then-current FA which **may be materially different** — open-ended future-burden risk.
12. **Disputes**: zero litigation, zero bankruptcy disclosed. Utah-only forum.
13. **Special risks** (state-mandated): out-of-state dispute resolution; sales-performance-required default; supplier control to parent.
14. **Concentration**: Kevin Santiago / **Gold Bowl LLC** operates ~26 of 30 franchised units (AZ, ID, NV, TX). Santiago is also a Cupbop director — unusual related-party governance.
15. **Registration state**: all 10 listed states (CA, HI, IL, MD, MI, MN, NY, VA, WA, WI) marked **PENDING** at issuance — not yet effective for sale.

## B. Fee stack, entry structure, initial investment

**Front matter & entity structure.** Cupbop Franchise, LLC was converted from Cupbop Franchising, Inc. (Utah corp) in December 2016 (no assets at conversion). Current parent Cupbop Co. owns the trademarks and licenses them to Franchise LLC under a December 15, 2020 License Agreement. Cupbop Co. itself operates Cupbop restaurants and supplies required products to franchisees. Other parents Gold Light Holdings LLC and MAK Holdings LLC.

**Fee stack (Item 6).**
- Royalty: **6% of monthly Gross Sales**, due 10th of each month
- Ad fund: **2% currently, up to 4.5% maximum** of Gross Sales
- Local advertising: **0.5% minimum, up to 1% required**
- Cooperative: up to 2% (currently not assessed)
- Total cumulative advertising-related cap: **6.5% of Gross Sales**
- Successor fee: $10,000
- Transfer fee: $10,000 (waived for entity-controlled transfers)
- Relocation fee: $10,000
- Annual franchisee meeting fee: up to $2,500
- Additional training: $400/day (training facility) or $600/day (franchisee site), up to $800/day
- Audit cost: payable only if Gross Sales understated >3% or reports missing
- Non-compliance: $500/occurrence + $500/day continuing
- Unauthorized advertising: $1,000/occurrence
- Late fee: 10% of past-due
- Interest: 18%/yr or max legal
- Management fee on franchisor takeover: $500/person/day plus costs
- **Liquidated damages**: sum of prior 24 months of accrued royalties (or remaining-term equivalent if shorter), payable within 30 days of termination

**Initial investment (Item 7).**
- Initial Franchise Fee: $40,000 (or $30,000 if existing franchisee)
- Training expenses: $1,000–$5,000
- Real estate / rent: $5,000–$15,000
- Leasehold improvements + FF&E: **$180,000–$475,000** (largest single bucket)
- Grand opening advertising: $10,000 (mandatory minimum)
- Opening inventory/supplies: $13,000–$15,000
- POS system: $2,500–$5,000
- Accounting software (QuickBooks Online): $200–$1,000
- Office equipment: $3,000–$5,000
- Signage: $10,000–$16,000
- Utility deposits: $1,500–$6,500
- Licenses & permits: $500–$1,500
- Insurance: $400–$600
- Dues & subscriptions: $300–$800
- Professional fees: $2,000–$5,000
- Additional funds (3 months): $27,000–$63,000
- **TOTAL: $296,400 – $664,400**

The **3-month working capital window is short** for a fast-casual concept; many comparable concepts disclose 6 months. Combined with the 50% sales-performance rule kicking in at year 2, undercapitalized franchisees in low-performing markets face compounding default exposure.

**Area Development Agreement.** $40,000 first restaurant + $30,000 each additional, totaling $160,000–$610,000 for 5–20 restaurants. Fully earned at execution; non-refundable. Each restaurant still bears the full $296,400–$664,400 single-unit investment on top of the development fee.

## C. Supplier control, operating control, technology burden, reporting burden

**Required-source items from parent Cupbop Co.**: proprietary sauces and marinades, proteins, branded paper goods (cups, bowls). Other Cupbop-branded items from approved third-party suppliers. Estimated franchisee at-spec purchases: **22–40% of total purchases at opening; 22–33% ongoing**.

**Approved supplier process**: written request, financials, samples, testing fee (paid by franchisee whether or not approved), 30-day decision window, 30-day revocation right.

**Affiliate revenue from franchisees**: $36,920 in FY2024 (parent Cupbop Co.). Franchisor itself takes $0 from product sales. No rebates currently disclosed; right reserved.

**Insurance**: GL $2M/occurrence, $2M aggregate (umbrella may satisfy), business interruption ALS or 12-month loss-of-income, workers' comp, employer's liability $1M.

**Training program**: 14 days at Draper, UT facility. **20.5 hours classroom + 95 hours OJT = 115.5 total hours**. 2 attendees free; additional $1,500 each. Trainers: Gina Silveira (3 months Cupbop, 13 years restaurant); JongBin Lim (4 years Cupbop, 21 years restaurant). Replacement GM training within 90 days of departure.

**Technology**: required POS hardware $2,500–$5,000; **annual POS maintenance $7,200–$12,000**; QuickBooks Online required ($200/yr). Franchisor reserves unrestricted access to all POS data.

**Operations Manual**: 108 pages, version dated 03-01-2023.

**Marketing fund spend mix (2024)**: 31% production / 29% placement / **40% administration**. The 40% admin allocation is high relative to typical casual-dining marketing funds (10–15%). No independent audit required; statements available on franchisee request.

## D. Territory, competition, channels, encroachment

**No exclusive territory granted under the FA.** Restaurants receive a "protected territory" but the size and scope vary by location characteristics, population density, and demographics — **no minimum is guaranteed**. Franchisor will not establish or franchise another Cupbop in the protected area for the FA term.

**Carve-outs (channel reservations)**: franchisor reserves the right to operate or sell Cupbop products via Non-Traditional Venues (arenas, stadiums, airports, hospitals, universities, military bases, food courts, department stores, supermarkets), internet, wholesale, catalog, and mail-order — both inside and outside the protected territory. Cupbop Co. **already runs concessions** at Vivint Smart Home Arena, Rice-Eccles Stadium, and LaVell Edwards Stadium.

**Online-order encroachment**: customers in your protected territory who place online orders may be served by the franchisor, an affiliate, or another franchisee — you have no right to those customers.

**Sales-performance kill switch**: starting in **year 2**, franchisees must maintain at least **50% of the trailing 12-month national average sales** of all Cupbop national restaurants. **Failure over a 2-year period is a default and franchisor has discretion to terminate.**

## E. Contract burden and legal mechanics

**Term**: 10 years from opening date (FA §1D). ADA term ends at last opening or last day of Development Schedule.

**Renewal** (FA §14): 10 years; notice 180–220 days before expiration; maintain possession of premises; remodel to current standards; pay successor fee; **sign then-current FA "which may be materially different"** — material future-burden risk.

**Termination by franchisee**: only for franchisor breach uncured after notice (or per state law).

**Curable defaults** (FA §15B):
- 3 days: health, safety, sanitation violations
- 10 days: monetary defaults, insurance lapse
- 30 days: third-party monetary defaults
- 90 days: relocation after losing premises

**Noncurable defaults**: site failure (120 days), opening failure (16 months), opening without written approval, training failure, abandonment, unapproved transfers, misrepresentation, felony conviction, dishonest/unethical conduct, manual/CI disclosure, tax failure, **Gross Sales understatement**, recruiting violations, repeated defaults, bankruptcy/insolvency, anti-terrorism violation, and **cross-default — any default under any other agreement with franchisor or affiliates**.

**Transfer** (FA §13): $10,000 fee (waived for entity-controlled), franchisor consent (not unreasonably withheld), full release, transferee assumes obligations, signs then-current FA, completes training. **Franchisor right of first refusal** (FA §13E).

**Death/disability**: 180 days to appoint qualifying representative.

**Non-compete during term**: cannot divert business, own/perform services for competing business, interfere with employees.

**Post-term non-compete**: **2 years**, premises plus **20-mile radius from any Cupbop restaurant** existing, operating, or under construction at termination. **The "any Cupbop" radius is broader than typical** (most concepts use a radius from the franchisee's own location only). For a system that has 59 locations clustered in UT/AZ/ID, this creates a near-total geographic block in core markets.

**Franchisor option to purchase** (FA §16E): franchisor may purchase or sublease the restaurant including premises at fair market value upon termination/expiration.

**Choice of law / venue**: Utah law, Salt Lake County Utah arbitration/litigation, mediation first (subject to state-law overrides).

**Litigation history**: none disclosed (Item 3).

**Cash flow FY2024**: Operating $2,148,096 (driven by $758,760 collection of related-party A/R) + Investing $(17,405) + Financing $(1,896,607) = Net $234,084. Cash $17,066 → $251,150.

**Key financial observations**:
1. **Distributions 185% of net income** — the parent extracted nearly all retained value plus prior accumulated equity. This is structurally similar to a pass-through cash management pattern but leaves the franchising entity with $21K in equity supporting $636K in liabilities.
2. **Member's equity dropped 97.6% YoY** — the entity is effectively de-equitized.
3. **Deferred franchise fees (contract liabilities) grew from $309K to $600K** — strong forward bookings (or area development fees collected but not yet earned). This is a positive sales indicator but also raises the contract liability burden.
4. **Related-party A/R collapsed** from $892,586 to $133,826 — parent paid down ~$759K of receivables in 2024, which is what funded the operating cash flow swing.
5. **Going-concern question**: while the auditor issued an unqualified opinion, the entity's equity position invites a future going-concern note. Franchisor financial support to franchisees is not an obligation under this FDD (Item 10 discloses no financing offered).

The financial statements are scoped narrowly to the franchising entity. **Parent-level economics, where the actual restaurants and store-level cash live, are not in evidence.** Prospective franchisees relying on franchisor durability should ask for parent (Cupbop Co.) financials separately.
