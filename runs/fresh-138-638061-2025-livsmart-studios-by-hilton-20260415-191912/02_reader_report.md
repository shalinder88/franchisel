# Reader Report — LivSmart Studios by Hilton (638061-2025)

## Pass A: Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand-new brand** — LivSmart Studios by Hilton is an extended-stay midscale hotel brand with ZERO open hotels as of December 31, 2024. All 73 signed franchise agreements are pre-opening.
2. **Franchisor**: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 2007, subsidiary of Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., ultimately under Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT).
3. **Formerly "Project H3 by Hilton"** — launched May 23, 2023 under temporary name, rebranded to LivSmart Studios by Hilton on January 9, 2024.
4. **Total investment**: $15,424,593 to $21,497,662 for a 121-guestroom prototype (excludes real property).
5. **Fee burden**: 6% royalty + 2.0%-2.5% program fee on Gross Rooms Revenue, plus Hilton Honors (2% of eligible folio), plus numerous distribution/technology/quality fees.
6. **No FPR** — Item 19 contains no financial performance representations.
7. **Item 20**: All tables show zero open, zero terminated, zero transferred. 73 agreements signed but not opened across 23 states. Texas leads with 16.
8. **No renewal right** — franchise is non-renewable. Re-licensing is at franchisor's sole discretion and may involve materially different terms.
9. **Trademark risk** — "LivSmart Studios by Hilton" and "LivSmart" are NOT federally registered. Applications are pending. This is a disclosed Special Risk.
10. **Dispute resolution**: Litigation in Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria) or Fairfax County, VA; New York as backup. Governing law: New York. Jury trial waived.
11. **No post-term noncompete** — franchisor does not require post-term noncompete for franchisee, but during-term competitor restrictions apply.
12. **Liquidated damages**: Post-opening termination = greater of (a) 60x average monthly royalty or (b) $10,000 x approved guest rooms. Substantial exposure.
13. **Franchise term**: New construction = 22 years from Effective Date. Conversion = 10-20 years from Opening Date.
14. **Financial health of franchisor**: Revenue $1.502 billion (2024), net income $1.488 billion. Entity is essentially a royalty-collection vehicle distributing $1.5B annually to Hilton parent. Assets pledged as collateral for Hilton's debt.

## Pass E: Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction**: 22 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion**: 10-20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership**: remaining term or as approved
- **No renewal right**. Re-licensing at franchisor's sole discretion, with potentially materially different terms and PIP conditions.

### Termination
**By franchisee**: Not authorized. Unilateral termination = material breach + liquidated damages.
**By franchisor without cause**: Condemnation or casualty (with LD release under conditions).
**By franchisor with cause (curable)**: Payment defaults (10 days cure), construction delays (10 days), insurance lapses (10 days), other defaults (30 days).
**By franchisor — non-curable**: Pattern of non-compliance, 3 defaults in 12 months, insolvency/bankruptcy, loss of possession, 5+ consecutive days non-operation, contesting marks, conduct damaging reputation, false books, unauthorized transfer, sanctioned person, guarantor breach, public health threat, becoming a Competitor.

### Transfer
- **Permitted Transfers** (no change of control): publicly traded equity (no consent needed); private minority transfers (no consent if <50%); institutional fund transfers
- **Permitted Transfers requiring consent**: to affiliates, family, on death, >50% cumulative private transfer — 60 days notice, $5,500 fee
- **Change of Ownership Transfers**: 60 days notice, $100,000 fee, new franchise agreement required, PIP, transferee must meet all qualifications
- No right of first refusal (but must notify of intent to market)
- Mortgages/pledges: generally allowed without consent if sole borrower and single-hotel collateral

### Death/Disability
- Transfer on death to immediate family member permitted; must meet requirements within 1 year

### Noncompete
- **During term**: Cannot own/license/franchise a Competing Brand (may be franchisee of competitor, manage competitor hotel if not exclusive manager, own minority interest under conditions)
- **Post-term**: NONE

### Dispute Resolution
- **Governing law**: New York (without choice of law principles)
- **Venue**: US District Court for Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria) or Fairfax County, VA state court; backup New York. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Puerto Rico**: Mandatory arbitration (AAA/JAMS), seat New York, venue Fairfax County, VA
- **Jury trial waiver**: Yes (Section 17.1)
- **Punitive damages waiver**: Yes (Section 17.2)
- **General release**: Required on transfer and re-licensing (Section 16.8)
