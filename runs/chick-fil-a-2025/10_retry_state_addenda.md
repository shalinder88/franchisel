## 1. California

**Pages**: ~264-269
**Governing law**: CA Franchise Investment Law (Bus. & Prof. Code 31000+) and CA Franchise Relations Act (Bus. & Prof. Code 20000+)

### FDD Modifications
- Cover page: DFPI website disclosure added; website not reviewed/approved by DFPI
- Item 3: Arrests not resulting in conviction excluded per CA law; no securities exchange suspensions
- Item 11 (Site Selection): CFA approval means minimum standards only; licensee solely responsible for site selection
- Item 17t (Integration clause): Provisions may not be enforceable under CA law; CFA reserves right to attempt enforcement
- Item 17: Termination-on-bankruptcy may not be enforceable under federal bankruptcy law
- CA Bus. & Prof. Code 20000-20043 provides rights re non-renewal, transfer, termination
- Non-compete extending beyond expiration may not be enforceable under CA law
- Liquidated damages inconsistent with CA Civil Code 1671 may not be enforceable
- Forum selection outside CA may not be enforceable
- Choice of law (non-CA) may not be enforceable
- Franchise agreements must be delivered with FDD

### License Agreement Amendments
- Sec 8.2 (Termination by CFA): Bankruptcy termination may be unenforceable under federal law
- Sec 2.5/8.2: CA Bus. & Prof. Code 20000-20043 rights re non-renewal/transfer/termination apply
- Sec 7.3 (Restrictive Covenant): Non-compete may not be enforceable under CA law
- Sec 3.2 (Responsibility): Licensee solely responsible for site selection; CFA approval = minimum standards
- Sec 10.8(a) (Caveats): Replaced -- success is speculative, depends on licensee ability
- Sec 10.10 (new): No waiver of franchise law claims via questionnaire/acknowledgment
- Sec 11.9 (Entire Agreement): Replaced -- licensee may rely on FDD representations; no oral modifications

### Material Franchisee Protections
- Non-compete likely unenforceable post-termination
- Venue/choice-of-law provisions may not be enforced (must litigate in CA)
- Liquidated damages subject to CA Civil Code reasonableness test
- Anti-waiver: No signed acknowledgment can waive franchise law claims or disclaim reliance on franchisor statements

## 12. Washington

**Pages**: ~317-322
**Governing law**: Washington Franchise Investment Protection Act (RCW 19.100.010-19.100.940)

### FDD Modifications (most extensive of all states)
- Applicability: Applies if offer accepted in WA, purchaser is WA resident, or business located/operated in WA
- Conflict of Laws: WA Franchise Investment Protection Act prevails
- Franchisee Bill of Rights: RCW 19.100.180 may supersede License Agreement re termination/renewal
- Forum: Arbitration/mediation site must be in WA or mutually agreed; litigation may be brought in WA
- Termination: CFA must provide 90 days notice, 60 days to cure (with limited exceptions per RCW 19.100.180)
- Transfer: CFA may not unreasonably withhold consent per RCW 19.100.180(2)(i)
- Repurchase: CFA cannot repurchase franchise without consent unless terminated for good cause
- Fair and Reasonable Pricing: Requiring purchase at more than fair/reasonable price is unlawful (RCW 19.100.180(2)(d))
- Treble Damages: Waivers of exemplary/punitive damages void except in negotiated settlements with independent counsel
- Good Faith: Franchisor discretion limited by RCW 19.100.180(1) good faith requirement
- Indemnification: Modified -- no obligation to indemnify for losses caused by franchisor's negligence, willful misconduct, strict liability, or fraud
- Attorneys' Fees: Only if franchisor is prevailing party
- Non-compete: Subject to WA law limitations
- Franchise Broker Advisory: Broker represents franchisor; franchisee advised to evaluate broker info carefully

### License Agreement Amendments
- All FDD modifications are mirrored in the License Agreement amendment
- Same provisions apply (90-day termination notice, 60-day cure, fair pricing, indemnification carve-out, good faith, treble damages, venue in WA, etc.)

### Material Franchisee Protections
- **Most protective state addendum in the entire FDD**
- Mandatory good faith dealing
- 90-day termination notice / 60-day cure
- Transfer consent not unreasonably withheld
- No forced repurchase without consent
- Fair and reasonable pricing requirement
- Indemnification carved out for franchisor negligence/fraud
- Treble damages available under RCW 19.100.190
- Attorneys' fees only for prevailing party (bilateral)
- WA law prevails over all conflicts
- WA venue for arbitration/mediation/litigation

## Notes and Caveats

1. Michigan appears in Exhibit H (effective date March 31, 2025) and in the Receipt (10 business day delivery requirement) but has NO Exhibit G addendum. This is consistent with MI requiring registration but not requiring specific FDD/agreement amendments.
2. Each addendum contains a jurisdictional savings clause: provisions are effective only if the state's jurisdictional requirements are independently met.
3. The integration clause amendment across all states contains what appears to be a copy-paste artifact referencing "California law" even in non-CA state addenda. This is present in the source document.
4. Washington has by far the most extensive protections, functioning essentially as a franchisee bill of rights.
5. New York has the most detailed FDD disclosure additions (cover page, Items 3, 4, 13, 17 multiple sub-items).
6. The base License Agreement is governed by Georgia law (Sec 10.5); state addenda modify or override this where state law requires.
