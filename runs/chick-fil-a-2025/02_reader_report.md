# Reader Report — Chick-fil-A, Inc. License Program FDD (638216-2025)

---

## A. Executive Snapshot

Chick-fil-A, Inc. is a Georgia corporation (formed March 23, 1964) headquartered at 5200 Buffington Road, Atlanta, GA 30349. This FDD covers the **License Program** — non-traditional locations such as universities, hospitals, airports, and corporate campuses — and is separate from the Operator FDD that governs traditional free-standing restaurant franchises. As of December 31, 2024, there were 425 domestic Licensed Units and approximately 2,684 domestic company-operated and franchised Operator Restaurants, totaling approximately 3,109 domestic Chick-fil-A locations.

The License Program has a distinctive economic model: **no initial franchise fee**, a **10% ongoing license fee** on gross receipts (7% for transportation venues), and Chick-fil-A retains ownership of neither the premises nor the equipment. The licensee is responsible for all capital investment ($585,500–$3,437,000). The system grew by a net +13 Licensed Units in 2024 (18 opened, 5 terminated), showing stable but modest growth. Net earnings for the consolidated entity in 2024 were $1.044 billion on $9.063 billion in total revenue. PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP issued a clean audit opinion dated March 21, 2025.

This is an unusually franchisor-friendly structure: no exclusive territory, no guaranteed renewal, no financing offered, no advertising fund contribution, and a broad termination-for-cause clause including pandemic/epidemic events. The system prohibits Sunday and Christmas Day operations, reflecting Chick-fil-A's well-known corporate culture.

## B. Pass 1 — Front Matter, Formats, Entry Structure, Fee Stack, Initial Investment

**Issuance date:** March 31, 2025.

**Format:** This is a License — not a franchise in the traditional sense. Chick-fil-A refers to its licensees as "Licensees" and the agreement as a "License Agreement." Two forms exist: one for Self-Operators (Exhibit B-1) and one for Food Service Providers (Exhibit B-2). A Food Service Provider operates through a contractual arrangement with a third-party site owner. The distinction is material because the FSP form includes additional obligations related to third-party operators.

**Entry structure:** There is no initial fee to obtain a license. You must be approved by Chick-fil-A, select a Licensed Site (subject to CFA's approval), and invest your own capital. Chick-fil-A does not guarantee you additional Licensed Units and expressly reserves the right to never offer one.

**Fee stack:**
- **License Fee:** 10% of Gross Receipts (7% for transportation venues), payable monthly by the 25th.
- **Training Fee:** $250–$400 per additional employee (first two employees are free).
- **Equipment Handling Fee:** 8% of purchase price if purchased through CFA.
- **Audit penalties:** Full underpayment + 12% per annum interest; if understatement exceeds 5%, licensee pays all audit costs.
- **Indemnification:** Variable, as incurred.
- **Late Payment:** 12% per annum interest on amounts 30+ days overdue.
- **No advertising fees, no marketing fund contribution.**

**Initial investment:** $585,500 to $3,437,000 (Self-Operator and Food Service Provider combined). Breakdown:
- Opening inventory/paper supplies: $11,500–$65,000
- Utensils/smallwares: $10,000–$35,000
- Equipment/signage: $100,000–$400,000
- Build-out: $300,000–$1,886,000
- Architectural/drawings: $15,000–$100,000
- Additional funds (first 3 months): $149,000–$951,000

CFA Supply and Bay Center (affiliates) are potential sole-source suppliers. The payment structure flows through approved distributors.

## C. Pass 2 — Supplier Control, Operating Control, Technology Burden, Reporting Burden

**Supplier control (Item 8):** Chick-fil-A exerts strong supplier control. Key proprietary items include proprietary seasoning, proprietary seasoned coater, and proprietary lemon juice (via Bay Center). CFA Supply may be the only approved primary distributor in your geographic area. Chick-fil-A reserves the right to be the sole supplier of any item requiring knowledge of its trade secrets. There are no buying or distribution cooperatives for Licensees.

In 2024, CFA's consolidated revenues from Operator/Licensee product sales and lease-related income was $1,778,691,073 (19.63% of $9.063B total revenue). CFA Supply received $1,996,943,030 from Operator/Licensee purchases. Bay Center received $186,616,788. These affiliate revenue streams are material and represent a significant profit center.

**Operating control (Items 8, 11, 15, 16):** The License Agreement requires compliance with all standards in the operations manual. Licensees must operate units in accordance with the "Chick-fil-A Culture." Units must not operate on Sundays or Christmas Day. Licensees may only sell Authorized Products from the Unit Core Menu approved by CFA. Employees must wear approved Chick-fil-A uniforms. Licensees control day-to-day operations and are solely responsible for employment decisions, but must comply with CFA's comprehensive standards.

**Technology burden (Item 11):** CFA does not currently require specific computer/POS equipment for Licensees but reserves the right to do so. Licensees must obtain and use a POS system and management PC. CFA reserves the right to require data reporting systems, POS data transmission, and participation in gift card, loyalty, and affinity programs. Estimated technology support costs for franchised restaurants range $9,500–$20,000/year (informational benchmark only — Licensee costs may differ).

**Reporting burden:** Licensees must submit Weekly Product Sales Reports, Daily Product Sales Reports, and Calendar Month Sales Summary Reports. Monthly Gross Receipts reports are due by the 5th of each month with POS-generated PDF supporting documentation. License Fees must be received by the 25th.

## D. Pass 3 — Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

**Territory (Item 12):** The license is expressly **non-exclusive**. CFA reserves the right to operate or license others at any location. The only protection: during the License Term, CFA will not operate or license another unit from the same Site where the licensee operates. This is extremely narrow — it protects the specific cafeteria or food court, not the campus, building, or geographic area.

CFA operates Chick-fil-A Restaurants (Operator program), Licensed Units, Captive Venue units, company-operated locations, and affiliate operations (CFA Asia, CFA UK, CFA Puerto Rico, CFA Canada, Truett's Group legacy restaurants). Truett's Group (approximately 18 Dwarf House, Truett's Grill and legacy restaurants) was acquired in October 2024 and is not counted as company-operated for Item 20 purposes.

**Channels:** CFA retains all rights to sell through any channel including mobile, delivery, digital ordering, and catering. Licensees may sell through Sales Channels including telephone, delivery/mobile app, and similar means received at the unit.

**Encroachment risk:** High. The non-exclusive license means CFA can open a full Operator Restaurant steps from your Licensed Unit with no obligation to you. The only meaningful protection is that CFA won't put another unit in the same physical facility.

## E. Pass 4 — Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

**Term (Item 17):** The License Term is stated as a blank in the template agreement — filled in per individual agreement. Renewal is addressed in the agreement (Section 2.5) but is at CFA's discretion.

**Renewal:** CFA has the right but not the obligation to offer renewal. There is no automatic right of renewal. This is unusual and places the licensee at significant risk of non-renewal.

**Transfer:** No transfer without CFA's prior written approval. CFA has the right but not the obligation to approve. No right of first refusal by CFA. No death/disability provisions identified.

**Termination by CFA (curable defaults):** Breach of material duties; damage to goodwill/reputation; failure to comply with hours of operation; material impairment of operations; loss of Licensed Site; failure to comply with government regulation.

**Termination by CFA (non-curable):** Material misrepresentations; felony conviction; conversion/embezzlement; gross receipts understatement by 5%+ on two+ occasions; insolvency/bankruptcy; operations frustrated by law, pandemic, epidemic, war, civil disorder, or labor activity.

**Non-compete during term:** May not sell nationally or regionally branded chicken products or substantially similar products at the Licensed Site.

**Non-compete post-term:** 12 months at the former Licensed Site only — may not sell nationally/regionally branded chicken products or substantially similar products.

**Dispute resolution:** Litigation only (no arbitration). Exclusive jurisdiction in Superior Court of Fulton County, Georgia, or U.S. District Court for Northern District of Georgia, Atlanta Division. Georgia law applies. State addenda may modify for CA, HI, IL, IN, MD, MI, MN, NY, ND, RI, SD, VA, WA, WI.

**Indemnification:** Mutual, but practically asymmetric given CFA's control of the system.
