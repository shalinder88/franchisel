# Final Diligence Report — Motto by Hilton (638064-2025)

## A. Executive Snapshot

1. **System size and growth:** Motto by Hilton is a compact urban lifestyle hotel concept with only 5 franchised hotels open as of December 31, 2024, and zero company-owned outlets. The system grew by just 1 hotel per year in 2023 and 2024. This is an early-stage brand within the massive Hilton Worldwide ecosystem.
2. **Entity structure:** Franchisor is Hilton Franchise Holding LLC (DE LLC, formed September 2007), a wholly owned subsidiary of Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc., which is itself a subsidiary of Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT). The franchisor entity is the single franchisor for ALL Hilton brands in the US — it is not a Motto-specific entity.
3. **Ownership history:** Hilton Hotels Corporation (1946) → Hilton Worldwide Inc. → Park Hotels & Resorts (spin-off January 2017, divesting nearly all company-owned hotels). The Motto brand itself was launched September 14, 2018.
4. **Investment range:** $19.2 million to $91.0 million for a 175-room new development prototype, excluding real estate. This is among the highest investment thresholds in the franchise hotel industry.
5. **Fee burden:** Monthly Royalty 5% GRR + Monthly Program Fee 4% GRR = 9% base load. Plus Hilton Honors 4.3% of eligible folio, Hilton Advance 1.35% of digital direct revenue, and numerous technology, program, and compliance fees. The effective fee burden for a Hilton franchisee is substantial.
6. **Item 19:** NO financial performance representations made. Prospective franchisees receive zero franchisor-sourced performance data.
7. **Bottom-quartile risk:** With only 5 hotels, there is no meaningful statistical universe for performance benchmarking. The brand's viability depends heavily on continued Hilton corporate investment and support. Pipeline of 8 additional hotels is modest.
8. **Financial health:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC (all brands) reported $1.502 billion revenue and $1.488 billion net income in 2024. Operating margin >99%. However, the entity distributes essentially all earnings to parent ($1.5 billion in 2024) and maintains near-zero cash ($317K). All assets pledged as collateral for Hilton parent debt.
9. **Growth trajectory:** Very slow — net +1 per year. 8 signed not yet opened. 1 projected new opening next fiscal year. No terminations, non-renewals, or closures in any of the 3 reported years.
10. **Territory:** Non-exclusive. No protected territory by default. Optional Restricted Area Provision available but time-limited and excludes other Hilton brands.
11. **Renewal:** NO right to renew. Re-licensing at Hilton's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms.
12. **Disputes:** Venue in Eastern District of Virginia or Fairfax County, VA, or New York, NY. Governing law: New York.
13. **Special risks:** Out-of-state dispute resolution highlighted on cover page.
14. **Gag clause:** Present — some current/former franchisees have signed provisions restricting their ability to discuss their experience.

## B. Fee Stack, Entry Structure, Initial Investment

### Front Matter and Entity Structure
The franchisor, Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, is the single licensing entity for all Hilton brands in the United States. It was formed in September 2007 as a Delaware LLC and is headquartered in McLean, Virginia. The Motto brand was added to its portfolio on September 14, 2018.

The parent chain is: Hilton Franchise Holding LLC → Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc. (formed July 12, 2016) → Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT, formed March 18, 2010). The ultimate parent is a publicly traded company.

Hilton, through its subsidiaries, operates 20+ hotel brands including Hilton, Waldorf Astoria, Conrad, DoubleTree, Hampton, Hilton Garden Inn, Home2 Suites, Embassy Suites, Tru, Spark, Tempo, Graduate, and others. In May 2024, Hilton acquired the Graduate brand, adding approximately $85 million in franchise contract intangible assets.

### Fee Stack

**Initial Fees:**
- Franchise Application Fee: $100,000 (new development/conversion); $150,000 (change of ownership); $100,000 (re-licensing). Non-refundable after approval, minus $7,500 processing fee on withdrawal/denial. In 2024, one fee was reduced to $50,000.
- PIP Fee: $10,000 (conversion, COO, re-licensing)
- OnQ Computer System: $99,050–$219,800 (varies by hotel size)
- Other Start-Up Fees: $5,100 (GRO, Digital Floor Plan, Opening Transition Tool)
- Training: $5,000–$17,500
- Opening Process Services: $20,000
- Restaurant Brand Initial Fee: $47,500 per brand (optional)
- Procurement and Services (HSM): 4%–10% of project cost

**Ongoing Fees:**
- Monthly Royalty: 5% of Gross Rooms Revenue. Ramp for first 10 approved applications: 3% years 1–2, 4% years 3–4, 5% thereafter.
- Monthly Program Fee: 4% of GRR. May increase by up to 1% over term. Covers reservation systems, advertising, quality assurance, technology programs.
- Hilton Honors: 4.3% of total eligible guest folio. Waived for on-property enrollments.
- Hilton Advance: 1.35% of Digital Direct Revenue, capped at $30 per stay.
- ResMax: 5.4%–5.9% of consumed revenue from ResMax bookings (required, 3-night max).
- Technology: OnQ connectivity $400–$600/month, hardware/software maintenance $1,932–$5,625/month, Connected Room $718–$1,058/month, Delphi $858/user/year.
- Training/Conferences: Brand conference $2,500/attendee annually (GM + sales director required). GM training up to $1,200/attendee. Sales skills up to $600/attendee.
- Guest Assistance: $200–$300 per handled transaction plus rebate costs.
- Quality Assurance Penalties: Brand Non-Compliance $65–$145/room (max $50K/6 months). Stay Score Failure up to $300/room. Service Quality Improvement $20,000–$50,000/6 months.
- Transfer: $150,000 (COO application), $5,500 (permitted transfer processing), $100,000 (re-licensing).
- Late Payment Interest: 1.5% per month.

**Event-Driven Fees:**
- Liquidated Damages (pre-opening, site reuse): $13,000 per approved room
- Liquidated Damages (post-opening, before 2nd anniversary): Greater of 60× average monthly royalty OR $13,000 × rooms
- Liquidated Damages (post-opening, 2+ years): 60× average monthly royalty
- Liquidated Damages (last 60 months): Average monthly royalty × remaining months
- Unauthorized Opening: $5,000/day
- Plans and Designs Late Fee: $8,000 every 90 days
- PIP/Renovation Failure: $16,000 every 6 months

### Initial Investment (175-Room Prototype)
The total estimated initial investment ranges from $19,235,559 to $90,960,697, excluding real estate, market studies, insurance, and interest. The most significant components:

- Construction/Leasehold Improvements: $8.8M–$65.6M (the main driver of the range)
- FF&E: $5.95M–$8.925M
- Inventory/Operating Equipment: $1.2M–$2.0M
- Permits/Licenses: $132K–$984K
- Pre-Opening/Project Management: $700K–$1.97M
- Contingencies: $880K–$6.56M (10% of construction costs recommended)
- Additional Funds (3 months operating): $800K–$1.2M

These estimates are based on Hilton's 60+ years of experience in lodging but the FDD notes that "because it is a new brand, we do not yet have actual investment information for a Motto by Hilton hotel."

## D. Territory, Competition, Channels, Encroachment

### Protected Territory
The standard Franchise Agreement provides a **non-exclusive license** to operate at a specified location. **There are no provisions granting franchisees a protected area or territory.** Franchisees will not receive an exclusive territory and may face competition from other franchisees, Hilton-owned hotels, or other Hilton brands.

### Optional Restricted Area Provision
Hilton may, at its discretion, agree to a time-limited Restricted Area Provision covering an area surrounding the hotel. Key limitations:
- The Restrictive Period is shorter than the term (typically tied to years from application approval)
- Does not apply to Restaurant Brands
- Does not protect against pre-existing hotels
- Does not protect against other Hilton brands (e.g., Hampton, Hilton Garden Inn can open next door)
- Does not protect against gaming, timeshare, or acquired chains of 4+ hotels
- May reduce in geographic scope over time
- Not typically granted for COO or re-licensing

### Franchisor Rights
Hilton and its affiliates explicitly retain the right to own, operate, manage, franchise, or license any business of any nature, in the lodging/hospitality industry or otherwise, under the Motto brand, a competitive brand, or any other brand, at any time and in any location. The franchisee waives all claims related to these activities.

### Channel Restrictions
No restrictions on customer sales. However, must comply with Standards for services, amenities, and products. Must participate in required sales, distribution, and technology programs. 24/7 operation required.

## E. Contract Burden and Legal Mechanics

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction:** 22 years from the Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10 to 20 years from the Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership:** Remaining term or as agreed
- **NO RIGHT TO RENEW OR EXTEND.** Re-licensing is available only at the franchisor's sole discretion. If re-licensed, the franchisee may be required to sign a contract with materially different terms and conditions, including new Standards, new PIP requirements, and potentially higher fees. This is a significant risk for franchisees making 22-year investments.

### Transfer Provisions
**Permitted Transfers (no change of control):**
- Publicly traded equity: no notice or consent needed
- Private equity (<50%, no control change): no notice needed
- Institutional fund transfers (same manager): no notice needed
- Affiliate, family, trust, death: 60-day notice, consent required, $5,500 processing fee

**Change of Ownership Transfers:**
- 60-day advance notice
- $150,000 application fee
- Full application process (credit, background, operations experience)
- Must sign current form franchise agreement (potentially different terms)
- PIP compliance required
- Not to a Competitor or Sanctioned Person

### Termination and Death/Disability
- Franchisee is NOT authorized to terminate before expiration; unilateral termination is a material breach triggering liquidated damages
- Franchisor can terminate without cause for condemnation or casualty (no liquidated damages if franchisee doesn't operate competing hotel at site within 3 years)
- Curable defaults: payment (10 days), construction (10 days), insurance (10 days), other violations (30 days)
- Non-curable defaults include: repeated violations, 3 notices in 12 months, bankruptcy, loss of possession, 5-day non-operation, fraud, unauthorized transfer, becoming a Competitor, cross-default
- On death: transfer to immediate family within 1 year, must meet then-current requirements

### Noncompete
- **During term:** Cannot indirectly or directly own or be licensor/franchisor of a competing hotel brand. May own minority interest in a Competitor, be a franchisee of a Competitor, or manage for a Competitor under stated conditions.
- **Post-term:** NO post-term noncompete obligation. This is favorable to franchisees.

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue:** U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Virginia (Alexandria), or if no federal jurisdiction, a state court in Fairfax County, Virginia, or New York, New York
- **Franchisor may sue where the hotel is located**
- **Puerto Rico:** All disputes settled by arbitration (AAA/JAMS), venue Fairfax County, VA, seat New York, NY
- **Governing law:** New York (without choice-of-law principles), subject to federal trademark law

### Litigation History
- **Active:** Two pending matters — a breach of contract dispute (AAAA Property Partners, M.D. Fla.) and an antitrust class action (Extended Stay Hotel Antitrust, N.D. Cal., involving IDeaS revenue management software).
- **Notable settled:** Soule v. Hilton Worldwide (Hawaii resort fee class action, settled 2015 for $178K + vouchers).
- **Historical:** US v. Hilton Hotels Corp (1970 antitrust, consent judgment 1971).
- **No collection suits against franchisees in 2024.**

## J. Unresolveds

1. **Marketing fund transparency** (severity: medium) — The 4% Monthly Program Fee is not separately audited. No spending breakdown or financial reporting is provided to franchisees. Source: Item 6 Note 2, p. 38.
2. **No FPR for $19M–$91M investment** (severity: medium) — With 5 open hotels, Hilton could theoretically disclose performance data but chooses not to. This is legally permissible but leaves prospective franchisees relying entirely on independent research. Source: Item 19, p. 88.
3. **Early-stage brand risk** (severity: medium) — Only 5 hotels open after 6+ years of franchising. The pipeline of 8 is concentrated in a few states. Hilton has 20+ brands competing for development resources. Source: Item 20, pp. 88–90.
4. **Hilton debt guarantee exposure** (severity: high) — Hilton Franchise Holding LLC guarantees parent company debt maturing 2025–2033. All assets and franchise contracts are pledged. While Hilton Worldwide is financially strong (HLT is a blue-chip stock), the subsidiary entity's assets are encumbered. Source: Note 7, p. 109.
5. **"New brand" boilerplate inconsistency** (severity: low) — Item 7 Note 22 states "because it is a new brand, we do not yet have actual investment information" despite 5 operating hotels. Source: p. 44.
6. **Subsequent events date typo** (severity: low) — Note 9 states "March 18, 2024" which should likely be March 18, 2025 given the March 30, 2025 issuance date. Source: Note 9, p. 109.
7. **Gag clause** (severity: medium) — With only 5 open hotels, the gag clause restricting some franchisees from speaking materially limits due diligence options. Source: Item 20, p. 90.
