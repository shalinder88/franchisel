# Reader Report — Motto by Hilton (638064-2025)

## Pass A — Executive Snapshot

1. **Brand:** Motto by Hilton — a compact urban lifestyle hotel concept launched September 2018, part of the Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (NYSE: HLT) portfolio of 20+ brands.
2. **Franchisor:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC, a Delaware LLC formed September 2007, headquartered in McLean, VA. Wholly owned subsidiary of Hilton Domestic Operating Company Inc.
3. **System size:** Extremely small — 5 franchised hotels open as of December 31, 2024 (AR, DC, NY×2, PA), 0 company-owned, 8 signed but not yet open. This is an early-stage brand within a mature franchisor.
4. **Investment range:** $19,235,559 to $90,960,697 for a 175-room new development prototype, excluding real estate. This is a high-investment-threshold franchise.
5. **Fee burden:** Monthly Royalty 5% GRR + Monthly Program Fee 4% GRR + Hilton Honors 4.3% of eligible folio + Hilton Advance 1.35% of digital direct revenue + numerous additional program fees. Effective ongoing fee burden is among the heaviest in the hotel franchise industry.
6. **Item 19:** NO financial performance representations made. No FPR data available.
7. **Financial health (franchisor):** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC revenue $1.502 billion (2024), net income $1.488 billion. Total assets $1.270 billion. Entity distributes nearly all earnings to parent ($1.5B in 2024). Guarantor of Hilton parent debt.
8. **Territory:** Non-exclusive license. No protected territory by default. Optional Restricted Area Provision available but time-limited and does not apply to other Hilton brands.
9. **Renewal:** NO right to renew. Re-licensing available at franchisor's sole discretion with potentially materially different terms.
10. **Term:** 22 years (new construction), 10–20 years (conversion).
11. **Noncompete:** During term only — cannot be licensor/franchisor of competing brand. NO post-term noncompete.
12. **Dispute resolution:** Venue E.D. Virginia (Alexandria) or Fairfax County, VA, or New York, NY. Governing law: New York. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
13. **Liquidated damages:** Post-opening termination: greater of 60× average monthly royalty or $13,000/approved room. Pre-opening: $13,000/room.
14. **Gag clause:** Present — some current/former franchisees have signed provisions restricting ability to speak.
15. **Growth trajectory:** Net +1 unit each in 2023 and 2024. 8 pipeline hotels. Very early-stage system with slow growth for a Hilton brand.

## Pass C — Supplier Control, Operations, Training, Tech (Items 8–11)

### Item 8 — Restrictions on Sources
- Must use OnQ proprietary computer system (no third-party alternative for software)
- Must purchase exterior signage from licensed vendor
- Must use HSM (Hilton Supply Management) for procurement (4%–10% fee) if chosen, but generally not required except for certain specified items
- Must meet all Standards for supplies and services
- Quality assurance inspections with fees for non-compliance

### Item 10 — Financing
- Development Incentive Program: contingent financial contribution from Hilton for new hotels
  - Not a loan; repayable if terminated early or transferred
  - Repayable amount decreases annually over term
  - Interest at 1.5%/month if repayment is late
- May occasionally allow installment payment of Franchise Application Fee
- No other direct financing generally offered

### Item 11 — Assistance/Advertising/Computers/Training
**Pre-opening obligations:**
1. Manual provided (electronic access on Hilton Intranet)
2. Review and approve architect, interior designer, contractor selections
3. Review and approve construction plans (cannot start without approval)
4. Review and approve proposed management
5. HITS Agreement for OnQ system
6. Purchase/lease arrangements for signs, equipment, supplies
7. Restaurant Brand design consultation (if applicable)
8. Training programs specified

**Computer System (mandatory):**
- OnQ System: $99,050–$219,800 initial; $400–$600/month connectivity; $1,932–$5,625/month maintenance
- Guest Internet Access (StayConnected): $84,039–$122,815 initial; $1,824–$6,300/month ongoing
- GRO System (IDeaS): ~$3,400 initial; no ongoing fee currently
- Connected Room: $39,975–$47,775 initial; $718–$1,058/month maintenance
- Delphi (Amadeus): $900–$33,000 initial; $858/user/year license
- Digital Floor Plan: included in start-up fees
- Digital Key: included in OnQ
- Refresh cycles: OnQ every 3 years; Guest Internet every 4 years

**Training:**
- GM and Commercial/Sales Leader training: up to $1,200/attendee (virtual, within 90 days of start)
- Hilton Core Sales Skills: up to $600/attendee

**Advertising/Marketing:**

- Does not cover costs of optional marketing programs
- Marketing fund not separately audited (incorporated into consolidated Hilton financials)

## Pass D — Territory, Channels, Encroachment (Items 12–16)

### Item 12 — Territory
- **Non-exclusive license** to operate at a specified location
- **No protected territory by default**
- Hilton may own, operate, manage, franchise any hotel (including competing brands) anywhere, anytime
- **Optional Restricted Area Provision:**
  - Negotiated on case-by-case basis
  - Restrictive Period shorter than term (usually tied to years from application approval)
  - Does not apply to Restaurant Brands
  - Does not protect against pre-existing hotels
  - Does not protect against other Hilton brands
  - Does not protect against gaming, timeshare, or acquired chains (4+ hotels)
  - May reduce in geographic scope over time
- Hilton may modify System, use System elements in Other Businesses
- Franchisee waives claims related to these rights

### Item 13 — Trademarks
- Marks owned by Hilton International Holding LLC (affiliate)
- Licensed royalty-free to Hilton Franchise Holding LLC
- Sublicensed to franchisees
- Multiple trademark registrations on principal register

### Item 14 — Patents/Copyrights
- Standard proprietary information provisions
- Manual and operational materials are confidential property of affiliate

### Item 15 — Obligation to Participate
- Must designate a general manager acceptable to Hilton
- GM must complete required training
- Management company may be required if proposed management not approved

### Item 16 — Restrictions on Sales
- No restrictions on customers
- Must comply with standards for services, amenities, products
- 24/7 operation required
- Restaurant Brand operations must meet specified standards
- Cannot change room count or make significant changes without approval
- Timeshare/vacation ownership at or adjacent to hotel requires written permission
- Cannot share hotel operations with another lodging facility without permission

## Pass E — Contract Burden, Legal Mechanics (Item 17)

### Term and Renewal
- **New Construction:** 22 years from Effective Date
- **Conversion:** 10–20 years from Opening Date
- **Change of Ownership:** remaining term or other agreed term
- **NO right to renew or extend** — re-licensing available only at franchisor's sole discretion
- Re-licensing may require materially different terms, new PIP

### Termination
**By franchisee:** Not authorized before expiration. Unilateral termination = material breach → liquidated damages.

**By franchisor without cause:**
- Condemnation: may terminate, releases liquidated damages
- Casualty: hotel must reopen within 18 months or franchisor may terminate

**By franchisor with cause (curable):**
- Payment default (10-day cure)
- Construction deadline failure (10-day cure)
- Insurance non-compliance (10-day cure)
- Other FA/Standard violations (30-day cure)
- Interim remedies: suspension from reservation service, directory removal, software disable

**By franchisor (non-curable):**
- Repeated same non-compliance within 24 months
- 3 material default notices in 12 months
- Insolvency/bankruptcy
- Loss of possession
- 5+ consecutive days non-operation (unless force majeure)
- Contesting System ownership
- Conduct adverse to reputation
- Fraud/false records
- Unauthorized transfer
- Sanctions/OFAC violation
- Gaming license risk
- Guarantor breach
- Public health threat
- Becoming a Competitor
- Cross-default from other Hotel Agreements

### Transfer
**Permitted Transfers (no change of control):**
- Publicly traded equity: no notice needed
- Private equity (<50%, no control change): no notice needed
- Institutional fund transfers (same asset manager): no notice needed
- Affiliate, family, trust, death: 60-day notice + consent + $5,500 processing fee

**Change of Ownership Transfers:**
- 60-day notice + full application process
- $150,000 application fee
- Must sign current form FA (potentially different terms)
- PIP compliance required
- Background/credit check of transferee
- Not to Competitor or Sanctioned Person

**Death/disability:** Transfer to immediate family within 1 year, must meet requirements.

### Noncompete
- **During term:** Cannot be licensor/franchisor of competing brand. May own minority interest, be franchisee of, or manage for a Competitor under certain conditions.
- **Post-term:** NONE

### Dispute Resolution
- **Venue:** U.S. District Court, E.D. Virginia (Alexandria), or Fairfax County, VA state court, or New York, NY. Franchisor may sue where hotel is located.
- **Puerto Rico:** Arbitration (AAA/JAMS), venue Fairfax County, VA, seat New York, NY
- **Governing law:** New York (without choice-of-law principles)
- **No jury trial waiver expressly stated** in the FDD table
- **No mediation requirement** (except potentially via state addenda)

### Liquidated Damages
- **Pre-opening (before Hotel Work, site reused within 1 year):** $13,000 × approved rooms
- **Post-opening, before 2nd anniversary:** Greater of (60 × average monthly royalty) or ($13,000 × rooms)
- **Post-opening, after 2nd anniversary, before last 60 months:** 60 × average monthly royalty
- **Last 60 months of term:** Average monthly royalty × remaining months

### Unresolveds
1. **Marketing fund transparency:** Monthly Program Fee (4% GRR) is not separately audited. No separate financial reporting on how these funds are spent. Spending breakdown not disclosed.
2. **No FPR:** With only 5 open hotels, the absence of Item 19 data is understandable but leaves prospective franchisees with no franchisor-provided performance benchmarks.
3. **Early-stage brand risk:** Only 5 open hotels after 6+ years of franchising. Pipeline of 8 is modest. Brand viability depends heavily on continued Hilton corporate support.
4. **Restricted Area Provision details:** The FDD describes the optional territorial protection conceptually but does not disclose what percentage of franchisees actually receive one or the typical geographic scope.
5. **Hilton debt guarantee:** Hilton Franchise Holding LLC unconditionally guarantees certain Hilton parent debt obligations (maturing 2025–2033). All assets and franchise contracts pledged as collateral.
6. **Graduate brand acquisition:** May 2024 acquisition of Graduate franchise contracts (~$85M intangible) may affect resources and focus.
7. **Subsequent events date discrepancy:** Note 9 states events evaluated through "March 18, 2024" — this appears to be a typographical error and should likely read March 18, 2025.
